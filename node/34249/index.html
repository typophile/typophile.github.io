June 5, 2007 • clauses

Hi there I’m trying to make a a MM font in Fontlab 5. I have one weight axis. I build the MM I’m working on from two endpoints, a thin and a black. The problem is that if I now try to delete a glyph, say the black ’m', and then paste a replacement in it’s place, Fontlab replaces both the thin and the black ’m'. I can’t find any mention of that in the manual – is it impossible to do?

Another thing is the kerning. How can I (can I at all?) import metrics and kerning from other files into the MM font? I’m using identical kerning classes in the thin and the black, but I would like to take the metrics and kerning from the two start designs, thin and black, and put them in the MM version. How to do?

Hi, the masters are connected, so you cannot delete/change only one. Normally I copy the new glyph to the mask and move the points of the Master to align with the mask. OR making to single master fonts and blend them and copy the glyph to the original MM font.

if you import metrics in the metrics window, they apply the the selected master. So you can select the Thin master, import the thin afm file, switch to Black and import the black afm file.

This is what I would do:

  1. copy the source glyph
  2. go into the glyph window of the target
  3. paste
  4. Cmd+K, Cmd+M, Cmd+X
  5. activate the master you want to be changed to the source
  6. Cmd+J

alternatively, if your source is already an “m” in another font:

  1. assign mask, select the source font and the right master (or assign all)
  2. activate the master you want to be changed to the source
  3. Cmd+J

cmd+j is the same as: tools > multiple master > mask to master.

what i would do in this situation is to paste a copy of the new black m to the mask layer (make sure there’s nothing else in the mask), activate the black master and then cmd+j.
