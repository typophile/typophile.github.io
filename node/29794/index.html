Cheaper Bookman Italic Swash

November 29, 2006 • Reed Reibstein

For my school’s centennial, we’re recreating the school newspaper’s past nameplates, one in each new issue. The one we’re up to (originally from around 1990, but I don’t think we were digital at that point) uses [[Bookman]] Italic (not ITC Bookman) with some swash characters. In looking for a font online to approximate it, I’ve only been able to find Jukebox Type’s <a href=http://www.veer.com/products/typedetail.aspx?image=JBT0000087>Bookman JF, which does match it almost exactly. The problem is that the font family costs $119, which is out of my publication’s price range (especially after buying Farnham and Mercury for our redesign). I was wondering if anyone knows of another digitization of Bookman that preserves the Th swash ligature and the R swashes present in the nameplate attached below. I haven’t been able to find one after much searching, but maybe someone around here could help me out. Thanks very much in advance.
