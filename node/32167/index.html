2007-03-13 12:59:39 • Mister M
> That’s my point I want to use a font that hasn’t been used heavily. There's a red flag. Why wouldn't you want to use something that works well, just because others have used it? Don't think that a designers job is to make things that are unique ... it is to make things that are usable. A form is a case where utilitarianism is probably the key requirement of a job. If you want to put your personal stamp on the project, then do so in the header and other elements of the form. Keep the body of the form (labels, etc.) as clear and simple as possible. Your clients will love it if your work minimizes mistakes and omissions, and will completely ignore whether it looks unique. One thing, while Freight Sans does look nice don't try to physically compress it ... this usually spoils the readability of a font. And don't reject Helvetica Condensed because "real designers don't use Helvetica." It is Helvetica that is over used, not Helvetica Condensed.

2007-03-13 14:35:04 • John Nolan
I would look at faces that stand up well to poor printing and photocopying. If they are pdfs, they will inevitably be printed out on a lo-res laser printer at some point. And forms will almost certainly be photocopied. I would also look at faces with distinct numerals. I would suggest Officina (sans or serif), Meta, or maybe FF Strada (which according to the fontfont blurb is intended for forms). They are robust enough to stand up to abuse. I would steer clear of any width of Helvetica, because at small sizes, poorly reproduced, the numerals can look very similar (3=8=9 etc) which is tiresome.
2007-03-13 16:07:45 • typofoto
At this point Helvetica and Univers condensed are not appropriate choices because their usefulness for forms has been superseded by a host of new designs that are easier to read in the same settings, and as a side benefit, are probably more economical. Choosing them now means that you have them sitting around, and it's easy because we're all used to seeing them on forms and we don't question that. Being habituated to their use doesn't make them the best choice. Freight is terrific but probably wrong for forms. The whole slew of vaguely-humanist, somewhat condensed info sans designs would be easy to mine for something appropriate. Consider Amplitude, Fago, Page Sans & Serif, Strada Condensed, Zwo, Slate Condensed, the Mentor family (serif and sans), Zemestro, etc. Lots of new options including superfamilies, width variants, and finely-tuned weights.
