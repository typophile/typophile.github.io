BLAST Magazine Serif Text face resembles De Vinne

September 12, 2013 • hashiama

Found this sample in [[http://dl.lib.brown.edu/mjp/render.php?id=1158591480633184&view=mjp_object|Blast Magazine]] by Wyndham Lewis.

Later digital versions that are similar but not quite (see the large ear in /g/ for example) include [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/hawthorn-lt/|Hawthorne]] and [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/bitstream/romana/|Romana]].

—start quote— Blast 1 was edited and largely written by Wyndham Lewis … Inside, Lewis used a range of bold typographic innovations and tricks to engage the reader…. —end quote— source: http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blast_%28magazine%29 Perhaps Lewis included tinkering with the DeVinne font, which was widely used in 1914, as part of his typographic innovations.
