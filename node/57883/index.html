I think what Maya is looking for – at least at this point – are fonts that have characters with underdots, not a discussion on the various ways they can be created.

It looks like the Times Roman Simon showed has this capability. I didn’t know of any fonts where all the Latin characters have been covered; looks like the new Times does this. Is the feature written so it works with InDesign, or just MS Word?

But the comment on needing consonants with underdots raises another issue, Maya. Are you looking for a mechanism which will let you put a dot under any character? If so, that would usually be a function of the layout program – usually termed a floating accent. To the best of my knowledge, neither InDesign nor Quark have this. TeX does, but the “floating accent” primitive is relatively simple, and positioning sometimes suffers. With TeX, you can write a more sophisticated routine for individual positioning, but I doubt that’s going to happen with InDesign or Quark.
