As a graphic design student, you know that it is more than font selection that lets you arrive at the proper mix of harmony and tension. (Don’t forget the tension – without it, all you have is bland.)

You can make Universe 45 Light work with Sabon in a book, where the text is, say, 10-point, and Univers is used for chapter titles, say 20-point. Now, how are you going to make the subheads work – subheads being roughly the same size as the text? Probably, you’re going to have to use Sabon.

The other point I’d make is unless you’re going to be a web designer, where I imagine you can’t control much of the space, “harmony” will be as much a factor of the white space as type. That, of course, doesn’t show with your sample; there is no top, bottom, left, right – no trim.

dear cory.lasser,

The problem of combining type is normally posed when setting extended text, as in the typesetting of a book, where the serifed face will serve the basic text, and a sans-serif the notes and other marginalia. Here we should not be surprised that harmony is the desired effect.

Let us turn the microphone to Bringhurst (§6.5.5):

Pair serifed and unserifed faces on the basis of their inner structure

Pay attention to particular letter forms, such the lower-case g (one story or two?). Compare both the roman and italics of both faces (very important). Read up on the faces to see if you can discern a common ancestry. You should quickly conclude that Univers and Sabon fail many of these tests.

The bard himself goes on to recommend pairing Sabon (a Renaissance face designed by Jan Tschichold) with Hans Eduard Meier’s [[http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/adobe/syntax/|Syntax]].

If you want to look further afield for the sake of ``design'' (or because you are not typesetting a book), you might fancy Martin Majoor’s [[http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/fontfont/ff-scala-sans/|Scala Sans]] or even his [[http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/fontfont/seria-sans/|Seria Sans]], two faces that themselves have a serifed pair that you might consider in lieu of Sabon.

Univers has little to recommend it in this game, but if you admire the work of its designer (Adrian Frutiger), Bringhurst recommends marrying [[http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/meridien/|Méridien]] with the eponymous [[http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/adobe/frutiger/|Frutiger]].

Of course, I won’t discount the chance that you are pairing two different typefaces in order to make a ``statement.'' This can be done, but you should be very clear about what the statement is.
