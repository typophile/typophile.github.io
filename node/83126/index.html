Remember that it's simple to distort letterforms, so you may be looking at something that has been stretched/flattened. Another trick that kills IDers is to swap a letter or two from a similar font. 2011-06-20 23:59:51 • bowfinpw
Hi Mike, Thanks for your comment - and everything you're saying is true. And the capital "C" is particularly squished in this typeface. Taking Mike's comment into account, I think the letterform that will help identify this face is the lower case "r" - it seems to be pretty unique and I haven't come across one like it with other similar typefaces. Others' stems don't stand up enough, and the open counters almost always have a curvature to it and rise above the top of the stem, instead of being short, truncated, and flat. The "M" is also pretty unique, with a very upright posture, slightly wider shoulders, and having an "empire waistline" with the location of the vertex. Anyone else have luck finding this typeface? Thanks! danse.
