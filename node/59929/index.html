Hello Luca, you are talking about old-style figures (OSF, also called [[text figures]], with ascenders and descenders), versus [[lining figures]] (LF, also ranging figures, all of the same height). The OpenType versions of Janson Text do have both versions, OSF and LF. So do most recent OpenType releases of text fonts, btw. You can view the available glyphs in the character map: http://www.linotype.com/en/422933/jansontextpro55roman-charactermap.html Yes, in savvy applications like Adobe InDesign, you can (separately or globally) switch the figure style via an OpenType feature. Not all layout applications support OpenType features. So although a font may have alternatives to the default figure style, they could be inaccessible. Therefore it's important to check which is the default style of figure in the font, as this can vary according to the foundry's opinion of what would be most appropriate.
