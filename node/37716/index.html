Imprimerie F.Schmidt, 1908

October 2, 2007 • lookleft

Hi there, I’m starting a Revival Project and I fell in love with a very nice French Type from a book made in the 1908, the editor is Louis-Michaud, the “Imprimerie” is F.Schmidt and they were working in Paris. I would like to know something about this type, but in Holland there are not may specimen of French foundries. Can someone help me? many thanks

I researched this in the Jaspert ‘Encyclopaedia of Type Faces’ and Updike’s ‘Printing Types’ and could not find anything to match it. However, there was something that seemed very familiar about the odd script-like letterforms, also like a vertical italic, and it reminded me of Benton’s ‘Announcement Roman’. Many of the lower case letters are similar, although many are not. This typeface has been revived by Canada Type as Odette.

I had missed this thread, but did run into its iteration on the ATypI list; here’s my reply on that list in case anybody is still curious about it:

" That font looked familiar to me, and I immediately looked at my copies of Audin’s books, since that’s such a singular repository for funky old French stuff. The roman is shown in figure 125 of volume 3 as “Type Beaudoire” #2 (the #1 is actually even more fascinating). The italic is a few pages down in figure 141, shown as the font “XXe Siècle”* by Mayeur. I remember from the time I translated Ponot’s article about Perrin that there’s a connection between Perrin, Beaudoire and Mayeur (and Marquet). IIRC one of them swiped a design from one other, with the help of another, or something. :-) I can find out if it’s important.
