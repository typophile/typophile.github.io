Abandoned or Forgotten Commissioned Typefaces

April 13, 2009 • Dan Gayle

In the Adobe Myriad CAD font discussion, John Hudson brought up the following point about some custom commissioned fonts: “I’ve no idea what happened to the fonts, but presumably IBM owns them, whether they realise it or not.”

With all of these financial institutions, tech companies, etc., going under, I assume that quite a few custom typefaces are going to hit the wastebin.

I think it would be interesting to try to make a list of all of the commissioned typefaces that have been abandoned and/or forgotten, and perhaps find a way to revert them back to their designers or even open source them. (GASP!)

Any thoughts?

I wonder what the legal side of that is if you design something and this happens. If your contract states what happens that is one thing, but if you have no clause regarding the question… Certainly it seem like the designer should be given back the rights but that doesn’t mean that that is how the legal system works. Also if the company has debts that IP might be seen as an asset. The easy thing might be to buy it even if it is for a low or nominal cost.
