2006-10-12 18:46:03 • pattyfab

A widescreen iPod is still just an LCD, and comes with all the qualities of LCD screens that killed the first round of ebook readers. I’m very excited by the prospect of rendering text with digital ink from Phillips, but since Sony seems to have the exclusive right to market the technology right now, I expect that it will be a while before we start seeing affordable ebook readers–much less ebook readers with an interface that anyone would actually want to use.

Microsoft’s new “Vista technology” for reformatting the New York Times sounds exciting, because I’ve read somewhere that there will be special readers produced for it that aren’t actually full-price tablet PCs. If this “Vista technology” (Cleartype?) end up on low-power tablet systems with bright OLED screens that should start appearing in the next decade it could be pretty sweet. Unfortunately it’s pretty much impossible to sort the truth from the BS that spews forth from the gaping maw of Microsoft’s horrid marketing department, so I’m reserving judgment until January.

Of course, if someone doesn’t reign in the publishers on the pricing front, it’s never going to matter. Consumers aren’t stupid, and they know that there’s no good reason an ebook often costs as much as a printed version.
