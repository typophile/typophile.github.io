Buying a font for a client and myself

October 24, 2006 • spirelli

Hi, I want to purchase a font from myfonts.com for a client. I think that they generally sell licenses that allow a font to be used on 5 computers. My client should be able to use the font on a limited number of machines as well as I on one machine. Can I buy a license over my myfonts.com account, so that the client can use it on 4 machines and I can use it on mine, or do I have to buy 2 licenses, which seems a bit of a waste of money to me. If I didn't use the font, could I still buy a license for my client? Or should they buy it themselves? Many thanks for answers.
No. You can't buy a 5-user license and split it between two locations. Technically, you are one user (locations) and your client another. You can buy a license for your client, but for the sake of keeping an ordered paper trail, I would suggest your client licenses it themselves.

Transferring is allowed by some EULAs. So, technically, yes, you could do the work and when you are finished transfer the rights of use to your client. Because I'm paranoid I would always call the foundry before licensing and let them know what I'm planning to do. Let them answer that it is ok. Cover all bets so to speak. Then when you are going to transfer the fonts, get back in touch with the foundry again and ask them how they require you to do it.
