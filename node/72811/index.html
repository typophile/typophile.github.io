2010-08-02 09:34:38 • Florian Hardwig

NTV, A Turkish TV station, employs this typeface to set the text in their news slider (located at the bottom). Weirdly enough, in order to set the descriptions at the bottom of news pieces, they employ the proper version of DIN, i.e. one with a curved hat above ğ, and a disjoint/angled hook below ç and ş. This has either gone unnoticed or is being done on some purpose, maybe out of a size consideration.

All told, I am led to believe there is an off-the-shelf package that features straight breves and joint/perpendicular hooks.
