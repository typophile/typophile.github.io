2011-12-21 22:14:25 • russellm
My gripe isn’t with the quality of OCR (it’s good beyond my wildest expectation) but with the willingness of publishers to put their name to the few but distracting errors which occur. Any OCR’d book was presumably adequately copy-edited and proof-read in the original, which dates from ‘back in the day’, so we don’t really need to repeat that unless taking issue with anachronistic punctuation etc., all we really have to worry about is mis-read characters in the OCR process. These invariably result in gibberish; it doesn’t need a skilled proof-reader to fix them, just anyone who knows how to use a spell-checker, and probably 1/10th of the time that proof-reading would take. I suspect the smaller and more specialised the publisher, the better this gets done, because their reputation is more sensitive to quality; certainly mine will be, and I take a lot of care over it. Of course my time is free while I have the day job. But I don’t buy cost as the problem for larger publishers, just the fact that no-one gives a damn, because repeat business is not at risk; the reader won’t generally know or care who the publisher is.
