2006-05-18 10:15:23 • SuperUltraFabulous
> Mahogany could be a knock-off of Monterey I couldn't tell you for certain what face Mahogany Script was originally based on, but I can tell you it was released in 1981 from the "Custom Type" group collection at Compugraphic. That means that a CG customer had requested the typeface to be produced based on another specimen for their specific use, most likely done for an EditWriter filmstrip font years before. Many of the early CG customers were "Mom & Pop" shop printers, so Mahogany Script was most likely based on one of the thousands of Wedding Scripts printers used for handset invitations. I am not trying to ID the original for you, just giving you a little background on how it got into the Monotype Imaging collection.
