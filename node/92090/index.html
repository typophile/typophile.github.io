Wanted: text on what the original type designers were trying to accomplish

April 13, 2012 • inkexit

I mean ‘original type designers’ as rather a loose term. Anybody from Gutenberg to Garamond. I mean “trying to accomplish” not as in making a profit, or maintaining legibility, but rather a more ambiguous thing such what aspects a font would have to have to be considered a good font in their eyes. I am looking for the secrets, not that which they may have openly talked about in a public setting. A type designer apprentice’s notebook, apprenticing with a great master, would be perfect, if such a thing could be found.
If you can read something other than English: Anne Cuneo, Le maître de Garamond, 2002 Is available in German and Italian too. It’s an historical novel (Adrian Frutiger did the fact-checking on all matters typographic) which, among other things, tries to answer your very own question.
