2015-01-26 04:50:19 • smashkirk

Mercury does have serifs that match those in the words Never Modern but it is indeed a different font.

Comparing Starling to Times Roman I noted the following differences: Some letters, such as a and n, are wider. Longer descenders. Cove serifs with thinner ends. Terminal balls, such as a and r, are heavier. Tips, such as e, are thinner. Various differences in stroke emphasis.

The two fonts are otherwise rather similar in proportions and are easy to compare because the ratio of lower case to upper case is almost identical.
