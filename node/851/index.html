"normal people" buying fonts

August 7, 2003 • cph

A friend of mine who is not a professional in any field that you'd buy fonts for ran across my Typographica article today and said to me:

"I was reading your font article. I like the Snell Roundhand Script combined with Stempel Garamond Roman used in Font of the Week [on the Linotype Library web site]. I'll have to think of something to use it for. I used to buy fonts now and then for my web designs but because I lack organisation, I lose them on old hard drives. Thus, I gave up looking. I'm afraid to start up again … it would be just like [buying books you like even though you don't intend to read them right away]!"

She also said:
"I consider myself the average user. I would say the average user purchases fonts, especially if they are say in the 25+ bracket. I buy them one at a time. I see one I like, I buy it."

I wonder what the ratio of people like this to font pirates is.
