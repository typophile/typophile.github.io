2005-07-04 17:40:51 • dezcom

I spent a lot of time working out the small caps. Small caps for sans serif fonts are somewhat unusual, so I was not quite sure how best to proceed. Choosing the small cap height turned out to be trickier than getting the weight to balance well with the caps.

At first they were going to be just a hair larger than the lowercase. I did it that way because in my research most of the other examples I found did it that way. It didn’t feel quite exactly right to me, but they were done, they were okay, and I really didn’t want to go back and start all over.

Then there was a discussion on Typophile (about four or five months ago I think) about the confusion that can arise with plural acronyms set in small caps with a lowercase “s” when they are close to the same height. It convinced me that it was worth redoing them. So I did, and I’m really glad I did.
