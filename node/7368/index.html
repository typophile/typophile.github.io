Dan, are you making a distinction between publishers and resellers? My definition of a reseller is a vendor who sells font licences to an end user for typefaces that are also available directly from the original designer or publisher and, usually, through other resellers. A publisher is a company that exclusively licenses a typeface from a design, often involving transfer of copyright, and publishes and distributes it as part of its own library, which may or may not involve arrangements with resellers.

The appropriate contract terms, royalty rates, etc. differ considerably, depending on whether the designer is dealing with a reseller or a publisher.

I presume you are also interested only in the retail font license market? OEM deals are a whole different ball of wax.

By the way, be sure to contact Jeremy Tankard. I'm a big admirer of the way he conducts his business with resellers, and think many of my fellow retail type designers can learn some stuff from him.
