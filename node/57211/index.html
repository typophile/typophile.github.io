Why would you need an opentype feature for the (french) Œ and (scandinavian) Æ in the first place? They are (AFAIK) considered separate and unique glyphs and are never typed in as OE and AE - always as a separate glyph on the keyboard. Unless your font is just for setting old, archaic text where you want to force OE into Œ and EA into Æ you don't need to program this in OpenType - all those who need these glyphs should know how to type them in directly...

Martĳn, if you want, you can duplicate your AE and OE glyphs as "A_E" and "O_E" for uppercase, and "a_e" and "o_e" for lowercase, and then include a "dlig" substitution for them. I'd also include them in "hlig" (historical ligatures). There are indeed cases (such as in English) where one might want to use a ligature in ae and oe. For example, in contemporary English, the word "aesthetically" is spelled with "ae" and not "æ" but in some cases, designers may want to use them to evoke a historical or more stylized spelling of the word, which would look like "æsthetically" then, but typing in the "æ" letter from the keyboard is not what they would usually want. Cheers, Adam
