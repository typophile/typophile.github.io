2007-01-27 07:02:19 • SuperUltraFabulous
Adobe still has a type group of about ten people. In fact, we just hired a new font production person and budding type designer last spring (and advertised the position on Typophile, my blog, and many other places) - Typophile regular Typophile thread My own Hypatia Sans Pro is in the works. We're running out of time, so Miguel and Robert will be doing the kerning, with me just keeping an eye on it and making a few suggestions. Meanwhile I've been spending my time polishing the outlines, with considerable input from Robert. Robert's typeface "Gauge" (working title only) is more of a prototype at this point, and I can't tell you anything about a release date, but it can be seen to some extent in the new Adobe product icons that have been blogged about Christopher Slye has been busy with the tiny project of doing technical fixes to our entire library, for a planned future dot release. Oh, and don't ask me about schedule for any of the above. I can't say, except that Arno and Hypatia Sans will ship with some Adobe products in the near future. So, we're not dead yet. Cheers, T

2007-02-06 16:21:57 • twardoch
Yes, it's named after Hypatia of Alexandria. I was looking for an interesting philosopher who was into geometry, so as to imply a straddling of the humanist and geometric genres. The "sans" in the name does leave the door open for other variants, but we'll see how exhausted I am after this one. 3000 glyphs per font is about 2000 more than I really want to do again. However, I do occasionally fantasize about doing a slab serif, and then finally a serifed face in the same extended family. However, I have another (smaller) type design project ahead of that in my queue, and I still need to finish Hypatia Sans. So don't hold your breath for any other variants. Cheers, T
