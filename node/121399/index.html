I’m having troubles finding the font(s) used in the Hennessy logo. For “Hennessy”, “Cognac” and the script font at top. I found some similarities with Gazzette, but the font used in the logo is more extended.

![](/images/old-images/hennytypophile2 copy_6444.jpg)

Sounds probably “lazy” but to me a slightly edited [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/adobe/times-new-roman|Times New Roman]] might work for ‘Hennessy’ and ‘COGNAC’ mainly squooshing it and curving top /n serif. [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/fontbureau/starling|Starling]] FB might be a good option to consider too. No match for the script. Some close: [[http://www.myfonts.com/search/Englische+Schreibschrift|Englische Schreibschrift]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/profonts/symphony-pro|Symphony]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/search/english+157|English 157]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/profonts/sonata-pro|Sonata]] BTW, this logo is freely available to download as vector [[http://www.brandsoftheworld.com/search/logo?search_api_views_fulltext=hennessy|here]].

URW’s Danmark is actually a pretty good match for the overall letter shapes/proportions—with a little manual condensing:

http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/urw/danmark/

Thicken the crossbar and refine the serifs on the |H|, add serifs to each |s|, and add a ball finial to the tail of the |y|, and you are pretty close.

Agree with Ryuk about Times New Roman for |COGNAC|—a little artificial widening, and it is a better than 95% match.

Nimbus Roman by URW is a close second: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/urw/nimbus-roman/

For the script face, Ballantines Script by Elsner+Flake is good for everything except the initial caps: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/ef/ballantines-script/

Novia isn’t bad, either: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/fontbureau/novia/
