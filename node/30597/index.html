Type design is such a niche occupation/hobby that any national over/under representation is probably just statistical noise. Although I think these country threads are often a bit silly (like this one http://typophile.com/node/15466). I think you can say that there are a few factors that influence the type-designer population and visibility in a given country…

  1. A thriving graphic culture - I think Brazil has that.
  2. Relatively simple writing systems (so you can churn out fonts quickly using free or cheap tools) - Brazil has the edge over China and India there.
  3. A DIY culture that says make it yourself rather than buy it off-the-shelf
  4. High levels of internet usage
