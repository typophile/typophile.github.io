I can't get the link to work, but the image you posted looks like a simplified version of Old English. Sometimes the blackletter types are not easy (enough)to read, so custom versions are designed to make the letters simpler and more legible to modern readers. The cliche of blackletter type for newspaper titles seems to be a kind of joke to me these days. No one thinks we could recognize a newspaper without that type style on the masthead? At least USA Today gets credit for that much sense. They made something recognizable without blackletter. - Mike Yanega
