Last fall I attended the TDC Type walk Paul Shaw led in lower Manhattan, and found his commentary very interesting.

Two interesting more general points were:

  1. A lot of the carved letters on buildings are influenced by models from architectural manuals. So, for example, the reason for many similar signs and letters in New York City that served as the model for Gotham is that the model was in fact in a book that the sign creators used. These architectural books are a source of letters to an extent independent of type.

  2. What works in type doesn’t necessary work as signage or carved letters. In particular he mentioned that Garamond, a great printing type, is generally speaking quite poor for signage…

His book on this promises to be a delight.
