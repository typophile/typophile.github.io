Hrant

Benton's pantograph actually predates Morris' Kelmscott Press by a few years. More often overlooked in type history is the role of the photograph, which in terms of public usage and availability really comes about at about this time. Morris, thanks to Emery Walker's inspiration, was the first to exloit the photograph for revival purposes. His faces were reinterpretations of historical specimens. Kelmscott's success led to similar activities by the foundries. The "theft" the camera allowed was unprecedented.
