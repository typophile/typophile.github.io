Jeffrey, this matter has been raised before in this forum - you can see a few links to Garamond related threads here: http://typophile.com/node/12481 (see bottom of page) - keep in mind that there have been dozens of different interpretations of Garamond typefaces.

As a personal taste, I’d go with Adobe Garamond Pro or Sabon Next, both well done and working digital typefaces.

But there can also be some interesting alternatives, like Epic http://www.typetrust.com/font/epic or Augereau http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/abramslegacy/augereau/

By the way, lists of Garamond-ish typefaces can be found here:

http://new.myfonts.com/search/garamond/fonts/

http://www.identifont.com/find?font=garamond&q=Go

http://www.fontshop.com/search/?q=garamond&x=0&y=0

A sans compatible with Garamond should be a Humanist Sans, I guess. Syntax is the first that comes to mind.

I have mixed feelings about Garamond Premier Pro. Yes, it has optical sizes, and yes, it is historically more accurate than Slimbach’s previous Garamond (Premier also has a wide range of glyphs and languages). But I find it too delicate, with its very detailed strokes; Garamond Pro seems more “strong” on the page, if you know what I mean (pardon my lack of technical competence). What I like in Premier, instead, is its economy - compare Premier (abobe) with Pro (below), and see how the text block looks more tight in the first paragraph.
