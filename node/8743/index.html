It depends on whether you mean *English* as used in the UK, or the other *English* used in the States. In the UK, a turned comma is used for an open quote, a raised comma for a close quote. A quote within a quote is two turned commas for the open, two raised for the close.

In the States, the same marks are used, but the opposite sequence
