DTL Romulus: opinions?

September 18, 2005 • Palatine

I’ve been browsing Dutch Type Library’s font collection, and Romulus caught my eye.

Have any of you used DTL Romulus? What are some of your “expert” opinions of this font? It seems like it would be a nice addition to my collection.

I’ve heard good things about DTL Documenta, however. This one looks very usable.

Romulus is one of the most beautiful types ever designed. Its slanted Roman, in place of italic, is not a success though. DTL doesn’t seem to have plans to do van Krimpen’s Chanceleresca Bastarda, the great script companion to Romulus.

In spite of their great beauty, van Krimpen’s faces always seem to have suffered from some flaw that prevented their wide use.

Perhaps DTL will make a usable version. I hope so. But I don’t believe it has been released. It has been promised for many years; now it looks like they do have a lot of it completed, but I have no idea when they will release it.

For a type that breathes Van Krimpen’s spirit, but does it with it seems more usefulness than Van Krimpen himself, it seems, see the magnificant (and unbelievably expensive) Trinite.
