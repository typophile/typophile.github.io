2007-08-06 07:58:47 • Sharon Van Lieu

Thanks guys. Eyehawk, in reference to your statement about it possibly appearing passe. My only response is that far too often I come across design and illustration sites that are difficult to navigate, graphically/aesthetically distracting, etc. I figured I would go in the complete opposite direction, strip it down as much as possible so that my work is all that is influencing the viewer, and that the user experience isn’t aggravating or annoying. That said, I too feel its a tad on the boring side, and I’ve got about 12 other comps with some fancy elements. I may end up having to implement one of those if too many people are concentrating on the boring/plain-ness of the site and not my work.

Again, thanks for the feedback!

2007-08-06 14:34:09 • pattyfab

Hey, Shane, I like a clean, basic website like yours, but I agree with Patty about the drop-down navigation. It’s a bit confusing that the headers WORK and INFO look like separate menu items and then they don’t bring anything up. If you keep the drop-down menu, maybe you could integrate the section names into the lists – by removing the dotted lines beneath INFO and WORK, for instance.

Also… I’m not sure it’s a good idea to have a practically empty page as the starting point… Think of that page as a cover for a book or printed portfolio of your work… You could use that space to say or show something about yourself – it’s the first impression you are making to a stranger visiting your site for the first time! Do you really want him or her to be greeted with a white page that says “NAVIGATE!”?
