2011-01-03 00:34:57 • forrest

This logo is a shame.

OK, it is beautiful, nicely done and quite good on colours (although it suffers when used in B&W). But the worst is it’s a plagiarism.

The dancing circle can be seen in William Blake’s Oberon, Titania and Puck, from 1786, which served as reference to Matisse’s Dance in 1910. The dancing circle is worldwide known and was already used in Telluride Foundation logo. This logo was itself also copied in 2004, in a Bahia’s carnival mark.

I’m pretty sure the creators of Rio 2016 (Tátil agency) know these references and former usage of a dancing circle, especially Matisse’s paint and Bahia’s logo. In their site, they claim to have done a very elaborated and highly creative work (!). No mention was made about all these antecedents.

Besides the plagiarism, there is also the “friendly” overused idea. As Babs pointed, there is a kind of obsession to picture Brazil as a paradise of friendship in sunny beaches. This is a bad tendence that our own designers like to propagate, producing a quite sterile and repeating aesthetics.

And one cannot forget that Olympic Games actually have almost nothing to do with friendship between nations. It’s plain competition. Coubertin advocated it as an oportunity to fair, edifying disputes between men in order to achieve longer, stronger and taller results, always within high spirits. But even he did not conceived the Games as a love and pace apotheosis. So, the idea behind this logo is also unreal and maladjusted.

2011-01-05 13:55:00 • forrest

I’m not going to add to the stream of arguments for or against the logo, but just want to add that even though many of us might like to see a purely typographical logo (like your modification, Tomi from Suomi), the current IOC rules regarding olympic emblems make it very hard to differ much from the concept we have seen for so many years now (logo on top of city name and year on top of olympic rings)…

The city name has to be included, it has to make clear reference to the host city/country, the rings must not occupy more than 30% of the logo area and must not be modified, and the logo cannot consist of the city name alone. On top of that, certain symbols like the olympic flame/torch must not be used (as Chicago learned during their bidding process). etc. etc.

(and in a way this even makes me appreciate the London logo a little more, it must have been a real fight getting it approved by the IOC)
