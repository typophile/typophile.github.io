I have CS3. AFIK, the Effects > Warp is the way Illustrator handles Envelopes, and the options are limited, whereas CorelDraw’s Envelope function allows complete user control over the finished product, rather than trying to force you to choose among preset options.

As far as Cabarga’s book is concerned, go for cheap (used copies may be available at alibris.com or abebooks.com). Some of the information is useful, but a lot of it is out of date, ca. 2011.

2011-02-03 17:47:39 • quadibloc

This would be my approach - 100% Illustrator, no 3D software required.

First, let’s agree that the arch distortion below and above the text are equal.

http://i.imgur.com/defxo.png

I approximated Clarendon Bold off the top of my head as a close visual match. Curving the type will squeeze the x-height (as I will demonstrate in the next few steps), but we can remedy that with a slight tweak.

http://i.imgur.com/0qNy1.png

Extending the height of our type to match the min. and max. height of the logotype helps compensate for the size reduction in the middle characters after we apply our bulge.

http://i.imgur.com/Bd8k5.png

So now that we have our nice, tall text…

http://i.imgur.com/rBHuj.png

let’s jump into Illustrator Effects>Warp>Bulge.

http://i.imgur.com/ESvIO.png

Tweak your settings. I like to overlay my text so I can get visual confirmation that I did it right.

http://i.imgur.com/01Da4.png

And with that, voila!

http://i.imgur.com/Ds7w8.png
