2007-07-24 05:31:55 • ChuckGroth

Well, you can start out as a freelancer, of course. The point others are making (which I agree with) is that you are only partially baked when you come out of school, and you have to have a few years experience working to be fully done. Now you can get those years as a freelancer, but is it a service to your customers to pay you to learn?

And when you work with someone with more experience, you don’t have to make the same expensive mistakes. Your cohort can explain why something won’t work, and save you the aggravation (and expense, if you are freelance) of making a major error.

So, while you can start as a freelancer, it makes a lot more sense to get a few years under your belt before you go on your own.

the billing which really is just as important as creative if you want to make a living in design.

I don’t agree, sorry. I think your priority should be learning skills at a studio or company that does work that you admire, and hopefully pick up some good portfolio pieces. Making good connections is also very important.

The business side is just not that hard to learn and if you put yourself in a good position, through your talent and your contacts, you’ll figure it out as you go along. It can also vary widely depending on what kind of design you want to do. More than billing, I’d say it’s important to learn what clients expect and how to deal with them.
