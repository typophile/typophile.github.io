Non-compliance with this provision voids any and all support rights and warranties granted by Linotype Library GmbH and represents a violation and breach of this license agreement. Furthermore, if the licensee or a third party or parties effect modifications to the Font Software despite the prohibition against such modifications, Linotype Library GmbH becomes the owner of that modified data. Such converted font software may be used only for your own customary internal business or personal use and may not be distributed or transferred for any purpose, except in accordance with Section 4.4 of this agreement.”

Make of all this what you will. Caveat emptor!

2005-10-19 21:03:52 • hrant

Here’s a response to your first question: Helvetica Neue LIGHT for text? How cruel! Is this a government document?

; )

I have had the most difficulty lately trying to read books set in light weights of Helvetica, and I’m not that old. The problem is twofold: Helvetica (as offered digitally) is spaced more for display than for text. Any use of any weight of Helvetica below 12 point requires some positive tracking; letters bleed together, destroying letter and word recognition. Also, Helvetica’s lettershapes push the boundary of ambiguity, meaning, a, e, s, all look the same, rn = m, and in English, you don’t want that kind of confusion. The deadly regularity of the strokes combined with the tight spacing makes for a singularly unreadable mess. Consider using a better-spaced type to start with, like Akzidenz Grotesk! Its proportions and spacing make it much more comfortable to read, though smaller sizes might require additional tracking.

I’m sorry, the problem is threefold: Why a light weigh for text? It’s stylish, I’m well aware, but is it readable? How much text is there? What size are you setting all this text? Could you consider a different style of sans, a Humanist, for example, to make reading easier and gain some economy of space in the bargain? Are you stuck with AG and Helvetica? Several families of Sans offer a variety of weights for text and display and are more suited for extended reading. I wouldn’t recommend any light weight for text sizes.

Now back to regularly scheduled EULA discussion.
