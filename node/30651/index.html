Corel Draw/Ventura vs Illustrator/InDesign

January 16, 2007 • metalfoot

So I’ve been following this forum for a while and I’ve noticed little to no talk of Corel’s DTP programs. Are they not that useful or has Adobe’s product simply become the new standard? The reason I ask is that I would like to get a decent DTP package and Corel is way more affordable, especially with their special pricing for non-profits.

Corel Draw has some fans out there, but Corel burned users with horribly buggy releases for so long that many people just don’t care anymore. For several versions it was not possible to draw a solid 45 degree line! I’m pretty sure that angry users even filed a class action over Corel Draw 5. Anyway, Illustrator and Freehand got so busy trying to outdo each other that most people pretty much forgot about Corel Draw.

As for Ventura, I’ve never heard of anybody actually using it, and never seen it on the list of formats that most printers accept. Given how much trouble designers have just getting printers to accept the latest Quark and InDesign files you might want to talk to your regular printer and find out if they can even use Ventura files.

If you’re only getting it to save money, just bite the bullet and get the Adobe Creative Suite. Between the fonts and the software, it’s worth every penny.

2007-03-23 14:44:27 • JeffM

I used to work on Corel Draw for vector graphics and light layouts but since I switched to an employee position (with macs which don’t run Corel) a year ago I have been usind Illustrator. I have started to like Illustrator a bit more than Corel but I still think that Illustrator might learn a couple of things from Corel such as:

  • Selection of objects can be done in the Illustrator way (being touched by the frame) or the Corel way (being inside the frame only) you switch by depressing a key only. Sometimes it’s a real pain to select objects in Illustrator.

  • BCPs are so tiny and hard to select in Illustrator it really desperates me. Corel has a better way of selecting and editing BCPs.

  • Corel has graphic handles for tracking, word spacing, kerning and leading in the artistic text mode so it’s very easy to adjust logos, headlines and type tricks.

  • Corel has a very practical way of joining and breaking apart paragraphs, sentences, words and glyphs so it’s ver easy to play with type as well.

  • Corel has a much more practical way of handling vector objects than illustrator’s pathfinder.

  • Shortcuts are better in Corel.

  • Lining, arranging and grouping objects seemed easier in Corel to me.

On the other hand transparency sucks, paragraph type control is hard, no open type support, it crashes a bit more and bitmap effects are bad. However being the economical situation in México as it is, Corel remains a very popular program and many respectable designers use it.

Corel Ventura, I’ve never tried it.

2007-03-23 20:33:15 • twardoch

I’m trying to make the switch to Adobe after using Corel Draw and Ventura Publisher for the past 17 years. Any Mac-head will tell you that Adobe is far superior. Advice: don’t listen to them. They’ve never used Corel so they don’t know. I’m amazed at how much InDesign lacks when compared to Ventura Publisher. Here’s just a few examples:

• Corel Ventura can vary the widths of individual column within a frame. Column widths within a frame must be of identical value with InDesign. This is an unbelievable fact to me.

• Paragraph breaks. Corel allows for ‘No Break’ paragraphs meaning that entire paragraphs can sit side by side within the same text frame. This eliminates the need for columns or threaded frames in Ventura (it’s still available, just not needed). Surprisingly this feature dramatically streamlines your work.

• Document navigation is far better in Corel Ventura. Ventura embeds chapters into one vp file and lists the chapters in it’s navigator window. A simple click takes you to that document. InDesign requires a separate document file for each chapter. (Ugh - a MAJOR disappointment).

• Interface. Corel relies heavily on dynamic context menus/pop up windows. This removes a lot of the clutter found with the InDesign interface. Additionally, Ventura let’s you edit several paragraph styles without having to close and re-open another dialog box.

• Ventura is considerably faster with long documents.

CS3 is far better with color, especially spot colors. Ventura has a hard time with spot color bitmap files. A huge turn-off for print shops.

Corel’s biggest mistake is the fact that it’s only on Windows. Since the vast majority of graphic designers are Mac based, Corel automatically reduced their fair share of the market thus eliminating any consideration as a competitive graphics package. A real shame seeing how much has gone into that program.

If you’re looking for something easy (actually kinda fun) with lots of features, then Corel is your answer. Well worth the value. Adobe has room to grow and I’m sure a lot can be expected of them in the next few years.
