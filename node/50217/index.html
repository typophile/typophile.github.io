If your source is a single text document, you cannot get it into InDesign other than as a single text document … Although you can cut-and-paste it into separate sections, ID will loose the ability to update all of them by something like “remembering” where you cut and pasted.

The Book suggestion is an excellent one, as you don’t have to worry about the number of pages per chapter. However, to have each and every separate document still linked to “an” original Word one, you will have to save the individual chapters as individual Word files. Nothing backwards about that – you have one file or you have many.

Is it really that crucial to always be able to update the text in Word? Every single edit action in InDesign, be it formatting, styling, or inserting a soft hyphen to prevent an ugly word break, will disappear as soon as you update the text. I suggest either to submit the Word document to your publisher and edit until it’s really ready to be put into InDesign, or do the lay-out and subsequent editing in InDesign itself.
