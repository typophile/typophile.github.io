2007-11-07 22:26:54 • jupiterboy

Aside from the kerning issue–which should be dealt with–a more important one is to show a more clear hierarchy. Everything being close to the same size and color makes it less accessible and compelling. I would say: decide on three things that are most important, and make these bigger, and make the others help in a subordinate role. Then the subordinate ones you can play with your typographic tool kit to get the right balance.

For example, the treatment of ‘save the date’ doesn’t seem to have the right emotional tone. It is surrounded with something flowery, and in the same script as the name of the dinner. The emotional appeal should be in the title, I would think, and “save the date” maybe tacked on in a bold sans, maybe asymmetrically or at an angle as a matter of fact advisory. Then the later notice can be the same design without the ‘save the date’ notice.

2007-11-08 02:03:37 • jupiterboy

I think you will find that if you set the type just a bit smaller, you can accommodate much of the advice (good stuff) you’ve been given. The size of space is every bit as important as the size of the type.

If you use the natural, unkerned space between, say, “R” and “Y” as the proper amount, then “kern” everything else (looser) to match that spacing, you’ll be on a good track.

One general use of small caps is to not visually break up a line of type with so many full caps. But when each word begins with a cap (as with a name), you have already broken the line – or, if you prefer, established a new rhythm. Personally, I would set the “III” in full caps, and that will mean that M.D. too should be in full caps.

Close your eyes. That’s the advice my teachers gave me. Not completely, but just enough to peer through your lashes. Single letters, words and lines dissolve into cloudlike shapes. That will allow you to consider the allover colour of the piece. And you’ll be able to see where blocks of matter are uneven or too close to each other. It even helps to hold the piece upsidedown.

BTW: It’s almost impossible to review something on screen. I would need a highres print.
