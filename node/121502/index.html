What font editor are you using? The thing is in any font editor it’s possible to set the left and right sidebearings to be negative, i.e. on the inside of the actual outlines.

BTW if you make the bounds exactly on the edge of the letterforms you might get funny rendering artifacts where two letterforms abut; they actually need to overlap slightly. I typically go at least two em units to the inside of an outline when I want a clean overlap.

Your examples look nice, but since they don’t show individual glyphs I might not fully understand your problem. It looks like they all have the same advance width, which probably is a good thing.

To set all left and right side bearings with FontCreator, just use the Transform wizard: [img:sites/default/files/old-images/bearings_6336.png]

Note: it is recommended to exclude empty glyphs (e.g. .null, nonmarkingreturn, and the space character)
