Normally I would have waited for #3 to run its course, but this scrap of paper on my desk is driving me crazy so I’m just going to throw this out:

Poppl Laudatio has been one of my favorite fonts since the mid-90s. I especially like to use it for on-screen work because it’s so shallow* without being devoid of character - in fact it’s one of the very few fonts that I use both (very) small/lo-fi and very large. I think Poppl really knew what he was doing, and we’re very lucky to have such a design available.

  • http://typophile.com/node/15367

The thing is, many people always complain about its ampersand. You can think “they need an education” but the fact remains it can be useful to have a “conventional” ampersand for it. So let’s make one.

Being a Berthold font it’s become hard to view through other font houses (can’t really blame them I guess)* like the always-great-for-a-preview Myfonts. You can see a cruddy GIF** on their site, but I would recommend downloading the the PDF that shows all their “Hybrid” fonts, from here: http://www.bertholdtypes.com/bq_library/BQ_library.html

  • In fact I’d strongly recommend remaining squeaky-clean-legal on this one. Maybe read the PDF usage conditions on their site, and definitely only work with just renderings of the letterforms, not original outlines.

** http://www.bertholdtypes.com/bq_library/90089.html
