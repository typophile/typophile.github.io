2004-01-27 00:00:47 • adriano
McKean's own studio's Hourglass film is a mixture of brush hand-lettering (for which you could help yourself using Contrivance or Stroke, as suggested, but doing it by hand it's always best), and my own Ottomat Bold, from Emigre (pride, pride!)
Dave even used the Italic of Ottomat when I released it around 1996 for the Death mini-series hardcover (1st edition) and in other personal corporate stuff (I've seen it in a book of graphic design work.
Recently, however, he mostly used customized versions of Jonathan's Virus faces, as Yves pointed out.
I tried to contact him a pair of times, but it's difficult. To think I sat right near him in an old comic convention in Lucca, way before I released typefaces...
