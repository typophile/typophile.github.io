May 28, 2011 • hrant

What are some good type-centric apps to put on an iPad? Also of interest would be utilities that let you zoom in to the screen bitmap. hhp
I don't know of any utility that lets you do that on the iPad itself, but you can easily make screen snapshots by pushing the home button and standby button at the same time. The screen snapshot is stored in the camera roll, which you can sync with a folder on your PC (via iTunes), and then open in your favorite image program to get a closer look. There are not really a lot of type-centric apps (yet), unfortunately. There is a very simple modular font editor (similar to FontStruct in concept) called Fontula. There's also a simple font editor called iFontMaker. Seems to be best suited to fonts that have a hand-drawn (or -scribbled) look. I haven't tried it. I think part of the reason there is a dearth of type- or font-related apps is that there is no way (yet) to install fonts beyond what it comes with. On the other hand, it does support web fonts in the web browser, so you can view other fonts on it if they are being served on the web.

> when you run an iPhone HD app on the iPad, > does it display at 960x640 with a border, or does > it scale up to 1024x768? There are 3 kinds of apps —1) iPhone apps, 2) iPad apps, and 3) Universal apps (designed for both iPhones and iPads) 1) If you run an iPhone app on an iPad, the image will not fill the screen and will have a large black border around it. Or you can press the 2x button and the image will increase to almost full screen, but will still have a border and will be very pixilated. 2) If you run an iPad app, it will fill the screen. 3) If you run a Universal app on an iPad, it will be hi res and fill the screen. I presume that Universal apps require that the developer create 2 sets of screens (iPhone and iPad), since the size & proportions of the screens aren't the same. In fact I believe Apple describes Universal apps as essentially being 2 apps (an iphone app and an iPad app) that are bundled together.

2011-05-28 14:27:23 • hrant
> And I assume an iPhone app that's HD will almost fill the screen without 2x. No, all iPhone apps, whether HD or not, are displayed small on an iPad unless you press the 2x button, in which case they are pixel doubled which make them larger but pixilated. Even the HD ones act this way. This may seem illogical, but there's a reason. iPhone HD apps must have 2 sets of bitmapped graphics, to accomodate the resolution of both older iPhones and newer iPhones. If you play an iPhone app on an iPad, it uses the smaller version of the graphics. Why? Because Apple doesn't want developers to think that an HD iPhone app is "good enough" for iPads too. Apple wants developers to create separate iPad versions, so the screen sizes are correct and all the interface elements are properly designed and sized for an iPad, rather than just being an iPhone app displayed larger than originally intended, which often results in clunky, oversized buttons, etc. So Apple makes sure that iPhone apps will play on an iPad, but won't look that great. That prods the developers to create real iPad versions.
