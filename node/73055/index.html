Learning the curve

August 10, 2010 • Typehuile

Anyone know of any good sites or resources on how to draw curves, especially for type design?

I’ve seen typefaces that use very few curve points to define the round parts of letters, and then I’ve seen other faces with 6 or 10 or 20 points on each curve. Wow. When I’ve tried my own efforts—I usually used a few points rather than many—the result was a curved letter that looked like it had little blisters or irregularities somewhere along the path, often more than one of those little bumps. It’s easily seen at medium sizes and looks deplorable at small sizes.

Any ideas?

Danke, mes amigos

Michael Brady

Work with perfect circles. Break them apart into four 90° arcs and manipulate the arc segments to form your curves. Keep your control points orthogonal, and they will be much easier to control.

and then I’ve seen other faces with 6 or 10 or 20 points on each curve.

Are you opening TTFs in a font editor? Often, that seems to result from conversion from cubic to quadratic splines.

I second the advice to keep your control points at the vertical and horizontal extrema. Some consistency makes it indeed easier to predict how moving points and handles will affect the curve.

Also worth checking out: Raph Levien’s spiro library and his thesis on curves.
