The book was published by Roma Publications in 2012. Only the book designer/publisher knows the source of the lettering. Because the match – Bernase Roman – was designed by Tom Carnase and Ronne Bonder, photo lettering and/or dry transfer strips would probably have been produced. The anonymous author of Berns may have used these sources. There is no indication of a previous digital match. The book cover designer might have used Berns or some of the old material. The only way to know is to ask Roma Publications. As for Bernase Roman this is a revision of the old font “DeVinne” named for Theodore Low De Vinne, and produced in various weights and widths. See http://typophile.com/node/99936 Hawthorn and Denver are other revisions of Devinne. Note that they retain the curved and pointed leg on the R, and straight cross stroke, found in the original DeVinne. Don

Thanks Ryuk for the leads. The speculation in my previous post is not accurate. I believe the correct match is Bernase Roman Copyright (c) 1992 Image Club Graphics, Inc. I rescaled this font and the “freeware” font Berns to a common size and determined that, allowing for the slippage of converting formats, Berns is a point for point rip of Bernase Roman, which was I believe produced by ICG from non-digital sources. Unfortunately the digital version of Bernase Roman floating “in the wild” is a font orphan. For the legal status of Image Club fonts see: http://imageclub.org/ It could, however, be reissued by the new owners of the Image Club products. Don
