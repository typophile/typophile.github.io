Looking for some good fonts to recommend for novel length romance books

July 28, 2011 • coreenm

Good morning, everyone!

I’m a freelance graphic designer. I currently work with a small publisher, doing all of their typesetting for their romance titles. I have been using Adobe Garamond Pro, and have been pleased with the results. It is readable and relatively neutral looking.

I’m now working with a second publishing house, also romance novels. They are currently using Book Antiqua for the body of the novel. I personally think this is an awful choice. I want recommend some alternatives, but I don’t want the books to end up looking identical to the ones I design for the other publisher. Some font choices I have been considering are Kepler, Berling, and Caslon. I’m looking for a Pro font that is very readable and relatively neutral in appearance, particularly for the quotation marks (one of the things I hate about Book Antiqua). I also don’t want anything that stands out as very modern, because many of the books are historical romance (which possibly explains the Book Antiqua choice…)

So, are there any recommendations? What is your favorite font for setting long pieces of text?

The danger in going with classic book faces is that they start to look more like literature (or history) than romance. How about Dante? With its large x-height it will set quite big, and still have a traditional appearance.
