Paul, You don’t say what stype the script is. Is it handwriting, copperplate, calligraphic, wild or tame? Some styles offer more leeway; some, like copperplate styles, limit your options for a joining scheme. An informal design can have no instrokes, only outstrokes. Then your only issue is getting everything spaced so the outstrokes fall on the correct spot (or area).

If the joins have to be very delicate and precise, I think it is better to have overlap; If gaps appear in rasterization, it looks broken.

I assume you’re going to take advantage of OpenType to get all your joins just right?

style of script?

anything i haven’t brought up that i need to keep in mind?

  1. letters you’re connecting to
  2. letters you’re connecting from
  3. connecting letters whose exit strokes begin in the middle of the x-height space — e.g. w, o, f, r, b
