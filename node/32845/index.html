Hey Everyone, Is there a book or website (or even a post here) out there that has some beginner tips for identifying differnt typefaces? I'm looking for something a long the lines of certain letters to look for, etc... Thanks, Mike
Stop Stealing Sheep -- and find out how type works by Spiekermann & Ginger is pretty good. The many comparisons of different fonts in the same genre, all spelling "Handgloves", allow one to become familiar with the genres, and with the differences between the faces in each category, in a personal way: the initial similarities account for their grouping by the authors, but then it's up to the viewer to "spot the difference" and refine his/her assessment of each face's personality -- and memorize its name!

2007-04-07 17:53:14 • ChuckGroth
Lowercase a and g are very distinctive glyphs, the way upper and lower case w is made also can be very distinctive. Number styles and uppercase A, R and M also help. A good start is trying to date the typeface, can it fit on the historic families? (humanist, garalde, modern, mechanical, etc...) does it looks like 20th century? postmodern perhaps? A good excercise is studying historic typefaces styles and then classifying fonts, this makes you look typefaces in detail and trains you to find those distinctive gestures. Héctor
