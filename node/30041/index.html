Typophile Archive

Simplicity, propriety of Century Gothic + flexibility, familiarity of Helvetica Neue

December 12, 2006 • Sam Serif

I am updating an identity for my branding, design firm and I have recommended a switch in our corporate typeface. I am looking for a few suggestions which fit our criteria:
  • capture the openness and friendliness of our current face (Century Gothic)
  • with a more credible font that sets well and is part of a larger system (similar to Helvetica Neue, which some designers would like to see used)
  • this larger system should include different weights, a nice italic, an expanded character set and solid numerals (distinct or non-lining figures would be a nice plus)
  • we are also probably setting our name as a word-mark or initialed in this face, so it must have enough personality (or brand) to sit on its own
Some designs that came to mind were: Verlag, Scala (both families), Dax, Neo Sans, Gotham, Whitney, or even Apex Sans and Galaxie Polaris. I know I've seen excellent suggestions on these boards. What do you guys think? Thanks in advance for any help, Sam

Verlag is a great typeface. I have had the opportunity to work with it and found it works very well at small sizes. However, because it is a geometric it will have moments of feeling stiff. But, on the other hand, your color palette, visuals, and other tactile elements will go a long way into imparting friendliness across the brand.
