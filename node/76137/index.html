2010-11-09 21:17:50 • nina

Typophiles like type specimens more than they like fonts.

I base that observation on the trade at Type events, and the experience that typographers will buy a font for use in a specific job, but not so much on impulse.

So perhaps a licence/certificate is a good idea, because the recipient can redeem it when the occasion arises. But I wouldn’t suggest just giving someone a font as a present, unless it comes with a nice specimen, or is in a House Industries package.

Gift certificates are a bit of a cop-out, third on the scale behind a cleverly-chosen purchase and something you made yourself specially for the recipient.
