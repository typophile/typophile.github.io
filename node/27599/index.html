Hello:

Actually your drawing is not that bad… keep going- keep perfecting. Push comes to shove: the ‘rough/irregular’ look is highly desirable these days. But if its not working for you, try it once or twice more, then you need to move on to modifying exsisting type so you don’t fail the assignment or run out of time. If you simply don’t have the money that’s okay. There are good free fonts out there but you have to look. FontShop being no. 1 on the list. And I would definately check out Maniakers. Look at the (mentionaed) Free faq on typophile. Also you need to have the concept down first. That will help. So when you go font hunting you know what to look for.

BTW…Don McCahill your comments are so false. There are many excellent free fonts out there. You just have to look (sometimes really hard). FontBook (or other 3rd party utility) checks for errors and stops you from installing bad fonts so that argument is out… you are using Mac OS X right? If your ‘friends’ are unwise enough to install hundreds or thousands of fonts (free or otherwise), have them all activated, and not even validate them they must NOT be a professional.

2006-08-13 13:42:07 • ill sans

It is usual to customise lettering for a logo, often this will mean amending a typeface to your design to improve on spacing and letterforms and is probably the most effective way of achieving it as somebody else (the type designer) will, with their knowledge and experience, have worked out a lot of the problems of stroke modulation etc. If your intention is just to create a logo rather than take a font change it and then use it as a font you are on safe moral ground is my perspective.
