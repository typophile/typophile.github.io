The Power of the Quote

April 14, 2007 • hrant

The quote is a tricky, powerful “shape-shifter” of a character, with abilities way beyond its formal uses for marking verbal expressions, titles, etc. We’ve all seen it used to mean “not exactly”, or even “not really, but this way you can’t sue me”, when a product is advertised to do something it doesn’t. I’ve also seen it used to mean “not exactly but there’s something to it”: during a visit to the Cayman Islands I saw a car rental place with a large “JEEP ONLY” sign, in quotes; it actually had other vehicles for rent too, but/so wanted to succinctly state: “This is the place for renting a jeep, but we have other stuff too”*. And during this past spring break I saw the quote used thus:

_ Can you guess what it’s doing there? _

  • Some years later I saw a similar thing at a motorcycle rental place in Hawaii: “HARLEYS ONLY”.

Those are Japanese quotes. As a translator I see them all the time. I won’t bore you with the details, but basically this is one way that people used to thinking in a topic-comment language try to make up for English’s lack of ways to formally mark the topic.
