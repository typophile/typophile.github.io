Hey everyone,

I was wondering how georgia and verdana were made to fit the screen. Is it just an endless process of testing? Then I came up with an idea. If I make a grid in Typetool at the size the pixels would be when at 12 points. The same thing could be done for other point sizes, so I’ll make different versions for different sizes. The idea is not to make a pixel based font, but a contour that is made to fit the pixels. Am I the first to think of this? Is there any chance it will work? How can I find out how much space there has to be between the lines of the grid in order for them to align with the pixels? Any help is much appreciated.

I don’t see the difference between fitting the pixels to the contours and fitting the contours to the pixels. You’d end up with a pretty similar result either way. As far as making different versions for different point sizes, that is basically the concept behind truetype hinting. The hints trigger which pixels are used to render the letterforms at every ppem size.

So it’d be tough to call this an original idea. Perhaps, you could call it an original perspective on hinting.

You could explore how the design of the letterforms influences the ease and quality of the hinting, as well as maintain the typeface’s design identity at low resolutions.

Two pages to check out about TT Hinting and ppem sizes (respectively) are: http://www.microsoft.com/typography/TrueTypeHintingVersus.mspx and http://typophile.com/node/14929
