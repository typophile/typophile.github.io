body: "<strong>[[Indices]] : [[Designers]] : Carol Twombly</strong>\r\n\r\nCarol Twombly
  (1959-) studied architecture at the [[RISD|Rhode Island School of Design]], and
  went on to become one of the five students to get master's degrees in type design
  from [[Charles Bigelow]] at Stanford. Twombly briefly worked for Bigelow as well.
  Her typeface <a href=\"http://www.fontshop.com/?fuseaction=catalog.fontpackage&searchby=manufacturer&displayfontid=BT.6414.0.0\">Mirarae</a>
  won the Morisawa type design competition, and was produced by Bitstream. She worked
  for [[Adobe Systems]] from 1988-99, producing many well-known typefaces, including
  <a href=\"http://www.fontshop.com/?fuseaction=catalog.fontpackage&searchby=manufacturer&displayfontid=LH.398.0.0\">Lithos</a>,
  <a href=\"http://www.fontshop.com/?fuseaction=catalog.fontpackage&searchby=manufacturer&displayfontid=LH.108134.0.0\">Trajan</a>,
  and <a href=\"http://store.adobe.com/type/browser/P/P_180.html\">Adobe Caslon</a>.
  She retired from type design and left Adobe in 1999 to do other crafts, such as
  textile-based design and jewellery.\r\n\r\n<strong>Discussion:</strong>\r\n<a href=\"http://typophile.com/node/519\">Carol
