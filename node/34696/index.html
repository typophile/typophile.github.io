2007-06-24 01:57:21 • blank
AFAIK, fonts were not produced during the era of heroic communism (too bourgeois). In the famous Constructivist posters, book covers and magazines, they used exisiting fonts for small scale typography, and lettered the larger stuff themselves – as was common practice elsewhere during the teens, 20s and 30s. Remember, that photolettering (typositor) had not been introduced even in the West then, and the best “pre-made” type for headlines was wood, and a bit rough anyway. So if you wanted nice big lettering, you made original artwork, and that was true whether you were working in the capitalist West and wanted some neo-Renaissance thang, or were a Soviet radical.

My own conclusion: type was a rich part of the posters, but a secondary detail, kind of like movie stars' teeth in the 1970s. =)

I’ve since thought that making a Rodchenko font that is true to his hand would be tough to pull off. How do you, with today’s reproduction fidelity, stay true to his variation and warmth of his lettering. I think it would look too mechanical or canned.
