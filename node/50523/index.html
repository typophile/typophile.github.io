Dissertation question* A comparison between the Graphic Design used during ww1/2 and that of modern conflicts e.g The Iraq War.

October 15, 2008 • benm

Im planning to investigate how during the first and second world wars there was a very small amount of anti-war material in circulation and total government censorship of work produced, and how this differs from modern conflicts such as Vietnam, the gulf, Iraq etc.

I also want to look at the change in public protest and opinion and the way that government leaders are portrayed, an example of this could be the way in which Churchill was used as an iconic pro-war figure during WW2 and in contrast how George Bush has been used to in anti-war material.

I’ve currently looked at:

Milton Glaser- The design of dissent Graphic agitation 1 +2 War posters: weapons of mass communication

Ken garland CND campaigns/ first things first manifesto.

Herb Lubalin, no more war in Avant Garde magazine (seen in a previous post)

Sagmiester’s imperialist comic strip

  • a selection of ww1/2 poster books/designers

I was wondering if anyone has any advice on the subject, or can point me in the direction of more material/areas of research! Thanks.

[[http://www.amazon.com/When-You-Ride-Alone-Laden/dp/1893224902/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&s=books&qid=1224091363&sr=8-1|When You Ride Alone You Ride With Bin Laden: What the Government Should Be Telling Us to Help Fight the War on Terrorism ]] by Bill Maher examines what could have been done in terms of propaganda for the current conflict, but wasn’t. The reviews on the linked page explain the book better than I could.

Images that stick in my head from the beginning of The War Against Terror include the shop signs that said “America is Open For Business” above an American flag with a shopping bag handle attached to the top, the photo of a group of firefighters hoisting a flag at the World Trade Center wreckage, and a badly photoshopped bald eagle head with a tear dripping from its eye.
