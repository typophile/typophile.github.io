2005-07-12 19:19:58 • Norbert Florendo
This might help those who have joined the hunt. By my recollection only (I'm not near my reference library), the Sans Serif Shaded was part of the T.J. Lyons Collection of Wood Types that Letraset originally licensed before Agfa-Compugraphic. CORRECTION: Sans Serif Shaded originally came from the foundry of Stephenson Blake & Co. and NOT T.J. Lyons The shaded version became wildly popular in late '60s -- early '70s, making its way onto many bookcovers and record albums (you know, those big black round flat things before CDs). I remember being amazed that the design originated from an old wood type, since to me it seemed very contemporary. Gee, it was old, but came back in style! Someone please correct me if my recollection is wrong, as I wouldn't want to create false history. ------------------------------------------------- Yes, I'm old, but I'm Sans Serifs Shaded!
