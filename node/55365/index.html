Mardi Gras & Type

March 1, 2009 • Helios05

Mardi Gras is a holiday unique to the New Orleans area, it’s an interesting time, where normal people, both local and from all over the world, come to let their more “wild” sides out. Acting crazy, wearing zany costumes, and shouting at the top of your lungs in an attempt to get some to throw you beads, it’s not you normal holiday. We also see examples of the creative side in people coming out, something they may not get a chance to express on a regular basis. This is mostly done through various “costumes” that people design for themselves, they’re kids, they’re poor dogs, etc. However, one does come across a few costumes which feature a use of type in them, often using hand-written lettering, here are a few examples: The Wagons This example shows people using type primarily on the wagons, such as in the lower right corner of the picture, where it says “Krewe de la Rue”, but the middle wagon which says “AIR” appears to have been done with a stencil. Body Type Ah alcohol, the things you convince us to do. Here is another example of handwritten type, this time actually on a person’s body. We also see an example of the kind of social commentary one gets out of Mardi Gras, mostly controversial subjects often ending up as lewd jokes, but there’s a certain blatant honesty about it. The Elderly Couple My personal favorite out of the set, showing how the energy and natural humor that is a part of Mardi Gras is shared by young and old. We also have and interesting contrast thanks to the type in this photo. One, we have the small child sitting in the ladder with “LSU” painted on the side, then look diagonally down to the signs the elderly couple are wearing, and we see how Mardi Gras is celebrate throughout the years by people both young and old.
