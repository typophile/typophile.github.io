The last character set in small caps, kerning question

April 5, 2009 • Mario

In InDesign if I set up a character style for small caps which kerns +25, what is the best way to avoid a larger space (due to the +25 kern) appearing after the end of a word set in small caps? How do you guys deal with this? It’s sort of time consuming to go through and change the kerning for each instance…
In InDesign CS3 I don’t believe there is any way to automate this with character styles, and I don’t think CS4 would offer any solutions either. If you’re concerned about adjacent punctuation (parentheses and the like) being asymmetrical, and if your font doesn’t have those pesky small caps versions of these, you can just include the punctuation in the small caps style. If your font has small caps versions of these and you wish not to use them, I do believe your stuck making the manual adjustments.
