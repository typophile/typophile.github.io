Best face and size for visually impaired?

July 16, 2004 â€¢ mga

My organisation is eager to meet accessibility guidelines for documentation. The result will be the default typeface on some 400 PCs. We're using system fonts, specifically Arial.

I'm not sure what the best way to proceed is. It's been suggested that the RNIB guidelines should be a starting point. This suggests a 2mm x-height as a minimum.

By my reckoning, 10.5pt Arial does the 2mm x-height trick. I was encouraged to increase this to 11pt, which is okay. This week, I find that some people in the organisation want it increased further, to 12pt.

What's people's thoughts on this kind of thing?

Personally, I feel that 12pt Arial doesn't do anyone any favours, and that other choices might be better for people with visual impairment. However, it's very difficult to put this view across to people who've read literature saying things like "A 12pt sans serif such as Arial or Helvetica should be the minimum, and 14pt would be better".

Is there any useful reference or research on this kind of thing? I found http://www.tireasis.org, but could do with something containing specific information about print.
