No firm definitions. Handtooled can mean a visual effect that suggests an embossed letter by using a variable shadowed section in the thicker strokes. Outline can mean that the outer line of a glyph parallels the next line inward, or vice versa. Such a font is sometimes called hollow. Sometimes terms like contour are used to describe the multiple application of parallel lines to produce a black glyph surrounded by a white stroke and then an outside black stroke. Inline can mean various things, including where the glyph consists of unconnected lines. A related term, cameo, is sometimes used when the typeface is white inside a black box. Don
