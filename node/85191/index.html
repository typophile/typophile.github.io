℗ is used in music publishing. Apparently – and I’ve not researched this in any depth – ℗ is used contrastively with © such that the former refers to the recording and the latter to packaging. One of the things this enables, I suppose, is the original ℗ date to be clearly identified when music is re-released with new artwork/packaging that has its own © date.

Not exactly, John. According to Wikipedia (and this matches what I’ve been told), the ℗ refers to copyright of the recording, while © refers to the composition itself (music and lyrics).

My understanding from people who know these things:

© is copyright for the song itself (by the musician or the person who wrote the song, not the particular recorded version) ℗ is publishing rights (who owns this version/recording of the song, usually the record company who releases it)
