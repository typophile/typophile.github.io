2011-01-18 22:17:09 • eliason

The symbols are Greek = eta (η), theta (θ), alpha (α), beta (β), and delta (δ).

I’m pretty sure you won’t find most of these in standard Georgia. The Georgia Pro that Ascender & Font Bureau are working on will have them, however.

The double arrow is not quite two “lessthan”s (<<), but rather a “much less-than” (≪). And there’s also a “geometrically equal to” (≑) in there.

(Georgia Pro will not have these specialized math symbols.)

There probably isn’t one. Perhaps we can be of more help if you could say why you want Georgia. If it is for Georgia’s screen performance, there have been many changes since Georgia was first created. Something else might be better. Screen display is not something I know much about.

If you just like Georgia, use it, and mix in the mathematical operators and even Greek symbols from another font. What we’ve always had to do – well, at least since the 1500s.

Bear in mind the Greek symbols used in mathematics are usually slightly different than for the language. It wouldn’t be so much wrong to use a Greek language font, but it would look odd.

For free OpenType math fonts, search for what TeX has to offer.
