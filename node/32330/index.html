What got you started. . .

March 17, 2007 • James Scriven

Just wanted to waste some time and was wondering what got all of you started with typography and or Graphic Design.

I’ve always been a doodler but never pursued art in my education. I studied Journalism and Communication at the University of Arizona and was fed up with life. It came down to some unknown calling to do one of the following. Join the Army (almost signed my life away) or find an art school. Can’t say why I wanted to, I didn’t have the foggiest idea about typography or design. Yet I found myself moving back to the Bay Area and pursuing just that. Still to this day don’t know what made me want to do it, but now I can officially call myself a Typophile (amonsgt other things . . . not ending in ‘phile’ though).

I love to hate it, im almost done with this degree but I have never felt further away from the end goal. But I am grateful for Typophile as I can now rant, rave, and post about stuff not to many other people notice or care about.

Anyway I would love to hear how everyone else delved into this realm of passion and pain. . .

Cheers (Slainte)

2007-03-20 03:40:20 • mjpatrick
James I think what prompted me to pursue typography in a serious way was printing letterpress books and setting the type by hand. The process, from beginning to end, from setting the type to printing the words, is, well, kind of magic. At some point in the process the shapes of the letters suddenly took on great importance and I asked myself why - why do the letters look the way they do and how is it that these little pieces of lead in printed form can look so very beautiful on the printed page. I knew somehow that Spectrum, Romulus, Romanee, Joanna, Gill Sans, Goudy Thirty were great designs but I’m still asking myself the question why. The more you look at the letters and their structure the more you come to understand the wonder of it all, the 26 letters. It’s Magic man! Sláinte agus saol agat! Jordan

2007-03-21 15:46:42 • hrant

When I was 14, I started working on the layout team of a Boy Scout newsletter. I don’t think that there was that much that was special about our design or layouts, but through the right active leaders, we got to use high-end pre-press tools that we probably never should have gotten our hands on…

Well, that got me hooked on the Mac, PageMaker, Photoshop, and eventually, Quark. I started developing black and white photos, and then figured that I should go to art school and study graphic design. This was actually a bit of a leap if you were to see the stuff I was trying (poorly) to do in high school, but I ended up at RISD and that was great.

I moved to Germany at the end of college, because I (naïvely?) thought that I would be moving somewhere whose culture was more typographically sophisticated than the United States of America’s. I saw old letters everywhere, and knew that I could not be happy unless I began drawing type.
