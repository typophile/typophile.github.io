> Today is the most tightly-spaced sans I've ever worked with.

That's actually a good thing, even -no, especially- in text setting. The letters have to meld into words (they have to become less letters).

I've come to believe that contemporary standards of spacing are too loose. The metal boys couldn't space much tighter than they did - but we can. There is certainly such a thing as too-tight spacing, but Today I feel is just right.

And I'll note here that the good readability of Times is due to its very tight spacing - overcoming its deficiencies (which could be attributed to its unintelligent conversion to digital) in terms of lightness and contrastiness.

Tight tracking in headlines is largely a matter of taste - but in headlines, largely function.
