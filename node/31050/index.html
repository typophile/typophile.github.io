Patty, this may be a completely crazy workaround to propose, but what if you have a small, second doc that remains open, parked on the pasteboard near your cookbook, and in it you have made all your needed fractions from boldface lining figures, reduced to the correct size, proper baseline shifts, your more leaning-over solidus between the two numerals, and a customized amount of tracking between all three elements. Then whenever you need, say “1/4 t.” you just go over, highlight and copy, then paste into the main document, and go on with what you were doing. Is that too fussy? That way you could use Electra or Dante or whatever you want, and you’d end up with fractions that have the numeral size/spacing/color that you most desire. (I find sometimes that the pre-made fractions and nut fractions are not as handsome or readable as ones I can make myself, just as the bullets that ship with many traditional text fonts are about twice as big as I want them to be, forcing me to reduce point size and do a baseline shift in order to use them.
