2009-11-17 18:56:59 • crossgrove

Putting ink on paper has been done in a number of ways, especially in the 20th century. What is a “good” typeface depends on how that was done – letterpress? offset? And within offset, repro-negative-plate, or direct to plate? And within a repro-negative-plate workflow, was the repro pulled from metal fonts, or photocomp fonts?

Refining that further, in the photocomp days, type was proprietary. What might be a good font on a Linotype 202 might not be a good font on a Monotype VideoComp.

Etc.

Though if you’re asking about design without regard to putting ink on paper, you might get different answers.
