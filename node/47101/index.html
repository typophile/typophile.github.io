Greetings, I'm trying to recreate the flavor of this delightful book cover, and I thought you fellow typophiles might have some suggestions for similars to the three typefaces—which are obviously hand drawn—that I could use as starting ponts. Your help and expertise is greatly appreciated, as always! PS: For our American friends—hope you're having a great holiday weekend. [img:sites/default/files/old-images/HGW_sample_6117.jpg]
Hi Mike, This is a very subjective question, since it's hard to guess how much 'tweaking' you want to do. I looked through hand-drawn lettering fonts for each of the three styles on the cover. (1) = HOW GAMBLERS WIN; (2) = OR THE SECRETS OF; and (3) = ADVANTAGE PLAYING. I looked for fonts without layering that you would use for (1) and (3). These would be fonts I might try for these (just ideas based on a quick look): (1): [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/ad-hoc/|Ad Hoc]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/fontfont/ff-rekord/|Rekord]], [[http://www.dafont.com/repent.font|Repent]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/redrooster/sinclair-script-rr/|Sinclair]] (2): [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/p22/escher-hand/|Escher Hand]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/bitstream/kooky-bt/|Kooky]] (3): [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/t26/toulouse-lautrec-moulin-rouge/|Moulin Rouge Solide]] - Mike Yanega
