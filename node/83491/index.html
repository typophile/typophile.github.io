2011-07-13 16:18:48 • brainvsbraun
This is a variant ampersand. I've seen this form before, and if you look at the construction you'll see that it follows the most common & form, only with the leg curled back to re-join the abbreviated lower left loop: this is a quickly written form, made with a single movement starting in the lower middle and moving up to the right. Coincidentally, during the type crit session at TypeCon on Sunday, Matthew Carter advised against a kind of swash numeral 8 that a designer had made on the grounds that it could easily be confused with this form of ampersand.
