2009-04-17 20:42:07 • phrostbyte64

The “spiky” triangular/wedge serifs are the defining characteristic of the Latin sub-group of serifed typefaces. Vendome, Warnock, Meridien, ITC Veljovic, and a whole bunch of typefaces with “Latin” in the name (Latin Elongated/Condensed, etc.) all fall into this category.

There’s an open question as to how blunt the end can get before you consider it something else, I suppose. Warnock makes the question trickier because the degree of bluntness depends on the optical size….

Back in the late ’80s or early ’90s, Richard Eckersley designed a book in Vendome that we set using a Linotron 202. Best I can remember, the only Vendome fonts available were the 18-point master. With that master size, they were a little thin and tightly-spaced at test size. We could fix the tight letterfit somewhat, but it all in all, it still looked like a display face used for text. I wonder how much of the “display only” feeling has to do with the master sizes used for photocomp & digitizations.
