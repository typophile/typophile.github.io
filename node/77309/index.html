1950's? Kaiser Gypsum logo.

December 17, 2010 • Galane

Anyone have some ideas on what fonts might be in this logo? It’s from the top of an old notepad. Note the phone number. It’s from the early years of 7 digit phone numbers, when people were still using the old two letter codes for the first two digits of the number. That dates the notepad to somewhere between 1947 and the mid 1950’s.

Another thing to note is the rather extreme italic angle, I’ve already done a re-creation of this for a personal letterhead, changed the words and number of course. To get close to that angle I had to use an italic font and apply pseudo italic to it in Photoshop then I had to further lean it over a bit using skew. I used some variety of Franklin Gothic, close but not quite, it has the lower tail on the G which this logo doesn’t, and some other little differences.

For the line of small text, I couldn’t find anything anywhere near close, so I just used a font that looked somewhat similar then used Photoshop’s adjusting tools to mush it around to fit the overall width and height.

I’d like to find fonts that are closer matches, if possible.

I don’t know about the logo offhand. The rest sure looks like Franklin Gothic. The original, of course, not the sanitized ITC version. That weight which is relatively bold was the “regular” in the original.
