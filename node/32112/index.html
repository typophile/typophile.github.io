Secondary school newspaper redesign

March 9, 2007 • samd

I am leading a redesign of my secondary school’s student newspaper. (I’m a student.) Critique would be gratefully accepted. This is just the front page, but it gives a pretty good idea of how the entire paper is going to look.

I’m using Minion Pro for body copy, Chaparral Pro for headlines, captions, and some text, Clan Narrow Bold for the flag, and Alternate Gothic No. 2 for the big front page headline and some other text. (All these typefaces are either included with InDesign or available for free from the foundry’s website - we don’t have much of a budget.)

The new design

Nice layout.

My feeling is that Chaparral and Minion are too cool and “bookish” for a newspaper. Newspapers need something in your face, tight, and rough around the edges, to make the news fresh and, well “edgy”.

You’ve captured that feel in the all-caps stacked head.

So I would see if Utopia is one of the free fonts, big on the body, it’s a proven news text font.

Sam, This looks really good. A few thoughts:

  • For captions which use quotes, hang them.
  • Because you are tinting the page numbers, I don’t think you need a full space.
  • Try to design around having to right justify. I’m specifically referring to the image/text combination in the bottom right.
  • The rules above and below the byline seem to be an odd length. This could be a personal preference thing.
  • The paragraph indents could be smaller.
  • The UPCOMING EVENTS box: I think that text could be larger, add more contrast to the overall page.

I love to see Chaparal being used. It is such a warm typeface, and it works very well with Minion.

The only thing that I’m not sold on is the flag. It seems squooshed into that space. DId you try right justifying it and having the rule below end right before it?

2007-03-09 19:19:55 • Don McCahill

Bitsream Charter is also a proven newspaper text face. The Seattle Times uses it. And you can get the 4 base styles for free.

Utopia is also available for the free software community, but in order to use it on a Mac you would need to convert it.

As to the design, I happen to be a Chapparal hater. If you insist on using it for a headline, use the bolder versions. The weight you have is too light to really stand out from the overall gray color of the page.

Also, you might consider losing a few of the horizontal rules.Your first column has 3 rules before you even get to your lead sentence. That’s too much eye dirt that a person has to fight through to get to the actual meat of the story.

Ditto on the indents. A good rule is to set the indent to the size of your text, an EM space.
