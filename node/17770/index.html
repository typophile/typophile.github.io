Have you seen the Wikipedia entry for 4AD? It says, in part: “It was originally formed as Axis Records, but this was quickly changed to 4AD when it became apparent that the name Axis was already being used by another music company. The 4AD logo is a spoof of the AAD Audio CD process designation.”

Don’t know whether this is true or not. However, there is a lengthier, very detailed history of the label (cited in the Wikipedia entry) on a site called Facing the Wrong Way. Go to their Features section and you will find the history of the label.

You might also try your luck searching in Rick Poynor’s book, Vaughan Oliver: Visceral Pleasures.

Another good bet is an older book, This Rimy River, which is the catalog for a v23 exhibition.

Last but not least, and hard to find: Issue 9 of Emigre magazine had interviews with Vaughan Oliver and Ivo Watts-Russell (4AD’s owner, at least back then)…

Regarding the origin of the 4AD label name, as far as I know it was a play on the words “1980 FORWARD” which appeared on an early flyer. As mentioned by Ricardo, the original label was called AXIS, but they had to change it because some other company already had the name. However, during the first year of the record company they had already put out a couple of records under the AXIS name. An AXIS flyer they had produced in order to advertise these releases (which were put out in 1980) had the following play on words printed on it:

1980 FORWARD 1980 FWD 1984 AD 4AD

From what I know, Ivo and Peter (the founders of 4AD) were scrambling for a new name for the company and took “4AD” from this flyer.
