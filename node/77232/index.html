Just to give some more info on Perec; he lived from 1936 to 1982, was a member of Oulipo (Workshop for experimental literature) and one of the best postmodern authors. In his work he often uses constraints and mathematical structures. He was jewish (both of his parents died in WWII) and this had a significant implicit impact on his work. Missing elements (like a novel without the letter E) or storylines that revolve around a void, a loss of memory.

I hope this helps give you some ideas?!

Alphaville is constrained to vertical, horizontal, and 37° strokes (and is named after a French film). Panoptica, which was designed for constraints-based verse by Christian Bök, is constrained to one case and monowidth (and is named after ideas in Foucault’s Discipline and Punish).
