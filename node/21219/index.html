Rene, That is a tough one to quantify into a procedure. There are differing opinions on what constitutes legibility and different solutions. I don’t think in the end that there is a recipe agreed upon. It is hard work trying then testing then changing then testing again. The size also matters. A type ballanced for 10pt has different needs than one ballanced for 14pt. My not-very-helpful suggestion is that you just have to fight with it long enough to train yourself to see differences and tinker to make it work.

One tidbit: Look at Gill Sans Extra Bold, and Gill Sans Display Extra Bold. Note the awkwardness of the counters of the “text” version, when enlarged. The huge variation of stroke weights in Gill Extra bold are there to provide adequate legibility at small sizes. That’s something you don’t do in a display face. Look at other robust text faces (Serif too), for more things like this. This is just an extreme example of optical compensation for legibility.
