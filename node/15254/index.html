" to research and create typographic specimens for" “supposed to connect our 3 typefaces through a theme to unify the spreads”

Maybe I’m misreading the word “specimens” and what you mean is “examples of good use”, but, Normally, when making a series of “typographic specimens”, the typographer shows the fonts in a variety of sizes within the family, a mix of styles, an “all-characters” composition, and if space-paper permits, an example of use, like a box of Tide, a business card or, etc…and if even more space permits in good contrast with other families of type.

In most traditional specimens, the connecting theme is the layout itself, which is commonly simple, allowing each typeface family freedom to be composed as the “voice of the face requests”, avoiding the need to make up “a history” of Germany in order to get 3 pretty different fonts into the same connecting “use” ;)
