Small cap figures

February 18, 2010 • agisaak

If you had a font which contained three sets of figures – lining, oldstyle, and small caps – would you set it up so that the oldstyle figures were accessible via the ‘onum’ feature and the small caps via ‘smcp’ and ‘c2sc’, or would you make the oldstyle figures accessible via both ‘onum’ and ‘smcp’ and the small caps via ‘c2sc’?

Or would you do something entirely different?

André

Personally, I’d take option 1, but I also know that several of my former colleagues at Adobe were quite convinced that oldstyle numerals were the right thing to use with small caps.

It gets more complicated than that, too. As the user could turn on multiple of these features, as the type designer you need to decide what happens in those conflicts. For example, which takes precedence on the numbers, ‘c2sc’ or ‘onum’? I’d vote for c2sc.

In my own fonts, I make the smallcap figures available through a combination of “default figure style” (as InDesign calls it, i.e. neither onum nor lnum) and “smcp” or “c2sc”. The only drawback is that it does not allow you to make a distinction between tabular and proportional smallcap figures.
2010-02-18 16:21:40 • Tim Ahrens

I make my smallcap figures override other figure styles. This enables me to employ both tabular and proportional smallcap numerals, based on the previous numeral style selection. If lining numerals are selected or default, these are treats as ‘cap’ numerals, and are not converted by the smcp feature, only by the c2sc feature. The mappings are like this:

numeral -> numeral.S* (as determined by default numeral style; in c2sc if lining, in smcp if oldstyle)

c2sc: numeral.LT -> numeral.ST numeral.LP -> numeral.SP

smcp: numeral.OT -> numeral.ST numeral.OP -> numeral.SP

2010-02-19 13:44:53 • kentlew

I have considered producing one set of tabular figures, and kerning them to produce proportional spacing.

So the user gets proportional with kerning turned on, and tabular with it off. (The same principle could be applied to monospaced typefaces in general: several people have inquired whether a kerned version of Panoptica was available, which is what prompted the idea.)

However, I abandoned this technique, as it would require too much metadata in order to be effective. In other words, it’s unlikely that users would be aware that such “a feature” was in a font, even if it was mentioned in, say, a pdf specimen.

2010-02-19 15:09:51 • agisaak

As a book designer, I would prefer that an option for having oldstyle figures with my small caps were available. Your scheme would prevent that without manually highlighting figures and turning off {smcp}.

If your preference is to have small caps figs subbed in with your {smcp} and {c2sc} features, then I suggest letting {onum} override them and ordering {onum} later in your feature file, so that users have some viable option.

I dislike stylistic features that are not easily “turnoffable.”

That’s my 2¢.
