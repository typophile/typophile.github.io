2003-11-10 21:21:38 • Hector Muñoz
1) That's an extreme example. It's from Armenia, where the money is very tight these days, so type fidelity -especially in newspapers- is very low. The typical low-print-quality Western paper looks more like this:



2) I don't know if horizontals gain more than verticals as a rule. But it could be, because of the cylinder press direction, and maybe the paper too.

3) In the Armenian sample the callendaring of the paper (at least I think that's what it's called) has a strong effect.

4) There's a hard-to-find article by Beatrice Warde in a publication called "Bowater Papers" (#3, 1954, from page 42) that shows many great examples of fidelity loss. I have a copy, but it's pretty bad quality itself!

5) There's an article by Philip Gaskell in issue #7 (1971) of the Journal of the Printing Historical Society called "Photographic Enlargements of Type Forms" (pp 51-53, but with 8 plates) with really superb close-ups of many classic fonts, printed optimally (as you can still see the gain). I have an original of that.
