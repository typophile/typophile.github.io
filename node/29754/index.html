2006-11-28 04:53:18 • Gman22

This isn’t meant to sound harsh, just honest:

First, the logo is in need of something a little more elegant if you’re specializing in ‘upscale weddings.’ It’s trying a little too hard to be the superman S but is failing miserably, to put it kindly. I don’t think the mason typeface is what you want for the ‘productions’ part of this logo type. It doesn’t say ‘professional’. It’s context is more of the demonic use.

Your tagline starts right under the ‘g’ and is just awkward looking underneath the logo.

I would give more space between your phone number and your website. You might also want to differentiate the two with maybe having one as a sans font. I would also set your name in a sans typeface to make it stand out so people know who to talk to.

And is it me or did you stretch yourself on the picture? It doesn’t look stretched by much, but just a little bit to throw it off. Maybe it’s just the size I’m viewing it at.

Hope this helps. Best luck with the redesign.

2006-11-29 06:54:14 • mili

Yes, definitely lose the photo, it could be seen as arrogant, and it is robbing space from the relevant information.

I’d bring the phone number in line with the job title and website (same type, size, style and right alignment), and lose the horizontal rule.

The logo could manage without the hyphen, and ‘Productions’ would look much more pleasant in letterspaced caps (or letterspaced small caps if they are available to you) in the same typeface as the other information.

With a bit of tweaking you could match the height of the ‘G’ to the rest of the logo and also make the horizontal rule line up perfectly with the serif of the ‘G’.

Once the picture was removed you would have the logo top left and details bottom right, with plenty of space (space implies quality), and nice tension across the card.

Also, could ‘Emcee’ be unfamiliar terminology to an upscale crowd? MC may be a safer choice.

Good luck with everything
