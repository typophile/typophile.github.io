[img:sites/default/files/old-images/type_4383.png] I've had quite a time trying to ID this type. Let's hope some of you have more luck. I've used Identifont's type identifier several times, and have found *dozens* of fonts that are ALMOST a perfect match (Times New Roman Seven, Century Old Style, Old Style 7, Varous Goudy types, some Garramond variants)... but each has a few glyphs that are different. Times seven, for example, is missing the descending capital 'J', and the italic lowercase 'v' and 'w' are pointed rather than curved. FFClifford Six is also very close, but the uppercase 'W' joins too high in the middle, the lowercase italic 'w' is slightly different, as is the lowercase italic 'f'... not to mention the fact that this type didn't exist in 1981. Some of the italic glyphs have been most difficult to match. Note that the 'f' doesn't quite terminate in a ball at the bottom. Also note the little loop at the beginning of the 'w' these seem to be rare characteristics that I haven't been able to find in any fonts that meet the other requirements. The numbers may also be of help. Happy Hunting.
