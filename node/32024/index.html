March 6, 2007 • ebensorkin

I have been excited to see the release of Softmachine ever since Nick was kind enough to show it to me a year or so ago. I really like the face’s personality which is much warmer than VAG rounded or Gotham. Plus It has a ton of great features including a really great use of one I have advocated for here on Typophile - Contextual Alternatives - made not to connect letters in a way that better simulates writting ( Nick has done that too in Handsome Pro ) but instead to do something even more essential, to get a better fit - or dare I say it? …A more ideal ‘Notan’*! This may be the ultimate logo font for Web 2.0!

Yes, the old “tight but not touching” typositor setting, but touching (!) Here’s a classic example of a tight-setting type design, and the way it relates to layout; from 1969, art director Woody Woodward.

2007-03-15 18:36:24 • hrant

Your point on the affinity of layering and outlining is well made.

Advert wasn’t initially designed for layering, the Rough is a “product extension”. It takes the basic letterforms, grunges them, and revises the metrics. But the process doesn’t go the next step and revise the letter forms, which could have been done without OT. In fact, the combination r_t in the word “Advert” is a prime candidate for changing to look more consistgently “outlined” in layering.

I had thought about a family for Softmachine, but decided that it’s OK to be single, like Impact.
