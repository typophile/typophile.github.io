Typical type in 1950s textbooks?

December 26, 2006 • rucola

I’m wondering which typefaces might typically have been used in science textbooks of the 1950s. My guess is that most British publishers would still have used a serif face (possibly even with oldstyle figures?), whereas American books might have something more modernist, maybe a slab serif or Franklin. But which ones?

If anyone knows of scanned images of such things, so much the better.

Most primer schoolbooks in the USA were set in Century Schoolbook - a square slab serif for easier letter recognition.

Math textbooks of that era were generally set in Monotype Modern. A minority would have been in Monotype Old Style. You’ll probably also find some in Times, Baskerville, Caledonia, and the other massively popular book faces of the day.
