Hi Sandro. I guess this is much easier for native Croatian speakers to get their heads around. Please take what I say in the spirit of objective critique only: Personally I have a problem with the loop of the 'g' doubling for the bowl of the 'e' on the lower line. In order to read the 'g' the 'e' has to become something else - so the arrangement compromises the readability of the second line anyway. Why a lowercase 'd' to start the logo and an uppercase 'S' halfway through it? Does Croatian capitalise proper nouns? Why the extended stroke of the finishing 't'? It doesn't balance the 'S'. The negative shape below the second line is awkward (it would be anyway with this letter combination, but...) The dotless 'i' and 'j' in the second line appear to be dotless only because their baseline is with the 'e' (not enough vertical space). And the 'e' is only that high because you want it to be part of the 'g' above... this is maybe not a strong enough reason for compromising the 'i' and 'j' by removing their dots. Are they a 'classical' band? Why use a 'classical' type style? What aspect of the idea of 'another world' is being incorporated into the arrangement? Where is the design actually crossing boundaries? Contrary to what Lore says above, I think it should look like a rock band, in a conventional sense. There are many other ways to start exploring this name visually, so I advise you to return to the drawing board.

2006-12-05 13:25:56 • garden
garden- i don't know how many variations you have tried, however, it looks like you've tried maybe 10 and are just stuck. don't get caught up in the typeface selection. like everyone's said, try sketching a bunch of versions. sure, the computer is easier to manipulate but without a strong concept, your execution will fail. there are lots of great typefaces out there. one of my new favorite foundries is www.typetrust.com you can find some great typefaces there that might fit what you are looking for. i would scratch your initial concepts due to the awkwardness and legibility issues. i am not opposed to the manipulation of the type, but if you're going to do it, have purpose behind it and make it appear logical, even though it's not. that's the beauty of ligatures. best of luck.
