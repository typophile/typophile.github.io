Do you have Stephenson Blake specimen books?

April 27, 2008 • blank

I’m working on a design based on the old Fry’s Baskerville metal types sold by Stephenson Blake. The specimen I have has no diacritics, old-style figures, or the italic. If you have a specimen book that contains these, would you please share the title and year of publication with me?
James -- I believe that it was not common for British and American foundries of the period to include any accented sorts in their regular fonts. If you look in catalogs from this period, you'll find that accents are generally provided as loose sorts, sometimes referred to as "piece" accents, in the "typographic accessories" section. My 1926 Stephenson Blake specimen shows loose accents on page 89. It's probable that a printer with regular need for accented characters could special order composed sorts of specific accented characters for the size and typeface of his choosing. The 1923 ATF catalog gives a list of the specific fonts & sizes for which they can provide cast accented characters and a generic showing of the variety of accents they make available. But they don't have any showings for specific faces. Also, I suspect that the accents themselves were all cut with generic punches or patterns, with a limited range of styles (if indeed any choices), regardless of the face. -- Kent.
