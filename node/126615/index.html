Many thanks, Jay. Looking at that specimen book, it appears that this Oxford 1940 is an exact match for Twelve Point Caslon Old Face. Indeed, it is page 16, but I first looked at absolute page 16, which obviously doesn’t match. Then started studying others at high zoom and came to the conclusion above. Then zoomed out and, boom, page 16!.

Note the major differences between ‘C’ at 12pt (no lower serif) vs 18pt and above. Also the length of the base of the ‘E’ at 12pt (longer relative width) vs 14pt and above.

So unless some other discovery arises we can consider this one solved. Thanks again, Jay!
