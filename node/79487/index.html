"You. I." serif face

February 27, 2011 • mkstudio

It’s amazing to me how much variation you can find in these four letters. I have looked at Garalde styles until I am cross-eyed. The closest I can suggest is Adobe Garamond Premier. Your sample seems a bit softer, but perhaps that’s an artifact of the resolution? If it is this font, it may have been vertically stretched a bit. What I kept looking at was the shape of the serifs and the counter of the Y (a sort of arrowhead shape)

My first feeling was that it was softened Bitstream Arrus, and that’s maybe the best alternative I can suggest. Arrus seems sharper than this sample, but maybe the image affected this, or some extra stroke was used to soften the points. Arrus seemed close because the serif edges are slightly curved, as they seem to be in your sample.
