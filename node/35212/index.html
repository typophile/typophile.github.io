Chiseled sans used on cheap backlit signs

July 17, 2007 • design_monkey

Hey All, We've all seen them - the cheaply printed letters on clear plastic that are placed into roadside or portable backlit signs. The kind of signage where numbers and glyphs sometimes do double duty for missing letters. Need a "D"? Why not flip and reverse that uppercase "P"? Out of the letter "I"? No problem! That's what 1s are for! Chances are that some of our members have either stolen or rearranged the letters to change the original communication. I'm just saying. Anyway, I'm trying to identify and locate a digital version of the font: [img:sites/default/files/old-images/apply_within_5293.jpg] Apologies if this question has been asked and answered before. Any help would be much appreciated, and I won't think less of you for pointing me to a free font that has horrible kerning pairs, slightly misshapen letterforms and only one case. ;)
You might find something you like here: http://www.mr-clipart.com/int/anzeigen_font.php?art_id=1&seite=1&Anzahl=16 This is probably not what you're looking for as it's more stylized, but it has a similar flavor ... Carved Initials by Gerald Gallo http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/gallofonts/carved-initials/ Also, Harold Lohner used to have a similar font called Pop Up. It's retired now, but if you make a request to Harold he might accommodate: http://www.haroldsfonts.com/retired.html There are a few kinda similar chiseled fonts here: http://www.stanford.edu/~rdinner/fonts.html Hope this helps!
