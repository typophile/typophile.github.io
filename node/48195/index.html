How does a font get on a wall?

August 8, 2008 • pdxuser

How does, say, Dom Casual end up in paint on the exterior wall of a mechanic’s shop? Does an artist just paint from a sample? I’m guessing the bricks aren’t shoved through a printer, but I’ve seen some extremely precise lettering and was curious if something else was going on that I’ve not thought of.
It’s probably either stenciled through a custom made vinyl mask, or outlined and then filled in by hand.
2008-08-08 04:18:53 • penn

depends on the surface of the wall. if it was a very smooth surface, the lettering might not be painted, it might be cut vinyl letters. These are cut into adhesive colored vinyl film (using a cutter much like a CAD plotter), essentially making a large sticker, which is then adhered to the wall, with a backing tape to hold everything in position when applying.

Alternately, the letter could be cut, by the same method as above, out of a “mask” vinyl, where the letters would be removed, the remaining mask applied to the wall, then you just apply paint, which only hits the exposed letters, and then remove the mask.

Finally, you can make a “ponce” pattern, where the same plotter device makes a dotted perforation line in a large piece of paper around the outlines of all letters. then the pattern is taped to the wall, chalk is used to mark the lettering through the pounce pattern, and the template is removed, leaving a light outlines of letter edges to paint on.
