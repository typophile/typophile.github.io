Joe, for someone so picky about grammar, it’s ironic that you would misuse “malapropism”.

What do you suppose “Fontographer” sounds like, but isn’t? Photographer? Pornographer?

This is more neologism than misuse. Deriving a generic noun or verb from a brand name may be considered as neologism, a form of proprietary eponym.

Fontographer, as a noun used to describe a person who makes fonts, is quite logical, because it avoids the ambiguity of using “typographer” for type designer

If there is no allusion (intended or otherwise) to the software product, “fontographer” follows the non-professional trend that assumes “font” to mean “typeface”. If “typographer” exists, why not “fontographer”?

Nonetheless, I still prefer to call myself a type designer.
