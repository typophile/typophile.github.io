Criticism for type layout

December 19, 2005 • Stephen Lording

Hi everyone,

I’ve always wanted to do this but have never had the courage. Criticism, even constructive, can be hard to take. But I’d rather learn and improve than sit in a cardboard box with a towel over my head, so… any comments you have about this layout would be greatly valued.

It’s for a garden, and will possibly be lasercut into an A3-sized block of wood. If not, it’ll just be digitally printed onto a panel. The typeface, Interstate, is used throughout the site. I’ve basically sought to reference the ripple effect of water, and to create rivers around the distinct phrases. I’ve used loose tracking to… loosen things up, they way the body does when you’re ‘running’, ‘talking’ and ‘walking’.

Is that enough of my thinking to go on?

Thanks in advance.

I’ll let others more competent than myself do the critiqueing. However, I’d like you to reconsider the box/towel covered head bit. Not because your stuff is bad, rather I feel like doing just that a number of times per day, and I suspect my life would be more peaceful if I gave in. . . . ;)

2006-01-10 22:36:41 • Stephen Lording

I get what you’re trying to do with the different sizes and loose placement of the words/phrases, but the effect - for me - is not quite as peaceful or calming the words suggest. Maybe try a little more leading and perhaps not quite as many sizes of type - or a little more uniformity to the sizes. Then the phrases might flow a bit more, rather than seeming to start and stop.

Personally I tend to find it hard to read type where the tracking is greater than the leading.
