body: "<strong>[[Indices]] : [[Designers]] : Ute Harder</strong>\r\n\r\nUte Harder,
  aka Frau Jenson, moved to Hamburg, [[Germany]] for university studies after unsatisfying
  jobs with a pharmacy and then a paper manufacturer. Another year followed with a
  variety of jobs including the great experience as a scenery assistant at the 'Ohnsorg
  Theater' in Hamburg. In 1994 she studied [[Illustration]] and Communication design
  at the University of Applied Science in Hamburg. In the typography classes of [[Jovica
  Veljovic]] she 'got infected by the typevirus'. In 1999 she digitized '[[Marathon]]',
  a hot metal typeface originally designed by [[Rudolf Koch]] which received special
  mention at 'Kurt Christians F\xF6rderpreis'-Contest. Marathon is licensed to the
  [[Linotype Library]] GmbH. Frau Jenson graduated with the 'Diplom-Designer FH' in
  2002 and since she is working as a freelance graphic designer, collaborating on
  diverse projects for Linotype and developing her own fonts. Frau Jenson lives and
  works in Hamburg.\r\n\r\nType designs by Ute Harder: <a href=\"http://www.linotype.com/58042/marathon-family.html\">Marathon</a>,
