Is that the actual way that ç it was written? From my understanding, at least in Spanish from where the letter was derived, it was an alternate way of writing the letter z that later got reanalysed as c with a squiggle, rather than them having consciously written c with a z underneath it in the manner that the e was written above letters. So, really I guess the "old fashioned" way to do it would be to have a z with a c above :) (kidding of course). http://medievalwriting.50megs.com/graphics/alphabet/visigothic/visigothz.jpg Basically, originally it was more or less the contemporary z but with an ascending (akin to the flourishes often seen on the 7 in contemporary handwriting) and descending flourish (like in many italic fonts). Overtime, the ascending flourish was reanalysed as a C, with the diagonal and descending flourishes seen as a diacritic (excuse the roughness of my example, I did it pretty rapidly): [img:sites/default/files/old-images/ztocedilla_4227.png] «El futuro es una línea tan fina que apenas nos damos cuenta de pintarla nosotros mismos». (La Luz Oscura, por Javier Guerrero)
