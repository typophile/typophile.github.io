The uppercase T is somewhat reminiscent of a font issued by the Classic Font Company, which they call "Amadeus":
http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/classic/amadeus/
See what you think.

I'm 99% sure the second sample is hand-lettered calligraphy. There are, however, dozens of fonts made from hand-drawn calligraphy, many of which are freeware. "Apple Chancery" is a commercial font, but similar--a traditional "Chancery Italic" style. I would particularly recommend Pia Frauss' "Francisco Lucas" family, available for free download at:
http://www.pia-frauss.de/fonts/fl.htm
IN terms of the uncials, there are plenty of good uncial fonts too, many free. Among my favourites are:
* MouserFonts' "Gourdie Uncial" and "Gourdie Uncial Deux" (go to: http://www.mouserfonts.com/ and search for "uncial")
* "Unciaal" (http://www.dafont.com/en/font.php?file=unciaal)
* "Cry Uncial" (http://www.dafont.com/en/font.php?file=cry_uncial)
etc.
Fontcraft's Scriptorium is a good starting place as well:
http://www.fontcraft.com/csa/fontcraft.php
