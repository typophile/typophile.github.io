Help on finding a Greek frutiger font

March 13, 2008 • Laura Griggs

Evening, I’m not sure if this is the right place to ask. As I’m new perhaps someone could point me in the right direction if it isn’t.

I have to produce a Greek report where the required font is Frutiger LT 45 Light. I understand there was a Frutiger LT Greek a few years ago. Does anyone know where I can purchase this?

If not, can anyone recommend a Greek font that looks very similar and where I may purchase that? Many thanks.

Since the Frutiger Next Greek Linotype commissioned from Eva Masoura a few years ago (which won a TDC2 prize) is not yet available, there is no “Frutiger Greek” you can use (although the regular Frutiger has a Cyrillic, it does not have Greek).

Myriad and Segoe are in similar veins to Frutiger—humanist sans—or at least that is what many people seem to say. They both have Greeks, though, so you could check them out. Depending on your system and or software, you may already have these fonts installed anyway.
