GEM Fonts are mostly the works of Graham Meade, from Australia (I think). I will not judge Graham’s work, but I have been generously helped by him in the past with samples and information on some of his script fonts. I think ‘originality’ is a difficult call when fonts resemble other fonts. Mikey has shown us fonts with strong resemblances to Helvetica, but then that might be possible for so many fonts, in general, especially sans families, that I am wary about making accusations. Well known foundries have often been willing to ‘tweak’ the designs of other foundries and offer them as their own. One only has to look at the old metal type catalogs of Linotype and Monotype to see how often they imitated each other (now they are parts of the same company).

Stepping down from soap-box now.
