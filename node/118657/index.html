I should mention that with the exception of the “drainpipe” curved foot on G, the unusual leg on R, the top lhs serif on A, and the bottom rhs serif on N and V the design of base letters is derived from a conventional typeface. A word about terminology. An “Egyptian” or “Egyptienne” typeface is a Roman typefaces with slab (or square or rectangular) shaped serifs. Typefaces that have bracketing between main strokes and serifs are known as “Clarendon-style Egyptians.” GOOD MORNING VIETNAM is a Clarendon-style Egyptian. See for example Egyptienne Bold Condensed by Linotype The width, horizontal contrast and serif length are similar but the the horizontal strokes are too heavy and the serif thickness is about twice that in GOOD MORNING VIETNAM.
