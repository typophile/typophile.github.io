if you wanted to give someone five distinguishing characteristics to hgihlight the differences between Adobe Caslon and Adobe Garamond (other than the lower case g and a), what would they be?
The serifs on the uppercase T in Garamond (including Adobe) are very distinctive. They point in the same direction, instead of being symmetrical as in Caslons. Adobe Caslon's W (uppercase) comes to one peak, while Garamond's looks like two overlapping Vs Adobe Garamond has serifs both at the top and the bottom of the uppercase C while Adobe Caslon only has a top serif. However, other Caslons, such as 540, have both serifs. The center bar on the E and F in Caslon don't stick out as far as in Garamond.
