I’ve been perusing ParaType’s massively good (plus really just massive) comprehensive specimen book (issued in 2004) and I found something that really picked up my spirits:

A post-modern font that’s actually good. It’s called Duetto, designed by Boris Popov in 2002. It takes a heavy and wide sans design, makes it counter-less, and uses a thin italic face to form its whites…

http://www.paratype.com/pstore/fonts/Duetto.htm

This is the only font I ever remember encountering that nicely conveys two voices simultaneously. A PoMo face worth using. Bravo.
