if your sending your jobs to a home printer, then use truetype. if your a professional designer and will be sending projects to various clients such as newsprint, offset presses including magazines... my god get adobe type 1 postcript. Why even chance it. Every press running in north america for the last decades use postcript rips and postcript fonts just work right. Postcript 3 rips do handle ttf very well, but when do you know who's using what. It boils down to this: 1) get truetype or opentype and some printers can handle it...some can't will fix it for you by using a truetype rasterizer or use their own postcript version. And some will send the job back or call you! 2) Get postcript and every printer will be happy! You choose!
