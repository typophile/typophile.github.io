Hey b, I’m from the school that chooses the text font and size first. This is usually between 8.5 and 11 or so points, depending on how the typeface relates to the em (not a big deal), the overall density of the text face you choose, and the age and/or reading abilities of the perceived audience and your column width. The last three are the bigger issues. There are a lot of other ways, but usually a mag in redesign know what the logo and the text look like as they fill in between.

Cheers!

Printing out a series of samples placed in columns works well. I would also do some research looking at different magazines and find ones that have a size that you like. Bring a ruler measure in mm for a more exact measurement or a pica ruler and then convert to points. you can download a demo application called the Art Directors Toolkit it is an amazing time saver, it also does grid calculations and more. check it out here: http://www.code-line.com/software/artdirectorstoolkit.html I would not go any smaller than 7.5pt sans serif face or 8pt serif, it is all about legibility and aesthetics in the end.
