2004-04-08 10:26:46 • timd
Emilie - As mentioned, the vinyl cannot be removed with success but it does however stick to many surfaces. I'm guessing you'll be sticking it to plaster or sheetrock walls - which it will do with ease. In fact, we used to have it on the walls of our old apartment. White on white walls can look nice. We used Fast Signs. Basically the Kinkos of vinyl. There are two main grades - normal and heavy duty. The heavy duty will work outdoors and they claim it even sticks to cement. I wouldn't suggest that, but the museum I used to work for has had the same vinyl on sheetrock walls for years. Usually you can just send an Illustrator eps of your artwork. Be mindful of the sizes the shop has as it can dramatically alter the price.
2004-04-08 13:41:23 • eolson
There are many grades and suppliers of vinyl for signage. There are removable or low tack vinyl and vinyl that that is extremely permanent that it is used for identification on jet airliners. I would suggest that you talk to a number of sign companies that specialize in vinyl cut signage about the cost and your application use. Depending on the amount of copy an alternate would be to print out the copy on your printer and use that as a template for cutting or tracing to the walls. You can purchase low tack vinyl from office supply stores that can be pre cut by you and affixed to the wall as a template for painting the copy. gln
