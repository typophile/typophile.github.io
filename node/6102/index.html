Signs & graphics

August 16, 2004 • kris

G'day, I have just been employed as a graphic designer/signwriter at a place called "Signz 'n Graphix". It seems that I have some at a fortunate time, as we have decided to totally redesign, starting from our internal processes to applied identity. At the moment we design, print (digital xerox and riso), vinyl and photographic signage, large format quality art reproductions. We want to keep it simple and apply the branding to business cards to outdoor signage. I have chosen Thesis Sans as the main typeface as it appears to work very well from large to small. I am thinking of a pallette of yellow+black, a homage to nz's road signs and blatant visiblity. At the moment the building is a purple colour and promoted as "the purple place". I think it is quite tired (15 years) and dated, as the road signage is pretty much invisible from a distance and a new building next door blocks the view of our premises from the road. The bottom image shows our current street sign from 50m down the road. I am trying to get the name spelt 'correctly' - signs & graphics instead of signz n' graphix, which is a pain to spell and looks amateurish.
This is the Front. mmm, purple.
