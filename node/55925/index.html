An interesting tangent you might follow in this pursuit would be to look at the big ATF specimen books from the early 20th century: 1906, 1912, 1923. Probably also the big BB&S book from that period. To illustrate how types might be used, advertisements for businesses were included on many pages. The businesses were for the most part made-up, just as today many design students create businesses on which to base their identity projects. I'm sure there were hotel ads. So it gets a bit fanciful, since these were not likely to be real businesses. But it may give a view of what was considered to be the "way" hotel ads were to look. & you'd probably find better composition than in the Biltmore ad Nick shows us. Have fun. powers
