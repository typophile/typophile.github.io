I visited Mission San Diego de Alcalá, the first Catholic Franciscan mission in San Diego, California. Beside doorway entrances I noticed this beautiful calligraphic brush work. You can tell this was freshly painted within the past 10 years. Probably an old style that is recreated by a skilled painter every decade or so. I’m really curious if someone has recreated this in digital form.

The painter was working from a copy of Columbus Late 19th century ATF typeface. Don

Don - Looking at more characters, it’s clear that both are terribly made fonts (the Sam Wang TTF must be a conversion from his Mac original. I don’t know how much error that introduces). Hard to say which is worse, though.

Font validation yields 96 glyphs (all of them) with errors in Columbus. Validation of Beaumarchais yields 24 glyphs with errors - some with multiple errors and more serious errors than Columbus. That doesn’t catch all of the straight lines where there should be curves - and both fonts suffer greatly from that.

Throw in that both fonts stray far from the original and, yeah, I guess there’s no reason not to go with the freebie.
