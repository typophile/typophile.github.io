>in my garage i have tons of old image club catalogs...

Heh. So do I. Imagine that.

>You never got any public credit for those faces, is that correct?

We never credited the designers in the Image Club or EyeWire catalogs, but we did include attributions on the web site. In fact, you can still view them in amongst the broken remains of the EyeWire site. I was also fortunate (or cursed) enough to have a designer profile written about my type exploits back when I was still at EyeWire.

>I see that Identifont has you in its database now. Woohoo!

Yep. I'm very happy to see that finally in place too. I emailed back and forth with David Johnson-Davies quite a bit to get all the Image Club information properly cleaned up and cross-referenced. Plus I was able to give him all of the nit-picky designer credits from my type library database.

>Did you have a script face in the TypeCon gallery?

Oops. That was mis-credited at TypeCon. I did the design of the sample, not the typeface. The typeface was Hucklebuck by Jason Walcott (Jukebox).

>2) Do you know what Agfa/Monotype's arrangement with Image Club fonts is?

My understanding is that AMT purchased the Image Club Type Library, the associated trademarks and the EyeWire website from Getty Images. They did not purchase the remaining Image Club or EyeWire products such as the clip art or stock images).

>Why do I ask? At $42 USD a pop, these fonts are priced considerably higher

>with older fonts at $22 and new releases at $22 to $32, something seems amiss.

The current pricing for the Image Club single faces is certainly higher than they were at EyeWire. Although, I always thought that they were underpriced to begin with. I don't think there's any conspiracy here, I suspect that AMT understands that there was some pent up demand for those faces during the dead period of the EyeWire business. Since they already own the library, I don't think there is any advantage to positioning them for failure.

Of course, I may be a bit biased in my opinions on this subject.

>3) Does the Image Club branding still exist in any way.
>It certainly isn't revealed on Fonts.com

Other than on my hard drive and in my Image Club archives, I don't think so. Agfa certainly isn't going to be using the Image Club name or brand for anything. AMT's own branding currently wavers between schizophrenic and that glutinous mass of sub-brands and third-party products on the Fonts.com site. I can't see them doing any Image Club specific marketing, even if they do own the brand now.

I'm pleased they got picked up as well, although in their current state (missing formats, missing weights, strange pricing, no full collection) - I wonder how long they'll last.
