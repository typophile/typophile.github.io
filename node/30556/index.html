2007-01-15 10:33:41 • Stephen Lording

In a true life situation, i do advertising for a little cafe nearby. One day, the owner had to fire one of his waiters. Later that night, the fired waiter returned to steal the two beautiful wooden signs outside the restaurant.

He never did say what he did with them. The police searched his house and all the nearby dumpsters.

I felt bad for the cafe, each sign to replace was about $2200 each. The signs had no value to anyone but the cafe. And yes, he had insurance, but a huge deductible.

To make new signs took several weeks. At the time I had a 36 inch poster maker, so we made quick ones placed on poster board and laminated to hold up in the weather. Funny, everyone knows the cafe is there – on the corner of an intersection, but without the signs, folks just couldn’t find it. Amazing…

So sometimes, they just get stolen for spite.
