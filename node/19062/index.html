Typeface Suggestions for Funkstyle Dance Crew Ligature

April 6, 2006 • Jeff Kwok

Hey Gang,

I’ve started to work on a project for a local funkstyle dance crew and was looking to get some suggestions for typefaces to help kick start a Ligature. The Dance Crew’s name is the Groovy Gees (GG will be their mark) The mark will be used on the following: Business Cards, A Website, t-shirts and 59Fifty Ballcaps, such as this: http://images.footballfanatics.com/productImages/_106000/FF151333-s.jpg

For those of you that do not know what funkstyle dance, here are a few videos of what it looks like:

http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uixvoZ5PSls http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eP-BJ72ZsBc&search=poppin%20pete http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nYf0O7c0y1U&search=poppin%20pete http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lHO-meQlGKU&search=campbellock

As for style, they’re heavily influenced by hip-hop culture, so basically they wear baggy tee’s, pants, and ballcaps as listed above. As for the ligature, they want to associate it with MLB Logos (LA Dodgers, Boston Red Sox, Atlanta Braves) but with the following qualities:

Groovy, Funky Feel (less ridged, and more fluid) Hand Written Feel

Any suggestions are welcomed! Thanks in advance,

Jeff

I’ve got to say that pencil or big marker and paper are probably your best tools for a job like this, a typeface isn’t really going to help because the Gs will be the same and you won’t be able to get fluidity from them on a computer as quickly as drawing. Tim
