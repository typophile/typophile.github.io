NCAA Gymnastics Championships Logo

February 26, 2008 • wildcoqui

I work for Georgiadogs.com, and UGA is hosting the NCAA gymnastics championships this year. The NCAA doesn't commission a site-specific logo, but they let the host school each year design an event logo (if they want), but it must be approved by them. So, I've been chosen to tackle this. I don't have a whole lot of logo design experience. I want to include a recognizable part of the town (the arch seen in the background), and have a visual reference to gymnastics (the silhouette). I'm sort of stuck here. Any suggestions? The logo will be used mostly on the Web. Thanks!
I don't really think the typeface for the date and place is working with the one used on the rest of the piece. Try using complimentary typefaces or just use the same one throughout. There needs to be either a certain contrast or unity to make things work. I can't say I'm really a fan of the background either. It's obviously just vectorized through some script and not done by hand to be precise. Could just be me. Good start!
2008-02-26 20:59:56 • tylor.reimer
To me, this doesn't look like a logo at all. It's more of a poster design. A trap that many unexperienced designers fall into is including too much in a single logo. Simplify, simplify, simplify. First, start sketching by hand, not on the computer. Second, design the logo in black and white only. No colours, no greys. Just black and white. Pick out one or two ideas you want the logo to convey, and focus on those. Then come back and post a few of your attempts and we'll tell you what is working and what isn't. - Lex
