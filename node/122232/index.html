Looks like several different styles of wood type, or something hand-drawn based on the same. To replicate the bulk of the nameplate, you could use Dharma Gothic or Kommissar Medium (closer for the |G|): http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/flat-it/dharma-gothic/ http://vllg.com/schwartzco/kommissar Swap out the |A| and |M| from Washington Bold (and condense them), and you come pretty close: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/typeassociates/washington/ The aptly named Revolution Gothic approximates the look of the subhead, save for the |A| and the |S|: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/flat-it/revolution-gothic/ American Captain would also work for the subhead, save for the |S|: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/fontry/american-captain/ Steal a similar |S| from Chi Town: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/nicksfonts/chi-town-nf/

EDITED I suspect that this newspaper letterhead is lettering rather than an extract from a specific font. Fonts of the era with similarity to some of the features are Willard T. Sniffin's Newprort with its sharp tiped _M_ and _A_ and lazy _S_. Digitized as Newport Land URW https://www.myfonts.com/fonts/urw/newport-land/ and Newport Classic SG https://www.myfonts.com/fonts/spiecegraphics/newport-classic-sg/ Far from a perfect match to other features, but I think Los Alamos https://www.myfonts.com/fonts/redrooster/los-alamos/ and Raleigh Gothic https://www.myfonts.com/fonts/redrooster/raleigh-gothic-rr/ and https://www.myfonts.com/fonts/grouptype/raleigh-gothic/ sort of harmonize with the lettering. Don
