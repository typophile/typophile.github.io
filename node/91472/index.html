2012-03-27 00:22:10 • Theunis de Jong

Cyrillic writing derived from Greek

Specifically from Byzantine Greek. The connections between some Cyrillic and Greek letters isn’t immediately apparent to people who are only familiar with the classical and modern Greek alphabet norms. I didn’t come to understand all of the relationships until just last year when I was working on a typeface for Byzantine sigillography and numismatics, containing many variant forms tracing more than 1,000 years of Byzantine letterforms. A light went on for me when I saw that H sometimes occurs as a variant form of N in Byzantine uncial, explaining how it came to represent that phoneme in Cyrillic.

I remember the old ikons in the Greek Orthodox Church that I attended as a child. The lettering on them was inherited from the Greek Byzantine forms John mentioned. Saint Paul brought the precursor of the Orthodox version of Christianity to Russia with most of the writings being in Greek. Russians had centuries of familiarity with the Greek letterforms through the Orthodox church. It was an easy way to get started with a new script when most of the people were already familiar with 24 glyphs. St Cyril was a logical choice :-)
