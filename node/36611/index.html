August 30, 2007 • austenite

Hello all,

I know next to nothing about typesetting and found this site from Wikipedia. I have an idea that I think requires some unusual sorts and I would be extremely grateful for any help this group could provide.

The organisation I work for, QMI Solutions, has a semi-experimental machine called an Incremental Sheet Former. You can see an article on the technology here: [[http://www.qmisolutions.com.au/article.asp?aid=160]]

It works by using a solid stylus (e.g. 20mm diameter ball nose) to form metal over a plug. In those pictures it’s making our stock demonstration piece, “The Face”. The CNC control means infinite flexibility from CAD models.

What I would like to do for our demonstrations is to form “The Face”, then have the name of whichever company it’s for to be formed as well. Now that I’ve done my 10 minutes research on Wikipedia, I think I at least know the words to use to ask the questions:

  • I would have a forme with say a 12 character capacity on our plug.
  • The sorts may be around 50mm/2 inches in height.
  • The font would suit tracing with a 20mm ball nose tool (possibly a smaller tool could be used), which means there are no tight concave sections.
  • Because the metal is being pressed over the font it needs to be "right way around" instead of mirrored.
  • For bonus points, the font is designed such that there is a common joining point between all the letters so that the outline of a word is continuous rather than having to trace each letter individually. This may mean the font is a script rather than a print, or it may mean there is a design feature like the lines joining characters on car badges.
  • For even more bonus points, the set is available commercially or second hand somewhere.

Now I’m stuck. Would anyone here be able to point me in the right direction?

Ideally we would be able to buy a set from somewhere (anywhere!). Alternatively if 2D or 3D CAD models (or even font files) of a suitable font could be obtained we could have them machined. I would have to find out how many of each character is a good number.

2D or 3D CAD shape definitions (in whatever format) would be required so that we could created the toolpath in our CAM software.

Thanks in advance for any help at all, James McIntosh
