2009-08-05 08:16:51 • Tomi from Suomi

Well, one could make an argument about Berthold faces, since they operated out of Berlin for 100 years. But they haven’t been in the city for a decade and a half, and times and styles change. Berlin is a different place that it was 70 years ago, 17 years ago, or maybe even 7 years ago…

Obviously, the FontFont library comes to mind, as FSI is the largest typefoundry working in Berlin. But in addition to those good selections, there are other Berlin typeface designers and foundries, like primetype (http://www.prime-type.com) or Hannes von Döhren, who is young and has quite a lot to offer (http://hvdfonts.com/).

My favorite Berlin designer is probably Verena Gerlach (http://www.fraugerlach.de/index.php?cat=3). Note that she also has typefaces in the FontFont library, as well as at primetype. Moreover, together with primetype’s Ole Schäfer, she designed the FF City Street Type family that Stephen mentions above.

2009-08-05 12:57:05 • J Weltin

I am working on a technical looking typeface called ISOGLYPH.

Website: http://www.fontgroup.com/isoglyph/ Datasheet: http://www.futureparc.com/fontgroup/pdfs/FontGroup_ISOGLYPH_Specimen.pdf

I am not German per-se, but am an admirer of the Modern German aesthetic. The attitude of ISOGLYPH is about humanising the somewhat geometric forms of DIN and ISONORM whilst retaining the performance (high words per line ratio) and open letterforms for clarity of type used at small sizes and for signage.It is designed so it can also be used as text. Current font is bold in nature to be able to accomplish the above and has a thinner Titling Alternative inside the OTF. Contact me direct if you are interested.
