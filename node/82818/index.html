BTW, samples with more upper case letters are especially helpful with serif fonts. This sample had only H, D and B, none of which is usually an earmark letter. From my point of view, which is using the Serif Guide, the E, J, K, M, R, U and W are keys, but also G, C and Q. The a, g and y are usually the most helpful lower case letters.
