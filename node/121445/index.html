Hello,

I am hoping some type sleuths can help me with solving this type mystery!

I absolutely love the typeface used for “Futurismo”, although I suspect that might be custom? What I’d really love to know is the name of the type / similar type for the green text, ie, “Benito Mussolini” and “Evviva il genio futurista di”.

I understand that in the early 1900s there wouldn’t be typefaces created, necessarily. I am looking for something similar—perhaps a newly digitized version of the linotype.

This cover is from the newspaper Futurismo, designed by Enrico Prampolini in 1933.

Much appreciated, Natalina

Edited: Looks like a version of Hermann Hoffmann’s Block of 1908 to 1936 for Berthold. Digital versions include Berthod’s Block https://www.myfonts.com/fonts/berthold/block-pro/ and Bitstream [condensed only] as Gothic 821 https://www.myfonts.com/fonts/bitstream/gothic-821/ Also a Paratype version as Bloc.
