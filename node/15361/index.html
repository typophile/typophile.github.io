But then I saw an "f" that needs a reverse nose job of elephantine proportions: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/meridien/roman/charmap.html Meridien is possibly Matthew Carter's most favorite font.* I really really like it too, from the moment I saw it. It has a regal but reserved "je ne sais quoi" that's as easy to admire as it is difficult to really wrap your head around. It was originally a metal font, but not a Monotype, plus the italic "f" is pretty good, so I really have to wonder what happened with that "f"... Unless we can think of some reason that the original form is ideally valid, I say let's try to make a better one. * See Robert Norton's unique "Types Best Remembered / Types Best Forgotten". hhp

Hrant, according to the info at MyFonts, Apollo is the Monotype version of Meridien. Meridien I assume was done first for linotype machines, which is the reason for the squashed f (and j). Both the f and j are 'fixed' in Apollo, as you can see here. Whether you like it or not is another matter, but going in that direction was my first thought when I looked at it. So to me, Apollo fixes the problems you (rightly I think) see in Meridien.

I saw that, but they're really not very close - at least not to us type geeks. :-) And one appeal of this type of effort to me is the -admittedly small- possibility of practical improvement, in terms of maybe providing some typographers with an alternate glyph (without treading on the original's rights); and Meridien is much more commonly available than Apollo.
