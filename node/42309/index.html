I don't believe "red" has to be in red, especially since it will visually break the word into two words red & thm (or sort of words). The box around the logotype bothers me on the smaller scale versions (paired with the drum head skull and the veggies) the negative space above and to the right of the "m" is distracting. maybe it needs to be optically centered and not actually centered. The drum head skull guy is cute and I like him. But is cute the look that best suits your friend's work? The style looks like "Hello Kitty" goes goth/urban. Admittedly I'm old and this may be stereotypical, but usually when people are pairing a skull with music they're going for an edgier look. Not to say that's what you should do - certainly going along with the trend just for trends sake is not what I'm suggesting. I'm just asking is this style appropriate for your friend? And I'm afraid I just don't get the stippled drawing of the beats. The drawing goes so against the other style you were creating. If I saw that alone I would think it is for an organic farmer and instead of being called green thumb the company is redthm (red thumb - that their hands are stained by manually picking their beats). That drawing, especially on the brown has a '70's folk music sensibility. And the "I heart beats" shirt makes me think you love the English Beat. Why wasn't it "I heart redthm"? The different styles are all over the place, are you trying to show your friend's eclectic style? If that's the case I still think what you have so far doesn't accomplish your goal. Don't try to blow your friend's mind. Try to nail who he is, and what he does, in your logo. Accomplish that and you will blow his mind.
