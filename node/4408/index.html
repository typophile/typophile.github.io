This may be old news to many, but I finally got around to reading the article in the current issue of Print on the development of Clearview. Clearview first came to my attention in the TDC newsletter a couple years back. It was quite a while before I learned that James had originally developed it as a highway signage type. Last fall, when I was driving through Pennsylvania, I got a kick out of seeing some of the more recent signs set in Clearview. Of course, I had to point them all out to my wife, who was not nearly as amused. Most of the article's subject matter was covered in James's highly entertaining presentation at last year's TypeCon. (Note to Tamye: This year, don't schedule James first thing Sunday morning – he deserves a primetime slot.) But it was fascinating to hear again about the long, molasses-swimming process of designing a project like this. For a general article in a design magazine, I thought it did a decent job of making this somewhat unglamorous aspect of the craft interesting for the average reader. And I commend the author for tackling the topic. It was a refreshing change from the glorified portfolio fluff that is so common. – K.
