This is a William Arthur invitation font. I believe it has been made into a font, and would love to purchase it. Thank you for any suggestions.[img:sites/default/files/old-images/T184_1_3612.gif]

I have not been able to locate that in my Script Font ID Guide, or other type catalogs. The sample is very small, but the initial letters seem distinctive, and the only G like that I found was in one of the Poetica Swash sets, I think. However, as varied as Poetica is, I don’t think it matches this script of yours.

I have found that many stationers, like William Arthur, use either pre-digital typefaces, or else custom typefaces, as a way to maintain an exclusive look that cannot readily be copied. I may be wrong, but I don’t think this is a commercial font.

In cases like this, my advice is to find something similar, instead of trying to duplicate it. The font that reminds me the most of your sample stylistically is Font Bureau Avalon.
