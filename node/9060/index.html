2005-02-05 13:49:24 • dezcom
Oh yeah! Jeez, how could I forget to mention Joe Treacy's TF Greyletter, after going to so much effort to figure out what it was? If Wexford Oakley is a duoline, then TF Greyletter is a monoline. It's available in ten weights, and is my favorite recent take on blackletters.

http://www.treacyfaces.com/oneliner09.html

If you prefer a fraktur to a textura, there's also Ingrimayne's PhrackSle, also a beautiful monoline blackletter.

http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/ingrimayne/phracksle/

There's also Linotype's Auferstehung, which is still sort of monoline, albeit with a much, much chubbier stroke. It feels more like a traditional blackletter, but for whatever reason I don't like it as much.

http://www.linotype.com/968/linotypeauferstehung-family.html

I like (and own) Gothic Gothic, but it's a very wide typeface, and would be challenging to set into the banner space you have available (making it curve gracefully would be a challenge all its own). Of the more traditional blackletter faces, Brauhaus and American Text are probably your best bets for giving a more "modern" feel, although all-caps American Text is probably only good if Chrome Hearts's goal is to market bling to the urban hood set (not that I have a problem with that). If CH's market is primarily Japanese (as some Googling seems to indicate), then the wilder and less legible the logotype is, the more the kids in Harajuku will get into it.

This looks like a fun project!
