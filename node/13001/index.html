2005-06-10 15:46:28 • William Berkson

Interesting project in stained glass.

If it’s a collectible, then I would focus on target market’s preconceived imagery of NYC.

I humbly suggest looking at retro scripts and display designs associated with “The Great White Way” (no, this is not a racial comment but a reference to the glitzy theatre district of NYC).

For scripts, look at Kaufmann, Murray Hill and Park Avenue. For marquee dispay designs look at Binner, Broadway and Rialto.

All of these are not “too over the top” and can be rendered on stained glass without much difficulty.

Hope this helps.

2005-06-10 18:42:11 • Will Miller

On occasion you might look for a little inspiration from Linotype web site’s “Fonts in Use” section.

It’s divided into several sub-categories, alphabetically by typeface, by usage, etc.

Oddly enough, you’ll find several instances of Broadway used for exterior signage in NYC, and one for a retro Art Deco site.

http://www.linotype.com/7-2106-7/byalphabet.html http://www.linotype.com/7-1508-7/posterssignpostingcalendars.html

Your only guideline should be what is best for the solution of the project and not what your personal taste or ego tells you.
