Drawing a river in InDesign

April 7, 2009 • bendy

Dear Typophiles,

I realise this is more cartography than typography but thought someone might be able to help. I have to redraw a map using InDesign (or Photoshop I suppose). It exists only as a flipchart with marker pen drawing.

The map contains type and normal map icons such as hills and houses. There is a river on the map. I want to have the river growing in width as it nears the sea. I don’t think a stroke can change width along its length so is there another way I can draw the line with a uniformly expanding width?

Thanks in advance :)

Draw the map in illustrator. Draw the river as a stroke, outline it, draw the river growing as a polygon and compound them. Or leave them as to objects…

What Allimac said. Usually what I do (I love making maps) is draw the rough basis as a stroke, then duplicate the layer just to make sure, outline the stroke and manually edit the outlines (because depending on map style, a river especially might actually look better when it’s not “mathematically correctly” growing in width). But yeah, definitely do it in Illustrator. Hell, you could even do it in FontLab. ;-)
