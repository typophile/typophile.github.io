Every style by every lettering artist had/has its own idiosyncrasies, and very few of these myriad personal styles were/have been turned into fonts.

And the lettering artists would also modify their styles all the time, they weren’t robots.

So it’s probably better to narrow down your selection to a few fonts that are close in feeling, and choose the one that best fits the particular project you are working on, considering, for instance, the issues of x-height and line-breaks as they relate to the copy you have to work with in the document format.

But if you are really in love with this particular lettering, why not digitize it? The “Speedball” style is quite easy to render—draw a path and stroke it.
