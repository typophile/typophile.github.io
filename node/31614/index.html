2007-02-20 21:03:58 • oldnick
Mike - I'm pretty sure that it is, as you first said, Roco. Most of Grock's letterforms are considerably narrower. Just compare the two S's in the Solotype Catlaog. I should say that it is mostly Roco. Casady & Greene always tweaked their "versions" of existing fonts a bit. I'm not sure if Roco had that A (or Jeff Levine's) as an alternate (Homage to the Alphabet doesn't show an alt) or if it was purely a C&G modification. There's also Fontbank's Lullaby, but it lacks Roco's original 5 as used in the poster's image. Right Bank is the only font I'm aware of that fully matches up.
2007-02-21 00:05:42 • Mike F
My notes on Lullaby show Right Bank as the equivalent, but I think you're right that Roco was the original. Levine's 'Regal Suite' mirrors Roco, including both A's shown in the Dover book (but not 'Homage'), and the big 5. It seems the A with sloped sides is the C&G 'innovation' ('tweak', as you said). With all these versions floating around it was no wonder it looked familiar right away. - Mike Yanega
