What's your match?

July 13, 2008 • Alvin Martinez

As designers and typographers, we all encounter moments where we need to make a decision on the mixing and matching of typefaces. And as purveyors of the letterform, we also know that it is not just a matter of throwing two typefaces together. While it is not a perfect science, establishing a good, functional match between two different typefaces is akin to striking design gold. It is an acquired skill to master as there are steadfast rules and tips to follow, but at the same time these rules need not hold back your creative sense of adventure. The idea behind this thread was to start a dialogue where any us can discuss, inspire and display their favorites "mixes and matches" of typefaces. Pairs that have been your tried and true solutions in a pinch, or ones that are on the edge of reason yet still seems to ignite an excitement of our creative senses. The most conservative or the most experimental matches are welcome I'd would like to begin with a pair of typefaces that I recently felt has become a very tight and strong pairing after weeks of research and experimentation. Berthold Akzidenz Grotesk with Hoefler text. A marriage of a mid-century classic to a modern take (and homage) to the typestyles of the Transitional Period.
