Font for dual language text

August 26, 2010 • Oilybird

I am making a design proposal for a contemporary art institution, and need to mock-up a range of posters, leaflets, catalogues etc.

I have settled on Museo Slab for headings. However, I would be grateful for recommendations for a font for the body text. The font needs to come in two versions (EG slab & sans; or serif & sans), as I need to set body text in parallel languages (Danish and English), and I would like to differentiate the languages with different variants from the same font family.

The body text font should obviously work with Museo, and should have an up-to-date feel. The two variants should have a similar colour on the page when side by side, and ideally they should be good for immersive reading too. My current favourite is Freight, using serif and sans versions… But I am also thinking that a font with slab and sans variants might work better with Museo.

Any thoughts?

FF Scala, as I understand it, is sort of a classic for this type of application; also check out FF Nexus, also by Martin Majoor (which incidentally is on sale right now). //Edit: This previous thread http://typophile.com/node/66389 also mentions FF Quadraat and FF Seria (which might be problematic wrt the Italic). And Rotis, of course. I can’t comment much on the Museo match, except this: You kind of have it backwards; especially when your text setting is sort of complex, you should let that lead the design; i.e. settle on the text font[s] before the headline fonts. Because there aren’t going to be thousands of families that work for your setting, and you’re additionally narrowing the field by first defining a headline face they need to go with. & consider also that for example if you end up settling on a family with 3 variants, such as FF Nexus, you might want to use its own slab (for example) for the headings, and maybe the other 2 variants for the text, for a super match.
