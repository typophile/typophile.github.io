Four typefaces on Tarkovsky soundtrack CD covers

March 28, 2008 • An Accident

Hello I’m trying to find the four typefaces used on this series of Tarkovsky soundtrack CDs released by Toei Music Publishing (Japan) in the mid-1990s.

• The italic used for the film titles – “Zerkalo” and “Stalker”. • The bold condensed grotesk used for “Andrey Tarkovsky” (possibly Akzidenz Bold Condensed?) • The glittery number “4” • The typewriter font used for the glittery Cyrillic version of “Tarkovsky”

I have a couple more images from other CDs in this series if anybody needs extra letters or numbers to work with, but they are a bit more blurry than this one.

I know it’s a lot to ask, so thanks in advance.

Andrew

To start, the 4 is Bodoni, or some clone…

Zerkalo-Stalker looks like a faux-italic (ultra-slanty), squooshed garamond (hook at the end of the leg of lowercase k)

It’s not AG bold condensed (descender on y is too stubby, arm on k doesn’t look right). Perhaps Helvetica Black Condensed? http://www.linotype.com/12573/helveticablackcondensed-font.html
