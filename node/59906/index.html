2009-07-13 16:31:02 • eliason

Maybe a food-service professional would recognize what the picture is, but to me it’s too abstract–just a corner with the four little round things. The color scheme and the typography say clean and high-tech, but they don’t say food. I get kind of a biotech or pharmaceutical vibe from this, although that’s partly because the name Univerzal sounds like a prescription drug. I realize they won’t let you change the name of the company.

Here’s the logo of one of the biggest food-service outfits in the US, Aramark (possibly somebody Univerzal would like as a client). They’ve been using this logo for 15 or 20 years, so it’s not exactly on the cutting edge, but it manages to look modern and techie without looking cold and antiseptic.
