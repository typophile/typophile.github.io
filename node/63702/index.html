I only know a good German source for printer tests: http://www.druckerchannel.de/ranking.php?tabelle=laser_farbe

They take pictures from the printed letter ‘a’ with the help of a microscope: http://www.druckerchannel.de/cache_bilder/std/und_beim_normaldruck_keine_veraenderung_im_treiber_ldafe0.jpg

It may sound theoretically, but there are big differences with regard to the precision and thickness, in which laser printers print letters. HP seems to print too thin, at least the monochrome printers.

You also should care about duplex precision and the document authenticity of the toner. For example it can happen, that the toner gets off, if you fold the paper, although it is resistant against finger nails and wetness.

I have heard, that the toner, that Lexmark uses, is document authentic. Samsung is not. But Lexmark tends to print thicker than Samsung, at least, if you compare the monochrome printers.

2009-11-09 01:34:53 • TF22Raptor

I bought a Xerox Phaser 6130 a year ago and the quality is very good. As I recall, it worked ‘out of the box’. The setup switch, which I found to be fiddly, is similar to the multi-position switches on mobile phones and cameras, so if you’re familiar with those, you’ll have no problem. I’ve not had to replace one of the 4 cartridges, but I’m a light (home) user.

As to whether it’s the ‘best’ or not, I can’t say, but it does reproduce text, drawn vector shapes and solid fills extremely well and photographs look pretty good. The problem is that one person’s ‘excellent’, is another person’s ‘average’. I’ve used standard laser/copier paper and photographic. The output is very good on both, but it’s noticeably better on the photographic paper. I’ve had a couple of inkjets and in every respect the Xerox is much better. It was reasonably priced, too.
