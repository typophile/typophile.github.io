New Release: Skolar Devanagari

June 25, 2012 • Rosetta Type Foundry

Skolar Devanagari - A typeface for complex typography

[[http://www.rosettatype.com/Skolar%20Devanagari|Skolar Devanagari]], the latest addition to the [[http://www.rosettatype.com/Skolar|Skolar]] family, is a collaboratiion of David Březina and Vaibhav Singh. It has been designed as a companion to the Latin keeping the nuances and characteristics of the Devanagari script foremost while providing a complementary design for multi-script typography. Primarily intended as a robust, energetic text typeface, [[http://www.rosettatype.com/Skolar%20Devanagari|Skolar Devanagari]] addresses the needs of serious typography. At the same time it furnishes the designer with the hallmark versatility of the family in display sizes.

The fonts support Sanskrit, Hindi, Marathi, Nepali, along with a wide range of regional languages that use the Devanagari script (Awadhi, Bhili, Bhojpuri, Garhwali, Gujari, Konkani, Kumaoni, Magahi, Maithili, Marwari, Newari, …).

The complementary designs (versions: Latin Pro, Cyrillic, Greek, Pan-European, including italics) can be found on the [[http://www.rosettatype.com/Skolar|Skolar page]].
