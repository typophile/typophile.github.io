Has your G5 died recently?

October 4, 2009 • Nick Cooke

Sadly, after nearly 5 years loyal service, mine passed away last weekend.

I took it to the not so local repair shop and the technician said that they were getting more and more G5’s with failing cpu’s. The repair would have been £700!. So I thought it wasn’t worth repairing as it would be obsolete very soon (as it doesn’t support Snow Leopard), and it’s over a third of the cost of a new Mac Pro, (which I duly coughed up for).

Now I’m not one who goes in for conspiracy theories of built-in death chips, but it does make me wonder…

Recently my intelG5 developed a Kernel Panic, and would not start up. None of the normal resuscitation techniques were working.

AppleCare sent a technician to the studio. They checked the RAM, and Hard Drives, and replaced the Motherboard. No luck. A second technician came a day later and replaced the video card and everything was hunky-dorey. This second tech told me that he had seen a rash of video card failures that were causing these Kernel Panics.

One strange interesting thing: I had everything from the G5 backed up on an external hard drive via Time Machine, and reinstalled from that to the new MacPro. All settings and applications as before. Great, I thought.

In the few days between the G5’s demise and getting the new MacPro I was working on my laptop on two FontLab files. With the MacPro up and running I copied the FL files from the laptop via AirPort. I then worked on these 2 files all yesterday, saved and quit. When I opened them this morning they were unchanged from the modified date on the laptop - nothing had been saved, all yesterday’s work gone. I then modified them a bit then quit and reopened them - nothing saved.

So I went to the laptop and copied them onto a Flash Drive and replaced them on the MacPro. I then modified them a bit then quit and reopened them - the modifications were saved this time. Modifications to other files copied from Time Machine were also fine - just not files copied via AirPort. Weird.
