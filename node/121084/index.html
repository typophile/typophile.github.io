Helvetica was released in 1957, two years before the book’s publication. From the samples in Jaspert’s Encyclopaedia of Typefaces [1970, p. 294] I don’t spot conflicts with Mergenthaler Linotype Helvetica Extra Compressed for man state war and Mergenthaler Linotype Helvetica Ultra Compressed for the rest. However a larger scale sample may show differences. Also this was the photo-lettering era so all bets are off as to whether this is manipulated lettering. Don
