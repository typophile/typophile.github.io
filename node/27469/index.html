If you move it yourslef, a pickup should do. Whether you move it yourself or have with a freight company it is best to go to where the press is stroed and take it apart. An iron handpresses whatever type is fragile. The cast iron is old and brittle and easily broken. Any of these type of presses in their assempled state are very vulnerable since the boss and bed are heavy and the legs very delicate. I have moved one press twice. On the second move the press was the property of a buyer and they didn't break it down. The movers smashed it up badly before it even left my driveway. I would place it on two pallets: one for the heavy bed and boss and another for the legs, ways etc. It is bests to carry the miscellaneous nuts and bolts away with you in a separate container. If any of these things are lost it is nearly impossible to replace them since the threads in most cases are of an odd count and specific to the maker. I hope this doesn't put you off, but it's best to be aware of the pitfalls. Best of good luck in your move. Jim [img:sites/default/files/old-images/]
