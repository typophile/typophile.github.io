Trading Services?

June 8, 2007 • TGaskin

I was thinking about designers trading services for other services or products, and was wondering what other designers have traded for…rent, computers, etc. I’m particularly interested in humorous trades.
I once designed a store sign for a hair salon, so my wife could get free haircuts for a year. That’s the oddest thing I’ve traded for. To be honest she sort of volunteered me for it, but since womens haircuts can be quite pricey I wasn’t complaining too much.

2007-06-08 19:08:51 • fontplayer
My experience is that you can almost never get the fair market value for design through trade. Always the person feels like their physical product is more valuable than the design work. Particularly problematic are situations where the trade is paid back slowly over time (never trade with a restaurant, they’ll get sick of you, and you’ll get sick of the food). Almost always the trader forgets the urgency that they had when they initially needed the service. At any rate good trading requires good accounting of actual values and “payments”.
