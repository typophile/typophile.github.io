2006-11-08 17:03:13 • missgiggles

Here in Washington it’s customary to have a plain resume and customize the design/text appropriate to the employer. A really wild design that might get you a job at a three-person design firm could end up right in the trash if it goes to the HR department of a big-firm looking for in-house people. Having something simple can also make it easier for you to use it for digital submission.

Consider producing a really good-looking promotional piece that showcases your work and include a well-typeset traditional resume along with it. This way you get to show off your creative ability without having to worry about offending people who don’t like creative resumes.

I would be very careful with the idea of trying to “add a little extra item thast creative and that they can have in their office.” You would have to do something very, very clever to be able to pull this off. Otherwise it will be another really lame attempt. I got an ‘interview’ once for a senior position (I was a junior) only because I had used Quadraat on my well-typeset CV. I didn’t get the job, but I got to spend 1 1/2 hours with a senior designer, talking about all sorts of things. Now that design firm is a client of mine for type-specific work. Funny how these things work out.
