I am a fourth year student of graphic design trying to design her degree show portfolio. I have a lovely bulky A2 portfolio case full of work but the layout just isn’t doing my work justice. Does anyone have any tips on how the A2 sheets should be best laid out? I will be designing and binding my own book of work at a later date but the A2 sheets are a requirement for asessment. What should the type image ratio be? How many pages to each project? This isn’t a plea because I’m too damn lazy to do it myself, its just this is a very very important show for me and panic seems to be clouding my judgement right now. sara

hmmm…I usually do an A3 portfolio because it is more managabe but as you say, it is required to have A2 then erm…yup but make sure you have a variety of layouts. I tend to do mine double page spreads or if needed, 2 double page spreads per project. Why not use Indesign to match them up and have them inline with each other. Also, make sure everything is lined up well, no spelling mistakes and that you have well designed pages. About ratio, well it depends. I had a full bleed photograph once on one side and then just a vertical strip of mini photographs on the other side to accentuate my main, best photograph for my photography project. White space is good! So I think you need to try different layouts. Be creative. You are a graphic designer by the way! ahem ahem :D
