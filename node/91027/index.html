March 13, 2012 • aaronbell

(This is a break away post from [[Please Read: Typophile (TEMP)]] because this discussion deserves to live outside of the comments on that thread)

To start, let me say that I am one of those individuals that has been strongly turned away from Typophile due to the overly harsh, pointless personal attacks and lack of respect for fellow human beings. But I have been following this discussion and the one on Type Board, hoping to find a return to civility that has been so sorely lacking. Well, I haven’t been terribly impressed by either. One bans those seen as overly hostile and comes off as isolationist while the other is overrun by negativity, driving away those who truly want to contribute and learn. Truly unfortunate.

Quoting Hrant from [[http://typophile.com/node/91007#comment-499372|here]]

But I don't think being gifted and being rude are mutually exclusive. To me anybody -talent or no- who can handle aspects of an environment that are unpleasant to him will derive more benefit from it, and will allow others to learn more from him as well.

What doesn’t kill you makes you stronger, eh?

Look, the internet is a marvelous place that allows for communication across vast distances and time. Awesome. In the early days, people realised that the anonyminity that the internet provided allowed us to say whatever we wanted and do whatever we wanted without fear of reprival. But the Type community is not large, is it? We know each other. I will likely talk to the lot of you at some conference or another in the next years. Since we are actual people who actually know each other, why should be insulting or antagonistic? Why can’t we be civil? Surely there is enough humanity in us to rise above the common denominator of internet commentators.

The really sad thing is that I’ve met a lot of you in person and you all seem like reasonable, friendly individuals. So why all the venom online? A number of folk I’ve talken to say, “Oh, but my internet persona is not who I am, it is separate”. To that I say bullshit. As the saying goes, the real measure of a man (or woman!) is how they treat someone who can provide them no benefit. So what can be said of someone who intentionally attacks others for no good reason (either instigator or responder), regardless of the medium? And even if someone else attacks you first, who says you need to lower yourself to their level and respond in kind, perpetuating the cycle of hate? Who you are online is who you are. Do not deny it.

There has been a lot of good to come out of Typophile, and a lot more good can be gained through having a centralised location for discourse and communication. I wouldn’t want to lose the extensive history documented here. That said, unless we all work to raise the quality of life around here, the community will continue to bleed and find other places to congregate. Once that voice is split, it will be significantly more difficult to regain it.

So I call on you all to check yourselves—don’t demand that the moderators control people. Show that you care and control yourselves if you don’t want to see this community die.

No, you can’t solve a problem by censoring it, but you can chose to respond and act with civility.

Thanks Aaron. It’s easy to point fingers and demand moderation, blocking and banning - and a lot harder to simply hold oneself to a higher standard. There are only a few people behind Typophile trying to make things happen, and the community easily outnumbers us 50,000 to 1. Any community will have strong personalities and differences of opinion. We need to pull together.

Here’s a tip from someone who’s watched this community over its lifetime:

I find some of the characters here have a bedside manner a little like Dr. House. But here’s the advice: If Dr. House is a genius then you put up with his drama, attitude and insults. But, if Dr. House’s work is not all that, then you can dismiss his advice and sidestep the drama.

So, go find the Dr. House that’s irritating you. Look at his URLs, his work, his fonts. Does the work inspire you? Does it look like he must know what he’s doing? Then pay attention and look past the bedside manner. But, if the work doesn’t pass muster you’re safe to ignore the whole package: ignore the advice and the work and the headache.

The one thing you can’t do is act like Dr. House to get back at Dr. House. You will lose and it will drive you insane because Dr. House will win. If you find you’re able to engage with Dr. House and come up with some awesome retorts: News Flash! You just might be a Dr. House yourself.
