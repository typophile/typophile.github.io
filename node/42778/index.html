2008-03-04 03:23:08 • Michel Boyer
If you get a Mac, you will need font management software to supplement the built-in Font Book...FontAgentPro is the best. FontAgentPro supports all major type formats, including PC TrueType, for use in your new Mac. So there should be no problem importing your PC fonts. Whether they will look as good as your new Mac PS fonts is another question. Also, get FontDoctor...it's the finest, most rigorous diagnostic test-and-repair software for checking and fixing your fonts, whatever their format.

The problem lies in PC-PostScript-fonts. These can’t be used on a Mac straightaway. You need to convert them with a tool like TransType. If your collection contains PS-fonts you bought, you could enquire at the vendors whether they’d be willing to crossgrade those (for a small fee, hopefully). BTW Using FontAgentPro to manage fonts is a good idea. There's also FontExplorerX, which is free and a viable solution too. (Don’t go the Extensis way — Suitcase has been a buggy product forever.) . . . Bert Vanderveen BNO

Warning: I found that FontAgentPro and FontExplorer have bad software conflicts in Mac OSX, so it's better not to put them together. FontBook, already installed in your Mac system, will display font sample alphabets adequately, though no better than that, but at least it operates seamlessly with FontAgentPro (which also displays sample alphabets, but not ideally). Not sure about support for PC Postscript...the FontAgentPro help section says they support all type one postscript, which would seem to include that. I'm sure I have some PC Postscript faces in there, supported now, but I can't swear to it. So make some inquiries, or do the conversion Bert suggests. By the way, if you get some Storm Foundry type families, you will have to install them in Fontbook rather than in FontAgentPro. Storm is the only foundry I know with that problem: only via FontBook do you get all fonts represented and linked properly. On the plus side, their text families are nicely priced and all fonts in the family are keystroke-linked, which is rare among boutique foundries. Regent II, Tusar, Jannon, and the best Walbaum on the market: each bundle is less than $80, instantly downloadable from myfonts.
