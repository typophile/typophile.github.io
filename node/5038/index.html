Would any of you be interested in the creation of a special interest discussion forum here pertaining to copyright protection and related issues?

I think that, given the lack of protection in the U.S. (as compared to the rest of Western Civilization), it's an important issue. I've also noticed a complexity (where legal protection issues are concerned) in type design that seems to be unequalled in other fields*–personally, I think it's up to us to clarify some issues that may be confusing to the powers that be… and I certainly think we're capable.

We've got a real opportunity as a community, and of course I'd try to get the TypeRight people onboard in some fashion too.

Let me know what all of you think.

*People have no difficulty understanding that if someone writes a novel, it's totally their intellectual property; they get confused, however, not only by the act of someone defining their interpretation of the alphabet, but the finer details of type design as well (metrics, etc.).
