Most authentic Palatino digitization?

April 17, 2015 • Bookalope

This question is a bit of a continuation of [[http://typophile.com/node/117317|this thread]]. In short, I am looking for a digitization of the Palatino typeface that is close to the original design.

Looking at [[http://luc.devroye.org/palatino2.html|this]] article, “The original Palatino,” I do like its liveliness and warmth. It seems to have been captured best by [[http://www.bertholdtypes.com/|Berthold]]’s digitization (see [[http://www.sanskritweb.net/fontdocs/pala92.pdf|this PDF]] which has the font somewhat incomplete embedded) but that digitization is no longer available. There is the [[http://www.linotype.com/57056/palatinolinotype-family.html|Linotype]] version, and the [[http://www.gust.org.pl/projects/e-foundry/tex-gyre/pagella|TeX Gyre Pagella]] version, and [[https://www.myfonts.com/fonts/urw/palladio/|URW Palladio]]. Which else? And which is closest to Zapf’s original?

Probably Zapf’s reworked Palatino Nova.
