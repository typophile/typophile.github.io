Hi everyone,

I’ve just “felt” on this old logotype that I created for me [ Dr Duckling | Design care ] when I started as a freelancer. I happen to really like it and want it to be a part of my visual identity again. So I thought I should make some improvements on it since I think that the old version really needed a “ravalement de façade”… So the logo on top is the old one the new version is the one below. Can you guys tell me what you think? Thanks.

Ok, maybe I should let you know about the symbolic work behind this logotype :

  • visually evoke the 3 Ds in “Dr Duckling | Design…”
  • visually evoke the C of “…Care”
  • the C also shapes a moon “croissant”, which I find very symbolic for women, which I am…
  • using a bird’s eye because a duckling is a bird (still)
  • using an eye to evoke (æsthetic) vision as it seems to be appropriate and reflects a designer’s maybe most important quality
  • the whole symbol is definitely inspired my native americans ones (as you may have noticed already), and this particular one was meant to protect (care…) and also to suggest an anticipative vision (watch, obsverse, be aware, anticipate) which is also very much part of a designer’s job to me.

Ok I really don’t think what I said is clear here, one more time sorry for my poor english…

2006-09-21 00:17:23 • Linda Cunningham

Tim: That’s a beautiful print! I’m a big fan of that kinda stuff. However, I must point out that it is not Inuit. It is Kwakiutl. The Kwakiutl are one of the tribes that live along the west coast of Canada. They are master woodcarvers, best known for their totem poles, cedar longhouses and ceremonial masks. But their artwork also encompasses stone sculpture, jewellery, painting and printmaking. Your kingfisher is a truly wonderful example of the graphic Kwakiutl style. Black and red are the traditional colours. Sometimes a dark green or blue is added. How long have you had the print?

Cheers, Ken
