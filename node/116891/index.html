Dick, the problem I see with identifying a Victorian sans is that they got little publicity and were not featured by the foundries. They were used relatively rarely. I think the sample is more likely to have been called a Gothic than a Grotesque, if only because of the leg on the R. Many foundries sold these by number and not by a specific name. If I had to guess a name for it, I would call it simply a Gothic Extended. Don
