Some general tips.

  • No globe in its current form: it conveys the message, but it’s way too clip-art-looking.
  • For a simple design like this, two typefaces (or even a single typeface in two weights) are more than enough. But you may want to differentiate the logo from the rest.
  • For a friendlier and more “bookish” feel, you could also try a serif typeface.

One positive: your home page is not too busy. It’s clear what you do and how to use the site. That’s a great start.

On version 1: Nix the arrow graphic and use a real word (English to Portuguese) or punctuation (English / Portuguese). If you really want the arrow, use a typographic arrow, preferably one from the same font as the surrounding text. But any arrow will work if it more or less matches the text.

On version 2: You should definitely still say what languages you translate somewhere prominent.

“File(s)” is unnecessarily fussy. Just say file.

Why “Translations” and not “Translation” in the header?

“Services” is more prominent than it deserves.

Definitely try a serifed font. And try muting the colors. Pick only two colors and see what happens. Your design feels very commercial; you might want to try for something more literary.
