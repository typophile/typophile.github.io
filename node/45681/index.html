Are they really experimental fonts or are they creative lettering done with contemporary tools? There seems to be a trend toward referring to these sort of experiments as experimental typefaces or experimental fonts, especially in academia, and I wonder how much this: A) leads people to assume that these sort of letters should be best left to experimental work and B) reflects a disdain for hand-lettered work in some circles.

That said, these are great. The smoke letters have a fabulous organic feel, and I can imagine this whole thing worked up as a 3D software plugin that could also do similar plant and hair effects. Swade really shows how much you care about your work; you elevated a contemporary design cliche to something exceptional. I don’t know what to make of curly—it seems to have been designed for a purpose not revealed as a simple specimen. And the graffiti letters are both authentic looking and (relatively) legible—that isn’t easy to do!
