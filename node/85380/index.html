Fábio, http://26plus-zeichen.de/fonts/edelsans/ My (admittedly unimaginative) typical formula is: * in print, use the sans of a pairing for titles and subheads, and use the serif for body text. * for electronic media, use the serif of a pairing for titles and subheads, and use the sans for body text. This particular customer won't be writing a book, so print use will be limited to posters, postcards, brochures, mailers, and other small-scale publications (forms, invoices, etc). And there will be a website, so I usually like to find a sans I think might work for website body-text, and a serif that (usually in its Bold weight, but not always) stands up to being embedded with @font-face.
