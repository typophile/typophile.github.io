Font for divergent group of floral designers

February 8, 2008 • Fitzg

I am looking for font recommendations from this intriguing group of font mavens. Project: A 160-page manual/workbook on judging floral design. Purpose of book: To provide a fresh look at a stale and entrenched judging process. Primary audiences are two divergent groups: • Perhaps 40% of the audience is sophisticated, trendy and highly creative floral designers. • Perhaps 30% of the audience, though very creative, is fairly regimented and regards the current judging process as sacrosanct. As conservative as they are, they are always diligent and anxious to expand and grow, though change comes painfully slowly, and only after much deliberation. Additional uses of book: • Manual for training new judges • Part of curriculum for students of floral design, primarily at the college level. Style of the book: • Most likely it will be a clean, well-designed contemporary look, signaling a fresh approach to the serious topic of judging. • Possibly a traditional look. My taste: It generally runs from such classics as Centaur, Bembo, Goudy Old Style (or Fabiol), Scala, Vendetta Light, Requiem, PMN Caecilia, to Cholla Slab, Gotham and Quartet to…well, what are your thoughts? What I’m looking for: Suggestions for serif and sans serif fonts that complement each other. I would be appreciative of fonts for both a contemporary and traditional version of the book, and for possibly a script font. Open Type suggestions are particularly desirable. Other suggestions or caveats? Thanks for giving your time and expertise to my project.
