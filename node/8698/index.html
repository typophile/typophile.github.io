Significant collections are available to those who can travel and can persuade the curators that they have a reason better than curiosity; keep in mind that most museums have an obligation to preserve, and it doesn't get much flimsier than working sketches and inked sheets. Some designers have had retrospectives issued in their name, or major exhibitions that, thankfully, were accompanied by substantial catalogues – these are pretty easy to locate. However, a major work has been announced for publication in a couple of months that has not appeared on any pre-publication reviews yet, so I will shamelessly take the opportunity to plug Printer's Type in the Twentieth Century. It covers its subject through well-illustrated case-studies that should prove manna to any hungry brain.
