Does anyone know of a good site/book/etc with research into what colours sell in specific reference to magazine covers?

I’ve always been told green is a poor selling colour and dark covers generally perform worse than light. Can anyone offer up anything more concrete??

Thanks

You may want to check out this month’s GD (Graphic Design) USA/May 2006 which is their Annual Color Forecast issue.

Here are just a few trends/forecasts by color marketers:

  • Palettes that reflect substance, an anchor
  • Multiple monochromatic colors used together
  • More emothional response to color; uplifiting, not shocking
  • Rejection of bold patterns
  • Texture as more important than patterns
  • Design that is moving away from perfection toward the need for human touch
  • Artisan-inspired design and true color, as opposed to digital color.

I would think the color palette for a magazine would depend chiefly on the target market and the main focus of the publication.
