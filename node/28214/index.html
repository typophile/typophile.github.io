Now I'm an engineer

September 13, 2006 • Nick Shinn

I don't enter competitions, but I do look at the calls for entries, because it's always interesting to see how they break things down. In the Design Exchange Awards 2006 typeface design would best fit under "Engineering". Of course, "Visual Communication" would seem to be the obvious choice, but they break it down into two halves, "Brand" (aaaagh!) and "Content". I s'pose that a commissioned typeface would be considered part of the client's brand, and a retail typeface part of the foundry's brand, but as it says in most EULAs, "fonts are software", and gosh, I even feel like a bit of a software engineer these days as I'm doing all my clever OpenType feature coding. Besides, I don't really have a foundry brand that is supported by the types I design; like most foundries, mine does an eclectic mix of faces. But there are quite a few foundries where the foundry brand and its types reinforce brand in more than a general sense. Virus, with Jon Barnbrook's taste for jagged ornament. H&FJ's traditionalism. TypeCo. And the ultimate synergy -- Font Diner!

I'm almost never able to find an appropriate match in lists of professions or categories for what I produce. Even when a company like Adobe invites me to register as a beta tester, I scan the occupation list in vain looking for 'type designer'. When given the choice between something like 'graphic designer' or 'software developer', I always select 'software developer'. Recently, I joined the Slow Food organisation, and they have a really delightful and very Italian list of occupations from which to choose. I particularly like the mixture of the generic and the precise, and the near synonyms suggesting that what matters is how you think of yourself: Artist Artisan Celebrity / Luminary Civil Servant Cook / Chef Employee Entrepreneur Farmer Fisherman Freelancer Housewife Innkeeper / Hotelier Journalist Lecturer / Teacher Manager / Director Merchant Pensioner Representative Student Undefined Various Winegrower / Winemaker Worker
