I’m sure you’ve already used these…but they are pretty sharp and clean, you’ve got to watch the paper your using though. You want some super shiny stuff, and very little pressure. [[http://www.cultpens.com/acatalog/edding_1255_Calligraphy_Pen.html]]

I’ve never managed to get a swelling nib (spencerian style) effect through a marker pen, you can cheat, draw a skeleton and then swell out at the right places with a fatter pen. It worked pretty good in my graffiti days. A southpaw nib will definitely help, or my oblique holder can reverse? i presume that has the same effect with non angled nibs. Also, reverse the angle you approach the paper at (as opposed to the way they show you for orthodox).

This is probably the only useful thing I’ll be able to share about calligraphic tools here, but we used the Parallel Pen too in the calligraphy classes I took, and one thing to know about it is that it uses a lot of ink, and the cartridges are rather expensive. You can just keep buying those all the time, or you can use a syringe to refill the cartridge you have from a larger ink bottle, which will yield almost the same quality and will save you lots of $ over the long run. (In my case the run wasn’t all that long but still. :-)
