Humanist, open counter, sans serif?

December 19, 2008 • Dot

Hello, I'm working on a series of book covers dealing with ecological, sociological and anti-globalist topics. Titles are as diverse as Seeds of Deception (GMO story) to delicate themes like Escape from Intimacy(sex adiction)to quite positive ones like Nudge (something like you can achieve big changes in life with small help etc.). Target are mostly women but I don't want to use "feminine" looking font in titles like Death of Nature of course. ;) I need sans-serif, humanist font and I feel like those with open counters work best. I see title in bigger size(upper case letters)because I want it to serve as attention catcher but not dangerous looking like warning (we don't want to scary people off) but to catch their attention and invite them to read the books which would possibly open their eyes on these many topics (we don't want people to live in fear after they read these books but to use them in their advantage in their lives). So I need sans serif, open counters, humanist font that would be neutral enough to be able to cover such diverse titles, serious but not scary. More like warmish. Only one I have right now is Fedra Sans and I will use it unless someone suggests a better one. What do you think? Any suggestions?
Ronnia, maybe?
