Self Promo Question

July 20, 2005 • jupiterboy

What is ethical to include in self-promotion? Specifically, can you include work you created while working at other shops? How do you deal with collaborative work? Too many details about what you’ve contributed can start to sound a little crazy.

Also, my wife is an editor, and I’m working with ideas to incorporate what she does and market ourselves as a package. How do I handle work she is involved with currently since she is employed by an institution?

Most working people build their portfolios while working for someone, whether clients or a boss. As long as you (and your wife) actually did the work you are claiming to have done, there shouldn’t be a problem. And, if you worked as a member of a team, be honest about that.
2005-07-20 18:10:43 • oldnick

Honest is crucial and your reputation is affected by credibility.

In my CV, I have a section called: Summary of Projects, Collaborations and Consultancies

My own guidelines for wording: For projects I did – created, developed at or for projects I directed – directed Part of project team – collaborated with, contributed to Consulting – depends on level – consulted, contributed, directed

Yes, I’m old, but consult my creative consultants when collaborating!
