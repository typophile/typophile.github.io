Problems with FL when generating font

February 20, 2010 • Igor Freiberger

I’m having some dificulties with FontLab when generating the OT font (using FL Studio 5.04 and Windows Vista 64-bits):

1. Identical stems are rendered/printed differently

In glyph edition, the vertical stem is identical to /l/, /i/, /m/ and /n/. But these stems become uneven when shown on screen and when printed at small sizes (<9pt). The on screen issue I understand is related to font hinting. But the print problem is a mistery.

I considered this may be caused by using recycled toner – which produces a lighter result in my printer. But if this is the issue, stem variation at small sizes must also occurs with other fonts, and it’s not.

Any idea? Is thre an optical effect in simple vertical forms, like /l/ and /i/, which makes these glyphs appear slighty bolder than others, like /f/ and /m/?

2. Baseline misaligned on InDesign

I’m usign overshoots to round glyphs and they works perfectly in FontLab. But in InDesign the text is shows totally misaligned. I need to increase ID zoom to 800% to see the text properly.

The crazy thing is the text prints correctly aligned to baseline. It’s a render issue but I cannot identify where is the problem.
