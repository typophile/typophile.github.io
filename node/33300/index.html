Looking for a fresh serif typeface

April 25, 2007 • Stephen Brown

I've been challenged with, in the word of the client, "freshening up" the brand for a mid-size bank that hasn't changed a thing about its look and feel since it was created in 1987. They are not willing to change their logo, which uses Eras as it's primary typeface. Unfortunately, the words "Eras" and "fresh" do not belong in the same sentence. So I think the best I'm going to be able to do is select a new typeface for their tagline, which could therefore be used as the primary typeface in advertising, stationery, etc. I'm thinking it needs to be a serif face, as to not conflict with Eras, but I'm open to other suggestions. I'm having a hard time finding a serif typeface that looks contemporary enough to compensate for the use of Eras in the logo, but also timeless enough to work in case it's another 20 years before they update their look again. Any suggestions?
What kind of a company is it? Is it a logotype, strictly type? Which weight is being used? How long of a logotype is it? What colors are present? Is there another element involved? Strictly speaking I don't think it has to be a serif. Two sans, heck 3 sans, can be used together happily, it just takes finesse.
