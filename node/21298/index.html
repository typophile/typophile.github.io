I think it somewhat depends on the rendering. From what I recall, Verdana was designed to be a screen-friendly font, back before anti-aliasing became more prevalent. However, now that most operating systems support some form of anti-aliasing technology, many more fonts can be “screen-friendly” without necessarily being designed for the screen. With anti-aliasing in play, Verdana still looks good–but not a whole lot better than [insert font here].

For me, the best sans-serif is a tie between Verdana and Lucida; the best serif is Georgia.

IMO, when printed on paper, Georgia still looks nice… but Verdana looks awful, especially at larger sizes.
