Hairlines

August 26, 2003 • kris

I am curious about the usage of the term 'hairline' in in reference to (or so i think) the thinner strokes on letterforms. Icame across the term in an essay by Stanley Morrison,and have heard the term thrown about in 'type' class without any concrete reason for it's use. It occured to me that it may have come about because they literally look line hairlines when printed at smaller pt sizes. I would love some clarification!
kris I think a hairline is a hairline at no matter what point size. Basically half-a-point, maybe a quarter-point, can't remember, most likely a half. Rule (lines) were cast at a minimum of one-point but the printing surface could be much narrower. I've printed cat whiskers. Real cat whiskers. Very nice gradation!!! Gerald
