There's a lot of material in Mac McGrew's "American Metal Typefaces of the Twentieth Century", but the bottom line seems to be that Post Black Italic looks most like what your sample shows, although the R is not as 'swashed' as your sample. There are a number of other faces, like Blanchard, Roycroft, Buffalo and Ben Franklin that appear very similar. You are talking about original designs dating from the early 1900's, so they weren't fonts then (not digital ones, I mean). I don't know which have been digitized.

I think Scriptorium has a version of Blanchard they call Ruffian. It's not italic either.

The problem is that often if people do digitize a classic typeface, they are cautious about using the name, and so you need to know it when you see it. I have tried to 'decode' the Scriptorium collection because David Nalle does a great job of recapturing some of the old designs, but he almost always changes the names.

So I guess, unless someone knows of some more interpretations, the answer seems to be that they are mostly in books right now.
