body: "<strong>The Khatt Foundation Symposium on Arabic Visual Culture</strong>\r\n<strong>24
  August 2007, Amsterdam</strong>\r\n[img:sites/default/files/old-images/khkuka-logo_6023.jpg]\r\n\r\nThe
  Khatt foundation in collaboration with Mediamatic will host a full day of lectures,
  presentations and celebration of new Arabic cultural expression.\r\n\r\nThe day
  events will include: \r\n- [[http://www.khtt.net/artefact-415-en.html|the one-day
  symposium]]\r\n- [[http://www.khtt.net/artefact-106-en.html|the launch of 5 new
  Arabic typefaces]]\r\n- [[http://www.khtt.net/article-266-en.html|the Typographic
  Matchmaking book]]\r\n- the international online community,[[http://www.khtt.net|the
  Khatt Network for Arabic Typography]]\r\n- [[http://www.mediamatic.net/artefact-18263-en.html|the
  El-Hema Exhibition]] \r\n- workshops\r\n- the El-Hema design competition\r\n\r\n<strong>The
  aim of this symposium is to discuss the positive role of design and typography in
  Arabic youth culture, how it can generate positive perceptions and productive collaborations
  that bridge the gap between Eastern and Western cultures.</strong>\r\n\r\n<strong>Speakers</strong>\r\n\r\nA
  selection of scholars and designers from the Middle East, the US and Europe will
  discuss the impact of historical and contemporary visual culture on Arabic identity.
  New forms of visual expression, such as new Arabic typefaces, and other cross-cultural
  design experiments will be the focus of the symposium. The symposium will introduce
  the Khatt Foundations\u2019 collaborative pilot design project [[http://www.khtt.net/artefact-106-en.html|Typographic
  Matchmaking]]. Among other topics the symposium will cover topics that vary from
  history of European printing of Arabic books, to expressive calligraphy, to the
  relation of typography and dance, to the relation between culture and technological
  advancement in the printing arts, to political activism through visual communication
  in the Middle East, to case studies of inter-cultural design project between Dutch
  designer and Arab institutions (or designers), and statistical information about
  the visual arts, design, new media and typographic expression in the Arab world.\r\n\r\nThe
  symposium\u2019s speakers: \r\n- Huda Smitshuijzen AbiFar\xE8s (Khatt Foundation,
  Dubai-Amsterdam)\r\n- Willem Velthoven (Khatt Foundation / Stichting Mediamatic,
  Amsterdam)\r\n- Dr. Goeffrey Roper (Institute for the Study of Muslim Civilisations,
  Aga Khan University, London)\r\n- Jelle van der Toorn Vrijthoff (President of AGI
  & principal of QuinX bv. Amsterdam)\r\n- J.R. Osborn (University of Callifornia,
  San Diego)\r\n- Brody Neuenschwander (Artist/Calligrapher, Brugges)\r\n- Jacques
  Koeweiden (Koeweiden Postma, Amsterdam)\r\n- Zeina Maasri (American University of
  Beirut)\r\n- Nadine Touma (Dar Onboz publishers, Beirut)\r\n- Peter Bilak (Typotheque,
  Den Haag)\r\n- Nadine Chahine (Linotype Library, Germany)\r\n- Fawzi Rahal (Grey
  Worldwide, Dubai)\r\n- Tarek Atrissi (Atrissi Design, Hilversum) \r\n- Pascal Zoghbi
  (Notre Dame University, Lebanon)\r\n\r\n<strong>Registration & Fees</strong>\r\n\r\nThe
  cost of the symposium is 45 Euro (20 Euro for students with valid ID). \r\nRegistration
  includes a free copy of the book Typographic Matchmaking and bonus CD (which has
  a retail price of 30 Euro), a lunch, and a reception.\r\nRegistration will be available
  as of next week at www.khtt.net.\r\n[img:sites/default/files/old-images/KhattFonts-poster_4969.jpg]\r\n\r\n<strong>El-Hema
  Exhibition</strong>\r\n\r\nThe symposium will be followed by an exhibition entitled
  El-HEMA which will take place in Mediamatic\u2019s gallery space (on the ground
  floor of the PostCS building). The exhibition will open on the night of the 24th
  of August and will go on until the 26th of October.\r\n\r\n\r\n<em>The symposium
  and related events, have been generously supported by the Fonds BKVB, the Khatt
  Foundation, Stichting Mediamatic, the Openbare Bibliotheek van Amsterdam, and BIS
  Publishers.</em>\r\n"
