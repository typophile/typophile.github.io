Peter, fascinating graph, but could you please explain it more?

I can see how Arabic is more horizontal where Latin is more vertical on some level, but in other ways it’s the other way around… Also, concerning Armenian:

  1. One of the reasons it was invented (the reason that got the not-particularly-godfearing King Vramshabouh to fund the effort) was to prevent the Armenian entity from being fractured between the two powers that were sitting on it; so leaning to one side or another was exactly what they would NOT have done.
  2. To me it seems that Armenian is MORE vertical than Latin! Stucturally the x-height region especially is severely vertical (with the stroke contrast being like Latin) and the script is also Cartesially more vertical. Have you run my sample through your process yet?

BTW, there seems to be a mistake in the Arabic: as far as I know the raHmaan (third word of the first line) has a “long-short” floating alif (fatHa) as the last vowel - no idea what the actual name of it is - but basically it floats like the fatHa, but it’s vertical, and it’s pronounced as a long alif.
