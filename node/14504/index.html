Ethics of copying/being inspired

August 10, 2005 • manard

Hello, I was just wondering, how many people in the type design industry use to be ‘strongly inspired’ by previous existing font? And for ‘strongly inspired’ I mean open the font with an editing software, make major on minor changes, and save it as ‘new’. Or grab some glyphs, make some tweaking, and include them in their own design. I’m telling this because I’m having a hard time in making a ’s' without model, its sinuosities are excruciating. What do you think about it?

thank you m.

Don’t take info from other people’s font files. That is a copyright violation (those curves are code, mathematical data; they are copyrighted as intellectual property, i.e., computer programs). It is “stealing” someone else’s work, and it is just plain not nice (in addition to often being illegal).

Looking at fonts, especially in books, is great though. Many, many people are influenced by older types. And they are often revived. But that is something different.
