More contemporary version of Lubalin Graph

June 22, 2009 • LoremIpsumLoremIpsum

Looking for a more contemporary version of ITC Lubalin Graph…but not trendy. A wide range of weights, too.

For example: I think of FF Kievit as a contemporary version of Helvetica…a bit more geometric than Helvetica but similar in functionality and feel.

In my eyes, Kievit is quite far from Helvetica. I wouldn’t say it’s more geometric neither. Kievit is a humanist, Helvetica a neo grotesque.

If you’re looking for another geometric slab serif (like Lubalin Graph), maybe you’ll like Rockwell or Register Serif BTN.

If you want a more humanist touch to your slab, take a look at Archer, Serifa and PNM Caecilia. Those are some of my favourites. Actually, Archer take cues from both geometric and humanist designs, and might be just what you’re looking for.

2009-06-27 11:35:42 • Florian Hardwig

There is a very interesting, long discussion of different slabs and the development of Archer, in [[http://typography.com/ask/index.php|this talk]] by Hoefler and Frere-Jones. The first half is Jonathan Hoefler arguing that revivals are dead, and then the second half has Frere-Jones talking about Archer and Escrow, their Wall Street Journal text face.

Frere-Jones by the way really hates Lubalin Graph, and the strict geometric slabs in general, which he says “just die on the page.”
