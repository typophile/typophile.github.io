Pre WW I spur serif design. Can’t decide if it is lettering or was a font. I did not spot an obvious digital substitute. Scale is small but it may include an arrowhead termination on the goose-neck terminals of C, G and S. The leg on R reminds me of some Goudy fonts from the era, such as Goudy Oldstyle http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/lanston/ltc-goudy-oldstyle/ Some general similarity Vertrina http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/gatf/vertrina/ Possible substitute, which differs in many details, is a Mark Simonson recreation of a 1920s lettering style called Goldenbook http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/marksimonson/goldenbook/ Don
2014-11-22 11:59:04 • donshottype

For overall letter forms, I think it may have been based on something like Goudy Medium (just raise the crossbars on the |H|, |G|, and |E| and rein in the serifs): http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/redrooster/goudy-38-rr/

For general look and feel, but still missing some crucial details (such as the |R| and some serifs), these might also work for recreations: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/aperto/ http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/fontbureau/grand-central/ http://www.t26.com/search?q=Glosso

There’s also a passing resemblance to Korinna, which was designed in 1904 (the serifs are way off, but the overall form isn’t bad): http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/adobe/itc-korinna/

Nevada Serial captures some of the details (especially the |E|): http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/ef/nevada/

The engraving or lithography used to make the lid design may have been made by an English craftsman who was familiar with a font sold in England as Wren, which has the sucking lemon G with a tall bar and a small spur on the chin, and various similarities in other letters. Don
