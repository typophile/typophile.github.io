The future of the 300 DPI tablet

September 25, 2010 • blank

Marvell, a chip maker just announced a new mobile CPU/GPU that might make 300DPI tablets a reality late next year. Marvell claims the chip is ”capable of driving four simultaneous displays at up to 2K x 2K resolution”. That doesn’t give it the capability to run a 7" tablet at 300DPI, but if I get sloppy with math and throw Moore’s law at it the version that gets released next fall will be able to handle a 7" tablet just fine. I have no clue how this relates to future iPad developments, but this thing will be great for small Linux tablets/readers at 300DPI. Of course, that all assumes somebody other than Apple can actually manufacture a 300DPI LCD, and I have no clue what’s going on with that side of the technology.
Apple doesn't manufacture LCDs... they pay for (I think) an Asian company to supply them. There are quite a few companies that are producing higher-PPI screens, and with the surge in digital readers and advanced reading technologies like the Kindle, I think your estimations are definitely correct. Let's hope these higher resolutions come to things other than simply tablets, though. I'd love to see what the next "1080p" will be.
