An Inspiring Script by Jessica Hische

September 9, 2012 • tbufano

This is not a direct type ID, but I am hoping I can get some thoughts on an existing font that has similar properties.

I’m working on an identity project for a local emerging fashion designer and after having done a cursory evaluation of her brand, I’m on the hunt for a font to pair with it. We are going for old Hollywood with a modern and fresh twist. Jessica Hische is one of my design idols and I happened across a [[http://jessicahische.is/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/magic-hours11.jpg|book cover]] she did and the look of it fits perfectly - if only she extrapolated this into a font!

Looks retro Still very modern and fresh Very clean Slightly feminine

Paired with the hand-drawn feel of the dot on the “i” along with the other illustrative details, it really puts this look spot on for what I’m looking for in a font.

As a starting point, can anyone think of a nice script font with a similar aesthetic? I’m open to non-script suggestions as well as long as it has that right mix of modern and retry. So far, I already own [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/corradine/almibar/?refby=tb|Almibar Pro]], but there’s parts of it that I fear might be unusable.
