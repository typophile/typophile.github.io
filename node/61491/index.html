Earliest boldface?

August 30, 2009 • Sindre

I’d really like to know, if this is at all known, the first example of a bold version of a serif typeface at text size. I know slab serif (and later sans serif) were used for emphasis in body copy in the early 19th century, what I’m after is the first deliberately designed contrasting boldface, intended for use as we (or some of us) use boldface today.

I’ve searched the archives without finding much of relevance. Can anyone shed some light on this matter? Or point to a thread I haven’t found?

The most obvious and concrete/provable example that comes to mind is Cheltenham, although I have a nagging feeling that I’m forgetting something else that popped up earlier in the decade. I wouldn’t be surprised to if Bodoni had cut heavy weights of his designs at text sizes, but you would have to hit up a library with a copy of the Manuale Tipographico to confirm that.
