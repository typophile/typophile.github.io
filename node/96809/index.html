The Scotch story is rather convoluted. At any rate, the Austin type is related to the Carter types Miller and Georgia: http://typefoundry.blogspot.ca/2007/02/scotch-roman.html

Really, you could use anything that has an early 19th century provenance, but not Bodoni or Didot. Richard Austin’s types were a reaction to that kind of cool neo-classicism, as in his foundry statement: http://typefoundry.blogspot.ca/2007/02/richard-austins-address-to-printers.html
