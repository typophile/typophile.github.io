Survey: Global - Regional

November 18, 2007 • thierry blancpain

I’m a student of visual communication in Bern, Switzerland. For a school project called “Global – Regional” i have setup an online survey. It takes about 3 minutes to fill out. Everything else is described on the website. This project aims to answer the question if design will be more regional or more global in style, colors, etc. in 2015 than now - and why that. Your answers will help me finding an answer. THANK YOU.

A quick and rather broad observation:

I’d imagine global would by 2015 include a lot more regional strands. That’s as compared to today when global is, by and large, Western.

On the other hand, regional styles would quite likely get more global in expression. That’s something we’ve seen a good deal in India over the past decade.

i agree with you. its not “global or regional” in my opinion, its more global + more regional strands. i was travelling in asia this summer so i got a good view into the local design culture + im looking to include as many asian designers as possible.

quote from an answer from an indian designer studying in the us right now: […] I think this has to do with the fact that colour has been played with in textiles and other fields in India for thousands of years, and this has led people to hit upon say, the perfect navy that sits right with that particular yellow ochre, and so on. Also, contrary to popular belief, bright colours are not the only ones that are used in India, subtle combinations of whites on whites, olives, greys, browns, powder blues are also seen used in traditional and current applications.

very interesting stuff. if you want to write more about your experiences with how indian design evolved in the last decade, MORE THAN HAPPY to read it!
