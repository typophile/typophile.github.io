November 15, 2010 • jillustration

I teach a high school graphic design class. It’s unfortunate that my class only goes for one semester and there’s so much to teach them! I have to teach them design software on top of design principles which is a tricky to cram in without overloading them. I unfortunately won’t be able to teach them EVERYTHING about typography (sad!). That being said, I was wondering if anyone knows any links to typeface/font lessons or has any ideas? I won’t be able to cover everything but I’m looking to give them a solid foundation and have it be hands on and fun at the same time. I am going to teach them basics of tracking, leading, point size, sans serif vs serif and a drizzling of the history of type to start, but I’d really like to tie this into a fun more hands on lesson and I’m drawing a blank. I’d really like to do something on how fonts convey emotions but having them just type words in a font they think conveys it sounds kinda boring to me right now. Any help would be MOST appreciated. Thanks in advance!
