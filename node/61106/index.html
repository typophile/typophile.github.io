Well, I’m looking at how this thing is using the space, as well as letter confusability issues. Unless you remove the tittle (which I wouldn’t advise) the ascender space is being used anyway and the “h” should take advantage, especially since its ascender keeps it from looking like an “n”; in contrast the “p” is sort of wasting the space below, and a short descender doesn’t make it look like any other letter. BTW, even looking at this as a font I’d say the descender space is too much (because of letter frequencies in real text). In my book making the descenders exactly the same length as the ascenders (which looks like might be the case here) is a Modernist simpletonism.
