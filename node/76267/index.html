Having problems with the R

November 12, 2010 • 1996type

Hey fellow typophilians,

I am currently designing my own typeface, called expletus which can be viewed here: http://www.typophile.com/node/72368 I am confident with all upper- and lowercase glyphs, except the R. It used to have a straight leg, but no matter how I many varaitions I tried, it just always looks out of balance. So I decided to make it more like Myriad’s R. At first glance I would say, the latest version (top) looks best, but I’m not sure if it looks ‘clean’ enough. What do you think? Any comments are welcome, including other critique on expletus-sans. Cheers!

How about just making the fancy terminal a straight cut – at first. Work out the whole glyph set that way (and test it in small text), and then apply the extra nuancing (if really necessary).

That may seem like a lot of work and going backwards, at this point in time, but it would resolve design issues by separating letter shape and letter detail, shape being the more fundamental and having priority, and be more amenable to adding extra weights.

Design the process.

(Many people work this way when producing rounded fonts, by producing a “normal” sans, and then applying the rounding.)
