Would you even have folios?

Well, I would like them not for ordering but to hold it together. For example Page 1/3, Page 2/3, Page 3/3. It just makes me feel safer because an incomplete copy is clearly marked as incomplete.

The audience are recruiters who rush through resumes are an alarming pace. From what I can gather, their eyes track the page top-left to bottom-right and if they get distracted or did not see what they are looking for, its in the bin.

However, with such a short collection of pages, some folio positions just do not work. For example, if you bleed the right hand edge then Page 3/3 causes them to look for Page 4/3 and they get confused (witnessed it first hand). While that is not an immediate problem for most jobs, its clearly flawed and may reflect badly if applying for design-related jobs - so its in the bin.

So where should they go? :)

It sounds like you have a much bigger problem with your design/hierarchy, something that folio placement isn’t going to solve.

Also, a three page resume is probably two pages too long, especially if it’s going to people who are just skimming them. Edit it down to something short and digestible.
