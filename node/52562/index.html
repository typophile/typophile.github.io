What do you all use and why? What do you think of Extensis Suitcase compared to Linotype Font Explorer X? And what about Font Agent Pro? What are the pluses and minuses for each? What would make a font management app essential for you?

2008-12-13 14:22:28 • Bert Vanderveen

Well, there is something to be said for releasing bug fixes as they are noticed, rather than to hold off releasing an update for months or years while there is a known flaw in the current release, then charge for a full upgrade price for what amounts to making the thing you bought work as originally advertised plus a token new feature.

Not to say that’s necessarily the case with any of the above programs, but I have experienced the latter treatment from some vendors.

I prefer the approach that releases (as) frequent (as necessary) updates with a built-in notification mechanism in the application. Of course it’s nice when they get it totally right the first time, but that just doesn’t happen in the world of software development anymore (and was rare when it did).

I must respectfully disagree.

In the absence of specific evidence to the contrary, I’d assume the same number of bugs are getting fixed per year, regardless of number of updates. 2008-12-14 18:24:59 • Thomas Phinney

OTOH: Releasing a lot of bug fixes in a short time means that one’s quality control is soemwhat lacking & the programming sloppy.

This is true; known issues should be eliminated before release whenever possible. However, when problematic issues do make it into a release, they should be addressed as quickly as possible.

I look back to the last year a certain small yet significant developer was around as a graphics software company. They had many products which were incomparable with their feature sets, but released on a schedule determined more by marketing concerns than whether the software was really ready. Some had so many bugs as to be barely workable. But, rather than releasing an immediate patch to address these issues, they let them go for extended periods of time until put forth as an omnibus upgrade. All in the interest of looking like their quality control wasn’t as sloppy as it was.

Then, for some reason they decided the would become an “internet company” and sold off all their graphics software properties to other companies (in some cases back to the original developers they licensed them from). I haven’t heard from this company since.
