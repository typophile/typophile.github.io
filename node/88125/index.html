2011-12-07 17:45:28 • Nick Shinn

What units of measurement use midpoints?

Midpoints are a sort of all-purpose punctuation mark, useful for separating items on the same line and for a variety of other uses in display contexts. They can also be used as a multiplication symbol (though that is probably a different Unicode character). They are not often used in running text.

Bullets, of course, are used in bulleted lists.

As to how to differentiate them, the middle dot is expected to be similar in size and shape to the period, whereas the bullet is larger such that its darkness stands out from text.
2011-12-08 23:34:21 • cerulean
For vector and matrix arithmetic, cross multiplication (denoted with ×) is a different operation from dot multiplication (denoted with the ⋅ operator), and the middle dot · makes an adequate substitute for the dot multiplication sign in a font that doesn’t support a large range of math symbols. And although probably rather rare, I expect there are sometimes occasions when vector arithmetic would need to be mentioned in a non-math typeface.
