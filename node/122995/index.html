I’m trying to identify the monospaced typeface used in the words ‘Australien’ and ‘Sydamerkia’ in the images attached. The images are of a map found in a Swedish encyclopaedia published in the 1950’s called Kunskapens Bok.

This is a linear slab serif design – monoline “Antique” or Egyptian from the predigital era. For a similar digital font consider Antique Central [img:sites/default/files/old-images/AntiqueCentralAsSYD_4838.gif]http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/aboutype/antique-central/ The main difference is in the leg of R. You will note that I did not include any typewriter fonts, despite the fact that they are generally monoline. This is becasue they are also monospace, which means that letters like M are compressed to a standard width and letters like I have very expanded serifs. However, there are a few proportionate spaced fonts with typewriter style letterforms, such as URW Typewriter Light [img:sites/default/files/old-images/URWTypewriterLightAsSYD_3898.gif] http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/urw/typewriter/

As a final comment I should note that before the last decades of the 20th century lettering on most maps was engraved or lithographed, or hand lettered and photo reduced, rather than taken directly from a font. Note that the map states “litografiska.”

None of the fonts I suggested is a perfect match to the lettering.
