Hi all, A first time for me on two accounts - first, being my post on Typophile and the other being font design. I've found reading the forums and articles to be very intelligent and constructive, so I'm hoping the response to my plea won't be an exception. I've been working on this font for sometime, albeit on and off. Each time I return to the design I find a different flaw or a cause to amend it, so I thought it wise to put it out there for some insight in the hope I can actually finish it. As it stands there is only the one weight and lowercase and caps. You'll immediately notice an inconsistency with the S and the kerning is way off. At this stage I'd like to invite comments/suggestions/an open critique, to help me see the wood from the trees. Thank you in advance. Grez

Interesting. The thing that bothers me though is the black dots of the terminals. It is definately a character trait, but I don't think it really works as it is actually distracting from reading the word. It looks like a random collection of bulletholes shot through your typeface ;) It would be interesting to see if you can tone down these dots. This is a very geometrical typeface, which is a problem with letters like P, O, a & q. It makes these letters look stiff, and the a becomes more like a circle with a stick attached, than a letter. To make your typeface a lot more interesting I would try working away from this very strict geometry. As is often said about Futura, THE geometric typeface: It's not actually based on perfect geometrical circles, and it has a slight contrast, which makes it a lot more beautiful and interesting. Study the shapes of Futura and it will give you an idea how to improve your typeface. Good luck. greetings Jeffrey [[http://www.infraordinaire.com/wordpress|infraordinaire]]
