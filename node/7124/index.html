Indesign is more of a collaging program than an art program unto itself.

You can click on swatches to make them RGB. Then you could do a batch automate in Photoshop, converting the placed images to CMYK. It's very slick, try it.

If you're ultimately going to export, you can continue to work in CMYK, then set the PDF or JPEG or whatever as RGB while exporting.

Just a warning: any automated CMYK to RGB conversion is going to be a bit off anyway, so doing it by eye may be your best bet. If you don't care, just shift-select the colors in the Swatches palette and double-click on one of the highlighted ones. The group options window will appear, then choose the color mode. I think that's it.

There are color managment options you can choose, but that's a larger, separate issue.

2004-10-13 16:26:05 • Miss Tiffany
Neither RGB nor CMYK is an absolute space. That is, they both only exist in reference to some particular parameters. So any conversion you do becomes a color management issue. You can of course ignore the issue, but that doesn't mean that color management stuff won't factor into the conversion: it's just that you won't control it.

Of course, depending on the nature of the job, this may not be particularly critical.

To echo John B, what is it that you are trying to achieve by switching to RGB? How will this document ultimately be output or in what form will it be viewed?
