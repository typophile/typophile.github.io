November 2, 2005 • hrant

Concerning http://typophile.com/node/15992 here's what I wrote to the ATypI list recently. I hope I'm not the only one who feels this way.. " > Neither process is easy, nor was the challenge faced by Peter > the Great in his efforts to open Russia's 'window to the West'. Not only difficult, but both also have a "dark side", especially in this age of globalization. I don't mean to be a troublemaker, but I hope there are enough signs now (such as my essay in Spatium #4) that something like Peter the Great's reform isn't automatically good in every way, especially as times and politics change. I think we can agree that at a minimum it's worth discussing potential "complications". I hope that somewhere in the celebrations there can be some small room for sobering dissent as to the true effects of this most notable case of Latinization on today's world, both within typography and on a broader scale. I hope that's not too much to expect of an *international* organization. As with anything that a group of people are proud of, this can be a touchy subject. But I think open discourse is still valuable, and I very much hope to hear expressions of fruitful ambivalence concerning this anniversary from other members as well. " It was Chris Lozos I think who recently made a small critique of the Cyrillic structures, and although I think formalism is always limited in relevance, I believe Chris does have a valid point, somewhere; and in fact to me any script has features and attributes that need objective attention in order to improve, or at the very least to be really understood. hhp
