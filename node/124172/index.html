Backwards slanting italic

March 28, 2015 • Anthony Barnett

I have read that one of the alternatives, before digital, to printing book titles in roman (which I find ungainly) in an otherwise italic sentence was the use of backwards slanting italic. Whilst it is easy enough to turn normal italic into backwards or even upright italic in, say, InDesign, are there any digital text fonts that include specifically designed backwards italic?

Backslanted italics are used in cartography, for example to write river names. One of the better known example is http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/kursivschrift/ But there also is the most expensive font on MyFonts: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/jha/bodoni-ritalic/

Digging a bit, you can find a few older threads on the subject.
