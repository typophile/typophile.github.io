Well, when it comes to scanning you really need to think about the final output: are your pieces printed well enough that better scans will result in better prints? And are you scanning really great color photos to begin with? I prefer Epson scanners—the drivers and scanning software are the best around, the output is always great, they’re fast, and you can buy them refurbished at Epson.com to save a lot. Whatever you do, don’t go near HP scanners, the output, drivers, and software are all terrible. Right now I’m really sick of printers. I’ve been using Epson inkjets for about five years now and I can’t recommend the new models unless your output is mostly glossy photo printouts. Epson’s newer Stylus printers (at least the sub $1000 models I’ve used) don’t work worth a damn. The sheet feeders are terrible, and on uncoated stocks streaking is common, even with Epson’s expensive inks and papers. Given the premium prices an Epson is only worth buying if you have an unpaid intern to keep it working. I’ve heard really great things about the new Canon printers, and Canon ink is cheap, so give them a look. You can find the best printer reviews on digital photo sites.
