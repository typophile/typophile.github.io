American Type Foundry Rock Stars

November 19, 2007 • jmickel

A friend of mine, who shall remain nameless, recently mentioned that he thought there were four American type foundries that were truly top notch.

He listed Font Bureau, House Industries, H&FJ, and Emigre, and implied that there weren’t really any others that came close.

I mentioned a couple that I thought were contenders, but he contended that these four were THE rock stars, and that any other than them were more like ‘indie rock.’

There are obviously lots of talented individuals outside of these, but what about other companies?

Adobe is certainly top notch also.

2007-11-19 05:29:23 • jmickel

Si is right. Also the Mono-Lino conglomerate has to be there, as well. They have a lot of both good and bad in their back list, and their new stuff is also uneven. But they have so many classics and enough good recent stuff that I would think you have to include them.

So from the point of view of ‘labels’ the big 5 (in no particular order) are probably: FontBureau, H&FJ, House Industries, Monotype Imaging, and Adobe.

I don’t include Microsoft, as its type is normally included with software, rather than sold separately. Beyond those 5, you would probably get more arguments, including about Emigre.

edit: On second thought, if you are thinking ‘quality’ any list of foundries is probably misconceived. The story of type in the past ten years is as much a story of individuals as foundries, though both are important. For example, Village, with its collection of outstanding designers, may be as much of a factor now as House or Emigre. And Christian Schwartz, who has published through a number of foundries and collaborations, is so productive and his quality so high that he on his own is a virtual foundry. Then you have one-man American shops like those of Typophilers Mark Simonson and Eric Olsen, who also have a large and high quality body of work.

And among distributors you have Veer and Bitstream (MyFonts), who also have high quality work that is exclusive.

So I don’t think a picture of a big 5 is very representative of what is going on in the production of type in the US.
