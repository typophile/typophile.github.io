Interesting. You bring back a memory of one of my first paid projects. Must have been 15 years ago. A medical devices company here on Long Island engaged me to put together a tri-fold flyer–they referred to it as a brochure for some kind of medical lamp they manufactured.

I recall being very excited about the job, despite how they didn’t seem to value it enough to pay me anything even remotely realistic. But I was so green, I just wanted something for experience. Anyway, they had so me very clear requirements for this project. One was that it was to be all-Helvetica: roman, italic, bold, and bold ital were all allowed—but that was it. Came out nice enough I guess, considering the limitations and my limitations at the time.

Now I’d use a nice-fitting, if unimaginative, serif like Adobe Garamod—something very readable, but not glitzy. Never want to distract the reader from learning about the product being pitched.
