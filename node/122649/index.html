[img:sites/default/files/old-images/ljv_6089.jpg] This comes from the cover of a 1951 edition of cello music, published in Nice, France by Georges Delrieu & Cie. These are the only letters I have; the publication contains an inconsistent mixture of other typefaces.
[img:sites/default/files/old-images/Untitled-1_6437.jpg] From the variations in the ends of the strokes (above), I suspect this was drawn using a [[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technical_pen|Rapidograph pen]] and a draftsman's template like the one below. The artist probably combined arcs from different-size circles or ellipses to make the V. [img:sites/default/files/old-images/r-12_3965.jpg]

Oops, my bad. Rotring didn't introduce the Rapidograph until 1953. But this title definitely looks like it was done by hand with some sort of drafting pen and template. The artist may have improvised these letters using [[http://www.mccoys-kecatalogs.com/KELeroy/3245-15L/3245-15L.htm|Leroy lettering tools]]. They have been in use since at least the 1930s, and according to [[http://www.musicprintinghistory.org/handwritten/tools.html|this source]], they were sometimes used in music publishing.
