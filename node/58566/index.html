What is the origin of the spike serif?

May 31, 2009 • blank

I’m confused about the origins of spike serifs (Licko’s Matrix being an example). Are these a nineteenth century decorative type innovation, or are they older? And when the term “Latin” is used to refer to them, does it refer to and older style, or to a newer style of decorative lettering that appeared in the 1950s/60s?

I think they originated at the beginning of the 19th century in wood types, and were called latin because the triangular shapes of the serifs bear some similarity to the terminal shapes inside traditional incised inscriptions, but I don’t know for sure. But the letter forms bear an even stronger resemblance to Roman coinage; perhaps that is the source:
