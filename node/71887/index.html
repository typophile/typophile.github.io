2010-07-02 16:12:56 • Jean Paul Beumer
Despite the messy layout, which is the cliché for "art", this seems off target. Unless sports are considered cultural, or the prize really is the trophy, the image -- raising a trophy -- is wrong, as it signifies sport, not art. It doesn't relate to the text, having nothing to do with voting, as sports are determined by scores, not votes. Sure, it's a metaphor, but it's not been fleshed out, nor handled with any wit. In fact, it looks like you're apologizing for the metaphor, by making it blurry, hoping viewers won't notice it's wrong. Further to the unstructured layout, this is a lazy and decorative way to say "art", in lieu of a strong idea rendered simply and directly.

I have to agree with James and Nick. I think that this is too messy, and there is too much going on, with too little contrast and hierarchy. Remember this is a poster, not a booklet.
