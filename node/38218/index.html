I want to check an assumption - when you make an Ohorn ( U+01A1, U+01A0 ) it is necessary that the ‘horn’ be joined with the O shape so that the O shape in the Ohorn cannot be a component.

(Although the Ohorn shape can be a component of other Unicode glyphs.)

Is this correct?

Put another way though - I might ask. Will a composed overlapping shape create an unwanted white space the way that a temporary ( unconnected but overlapping) leg on an upper case R does?

Thanks!

What happens can be rasterizer-dependent and in some cases size-dependent. But also consider what will happen if somebody ever does an outline or stroke effect on the text. You really don’t want overlapping paths in that case.

…if somebody ever does an outline or stroke effect on the text. You really don’t want overlapping paths in that case.

That’s why I weld (remove overlap) cedillas and ogoneks onto accented characters. Shipping a font with diacritics in component form only saves a few kilobytes in font file size and adds nothing to the quality of the product.

Most fonts I’ve produced so far are display types, or semi-display-demi-text, and designers will inevitably render them in outline form. As long as your horn glyph, cedilla glyph and ogonek glyph exist stand-alone in their own cells with legit unidoce index numbers assigned, they can be components of diacritic characters combined on-the-fly by applications—but you knew that already.

Will a composed overlapping shape create an unwanted white space the way that a temporary ( unconnected but overlapping) leg on an upper case R does?

The whitespace is generated in OT fonts with Type 1 outlines, but for some reason not in TT fonts with TT outlines.
