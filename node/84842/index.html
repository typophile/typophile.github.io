2011-09-06 18:09:03 • Andreas Stötzner
It's unrealistic to expect the typeface to punch all those buttons. Another strategy is to "play against type", i.e. choose a typeface that has some of the characteristics, then add those it lacks by means of the setting. You want strong and girly?—set bold in pink or grunge up an elegant script. Many faces have heavy weights (deemed masculine, for some reason, despite heaviness being equal opportunity), and light weights (deemed feminine, notwithstanding Peter Crouch), in which case font is more important than face.
