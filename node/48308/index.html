buying type

August 11, 2008 • nitrospectide

I just joined the site, and have a question about how everyone buys their type.

I have a single person studio, and for things like software and hardware, I just buy them outright, while for stock media (photos, audio, etc) I mark up and bill to the client (since they are project-specific). How does everyone handle it with type? What do you charge clients for a new face bought for a project? Type has the added wrinkle that you have to buy it before you use it… there’s no way to ‘comp’ with type without paying for it. So you’re taking a risk just to present an option. Because of these things, I have largely stuck with my base collection of classics. But I’m wanting to branch out more.

Thoughts?

The answer may relate to the services you’re selling to your clients. Perhaps you could describe typical projects.

2008-08-11 20:27:29 • nitrospectide

An ident project seems like the most obvious spot,

Two thoughts - most foundry/distributor sites have online type-testers so there’s no reason you can’t do basic comps.

Secondly, the client is probably going to need a license to the font themselves, so maybe look at vendors that allow license transfers, and then pass on the font at cost (+ your usual markup) along with the designs.
