Large format inkets and papers

April 1, 2007 • blank

I have access to 24" and 36" Epson inkjet printers that I can use for printing school stuff and I am running into problems with paper. Specifically, these printers are generally used for printing massive photos, posters, and banners, and the official papers are pretty thick (but not stiff enough to be useful) and coated on one side, which is problematic for items that need to fold up or be double-sided. Does anyone know of a double-sided paper I can use in a large format inkjet, preferably available in rolls? Second, I often run double-weight bristol through smaller Epson inkjets with no trouble at all; has anyone done this in a large-format printer?

It depends a lot on what sort of paper you’re looking for (rag? matte? glossy? colored?), but there certainly are two-sided, inkjet-coated papers available in rolls.

Hahnemuhle PhotoRag Duo is one. That link has lots of other papers as well.

As for your question about running heavy paper – most large-format printers are better at this than smaller-format. So if you’ve had success so far, you shouldn’t have any trouble with the larger printers.

–John

Can’t you ask the person/company that owns those printers for different types of paper? Such rolls of paper usually cost something like 300 to 400 euros or more (about 400 to 500 USD). At least the paper-types we used in a printing company I worked at a couple of years ago.

I do know from experience, that printing double-sided on such printers, can be quite problematic, because they are not very precise. You might find it difficult to get both sides aligned. How thick the paper can be, differs from printer to printer.

We’ve actually had a long-running discussion about running different papers through large-format printers on the Book-Arts list lately: the general consensus is that almost anything up to 140 lbs works well. I’ve been using a lot of 80 lb Strathmore Aquarelle II to edition a book on my little SPR300 and it works great.
