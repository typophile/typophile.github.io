2006-06-27 01:37:45 • bowfinpw

It looks like a customized version of a free font that came with my Dell Inspiron 6000 notebook: A font•a•licious font called “Alba.”

The stock version of Alba is not an exact match, judging by the lowercase “g” glyph (the one in the stock font has a more elaborate descender). However, it is very easy to alter the glyphs in such a typeface using mainstream drawing tools like Illustrator or CorelDRAW. The “f” is an exact match.

I will go so far as to say “Alba” or whatever face Alba may copy is indeed the source for that type.
