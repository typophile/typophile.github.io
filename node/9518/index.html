2005-03-02 20:46:39 • pattyfab
I agree with you about the loss of fonts, and thus I think it becomes a personal judgment about making a good faith effort to see if anyone still holds a copyright to the font before you decide that a design is 'dead' and in need of 'revival'. In this case NovelFonts was cautious enough to rename all of their fonts, although many, if not all, of them were old designs that were never digitized.

But I guess those statements were really about when you could revive a design. As far as 'sharing', I have always been VERY reluctant to do that, unless I believe the font is no longer commercially available, AND the individual I am 'sharing' it with is someone I know will not be using it commercially or selling/distributing it themselves. It's a slippery slope, so I would rather err on the side of conservativism, and avoid the practice altogether.
