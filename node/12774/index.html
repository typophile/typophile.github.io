The little logo in the pic says Inland Type Foundry. Inland Printer was a trade journal. Inland Type was absorbed by the ATF conglomerate in 1912. So Commercial Script would either have shown up in early ATF specimen catalogs (and later dropped) or its matrices/punches were destroyed as a consequence of the acquisition. The later ATF version (designed by Bennett I think), nor any of the versions that have been subsequently digitized, that I have seen, don’t look like this design.

Now that I see the sample, it doesn’t look like anything I have seen or collected Paul. The Inland/ATF connection was the reason I used the McGrew book’s ATF ‘Commercial Script’ when I suggested the one sold by Monotype.

The sample you posted looks like some of the Photo-Lettering Spencerian Scripts (if that’s the right term). There are several by M. M. Davison that look very close to this, but the Photo-Lettering catalogs only show partial samples. In any case, they would only have been ‘inspired’ by this style, since his work was much more recent.

Until House does something with these, they are not likely to see digital format any time soon. Pity.
