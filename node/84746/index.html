Pairing Helvetica with Lucida Grande

August 20, 2011 • rodrigogalindez

Guys,

What do you think of pairing Helvetica (for titles) with Lucida Grande (for body copy)? My client thinks that at least we need to use Helvetica somewhere, since the logo is on Helvetica too. In the first iteration I used Museo Sans for the titles, which seemed a nice pairing with Lucida Grande to me, but it wasn’t approved.

I’m not sure of using Helvetica for the main body copy font; I found it just too much uninteresting, used everywhere, and lacks the humanist touch I want to achieve with this design (rounded corders, subtle shadows, etc.). Besides, Lucida Grande works amazingly well in 15px and 13px (and yet even in 11px!). It’s very versatile.

I’m attaching a screenshot of this possible pairing. On left, the initial iteration, and on right, the one with Helvetica. I think it could work, but I’d love to hear your thoughts.

Best,

Rod

I’ve been thinking about this sort of thing for quite a while.

In essence, the static closed grotesque forms of Helvetica are perfectly suited for headlines. They fill the surface, grabbing attention, forming an almost solid unit (the word). It is tightly spaced, has a huge x-height, tiny descenders and ascenders: perfect for stacking.

Lucida Grande on the other hand: a humanist, dynamic shapes, loosely spaced, open forms (and although not that dramatically different in vertical proportions – let’s for a moment think about the very concept humanist) is obviously rooted in the tradition of text type.

The two skeletons are from completely different spheres, and thus this pairing is against all [[http://typophile.com/node/21000|rules]]. However, I think something like this is most sensible … in theory. In practice, you need some echo of the first in the second. Preferably you’d also want a clear distinction between the two, not something so close it just looks like an error.
