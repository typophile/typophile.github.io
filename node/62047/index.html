I was looking at it again and must admit that I'm still stunned by the idea but there are some details that makes it too much "2" and "3" and too little "one". To make it "one" as well as "2" and "3" I'd suggest you to first take "2" and "3" and positioning them the way you want them and then overlay them with "O N E" to tweak the numbers into letters. Since it's a little hard to explain I've prepared a little mock-up. Hopefully you don't mind since it's just for explanation and showing you what I mean. In this case I choose Avenir 95 since it's very geometric and has some nice numbers but I think that you can use this technique for any kind of typeface. I hope this helps you. Keep on going and I think you will end up having a great logo. [img:sites/default/files/old-images/contours_3981.jpg] [img:sites/default/files/old-images/one_4427.jpg]

"The top-heavy E made certain sense because “one” was read left-to-right, “two” - bottom-to-top, 90 degrees ccw relatively to “one” and then “three” - right-to-left, in another 90 degree turn." That's exactly what I was thinking, but then I started to realize that this might be the reason why I read it as "2" and "3". The first thing I've seen was a flipped "3" -- simply because it has to little in common with a typical "E" -- which is the main reason why the shape of "on" isn't too important because it will always be perceived as "2" only because it makes sense, seeing a "2" on the left side of a "3". And since in the original post the logo is called "one" I think it's important to read "one" at first and then realize -- after you've analyzed the wordmark -- that the word is actually made up from "2" and "3". This kind of adds an element of surprise while otherwise it might appear a little "conflicted" and maybe slightly awkward. Nevertheless I totally agree with riccard0's "emotional impact" of a top-heavy "E" and epsilicon's 90 degree turns, but I'm not sure if this two bonuses are more important then a good readability. In a perfect world you might be able to combine "emotional impact", 90 degree turns and readability. I wish you all the best for this and I think the cleverness of the idea should be motivation enough for you to design something better then our two versions.
