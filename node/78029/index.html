Book design taken to the next level

January 14, 2011 • Jean Paul Beumer

Some time ago we got the latest issue of Textiel Plus in the mail, a magazine about textile arts. Subjects vary from felt art to knitting art. I know, it sounds corny, but there are some great projects in it. In this issue there’s an article on Boukje Voet, a Dutch artist who transformes old books into fantastic objects by cutting, ripping, folding en pasting them. Perhaps not to everyone’s taste (especially book purists), but certainly to mine. It’s book design taken to another dimension.

Check out this link: http://boukjevoet.exto.org/
