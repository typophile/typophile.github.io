Hi! Long time lurker, first time poster, and now I need your help! This is the title page of “An Enquiry Concerning Human Understanding” from 1748 by the Scottish philosopher David Hume, but what font is it? [img:sites/default/files/old-images/66959_2_4740.jpg]

Based on the E and A, I would say this is a Caslon typeface. It is unlikely, I think to find this exact type in digital form, because each size in metal type was often a slightly different design. There are at least 6 different type sizes on this title page.

The most similar font I have found is [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/lanston/ltc-caslon/?refby=bowfin|LTC Caslon]]. Unlike many Caslons it has the single-serif C (as does Adobe Caslon Pro).
