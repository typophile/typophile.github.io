2010-08-17 14:50:07 • Chris Dean
The Kindle iPad app doesn't actually use PMN Caecilia, Hrant. It's all Georgia, which reads pretty well on the screen. The Nook iPad app, actually, is more typographically sophisticated. You can tweak the point size (five choices), leading (four choices), font (Amasis, Century Schoolbook, Georgia, Joanna, Times New Roman, Ascender Sans, Gill Sans, and Trebuchet), text color, page color, margin widths (four choices), and page brightness. There is an option to turn full justification on or off, but I have trouble turning it off in some books. There is also the option to "use publisher settings," which, on occasion, sets the text in Charter.
