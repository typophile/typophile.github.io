  (site of Tuesday\u2019s Pecha Kucha Event)\r\nJune 7 - July 18, 2008\r\n<strong>Critical
  Art Ensemble / Institute for Applied Autonomy: SEIZED</strong>\r\nFollowing the
  four year long ordeal of CAE founding member and University at Buffalo Art Professor
  Steve Kurtz\u2014accused by the Justice Department of \"bio-terrorism\" and later
  indicted on charges of mail fraud for procuring harmless bacterial cultures for
  use in an educational art project\u2014SEIZED presents the artworks behind this
  case which has attracted worldwide attention and propelled an international arts
  community to rally to Kurtz's support and on behalf of freedom of expression.\r\nhttp://www.hallwalls.org/visual_shows/2008/show_seized.html\r\n\r\n<strong>Karpeles

  1, 2008 \u2013 September 1, 2008\r\n<strong>Star Trek Drawings</strong>\r\nStar
  Trek is one of the most successful TV series of all time.  The production of the
  TV program requires the efforts of artists to model every aspect of the story and
  all the devises used. It is these artists sketches and drawings that we present
  in our exhibit \"Star Trek\". Drawings include designs for \"The Bridge\", \"The
  Engines\", \"Deck Plans\", \"Corridors\", \"Medical Instruments\". \"Phaser Guns\",
  \"Holodecks\", \"Alien Ships\" , and much more.\r\n\r\n<strong>UB Art Gallery at

  through July 25, 2008\r\n<strong>NLXL a design studio for visual communication and
  interaction</strong>\r\nNLXL experiments with the possibilities of combining visual
  and interactive elements within single design solutions. Content, form and technique
  are carefully considered in each and every solution. NLXL designs logos, corporate
  and visual identities, web sites and web-identities, event styles, forms, posters,
  animations, books, content management systems, digital presentations and other digital
  applications. The exhibition will feature graphic posters and an interactive, multimedia
  installation.\r\nhttp://ubartgalleries.buffalo.edu/pages/UBArtGallery/cfacurrent.shtml\r\n\r\n<strong>Albright-Knox
  Art Gallery </strong>(site of Friday\u2019s Eric Spiekermann talk)\r\n<strong>Op
  Art Revisited\r\nSelections from the Albright-Knox Art Gallery</strong>\r\nJuly
  18 \u2013 October 26, 2008\r\nOp art, also know as \u201COptical art,\u201D refers
  to the work of a group of abstract painters in the early 1960s who\u2014under the
  direction of artists Richard Anuszkiewicz, Bridget Riley, Julian Stanczak, and Victor
  Vasarely\u2014utilized parallel lines, concentric circles, and electric colors to
  create works that give the visual effect of afterimages and illusion of movement.
