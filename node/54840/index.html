Strange Didone from "He's Just Not That Into You" poster

February 16, 2009 • exfish

I was staring at a poster for this movie while waiting for the subway, and was struck by the odd lowercase g, y and the slightly-off serifs on the cap C and S. Identifont and Whatthefont weren’t much help, but I did learn that those lowercase oddities suggest a Vibert-influenced Didot. The closest I can find is [[http://www.typofonderie.com/alphabets/view/Ambroise|Ambroise]], but there are some definite differences (t, K, and j to name a few).

Any clues? Is this perhaps a custom mash-up of Didones?

Thanks!

—Noam

Is it? I don' see it in the PDF specimen. It’s not just the s. The cap K and lc k, f in Ambroise are funky in ways that this poster is not. The lc t has a different top, the Ambroise cap G has a downward-pointing spur, and all the ball terminals in the poster feel a tad more lachrymal than your average Didone.
