My understanding is that William Arthur developed some scripts to make its products distinctive from those of competitors. If this is in fact the case, I would have some doubts that you will find an exact match for T-184.

Your image is of a good size and contains information on the details of the script. I have done some editing to pop up this information and rearranged and resized it to fit in the small window provided by Typophile for viewing without significant scrolling.

This writing style is derived from the corsiva script developed in Vatican in 16th century. Compare the general letterforms to Cancelleresca Corsiva https://www.myfonts.com/fonts/flat-it/agedage-cancelleresca/ From the metal type era – and thus very simplified – see Hermann Zapf’s Zapf Chancery http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/itc/zapf-chancery/ For another very simplified – and rather boring – version see Monotype Corsiva https://www.myfonts.com/fonts/mti/monotype-corsiva/ Another in this general style Poetica http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/adobe/poetica/ Plus Felipa, a Google Web Font. For more fonts in this genre see http://www.identifont.com/find?font=Corsiva&q=Go and follow the links on the ringt hand side under the label “See more similar fonts.”

A little more interesting Goteborg https://www.myfonts.com/fonts/mti/goteborg/

A somewhat livelier substitute, Laura Worthington’s origins, https://www.myfonts.com/fonts/laura-worthington/origins/ which unfortunately is available only in a distressed/antiqued version.

I do hope another typophile finds a font that better fits the flavor of your sample.

Don
