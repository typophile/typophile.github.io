2007-08-04 15:08:33 • sandrosandro
It's just an on-screen rendering problem. As soon as it goes to (a modern) RIP it will be trapped automatically. What you could do to make absolutely sure that it won't be a problem, you could either mark the black text as overprint in Quark's attributes palette. Second alternative is to do it in Illustrator and give the black text a thin outline of the same colour and set the outline for overprint.
2007-08-04 15:27:29 • clauses
Select the type that is causing the problem, then look at the attributes window (making sure you have the fill box to the front), I suspect that the overprint box is not ticked, leave it unticked; if you are concerned there is a trap option in pathfinder alternatively add a fine stroke (0.125pt should do it) to the type, then tick the stroke as overprint, this will ensure that a mis-registration shouldn’t leave a white area around your type. Claus is correct most RIPs will deal with the trapping, for peace of mind ask the printer to provide you with a proof, even a post-rip pdf, which you can check and sign-off before it goes on press (you should do this for every job). More on trapping in CS3 http://livedocs.adobe.com/en_US/Illustrator/13.0/help.html?content=WS714a382cdf7d304e7e07d0100196cbc5f-6490.html Tim
