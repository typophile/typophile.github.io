1920s Emory Roth Building NYC - Carved in Stone Caps Serif

October 31, 2013 • talschori

1920s Building by Emory Roth in the Garment District in Manhattan. “FREIGHT” was carved over the freight entrance. Above the main entrance it once said “No. 135” but mid-century renovations have ruined the original details. I’m looking for the original font used, or something I could use as a replacement. More images of the original here: http://www.skyscraper.org/EXHIBITIONS/URBAN_FABRIC/wall06.php

Thanks!

Perhaps a redrawing of the Trajan Roman monumental letters by Emory Roth? It was a point of pride for architects of the era to design their own lettering and not use something off the shelf. But then again there may be a font from the era or earlier with exactly these letters. don
