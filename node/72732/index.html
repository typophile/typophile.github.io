A rant from work.

July 29, 2010 • bstrom

They just keep watering down design here. I wanted to use Trade Gothic Bold Condensed No. 20 Oblique for branding on our presence at a conference. My boss told me to simply take Interstate and add faux italics, making it “close enough.” They are still miles apart. Gaaaaaaaaaah.

Interstate is great, don’t get me wrong, but its used on all our literature. I truly do not believe that using a different font for a temporary logo would undermine our corporate identity. I believe that half-assing design by using faux-italics is more likely to undermine our identity.

I am bothered by two things:

  1. Suggesting that a faux-italic Interstate works the same as my Trade Gothic, and
  2. Suggesting that I should adhere to #1. The fact that I am now expected to do this is infuriating to me as a designer. The most frustrating thing? I know I am right, but I don’t have the stones to say anything about it. How can I ever be a good graphic designer if I’m so damn passive aggressive…
Uh, why not just get the bold condensed italic style of Interstate?
