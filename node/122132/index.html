Edited: The closest digital font that I can find for the lettering _JOSÉ E VIEIRA DE SOUZA_ is Michael Hagemann's Livery Stable [img:sites/default/files/old-images/LiveryStableAsJOSÉ_E_VIEIRA_ DE_SOUZA_4862.jpg] http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/fontmesa/livery-stable/ This is a revival of the nineteenth-century typeface Glyphic, by Hermann Ihlenburg at MS&J, 1878, which does not mention the source by name. Alan Meeks did a knock off of the design in 1988 under the name Algerian [img:sites/default/files/old-images/AlgerianLETAsJOSÉ_E_VIEIRA_ DE_SOUZA_4746.jpg] Michael Hagemann's version, Algerian Mesa, adds a lowercase http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/fontmesa/algerian-mesa/ These designs are embellished versions of a widely used 19th century font with triangular serifs called "Latin." Consider the digitization as Latin MT Condensed [img:sites/default/files/old-images/LatinMTCondAsJOSÉ_E_VIEIRA_ DE_SOUZA_5434.jpg] http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/latin/ Note that the lettering has slightly softened serifs.
