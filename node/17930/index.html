I think your basic approach is sound and some of the letter combinations are working well, as you say patience and perserverance are going to be required, you could look at this thread for some ideas. The approach I take is to draw the letters from one direction, turn the page and amend to work the letters from another direction, trace it and start the process again, whether I am working on paper or direct on the computer. Combining letters or using swashes to create a letter* from the opposite direction can be productive, especially when you have different number of characters in the two words.

Keep at it, Tim

2006-02-17 00:31:24 • K

I think you’re onto something with your ‘k’ becoming an ‘is’. Just needs a little straightening out to make it work with the other characters.

I think you could eliminate the stroke leading into the a in Francois and bend the bowl of the a back to the right a bit to make the a more obviously an a without compromising the n. I believe the o in Francois could also get rounder as long as you keep that incoming stroke that makes it an a. Finally, the F might read better both as F and E if the top horizontal was not detached from the stem.

It’s very cool to see sketches in progress - save these when you’ve arrived at a result, they’re great. And don’t give up hope, you’re almost there.
