  It!</a></b>\r\n\r\n<b>Reviewed by Poltroon:</b>\r\nThis book started out to show
  how Dadaism and concrete poetry were foreshadowed in the anonymous nonsense texts
  of type specimen books in the nineteenth century and grew into a much larger cultural
  study of the reading habits, politics and amusements of that era. The type specimens
  (many of which are illustrated) are a springboard to a wider discussion of many
  topics from hermits to the alcoholism of printers. Most of the fun occurs in the
  footnotes (there is even a fake footnote that can be deciphered as a reference to
  PALE FIRE by Nabokov). There are checklists of rare specimens in public & private
  collections in Britiain & the US which can be consulted & very useful family trees
  showing how the holdings of various typefounders passed from hand to hand. In addition
  new light is shed on the earliest American wood types cut for display in the 1830s
  as well as some interesting stuff on the graphic appeal of large type.<br>\r\n\r\n<b><i>Do
