Does anyone know how the term “justified” came to describe text set with both a left and right flush margin?

Thanks in advance to any combination language and type history buffs.

Justify > Adjust > Align are all closely related, so that justification CAN mean alignment to left, right or center, or alignment of both margins. “Justify to center” is perhaps a little archaic (or at least arcane, since “justified” has come to mean type with both left and right margins aligned.
