Maybe this will help. I use a digital microscope for imaging those small specimens; for larger type such as 18 point and above I use an 8mp digital camera in Macro mode. Then I do post-processing in Photoshop to achieve what I want to work with. I also only use specimens printed from original metal (hopefully not too worn) but none that have been reproduced by offset printing.

You may find Zapf’s Palatino interesting. The original metal cut was actually quite lively and there’s an incomplete digitalization (look for Palatino BQ). Then along came Linotype, digitalized Palatino and it seems that ever since we’re stuck with a somewhat flat and limp version of the original metal cut. Sad, really… Not sure if this helps but it’s a nice illustration…
