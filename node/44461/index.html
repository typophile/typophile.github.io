The one that seems to have come from the Church is 'chapel,' which, [[http://www.metaltype.co.uk/stories/story18.shtml|as this explains]] is a society or group of compositors. The Oxford Concise Dictionary has it as the members of a printers union at a place of work. According to [[http://www.oakknoll.com/detail.php?d_booknr=11631&d_currency=|this book]] the printer's chapel was a precursor of modern trade unions. As printing was the first means of mass production, this makes sense...
