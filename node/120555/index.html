William T. Sniffin’s design of Rivioli for American Typefounders is essentially a clone of Rudolf Koch’a Antiqua/Eva with the addition of the echo lines. It seems clear to me that the engraver adapted the Antiqua/Eva/Rivoli design for the Pyrenees logo. BTW, the original Rivoli series, as shown in the American Typefounders catalog of 1934, also had a very attractive Italic which AFAIK has not been digitized. The Italic included alternate swash capitals for A, B, D, E, G, H and a half dozen more letters. IMHO Rudolf Koch was one of the greatest type designers of the 20th century. His designs, including this one, are definitely not industrial or mechanical. Don
