Not only is kerning more an art than a science, opinions differ, with few completely “right” or “wrong” answers. With really bad kerning, you might get 98 out of 100 people to agree it is bad.

As Stephen Coles pointed out, text fonts are kerned at text sizes. Moreover, foundry kerning has to assume any mix of letters. WA has to be kerned for a following “L” or a following “V”; only one value permitted in the font.

The rule at our shop (composition) is that display type must always be hand kerned. No font-based kerning can cover the all the sizes, the variations in letters used, the variations in the number of words in the title. These are all at play.
