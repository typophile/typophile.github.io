Strange letter replacement in PDF-files.

September 14, 2007 • Goran Soderstrom

A very strange thing happened on a font we are working on:

Sometimes – when we do PDF-files the print of the PDF looks like this:

In InDesign, the font chosen is the regular, but notice that the a u r t and s are bold. And that the ligature is italic.

In the PDF (on screen) everything looks good.

When we print from MacOSX preview everything look good.

But… when we print from Acrobat some letters are randomly replaced by bold or italic letters. It looks very strange.

In this font there is an old “Type 1 Unique ID number” taken from the serif version of the font. Could that be the issue? It is an OTF we are producing.

Anyone seen this before?

Out of curiosity, did you manage to solve the problem?

Artur

2007-09-19 13:10:28 • Artur Schmal

Remove both UniqueID and XUID numbers from the OTFs, these are not required.

See Thomas Phinney’s last post here: http://www.typophile.com/node/35480

2007-09-19 13:20:57 • k.l.

I’ve had the same thing once with Type 1 fonts way back. Screen appearance was fine but on print it regular, smallcaps and italic were randomly mixed. Removing conflicting ID’s solved the issue. There’s no need for them now anymore, so you might as well completely remove them.

Artur

edit: Ofcourse ‘conflicting ID’s’ didn’t solve the issue, removing them is what solved the issue. :)

2007-09-20 08:40:13 • Goran Soderstrom

From my Mac OSX - I have the same problem with Helvetica Neue Extended Thin – the word Institute - the I always comes out fatter than the rest and looks like a different font.

The only solution I could come up with the get the job out and be able to use it as a “logo” was to open it up in Illustrator, outline it - and adjust the I so it looked like it should have looked from the beginning.

I know, not much of a solution, but it has saved me a lot of grief, since I do a lot of work for these folks.
