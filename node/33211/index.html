2007-04-21 16:38:09 • hrant
It's nice calligraphy, although I've always thought one of calligraphy's niceties is that it's not as mechanical as typography. You can't yet (that I know of) control thinks like ink flow and coverage in existing font standards. Imagine the interesting things we could see if you not only defined shapes and outlines for fonts but also opacities or stroke directions. But individual variation between letters, spacing, etc — often in spite of an attempted supression thereof, at least in Latinate calligraphy, Arabic and Asian of course being very different stories — that's what gives calligraphy it's "charm". Type, like I said, is pretty mechnical, even though we can fake having it lose that mechanical property. At least, this is how I've always related the two. Now my turn to be rude :) ... 2007-04-22 02:25:47 • guifa
I like the 'meaty' quality of your calligraphic type. You should compare your effort to other typographic versions of the Carolingian miniscule. Most of them are more sparkly, so there may be a place for this as a display face. For type and consistency of spacing you have to look out for things like the terminal on the u being much longer than on the a, which presents problems for spacing. The a is wider than it would be traditionally, and maybe too wide.
