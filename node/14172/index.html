Kodachrome was indeed king. I spent several years in the audiovisual business, preparing multi-screen, multi-projector slide shows (yet ANOTHER skill/profession that I once practiced that has vanished). For the vast majority of our work (graphics), we always used Ektachrome slide film because it was cheaper and faster/easier to process, and we could easily correct for the distinctly bluish cast the film had. But, for the money shots (people and products), we always used Kodachrome, because of its brilliant (and much truer) color rendition.
