Uh, sorry but why are you setting that text at 7pt? I wouldn't go any smaller than 9-10pt tops. There are 2 types of business cards... fancy ones and useful ones. Sadly, Real Estate cards fall into the latter category. So, you'll have to get clever if you want to create a well-designed but still useful business card. Have you considered icons or abbreviation, instead of extra text? Also consider condensed versions of a font over a reduced type size. Business cards need to be readable, sadly that is one of that caveats of designing them. You might also want to consider a font with a fairly large x-height and contrast, so you can use a smaller text size while not reducing readability. Have you tried a vertical layout as well? There are also options when it comes to printing... a folded card, doubles the amount of space... and allows for a more stylish design. Even printing double sided can open up room. Consider your other options before resorting to reduced readability.

Well, if I were to suggest some fonts, I think Hoelfer & Frere-Jones have a few nice ones. Like Mercury or Chronicle. I also like Gerard Unger's Swift. All have nice large x-heights and suitable contrast. If you're looking for a sans I'd suggest, Jeremy Tankard's Bliss or Benton Sans by Morris Fuller Benton, or even Vista Sans at Emigre. I'm not sure the direction you're going style wise, so it's hard to really suggest something and know if it's suitable for what you're trying to do. I know you said you like Scala Sans, that has nice contrast, but the x-height isn't super large, so going small like 7pt will be difficult to read. A word to the wise, if you mix condensed with regular, you can get some very nice contrasts between information. A line like "Phone: (123) 456-7890", you'd use a bold condense for the "Phone:" and a regular non-condense for the numbers, if they're old-style numbers, even better. Well used color, even muted, can also help make important data pop.
