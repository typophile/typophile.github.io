Does anyone recognize the typeface used in the titles for the new Robin Hood? While I know that designers of titles and credits for the movies will often create their own, I’m quite sure I’ve seen this one before.

Only caps and small caps are used. One of the variant forms includes a capital R with an exceptionally long leg, which sometimes forces a smaller version of the letter following it; and inside the O is a dot, which to my eye looks diamond-shaped. So I thought of Adobe Jensen, which uses diamonds in its dotted punctuation marks, and the D and the H are similar, though in the titles the H looks taller, narrower. And there are no variations of the R or O that have those special features. In the closing credits, I also noticed a variant M, in which the three legs were roughly parallel but the tops were rounded, and sloped downward to the right – a distinctly medieval form, though it wasn’t used frequently in the credits.

There are illuminations in some of the bowls, though I assume this was done by hand rather than it being a regular feature of the font.

I’ve attached a screen grab of the title, but couldn’t find any photos of the credits, where most of the variation took place. Does it look familiar to anyone?

I have been spending some time searching antique serifs and found one font with center diamond-ish marks (Brogue), but almost nothing else about it seems even similar. The font 1592 GLC Garamond seems to have a similar overall feel, except for the diamonds. It seems the R could be made using one of the Q tails, but I think it’s not too likely we will find the exact font, because I have a feeling that they mixed and matched from several fonts and then customized with noise, squooshing and antiquing tricks.

Usually when I get nowhere at the commercial sites, I check Dafont to see what the amateurs have done, and found a remarkable bargain (how can you beat free?) by Igino Marini called IM Fell Types, a 16-font family based on old English typeforms. There seems to be about 3 or 4 basic typefaces and at least one or two include SC versions. This ought to have enough variants to work with.

Bottom line: I don’t think this is a single font that you’ll be able to find, because I think it was cobbled together for this purpose. If you just want to make something that looks similar, that should be easy enough.
