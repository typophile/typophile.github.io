IF the "o" was turned red to indicate an open contour when you first generated the font, and you have corrected the problem, you will have to manually change the red indicator by selecting the glyph and choosing "Mark" from the contextual menu (right click or control click on a mac with a one button mouse) and selecting "None" from that menu. The red indicator can also mean an incorrect Unicode assignment, but that red warning looks different than the one in your example. Slash and backslash always have to be typed twice, since typing them once is a prelude to other actions in FontLab. James

1. gaps between glyph boxes, though I snapped the metrics to each box. If you mean the thin line between boxes: No kerning required, better make boxes a little wider than the sidebearings, say 5 units more to each side. In the Metrics Panel you can check that each left & right sidebearing is -5. It may be that in some apps still a white line appears on screen, but in print it should be ok.

Here are some recommendation from Microsoft regarding the thin line between boxes. "When designing connecting glyphs to avoid pixel dropout caused by rounding, it is necessary to provide for a minimum of a 70 font unit overlap when a 2048 em square (recommended value) is used for TrueType outlines. The amount of 70 units will allow for at least the width of the character to be maintained during the rasterization process at all PPEM sizes." ref: http://www.microsoft.com/typography/CursiveScriptGuidelines.mspx Best regards, Atif

Make a new font in FL. Digitize the x and y bounds of the whole em in the font's "n" glyph. Generate a font and compose that n in illustrator. Scale the Em to the size you want it in Illustrator. Draw or place each glyph Staying Inside the lines. Import glyph and em. Delete Em. Hope is, this helps. Cheers!
