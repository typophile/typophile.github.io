The image is small scale so I can't make out the details of the lower case. A better view of g would be useful So here are some possibilities.Triumph is a British product in business since 1903. Perhaps a British font in 1966? The letter forms available in 1966 suggest that this could perhaps be a sloped and edited variation of Coronation, Stevenson Blake c. 1937. [designer?]. But the M is not right. [img:sites/default/files/old-images/coronation@20_5634.jpg] Somewhat similar were Corvinius, designed by Imre Reiner for Bauer 1929-34 which had lighter serif features on T and E. Also somewhat similar is Eden, designed by R.H.Middleton in 1934. As for the g Coronation or Corvinius might match, but not Eden I did not come across any digital versions of any of these fonts as an italic. Don
