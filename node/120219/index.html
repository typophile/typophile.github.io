Don’t know the typeface, but as a german speaker I can give some corrections to the translations:

The priority in the shown spread is on the frames. The blurry line reads »Einfassung Serie 613 (or G13?) 9 Cicero. Geschützt« which means »Frames serie 613 (or G13?) 9 Cicero Trademarked«. So the frames are what it is all about.

The sujet itself should be an (virtual?) ad to a hotel named »Gold’ne Traube« (Golden Grape), occupied obviuosly by Woldemar Bodenbach. The text reads:

Goldne Traube (Golden Grape) Vornehmstes Fremdenhaus (Most exclusive Hotel [literally «strangers house«]) Großer Konzertpark – Herrliche Umgebung (Big concert park – Magnificent surroundings) Direkt am Bahnhofe – Eigene Weinkelterei (Next to the railway station – Own winery) Woldemar Bodenbach

The typeface itself reminds me a bit of Eckmann …

I believe that I completely misinterpreted the source of my original image. From looking at the images immediately before and after in the photostream I now interpret this as a page from a Schelter & Geisecke specimen book at the Tipoteca Italiana museum rather than a page from a Tipoteca Italiana specimen book. So the font might have been produced by Schelter & Geisecke as a variation of its own Edelgotisch. Based on information in Ralf Hermann’s page – Thanks Renko for the reference – it might be that Albert Knab designed the Edelgotisch familar to us and this was produced by Schelter & Giesecke in 1901. The foundry produced a schmalfette [narrow bold?] version in 1904 . I suspect that the specimen I am seeking to identify is in fact this schmalfette version. Don
