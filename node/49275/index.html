I’m a little confused. In the example, there doesn’t seem to a full blank line between paragraphs, only extra space. In these cases, for lines to line up across columns, the space after paragraph has to equal the leading or a whole number times the leading. / Make sure your Align to Baseline Grid button (in the Paragraph panel or Control panel) is pressed. / By the way, in your example, I would go with no extra spacing and an indented first line (indentation equal to body size). This makes it clear to the reader that he’s looking at a coherent text, not a collection of excerpts, and it makes sure that the reader is never in doubt whether or not there is paragraph break hidden in a given column or page break coinciding with a period.

2008-09-09 23:02:46 • khomlong

Another option, if you prefer the look of space between paragraphs, is to use a blank line of half the leading. (Personally, I’d probably set it up as Space Before, rather than a hard return). You can also then set your Baseline Grid to half the leading to facilitate layout.

By using exactly half the leading, you’ll get a fairly pleasing syncopation between your columns: lines will either be exactly aligned or a half beat off, coming in and out of alignment in a regular rhythm with each paragraph.

This can be an acceptable scheme in some layouts. You won’t be able to count on aligning the bottoms of all columns, so it works best with a looser, less formal page design.
