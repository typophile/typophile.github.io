2005-08-05 06:11:47 • Joe Pemberton

This thread is all over the place but for what it’s worth, I know someone who bought one and this is what he had to say:

Well so far it’s been just trying to say something new about what is essentially just a mouse.

Ran the installer which required 152mb of space?!! Mighty is not the word. Restarted and set up the preferences to use the scroll ball as Expose–> Show desktop and the side buttons to Expose–>Show all windows

The scroll ball is quite springy in that pressing down. I was expecting it to be quite rigid but any amount of force will press it down into it’s hole. I would wonder about the durability of this as it does seem mounted on a soft spring. The side buttons do require a good squeeze between the thumb and ringfinger. The sound they emit is very quiet though the scroll ball sound is very realistic

I have put up the tacking speed on everything to it’s highest as I like a fast mouse. The right click action is identical to the action I use on the intellimouse. Been using it in Photoshop/Preview in place of the usual spacebar action to move around the document and it works well though a bit jerkier.It means a quicker workflow in some instances.

The short cord is actually good for powerbook users as I use it on the right with the cord plugged in on the left hand side. It definitely would be awkard plugging it into a g5 under the desk and using it that way.

All in all a good mouse for creative work but I’ll have to road test it a bit more.

He’s an icon designer, if you were wondering.
