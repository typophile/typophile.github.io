2003-11-21 17:23:49 • jamesaz
James, a lot of graphic designers (and all major design associations) do frown on spec work. It devalues the work, invites amateurism, and opens the artist to all kinds of intellectual-property vulnerabilities. Design contests are often tolerated when run by nonprofit organizations, but that's about it.

Good design involves more than just coming up with a neat idea. It requires research, investigation, and getting to know the client. If you want good design, hire professionals and pay them for their time and work.

Something I often suggest to potential clients with limited budgets is to approach local design schools with their project proposals. Some schools will take on business projects as class efforts, give design students school credit for taking on such work, or suggest student designers who might be able to work within the client's budget limitations.
