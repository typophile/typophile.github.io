Are you sure the newspaper’s name isn’t Kvällsposten?

The typeface looks like some home-brewn Futura to me – probably custom(ized). If you are looking for a similar retail font with such compact diacritics, see Scangraphic’s Supertypes Series, labeled with SH – e.g. Futura SH. This series started in the 1980, i.e. before OpenType. The compact alternates are still not properly encoded as alternates, but can be accessed via ~ (tilde) for Å and { for Ö. In the early 1990s, URW released its Poster series. In Futura P Extrabold, such integrated accents are the default.

Albert-Jan Pool kindly provided me with this information for my recently published article on unusual umlauts for TypoJournal.
