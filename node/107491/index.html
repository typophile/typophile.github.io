Lettering, nothing digital anywhere close to this. Simplified Fraktur, like your sample, was produced in many variations before WW II. A few were made into fonts. You might be interested in National by Walter Hoehnisch, 1933-1935: Convert some the harsh angles into curving brush strokes and you are in the ball park.
The war with the Nazis stigmatized these font designs. But time passes and there is an increasing appreciation of the the qualities of blackletter fonts. Perhaps a digital revival may soon be produced that would have some similarity to your image. Don
