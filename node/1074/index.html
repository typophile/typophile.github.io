2003-09-03 07:50:10 • cheshiredave
>I'm sorry to ask what may seem like a stupid question to most of you, but does this mean that if it were a commercial font, it wouldn't violate any licenses? No. If it wasn't being distributed at all, *then* it probably wouldn't violate any licenses. (Adobe has a generous EULA regarding derivatives.) If it was a commercial font, then it would still probably be a violation, unless the creator got proper permissions from Adobe. (Of course, this could be true of a freely distributed font, but I highly doubt it.) This all assumes that the font in question was derived directly from the original Adobe Garamond outline data. Which I suspect. If it was merely scanned and then autotraced and then tortured, then there's probably little legal grounds, but that doesn't make it right, imo. >Clod Garamond :-> – K.
