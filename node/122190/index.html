Please help identity this typeface from a scanned copy of John Ruskin’s The Stones of Venice. Any typeface similar to this would be very helpful as well.

The work is a three-part series originally published from 1851 to 1853. Without knowing which edition this is from, hard to ID the metal typeface.

Scotch Modern was popular at the time of the book’s first publication, but your sample lacks the thick/thin contrast common to the face: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/shinn/scotch-modern/

Among contemporary digital typefaces, a condensed version of Century or Clarion might do: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/adobe/itc-century/ http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/bitstream/century-725/ http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/mti/clarion-mt/

Madison Antiqua is another option: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/madison-antiqua/
