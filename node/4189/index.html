Don't know if it's too basic for your needs, but at an educational price of 99 USD, the Adobe Type Classics for Learning CD would be hard to beat.

About 400 fonts in all, including all of Adobe's Pro families, including "Opticals", except, I think, Kepler and Brioso. (Might be wrong about that.) So: Minion, Adobe Jenson, Cronos, Warnock, Utopia, Sanvito, Chapparral. Also includes the non-optical Pro families, including Adobe Caslon and Adobe Garamond, Myriad, Lithos, Trajan. And quite a few others.

There's a fair amount of overlap with the fonts bundled with the Creative Suite package (which does include Brioso Pro). The educational price for the "Premium Suite" is just under 400 USD.

The $99 is the price for a single user for Type Classics for Learning (unlike most Adobe font products, it's not a 5-user license). However, if you're licensing for multiple users, I think it goes down to $50 per user after the first. You'd have to contact your educational software purchasing folks for details.

I believe Victor's right about Kepler and Brioso not being on Type Classics; they were released after Type Classics shipped.
