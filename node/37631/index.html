2007-09-29 22:19:56 • Stephen Rapp
Good point that you would only need a single "00" ligature, if that's really what you want. You don't want small numbers for everything? And if you have numerators or superiors that suit the purpose, I think you could just use those, e.g. sub period @figures' by @figures_superior; sub period @figures_superior @figures' by @figures_superior; (I'm leaving out extra context, like preceding currency and numbers, like Georg wrote, but you can put as much of that in as necessary.) One thing to keep in mind is that different countries write such figures differently -- such as using a comma where we in the U.S.A. use a period. Your font could very well end up annoying people in Europe! In theory, you could make different contextual substitution rules depending on the particular currency symbol that's used, but in my experience, these tricky kinds of things usually end up getting too complicated -- and one ends up making the substitution discretionary (i.e 'dlig') instead of on by default (i.e. 'liga'). It seems like there might be a more clever way to do all this, but rarely does such cleverness come to me immediately. :-)
