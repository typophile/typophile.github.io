Where to buy?

August 3, 2008 • ferry

Hey all, I'm looking to purchase Miller Headline for my student paper, but I don't know where to buy it. The Font Bureau offered us a student discount, but it's still not too cheap. On the other hand, the FontShop offers Miller Display in more styles and for less cash. What's the difference here, am I missing something important? Dave
The price should be about the same. The Font Bureau discounts as you add more weights (you may have missed volume discounts in their cart). And both outfits offer a student discount. In other words, you won't get these fonts cheaper no matter where you buy it. Just shop where you feel comfortable.
2008-08-03 10:41:17 • Stephen Coles
The primary difference between Miller Headline and Miller Display is that the Headline version is narrower (about 92%) and fitted more tightly to better suit — what else? — newspaper headlines (originally for the Boston Globe, I believe). Miller Headline is not part of the Retail library, so you won't find it available for license from any of FB's resellers. Here's a direct comparison of the two (Display above, Headline below).
