A good thing they are drifting away from Helvetica.

Erik Spiekermann (cofounder of the FontShop) says that building a custom typeface is much cheaper for Apple than licensing one.

“If you get a complete character set, including Cyrillic and what have you, it’s way, way more expensive to pay licenses. If you’re talking about millions of units at a cent each, that’s a few hundred thousand dollars. You can design a hell of a typeface for that money."

Also there is branding. “That’s been my other criticism about using Helvetica—that’s not their brand,” Spiekermann says. “Anybody can imitate that.” Not so with a custom typeface.

(He also mentions that he was offered a job at Apple this summer but turned it down because he’s retired.)
