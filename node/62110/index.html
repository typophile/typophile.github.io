2009-09-19 17:04:27 • nina
The price of something in a particular market is determined in part by ability and willingness to pay, which is why the Internet might function as a global delivery system but not as a global market: ability and willingness to pay remain regional. Companies like Adobe have European marketing and sales staff or associates, and they determine the price for the local market, based on their experience of what buyers in that market are able and willing to pay. Sure, not everyone in any market is able or willing to pay the asking price, but the pricing is based on maintaining some number of sales in given time period. If not enough sales are made, the price will go down, but as long as the product is selling at the current price that price will be maintained or may go up. It may seem unfair that people in North America pay less for a lot of things, but then it seems unfair to a lot of us that Europeans get such long holidays.

I have more information in regards to the transfer of Adobe Font Folio.

I emailed: Section 4.4 of the 5-seat US English EULA for Font Folio 11 seems to say that as long as the package hasn’t been opened one could transfer the license to the Font Folio. Am I wrong? And could this be done across borders?
