Hello, could anyone identify this font, please? It's from the John Ford's film "The man who shot Liberty Valance". Thanks in advance! David
Not a typeface, but hand lettering (as was most poster display type of the era). The lettering appears to be based on an old woodtype style called Latin: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/mti/latin/ Some digital typefaces with a similar vibe (but by no means a match to your sample): http://www.houseind.com/fonts/edbenguiatfonts/ [Ed Roman] http://www.google.com/fonts/specimen/Fontdiner+Swanky

AFAIK nobody has made this lettering into a font. The capital letters are fairly standard Roman letters with pulled out serifs. Fonts that include this feature include Ronaldson [img:sites/default/files/old-images/RonaldsonAsTMW_5765.gif] http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/canadatype/ronaldson/ and Tiffany [img:sites/default/files/old-images/TiffanyMedAsTMW_5064.gif] http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/itc/tiffany/ The other letters are an unusual mix of roman letters with big ball terminals and sloped letters. For some ideas see Everafter Bold [img:sites/default/files/old-images/EverafterBoldAs_anho_4640.gif] http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/typetrust/everafter/ and Oxtail Black [img:sites/default/files/old-images/OxtailBlackAs_anho__5811.gif] http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/macrhino/oxtail/ Don
