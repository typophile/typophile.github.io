Break-in & subsequent break-down

September 10, 2004 • jlt

My home was burgled on Wednesday when my wife and I were at our respective offices, as we frequently are mid-day. In addition to years worth of Typographica archives on my laptop and a few unbacked-up client folders, I lost all my photographs - including much of what I shot at Typecon. Of course, the world being what it is and I being who I am (and thus probably worthy of whatever negative karma is hanging over my head), I had just taken down the .mac galleries with my own & others pictures.

I imagine it's far too much to ask & that nobody could possibly have them, but did anyone actually download any/many of the pictures that i took?

Also, if anyone sees the following floating around, they were also stolen: a huge collection of Criterion edition Kurosawa, Hitchcock, Godard & Truffaut DVDs; the Buster Keaton box set from Kino; the Harold Lloyd collection; lots of Japanese & Korean contemporary films on DVD; Kieslowski's Decalogue on DVD and his Red/White/Blue on DVD.. And of course my entire camera collection incl. various old manual Nikons, rare rangefinders, my Leica 24mm Elmarit lens, and the Canon Powershot s50 that I bought recently and carry with me everywhere. Just, you know, in case you see it around.
