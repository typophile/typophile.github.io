Don’t get me wrong, I like the approach that you are taking with the logo mark type. I thought immediately of the Reebok(RBK) logo, but so what. <rbk.com>

I’m guessing you have drawn the type yourself, and think before you go much further you need to address the aesthetic a bit. The left side of the ‘A’ looks really heavy compared to the ‘B’ and ‘K’ also the bottom curve on the ‘B’ looks a fraction disjointed from the rest of the character. I think ‘white’ spaces are just as impostant as the ‘black’ marks, they need to relate to each other, this is seen in the horizontal white space you have created and the difference in space you have between each letter. In a similar way curves also often look better when they are consistant with each other.

After that, I think you will naturally find your search for a subtext font an easier process.
