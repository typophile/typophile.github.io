I think it has been done with 3 different fonts:

  • ISEN: I can’t be affirmative but something like [[http://new.myfonts.com/search/bell+gothic|Bell Gothic]] (or even more [[http://new.myfonts.com/search/letter+gothic/|Letter Gothic]]could do the trick at small size). [[http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/scriptorium/onuava/|Onuava]] is also close. Having a look to their “similars” at MyFonts could be helpful too: [[http://new.myfonts.com/morelike/40300/|here]], [[http://new.myfonts.com/morelike/40362/|here]] and [[http://new.myfonts.com/morelike/29887/|here]]
  • Brest: first thought was “freebie” or at least, custom from an existing font “not-so-well-designed”. Could be [[http://www.dafont.com/yorkville.font|Yorkville]]
  • école d’ingénieurs: looks like Myriad but I’m probably wrong. The other solution would be to switch to their actual corporate typeface, [[http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/t26/target/|T-26]], as it could be seen on [[http://www.isen.fr/|their main website]] and use Myriad as complementary.
