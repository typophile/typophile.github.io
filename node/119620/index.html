Anyone know why the 'W' sits slightly below the baseline? Ie next to the A Just designing a logotype. Going to Align it but just thought I would ask as it obviously is intended to do that? Thanks heaps Barry
This is what we call “overshot”. Round and pointy shapes need to extended slightly above and below square shapes to look equal to our eyes. Put an H next to them, and you’ll see that the A also extends.
2014-09-17 09:18:17 • Frode Bo Helland
While overshoots below the baseline and above the cap height for letters like ‘O’ are sort of universal in well-designed typefaces, there is more variability in letters like ‘W’ (below the baseline) or ‘A’ (above the cap height). Checking some widely used serif typefaces, I found that about two thirds have an overshooting ‘A’ and somewhat fewer have an overshooting ‘W’. FF More by Łukasz Dziedzic is an example that has neither of the two; Utopia by Robert Slimbach only has a very slightly overshooting ‘A’. Obviously, the presence of overshoots depends on the design: A typeface like Garamond Premier Pro, also by Robert Slimbach, wouldn’t work without considerable overshoots in both letters. In sans-serif typefaces, overshoots are a lot less common—for a reason. As Frode said, overshoots are meant to optically compensate for round or pointy shapes, but the top of ‘A’ or the bottom of ‘W’ are neither round nor pointy in many sans-serifs. Nothing to compensate for, thus. I think the designers of Gotham are overcompensating here. Mark Simonson would probably agree because in his Proxima Nova, a typeface with a design quite similar to Gotham, ‘A’ and ‘W’ don’t show any overshoots.

If you are going to create size-specific adaptations of the wordmark, you could leave the overshoot in the smallest versions (where it may invisibly improve the perceived vertical position) and remove it from a certain size up (where it will be visible to the naked eye). As I said, I think it is an overcompensation in this typeface and weight (but the designers of Gotham might know better), so if you only have one version of your logo, I would remove the overshoot.
