Driving me mental

August 28, 2004 • glutton

Got a client who designed her packaging with "Kabob Black" – some mystery font. She had included the font with the keyline (done in Acrobat) but it was a PC font. I looked it up on the web and it was on a free font download site, PC only. Well, I looked at that "2" and said, wait a sec, this is fricking Kabel! Yes indeed, they used a knockoff Kabel for their packaging. Which was cool because we had Kabel so I could substitute one for the other. So I send an email to the client and urge her to simply license her own damn copy of Kabel – she had been running into problems with Kabob with printers and other design agencies. Her response? Maybe we could just use something else that's free. Woman, it costs $29 to license one weight of Kabel. It won't break the bank. I just had to vent.
We did the exactly the same thing with a cover set in that godawful Creampuff: we purchased a license for Eclat and used the proper typeface for the posters, banners, ads and whatnot. I understand your feelings, this sucks big time.

John, you probably have done this already, but perhaps suggest that working with free fonts can present more trouble than its worth (as her experience already attests) and show her a simple cost breakdown like: New font: $30 for x number of seats/devices My time: $100/hour for 1 hour fixing problems. Your SAVINGS: $70 (more if you add time and trouble at prepress or printers) Bad fonts = bad business Rip-off fonts = false economy Spend wisely Personally, I don't think free (speech/beer) fonts are bad, I think bad fonts and rip-offs are.
