Unless another font copied the numbers, I agree that most of the numbers in the first sample indeed match Walbaum Roman But the 3 is round top, which does not match Walbaum. Also the Italic 4 has a gap in the upright stroke, which does not match Walbaum Todays trivia: Walbaum numbers were very popular for clock face-plates during the 19th and early 20th centuries. Don
2015-02-02 22:48:18 • donshottype
All of the regular numbers seem to match an Intertype font called Waverley (1941). I don’t have a sample for Waverley Italic. Jaspert notes that Waverley is very similar to Walbaum, which together with the numbers, prompts me to think that it is fairly likely that Waverley is the font used in your book. Don
