Charge for Printing?

July 11, 2010 • evilfansanfran

When you guys have some things printed for a client, do you charge a fee if you find the printer, or do you just include it in the cost. Like carpenters get a cut of all materials bought.
If you pay for printing costs you should be marking the cost up 5–10% to cover your associated labor, costs, the risk you take of losing the money if the client doesn’t like the final product, and the time your money is tied on the line while you wait for the client to pay you back. If the client doesn’t want to pay your markup, you give them the files on CD and tell them to deal with printing themselves.

I would keep time & expenses entirely separate. If you need to spend time picking a printer, getting printing bids, doing a press check, etc then there’s no reason to hide that in your markup. It’s a legitimate part of your design fee.

Expenses such as printing should be itemized separately on your invoice, and it’s customary to add a markup (in the ballpark of 15% is fairly common). And always mention the markup in your estimate (“a standard markup of x% will be added to all expenses”) so there are no misunderstandings later.
