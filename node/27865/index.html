I am looking for some typefaces to jazz up Fournier for an extended publication. Every schoolboy knows that Fournier-le-Jeune designed dozens of typefaces in many sizes and weights, and that only a fortunate handful of these have been in use outside their first appearance. I have been using Fournier STD for some time, and it’s great. But I need more type to spice it up a bit. I know that there were several decorative faces cut for his type manuals, but I can only find one that has been digitized: http://www.p22.com/lanston/products/fournier.html

If anyone knows of any other early French transitional/rationalist decorated type, or other historically faithful digitizations of any of the Fournier family’s designs, that would be really helpful. I think there are some similarly decorated designs by Bodoni some of which might also work. I know he did some display designs and other oddities for his Manuale Tipografico, for example a design resembling the type shown here, but without the horizontal lines in the fill: http://www.discogs.com/viewimages?what=L&obid=52249
