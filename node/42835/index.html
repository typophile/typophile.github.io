Hi there, about a decade ago AIGA sponsored an exhibit on engraved letterhead and the accompanying catalog is the best I have ever seen. Today, my graphic design business is devoted to engraving which is even finer than letterpress for beautiful type. Buddy up with an engraving company (there are few anymore so find one now before they all go away.) FYI: In America, the trade term "engraving" refers to any intaglio printing process but most is actually etched, not engraved. You can go to my website for some looks and tips. Hi Nancy, Wow! I have been on your site and I love it! I bookmarked it. The fine details on illustrations... Metal plates are optimal for fine details and don't get busted by pressure. As you know today's letterpress goes mostly with photopolymer plates. In my case I realized that photopolymer is good, if the thinner details are not smaller then 0.3 of a point. When reproducing screens, the line screen amount is low, around 50 lpi. I remember back in the seventies the shop I worked for used brass plates for long run jobs, and also for fine detail and screened jobs.
