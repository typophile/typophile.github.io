2003-11-01 18:12:47 • Grant Hutchinson
David Carson did indeed start GarageFonts circa 1993, with his former intern/employee/something-like-that, graphic designer Betsy Kopshina. Betsy's husband, Norbert Schulz, became involved at some point, not sure if was at the very beginning or a bit later.

GF was founded primarily as a vehicle to distribute the typefaces (mostly) other designers created for use in RayGun mag. Carson was no longer involved in GF after a couple of years, and Betsy and Norbert continued to run the foundry.

They decided to sell GFin the late 90s and concentrate on Betsy's busy design concern (www.adesigngarden.com) and Norbert's own efforts.

Ralph Smith from Phil's Fonts took it over at that point, and started releasing new fonts from a slew of international designers. Now GF is happy and thriving with the folks at Phil's, still under its own indie label.

Some of the old RayGun types are still available at GarageFonts... www.garagefonts.com
