Coffee-table book font suggestions

April 6, 2009 • j_estner

I keep noticing that a bunch of coffee-table books are set in various sans serif fonts. I am in the middle of putting together a coffee-table book which has some paragraphs of text here and there but otherwise is mostly photographic.

I am not overly in love with Hadriano Std (which is what I’m currently using) and am planning a font switch. At this point, having never done a coffee-table book before, I am wondering as to whether a sans serif typeface is recommended? Or, apart from the sans serif / serif debate, what some recommended coffee-table-type fonts may be.

Thanks much!

Not sure if there is anything like a coffee-table font. I think it really depends what feel you want to communicate, what the photos are about (sad, funny, etc), who is going to read the book (old, young, male, female, etc)?

I think in the end you have to treat the book like every other design project and by answering the basic questions it will get you closer to your font choice.

I wonder whether your approach has to do with captions. There was a school of thought that held that if the text is serif, the captions can be sans-serif. So it, sort-of, follows that if you only have captions, you should be in sans-serif. However, in most books the main face is serif (for various reasons). Check some cook books.

I would say that you are free to do as you wish. Whatever you fancy?

“I wonder whether your approach has to do with captions." This is an interesting thought. I’ll admit that for the two coffee-table-ish books I’ve worked on so far, I leaned towards sans serifs as well (once Fedra Sans Alt, once Legato), and your explanation for this sounds pretty good. FWIW, I mostly feel that whoever looks at a coffee-table book doesn’t usually want to read for hours, and sans serifs seem to be good at communicating that “don’t worry, this text isn’t long”, plus “this book is not primarily about reading”. I often feel that usual seriffed/“book” text faces would give the text in a coffee-table book too much of a “bookish” flavor. If that makes any sense.
