Does this classic typeface fly in a technology piece?

July 20, 2004 • midnight9

I need expert Typophile advice. Working on a 20-pg brochure for a web-based medical software product. Sample pages below. So far I'm using an old favorite, Avenir. But as the product positions itself as being innovative but relatively neutral to some of the trends in technology advertising, I'm attempting to introduce Clarendon into the picture. Here's a sample spread with Avenir for the headline, somewhat Apple-esque and relatively "safe": http://www.cameronmoll.com/misc/foldout1.gif Now here's the same piece with Clarendon, a bit shy of the norm and somewhat daring: http://www.cameronmoll.com/misc/foldout2.gif Does the Clarendon headline fly? Or is it too daring? Feedback appreciated. (If you'd prefer to see other pages to get a better feel, lemme know.)
If you want a real refreshing break from the ordinary, ditch the oldies and check out some truly daring and shy of the norm typography. First thing that pops up in my mind is Psy/Ops. Check the sans, for example: Default Gothic | Faceplate Sans | Hydrous & Reykjavik. Those are all very modern, forward thinking designs, but without shouting it into your face if you grasp what I mean. Perfect choices for what you're aiming for. Somewhat more adventurous are Reform | Sophisto | Torus & Transfer.
