2005-08-01 12:45:25 • oldnick
>I find difficult to exactly match the style along the glyphs, It sounds like you are biting off more than you can chew. Try doing a design with a very simple structure, where glyphs are constructed from a small group of basic elements which are copied and pasted. Most simple: a sans serif "techno" font, somewhat condensed, where many characters have a common width. (There is still room for subtleties such as variation of stroke width between horizontal and vertical, and between caps and lower case.) Concentrate on getting this to look good in words, by working on the widths of the sidebearings as part of the design process. If you can tolerate this procedure, and produce a font you are not ashamed of, there's hope for you yet.
