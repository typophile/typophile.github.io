2009-08-25 22:32:24 • SuperUltraFabulous

Always space before percent-sign in Norwegian, Frode. I recommend thin space.

Entering the discussion late, and maybe off-topic. Sigh. The question is, are there any circumstances when you would not thin-space the percentage sign? If not, the extra space should be in the (left) sidebearing. Of course, that might screw up people who had learned to use always use a thinspace. Alternatively, you could have a kern – a class kern – which would properly space the percent sign when no space was used, but would not be applied when any sort of space intervened between [0-9] and percent.

I feel the same thinking applies to the hashmark (aka numbersign). There is often a fair bit of kerning/sidebearing adjustment needed with marks that appear with numbers. When those marks can also appear with letters, kerning is all you have. For the rest, why not adjust the sidebearing?

BTW, I usually make up an oldstyle hashmark. Typically, they are too broad, and sit too high. Sometimes they seem to be drawn too broad for even the lining figures.
