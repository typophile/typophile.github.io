“medical professionals involved in real estate transactions” That sounds like an unusual business :)

“The stethoscope is supposed to read “y” Do you see that or have to look for it?” I got the ‘y’ readily. If you hadn’t written it, the stethoscope may have been harder. But once you draw it better, I guess that shouldn’t be a problem. And anyway, you’d get it by association.

Of the two options, I prefer the one on the left where the profile of the home is more simple. Why not try an option where the two words, Healthy and Realty are stacked - in such a way that you have the stethoscope making a common ‘y’?

As for typeface, I’d definitely go for something less neutral. Do see if [[http://www.fontshop.com/fonts/downloads/fontfont/ff_speak_ot|FF Speak]] works for you. The lines of the font may cue a way to draw the steth.

You may also want to look at replacing all-caps with title case.

“The other idea I’m considering now..” Looks interesting. I’m not sure you really need to draw the house so literally. Probably just the roof profile, as you had done earlier, would be sufficient.

“but my friend wants the stethoscope to only act as the lower “y”” I’m sure you’re friend was just giving a suggestion to get you going, and would be quite okay if the idea evolves a little, as long as it addresses the client brief. Here it looks as if it is.

“Should a 1200 px wide, 360+dpi .png image be sufficient…” If it’s anything other than an on-screen application, you’d be better off creating it in vector so that it can be scaled to whatever size. That means drawing it in a program like Illustrator, Freehand, Corel Draw etc. If you need an on-screen version, you can always create a jpeg/bmp from that.
