If you think of fonts as tools, you can use the ones given to you by your uncle Steve (the bundled fonts), you can go around yard sales, Home Depot or break into sheds, and assemble a tool collection. Then when you have a project you can randomly try each tool until you find one that works.

An alternative plan would be to acquire a basic tool set and buy additional tools as needed.

Fortunately with fonts, there’s the undo button, so there’s little chance of flooding your basement, or setting fire to your roof, by using the wrong font.

etahchen,

As you probably noticed, even the suggestion of trying to find a lot of fonts without paying for them rubs many here the wrong way–thus the terse responses.

I know from experience that trying to make my own typeface increased my appreciation of the work the professionals do, but making your own typefaces is not a serious option for most.

If you want to legitimately expand on the choice of typefaces your system offers without spending an extreme amount, these typophile resources might be useful:

Good Libre Fonts: [[http://typophile.com/node/18207?|http://typophile.com/node/18207?]] Font Starter Packs: [[http://typophile.com/node/30047?|http://typophile.com/node/30047?]] FAQ Free: [[http://typophile.com/node/44453?|http://typophile.com/node/44453?]]

If you are a student/faculty member, Adobe offers two very good type collections for Education: [[http://www.adobe.com/education/products/fontfolioeducationessentials/index.html|Font Folio Education Essentials]] [[http://www.adobe.com/education/products/typeclassics.html|Type Classics for Learning]]

I’ve found that a combination of Open Source fonts available on Linux and Adobe’s type collection for Education has given me a very nice tool set of typefaces to try out–representatives of several periods and styles, without so much that I get lost. With that as a foundation, I’ve been able to buy other typefaces as I need them. Of course, I’m just a Mathematics instructor, so you may be beyond this point. Still, I wanted to try to offer a helpful post.
