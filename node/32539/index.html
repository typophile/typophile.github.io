Hello everyone, Please forgive me if my post seems out of context for this site. I have a Gutenberg coin dated 1837. It appears to be silver and larger than a 50 cent piece. It was apart of my mother-n-laws estate. Can anyone shed some light as to what these coins were issued for and the value of them.
Brenda Not certain what this is worth but it is a bit weird. The interest in Gutenberg (as the inventor of movable type in the Western sphere) didn't kick in with any real zealous until about mid 19th-century and took another fifty years to solidify. Even Joseph Moxon (late 17th century and the first writer on printing) did not recognize him for his possible contribution as inventor. The grandson of Peter Schoeffer (partner of Fust and Gutenberg) had written him out of history by 1515. Personally, I think they were correct, but likely, a valuable coin. There are better places on the web to find out the value of coins however. Gerald
