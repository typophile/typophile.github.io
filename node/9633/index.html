My personal theory is that ITC was trying to make everything look like Helvetica, particularly its x-height. But, they weren't alone.

I do remember that the prevailing wisdom in the seventies (promoted by ITC and others) was that typefaces with large x-heights were easier to read and used space more efficiently. There is a certain amount of truth to this (for example, newspaper faces), but it was mostly just the changing winds of taste and fashion.
