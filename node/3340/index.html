Lost in Translation, or elsewhere

February 27, 2004

Thanks to an extremely short attention span I've had the pleasure of reading the two english essays in
Sem Hartz otherwise dutch Essays for the last two weeks.

It's a beautiful book, designed by Bram de Does, and set in Emergo with examples of Jan van Krimpens Spectrum semi bold (the metal version, obviously) and Hartz own Juliana.

What strikes me is: Why is there no digital versions of Emergo and Juliana?
While Zapfs (the Master craftsman) designs are being updated and honoured these two little gems could fall into oblivion.

Another thing, type that actually have been digitized, like Bembo, but somewhat lost a bit of soul in the process, who'll take responsibility to save them?!

Do You have better examples? Do You feel like taking some responsibility? Am I wrong?

This little book is indeed a gem. My favorite thing is the actual printed sample of Linotype Juliana from a novel on cheap stock. (Emergo btw is less impressive to me.)
