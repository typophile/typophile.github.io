You’ve reminded me of the particular “swash factor” in Baskerville. Because the original italic caps had a strange “swash distribution”, in that some glyphs were very “straight” while others were overly swashy, revivers of Baskerville have elected to either preserve the original scheme or do away with the swashes entirely. One elegant solution can be found in ATF’s metal Baskerville: non-swashy main sorts, but swashy, in fact extra-swashy, alternates. Here’s a scan from a print I did at Archetype last year:
