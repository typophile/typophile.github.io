2010-08-04 19:39:43 • eliason

Old Nick Please allow me to bring a little French-speaking perspective in the debate about the word “Realist”. In his classification, Maximilien Vox gave the name “RÉALES” to the category of typefaces which seemed to aknownledge the influence of the “Romain du Roi” (end of the seventeenth century) — which corresponds broadly to the “Transitional” word used in English-speaking countries. The word “Réales” itself doesn’t exist in French (it is another of Vox’s infamous neologisms) but etymologically alludes to “royalty”, indeed. I’m not sure you can equate it with “Realist” without causing some ambiguities : for example, as it was already mentioned, Bringhurst uses this very term for nineteenth-century typefaces (Clarendon, etc.) in reference to the Realist painters (Gustave Courbet et al.) of the same era.
