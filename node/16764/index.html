I just found this WWII anti-Nazi propaganda poster on the National Archives site. What surprised me was how contemporary it is in styling. It looks more like a 1990s poster for a social cause on a college campus than it does 1940s propaganda.

http://www.archives.gov/exhibits/powers_of_persuasion/this_is_nazi_brutality/images_html/this_is_nazi_brutality.html

In any case, does anyone know what typeface was used for the red headline? It looks strikingly like Franklin Gothic Condensed, but was that around in the 1940s? It’s so unlike the typefaces seen on other propaganda posters of the era. It’s partially because of this that it looks so contemporary, I think.
