Copy on reverse of a laser cut card?

May 25, 2006 • tflavin

I’m working on a postcard that features a word (wordmark, specifically) laser cut from (rather than printed on) the page. It is roughly one by two inches of a six inch by six inch page.

I am interested in opinions on using the reverse of the page. Although I could use the space, I am concerned a backwards reading wordmark would be too distracting and confusing, esp if I set text around it.

Has anyone worked around this before? Is the reverse a total loss?

Too bad the wordmark isn’t an ambigram. That would have been clever ….

Thanks, Tim

Hmm… flip the card vertically, so that the cut letters are upside down but in the correct order. It will read right, approximately, and thus be less distracting.

For a clever effect, you could print the back with a solid color or grey, and have the wordmark again in white with some perspective distortion, making it look like light is shining through the holes and projecting the correctly-reading wordmark.
