Hi Emily! Yes, this request is way too broad, sorry. One or maybe two typefaces – okay. Everything else is really time-consuming. You could at least state what you have found yourself, so far, or what medium/period etc. you are focussing on. See also this similar request.

All I can do is give some general advice for finding typefaces in use:

Search typophile.com. Most of the faces you need have been asked for before. There’s a lot to find.

Linotype.com has a pretty neat in use section.

A lot of other foundries maintain such showcases aswell.

Especially if you’re looking for images, searching Flickr (or similar services) has proven helpful, to me. You might try adding ‘font’ or ‘type’ to your query, when searching for short/ambiguous terms like ‘Arnhem’, ‘Bell’ or ‘DIN’.

You wrote: ‘avant garde font was made specifically for avant garde magazine? what a gorgeous magazine… wow’

Actually, Herb Lubalin designed a logo for the AvantGarde Magazine, and this became later the typeface. The original by Letraset for instance had very interesting alternatives, which were not easily available digitally until recently. At ITCfonts.com you can see them now in the AvantGarde Pro series. But when you look at (old Letraset catalogues and) the ElsnerFlake EF alternative designs, you will see a few very distinct letters (e, v, w, y) that did not make it to the Pro obviously.

Many gorgeous headlines and logos resulted, and i am sure that you will find samples everywhere. Although it seems that AvantGarde is not extensively used recently.
