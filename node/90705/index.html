Beautiful typography on an old thermometer...

March 4, 2012 • SWriverstone

Saw this beautiful old thermometer on the wall at White Grass XC ski lodge in West Virginia: http://db.tt/nod1nwOX I love the typography on the face. It's vaguely architectural, and has an almost Scandinavian quality (appropriate for an XC ski lodge). I have no idea how old it is, but I'd guess it's at least 40-50 years old, maybe more. The typography looks handwritten, with a thicker stroke near intersecting lines. And I love the triangular zeros! Finally, the thermometer was made in the US. I have no clue if this is an actual font or a one-of-a-kind hand lettering job. If anyone has any ideas, please post up! Thanks, Scott
I would guess lettering given the context. Plus, the /F/ and /T/ lean in ways that would make no sense in running text but fit their initial and final positions here. And, the crossbars of the two /E/s are at different heights.
