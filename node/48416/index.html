2008-08-14 18:54:48 • Si_Daniels

Original Eurostile never had official italics, although several foundries subsequently released oblique companions.

URW’s Eurostile is relatively well-drawn, and has a fairly full complement of obliques as well as other variations. It’s also less expensive (per face) than many of its competitors.

Recently released, Linotype’s Eurostile Next (redrawn by Akira Kobayashi from Alessandro Butti’s and Aldo Novarese’s originals) also looks promising, although it seems to lack the obliques.

Definitely go for Eurostile Next: http://www.linotype.com/5324/eurostilenext.html

The reason for which Akira Kobayashi did not include an Oblique is very simple: Eurostile was never conceived as a “calligraphy based” typeface. It derived from the previous Microgramma (designed in the early 1950s by Alessandro Butti as an all-caps typeface for very small point sizes), and Novarese consciously developed the idea, strictly connected with a perception of modernity which put out of the question the inclusion of an “Italic”. So, I guess all the Oblique versions you find around are hacking its essence, and Linotype’s version is probably the first to get rid of the clutter and to offer it in an authentically “filologic” digital form.
