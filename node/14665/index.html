2005-08-19 01:22:56 • William Berkson
The Briem site is good, also look on Google for Chank. Some might not like him, but he is there and has been. A good book, among many, is "Designing Typefaces" by David Earls, more history than how-to, but best of all are the interviews with designers. The Bringhurst book never leaves my sight, nor does Tracy's "Letters of Credit." Also good is "Typographically Speaking The Art of Matthew Carter" by Margaret Re, with essays by Johanna Drucker and James Mosley. Also very good is "Goudy's Type Designs" with text by Goudy. Goudy's commentary is very interesting and often amusing.

2005-08-19 07:39:32 • antiphrasis
[[Fontographer: Type by Design]] by Stephen Moye It is currently out of print but can be found in digital form on the net. If you'd rather take the non piracy approach, some members had copies for sale. Most of the information in the book is not specific to Fontographer and it covers the correct drawing of outlines from a technical perspective particularly well. Opinions as to its usefulness were mixed among typophile members, but I found it contained some very interesting information that I had not found in other books on the subject.
