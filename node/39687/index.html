Yes, you are! You have read your [[Hans Peter Willberg|Willberg]] well … ;°) According to his classification, Clarendons are a subgenre of the ‘static egyptienne’ – characteristics would be a distinct contrast of stroke widths; double-storey ‘g’, open ‘a’; ‘a’, ‘g’ and ‘r’ with ball terminals; among others. Originally, Clarendon is the English version of the slab-serif and it therefore occasionally is called ‘Englische Egyptienne’ in German. Emerging as early as in the 1820s, it has more ‘human’ forms than its continental counterparts.
