Geometric Elements

August 2, 2006 • wormwood

I thought this might be an interesting (and fun?) exercise for all you symbologists(?) out there.

I doodled 6 geometric patterns almost by accident. They can’t be original and are probably all in a dingbat, crochet pattern book or cave painting somewhere. But anyway, because there are 6 of them and because they look a bit oriental i thought about associating 1 of the 6 elements in chinese philosophy to each of them, which are (in no particular order)…

earth - air - fire - water - metal - wood

After spending way too long pondering it I’ve still not totally made my mind up. Patterns within patterns, associations and abstractions etc…

I’m curious to see if there will be any kind of consensus of opinion, or if you will all tell me to sod off and join an occult or theology forum.

If you are gonna bother posting your matches just don’t look at other peoples answers first if you can help it.

BTW each pattern is numbered, just in case you hadn’t noticed.

I find them very appealing for some reason and thought about making them available as 6 small canvases (6"x6" obviously) maybe screen printed. (hence the photoshop mockup) Anyones thoughts on the matter are most welcome.

fire - earth - wood air - water - metal

But don’t ask me why ;-)

Geometric Elements - The Result

Firstly, thank you Tom, James, Tiffany and Simon for posting your choices.

After you guys posted i persuaded 5 of my friends to give their opinions, plus me makes 10.

I had hoped for a greater response, so the larger the sample group the clearer the consensus would be. Anyway, the results were…

1: earth 3 / air 1 / fire 1 / water 0 / metal 5 / wood 0 (majority = metal) 2: earth 5 / air 0 / fire 1 / water 1 / metal 0 / wood 3 (majority = earth) 3: earth 1 / air 2 / fire 2 / water 1 / metal 1 / wood 3 (majority = wood) 4: earth 1 / air 3 / fire 3 / water 0 / metal 1 / wood 2 (majority = air/fire) 5: earth 0 / air 4 / fire 3 / water 2 / metal 0 / wood 1 (majority = air) 6: earth 0 / air 0 / fire 0 / water 6 / metal 3 / wood 1 (majority = water)

It’s interesting to see, even with such a small sample group, how opinions still frequently corresponded on something (apparently) so abstract.

Here is my choice…

1: metal 2: earth 3: fire 4: wood 5: air 6: water

…which i don’t think i’m being too fascistic in sticking with, as it only breaks from the democratic consensus by switching 2 of the elements which were fairly closely drawn anyway :)

And thank you James for the link to the “Magician’s Companion”. I don’t think i can resist getting a copy now. I would like to know what symbol and element associations the master, Bill Whitcomb, would make.

Look out for the symbols on a t-shirt in your neighbourhood sometime soon ;)
