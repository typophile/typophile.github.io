Technology progresses society, and as long as it doesn't take away from our true nature (like email does in a way for example! :-) it's a good thing. There's nothing natural about handwriting - it's one of the most artificial things we do.

Handwriting will become an elevated hobby/craft (kind of like type design!), something you do because you love it, something that the general population might admire, but not really understand any more. Kind of like making your own pottery at home - it used to be the norm, now it's an aberration.

> so why we need to learn..... to talk?

To communicate, as broadly and sincerely as possible. Handwriting is very bad at the former, and it's only good at the latter when the message is highly personal: like if you ever have to write a letter to propose marriage to somebody, make sure you handwrite it (unless it's totally illegible). But for example when you're designing a font that can be used for virtually anything, chirography generally confuses things.
