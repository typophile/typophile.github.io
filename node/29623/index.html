Typographical pun-fest in print

November 19, 2006 • tikitu

There’s a short-short story, “A Punctuated Romance” by Mary Turzillo, in the latest Electric Velocipede (f/sf ‘zine, $US4/issue) which packs more typography-related puns into one page than I think I’ve heard in my life. Although I wonder how long it’ll take Typophile.com to top it… In popped the maid, Tilde. “Your case may be more acute than that, my poor darling. Many is the night I’ve seen you kerning on the porch with young Virgule. Law, last night I thought the angle brackets would give way.” (more…)

(I’m not involved with EV. I also don’t hang out here nearly often enough, but I thought you lot would appreciate the headsup.)

Edit: there’s a different excerpt on the EV site. I kept mine short because the whole thing is only a page long, after all, so between the two there’s still some of the story you haven’t seen.

I love puns. In fact, I’ve just sent a big project off that requires a lot of handwork. Since several hundred thousand of them will be produced, the project is being done in China. So I told my coworkers that for the Chinese, it is about to be fold and glue season.
