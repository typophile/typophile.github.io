Ah, [[http://vellumpaper.info/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/vellum-paper.jpg|Vellum]] is the best quality to use. There are other types of semi-opaque paper that you’re looking for out there.

As the “paper” doesn’t absorb, most ink-printing methods don’t work too well. Laserjet is your best bet for good quality. You can’t get too light of a paper (such as the onion paper/bible paper Theunis mentioned) or it may melt… in that case, as he also mentioned, a press would do the trick.

2010-06-08 02:34:10 • Steven Acres

Are you just doing a few sheets for a personal project, or will a professional printer be doing the job?

If it’s just a personal project, then some of the arts-and-crafts websites may have advice on hand stamping or using a desktop printer.

If it’s being professionally printed, I suspect that many print shops would farm out a job like that to a speciality printer. Try to find a printer that has a lot of experience with that kind of stock (ask to see samples) and get their advice. They may not be experts at design, but they should be able to give good technical tips.
