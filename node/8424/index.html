January 3, 2005 • phi1618033988

I'm planning a design/layout makeover for a security and defence policy magazine. 58 pages approx, full color, pictures; "Neewsweek format".

Open for suggestions for font combinations for body text and headings. As for the body text font is concerned, readability is important of course, but it must not be too gray or conservative. And it's definately good if it came in a range of variations, such as thin/normal/semibold/bold, condendensed/narrow, etc.

When it comes to the sans serif for headings and small articles, I'm quite fond of Helvetica New. Would that be a very bad choice (I mean, is it too boring?) I like the Frutiger too, though.

Suggestions? Advices? Thanks in advance!

William W.
Frutiger and Helvetica are solid but boring. They've been used, used and used again.

If I was in you're shoes, I'd look at Farnham as a serif, and FF Bau or FF Super Grotesk B as sans options.

Farnham, recently released from exclusivity by Font Bureau, is a lively serif that won't alarm a conservative audience, but looks like it would a lot of fun to work with. You could use it for display purposes, too.

FF Bau and FF Super Grotesk B (note the B: it's the version with the two-story a and g) offer more interest and character than Helvetica, and their liveliness, especially in the bold and black weights, would match Farnham's "exuberance," as the Font Bureau site puts it.

You might consider adding a slab serif to the mix for display purposes. Since you'd be using it sparingly, as a spice rather than a main ingredient, something a little quirky, like Oxtail (from PsyOps), might work.

William, I'm quite fond of Helvetica Neue as well. If you decide to go with Frutiger instead, take a look at Frutiger Next, a revised version of the family released a few years ago. It includes, amoung other things, a true italic (instead of the old obliques).

http://www.linotype.com/53781/frutigernext-family.html

Another interesting headline option, if you like Frutiger's work, is the new version of Avenir that Adrian Frutiger created last year with Akira Kobayashi, Avenir Next. This version of Avenir is far superior to the original 1988 release. It has a better family system (more clear differentiation of the various weights), condensed weights, small caps, and old style as well as lining figures. Plus, it is available in OpenType format.

http://www.linotype.com/90672/avenirnext-family.html
