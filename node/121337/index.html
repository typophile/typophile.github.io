Secret Of The Incas Movie 1954 -- faux building stones in 3-D

November 15, 2014 • donshottype

_Secret Of The Incas_ a Paramount movie released in 1954 has a main title lettered in 3-D faux building stones that evoke the spirit of the Inca monumental buildings. [img:sites/default/files/old-images/SecretOfTheIncasSM_5111.jpg] I wonder if a fan, or anyone else, has used this as model for a font, either flat or 3-D. I can't recall any similar fonts that are particularly close. I would be interested to learn what might exist in this rather unusual genre. A larger scale image is attached. Thanks for your attention. Don

I would bet somewhere in one of Dan X. Solo’s exotic alphabets there is something like that. In my cursory search, decorative typefaces visually inspired by stone tend to presents each letter as carved from a single piece (albeit often crumbling) instead of composed by blocks, while those composed by bricks tend to have a very flat and clean cut. Regarding the 3D aspect, one I found, not at all similar, but somewhat as much monumental, is http://www.fontspace.com/manfred-klein/mk-typobricks-rough Another search term could be “block”, example: http://www.fontspace.com/crolrene/wodenbloks Lastly, a place where the concept has been explored is certainly Fontstruct. Unfortunately its content isn’t easy to navigate and filter. Two samples which could or could not be of interest to you: http://fontstruct.com/fontstructions/show/levelrebel http://fontstruct.com/fontstructions/show/brickybrickbold

Thanks Riccardo and Ryuk for the interesting answers. The concrete blocks photos are good. Could be the basis of an interesting font. Same for The Incredible Hulk lettering. As for the existing scalable fonts: Rock Steady by the excellent comic book lettering artist, Nate Piekos, has an appropriate treatment for the face. Could be applied to other face designs, including the polygonal stones used by the Incas. Could be extended to the 3D treatment. I agree that his company, Blambot, and the other comic book company, ComicCraft are the most likely places for finding something in this genre. Perhaps also PizzaDude. Megalithic has some promise. Unfortunately the author, Daniel Ortega, did not refine the Dan Zadorozny's Hulkbuster design so that the structure works as well in basic black and white as it does in the version showing shades of grey. The other suggested fonts also give hints as to what is feasible. For example MAWNS Rock shows what can be done with a simplified graphic treatment of wear and tear, but would be much more useful in a version without all the loose rubble. You have both been most helpful. Shows its worth digging further. Don
