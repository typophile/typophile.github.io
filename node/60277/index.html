Can anyone identify the fonts used on the covers of books published by the New York Review of Books, e.g. [[http://www.nybooks.com/shop/product-file/31/thes7931/product.jpg]] [[http://www.nybooks.com/shop/product-file/22/bewa5422/product.jpg]] It's a recent sans (looks post-1980s, if not later), but I don't know those fonts well enough.
All-caps sans are really hard to ID (for me). In these samples the earmarks I noted were: splayed M (vertices not pointed), J below baseline, no serifs on I or J, W like two V's joined, G has a jaw, but no beard, R has straight leg starting on bowl (not stem). One font I found that meets all these is [[http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/typotheque/jigsaw/|Jigsaw]] from Typotheque, designed in 1999. There must be others. When were your books published? - Mike Yanega
