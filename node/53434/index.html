2009-01-14 03:44:18 • russellm

(Forgive me if this sounds too highfalutin; the language is more romantic than I intend but the thoughts are sincere.)

Type design is a tool for discovering entirely new possibilities and a tool for discovering the wisdom of the ways things have been done before.

Type design is an exercise in letting your impulses take shape and an exercise in growing to understand the needs of others whom your creations will serve.

Type design is a reminder that consistency requires steady effort and discipline and a reminder that unbending application of systematic thought leads to failure.

In these ways, figuring out how to design type strikes me as a microcosm of how to approach life.

2009-01-18 03:24:26 • guifa

One fascinating thing about type design (typography is also cool, but not quite as exciting) is that, while I still think it’s some sort of pinnacle of graphic design in terms of the clarity, simplicity, and detail precision that’s needed, it’s not just about designing pretty little shapes.

So you design a lettershape. You put it in context, and it sits there. And then, it says something. Takes on a life of its own. Talks to its neighbors. Looks angry, or happy. Frowns, chirps, sings. And it interacts with the content that it’s helping to express, voicing it in its own tone: the one you formed.

I find that’s quite magical.
