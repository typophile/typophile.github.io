Rick, the "f without a crossbar" is called the long s and was commonly used in the Latin alphabet until the 18th century, and also later in blackletter. See: http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Long_s http://www.myfonts.com/unicode/017f/ If you use Microsoft Word for Windows, you can type it by typing 017f and pressing Alt+x after that. You can also assign a keystroke to it using Word's record macro function. You did not indicate into which language you are translating. Also, I don't quite understand what you mean by a "key" that would show 17th century Spanish letters vs. English letters. Regards, Adam
