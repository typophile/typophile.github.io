Hi All! Haven't dived into making your own fonts yet? Want to learn to make fonts with FontLab in a casual, fun atmosphere? Then, come join us for GRPH 152 Digital Font Creation in the Graphic Communications Department at CCSF. NITTY-GRITTY CLASS INFO GRPH 152 Digital Font Creation 8 Monday Evenings: 1/29, 2/12, 2/26, 3/12, 3/26, 4/9, 4/23, 5/7 CCSF Ocean Campus, Visual Arts room 105, 6-10pm Prereq: GRPH 25 Intro to Mac Graphics, or equivalent Instructor: Amy Conger, aconger@ccsf.edu CRN: 38026, 1 unit OVERVIEW This is an intro class to making fonts in Fontlab Studio 5 for Macintosh, in practice and in context. Whether you want to make a ton of fonts or you just want to get more intimate with the ones that exist, you'll find we cover a lot of interesting topics like these: * Altering existing fonts in Fontlab * Drawing letterforms in Fontlab * Importing letterforms from raster and vector drawings * Automating accented characters in Fontlab * Building Kerning pairs into a font * Avoiding common font snafus * Pixel Fonts * A quick survey of the history of type design tech * Font files and management * The state of the industry, inc. licensing and sales * Specimens and critique You will come away with a font of your own design and some basic skills in FontLab Studio 5 and ScanFont. TEXTBOOKS The required textbook is "Learn FontLab Fast" by Leslie Cabarga. It is available here: http://www.logofontandlettering.com/ The optional textbook is "Designing Type" by Karen Cheng (ISBN 0300111509), available online. You may also want to have a copy of the FontLab and ScanFont manuals. Those are available as PDFs respectively here: http://www.font.to/downloads/FLS5MacManual.zip and here: http://www.pyrus.com/downloads/SF4MacDoc.pdf And you can also optionally purchase a printed version through me in class. (It should run around $20) REGISTERING - Everyone with a little Mac skill is welcome! - The class will be available for web and in-classregistration. - If you do not have the pre-req, add the class then email me. - You may also add the class on the first day (provided you are enrolled in the college). - If you are not enrolled in the college, please go register in the credit division: http://www.ccsf.edu/Offices/Matriculation/step1.htm - The class itself is $26; registration fees are about another $20. GRADING & ATTENDANCE - This class is available for Credit/No Credit grading if you are not applying this class toward a degree or certificate. - If you cannot attend in person, you may join the independent study group that I will support from a distance. Please talk to me if you are interested. THANKS! I look forward to seeing you all in class! Please write with questions.
