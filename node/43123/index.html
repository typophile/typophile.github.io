Logo for trading company

March 13, 2008 • t-boo

Hello,

Here are some tests for my little company. We mainly imports products (most of them from italy). We do not sell directly to the final customer.

As you can see, I’m not especially a master designer (far away from that, I’m web developer) but this is going to passionate me more everyday. So I certainly made evident big mistakes but I don’t know them : every constructive critics are welcome !

So here is how I though:

  • D L V letters can be the same character with 45° rotation if you delete the vertical bar from the ‘D’.
  • The 2.5D box made with these letters could represent the boxes we deal everyday with (not really inspired)
  • The circle in the middle represent the company, which buy goods (vertical simple line) to supply its retailers (multiple dispersing lines)
  • 1, 3 & 4 could represent an UP arrow, synonym of prosperity

The typo used is a classic Gill sans MS, at this stage I give priority to the logo itself.

Thanks in advance ;)

Hi,

for a web developer your work looks pretty good. (Actually, I am web developer too!)

Maybe you are having too many thoughts about what the logo should represent. I think it’s best to pick one of the ideas/bullet points above and then develop it further. At the moment, I like logo #1 the most. The other ones may not scale down well, due to the many thin white lines.

If you say, you are mostly selling Italian products you might also want to consider using a colour scheme that feels more Italian, e.g. terracotta or light brown.

Good luck!
