The base letters (save for the curved crossbars on the |A| and |E|) could be created from the following: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/ihof/p22-curwen/ http://www.myfonts.com/search/Alexon/fonts/

Add the |R| from this typeface, and elongate its swash: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/canadatype/orpheus-pro/alts-ii-bold/

And the |S| from this typeface, and edit it a bit: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/edwardian/

Or, you may just want to start fresh with similar typefaces from Letterhead Fonts or Font Mesa, which specialize in western revivals: http://www.letterheadfonts.com http://www.fontmesa.com/index.htm

Did you notice the S shaped counters in the O in the bottom line? This design feature seems to have originated with Nicholas J. Werner who designed the font Inland for the Inland Type Foundry in 1895. Your O has a less emphatic version of the S counter shown in This is from Claude Pelletier’s Bizarrerie, the digital version of Inland. I can’t link direct to the font.
