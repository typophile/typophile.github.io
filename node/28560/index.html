My point is that you must not choose a firm name (no matter how small a firm) that becomes an obstacle. If your grandmother meant a lot to you, then fine. But if everyone that sees the mark misinterpret it, then you should not use it, but change to something else.

I was in fact only talking about principles. And choosing a name for personal reasons that are not obvious to the spectator must be the wrong way of thinking.

That said, your mark probably works just fine. ‘513’ has really no irrelavant connotations. I was more worried about the Magnolia case.

But one weakness about your mark is this: when people see, they go: “It reads 53” or “It reads 53 or 513” (the middle digit has a different style than the other two).

I like the way you have flipped the 5.
