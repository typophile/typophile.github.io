You should always carefully track type on a curve, and check all individual kerning pairs, as being along a curve messes up the negative spaces between the characters. This is something that requires extra time and attention. The fact that Microgramma has very wide characters makes it less suitable for setting on a curve, as the curve visually breaks up in small straight bits. Tiff, "warp feature". Ugh, Idonnoaboutthat…

5{re are two factors affecting this. Firstly, the characters are positioned on the path according to their base point, which is on the charcter's baseline, at the left of the character. So on a perfect circle, they would "step" as in your example. Secondly, how perfect is the curve they are on? You may have more control if you create the path and type in Illustrator (and nudge the path to get the type nice), and then drag the graphic into Photoshop (I think you have to hold down the option, or perhaps the command key).
