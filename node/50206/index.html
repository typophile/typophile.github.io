Hy Arjun, first of all its really good to hear Patel Sir’s name here, he is really a great mentor, fortunately i got a chance to work on some projects with him.

Well LCM grid is a technique to find out no. of columns and their optimum width suitable for a particular page size, so you get the consistent divisons and there wont be any extra space left after drawing columns.

The most commonly used LCM is 12, because its a divisible for both even and odd, so you get a flexibility of working with 2,3 and 4 column chunks.

Previously designers had to use a formula for manually calculating the column widths accordingly to desired LCM, but now with InDesign you can directly define it easily.
