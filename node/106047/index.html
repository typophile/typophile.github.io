For the bottom image, ARMED FORCES POLICE, I thought I had a list of several sans with this boxy S, but all I have at hand is a condensed version, Schmallfette Binder-Style, designed by Joseph Binder and released by D. Stempel AG in 1959. Digitized as Bindlestiff NF by Nick Curtis.
If you expand it, and adjust the M, you will be fairly close. Don

An alternative, and perhaps easier, approach is to start with Morris Fuller Benton’s Bank Gothic, make it narrower, square the sloped terminations and do other edits. A useable starting point is the Linotype version Morris Sans Com in the Heavy Condensed Weight. Don

ARMED FORCES POLICE: no perfect match. Some interesting alternatives: [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/nicksfonts/route-66-nf|Route 66 NF]] (matching almost everything, not enough bold though), [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/letterbox/berber|Berber]] King Caps Bold (using alternate /[[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/letterbox/berber/caps-bold/glyphs.html#glyphs/508403/342|A]] and reworking /M centre vertex), [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/marksimonson/refrigerator-deluxe|MS Refrigerator Deluxe]] Bold (using alternate /[[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/marksimonson/refrigerator-deluxe/heavy/glyphs.html#glyphs/488468/183|S]] and reworking /M centre vertex)

AOYAMA CITY: [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/chank/nicotine|Nicotine]] should work nicely. Alternative: [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/redrooster/los-alamos|Los Alamos]] Fat Man

MY RUGGED 211: nothing convincing, reminding me of this previous [[http://typophile.com/node/99394|discussion]], feels really “custom” to me (mainly because of this unconventional /G)… Anyway, some alternatives: [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/coniglio/nomad|Nomad]] (reworking almost everything…), [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/suitcase/vafle|Vafle]] Condensed, [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/fdi/iwan-reschniev|Iwan Reschniev]] Black (reworking /G and /S terminals, not enough bold), [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/chank/nicotine|Nicotine]] (mainly reworking /R leg, /S transition and adding a leg to /G), [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/jnlevine/sign-kit|Sign Kit JNL]] (reworking /G and digits), [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/dizajndesign/komu|Komu]] A (the closest I guess, only needs little tweaking on /M centre vertex and /R leg to do the trick)
