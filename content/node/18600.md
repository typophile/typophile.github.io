body: "<strong>[[Indices]] : [[Designers]] : Dr. Rudolf Wolf</strong>\r\n\r\nGerman,
  born 1895 in Hechingen, Germany. Died by suicide on September 7, 1942.\r\n\r\nDesigner
  at the [[D. Stempel AG]] in Frankfurt am Main, Germany.\r\n\r\nAmong other things,
  Dr. Wolf was the designer of the famous [[Memphis]] type family, and the possible
  creator of the famous type design test string [[hamburgefontsiv]].\r\n\r\nDr. Wolf
  received his PhD from the University of Frankfurt, and taught typography in addition
  to his designing and advertising duties at Stempel.\r\n\r\nThe [[Klingspor Museum]]
  in Offenbach has a <a href=\"www.klingspor-museum.de/KlingsporKuenstler/Schriftdesigner/Wolf/RWolf.pdf\">PDF
  online listing all known typefaces attributed to Dr. Wolf</a>."
