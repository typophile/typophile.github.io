I'm looking for some Great examples of classic spanish typefaces. Preferebly of the latin american tradition. Thanks.
John, 'Classic spanish typefaces' and 'latinamerican tradition' are for the moment (and sadly) hard to conect. The Updike's book ('Printing types') has an interesting chapter on historical spanish fonts. If you look for revivals, you can try looking for 'Pradell' or 'Ibarra' fonts, like the one of Andreu Balius. According to the Latinamerican 'tradition' (a very short tradition, I have to say), I think there are interesting things to look at in www.tipografia.cl (Australis), www.pampatype.com and http://www.letraslatinas.com/html_tex/a.shtml (However, in this last link you will find a mix of styles and quality… but this is the state of the latinamerican font scene today.)
