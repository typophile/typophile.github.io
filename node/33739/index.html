The CV is not so important, what matters is the portfolio, and more precisely the contents of projects in the portfolio, not how fancy you made the portfolio. But by all means attach a CV, but keep it to relevant points. Don’t list your paper route from high school and that kind of stuff.

So remember that the CV says close to nothing of relevance to employers in the design field. It’s all about your work. In advertising (art direction) it’s much more about your ability to deliver ideas on time and sell them - people skills i.o.w.
