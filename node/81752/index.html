2011-05-01 00:30:58 • arthurmagill
That style of 'a', along with the circular 'C' are characteristic of a kind of geometric typefaces of which the most known is Futura. The serifs on 'I', 'i' and 'l' (and the narrow 'm') are typical of monospaced fonts. Unfortunately, the monospaced version of Futura I found (http://www.fontmenu.com/site/_Normafixed.html) isn’t the right one. But it could be Avant Garde Mono (http://www.fontshop.com/fonts/singles/elsnerflake/itc_avant_garde_mono_tu_bold/) or one of the others mentioned in this older thread: http://typophile.com/node/80582
