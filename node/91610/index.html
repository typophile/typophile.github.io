As long as you don’t mind losing money with a less-than-ideal logo… :-/ As the saying goes, you have to spend money to make money.

Two other points:

  1. Although there are talented graphic designers who frequent Typophile, we’re mostly focused on letterforms, and in your logo there’s only one, plus it’s a simple one. So as things stand you might not get very fruitful feedback.
  2. Context is everything. You know what your product is about, but for a third-party to help you he would need to know a lot more about the nature of the clothing, the potential customers, etc.

In any case, good luck.
