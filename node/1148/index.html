2003-09-05 20:01:37 • lapvance
The lower part of a script f would not normally be a swash, unless it was unusually flamboyant. Generally swashes are extra/alternate characters for a Script font, and while they are most often embellishments of the upper case letters, they could also be special beginning or ending flourishes on lower case letters. Adobe Bickham is a good example of a font with both kinds of swashes. See attached.

If I understand the question, I understand that it is a play on the word "swashbuckler". It paints its own picture.

I'm sure you are aware of what a swash is. In type it's an ornamental extension or pickup stroke on a cap, or a finial stroke to a lowercase e,m,n,t, in the italic.

In a script face like Bickham there is a lot of opportunity to swash.
