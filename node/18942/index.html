I’ve not seen this exact font before. It appears to be, however, a somewhat simplified (really) version of a typeface originally called Fournier Geperlt (Pierre Simon Fournierlejeune, 1712 - 1768). A later foundry resurrection called it Dresden (P.A. Demeter, 1922). The only digital versions I know of are, unfortunately, from questionable sources. The notorious Southern Software offered it as Trellis Display Caps. The German knockoff company Softmaker has it as Dresden and someone created a decent lowercase for it and called it Weinbeck.

No, it looks like you called it perfectly, Tiffany. Dreseden was an actual typeface, whereas Bodoni Classic Floral Initials are, according to Gert Wiescher’s writeup at MyFonts, his own creation. To me, they have a great deal in common with Dresden, though. Nice find!
