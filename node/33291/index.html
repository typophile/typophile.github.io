or if you are not a purist. Import or scan your base 'type' design. Needs to have a photographic background or similar to work, like in your example Copy the 'type' layer in Photoshop four/five+ times. Blur each layer to different amounts, nudge them about a bit, alter the colours, impose different blends on each color dodge/screen/overlay. Basically experiment to you find what you are looking for.

Yep, along with belleisle advice there's lots of ways to achieve the neon glow effect in Photoshop. [img:sites/default/files/old-images/GlowLines_4921.JPG] One real simple way is to use the Pen tool to create your shapes and on a new layer above it fill with colour and set the blending mode to Color. You could copy your shape to a new layer below it and apply to slight gauss blurr to achieve a diffused effect. In my example I just used two layers on a black bg. clauses, groovy linkage.
