Is there a semi-serif version of Verdana?

October 4, 2011 • Pooja Saxena

I’m reading Ari Davidow’s essay “Computer screens are not like paper: typography on the web”, which was published in Computers and Typography 2. In his essay he writes,

“…Verdana is a masterpiece of font design, a sans serif at small sizes where each pixel is critical, and a semi-serif in larger sizes.”

Is there, or was there once upon a time, a semi-serif version of Verdana?

I think the author missed there something and was thinking of the seriffed capital ›I‹, which is quite distinct in Verdana, when he wrote this.

2011-10-05 10:18:47 • bendy

Perhaps he is referring to the way that turning off font-smoothing at a small size threshold and rendering tiny text as a high-contrast pixel-font bitmap will render C, for instance, as three straight lines, with no “semi-serifs” at the terminals.

This is not font-specific; however the distinction for Verdana is that the small-size bitmap was designed that way, with separate size-specific forms, rather than having the pixel-font as a compromise or adjustment made to the “proper” higher-res iteration.
