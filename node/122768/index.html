Subtle emotion in typefaces

January 23, 2015 • arvebaat

For my bachelor assignment I want to investigate which subtle variations within the established framework of letters causes which emotions. As Spiekermann states in this interview [[http://news.gestalten.com/news/interview-erik-spiekermann-typography|in this interview]]: "The skill in type design lies precisely in working within this 90 to 95 percent established framework. An a is an a. Once again, it's like music: you work with clearly defined notes, but an oboe A sounds different to a violin A." I am interested in how type designers integrate emotions and moods in a typeface within this established framework. Which arch, contrast in stroke or terminal endings in the letters shape represents which feelings or emotions. I am not looking for concrete facts, but concrete views. And I am planning to interview type designers. But I need some literature. Does anyone know of any literature available that might help me with the subject? Some books I've already looked at The Geometry of Type, Coles Designing Type, Cheng The New Typography, Tschichold Typography: when who how, Feierabend et. ak. Hello I’m Erik, Erler Lettering & Type, Willen & Strals. All help is welcome. Thanks!
