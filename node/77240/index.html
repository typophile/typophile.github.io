FontShop has a managed program of font development and release, and probably other foundries plan ahead like that. After all, once one has committed to a prototype design, it can take many months to produce the full range of fonts for that face, with all the language support and OpenType features.

However, I don’t think the idea has occurred to anyone in this business that we should “pre-release” out type plans, to assist designers in coordinating their type choices with other aspects of design.

Fear of having some other foundry steal one’s thunder by rushing a cheap and nasty version of the idea to market (as happens in the fashion/clothing industry) is no doubt the reason.

It’s hard to say which of the trends popular in 2010 will continue. I would say wait till all the “Best of 2010” reviews (especially Typographica) come out, then make your analysis. Your prediction will be as good as the pros. Besides which, you have the opportunity to lead, rather than follow.
