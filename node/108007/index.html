Transitional serif text font used in 'The Palmer Method of Business Writing' (1935)

January 5, 2014 • altsan

I quite like the crisp rationality of the text typeface used in this book, but I can’t identify it. I suspect it’s a Linotype machine face, judging by the buttonhook ‘f’ and the overly wide italic.

http://www.iampeth.com/books/palmer_method_1935/palmerMethod_1935_page2.html

I particularly like the ‘g’, which strikes me as rather distinctive.

It looks close to [[http://www.findmyfont.com/index.php/fonts/font-preview?fset=Adobe&ffam=Century%20Old%20Style%20-%20Regular&fid=d2353b72245ef934893e134fa27d521f&fsize=48&text=AN%20EXPLANATION.-The%20object%20good%2C%20practical%20business%20follow&wrap=2|Century Old Style]]
