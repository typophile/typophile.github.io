Labour Party uses Open Sans for its campaign

January 22, 2015 • rayzb92

The British Labour Party apparently decided to use Open Sans for its podium design. Open Sans is probably the most used Google font. So what do you guys think? Lack of originality? Very easy to copy, as you can see in the attached image.
No specific problem with the font choice - it's readable, clean and modern as reflects their (self-)image. Given their financial problems, I'm not entirely startled to see them using a freebie font - at least they picked a well-designed one. I'm not too keen on those apostrophes, though, and the spacing around the top 'r's' looks very off. Not awful, but the whole thing is lacking in flair. And using a generic font is a mistake, as you say, for any organisation that expects to be mocked and parodied. Anything that helps pranksters create fake Labour posters easily is a big mistake. The Tories (Britain's Conservative party) found this out the hard way last election - within days of their poster launch there was a [[http://mydavidcameron.com|website that let people make parody posters]].

Even though I am not from Britain (but from its neighbour to the east), I think the political party visual identities are rather nice over there.
