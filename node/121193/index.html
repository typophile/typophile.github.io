Early 1900s Blackletter fonts on a wine label

November 10, 2014 • fuenfwerken

Hello, I have this old wine label from the Ahr area in Ahrweiler / Germany. It is from around 1910-1940 and has several blackletter fonts. The big one I already identified as Maximilan (3rd from top). For the other ones I have no idea. Does anyone have a guess? Especially the first and the Italic (or rather Oblique) ones would be helpful.
I agree with your call of Rudolf Koch’s Maximilian (Gotisch) which he produced for Gebr. Klingspor in 1914-1917. Several revivals of varying quality and legitimacy. Consider the version by Delbanco http://www.delbanco-frakturschriften.de/artikeldetails/kategorie/gotisch/artikel/ds-maximilian.html and Gerhard Helzel’s revival. Alter Littera has a version under development that, based on their other blackletters, should be of excellent quality http://www.alterlittera.com/al_htm/oldtype.htm As for the boxy letters in the top line, perhaps they are also by Koch and or Klingspor? I have seen several similar attempts to simplify blackletter pre-WW I, but I don’t recall this precise design. If there is a digital version, the best place to start might be to go through Gerhard Helzel’s specimen book http://www.romana-hamburg.de/Fraktur.htm Don

I was aware of the Steffmann Maximilian but did not mention it because in my opinion, based on examination of quite a few of Mr. Steffmann’s other fonts, the digital outlines are probably stolen from the work of another digital fontmaker. Mr. Steffmann’s practice was to take an existing digital font, add additional characters, do some minor tinkering, and share the result as his own work. Hardly fair to the person who made the font, and an abuse of the idea of a “free” font. Don
