Fans of the classic Doctor Who program will recognize the attached photograph as William Hartnell, the first Doctor from 1963, entering his time machine, a.k.a. the TARDIS.

Assuming that plaque on the door wasn’t simply a hand-lettered sign, I’m hoping to identify the font used on the original prop. The plaque changed many times over the years, but it always said (basically) the same thing:

POLICE TELEPHONE

FREE FOR USE OF PUBLIC

ADVICE AND ASSISTANCE OBTAINABLE IMMEDIATELY

OFFICERS AND CARS RESPOND TO URGENT CALLS

PULL TO OPEN

The sample photo is small, but the ‘U’ in ‘PUBLIC’ is distinctive. Also, it appears the center vertex of the ‘M’ floats above the baseline while the verticals of the ‘M’ are parallel. Closest I’ve come so far is a face called ‘Apolline,’ but it’s not quite there.

Any help is appreciated!

You’re back! I caught your earlier post and started looking, only to come back and find the post deleted.

I was very into Doctor Who back in the eighties and have a few books about the show, one of which has a large reproduction of your sample photo and one other that is similar. Both are a bit fuzzy, but I think it’s quite likely that it was hand drawn for the prop. Nevertheless, I think Weiss is going to be very close, and may be what it was based on, if it was drawn by hand.
