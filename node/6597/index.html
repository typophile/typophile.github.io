"Oriental" is not considered a respectful term these days. It is also vague. There is important work by Edward Said on "orientalism," by which he means West Asia, or what is sometimes called the Middle East. The question, as Eduardo notes, is much too broad to be answered usefully. Asia accounts for three-fifths of the world's population. It is extraordinarily diverse. Its traditions are ancient and distinguished. You could say that most of the world's design work is Asian, and to ask how it "influenced" design, as if design were some Western thing, is remarkably short-sighted. A more useful approach would be to look at the influence of one area of Asia – East Asia (Japan, Korea, China) for example, or, better, just one culture – in a particular style or period. Around the beginnings of the common era there was a lively interchange along the Silk Road between Persia and China (to cite just one example of influence), which had a distinct influence on the development of Western art.
