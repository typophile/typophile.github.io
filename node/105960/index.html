2013-09-02 02:01:43 • bojev
Should have mentioned about Eastlake that the 19th century font had an uppercase and numbers only. Solotype has a fairly accurate recreation of them but Dan X. Solo added a lowercase which is less than fully appropriate to an 1879 design. A better match might be the lower case to Lightface Celtic, designed at almost the same time as Eastlake. They are an appropriate fit in all respects, except that they are not monoline like Eastlake. Most people would barely notice this. Lightface Celtic is digitized as Brioche. Here is the Brioche lowercase width narrowed to match the Eastlake uppercase. Only the lowercase k needs editing to replace a swash with a regular serif. Don
2013-09-02 10:31:23 • donshottype

For the headline condensed sans, I wasn’t able to spot a good alternative that could be close enough to both letters and digits. Actually, it came for nothing for the digits. So, here are only some I like for the headline sans: [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/fontbureau/garage-gothic|Garage Gothic FB]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/urw/nimbus-sans|Nimbus Sans]] Condensed Bold (probably the “closest” but /R leg terminal needing to be straightened and /1, /2 and /8 reworked), [[http://www.behance.net/gallery/Dense-typeface/10231891|Dense]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/hvdfonts/cheap-pine|Cheap Pine]] Sans (nice vintage treatment), [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/dtptypes/newhouse-dt|Newhouse DT]] Super Condensed Bold (quite same work as Nimbus Sans), [[http://www.typography.com/fonts/knockout/overview|Knockout HTF]] (cf Nimbus Sans), [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/mti/placard-mt|Placard MTI]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/k-type/poster-sans|Poster Sans]] (squooshing it), [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/flat-it/rama-gothic|Rama Gothic]] (NS), [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/redrooster/gothic-rr|Gothic RR]]

For the serif, 2 similars: [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/scriptorium/chambord|Chambord]] (very nice this one), [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/versailles|Versailles]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/jagjagvi/old-earthy|Old Earthy]]

May be having a look to [[http://waldenfont.com|Walden Font Co.]] collections would worth a shot too. Even if it’s a US poster specialist, I guess some fonts could fit your needs.
