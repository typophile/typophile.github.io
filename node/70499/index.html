Unfortunately, it is limited to only init, medi and fina forms... :( "The jalt table maps the initial, medial, final or isolated forms to their corresponding alternate forms" http://www.microsoft.com/typography/otspec/features_fj.htm Also I have seen another rare feature called "Stretched Alternate". But I am not sure what tag it uses in opentype. Any suggetions? :)
'Stretched Alternate' is not a registered OTL feature. It is a private feature used by WinSoft in some of their older fonts.

Thnx John. My problem is solved using "Jalt" Justification Alternate. What i did it is that I let nastaleeq ligatures "rlig" correspond to their kasheeda alternates in the "jalt" table. Doing so creates "auto-kashida" in indesign cs5 when Kashidas and Naskh Alignment is checked :)
