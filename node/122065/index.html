I was digging through some old Type ID threads and saw this typeface that donshonttype had posted. Does anyone know what typeface this is or what it is derived from? I suppose donshonttype would know best.
The base font for this lettering is CastleType’s Latin CT, and uses a blend of the condensed width and regular width CastleType’s products are well make and worthy of purchase. Through an oversight I did not mention the foundry in my earlier post. Here is a link to the fonts https://www.myfonts.com/fonts/castletype/latin-ct/ The spurs are added to demonstrate the ease of making something similar to the “Bowery” style of lettering that Nike has used for its clothing http://typophile.com/node/103278 Have fun making your own version. Don

Thank you Tylor for the kudos. The result seems to be successful as a Latinate serif with spurs, but I find the spiky tone of the resulting design to be somewhat aggressive and appropriate mainly for bikers, angry rock bands and the ilk. When the same spurs are used on a font with a softer serif treatment, such as a plush fish tail Tuscan, the effect is more of an ornamented space filler that producdes a font that has a tone suitable for applications from stock certificates to restaurant menus. The spurs might also work for a similar wide range of applications if it is used on font with a Clarendon or thick slab serif. Don
