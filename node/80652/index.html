Legible all-caps font for news copy

March 30, 2011 • ArtMan

Back in the day, the broadcast news organization where I worked produced news copy in ALL CAPS.

This was not a typographical decision, merely a legacy of the [[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baudot_code|Baudot code]] used to drive the wire service (a/k/a “Teletype”) machines we used.

Later the printers were modernized to dot matrix and then daisy wheel models, but ALL CAPS was still the format.

(The theory was that it was faster to type without hitting the shift key. When we did change to mixed-case scripts, much time and energy was expended on deciding what words should be capitalized, but I digress….)

Fast forward to today, when I have data files with thousands of those scripts, and I want to print a bunch of them.

To avoid a very tedious conversion task, and to maintain historic accuracy, I want to keep them in ALL-CAPS — and in a monospaced font at that. For aesthetic reasons, I want all documents in the same font, regardless of the era of creation (and thus, how the original document appeared when printed on its contemporary printer).

So I ask the Typophile community for thoughts on a (relatively) legible, monospaced, font that will not be too hard on the reader and won’t require massive leading to be legible.

Also, because this is a non-revenue project, I especially appreciate open-source or otherwise budget-friendly solutions.

I’ve tried the handful of monospace fonts I have on hand, and don’t like what I’m seeing. Maybe the task is hopeless, but I’m confident that if there’s a reasonable solution, a Typophile-phile will know it.

Regarding the conversion being tedious, you could do it in seconds by pasting the text into a blank Word document, select all, and then select Format > Change Case > Sentence Case. (Or put it in Title Case if you preferred.)

There might be a bit of cleanup, like if there was an acronym you wanted to keep in all caps, but in general it should be very fast.

Not as historically accurate, though.
