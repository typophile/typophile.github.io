December 6, 2006 • Chris Dean

Does anyone know of a version of Century that has small-caps and old-style-figures? I currently favour ITC as it has a large family and a strong italic, but New/Schoolbook and Oldstyle have their merits. If any of them had an expert set, I'd select it as a standard in a second. If not, does anyone know of a font like Century (ITC pref.) that does have SC+OSF? Thanks in advance.
Linotype Centennial might be a good option. http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/centennial-2/ Adrian Frutiger used Century as his inspiration, and it is functionally very similar. It also has a full compliment of small caps and oldstyle figures. As far as I know, though, it does not have a full set of ordinals, fractions, extended ligatures or other elements common to "expert" sets. Still, it's more functional for professional typography than any cut of Century of which I'm aware. David Thometz

Miller from Font Bureau is my favorite "Century."* It has small caps and hybrid figures -- 3/4 height numbers with a tiny bit of ascension/descension. *I know, strictly speaking, it's not a Century. But I see enough similarities to suggest it, especially if Century Oldstyle is an option, which seems further away from the idea of Century-ness (as embodied by Expanded or even ITC) than Miller.
