Fontlab/Superpolator/Font Remix Tools workflow questions

December 13, 2008 • blank

I’m trying to plan a workflow that will allow me to build out a mega family leveraging interpolation and macros. My plan would use Superpolator to interpolate and Font Remix to generate additional masters that can be fed into Superpolator. Does the following workflow makes sense?

• Design the ultralight, regular and bold weights • Use Superpolator to interpolate light and semibold and extrapolate super • Manually tweak the regular into a book weight • Create an MM font and tweak it into a black weight with Remix • After working out the design of condensed and extended widths, create masters by using Remix to do the heavy lifting, finish the work manually, then use Superpolator to interpolate the additional weights

Is there a reason for designing the regular weight between the ultralight and bold, I mean could you not design the regular and generate it with Superpolator using the ultralight and bold ?

I haven’t really used MM to design fonts, but if I did I thought I should design the extremes and then interpolate the weights between.

2008-12-15 14:46:18 • blank
I’d say, worry first about getting your g right. Making a superfamily is a pointless exercise in complexity until you’ve got A-z perfect. All you’ll be doing is proliferating errors. Here you’ve got the classic problem of a glyph design that, light, just might print well at 8 points, but that, bold, will certainly clog. Do you redesign the character to something that interpolates more successfully, or do you change the design more radically, and uninterpolably, as weight progresses? Use a lot of paper!

James, don’t forget what normal MM inter/extrapolation in FL can do. Some of the things you describe can simply be done with FontLab’s “Generate Instance” or “Assign Mask” commands.

Of course, Superpolator and Remix Tools have their special superpowers but for ordinary (global, isotropic) inter/extrapolation the results of MM/FontLab’s tools will be identical.
