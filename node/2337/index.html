Some high resolution inkjets (especially HP ones) can create excellent type when used with special inkjet coated papers. This is because they are coated with (I seem to recall) a ceramic-derived coating that absorbs and dries the ink immediately, stopping ink from spreading through to the fibres of the paper itself. However, the paper is very expensive, and so its not really practical as a medium for testing fonts, as you should be making LOTS of print outs through a face's development cycle. Far better to use a laser printer, where it can provide good quality results consistently and inexpensively. Then, when its close to completion, sent it to an imagesetter at an output bureau for the final tests.
