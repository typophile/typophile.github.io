Book set in Sovereign Light

October 9, 2007 • nert

Is it just me…

I bought a book (The End of Mr. Y by Scarlett Thomas) which I’m greatly enjoying aside from one tiny annoyance - it’s set in [[http://www.type.co.uk/index.php?p=a_to_z_listing&let=S&id=8157|Sovereign Light.]]

I don’t know whether it is just me, or the fact I think it’s been set with kerning and leading that’s just not “quite right”, but it seems a strange choice for a novel, and one I’ve never seen before. I find the face itself quite distracting, as though it’s fighting with itself to decide what to be (“I want to be a display face! Oh, wait, no, too scary standing out, I’ll be the weird kid instead.") if you know what I mean?

No idea where I’m going with this, other than - thoughts? It is a good book, I’m just finding it hard going reading it. Has an interest in typography really come the point of visual aesthetics over content?

I’ll go away now.. :)

I certainly wouldn’t want to read a whole book set in that. Okay for display, or very short text in certain advertising or brochure usage. Within the more modest range of good “transparent” text typefaces there is still plenty of diversity for variations in the tone the typographer wants to convey.
