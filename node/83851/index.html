AE or Ae?

July 17, 2011 • daverowland

In a script font, where all caps use is a massive no-no, would it make more sense for the AE glyph to be Ae, same with OE going to Oe? I'm thinking of doing it, not even as alternates but as the main glyph, since nobody would ever use AE or OE in anything other than an all caps setting... Or would they?
From my understanding, Æ is meant to be its own character just like /A/ or /E/ or any other letter. Hence the reason why it's common to find /AE/ and not /Ae/, since it is treated as a single letter and not two different letters (as in the case of /f_i/ or most other ligatures). But I may have the wrong understanding so don't quote me on this.

2011-07-18 18:58:15 • daverowland
Sure you can do Ae and Oe ligatures if you want. They would be contextual ligatures and you can name them A_e/O_e. Just don't put them in the slots named AE and OE. Those are distinct letters of certain alphabets and cannot be replaced by a non-ligature version, nor by an uppercase-lowercase mix like Ae/Oe. That's like writing “Water” in this way: Vvater.
