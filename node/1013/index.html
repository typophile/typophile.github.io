Simon and Andreas, I don't think eszett qualifies, because Hrant asked for the best letter of the Latin alphabet. An alphabet is a set – sometimes the full set but most often a subset – of a script used to write a particular language. So 'Latin alphabet' implies the letters used to write the Latin language, as distinct from the superset of the Latin script, which is used to write many languages with a great variety of alphabets.

Quite in time–in today's USA Today–a reference to the design method used by Matthew Carter, who does not post to this list: 'Carter typically starts a new font by sketching the lowercase letters h and o on his Mac. Then he creates related straight letters like i from the h and round letters like c from the o, keeping a close eye on their weight and overall form by often printing them out; <…> He works last on more "capricious" characters, like the lowercase g and uppercase Q, whose curlicues allow for more artful flourishes. (Experts typically identify fonts by the traits of these letters.)' [www.usnews.com/usnews/issue/030901/misc/1font.htm] I understand that this thread is not about the sequence of steps in designing a typeface. And yet, it looks like the most fundamental features of Carter's typefaces are pretty much set before he gets to draw those 'more "capricious" characters', and that the identity of the typeface depends more on the 'plain' letterforms than on the 'fancy' ones. ps: No, Cyrillic zhe–looking like }|{–is not my 'favourite' character.
