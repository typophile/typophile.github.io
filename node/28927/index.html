Digital whiteboard technology has been around–and sucked–for at least five years. The intuitive drawing interface has been done before–take a look at Alias Sketchbook Pro–and Pixar created a similar program with automatic vectorization while working on The Incredibles. An automated 2d physics simulation program is next to useless for most people, and those who would want one are going to need to stop constantly and plug in new variables at the keyboard, making the giant drawing area useless.

WIMP still wins.

Windows, Icons, Mouse, Pull-Down Menu

It’s an acronym for the standard computer GUI that came out of Xerox PARC in the late 70s/early 80s and was “perfected” by Microsoft, Apple, and other software companies that helped define what GUIs came to be. Combined with CLUI it’s created a computing experience that has generally not been beaten; although in certain circumstances–especially interfaces for the handicapped or tablet drawing–better things have come along.
