2010-01-02 00:40:29 • bowfinpw

You need to create a PDF with a font, deactivate the font, then open the PDF in Adobe Reader or Acrobat to see the substitute font. If you’re seeing Myriad, you must be deactivating the font and viewing the document in the source app, such as InDesign or Illustrator. You will never see the Adobe Serif or Adobe Sans fonts in graphics apps, only in PDFs.

By the way, the Adobe Serif and Adobe Sans substitute fonts might be tricky to create samples of since they don’t have a “natural” state–they take on the proportions and weight of whatever they are trying to emulate. So the appearance will depend a lot on what font they are trying to emulate. Still, it is probably enough to show the general characteristics of the fonts which are present regardless of weight and proportion (serif styles, general construction, etc.).

With this in mind, you might want to include a note about what font is being emulated, or maybe even create samples using several different fonts with different proportions and weights.
