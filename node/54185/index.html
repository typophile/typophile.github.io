Ligatures involving punctuation?

January 30, 2009 • someonesplendid

Hi all, was interested to see if anyone has ever come across examples of ligated forms involving punctuation. ideas that spring to mind would be apostrophe + lower s and o + apostrophe. surely given the long and rich history of type design and experimentation, there should be some great examples out there.

A ligature of o + apostrophe might look an awful lot like an o with horn, which is a single letter in Vietnamese.

Hrant, I was not aware that OT was the starting point for anything new under the sun. The point I’m dancing and drumming around, waiting for either Ginger Rogers or Ginger Baker to join in on, is that punctuation is normally, (and this is a long, old and wide normality), designed, spaced, and composed to stand apart and be noticed, not ligated into the word, or blended like a frappe.

I have recently designed, spaced and kerned a terminal swash e that embraces .!?, but I left a wide berth for recognition in the composition for the reasons I’ve stated above. I would love to see some appropriate use of OT for making better l.c., u.c or figure specific punctuation, especially in faces with divergent parameters between character groups, like fonts with very small x-hts, large differences in uc/lc weight or widths, but I think of this as involving non-ligaturish design.

Cheers and thanks, if you meant something nice above.
