Question about InDesign Master pages

November 8, 2008 • kiko

Hi all

I’m working on a book in InDesign CS3. It has 20 chapters and i’m using two master pages, one for numbered pages and another for unumbered pages. I want two use a header with the chapter name for each page of that chapter. How can i do that? I thought in using 20 child masters (being the numbered master the parent) for each chapter but in seems to me that adding so many masters isn’t the right way to do it.

If anyone can help i’ll appreciate it

Thanks in advance Regards

There’s a way to do this without a bunch of “children.” It’s similar to the way you insert an automatic folio on a master page.

On your Master pages, when you set up your running head (or foot or whatever), in the text frame where you want your folio and running head, use Text > Insert Special Character > Auto Page Number to insert a folio placeholder and Text > Insert Special Character > Section Marker to insert a running head placeholder.

What you’ll see is a letter (or number) equivalent to your Master Page prefix standing in for your folio and the word “Section” standing in for your chapter head. Format these placeholders as desired.

Then you want to set the first page of each chapter as the start of a new section and in Numbering & Section Options… input your chapter name in the Section Marker field.

Then the “Section” placeholder on your master page(s) will be replaced with the contents of Section Marker field for every page of that section.

I hope this makes sense without visuals and accomplishes what you’re after.

– Kent.
