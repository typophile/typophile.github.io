November 17, 2014 • arildkumar

Hey!

I ’m currently in post-production on a short film I shot in the Democratic Republic of Congo - a portrait of three heroic Congolese women. However, I have been struggling with typographic choices, choosing fonts.

I’d love it if you would take a look at the (VERY INCOMPLETE) first cut, and give me suggestions on fonts for titles and subtitles: https://drive.google.com/file/d/0B8ALFcHTB2v0dU95VnFLV3ZZTjA/view?usp=sharing

I tried out different “African style fonts” for the main title, but they all looked stereotypical and wrong. I much prefer what I call “respectful fonts” that emphasize the heroic aspect of these ladies, but it would still be nice to get something organic in there. I considered hand painted fonts, but haven’t found anything good.

As you can probably tell I’m a noob in the world of typography, so anything you could tell me would be greatly appreciated. :)

You are right not to use stereotypical ‘African’ faces. The font doesn’t need to reflect Africa or the struggle of the women. Its purpose is to aid communication. Something simple, that works well on screen (mobile/desktop/television - wherever your film will be seen) will need certain characteristics such as large x-height, if serifed they should be sturdy serifs, it should be a low stoke contrast (between the thick and thins) so anything that adheres to these paramenters will work. Ideally something that was designed specifically for screen will match, take a look at the work of Fontsmith, they work in this field a lot, albeit with a branding slant… best wishes for the film

What John said. The contrast between a “neutral” typeface and the powerful images will serve better the message (among other considerations, even a non stereotypical but “pretty” typeface would give the wrong connotation to the video). You can however, use different typefaces for titles and subtitles, where, in the titles, you could have a bit more personality, perhaps using a slab-serif, as suggested. Before I can give more specuific suggestions, it would be useful to know what kind of budget do you have.

Here some possibilities, from sturdier to more vibrant:

Aleo (http://fontfabric.com/aleo-free-font/) for titles, Lato (http://www.latofonts.com/) for subtitles.

PT Serif for titles, PT Sans for subtitles (http://paratype.com/public/), perhaps the sturdier Caption cut.

Alegreya (http://www.fontsquirrel.com/fonts/alegreya) for titles, Alegreya Sans (http://www.huertatipografica.com/fonts/alegreya-sans-ht) for subtitles.
