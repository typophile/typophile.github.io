Typeface feedback/suggestions for poster series

January 17, 2007 • ms. pater

I am working on this hypothetical poster series: [img:sites/default/files/old-images/blossom_3557.jpg] [img:sites/default/files/old-images/leaf_3574.jpg] [img:sites/default/files/old-images/spruce_5399.jpg] My target audience is men and women ages 20-30. It focuses on preserving nature and is designed to encourage people to recycle. The posters measure 8x16 inches. The pattern on the left of the layout is a grid of recycle signs to add depth and create texture. The bottom right corner of the series is rounded and flows with the recycle sign to reinforce the theme and draw your attention to a line of information on the bottom. It reads, “The Rethink Nature campaign strives to protect, manage, and restore the world’s forests.” It also has a link to go to if you are interested in learning more about the environment and want to make a difference. I am struggling with the main typography. My prof wants me to choose typefaces that resemble the characteristics of the illustration, each being different. She says the Spruce poster is fine, but the blossom should be more organic, but helvetica rounded is too much. I am thinking serif for the Leaf poster, maybe something something pointy? I am currently using Whitney. I am open to all suggestions. There is also talk of moving the type up and bumping the illustration down to seperate the text. It makes the illustrations harder to work within the space, so I am looking for a solution we both can agree on. Thanks
Whitney works great for me. If you want to go serif, try softer, more organic faces like Dolly or Winchester. To get around the problem of choosing different typefaces (and thus risking some consistency), play around with the words. If your message is strong enough, the type doesn't have to be treated much differently. You can use color to accentuate the keywords, too. After all, aren't we talking about a poster series here? By to by, this definitely calls for some Chris Lozos action :-)
