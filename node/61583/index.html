Best practices for laserprinting fonts on Mac OS X?

September 2, 2009 • butterick

After 10 yrs in the wilds of Windows I returned to the Mac at the beginning of this year. For the most part I’m happy, with one glaring exception — I am stunned at how difficult it is to get good, consistent typographic output on a laserprinter.

In days of yore, every app would offload page rendering to the printer driver, and thus fonts were rendered the same way in every program. Now, every app seems to use a hodgepodge of system services, proprietary logic, and printer magic to get the job done, and 10 pt Sabon will look slightly different depending on the path it takes to the printer.

Is there a preferred method for getting the highest-quality laserprinted output? Believe me, I’ve asked on the relevant technical support forums, but talking about rendering problems of 10pt serifs is like convincing people that your imaginary friend is really coming to dinner.

Might be worth going via PDF and trying different PDF viewers to determine which provides the best output.
