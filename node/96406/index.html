Yugyug, that font you used 100 years ago would seem to be Insignia http://www.myfonts.com/search/insignia/fonts/

As for the Italian metalwork: it’s easy to find Art-Deco-inspired fonts that have SOME of those characteristics, but I don’t know about finding a single font that has them all.

Casablanca has the through lines and the round O, but not the distinctive R http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/redrooster/casablanca-rr/

Mark Simonson’s beautiful Mostra Nuova would be good for setting the word “ROMA” at the bottom of your photo. Mostra even has the triangular crossbar. http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/marksimonson/mostra-nuova/

An example of a font with some Art Deco characteristics: Copasetic http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/cheapprofonts/copasetic-nf-pro/

And if you want to spend a few hours poring over a thousand other fonts like that, a search on myfonts.com for “Deco,” “moderne” and “geometric” turns up a TON of possibilities (though I wouldn’t expect any exact matches)… http://www.myfonts.com/search/deco+moderne+geometric/fonts/
