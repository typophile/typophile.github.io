Anyone else find this character a nightmare? Any tips for moving from the ‘o’ (which I assume precedes the ‘e’ for most designs) to the ‘e’?

(Is my question a little broad?)

Don’t neglect their close relative “c”. The bottom half of the “c” is usually echoed in the “e” but can be considerably different from the same curve segment of “o”.

From there you need to decide how you want to close the bowl, and there are lots of ways to do that. Could you post an image of what you have so far?

2009-03-04 16:20:19 • xtianhoff

I don’t think there is one letter or glyphs harder to draw than an other one. To my point of view the hardest thing is to determine the way I will draw the stem (H, l), the bowl or curve (Oo) and the serif if it’s a serif one. Once those things are set up, the rest will be easier to do. One thing I found, may other one has probably found out also, is to work with no extrema point for the curve when I draw the basic design. Once you’ve found the right design than you place those extrema point. That way to me works pretty well.
