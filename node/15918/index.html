2005-10-30 13:40:05 • Martin LAllier
I think that, when one is making new fonts, one should make the numbers match the letters. Many historical blackletter types did try to do this. Many others simply took figures from other designs that did not match. Today, designers never know what customers will use their fonts for. I say, give them the best tools possible. A "literal" blackletter revival is not an adequate tool: new characters will have to be designed and inserted into the font (i.e., accented characters for languages other than German, the Euro symbol, mathematical symbols, etc.). Why not numbers, too? If you are a designer working with a font whose numbers do not "match", maybe you could try to pull numbers from an other font that are a little better? Or, maybe you don't really need so many numbers (in which case, it isn't a big issue). Or you could just use what the font delivers, and make a note to yourself to keep these questions in mind for future projects and/or licensing decisions?
