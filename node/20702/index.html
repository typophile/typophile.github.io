2006-06-16 01:59:08 • bowfinpw
I never used the LeRoy system, but I did use a Varigraph and another mechanical pantograph system whose name I can't recall. The Varigraph had several sets of templates with several lettering styles. I can remember using Stymie Bold and Franklin Gothic(ish) styles. You traced using a stylus in the template (metal), the pen nibs were connected to the other end of a pantograph arm. You could size and slant of desired lettering by adjusting dials near fulcrum of arm. After outlining in ink, I had to fill in the characters by hand with India ink. These kind of lettering devices were very common with architects and engineers. As for the Post Old Style, on closer look the "A" is somewhat similar. (Sorry about poor repro... quick snapshot. I could scan tomorrow if you'd like.) Obviously my sample is the bold weight (Post Old Style Roman No. 2), but notice the difference in the cap S treatment between 10 pt. (slightly slanted) and the 24 pt. (upright). Also note the 24 pt. lowercase "y"... I love this old stuff!!! (edit-a-bit): There are 2 cap "S" in the 10 pt. sample. One is roman (POST) and one is italic??? (STYLE)! [img:sites/default/files/old-images/Post-oldstyle-1_3880.png]
