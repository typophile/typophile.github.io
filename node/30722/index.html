2007-01-18 22:55:47 • noftus

No, it isn’t.

A new font is almost never needed for the creation of one, single logo. If an existing font does not meet your or your client’s needs, it is usually better in terms of time and budget to just create a vector art logo. A fount entails so much more work and investment that the few letters or symbols found in a logo.

2007-01-18 22:56:00 • dan_reynolds

If you’re talking about incorporating letter shapes into a logo design, most font licences specifically allow it, and with those that don’t it could be argued that you can since this is a customary fair use of fonts. (Be careful who you argue with, though.)

Notable exceptions include most dingbat and picture fonts, since the glyphs in those fonts are essentially logos already.

If you have the time and your client has the budget, by all means create a new custom font for the project.
