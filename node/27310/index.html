timd…I think that’s more of an old wives tale than anything.

Yes, you own the copyright BY DEFAULT on any work you create. In the US, though, the damages you could potentially collect from an infringment case are very much reduced unless you REGISTER the copyright. That doesn’t require a lawyer. $45 and a completed form is all you need:

http://www.copyright.gov/register/

Registering a Trademark is a separate issue:

http://www.uspto.gov/

TMs are where having a lawyer can come in handy.

the whole mail-it-to-yourself envelope things is indeed an old wives tale, bogus. Mostly becasue they can be faked easily, and are thrown out of court

If you mail it to yourself via REGISTERED MAIL, the US Postal Service maintains an UNBROKEN CHAIN OF EVIDENCE (signature logs) of every individual who handled the piece, from the time the Post Office accepts it until the time it is delivered into your hands. The received envelope MUST remain unopened in order for the chain to remain unbroken. While a person may be able to fake the USPS round stamps that mark every seam on the envelope, the signature log CANNOT be faked…unless, of course, you have access to the safes in which the logs are stored.
