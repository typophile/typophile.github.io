Not one of the riders of Apocalypse

March 30, 2006 • phase23

I was directed to this link

http://www.thedesignerslife.com/

and had to share it. If you can stand to actually read the copy, it gets funnier (and in many ways sadder) as you go.

I remember years ago there was a big concern about “desktop publishers” proliferating the world with bad design and undermining the design profession’s reputation and cankering the value we provide our clients. I think it is pretty obvious that clients that would use the type of service above, aren’t the clients I/we look for.

Maybe it has been simply my own maturation process, but I think it there has been an industry wide shift in our definition of ourselves, and subsequently the way our clients perceive our value. We are not wrists that simply push type and pixels around on a screen, but we are strategists, visual and emotional anthropologists, interpreters and above all communicators.

Sorry for the mini-manifesto.

J.
