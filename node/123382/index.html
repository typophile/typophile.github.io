This is a font that was used for a monogram for a baby announcement that was produced by Cranes. I’m trying to laser cut it in wood and need the font to do so. Thanks for your help.[img:sites/default/files/old-images/423134_346665608705803_874370002_n_6266.jpg]

AFAIK this is not a retail font. It could be a modern hybrid of styles produced for Cranes. The base letters are in a style developed in the early 19th century, which is known as “Tuscan” and is characterized by the fishtail terminals. The clean line rationalist structure and minimalist flower ornamentation reminds me of some 18th century initials. If you only need the letters L, C and I your best solution might be to have them scanned to make vector outlines for your laser cutter. Needless to say, I could be wrong and someone has made this into a font. All I can say is that I have not seen anything like it in the sources, both printed and online, that I usually check. Don
