I was just wondering if you guys or gals could inform about what graphic design conference I shouldn’t miss.

It’s not graphic design per se, but it IS related. Have you considered the International Calligraphy Conference? If you have any interest at all regarding letterforms, then I would say don’t miss this. I think anyone who loves to design type should go to at least one of these conferences in their life time.

This year it’s in Boston in late July. It’s not your typical conference. It’s very hands on, focusing on learning as much as possible in the Lettering Arts. Workshops all week by the top designers in the world. Back in 1997 Jovica Veljovic taught Font Design. This year I plan to take Stephen Rapp’s Font Lab workshop (since I’m just now learning how to use it).

Some of the benefits:

  • It's not expensive... $1300.00 (US) covers food, lodging and workshops.
  • It's a week long with plenty of extra activities in the evenings (ie. lectures, parties, etc)
  • Meet top professionals and see them work.
  • The gallery of participants' work is amazing.
    • --- The last conference I went to was in 2004 (I taught a couple of workshops, but had plenty of time to soak in all the things happening around me). When I got back, I was inspired for weeks.

    You can get more info at the Odyssey website: http://www.2010calligraphyconference.com/
