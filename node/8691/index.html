Metal Letters for Signage

January 17, 2005 • Zara Evens

I recently came across this set of metal letters amongst my late grandfather's possessions. I assume they were once used for signage of some sort, though I haven't seen anything like them and am quite curious as to their origin. The box is quite old, made of wood and has absolutely no identifiable markings.

They are made of a very thin metal, (about one inch in height) perhaps aluminum or something similar and are hand painted orange and white. It contains the full alphabet with numerals and punctuation. On the right side of the box (see photos) there is a small metal board with what resembles lead em spaces, though made of a lightweight magnetic material.

Has anyone seen a set like this?



Is that cool or what.
Looking at the shapes, colors and letter distribution, my guess is they're for placing down and photographing.

My guess is that Hrant is on the right track, and that they are for photographic titles, in films or for slides.

The magnets could act as spacers, so you can create a nice drop shadow.

I've seen a similar style of art deco lettering used for titling in a 35mm colour slide show (but made out of a thick white material). The slides dated from the mid '50s.

The appeal of this "Broadway" kind of Deco style at that late date would be because of its ease of manufacture.

While there were commercial lettering kits available, it's the kind of project you would find in Popular Mechanics magazine. Or maybe even an amateur photography magazine.
