I’ve found that trying to read the mind of the person or people who did this is surprisingly instructive; there are a lot of twists to it (even though they might have in fact followed the main river, not exploring all the tributaries).

Why did they avoid the UC form in the first place? It’s not like they were avoiding diagonals, since there are two diagonal “A"s there.

Once they decided to use a lc form, I guess making all the stems thick and implementing stoke contrast normally (via thinner horizontals) made the “m” too wide/strong.

One way making [only] the rightmost stem thick is better distribution of color (since the right side of the “O” is heavy while the left side of the “A” is very light). I believe this is the logic behind it.

But if they had h-flipped the “A"s they would have:

  • better filled the “TA” gap.
  • had the option of making [only] the middle stem of the “m” thick, which is less jarring.

The “A” must have a thin left diagonal and a thick right one? Bah. http://www.flickr.com/photos/48413419@N00/4091231975/in/set-72157627102851774

Moral of the story: if you’re going to break some rules, break all the ones that don’t work.
