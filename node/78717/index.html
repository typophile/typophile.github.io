2011-02-04 19:01:41 • Mark Simonson

Ok, so I give the iA Writer a try. 452 words so far and the overall impression – passable.

Two biggest issues with it so far

(a) It sometimes gets sluggish, by a fraction of a second, but it is noticeable and it is annoying. I would type - tap, tap, tap, tap and it would go - click, click, click, pause, click. This also causes auto-corrections sneak in when the pause happens right before the space is hit.

(b) In a portrait mode the keys are too small and it is fairly inconvenient to hold iPad on a lap and type (while sitting in the armchair). Latter leads to a strange posture, which will probably give me some odd sore muscles tomorrow, and former causes me frequently hist spacebar instead of the keys in the bottom row. That in turn seems to cause the staggering from (a) and needing to go back and retype the whole word. And there is no way to backspace the whole word at once, so it is a hassle.

In a landscape mode things are better, but the usable space where the actual typing occurs becomes too small and so it’s easy to loose the context. All in all though, the landscape mode is a way to go.

–

Positioning the cursor with a finger tap worked out to be not a big issue. The font size is big, so it is easy to hit the right position. Plus they also made the cursor jump to the side of the word when tapping on a word - this is a very nice touch.

In short – not ideal, but it is $1.99 well spent.

It might be worth mentioning, I do have a Bluetooth keyboard and paired it with my iPad when I first got it. Works great. However, after about a month or so, I realized I never used it. I stopped bringing it with me and eventually un-paired it so it could be used for something else.

In general, if I have something substantial to type, I don’t do it on the iPad. For the typing I do do on the iPad, the landscape view virtual keyboard is fine. I only use the portrait view keyboard when I’m too lazy to switch to landscape view just to type in a password or something short.

That said, a Bluetooth keyboard is the best option if you intend to do more than a little typing in portrait mode.

I wouldn’t recommend the dock keyboard. Clever, but a one trick pony. You can’t use it with the iPad in landscape orientation, for example.

One nice thing about using a Bluetooth keyboard with the iPad is that the arrow keys and a lot of common keyboard shortcuts (such as the ones for copy, paste, undo, select all) work, which is not true of the virtual keyboard. Plus, the virtual keyboard disappears when you’re using a physical one, giving you the full screen for your document.

2011-02-05 15:19:45 • Mark Simonson

There are pros and cons to the dock. Some pros include that you can charge your iPad while typing, which you generally can’t do if you just stand your iPad in portrait orientation. (Of course not everyone wants to type in that orientation.) Also it’s very stable, and has some special function keys which a bluetooth keyboard doesn’t have.

But there are some cons, too, including that it only works in one orientation, and you must remove your iPad from its case.

Here is Macworld’s review which compared the dock to a bluetooth keyboard: http://www.macworld.com/article/150600/2010/04/ipadwhichkeyboard.html
