Hello fellow typophilians,

As some of you already know Expletus Sans Basic will soon be released in the Google font directory. I am also working on Expletus Serif, but I just need to work on something new and exciting. So I started off closing the gaps in Expletus Sans. Nick Shinn already pointed out to me I should do that, but I was stubborn. Yes Nick, you were right. Anyhow, ‘just closing some gaps’ (it’s not that easy off coarse) wasn’t satisfying enough. First off all I want it to become very readable. Secondly, I want it to be very readable ON Screen, for web use on small sizes. Since @font-face I am disappointed with the number of rivals for Verdana (readable at small sizes). So, naive as I am, I decided to give it a go myself. I’ll upload a picture of how it performs in a few weeks in the critique section. To get to the point, I would like to know if any of you have some good information or links on what makes a typeface perform well on screen.

Thanks in advance,

Jasper de Waard

Dave Berlow and Roger Black preach what they call the Verdana principle: if you want it to work as screen type it needs to be as wide as Verdana, the x-height needs to be as high as Verdana, and the apertures need to be as big as Verdana. And, of course, have it hinted by experts.

But a little advice: don’t get hung up on technical stuff this early in your font design career. I feel like I made a mistake by getting wrapped up in stuff like language support, big families, legibility, OpenType, scripting, etc.. Doing that stuff is really tough without a solid foundation in creating good looking fonts without bells and whistles. Right now I have several type families in development that took far too long because of this. I’m going to get them done and just focus on simple stuff for a year or two. Do yourself a favor and don’t get into this position —just work on the basics for a few years.
