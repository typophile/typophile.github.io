Egyptienne F ☞ such a small family

July 15, 2010 • John Lyttle

Hi. [[http://www.fonts.com/FindFonts/Detail.htm?pid=242729&/cgi-bin/MsmGo.exe?grab_id=0&page_id=8307&query=EGYPTIENNE%20F&SCOPE=Fonts|Egyptienne F]] makes me curious because it’s such a limited typeface, especially considering Adrian Frutiger is the patriarch of this family. There’s 55 Roman, 56 Italic, 65 Bold and 75 Black, but why not a 66 Bold Italic and a 76 Black Italic?

I am designing a brochure and using Egyptienne F in combination with [[http://www.fonts.com/FindFonts/Detail.htm?pid=421588&/cgi-bin/MsmGo.exe?grab_id=0&page_id=57440&query=UNIVERS&SCOPE=Fonts|Univers]]—a whopping 27 fonts in this family pack. However, the lack of bold-italic in Egyptienne F is a bit troublesome.

It seems there must be a story behind why the type design screeched to a halt with just four fonts. Anyone here familiar with the history of this typeface?

Univers was the first mega family being released (1957). Before that 4 cuts was normal (Egyptienne F 1956).
