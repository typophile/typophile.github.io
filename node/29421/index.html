Agree I would take a close look into Gill’s attributes. If you are using the semi circle as a “C” then you might look at the ending angles. On your current “C” of the gill sans ends up on a vertical ( 90 degrees angle) and the bottom one at a different one.

Also take a look at Gill Sans “@” and compare the stroke weight. I agree that the ear of the “g” is too long. Play with it so that the “g” is still centered. You might want to shorten the “C” on top a tad resize it but keep the stroke weight and the original ending angles or if you modify them, keep both at the same angle.

Show us the accompanied text!

Hope this helps. Thanks

2006-11-13 22:21:49 • kco

I’d hate to ruffle any feathers here, but I like the meta direction much more. However, I do agree even with this version that the terminal angles are not working well together. Maybe try one of the following things:

  1. Alter the upper terminal to relate to the lower terminal.

OR

  1. Pull the bottom C terminal up to be even with the g’s, then slice it flat (even with the g’s). Use the resulting implied frame (produced from the C’s upper and lower 90 degree terminals) to begin your logotype.

Either approach requires more refinement than is stated, but it’s a start.
