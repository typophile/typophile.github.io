November 13, 2017 • JamesBern

I have a number of 1890s-era maps from the USGS that pair Didot and an uknown (to me) sans-serif with matching proportions. This series of maps always strikes me as particularly satisfying typographically, but also leaves me wondering about the sans. I’m not aware of too many in existence before the mid-1890s, and this one looks fairly mature and particularly well-matched in proportion and character to Didot. Any ideas what it might be or where it came from?

All the type on your maps is hand lettered / engraved, not typeset.

Sans serif type first appeared in foundry specimens in the early 1800s (1816, if this source is correct): http://www.designhistory.org/Type_milestones_pages/SansSerif.html
