2005-10-07 05:47:14 • Thomas Phinney

My projects tend to branch off in the exploratory/sketching phase. I may sit down with one idea, and as I’m finishing up the basic charset, I realize how it could be different and set the first draft aside as I explore the other angles. Sometimes I take the tangents so far that I have to set them aside as completely different to-do projects. Depending on what these tangent explorations turn up, I may focus on one of them or set them all aside and return to the original draft. (This is all in terms of text designs where the slightest variation could lead to a legitimately separate family.)

ADD, perhaps. It makes for quite a few ‘unfinished’ designs, but I consider them my sketchbook as almost all of my work is done digitally.

However, if I begin with a clear vision of the final product, I hammer out the basic charset in one sitting and focus on the adjustments over a few days until it’s usable. Vandermark is a prime example of a leisure project that had my undivided attention for a string of weeknights. I still have broader plans for variations, but I felt that the core concept is there as a single font. Inasmuch as it has a full charset and kerning, and I’m sufficiently pleased with the look of things, it’s a finished product.

Which leads me to ask… when do you all consider a project ‘finished?" When you can retail it? When it has 8 or more weights? When it doesn’t crash your system when installed? I have quite a few functioning fonts that are still under construction in my opinion.
