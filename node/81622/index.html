The Radak says the t'nu'ot ge'dol'lot, and t'nu'ot ke'tan'not, define what is and is not a shvah-na and shvah-nach. Some people mistakenly thing that a t'nu'ah is a sound pattern, and can not define what is and is not a shvah-na and shvah-nach by analysis of context. Of course, this is an incorrect understanding of what is a t'nu'ah. According to the view of Rabbi (Shlomo) Zalman (of) Henna (Henau), a shvah-na and shvah-nach can in deed be defined by analysis of context. As Prof. Aron Dotan of Tel Aviv University (retired) told me explicitly, "everything is in the nikkud". Rabbi Zalman Henna defines a shvah-nah as a shvah which follows a t'nu'ot ge'dola, and shvah-nach as a shvah which follows a t'nu'ah ke'tan'nah. Here is his chart from Henna's book, "Yesod HaNikkud".
