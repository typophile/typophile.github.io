IMO architects' taste in typography is anything but contemporary. They seem to be stuck in the modernist vein, and these examples are conservative like that, traditional modernist identities.

My parents were architects, and used a lot of Clarendon (Letraset) back in the day. I made a Christmas card, a painting plus words in Letraset Clarendon, c. 1960. Sorry, Catalonia, that’s retro.

Not quite sure what the box kite has to do with Cooper Union. Yeah, isometric perspective as optical illusion, we get it – a cliché/meme used in the Harvard logo too, more about the graphic designer’s take on architectural design than communicating a business/institution to its audience.

Why does architecture still need to be evoked by referencing the simplest of mechanically drafted shapes, those made with compasses and ruler? How very masonic! Or is it some kind of de Stijl thing? Strange, after such iconic progressive 20th century buildings as the Ronchamps chapel and the Sydney Opera House, and any number of polymorphic Autocaded blobs by Gehry and Foster.
