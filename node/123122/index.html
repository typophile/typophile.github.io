Merriam Webster site redesign

February 5, 2015 • jmbrown

Today I noticed Merriam Webster has had its website redesigned. The new site leans toward text-based design, which is in keeping with a certain trend, and seems to lean away from traditional, reference-publication design; to me, the geometric sans-serif now used for the entry words says, “twee,” more than it does, “authoritative.” I don’t especially care about the brand identity MW’s trying to foster, but I do think the visual hierarchy of the new site is hard to navigate. Below are images of an entry as of Jan 2014 and Feb 2015. Thoughts? [img:sites/default/files/old-images/MW original_5737.png] [img:sites/default/files/old-images/MW redesign_3725.png]

“text-based design”

My guess is that dictionary companies are a lot less profitable these days. Why buy a dictionary when you can look it up on the web? Going with mostly text saves bandwidth and reduces cost. I like the old layout better, but the current trend is for lighter-weight fonts.
