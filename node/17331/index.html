2006-01-16 19:20:56 • Miss Tiffany

How much are you looking to spend per night, Dan? In my experience, the difference in quality between ‘cheap’ accomodation in London – which is never actually cheap – and moderately expensive is huge: much bigger than the difference in price.

Beware of discount hotel booking websites. Find the direct contact information for a hotel, phone them and ask them what is the best price they can give you. Most hotels have promotional rates of various kinds that may not be listed on their websites. If you make it sound like you are shopping around, you can usually get a good deal. [This is true just about anywhere, by the way, not just London.]

This is the cheapest place I have stayed where I would even consider staying again:

http://www.a1tourism.com/uk/ruskin1.html

This is where my family always stayed when I was a child. It is clean, which is the main thing you want. You do have to share a shower, though, and last time I stayed there the bed sagged badly. The best thing about the Ruskin is the location, which is in Bloomsbury, directly opposite the side of the British Museum, and in easy walking distance of lots of bookshops.

Now when I go to London, I almost always stay at The Rembrandt, which is more expensive but is directly across the street from Brompton Oratory.
