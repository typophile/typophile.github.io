“…when comparing FF Johannes G (which I was aware of prior to my first post) with Gutenberg Bibelschrift, the former seemed very chunky and misshapen, while the latter appeared more elegant and sympathetic to the handwritten forms…”

I think the choice is more one of purpose here. While Bibelschrift has a cleaner look more reminiscent of calligraphy, FF Johannes G tries to get at the feel of early letterpress printing. If you are going more for the likeness of the printed book, FF Johannes G is the better choice. If the calligraphic hand which inspired the type is what you are after, then you might want Bibelschrift. Both are valid.
