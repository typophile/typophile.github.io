Tax Season: Do your taxes for free online

February 5, 2010 • SuperUltraFabulous

For my fellow type-lovers here in the USA you just might qualify to prepare your taxes for free online (Of course you never have to pay to file your taxes the old fashioned way… I’m just not that committed—or smart).

Browse these free file recommended companies from IRS: http://apps.irs.gov/app/freeFile/jsp/index.jsp?ck

For the most part, to qualify you cannot have earned more than $57,000 (sorry David Berlow)

If you make under 31,000 you can use the very slick TurboTax Freedom Project Edition for your federal taxes, which also allows you to file you state taxes for free in the following states AL, AR, AZ, GA, IA, ID, KY, MI, MN, MO, MS, NY, NC, ND, OK, OR, RI, SC, VT, WV: http://turbotax.intuit.com/taxfreedom/

I live in California in which state taxes cannot be prepared for free with Turbo Tax.

I checked with the California Franchise Tax Board http://www.ftb.ca.gov/

If you made under $160,739 (sorry Stephen Coles) you can file your taxes directly the State of California. Check with state’s tax board to see of the have a similar program.

Then when you are done preparing your taxes for free and saving all kinds of money, think of me while you roll around your bed sensually with your fat tax returns :-)

Perhaps you may not qualify (’cause sexy type designers are super rich), but you probably know a student, friend, or relative who does. Pass this info on and help a brotha’ out.

Mikey :-)

PS… Pay special attention the Earned Income Credit.
