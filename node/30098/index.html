2006-12-21 13:44:58 • dezcom
Chris, I'm somewhat surprised that you found the InDesign kerning numbers confusing, as they are exactly the same as what you are using when you design your fonts--1000 to the em. If you use keyboard short-cuts to kern, I see it would be confusing shifting from one system to another. Googling I found this tip from Ilene Strizver: "If you use both QuarkXPress and InDesign (or another Adobe design application), remember that these programs kern to different unit values. QuarkXPress kerns to 1/200 of a unit, while the default for InDesign is 1/1000 of a unit. This means that a kern value of -5 in QuarkXPress is equal to -25 in InDesign. You can make the transition back and forth simpler by changing InDesign’s default unit value to 20/1000 (equivalent to QuarkXPress’s 1/200) in Preferences/Units & Increments." On the shortcuts in InDesign, from the help file: "Press Alt+Left/Right Arrow (Windows) or Option+Left/Right Arrow (Mac OS) to decrease or increase the kerning between two characters. When you kern using these shortcuts, the increment used is 1/50 of the em space for the current type size. Holding down Ctrl or Command when you use these keystrokes multiplies the kerning increment value by 5 with each keystroke." Translating that into 1000-to-the-em points, Alt/Opt gives you 20 points (4 Quark points) and adding the Ctrl/Command gives you 100 (20 Quark points). [note: I was editing and clarifying this while Chris cross-posted. I came to see the source of the confusion--which Chris explains below--while looking into it further.]
