2004-11-08 22:40:54 • Mark Simonson
The reason I picked Schneidler has to do with the proportions of the arms on the E. The top two arms are very close in length and the bottom is quite a bit longer. I also noticed the shape of the D, with the underside curve near the stem. Also the serifs on the T move the same directions as those in Schneidler, but appear to have been cut off at the tops. The stroke thickness can easily be doctored in Illustrator, as we know.

The E in Monkton doesn't have the same proportions. All three we have mentioned have the same M (along with Trajan and Giovanni, as two more I can name), but I think the resemblance ends there, except with Schneidler.

I guess if we are allowing that this font has been modified, debating which is closest is a bit academic, but that's my vote.
