I think Mystique and Method are opposed. Formality impedes instinct. Just drawing your font takes care of all that "concept development" and "research" stuff (assuming you're not just reviving/remixing something that already exists). On the other hand, doing research into things like readability is useful as a foundation - that any many other things (like exposure to the work of others over time) implicitly guide your drawing anyway.

New Typeface Design Thesis

1. Objectives of the Project
- Objective functional
- Objective semantic
- Objective communicational

2. Foundations
- Personal Foundations
- Environmental Foundations

3. Development

3.1 Presentation and concept
- Introduction to the project
- Conceptual Development
- Presentation of external antecedents
- Previous references

3.2 References of Development and formal analysis
- Methodology and processes to occupy
- Analysis of the evolution of the project
- conceptual formal Analysis
- detailed formal Analysis
- Analysis and conclusions of tests

3.3 Final process presentation

- To present/display the finished typography
- To show particularitities that it has
- To show to particularitities in its use

4.Conclusion

5.Bibliography

6.Specimen


If anyone have other similar structure for a thesis please post it here or send it to me. I think this kind of information is very useful for a professional typeface project development process. Maybe those who make a typeface for a thesis can contribute here, or something who was study in a typography design school.

Miguel



In idle moments I more or less "write" letters with a soft (4B) flat sketch pencil. This is a habit of a lot of years of comping layouts in pencil. In the early days, a person developed a representative sans and a roman etc. that could be used in a layout, and substitiuted for almost any typeface that was decided on in the production process.

I save all the doodles, and go through them from time to time to find out what I think will work as a type.

The scale I work at mostly is about a 3/4 to 1 inch x- height. Once I have cut in with a fine point to delineate the forms.

People work in many different ways. Whatever it takes to get there is good if it works for you.

Jim Rimmer
