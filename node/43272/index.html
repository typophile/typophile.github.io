This older thread lists ‘some recent square sans designs’. For me it all started with Thesis TheSans I, for one, would never have made the connection to [[Thesis]]. To me, [[Thesis]] is a milestone, too. But I rather see it in the tradition of the [[sans serif|humanist sans]], a thing started by [[Syntax]] or [[Frutiger]]; expanding this formal principle into a massive clan (like [[Corporate ASE]], [[Stone]] or [[Rotis]]) – but with a wide and well-balanced repertoire of weights (like [[Univers]]). The recent square sans thing is more – well – square, i.e. not-so-humanist, rather technical. Then again, as Chris has mentioned in that thread, Camingo was designed by [[Jan Fromm]], a former student of [[Luc(as) de Groot]]. So, yes, there is a connection. F
