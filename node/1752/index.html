Royalties Based on Usage

October 21, 2003 • Diner

I decided to post this under the General Discussion forums rather the Release forums because I'm interested in hearing a wide range of opinions on this . . .

Lately it seems I've been getting a good amount of e-mail from customers asking if there is an additional royalty for using our fonts for: books, websites, posters, use on products.

Are there that many foundries out there that have a license for every instance of font usage that they charge for? I'm familiar with embedding licenses, broadcast licenses, embedded devices, but I've never heard of restrictions on usage I would expect the font would be used for in general anyhow.

It's just an odd trend I've noticed and wondered if perhaps this is commom practice among other foundries or perhaps if this speaks to an upswing in customers awareness of aquiring appropriate licensing.

I'd love to hear everyone's opinions on this . .

2003-10-22 07:10:39 • Joe Pemberton
There are a variety of different licensing agreements used in (stock) photography, and the fee will vary based on the kind of usage.

There is one kind of fee stipulation used by some stockco's, relating to using an image, unmodified, as the single image and "reason to buy", in instances such as a book cover, or on a mug. This is what may have given your customers the idea that if they use a typographic image, for instance a single character, as a primary graphic, this requires extra licensing.

With fonts, different foundries have slightly different licence arrangements, but it's all about restrictions, and not about graduated fees.

I did have one customer ask me if it was OK to use a single, unmodified character from one of my typefaces (Morphica "D"), as a logo – and then trademark it. Without consulting my lawyer, I said it was OK. In retrospect, probably not the right thing to say. I hope his client doesn't sue me for continuing to sell the font.
