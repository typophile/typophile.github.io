There still are subtle differences, some of which could be due to a sort of emboldening/shadowing (depth of counters on |R|, |N|, and |W|, for example), or modifications (|A|’s crossbar, maybe |P|’s bowl height, and |M|’s bottom vertex). Depending on the origin of the sample, it could be a non-digital cut of the typeface.

Yes, the embossed effect makes it difficult to determine the precise outline of the letters to which it was applied. Could be a narrow letter that was thickened or a thicker one that was narrowed. Helvetica Ultra Condensed is a fair fit, but is not the only possible source. There grotesques from the 19th century that are not all that different. This could also be pure lettering and not a font as such. This would be consistent with the craft ethos of this company. http://nydistilling.com/about/ Also http://www.timeout.com/newyork/food-drink/artisan-of-the-year-new-york-distilling-co-the-shanty
