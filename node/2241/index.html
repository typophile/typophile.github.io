Oops, one last comment (almost overlooked that one). > But Yves, if it was stroked that wouldn't make the serif shape change would it? I would have thought the stroke only would make the type look bolder. Actually, that's the funny thing when stroking characters: it changes the properties of a typeface and makes it shift from one category to another. I once sat in on a type design basic course at a Monotype Conference ten years ago, and one of the exercises was to add an equal amount of weight to a character. What happened was that for example a modern face became an aldine, an aldine became a slab serif etc. Very interesting stuff. I can't fill you in on the finer details as I'm no type designer and it's a decade ago, but maybe one of our esteemed colleagues can provide more details.
