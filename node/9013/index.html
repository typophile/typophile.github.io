2005-02-08 11:59:14 • Jordan Harper
I almost didn't mention any names because I don't want to have too specific of a feel. The main thing is that it will go on acoustic guitars that don't need amplification. Some other ideas I'm trying to go for are: natural, man-made, and quality.

This version is an angled script like D'Aquisto, Fender, and Gibson. I think the logo should look like a guitar logo, but hopefully it can still be unique.

I'm also concerned about the loop in the H and the loop in the y balancing. Also the n is not at exactly the same slant as everything else (it's baroque! -er, not really) so that the left stem of the n didn't get too clogged up where it meets the e and the stroke at the top of the n. I took out a small wedge at the top of the n to help. I'm not sure how much this will come into play if we need to screen it. But maybe this non-perfect angle makes it seem less mechanical.

I do still want the final logo to feel personal and have some character even though I might not be going directly for 'blues'. So I just might end up giving it some texture, as long as it doesn't take too much away from any quality aspect.

2005-04-04 15:35:31 • aluminum
My next revision is below (f). While looking at the fi (good catch!), I also noticed that the 'ry' ligature is too tight, creating an optical illusion almost (The further back you are when you look at it, the more you can't tell where the r ends and the y starts). In (b), I tried making the r-y joint rounded, but pulling the pen back inside helped define the r, especially at small sizes, so in the last version (f) I redrew the joint so that it was the same style but spread apart more.

(a) is the version from the last post. (c) and (d) show two versions of the h, to get rid of the appearance of "fi".

I also spaced the letters out more. This helped them match up with the new 'ry' spacing and also made them easier to identify.

The letters involve a few sharper angles where previously there were curves. Also, the first downstroke on the h, n, and r have a bit of a flair, and this also happens on the upstroke of the y. I know this still doesn't have the personality of some of the previous logo versions, but hopefully that at least gives it something.
