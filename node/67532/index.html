February 17, 2010 • fredcastle

what are your thoughts on centering website content? people have such big screens now, there’s a lot of white space. but it seems like people are fine with content on the left side?

thoughts?

Yeah, claiming all of the space is a no-no. Use the means available to put as much information in as little space as possible and do consider readability and usability. I, for one, like to use my 23-inch screen to multi-task and I detset websites that upscale to full screen.

It’s a minor issue in the grand scheme of things, but for overly wide web browsers, I prefer to center the site.
2010-02-17 23:03:47 • aluminum

I tend to be a full-screen browser most of the time, so I’ll see that the material is centered, and I’m as interested in someones presentation as I am the content itself. I think it really depends on the content you’re presenting.

If you’re showcasing a portfolio, then I don’t think claiming all of the space is a no-no. Anybody who is seriously looking at your work for 2-5 minutes should also be interested in the full scope of the presentation. Not just to say ‘full-screen’ presentation, but interested in all the considerations you’ve made.

If it’s a blog, or a news site or… whatever, I’m probably seeing the content in Google reader… and I won’t know that your type is hot pink on a blue backdrop…
