2007-03-18 20:56:07 • HelveticaKnows
Verdana, is the way to go acording to them. They don't discount serifed type either. I am creating a typeface for this. While on principle I wouldn't want to discourage novices from designing type for the reading-challenged (two words: Louis Braille), I don't believe that their efforts will be any better than any standard typeface by an experienced designer, in fact, much less effective. Sure, it's great that some publishers are using such types, but it says more about committment and empowerment than pure functionality. From both a scientific and design process standpoint, it would make more sense to analyze exisiting typefaces for their suitability for the reading-challenged, and then base one's typeface design on that knowledge, rather than working from abstract principles such as "disambiguation of shapes".
