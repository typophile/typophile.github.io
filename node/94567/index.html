Your sample is probably an alphabet that was purpose-built for stamping license plates. The practice of taking alphabets designed for printing on paper (fonts) and adapting them for other reproduction processes (such as embossing letters and numbers on metal license plates) didn’t become widespread until 15-20 years after this license plate was made. In 1966, the word font meant a set of letters for printing on paper, so you won’t find these letterforms on any list of fonts that existed in 1966. If you research modern-day fonts using the attributes of these letters (monoline, sans serif, square corners) I imagine you’ll find a good match, or something that will match if you tweak it a bit.
