Futura is surprisingly nice paired with Galliard, which has some of the same genes as Garamond. Something about the simplicity and hardness of Galliard and the proportions of Futura keep them in harmony. But if you really want Adobe Garamond, which is much softer, you could look at Cronos, or one of OurType's sans designs might be right: Eva (new), Fresco Sans or Versa Sans.

I like my Baroque paired with a humanist. And two of them that has worked the best for me, believe it or not, are Scala and Quadraat Sans. For a less common alternative, try Fontfarm's Kofired or John Sans. For an alternative to Futura, try an architectural sans (how do we come up with such term anyway?) like Verlag or Neutraface.

I would not combine a Rennaissance-Antiqua with a Futura. E.g. the italic version of Garamond is a very pretty one. Combining it with the Bauhaus style of Futura does not make sense for me. If Futura with an antiqua, I'ld look for e.g. Memphis, Beton or a Monotype Rockwell. Georg
