Home Remodeling Is The Key To A Higher Resale Value

September 12, 2014 • jerrodbtos

The home or condo you own may seem entirely comfortable to you. However before you choose to place it on the real estate market, you need to see each room as would a stranger entering for the first time. They will not place an sentimental value on what they see, only how attractive the premises is and if it meets their own personal taste. While you can’t be a mind reader, you can renovate those rooms that get the most attention from prospective buyers.

When looking at a new property, everyone tend to look at the floors and walls. Such rooms as the kitchen and bathroom will get a lion’s share of the attention as well. Bathroom vanities should be modern and not have damage on their doors. Switching out a well worn bathroom vanity for a new one will update any sink area.

Replacing cabinets in Lakeland kitchens is something that has also brought many sellers a better price during sales negotiations. Along with floors, cabinets can thoroughly change the look of an aging kitchen.

Improvements should not be limited to what buyers see when touring the kitchen and dinette. Choosing modern bathroom design home buyers will appreciate is another route to success in the real estate market.

Flooring can vary by room, with certain rooms allotted more functional selections. In particular, kitchen and bathrooms generally have vinyl flooring or tiles due to the the usage of water. Hallways and common rooms tend to have flooring that is based both its durability, in addition to how it complements the household décor.

Despite these generalities, it is not uncommon for the homes of older individuals to be completely carpeted. For the successful remodeling, planning is the base. Making product selections early can prevent delays later. Proper planning can also help keep you on budget. “You’ll end up making the same decisions, but you’ll know what they’re going to be and what they’re going to cost ahead of time,” explains Ridley Wills, founder of The Wills Co., a design-build firm in Nashville.

Long-term-maintenance, energy-loss, and repair expenses can add up quickly. Make sure you include them in your calculations when comparing prices.
