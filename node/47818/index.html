(x) Mid-20th-Century wide serif book face - Estienne {crossgrove}

July 28, 2008 • tww44

A fairly wide serif used as the main text face for Airman’s Odyssey, a collection of works by Antoine de Saint-Exupéry. The book was published by Harcourt, Brace in 1942. I've tried to represent a variety of characters in the main images; samples of the italic used are also included. Thank you.
I'd say it bears a strong resemblance to Garamond Premier Pro. The only large difference I see is the capital "W". But if I had to say, I'd definitely call this a Garamond of some flavour.

The W must be an important key, because most Garamonds have crossed legs in the W. In looking at the Jaspert "Encyclopaedia of Type Faces" the Linotype Granjon seems very close and was available after 1930, and does have the same W and the large bowl of the 'g' like this sample. The only obvious difference I see is the 'R' in the ao2 sample, which extends past the baseline. The Granjon sample I have does not show this glyph in the set. - Mike Yanega
