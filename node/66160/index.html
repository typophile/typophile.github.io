Stephen Coles mentions that it “takes a lot of work” to use Dante. Would someone please tell me why?

I’ve got a license for it (that is, Dante MT Pro) and I plan on using it with the following:

True 1200dpi laser printer Pages (iWork) + InDesign 24lb 8 1/2 x 11 linen (a nice creamy colour)

Typographic details:

Margins: 1.57" left and right, 1" top/bottom Font size/leading: 12.5/14.5 Avg. 10-12 words per line

I’ve just printed off a paragraph and it looks gorgeous. Textism says that “Of the widely-used book typefaces of the North American mid-20th century . . . Giovanni Mardersteig’s Dante is, to my eye, the only one to have had a successful translation to digital from metal.”

It certainly isn’t as spindly and anemic as the others, but it isn’t a heavy face either, like Dolly or Feijoa. I find it extremely easy to read. This OpenType version seems quite handy.

Dean Allen is right that Dante survived the digital transition very well, but FF Clifford has replaced Dante as my favorite traditional text face.
