I am looking for different variations on the [[aesc|ae ligature]]. Does anyone have any sugestions for a good serif typeface that contains the ligature? I you know of any uncommon variations of this ligature that would also be greatly appreciated.

Thank you fellow Typophiles.

Gustav

æ is a distinct letter in quite a few languages, so the design tends to be quite conservative. You might find it difficult to come up with ‘uncommon variations’.

Where there is some interesting variation in approach is in italic types, but this is due to the practical difficulty of distinguishing æ from œ in styles with a unicameral a.

Robert: This combination is called a dipthong. [sic]

A diphthong is a phonetic phenomenon: a glide between two vowel sounds within the same syllable. The æ character represents a diphthong in some languages, but in others it represent a pure vowel sound.
