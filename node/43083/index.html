March 12, 2008 • design-ed

Hey, Working on a new id marque for a client. The concept has been approved – so I'm just refining it now and would be good to get some advice from the masters among you! As you can see from the original plain typeface, the letters 'a' and 'h' don't sit together that well: [img:sites/default/files/old-images/ah_original_small_5315.png] So what I've tried to do is combine the 'a' and 'h' with a ligature to make a more unified and unique marque, and allow the kerning to be much tighter. I'll be using this at quite large sizes so I've also reduced the ellipses and the spacing in general: [img:sites/default/files/old-images/ah_modified_small_6528.png] I think the ligature is working – but I'm not 100% happy with it. It seems a shame to lose the character you get from that little flick on the 'a'. So it would be good to get your thoughts about if this is the best way I could join them, or any other ways you think this could be improved? Best Regards, Ed
I think in the second instance the two characters are way too close in relation to the counters. There is no reason for ligatures here! my2c Best, Diederik

You might want to mirror the top curve of the "a," with its nice smooth contrast, in your link between the a and h.
2008-03-12 16:36:29 • FeeltheKern
You could also reduce the size of the lower left serifs on both legs of the h ( a popular modeling style for serifs now anyway) to make the a & h fit together. I think your lig is okay too - it could be opened up slightly - but the ellipses need to come closer to the ah.
