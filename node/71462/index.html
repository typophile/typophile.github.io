2010-06-18 08:36:11 • Renko

I remember when I was oblivious to that too, even after repeated use of typefaces. It’s something you easily ignore. Sometimes, because of the fact that I am now aware of that and the fact that I became aware of not being aware, I find myself looking at the multitude of things around me and thinking “someone actually made this chair, the carpeted floor, the windows, the freakin' building, and I take it all for granted!” When we use a pen to write we never think we owe something to the people who made the pen. Or a hammer or whatever.

Yes, I know: you don’t just come by a hammer; you have to buy it (or at least borrow it). But typefaces just appear where you want to use them, nowadays. And the pen is actually not a bad example… people just give out pens! :P

Patience and understanding required. :)
