Mr. K.T.K Möller:

I should like to add that Ratio-Latein was usually used as slug casting font, not as hand-composition font. As a slug or line-casting font, Ratio-Latein had identical alphabet-lengths of Roman/Italic/Bold, the variation of stroke (contrast between upstrokes and downstrokes) was mininal, and kerns (of letter f etc.) did not exist.
