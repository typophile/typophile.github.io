Tablet or Mouse

March 16, 2006 • Mister M

Which design tool is better for type design on the computer, A Wacom *spelling?* Tablet or the mouse? Is one more accurate than the other? If so, which one? Or are they both the same and really all up to preference? Thoughts anyone? Mister M
Tablets are generally used more by retouchers and the such who 'digitally paint'. Type design (if you're talking actual production) is purely manipulation of single points. I don't see how a tablet would be beneficial. That's just my opinion though. If you're talking about brainstorming phase, ideas, sketching etc. then I suppose you'd be better off getting your concepts out with a tablet.
2006-03-16 03:07:05 • dave bailey
After making the switch to a tablet, I don't think I'll return to using a mouse for anything other than web-browsing. Everyone works differently, but for me, whether it is drawing with vector tools or pixel pushing, my hand can move much more freely across the canvas, and with more accuracy. I find that it is more comfortable than using a mouse, however, strangely enough, I have developed a callous on my pinky finger (aka Wacom Callous™) from the way I hold the pen. :) Don't let this scare you -- I haven't yet come across anyone else with this problem.
