There’s a passage in the Sign Painter section of the House Industries book about John Downer coming out and teaching them some stuff, try playing that one up. Also, have you tried searching The Design and Applied Arts Index for articles? I find that it’s often the only useful database for tracking down good stories about designers, especially type designers. If your school library doesn’t have access to that database, get the librarian to direct to a library that does. Because you’re in New York you have access to some great libraries, so get out there and ask the librarians to help you look stuff up. You should see if the TDC library has some stuff! If you still have trouble, try printing all the good internet articles you can find by or about John and showing them to your professor, and pointing out that in a field as esoteric as type design, sometimes the internet is a better resource than a library. Also, check online articles carefully, sometimes they are reprints of articles that appeared in obscure magazines that aren’t in libraries, so you can just cite the original magazine.
