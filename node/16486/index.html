David, sounds like some of your old Fog points (aka "nodes") were not on the grid exactly, and had crept up north of 33.5 ems. Fontographer "supported" decimal places, whereas FontLab won't let you use them. Because PostScript doesn't support them either. You see, it turns out that Fontographer was bluffing the whole time! It "flattened" the points when you generated fonts with off-point points and handles. When you FogLamp the files, you are getting whole-point flattened nodes and BCPs. If you had generated the same file from Fog as a TTF or PST1 file, then opened it in FLab, you would get the same result.
