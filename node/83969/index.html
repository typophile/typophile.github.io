2011-07-21 22:44:07 • Igor Freiberger

The fact that this is cast iron leads me to believe that it is genuine. Imagine the technique required to produce this if it were done with a sand form. The craftsman would use a template (a blank slate with bevels and such), and paste the letterform onto that (maybe using rolled bits of clay — tightening up forms as needed wit a quick slash of a knife). Makes it feasible that doing serifs and other refinements was out of the scope of the persons’ capabilities or even not very economical. Master pushed into sand. Melted iron poured into that. Presto.
