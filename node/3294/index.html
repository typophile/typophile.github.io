However, from what I know about copyright law in the US, it's perfectly legal to do it. Due to a really early ruling about typefaces, the only thing you can copyright is the name of the face, not the design of it. Thusly, that is why on every typesetting system there are fonts that look alike, but have different names (Helvetica, Heldustry, Arial, etc). Sometimes the rip off wouldn't be blatant, but would contain subtle changes (like Arial). Othertimes it would be pretty much a 100% grab (Futura's clone 20th Century from Monotype).

I think there has been good movement to protect typeface designs in Europe, but it will probably be years before that makes it's way to the US.
