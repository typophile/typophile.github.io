There are various techniques, such as small size, letterpress, coloured paper, or coloured ink. it’s not really a question of toning the typeface down, but making the funkyness of the type part of the general funky effect, not something that might otherwise draw attention to itself.

Well, your poems have to be the main thing, not the typography, which should just serve them. I would just take any solid text face, graceful, easy to read, and quiet, and set your poem well in it. Then send it out without agonizing further. Then try another font. You’ll soon settle on what you like, for a while anyway.

These are certainly context typefaces. Depending on the context they can be exceedingly trendy, and thus boring, or they can be a tasteful nod to the past. If you set with them, make sure to use period typographic techniques like old style numerals, small caps, etc. (For some reason, they loved to use Italics a lot more in the past than we do. I dunno?)
