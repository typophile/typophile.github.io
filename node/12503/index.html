If you’re setting tables of financial data and blocks of text, a face with lining and oldstyle numerals is probably a good bet. That’s more common in serif faces, but is becoming more common in contemporary sans.

Without knowing more, that’s as specific as I can get.

2005-05-17 18:09:06 • Will Miller

I would think Giacomo, a sweet sans by Terminal Design, might be ideal. It has tabular lining figures as well as proportional oldstyle figures, and looks great in a magazine.

Edit: I just missed your second post. In that light, here are two more that might fit the bill: Apex Sans from Thirstype, and Section from Lux Typographics. To me, each conveys strength but with a modern flair.
