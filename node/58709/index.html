Dalton Maag releases Grueber and Effra Italic

June 4, 2009 • bruno_maag

Grueber, a new release by Dalton Maag, is a two weight slab serif for display usage. The typeface is based on designs by Austrian architect Paul Grueber in the early 1900s for castle Hoch Osterwitz. Dalton Maag also releases true Italic forms for our popular typeface Effra. The addition of Italics makes Effra a versatile choice for any design project. www.daltonmaag.com

Nick, no Grueber has no l/c alternates - yet at least. In fact, on the original carvings at the castle, what are now alternates were the actual letters. Together with Lukas we decided that we need more traditional designs in the base keyboard positions to cater for the more conservative tastes. As the l/c had no template to work from we decided for the time being not to include alternate designs.
