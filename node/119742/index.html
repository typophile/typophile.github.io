This was probably all hand-drawn (the brand was introduced in 1876, and this design probably dates from the period of 1920-1940), but here are some typefaces that mimic the look (if you are willing to make some minor alterations).

BOYARD

Pyriform Tones by Nick Curtis: http://www.fontspace.com/nicks-fonts/pyriform-tones-nf

Milton Demibold and Melbourne Serial appear to be the same typeface: http://www.ascenderfonts.com/font/milton-demibold.aspx http://www.myfonts.com/search/Melbourne-%20Serial-/fonts/

Verona: http://www.myfonts.com/search/Verona-%20Serial-/fonts/

Barataria: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/scriptorium/barataria/

CAPORAL

PS Sherwood (though the angles of the serifs on the C aren’t spot-on): http://www.myfonts.com/search/Sherwood/fonts/

20 CIGARETTES

OL Round Gothic: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/ortizlopez/olround-gothic/

Neutra Display Medium Alternate by House Industries is a better match, if you can live with the lower crossbars on the E: http://www.houseind.com/fonts/neutraface

Don’t you think they all owe debts to typefaces like Belwe and Windsor, which have similar proportions on the B and R? Bellini and Progreso could also be precursors, and date from the same general period.

And they all derive from hand-lettered poster design from the turn of the century (c. 1900)….
