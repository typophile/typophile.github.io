The beginning and end are not problems. Tschi is pronounced like chi in 'chicken', and 'old' as normal. The middle 'ch' in 'Tschichold' is, however, a problem for English speakers. It is properly pronounced like the 'ch' in 'J.S. Bach'. This is the gutteral h, a sound not normal in English. I have heard English use the English 'sh' sound instead. So if you can manage to say the composer 'Bach' correctly, then you can go for: 'chi-CHold', with the 'CH' the german gutteral h. Or if you want to Anglicise it it--which is perfectly OK, I think--it is 'Chi-shold'. edit: I guess the natural way to Americanize it is 'chickold', but I think 'chishold' is a bit nicer and closer to the German.
