2007-07-18 00:50:47 • Miss Tiffany

Since a few people have responded to the crab tip, I should probably provide a little more information in case people are surprised. Crabpots is not fine dining in terms of atmosphere. It’s very kid-friendly and can be quite loud.

You come in. You sit down. They tie a plastic bib round your neck. You order. They lay out a big sheet of butcher paper on the table. They bring you a wooden block and a mallet. They bring a big metal tub of freshly steamed seafood. They dump the seafood on the table. You start grabbing and hammering.

Great fun. Very messy. Very tasty.

Those of us who have to use the public transit system in Seattle have no comment on the above ;(

Ok, I lie. A car would be awesome, except you’re gonna have a damn time trying to park the thing, for free, that is. If you want to pay, there are plenty of spots.

Most of the spots we’ve been discussing are within the downtown area, so there should be no problems should you decide to hoof it. Cabs are also an option, but I hate those scalpers.

The Metro bus system is really good at getting you North and South. It sucks big time when it comes to East>West. It’s not the bus' fault, it’s the stupid city planners who made no major E-W arterials anywhere in the city that don’t suck.

My experience as a bus rider for 5 years says that you shouldn’t rent a car. Unless you want to go outside the city, like to the Eastside to check out the wineries or whatever. Those buses will take hours and never conform to any reasonable schedule.
