2008-01-24 12:17:05 • Nightshade

Like Nightshade, it feels more surf shop than adult store to me. That curving triangular shape is more closely associated with the shapes on surfboards, sharks, and sails for many people.

I scaled down the image, to simulate it being seen from a distance, and the woman gets pretty jumbled with the A form. It’s not illegible, because it’s similar to the truck flap girl that everyone knows, but it’s confusing if you don’t know what to expect.

Part of this is the details, the other part is the super-thin lines. And most importantly, I think the lady needs some help. Her silhouette may be accurate to a photo, but her body makes lots of weird shapes when in a silhouette. Imagine if someone could only see her upper body by covering her legs and hand - the shape that’s left over doesn’t read as a human form to my eyes. I think the drawing needs to be stylized a bit, or at least considered further. The truck flap girl works because they use a profile silhouette, which allows them to emphasize her bust and show the profile of her face. This helps to make each part of her body feel like a part of a body on its own. The human form doesn’t contain straight lines, so be careful when using them, particularly for feminine forms. Her back and legs should not have straight lines. Also, her arm is a bit anemic and her elbow’s a bit knobby. Don’t take it personally, I’m sure she’s a great person. But figure drawing can be tricky and these are key points to help a figure feel more vital and sensual.

Perhaps you should look at some old pulp novel covers or some Varga girls for some better buxom, curvy silhouettes. Going for an archetype is a better bet for a logo, which must be a bit more iconic than a simple illustration.

I look forward to seeing where you go with it.
