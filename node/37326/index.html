2007-09-21 07:23:08 • brampitoyo
Pasting my comment from Speak Up:
"...Copperplate was designed for letterpress (as all the type of it's time was, obviously). But, it was specifically designed to feel like a sans serif, yet hold it's crispness at small sizes. This is the reason there was no lowercase... The metal simply would not have held the small shapes. This explains it's very wide with very fine serifs. "It does what it was designed to do very well. It was never intended to be a display face. (Same with Bank Gothic and Bell Gothic!) Fred Goudy must roll over in his pantheonic grave when Copperplate ships on hard drives everywhere. "With this context I actually think it's a bit of genius. Makes me want to take a second look at the Goudy stuff I usually pass over.
