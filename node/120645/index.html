Suggestions on Pairing Sabon

October 19, 2014 • grubstreet

I’m designing my portfolio website and am thinking that I should brand myself with a combination of two typefaces. I fervently love Sabon and will be using Porchez’s Sabon Next, but now I’m in the struggle to find another typeface to pair it.

I have thought of only using Sabon itself, but that would look too antique and delicate – a sense, although beautiful, not appropriate for the mood I want to achieve. I do want it to look modern and neat, but not the kind of über-sanitized cleanness that you see in Swiss designs (yeah just go away, Helvetica). I would very much appreciate some contrast in both typefaces’ personalities (i.e. one tender and one powerful, one contemplative and one welcoming, etc.) It seems that humanist sans may be a good way to achieve, but I’ve already tried Myriad (regular 18 as title with Sabon 14 as body, in my browser reader) and it doesn’t work well.

Of course I know that I can just find a whatever-neogrotesk to pair, but I don’t want to just stop at that point. I am also thinking if I can find another serif to pair, but I really have no experience at all with how to pair two serif faces.

Any suggestions?

A few humanist sans that might work: Scala Sans (Martin Majoor), Calluna Sans (exljbris), Cronos (Robert Slimbach) (but this might have a bit too much personality of its own). You could also go with something like Futura for greater contrast. Something grotesquey like Shinn’s Figgins might be a possibility. Be wary of pairing two serifs. Unless they are very different, this often doesn’t work — and if they’re too different, it can also not work. You’ll want to find two faces that speak a similar idiom but where one is an exaggerated form of the other — like Century Schoolbook and Clarendon. Problem is there’s not really such a thing as an exaggeration of Sabon, which is a muted version of Garamond. You could try Sabon for text and a more refined Garamond for display, but the sizes would need to be very different.
