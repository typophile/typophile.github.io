1970s-era building signage

November 28, 2013 • alanmichael

The high school I graduated from has a locally iconic sign sprawled across the southwest lawn, and ever since I was a student there, I’ve wondered what font/type was used. The school was built in 1972-1973, and I presume the sign was made at that time.[img:sites/default/files/old-images/hlb1_6472.jpg]

Given the era of this sign, it is likely to have been hand painted, and therefore not a commercially-available typeface. The style is reminiscent of some of the broad slab serifs or Egyptians of the wood type era, so this would be the first place to start in searching for a close approximation. Key features are the lateral oval shape of the O, double vertical serif on the C and the curl on the leg of the R, etc. … the over all squarish countenance of the letters (e.g. the Eurostile-ish S) and the lack of stroke contrast.

But notice in this sign how no two letters are quite the same, with varying widths and weights further attesting to its hand-painted provenance. I fear you’ll never find an exact match, but you may find something close.
