One of the inspirations for Fontesque was the furniture in a room in an old military building in Quebec City, an officer’s club. All the chairs are distorted, some with legs of different lengths, others with a short arm on one side and a large on the other, and so on. It’s like you’re tipsy before you even start drinking.

However, as with much substance-inspired art and design (although music is somewhat different), it’s only the inspiration that occurs when high, the execution is done straight. There are some forms of art that can be done with shaky hands, but I don’t think making glyphs is one of them.

Of a type designer and liquor, slightly off topic insofar as the type designer in question is not an alcoholic.

Matthew Carter tells the story of a time he was flying from the UK to the US and stopped in the duty free shop at Heathrow. He spied a bottle of Scotch whose label was printed using one of his types, so he bought it. As he exited the shop, a woman holding a clipboard asked him to take a market-research survey. When she asked him why he chose that particular brand of Scotch, he answered, “Because I designed the type on the label,” and watched as the researcher puzzled over her questionnaire for a check box that corresponded to that answer.
