Type & Design Education Forum at TypeCon

June 28, 2007 • kentlew

Design educators in our midst are going to want to take note of the Type & Design Education Forum that SOTA is presenting at this year’s TypeCon.

More information about the Education Forum is now posted at the TypeCon website: http://www.typecon.com/education.html

This should prove to be quite a unique opportunity for design educators to find inspiration and share information about teaching type and typography to the next generation of designers.

The forum topics will cover a broad range of subjects – from overall curriculum development for different kinds of programs (2-yr vs. 4-yr, liberal arts institutions vs. art schools, etc.) to specific assignment presentations.

There will be break-out sessions for mixing it up with idea exchange, dialogue, and networking. And the final panel will focus on ideas and methods for instilling in students the kind of fascination and passion for typography that we know so well here at this site.

The Forum is being organized by SOTA board members Ilene Strizver and Mark Jamra, both of whom have spent many years in both the type industry and design education field.

Fellow Typophilers Will Powers and James Montalbano will be presenting. Perhaps next time they pop by, they can chime in with a preview of what they’re planning to bring to the table.

I am not really presenting anything. I will be acting as moderator for one of the panel discussions. What the panel brings to the discussion about creating a passion for type will dictate what role I play. Maybe referee, or maybe bouncer.
