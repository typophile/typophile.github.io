We do have a changelog from 5.0.0 to 5.0.2: http://www.fontlab.com/The-News/Announcements/FontLab-Studio-5.0.2/ A "changelog" from 4.6 to 5.0 would be 250 pages long since version 5.0 includes two years of intense development. The highlights of 5.0 compared to 4.6 are listed in the preface to the manual that you can download from our website (http://www.font.to/downloads/manuals/FLS5MacManual.zip). The highlights are also summarized in my introductory article (http://www.font.to/downloads/documents/FLS5MagTypo14.pdf ). FontLab Studio 5 definitely offers improvements for people who do font modifications. FontLab Studio 5 is the first version of our font editor that can handle OpenType Layout features in an non-destructive manner (i.e. you can change some glyphs or names without touching the compiled OT tables), the handling of composites has been much improved, many bugs and problems have been fixed in handling of OpenType fonts, support for non-BMP characters of Unicode 3.2 was added, new aspects of the OpenType font format specifications have been incorporated, autohinting has been improved. The new automatic classes generation tool allows the user to take existing plain kerning and fully automatically convert it into class-based OpenType kerning. That's just for a start :) Regards, Adam Twardoch Fontlab Ltd.
