It looks like I have a little book coming up, at least parts of which I will be able to set unjustified (flush left, ragged right). Not sure whether the “staircases” I meant are called rivers in English (we call accumulating huge word spaces in justified text rivers, or rather creeks). I was referring to the form of the ragged right edge. I like to avoid any kind of strange, distracting shapes forming there, like staircases, triangle, holes and peaks etc.

Also try toprevent small words, or hyphenated parts from falling down at the end of a line. If there is no word under it in the line below, take it down into the next one. Best work with non-breaking word spaces here so that the line break vanishes when the text shall flows differently after a correction or something. Better not hyphenate after or before just two letters, at least not automatically. You can add those manually at places where you need them to elude a big hole. And last not least my favourite: avoid what (I think) Hochuli once called the swiss-erization of set text—hyphenation after …li-

2010-10-22 20:52:26 • nina

Absolutely no worries, Nina. It doesn’t take more time, nor do you have to be concerned about see-through issues unless you have really thin paper (what you probably won’t have in a novel or the like). Just make sure you set the text registerhaltig (see, another one … maybe I should switch to English in my layout programs to teach me) and that there isn’t a major Flatterzone. If you want to have like a 10 mm margin left and right you should set the ragging right one to a smaller measure to optically fit. But it depends on how good you succeed in making the edge harmonious and even.

But, to save time, you should work with automated hyphenation switched on (take care of the settings!) and yes, single-line-composer, though even he won’t do what you want in many annoying instances. Then go along the edge and check the hyphenations and manually adjust for better breaks (no hard hyphens), undo unpleasant ones, wrap small words or parts to the next line etc. Working manually is possible also, but really time consuming for longer text, and you have to read a good part of it to hyphenate appropriately.
