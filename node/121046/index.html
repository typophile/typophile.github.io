The ampersand has an ancestor in the 18th century Caslon Italic ampersand. Here is the ITC version: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/itc/caslon-italic-and-swashes/ The terminal in your Q&A has a Tuscan [Fishtail] terminal. These are pretty scarce. Here is one on Quaint Gothic http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/spiecegraphics/quaint-gothic-sg/ Don
