Veer's top 18 of 2005

January 20, 2006 • Norbert Florendo

Veer posted it’s Top Type for 2005 which shows the 18 “hottest faces” selected by its customers.

Even Veer humorously notes that a few aren’t even scripts.

All in all, I feel it’s always good to give your customer base feedback like this. Holding up the mirror to your shoppers and letting them know what most of them (I assume) bought does in fact reshape the mindset for the next purchase. Some will jump on the bandwagon and buy a script, some will jump off and try another typestyle next time.

Remember, Top doesn’t necessarily mean best when merchandising, and it’s ALWAYS nice to have some top sellers.

Interestingly, all top 18 are display faces.

2006-01-22 06:31:41 • hrant

But then how does that jive with the top-N lists of some other font houses consisting mostly of neutral sans faces?

It depends how you calculate a sale, I think.

For instance, say that Foundry X has a standard EULA, which is good for 10 workstations. Foundry X only has two fonts in its library, Script Face 123 and Grotesque 567. Foundry X sells 100 transactions of Script Face 123 and 50 transactions of Grotesque 567 per month via its online store.

Technically, Script Face 123 is the foundry’s best seller, right? Not necessarily. Lets say that all of the Script Face 123 transactions were with the standard, 10 user license. But half of the Grotesque 567 transactions were for more workstations… anywhere from 11 to 11,000.

Now Grotesk 567 is the foundry’s best seller, because it licensed the most workstations in a single month, even though it had less single transactions via the store.

You should always ask yourself which numbers you’re looking at in top-N lists.
