Perfect partner for Franklin Gothic?

June 14, 2010 • JDL

Hi Currently doing some editorial design work and selecting two typefaces, a serif and sans, for the project. I've settled on Franklin Gothic for the first type family due to legibility, style and weight variations, but for extra flavour and for body copy in feature articles, I'm looking for a serif that matches the same kind of x-height and will harmonise nicely. I thought of Caslon but think perhaps the x-height is too small; Palatino compares in scale however I'm not too sure about the combination of style - any thoughts on these or any serifs that may play happily with Franklin? Thanks
A good place to start font-matching is by considering era, genre and designer. Franklin Gothic dates from 100 years ago, and was designed by Morris Benton as his update of the 19th century grotesque genre. So his Century Schoolbook, and Century Expanded, would be good companions, as they were his updates of the 19th century Modern style of serifed text face. All through the 20th century, Franklin Gothic was paired with such faces in newspapers and magazines. Other vintage faces that have a track record of performing well with FG: Clearface (also by Benton) and Times Roman, of course.
