2010-08-24 23:53:48 • uzapucax
When discussing this you have to think in terms of traditional book production. A signature could consist out of 16 or even 32 pages that originated in one sheet of paper. The only constant is the inner-margin (excluding the effect of thickness of paper), and the outer margins could vary — not with extreme measures, but nonetheless a significant amount — on account of the trimming of the book-block. So it made sense to use outer margins that were larger than the inner margins. And as in every industry — necessity will be rationalized and ‘rules’ will be established… The one and true way to establish margins is what is called ‘trial and error’: make proofs, look at the, change things, try again etc, etc. BTW in the war of margins there are dozens of established principles, be they based on the sequence of Fibonacci, the golden whatever or … In the end the eye has it: what looks right is right : )
