2011-02-17 17:08:52 • oldnick
I am a fan of humanistic sans, which I find maybe for most of designers here already a bit dated, but in a non-designer environment still a fresh appearance. Therefore I make us of Frutiger. The MS rip-off “Segoe UI” is sufficient too. The new Office font Calibri is too “bundled” and round. Most people I know use Arial. As a serif font I read the recommendations of Georgia, Palatino and especially for conservative business Century Schoolbook. Personally I find with a serif, it should not be too decorative and I prefere a large x-height, as a size of 10 point (not smaller than 9 I would say) should still be readable. Good candidates to fit this brief are in my opinion Droid Serif and Utopia.

2011-02-21 16:30:14 • bendy
One problem I have often run into is when s/he want’s to update it themselves in the future when I’m not around and they don’t have your fonts or software. It’s possible to pull it using Times and Arial in Word. It's not the font, it’s how you use it. While presentation counts and you want a salient typographic hierarchy that is congruent with the informational hierarchy, there is a law of diminishing returns when it comes to pushing type around on a page for a document such as a CV. At the end of the day, content is more important than form. < /soap_box >
