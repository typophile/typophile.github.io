2004-11-16 06:13:25 • eismont
For a serif with Proforma's "tradition with a twist", take a look at Borges from PampaType. I just got the printed specimen book in the mail today, and it's a beauty.

http://www.pampatype.com/English.html
http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/pampatype/borges/

Jeremy Tankard's Enigma is worth a look:
http://www.typography.net/type/enigma.htm

Then there's Delicato, whose designer professes to have been inspired by Enigma and Proforma:
http://www.veer.com/products/typedetail.aspx?image=FOT0000073

As far as sans go, I prefer News Gothic to Trade Gothic, if you're looking for something that's *really* similar.

If you want a relatively neutral modern sans, that still has some of the character of the the gothics, consider FF Kievit:

http://www.fontshop.com/index.cfm?fuseaction=catalog.fontpackage&amp;displayfontid=FF.11231.0.0
