Need some eyes for a logo sketch

December 6, 2008 • nina

Hello Typophiles,

I’ve been working on a logo for a psychologist / certified mediation expert offering mediation and coaching related services on a freelance basis.

This is the one concept I did a couple months ago and still actually like. I will sketch out a couple other ideas as well, but would first like to get this to a point where I feel it is presentable as a rough (and I’m meeting her next Tuesday). Right now, I’m not quite sure it works.

Some notes —

  1. That’s her name, and that’s her first name which is upside down. At the time being, I’me not aware of any sort of by-/sub-/tagline to go with it, although I’ll probably suggest using one.

  2. I decided to try and focus on the wordmark instead of a ‘pictorial’ logo, mostly because all the ideas either she or I had for that felt either unfitting, too literal, or boring (paths merging and winding into the distance, across mountains etc.; hands joining together; talking heads)

  3. Ezzo feels like it matches her quite well; she’s young and a very open and friendly person; she speaks German with a quirky and likeable accent, and is the kind of person who makes other people instantly comfortable with her – down-to-earth would be a good word, and very professional at the same time. (Suggestions for other typefaces with this kind of ‘feel’ are very welcome.)

  4. As her name has a bunch of ‘boring’ letters, but two gs (yay!), I started playing with those, and with the idea of overlapping / linking their descenders (is that the bowl or the loop in English?), drawing on the idea of mediation being about bringing different sides together & acknowledging different perspectives on the same thing. I tried to align them so that they don’t clog at small sizes, and I seem to ‘see’ a hairline connecting them smoothly which isn’t there, but maybe that’s just my brain on too much orange juice trying to fend off the flu.

So, I don’t know. Is it too literal? Too minimal? Boring? Too little, too much? Does it work to put her first name upside down like that? I think I’ve stared at it too long. And I’m thankful for any comments.

FWIW - the Gs are paired very nicely, their combination looks very fluid and natural.

However the “Inge” part is quite hard to read. This is primarily because the “ge” part looks like a reflection of its upper counterpart, and so it implies a form of symmetry in the mark. But the “In” part breaks the symmetry and the eye just keeps wandering around trying to find it .. so it takes an effort to stop doing that and to actually focus on reading the name.

2008-12-07 01:35:44 • gerald
As is, it bothers me that it reads “Wegmann…Inge,” when the name you want to convey is “Inge Wegmann.” A potential change that might help this: did you consider having the words go vertically, so Inge would run upwards on the left and Wegmann downwards on the right? That would make the order better - I also think side-to-side “confrontation” might say “mediation” better than the dominator-dominated arrangement you have. So semantically it might work, I’d think, but visually you’d have to try it and see. Hope that helps.
