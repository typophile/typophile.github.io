2005-11-01 04:08:31 • marian bantjes

Bodoni is a bad choice for text

As if there was only one Bodoni! We’ve been setting a magazine in Filosofia, Zuzana Licko’s Bodoni, very successfully for a dozen or so issues. And I am the first one to complain about type being too small, too contrasty and too tightly tracked. ITC’s Bodoni is ok as long as you use the smaller sizes, and if it wasn’t for other reasons, I’d use GG Lange’s Bodoni Old Face which is warmer and less contrasty than Monotype’s and most other Bodonis. Futura actually goes well with most other serif faces, as long as the x-height doesn’t approach ITC levels from the 70s. Futura – if tracked enough – makes for even, well-balanced and quiet lines, so the text to go with it simply needs to avoid looking too constructed and mechanical. In other words: no ITC Garamond, no Didot, no Bauer Bodoni. Then again, one wouldn’t use any of these for text anyway.

That’s a very good point. And Tiffany (with her “You do need to find a version, such as ITC 12, that has had the contrast minimized”) alluded to that too. What I wonder about though is the “essence” of Bodoni, and if optically corrected version with reduced stroke contrast are still Bodoni… And who decides? I wonder about a test like this: put three settings at let’s say 10 point: Times, ITC Bodoni-6, and ITC Bodoni-72. Ask people which one, and also which two, is/are Bodoni. What answers would you get, from what type of people? My hunch is that the more you ask people who matter (people proportionally distant from type designers) the more the Bodoni-6 looks closer to Times than to “Bodoni”…

So I think that scale actually throws off our attempts to apply a consistent style, at least to some extent. The more readable you want to make the smaller sizes of a “stylish” design, the more you’re going to have to act pragmatically, to the point of dumping a big chunk of the style. There is a threshold between Display and Text, and since the former is where conscious deliberation of style and formation of perceptions of style happens, while in the latter the participation of style undergoes neutering, it’s pretty unclear to me what something like “Bodoni” actually means in text sizes.
