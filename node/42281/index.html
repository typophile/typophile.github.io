Grunge type is probably going to look dated to kids who are in high school now. It has the feel of a big marketing firm trying to reach out to the kids with that rock and roll stuff, very much like the anti-drug campaigns produced by the US Government in the 1980s.

Your photo piece works well conceptually. It would be very easy to botch the job by using photos in a way that reinforces the associated negative ideas in a shameful way, and I think you’ve done a good job of not going there. I also like the oddly clean but slightly burred edges that you used. I don’t know if that’s intentional or if these haven’t gone through production yet, but it’s a great play on contemporary photomontage. The layout of the type works well, although a face with a little more personality might work better, right now it feels kind of commercial.

If I were you I would take these to a mall and swap kids food for crits.
