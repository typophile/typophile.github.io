If you’re looking for a clean sans that has similar letterforms without the slabs…

…the sans serif that you may want is New Rail Alphabet. New Rail Alphabet is a new interpretation of the sans typeface Rail Alphabet by Kinneir and Calvert back in the 1960’s. Calvert designed Calvert for the Tyne and Wear Metro. It shares a lot of similarities with Rail Alphabet but obviously has big slabs attached.
