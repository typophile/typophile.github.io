2006-11-19 17:00:33 • hrant
Looks very much like Adobe Sans, the new default font when the proper outlines are not embedded properly. That used to be Courier and thus obvious, but Adobe Sans (and Serif) make such a good job at mimicking the real typeface that most people never notice. It takes the width from the real font and just makes the generic outlines fit the same space as the intended font. I have a large collection of these replacements which seem to happen a lot in a non-PostScript environment, like on plotters and vinyl-cutting machines. Vans and trucks and shop windows show more Adobe Sans than printed paper. As do airplanes, apparently.
