2010-12-11 18:17:52 • hkuiper

If you intend to keep the same all-caps formatting, I might suggest letterspaced caps in one of the following:

Adobe Garamond (which I think has special display caps, at least in some versions, and a very nice italic) Trajan (famously overused and often misused, but may be appropriate and effective here) Perpetua Titling (looks similar to what you have) Waters Titling (gorgeous) Brioso Pro (for a little more personality)

Note that at least the first two faces mentioned above come free with InDesign, and maybe other Adobe software, so depending what program you’re using, you may have them already.

Using small caps for the smaller text underneath the name, with oldstyle figures, will add a classier look. It looks like you may already have true small caps, but the osf will help. Letterspace everything more than feels necessary: for the name, you might start with 200 “units” (1000/em) = 20%.

Also, I sometimes find that using large small caps can be better than full caps in this kind of situation, as they’re wider and seem to have a stronger stance. (I would use the small caps from the next optical size up, ideally.)

I hope this helps.

Josh
