There aren't many Brazilian text (serif) typefaces; I don't think there's any with the characteristics you're looking for ("taut and beziery, with M-formula-type curves"). Yomar Augusto's Den Dekker and Eduardo Berliner's Pollen [PDF] are two nice typefaces with a strong calligraphic influence on letterforms. Rodolfo Capeto's Houaiss is a custom and exclusive typeface for the Houaiss dictionary. And there's my own Lalo (still work in progress, to be finished soon); it will be available in 3 optical weights: [img:sites/default/files/old-images/omine-tipograficamente-lalo_5677.gif] -- omine.net
