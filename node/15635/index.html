*gasp* *reaches for [[elements of typographic style|the bible]]* According to [[Robert Bringhurst|Bringhurst]], you should "Choose a typeface...that will honor and elucidate the character of the text. He also says "Choose a face whose historical echoes and associations are in harmony with the text." If you have a copy (or can get your hands on one) of The Elements of Typographic Style, I suggest reading the section "The Grand Design" from Chapter 1 as well as Chapter 6: "Choosing and Combining Type".

2005-10-14 16:56:42 • elliot100
As typographers and designers we should be looking for solutions. If the solution happens to coincide with the "rules" then fine. If the solution does not coincide with any "rules" then that's fine as well. I have personally always been a strong proponent of process and solution. I will not ask anyone to adhere to rules, but to apply them accordingly and wisely IF the solution warrants it. So if am trying to design a facsimile of a period piece, then ask the right questions: Is this trying to look like a 1930s publication from France on French poetry? Or is it a nineteenth century American book press displaying a French poem?
