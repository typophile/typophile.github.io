Just for fun, I compared the font files of DigiGroteskNEF-BoldCondensed (Elsner+Flake) and DigiGrotesk LT BoldCondensed (Linotype), and it turned out that most glyphs of the Linotype font are identical with the Elsner+Flake font, except that the glyphs of the Linotype font had been enlarged to 118%.

That is how you may do it in this funny font forging industry: You enlarge (or reduce) the glyphs of a given font by X percent, and bingo, you may proclaim yourself the “copyright owner” of a “newly created” font.
