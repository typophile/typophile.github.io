Hello, I’m hoping someone has a lead on this special speciman “book” format. I don’t believe Font Vista nor Font Book is the answer I’m seeking. I think they would do well, though, to implement this format into their Apps.. Basically, I’m looking for a font speciman page layout app. that will just print out the fonts by name alphabetically IN THE FONT STYLE itself; much like the way Illustrator previews it’s font list. I don’t want the a-z/1-10 options- just the name of the font in its style. Takes up musch less paper, easier to scroll through, and if you see the font name in its style it makes it easier to recall that font. Anyone feel me on that one? Does anyone have a lead THANKS!!!

Unless you’re definitely interested in producing a binder limited to the fonts in your library, the $99 invested in [[FontBook]] could be worth the considerable effort that would be involved in printing a large font catalog.

Benefit: an organized, cross-referenced, hardcover archive of thousands of available fonts.

Drawback: FontBook doesn’t have everything. You would miss a few commercial collections and free fonts.

Disclaimer: I was involved in the production of FontBook.

2007-01-05 01:44:35 • Stephen Coles

I use High-Logic’s MainType for my font management (since the options on Windows are pretty slim). MainType does just what you want, allowing you to export either a Rich Text File or Web Page with whatever info on the selected fonts you want, set in the fonts themselves. Unfortunately, it’s PC-only, so someone might be able to recommend a Mac solution. If you have a PC, you can download the demo and create custom specimen books to your heart’s content.

I second Stephen’s suggestion of FontBook; I just got it, and I’m thrilled. But it is signicantly big, both in cost and physi cal size.
