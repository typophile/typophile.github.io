Microsoft just now put up a spec and other info for Metro, their new document format. I haven't had a chance to read the specs carefully yet, but I will be over the next few days, as this is potentially an important subject for my work. Things certainly promise to get interesting in the document format world.
Can something written in XML really rival Postscript for sofisticated WYSIWYG printing?

What about font embedding? document signing, user notes, zoom (free scaling) etc?

Microsoft tried and failed to unseat PDF with the e-book format. Metro does sound scary - that its free, like IE was free back in the 1st browser wars. It will probably be tied to MS Office Suite for authoring, or any office suite for that matter, since its nuts and bolts are available to all.

2005-04-26 08:11:33 • edwh
ed: at the syntactic level, being based on XML vs. the Forth-like stack language in PostScript is pretty much a wash. It will no doubt result in large files and take lots more memory to run, but what else is new? Also keep in mind that PostScript hasn't seen any new development for about six years; Adobe's been putting their resources into PDF instead.

Metro is definitely resolution independent, being based on the Avalon imaging model.

I don't yet have authoritative answers to your other questions, but from what I've seen so far I would be very surprised to see Metro lack any significant and useful feature present in PDF.

"Can something written in XML really rival Postscript for sofisticated WYSIWYG printing?"

XML is just a descriptive language like Postscript. XML is just a container for data, so I imagine the document format can be as elaborate as the data entered. (SVG is all xml based too).

I agree with Ed, MS (nor adobe, really) are currently after the DTP market. They're after the enterprise document management market. The one advantage of PDF is that is has a rather strong following outside of Adobe (Ghostscript, etc.) and the one advantage of MS's offering is that it's XML based...but there can be a big difference between XML and 'MS' XML. ;o)
