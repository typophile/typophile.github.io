A brand is any consistent graphical treatment associated with a particular entity (whether an individual, company or product). So if your client has established or seeks to establish the use of smallcaps for her name as part of a brand, then of course it should be done consistently. Of course, brands can backfire, as when people come to habitually think ‘Oh, yes, she’s the pretentious one who sets her name in smallcaps all the time’.

Quicksilver for OS X is like that. I’ve never had this problem, but I’ve always wondered if there might be software out there that doesn’t recognize Unicode small caps as being the same as regular letters for querying purposes.

Anyhow, it’s been popular in Asian countries for a long time to get specific about the way names and titles are written in branding and other pop culture contexts. And there’s always e.e. cummings, AFKAP, etc. This doesn’t seem that much weirder than those examples, but I would personally think twice before getting touchy about anything that makes it more difficult for other people to write my name “properly.”
