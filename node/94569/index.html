In the late 70s and early 80s, several companies were making typesetting machines (first optical, later digital), that worked with proprietary fonts available only from the device manufacturer. Compugraphic was one of the leading makers of such machines, and issued their own versions of then-popular typefaces with comically bogus names, such as Mallard for Melior, Palomino (or maybe Palladium) for Palatino, and Oracle for Optima (Hermann Zapf was pretty much the boss in the late 7os). Apparently somebody in Singapore has still got one of those old Compugraphic machines running.
