2008-07-04 21:28:51 • Jens Kutilek

Many recently released sans faces, in the OpenType format, have both lining and oldstyle figures.

This solves the compromise found in faces of a certain age, such as Helvetica, which haven’t been upgraded, and only have one set of three quarter height figures that are meant to accompany both capitals and lower case setting, but aren’t perfect for either.

Some of these new sans faces have lining figures that do, in fact, line, at cap height.

Also, as part of the OpenType feature set, they may have alternate punctuation which automatically provides the adjustment Jens mentions, when “all caps” is selected–and have the option of tabular or proportional widths.

Even an older face which does have lining figures, such as DIN, is, IMO, not quite right for all cap setting, because the figures are somewhat condensed in relation to the capitals. Again, this is because they are required to work with the lower case as well.

You should contact FontShop.de–they should be able to help you find something suitable.
