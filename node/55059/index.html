Hey Javier

The school usually purchases (volume discount) site licenses for all of its software – fonts included – and any such license specifies the number of machines (onsite and/or offsite) that the software is for. By machines we mean computers, servers and printers.

As a student, you will be covered by the license agreement if you are working on school machines during school hours on school assignments. School projects are – by default – not-for-profit assignments, so no problem there (winning cash prizes on big design competitions might be the only exception to this).

As Guerrizmo says, where it gets messy is when students copy fonts illegally from the school library and take them home to do commercial assignments. Most instructors I know warn students against this.
