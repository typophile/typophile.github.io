Corporate typefaces in the automotive industry

March 25, 2009 • Bald Condensed

Audi recently introduced its new Audi Type, as did Toyota their Dalton-Maag designed custom typeface in January. I know BMW, Skoda and Mini also use exclusive Dalton-Maag designs, Opel’s was drawn by Robin Nicholas for Monotype Custom Fonts Solutions, Volvo has Volvo Broad, Jaguar has its eponymous corporate faces by Adrian Williams, Volkswagen uses a custom version of Futura art directed by Spiekermann and designed by Luc(as de Groot for MetaDesign, and Mitsubishi Motors Europe acquired exclusive license for Alpha Headline fonts until the end of 2012.

The Daimler-Benz corporate typeface is called Corporate A·S·E. It was designed by Kurt Weidemann in close collaboration with URW. A stands for Antiqua (roman serif), S for Serifenlos (sans serif) and E stands for Egyptienne (slab serif). The slab serif is rarely (if at all) used. Corporate A·S·E started as an exclusive corporate typeface, but a part of the deal with URW was that this restriction would only last several years. That’s why it is generally available for licensing.
