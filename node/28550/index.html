Designing a bilingual booklet - help please

October 3, 2006 • Kristina Drake

Hi,

Upper management where I work just decided that the Convocation booklet (the order of proceedings and list of graduate names for the graduation ceremony) ought to be bilingual. And I think I may be bald by November.

It’s a small-ish book. (8.5 x 11 folded) and there are a few pages of text like “message from the President” and “A word about Concordia” etc… I’m having a tough time figuring out how to incorporate all this French text–Even the titles of the dudes presenting degrees (Vice-Dean of Fine Arts, Provost, etc) need to be bilingual.

So what I’m wondering is this: Can anyone point me to examples of other similarly sized bilingual books? I think this would probably be a lot easier if I were working with a full letter sized page. Somehow, though, I don’t think I’ll be able to convince them to change the booklet size.

As I see it I have three options: 1– do a flip-over back to back booklet. This means double the pages and also double the work importing and updating names in two different places. 2– Double columns left-english righ-french. This may get confusing for the reader and I think it looks cluttered. Results in short, newspaper-column-like line lengths. 3– alternating so that French always appears on the left hand page and English always on the right.

At least with option two and three the list of graduates names only has to be included once, with the headers (Bachelor of Fine Arts) appearing in both languages above the list.

Does anyone have any thoughts or experience with this? I’ve never done a bilingual publication–usually we do them separately. I’m feeling a little lost and dejected about this. The book had just been re-designed and was looking very lovely. No more. sigh

Thanks for any input you can give. Kristina
