2005-11-23 04:19:28 • Stephen Coles

I’ve always heard the “old timers” at Compugraphic speak highly of [[Hollis Holland]], who was the first design director (Senior Typeface Designer) for the company. He was an accomplished calligrapher and book jacket designer years before he came to CG.

Here’s a snipet from the archives:

1973 - Compugraphic enters into a new technology with introduction of the VideoSetter® I and II. The VideoSetter has an operating speed in excess of 400 lines per minute, making it particularly attractive to the newspaper market for handling classified advertisements and stock market text, as well as body text. This year marks its first release of a typeface exclusively developed by Compugraphic. Named for its creator, Senior Typeface Designer Hollis Holland, Holland Seminar was promoted for high-quality promotional literature.
