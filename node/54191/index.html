Hi, i’m a complete beginner at font design, and to get me started i am experimenting with drawing the glyphs and autotracing into illustrator.

I’d like to know your process when making this type of font. Do you manipulate the glyphs much in illustrator/fontlab or do you leave them as they are? In particular i am finding that i have very rough edges from the pen when i zoom in. Many handmade fonts i have seen don’t have this. Is the best way round this to adjust the individual nodes to smooth out the edges?

thanks for any input…

I don’t autotrace a scanned drawing. I usually draw in pencil on squared paper and then redraw from scratch in Illustrator using a grid. Sometimes I will trace over a scan.

Have you got the [[http://www.nineblock.com/products.html|Better Handles]] plug-in for Illustrator?

Leslie Cabarga’s book the “Logo, Font & Lettering Bible” lives up to its name for me. Search for the ISBN: 0715316990

Also have a go at [[http://fontstruct.fontshop.com/|Fontstruct]], for a very different approach which can then be adapted to Illustrator, or indeed your Fontstruct fonts can be tweaked in Illustrator if you convert your text to outlines.
