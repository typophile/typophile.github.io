2006-08-04 16:18:11 • Si_Daniels

Oops… a little late to the discussion here… let me catch up.

@ Jesse - It’s certainly possible to provide a more print-compatible version of the email, but it would take resources that we just don’t have at the moment. Like you mention, the simplest way to do this would be to provide an alternate style sheet specifically for print that strips out all of the superfluous marketing pap and leaves the tidy type specimens. Since the email campaigns are designed to work with rich content email clients and webmail interfaces - I have specifically designed the HTML formatting of the campaigns to look good in those contexts. Your point is well taken, and perhaps I can do something in the future to improve the situation.

@ Jonathan - If you didn’t receive the latest Activity Book, we have them available for a very reasonable price in our merch store. The previous year’s issues are still available as well. [2005 | 2004]

@ Ken - " I tend to consider the email updates as spam." That’s unfortunate, but I can understand why you might feel that way. If you like, I can remove you from our email marketing list only. Let me know. Heck, since you’re here in town… stop by in person.

@ Yves & Stefan - You have both nattered away at me about this before, and I know it’s frustrating - to me as well. Again, it’s a question of resources, but we are working on getting a more substantial marketing presence across the pond. Once we get a better grasp on how to get printed pieces into Europe efficiently, you’ll have many more lovely bits of paper in your mailboxes, I promise.

@ Si - I just looked into the information we have on file for you. There’s no email address associated with your account, so you would never have received any of those missives from us. I think the reason that you have not received any printed pieces is because you were marked as a prospective customer without any account activity since 2003. Basically, you’re account isn’t filtered, but it’s not exciting enough for our mailing list guys to want to send you something on a regular basis. I’ve given your account a bit of a ‘nudge’ in the database, so something should arrive within the next few weeks. Otherwise, I could add your email to the account in the meantime. Contact me via email if you like to change your preferences.

I think I need to pay for the next cab ride.
