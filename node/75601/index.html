I would be more concerned over the width of the letters. Each glyph in a typeface is something of a compromise, optimized to work with every other glyph, so any particular combination in a word may not be ideal. In this word, the M is really wide, and the F quite narrow, drawing attention to the middle of the word, when really the initial letter is the most important. So why not make the F a bit wider (and possibly the T), and the M narrower, and see if that improves the whole? BTW, please make images no wider than 588 pixels, to avoid scrolling.

2010-10-26 00:39:24 • dberlow
Since it’s a logo, why don’t you just try drawing it in pencil first? You may use Eurostile Extended or whatever, as a starting point, but then, again, you have the advantage of designing a logotype: i.e. the letters which need to work well together are – in your case – just nine, and not twenty-six. After you are satisfied, even if you are unpractical with bezier drawing, you can use your sketch as a basis, and the letters you need, as a starting point for your final, vector-based version.
