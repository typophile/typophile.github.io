2007-08-01 17:28:17 • leewilson78
I'd bet on the semi-bold. With this sort of printing you want to eliminate any chance for the printer to goof. You might inquire about the line screen. 175 or above has a much better chance of keeping the skinny bits from closing up. Also, when choosing the color, fewer plates is better. So if your CMYK mix has less than 5% of any color you might consider reworking the mix to eliminate a color. Ask about minimum coverage percentages. The light screens could be an issue for the printer.

2007-08-02 01:51:29 • leewilson78
One thing to keep in mind when designing a two-sided business card is that only one side will be visible at once. When you hand it to the person, it is easy to turn it over to see the other side, but remember that many, many cards get placed in folders or rolodexes, and in these cases only the one side shows. You want to make sure that at least one side includes all the important information that might make a customer call you, rather than the guy on the next card.
