2011-01-07 21:46:09 • Bert Vanderveen

In heavier fonts the distance of the overshoot usually needs to increase to proportionally increase the surface area of the overshoot to the surface area the letter gains from its extra weight.

When I open up some fonts in Fontlab, it seems that the overshoot is only made larger at the top, not the bottom.

What fonts are you looking at? I have rarely seen type designed that way.

I see that darker weights appear to have a smaller x-height, but it seems much more logical to me to just increase the x-height instead of the overhoot.

Most of the time the x-height must increase slightly as weight increases to keep the letters from filling in. This requires a matching increase in overshoot distance in addition to the increase that comes with weight. Rarely a font is drawn with a consistent x-height, but that’s a special challenge for special designs.
