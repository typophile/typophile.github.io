CamelCase: What do you call it?

August 13, 2004 • Joe Pemberton

Help solve an internal squabble here in our office. CamelCase: Is
that the real name for it? What do you call it? Personally, I like
the term because camels are mostly ugly creatures and so is that
setting most of the time.

camelCase: as in the syntax used by programmers in terms like
"openWindow" or "startNow."

CamelCase as in the format used for every new product trying to
seem hip. iTunes, eCommerce, iPhoto.

I can see the new Adobe apps adding camel case to their list
of cases you can convert your text to.
The more common term is "intercap". And if I were being picky, I'd say that DromedaryCase would be more accurate. :-) And here's a typographic twist: the "D" in Dromedary has one "hump" while the "B" in Bactrian has two!

BTW, how do you judge beauty? To me, functionality, resilience, self-sufficiency are beautiful. Try leaving a Golden Retriever in the desert for a day, much less weeks.
