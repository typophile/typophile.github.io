2007-04-26 16:32:03 • dezcom

Chocolate grinders, too. And ****ing a lot of women. Crossdressing (as Rrose Sélavy), optical effect devices, inventing readymades, graphic design (I’ll get back to that), declaring painting dead, staying with friends and patrons (for years at a stretch), living of the same work for decades by producing official replicas, watching dust grow & being impossibly smart throughout all. You probably noticed: MD is my all time hero and fave.

Graphic design: MD did some remarkable work in this field, not dilettantish at all — he really dove into it. A very nice one is a cover he did for a box with fascimile in 1934: the titling was done with punched out holes. Couldn’t find a pic of that online. Other stuff, to give you an impression:

That’s a cover for a chess book he did. And this is poster for a gallery:

In his high days Akzidenz Grotesk-like type was en vogue, as well as strangely ornate Nouveau-ish display type.

There used to be a font based on his handwriting available (from P22), but no more:

http://www.p22.com/projects/dufont.html
