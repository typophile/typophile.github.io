I’m interested to see where the vitruvian H came from, since it is about to be literally etched into stone at various places around our little campus. I found a similar font called ‘Leonardo’ and one that comes close called ‘Codex’. Still not exact. Look at the H watermark behind the UAHuntsville (which looks to be Arial bold).

http://www.uah.edu/govrel/

I think it’s more a reference to the “mathematically constructed” type of the Italian and Northern Renaissance (e.g. Duerer’s work). There was the idea that the Roman inscriptional letterforms (see Trajan) were based on an ideal geometry, so many early designers set out to make letters solely with perfect circles and ratios, with less-than-harmonious results.

And it’s not Arial - it’s a serif type, my guess being Georgia Bold. It doesn’t really work with the Renaissance surroundings.
