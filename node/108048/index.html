2014-02-10 23:17:24 • shaugaard

Hi Scott, You may find lots of handwriting fonts on Dafont, but none of them have the Opent Type magic. As Nick and Cristobal suggested: Olicana, Rollercsript and Suomi Hand are the closer you will get to real handwriting. If those are not enough, then you need a custom-made font for your client.

I recently did a custom handwriting font for a huge corporate client, with 5 variations of each letter, pseudo-random lowercase, pseudo-random uppercase, automatic small-caps, all 105 Latin languages including Vietnamese, etc, etc. But this kind of custom jobs are expensive, around 30.000 USD. I don’t know if your client can afford it, or if they want to spend that much.

In summary, the options are:

  • Search dafont for handwriting fonts, but all the letters will be the repeated (not natural effect)
  • Use one of the best already-available fonts (Olicana, Rollercsript or Suomi Hand)
  • Hire someone to make a custom font for your client (Best solution, but expensive)
