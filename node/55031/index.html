It's a bird, It's a plane, Oh No! It's Bad Type!?

February 20, 2009 • Reba Joy

That’s right you heard correct it is bad type, and unfortunately I have to say the guilty party are repeat offenders, guilty of one thing and one thing only, creating rivers and valleys in their type. The type has fallen victim to the act of justifying type without adjustment of letter spacing.

[img:sites/default/files/old-images/rivers_5289.jpg] ( you can almost imagine a ball flowing through the rivers in this text, like a pinball machine)

Why is this such a horrible offense you ask? Because it creates a terrible valley in the line of communication. The readers eye now has too much room to breathe and is left to fend for themselves without a solid direction or guide. The eye falls and rests abruptly when rivers and valleys are created in the type.

How is “good” type different? Good typography would enable the reader to read and absorb the information at a steady pace. Enabling a clear line of communication. Good type would not be overcrowded, it would maintain the appropriate number of characters per line, as well as create proportionate letter spacing.

What do you deem to be “good” type? and just for the sake of asking what do you label as “bad” type? post examples!
