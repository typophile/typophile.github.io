Stems appear thicker at text size

December 25, 2010 • 1996type

Hello Everybody,

For the past few days I’ve been working on a new typeface called Sensa (http://typophile.com/node/77453#comment-444957 In the first test prints the stems on d h p q h l b, etc (basically all stems that touch the ascender/descender) appeared much to thick. It seemed that the smaller the size got, the thicker they looked. Logically I made them thinner and they look fine to me at text size now. I thought it was problem solved until Nick Job noticed that those stems looked a little thin. They look fine to my eye (also on display size), but perhaps my eyes have got used to the thin stems. So I would like to know if anybody else has ever encountered this problem (if it’s there in the first place) and how to deal with it. I suggest you print the pdf because it might not render the way it should on screen.

Merry Christmas,

Jasper de Waard

A lot depends on the device and paper. Putting ink on paper can have unpredictable results. How things look coming off laser or ink-jet printers – even the so-called “high end” digital presses – is different than how they look coming off an offset press. As far as that goes, type will look a bit different on different papers, or from different offset press setups.

I would keep the stem thickness the same for ink on paper.

From some of the discussions on Typophile, something similar can happen with pixels on screens.

Really? you ever thought of rounding errors? I’ve found I can’t finalize kerning with a 1200 dpi laser printout. Have to wait until the book is printed offset. Even with determining the proper distance between say, the stem of the “i” an the stem of the “f” in the f_i ligature is difficult using the laser printer. Pick the distance that looks best on the greatest number of occurrences, then wait for the book for any final correction.

BTW, you can taper the long stems in the middle for a little thinner look.
