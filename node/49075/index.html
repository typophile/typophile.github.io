What is this Gormenghast Trilogy font from Vintage Classic?

September 4, 2008 • Spix

Hi, I am looking for a specific font, and it says in the guidelines to ask here.

There is a font from Mervyn Peake’s Gormenghast Trilogy that I am having a hard time identifing. It seems close to TheAntiquaSun and in some aspects, Leipziger Antiqua.

[img:sites/default/files/old-images/textsamplegormenghastrawd3_4275.jpg]

The biggest indicator that it is different from any other font I’ve found are the commas and the apostrophes. In AntiquaSun, the commas and quote marks are curved - like most fonts. In the text sampled above, the commas are straight, almost like a stroke from a painter’s brush. They are dashes, or teardrops.

The letters above are “sharper” too (i.e look at letters “y” and “c”) but are not coarsely angular like the Leipziger. For instance, the tail in the letter “y” ends in a straight manner. In AntiquaSun, the end of “y” is curved.

The Gormenghast book version is from Vintage.

If anyone can enlighten me as to what font this is, I would be most appreciative.

Spix.
