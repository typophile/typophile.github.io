Although it was not widely accepted in its early years, the use of vivid, non-naturalistic, and exuberant color is what makes this time period unique. This was the first period in which color ruled supreme. I rather like the unnatural use color used to spice things up a bit. [img:sites/default/files/old-images/picasso_1_4601.jpg] Invented by Pablo Picasso and Georges Braque, Cubism to me is the most radical and most innovative time in art's history. These guys took spicing things up to a whole other level. I don't necessarily like all of it, but the concept behind the work is pretty solid. They distorted images, wanting to break away from the conventional. The works are broken and abstracted, meant to demonstrate the mind's ability to abstract the natural world. [img:sites/default/files/old-images/PotsdamerPlatz_Kirchner_4618.jpg] The exaggerated imagery displayed in the Expressionist period really speaks to its audience. This time period used symbolic colors within ordinary, everyday scenes. These works reflect the artist's state of mind rather than the reality of the external world.
