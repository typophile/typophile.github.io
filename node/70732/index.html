I think it's quite likely that the publishers had a font made to resemble Seuss's lettering, but it's proprietary. I looked for styles with a similar feeling and would suggest these as alternatives: Bella by Alex Grecian, Everafter by Silas Dilworth and Farmhouse by Noah Rothschild. By making them outline letters and then individually positioning each letter I think you could produce something that would have a similar look, even if it was not identical. - Mike Yanega
