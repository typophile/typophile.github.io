Pagination for the Web

March 25, 2009 • Abigail Smith

Anyone have thoughts on average page length/word-count for articles posted on the web? A cursory review reveals the following standards on news-related sites:

New York Times Article: 800 Words CNN: No Pagination, Article Length 1,000 Words International Herald Tribune: 800 Words

Seems also like a lot of sites simply limit the length of articles written for the web (a good thing, but not always possible).

Commercial news site pagination has little to do with article length and everything to do with ad impressions.

Some newspapers break up their online articles into “pages” but give the reader the choice of seeing the entire article as a single “page.”

The prevailing wisdom is that brevity is better for online reading, but as you point out, some content demands length – short attention spans be damned. ;-D
