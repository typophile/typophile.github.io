Paper for Bookbinding

April 6, 2008 • BaileyDwyer

Hi, I’m a student working on putting a bunch of portfolio books together for the end of the semester. I have to print all my work with my Epson R1400, and I have been having trouble finding good paper to print on. The main problems I run into are that the paper is not double-sided, it is not made for an inkjet printer, or the paper is too thick for pages in a book. I printed one of my books on Ilford Galerie Smooth Matte Multi-Use Dual Sided Inkjet Paper… but I can’t seem to find the paper any larger than 8.5 x 11, which was fine when I was perfect binding, but now it is too small.

So… Does anyone know of any good inkjet paper to print images on for a book? Any help would be wonderful. Thanks!

Hahnemuhle has a couple of dual sided papers that might be nice. The Natural Art Duo is .15mil and Photo Duo is .18mil. They come in sizes up to 17x22 inches and may come in rolls. You can check out www.itsupplies.com or www.atlex.com. You could also check with www.shadesofpaper.com. Any of those vendors are helpful.

I don’t really know if these would be too heavy or not but they are nice papers. Epson’s enhanced matte dual-sided papers are nice too and don’t seem overly heavy. I don’t know about the sizes but it should come on a roll.

Sharon

I do a lot of my bookbinding work using cut-down sheets of Strathmore Aquarius II (it comes in 22 x 30 sheets with side deckles) and it runs through just fine on my Epson SPR300, even though it’s supposedly “not recommended.”

You might want to find a god art supply store that has packages of paper samples (Talas, for example, since it’s in Manhattan), buy one, and try running as much as you can (and like) through.
