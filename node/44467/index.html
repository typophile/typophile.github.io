The Brewers' Tale

April 21, 2008 • Reed Reibstein

This week has proved a typographic adventure. After designing the Yale typeface article, I had the opportunity to work on the Yale Daily News' arts and living section, “scene.” My colleague, Greta Carlson, and I designed the cover, which is traditionally highly-themed, after Jost Amman’s “Der Bierbreuwer” in Das Ständebuch. Below I’ve attached the cover and jump page for the article (about student beer brewing at Yale), along with a close-up of the cover. The type used is Litteratra Basic for the headlines, subheads, and page 1 text; LTC Goudy Text Lombardic Caps for the initials; and a slightly bigger size of our standard text type, Miller Daily Grade 3, for the jump's text. As with the Yale typeface article thread, criticism is welcome, though naught can be altered now. Two problems I see are that the first initial “S” should have been nudged up a tad, and the justification is a bit wonky (owing to my turning off hyphenation because of the problems test readers had with Litteratra’s hyphen). Nice spread of matching illustration and text. I like the colors used. The designer of Litteratra, Karsten Luecke will be pleased with this use of his beautiful font. He is a member of Typophile where he is known as k.l. In the Middle Ages I was art editor of my university newspaper and have fond memories of clunky Linotype machines casting hot lead lines of letterpress that are then set into a matrix, inked and a paper proof made. If one of us hardworking students finds a mistake that line is tossed back into the machine, melted and cast again. The illustrations were made by another process whereby an acid bath eats away portions of a photographically-exposed zinc plate leaving the lines or screen dots that will be printed. No hissing sounds or acrid smell of hot lead as we now move mouse on mousepad, is there!? Great!
