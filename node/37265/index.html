2007-09-20 11:19:24 • Jackie Frant

Patty et al., I’m really glad to hear this kind of feature request and I’m sorry the IM feature is missed… As much as it pains me to admit; the reasons were purely technical. An IM feature is not a flawed idea, but it was a flawed piece of code (and in our view, fatally so).

As you might be aware, the IM component was a piece of open source code (a Drupal module for the PHP inclined among you) that never quite functioned as it should have. Messages were not stored by the database and neither were the responses. If you dismissed a note it was gone forever. In that sense it was fleeting at best, unreliable and misleading at its worst. Moderators had no way of tracking responses, users had no way of knowing if a message was received. It was interruptive (in your face), when it should’ve been passive.

The second major problem was if you wanted to leave the IM there, so you could eventually respond to it at your leisure. To Mili’s point, this implementation had no inbox or save option. It meant getting duplicate messages every time you hit a new page. The result was a nice feature, poorly executed.

But, the nail in the coffin for this implementation of an IM feature was that it would’ve required more work to re-implement this feature that was not supported by the Drupal upgrade. We did not believe the benefit lived up to the level of effort required.

Finally, I would clarify Zara’s comment this way. That unless the IM module gets significant improvement we won’t be brining this implementation back. We are very interested in finding ways to further people’s ability to make connections, foster community and be networked. We’re investigating ways to enhance each person’s profile page to become a “wall” of sorts for public and private kinds of messaging in addition to the contact, blogging, bio and links already afforded there.

On email notification

William, you can let others contact you without publicly posting your email address.

To turn on private “email notification”:

  • Going to your profile by clicking on your name,
  • Click edit,
  • Scroll to the bottom,
  • Under “Contact Settings” turn on the checkbox.

You can contact anybody who has opted in to this feature:

  • Click on the users' name.
  • Click the “Contact” tab,
  • Type your subject and message and hit send.
  • You can even copy yourself so you have a record of the email.
