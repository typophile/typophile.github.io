for example in a date do i use emdash or endash ” 17-05-09”

In this case I would use neither, instead use a regular dash, or if you can, a figure dash.

also for example on a poster say an expo runs from 17 “until” 20 march 2009 (meaning that actualy the expo ends on 19 march. do you use en or emdash between “17 and 20”)

Here you would use an en dash. En dashes are used anytime you have something in a range (times, dates, etc.).

As far as em dashes go, you use them mostly in body text when interrupting a train of thought.

…in the rare case in which a sentence is broken off abruptly without being completed, a single dash is also used:

2009-11-05 11:24:40 • bendy

Am I right in remembering English style train of thought is interrupted by space-en dash-space, while Americans interrupt using the em dash solus (w/o spaces)?

That is certainly what I do in documents – described in Bringhurst as ‘a phrase marker’ in a sentence with normal word spaces. I have seldom used an em dash except when I found the en dash too narrow.

However, to indicate 17–20 March (meaning something runs from 17th until 20th March inclusive) I use an en with no spaces. For a date as numerals I would use 17/05/09 although that might change if using non-aligning numerals as here.

Bringhurst also mentions the three-to-em dash (which I have never used), he also gives a pithy criticism of the em dash as belonging in “the padded and corseted aesthetic of Victorian typography”.
