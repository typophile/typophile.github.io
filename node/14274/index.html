Phil, thanks for asking for the opinion of the Typophiliacs; I'm sure that you will get many good recommendations. Many of which will be for you to read "The Elements of Typographic Style" by Robert Bringhurst. Since its publication in the early 1990s, this handsome book has become the bible for type lovers. It's very well written and easy to read, didactic but not dogmatic, and contains valuable insight for readers at all levels of knowledge, from neophyte through veteran. I can't priase the book enough. Ellen Lupton's recent "Thinking With Type" is a more basic book, and it is very approachable. Ellen is an educator, and this book is a de facto text book. There is a great companion website: http://www.thinkingwithtype.com/
