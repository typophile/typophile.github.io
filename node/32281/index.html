To start, i’m new to the site and i think i just found hours worth of reading material..good stuff ;)

Now on to the beginning logo sketches; Client is a company describes themselves as a design,marketing and advertising company. It’s a local startup with hopes of attracting small to mid-size business with a decent budget for advertising. In short, between mom and pop shops and big corporate.

-some (rather philosophical) guidelines they gave: -The name they have is Dynamis Media -definition: strength, ability, power, “inherent power, power residing in a thing by virtue of its nature, or which a person or thing exerts and puts forth” -Red is a power color -Red was crucial to alchemy. Preliminary stage to the “Philosophers Stone” - a magic substance which was capable of converting cheap metals into gold.

I’m in the beginning stages of sketching and concepts….some help would be greatly appreciated :) Attached are a few I picked, leaning towards the first and last ones. your thoughts?

I think you might need to start again - the typeface itself doesn’t feel powerful to me.

One final thought on the Philosophers Stone - true alchemists understood that this was a metaphor and that what they were really trying to do is convert themselves to “gold”. In essence the purification of the self. Of course there were a lot of charlatans…

It seems like they want more red than you have given them. That being said I think it’s good to sketch out your ideas in black and white and then worry about colors.

Find what you consider to be a powerful typeface and then go from there…

Alchemists used alembics, crucibles and fire to conduct their experiments. It is also the forefather of chemistry. There might be something there, perhaps a chemical symbol…
