2008-12-24 11:29:28 • Nick Job
  1. Because figures are narrow. Only a, r, s and t are narrow letters (not including 1, i, and l), and m and w are very wide. Therefore figures need to be scaled up a bit, or else overall they seem too condensed in comparison.
  2. It helps differentiate between o, and slightly higher, wider, zero.
  3. Also, more figures have descenders than ascenders, and the top of 4 is weak, therefor their overall centre (vertically) is a bit low, compared to the minuscule letters, where there are more ascenders than descenders.
  4. As Nick says, to align with small caps, which are taller than x-height, for similar reasons.
