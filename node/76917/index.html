2010-12-05 18:31:43 • Kristians Sics
@Hrant: that's an unfair -if convenient- blanket statement. It's accurate. Professional organizations such as the AIGA and GDC don't just frown, they prohibit members from spec work. I consider all type competitions (including type annuals that request submissions) to be spec work, because they require type designers to create a specimen. And they don't even reimburse winners for the cost of doing that. I was on Creative Review's mailing list for its new type annual, to which I replied, "Thank you for your interest in my work. However, I am not disposed to compete for the privilege of providing your publication with free content." ...and was subsequently "followed up on" several times with comments such as, "I am aware that previously there was an issue with the fee for an entry, therefore if you are interested in submitting a typeface or typefaces we would be willing to offer you a further discount." The ethics of not doing spec work can seem out of place in the digital era. ** To return to the topic, even if a client commits to a custom solution, the first step is often narrowing the focus of the brief by seeing which off-the-shelf designs come close to the desired tone and style. I'm having difficulty visualizing a vaulted-cellar bar as being either upscale or contemporary, but suggest you set up "R55" in the text field and search at MyFonts under the keyword "luxury", "upscale", or "contemporary". - Nick Shinn, MGDC

These are the specs: For each typeface you are submitting please provide a full character set. This should at minimum consist of complete upper and lower case alphabets and numerals 0 – 9 but, in the case of extensive Open Type faces, please also submit a representative sample of extra characters. For Arabic or other non-Latin faces where character sets may vary, please exercise discretion and submit what you consider to be a sufficient character set for the judges to be able to make their choice. These should be supplied at 48 point, as should, as a bare minimum, the sample sentence The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog. Non-Latin entries may substitute a similar or equivalent phrase. We would also encourage entrants to submit additional samples to show their typeface in use. You may include headlines, short or long passages of text, or sample layouts showing the typeface in use in eg advertising or publications. With Display or Bespoke entries where entire upper and lower case alphabets or numeral sets do not exist, please supply all the characters available. If the typeface is to be used to set text, please also submit 200 words of dummy text of your choosing set in the following: 8/10 pt, 10/12pt, 12/14 pt. This applies to all Text entries and may also apply to Bespoke or Non-Latin typefaces. Each entry should be set in 100% black on A3 and be provided as a separate 300 dpi PDF, with a maximum file size of 5mb, with the name of the typeface clearly marked on each page.
