Strokes thicker at the top than the bottom (top heavy tapered strokes) are a 1920s fashion for lettering. Rudolph Koch and then Morris Benton did some fonts in this style. For example Morris Benton’s 1928 ATF Paramount, which is included with Jim Spiece’s series of Rudolph Koch’s Eva (Locarno) designs. Here it as as Eva Paramount http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/spiecegraphics/eva-sg/ Ignore the serifs and the similarities of concept are similar. And for a very sharp version of the idea check this old poster for the film Metropolis which is the inspiration for a font called Metropolis by Alphabet and Type http://www.alphabetype.it/metropolis_font.html With some digging it should be possible to locate a rounded hand drawn sans with this thick top style. Don

Revised: I gave up on finding a top heavy tapered wedge with a soft blobby feel and looked for soft blobby fonts that were fairly lightweight and tapered to the tips rather than having tips bigger than the stokes.
