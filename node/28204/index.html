2006-09-13 15:50:50 • Termopolium

As usual in English there are several ways of putting this, but using your copy as a start

Open (could be all uppercase) Mon. to Fri. 10am to 6pm (24 hour clock means you can drop the am and pm so 10:00 to 18:00, although the 24 hour clock causes some problems, especially to people without a background in the military or organisations which use radio communications)

Using an en-dash (for UK, em-dash for US) Open (from is not really necessary) Monday–Friday (or Mon–Fri) (or Mon.–Fri. although the full stop does make a space before the en-dash which you might want to compensate for with a thin space after the dash) 10:00–18:00 (or 10.00–18.00) (or 10am–6pm)

Slightly edited copy and my preference Opening Hours Monday–Friday 10am–6pm

Tim

Edit: despite Typophile’s preferences for old style (non-aligning) numerals in this case aligning numerals would be easier to read

2006-09-13 16:03:09 • timd

The conventions in England and the US are sometimes different, so you will need to decide which to follow.

In the US the Chicago Manual of Style is the most widely used style book. It recommends either 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. (note the space after the numeral and the periods in the abbreviation) or 10 AM to 6 PM with AM and PM in small caps, no periods.

The 24 hour notation is used even less in the US than in England, though it is used.

edit: also in the US it is 10:00 a.m. not 10.00 a.m. If you have to set English often the best thing is just to buy a style book for either England or the US, depending. These questions are endless, even for a native speaker.
