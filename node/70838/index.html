Looking at Mac McGrew’s book of American Metal Typefaces there is a bold version of Goudy Oldstyle that was designed by Morris Benton between 1916 - 1919 to go with the Goudy-designed typeface. The swash M is a slanted version of what’s on your card. The Roman version of Goudy Bold type has the same g and the i’s have the same diamond shaped dots. Its ‘n’ matches the normal n on your card. The swash ‘n’ doesn’t look like anything from the typeface samples in this book. It does resemble Bookman swash (in the version done by Jason Walcott of Jukebox), and that even has a similar M, although the swash elements are not right. I think Jason’s version is based on an older design for Bookman than the ITC version.

2010-05-29 17:19:46 • Florian Hardwig

Goudytype is shown in the McGrew book, and the M is similar, but not identical. it has only swash capitals, no lower case swash letters.

I looked again at Goudy Bold Italic, the ATF face that has the swash letters. The M is closer to the sample above, but still not identical (the lower curl is bigger than the sample posted), AND it is slanted. It does have a swash lower case, but the tails on the letters, like the m and n, are along the baseline, not below it.

It may be that the original was drawn using Goudy/Bookman samples as ‘inspiration’.
