2006-10-29 16:21:14 • hrant
Take classes in formal calligraphy. Firstly, basic exercises tighten up your form and give it a pleasing consistency. Secondly, the joining rules turn writing into an intriguing game, because when it comes to writing an unusual word, or one that requires “notwithstanding” sub-rules, writing then becomes a process where the writer is trying to implement the rules correctly, and this engagement can impart an expressive personality to the writing. Or else it turns into a very “proper” style – but either way it’s an improvement.
