Once again, I think this lettering is from the large selection of film types available from the 60’s through at least the 80’s. I wouldn’t be surprised to find a near match among the samples in the middle part of the Solotype Catalog, which I don’t have with me right now. There were hundreds of brush drawn scripts in all combinations of weight and slant, as well as letterform styles from casual script to poster-style printing, with and without serifs. Dom Casual was one that made it into digital form early (was it metal first?), but there were many others. URW has digitized some of them, and so has Bitstream, and a trickle of others have been copied in other collections. This was obviously a trove of designs for type users in the pre-digital age.
