I used the Serif Font ID Guide and got [[http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/urw/baskerville-old-face/|Baskerville Old Face]] (or [[http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/bitstream/frys-baskerville/|Fry’s]]) as what seems closest. The W looks to have a middle serif, which would eliminate Mrs Eaves and most other Baskervilles. The C does not look like it has 2 serifs, which also points to Old Face/Fry’s. The white on black lettering looks bolder than the reverse, so it seems to have less contrast than the black on white way has, but I think these Baskervilles are most likely.

The sample Spire posted does not lead me to the same conclusion as before. It seems to be the same typeface, but it certainly looks different to me seeing it that way. Strangely, what it seemed closest to, structurally, was Font Bureau’s relatively new font [[http://www.fontbureau.com/fonts/ZocaloText/styles|Zócalo Text]]. However the italic is way off and the rest is not dead on by any means. The E is especially odd, and I think should be a key letter. Zócalo has the long, underslung lower arm, and the L is rather wide, but not quite as exaggerated as the sample. The W looks more stepped than the Baskerville’s I mentioned, and is more like the W’s that Frederic Goudy often used, however, none of his fonts have an E like this, as far as I know.
