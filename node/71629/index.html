I bought the Mendoza book a few weeks ago and there is one mention of a book using Photina: Methods of Book Design, by Hugh Williamson (Alden Press).

Also a mention that the Oxford University Press adopted Photina for their books. Photina was launched in December 1971, so that should give you a start of that period…

One importand book typeset in Photina is the third edition of Methods of Book Design by Hugh Williamson (New Haven and London: Yale University Press, 1983). Williamson was a great fan of Photina, ‘an early example of a type-face originally designed for photo-composition’.
2010-06-24 16:00:05 • Maxim Zhukov
Among the Oxford books set in Photina is the fourth edition of the Oxford Dictionary of Quotations (1992, rev. 1996). Univers is used with it to a limited extent, for the serial numbering of quotations on each page and in the index.
