Yes, the manual isn’t very helpfull and there are not too many tutorials around either.

A good way to learn where to put T1 hints and HRs, is to open a T1 font, which has been hinted (not all T1 fonts are hinted, or hints are not visible in FL).

In general, hints are placed at node extremes (inside counters and outside of the outline). Sometimes, as with the ‘B’, the zone (area between hints) overlap. This is indicated in the glyph window by a light yellow hatching. The rasterizer, which is part of the glyph renderer of an OS, ‘walks’ around the outline and determines where pixels should be turned on. If it arrives at a part of the outline within this overlap zone, it doesn’t know which hint to apply. It could be the hint from the start of the overlap, or the hint at the other end of the overlap. By placing HRs, particular hints are applied to a particlar part of the outline.

The way to learn is to actually hint the B from the manual, making sure the nodes are numbered the same way and the direction of the outlines are the same. You can check if the appropriate HRs are applied by clicking on an outline, which was in the overlap zone. Only the hints that apply are turned green.

I think that autohinting a T1 font isn’t bad at all in FL. It has been suggested as the process of choice: first authint, then do some tweaks here and there.
