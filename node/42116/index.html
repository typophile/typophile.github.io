2008-02-15 06:48:22 • Sharon Van Lieu

It’s got some issues.

First, it’s too complicated. I always look at my work at an inch square - about the size it would appear on a business card. I simplify (and simplify) until it’s recognizable at a glance when it’s THAT small.

Gradients are never a good idea in a logo - they don’t reproduce well on photocopies or faxes.

The font looks smashed together on the left and too spaced on the right; but before you work on kerning, look around at other fonts. All caps doesn’t score high for readability, but I think you could find something more elegant. Neutra Display Bold comes to mind.
