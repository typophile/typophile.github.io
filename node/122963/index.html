The ampersand is the clue here: this is a variation of Copperplate, and as Don said, most likely hand-engraved: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/copperplate-gothic/ Widen Engraver’s Gothic a bit, and it might also work (though it has a distinctly different ampersand): http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/paratype/engravers-gothic/

2015-02-01 21:46:47 • mvor
Copperplate Gothic was designed by Frederic W. Goudy for ATF in 1901. The condensed versions shown in my 1923 ATF type book are a good match for the digital ones now available from Linotype https://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/copperplate-gothic/ I have not checked the earlier ATF books, but I expect that the condensed versions were offered before 1914 and would probably have been a familiar face to the French Engraver. Clip the mini serifs and Voilà! Don
