Commissioners are sometimes quite fussy and always ignorant about fonts. So don’t try to persuade them. Especially if they are 45+ women. No sexist intentions there, just mere experience. If a commissioner has the time to quarrel over fonts and to infringe on your design, likely he or she is someone fitting the Peter principle. Be pragmatic, ignore the pedantry, do as you please and get the approval of someone higher in rank, someone with no explicit opinions about fonts, but who’ll sure acknowledge the designer knows for the best. He/she will really see the difference, especially if you say him/her Arial is a discount wholesale product, lacking each and every distinction.
2007-08-10 14:48:45 • Rhythmus.be
For 100 pages you really want a serif typeface. But stick to one face and one version of it for the body text, headers, pull quotes, etc. With italics and size variations you can achieve contrast. If you want a contasting face for certain elements, use a good sans.
