Photoshop CS prevents currency scanning

January 9, 2004 • Chris Rugen

According to BoingBoing.net, "Photoshop CS automatically detects images of US currency and prevents the manipulation, copying and pasting of same."

The article is linked from a Slashdot article, which links to the Adobe forum on the subject.

Interestingly, it prevents users from engaging in what the US government defines as legal usage of currency images. This is both depressing and scary. Apparently, it's restricted to only certain denominations, but isn't restricted to just US currency. Also, there are many caveats to what exactly will and will not open. Does anyone else know anything about this?

2004-01-12 23:24:51 • Adriano Santi
If you're wondering how this works, Photoshop CS looks for the "EURion Constellation" hidden in modern currency from Canada, Europe, Britain, Germany, the US and elsewhere. No fancy histogram checks, no image content recognition. Just a simple pattern detection algorithm.

New versions of JASC Paint Shop Pro and Ulead PhotoImpact also contain this currency detection algorithm. A current proposal will require this of all image editing software.

There are ways around the system. Opening it in Photoshop 7, hiding the layer and then re-opening it in Photoshop CS works. Or you can scale the image larger than 150% or smaller than 75%. The Treasury Board also said they will give out electronic versions which do not contain the protective pattern if you ask nicely.

I'm curious to see the result of adding this pattern to an original artwork. Will it still open in Photoshop CS? Does Photoshop look for more than just the EURion Constellation when determining what is a bank note?
