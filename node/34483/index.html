Garamond is a rather formal (Classic) typeface, so I am assuming that you are thinking of a rather formal script (Bickham and Avalon are a mix, with Avalon less formal, in my opinion). However, your logo has a more modern, casual look to it (and it’s nicely done, by the way). Which way do you want to go?

Frankly, I think Bickham gets overused, especially with the huge swash initials. No complaints from me about the regular Bickham though, as a classic script. But if you are wanting to find something that might work with your logo idea, possibly Font Bureau Sloop would give you a somewhat similar R in an elegant script that I think would go nicely with a classic serif like Garamond. This is very subjective, of course. Only you can tell if you think they look OK together.

For more ideas, you might look at Parts 1 & 2 of the Script Font ID Guide for more formal styles and Parts 7 and 8 for more casual styles. Another idea is to use Antique Handwriting, as if from the Garamond era of the 1700’s, and for those styles See Part 10 of the Guide.
