Business: All leather, handmade, vintage products - pants, wallets, belts, etc.

Looking for a typeface with Latino influence. Help.

The first thing that comes to mind is Sudtipos type foundry. They’re an Argentinian foundry and have a number of their fonts available from Veer.com. Many of their types have both a great Latin feel and a certain cool, vintage quality - seems like a good match for your product. A ton of their fonts would be a nice match, but the ones that appeal to me are Argenta, Candombe, Murga, Plumera, Suave, Sudestada, and Titanes. Although it doesn’t especially match your look, I want to mention Affair, too - I bought it recently, and I think I’m in love (with OpenType alternates, of course)!

Two other nice ones: Rayuela by PampaType (interesting in that it’s like a straightened italic, in the vein of Jeremy Tankard’s Aspect) and Sans Merci by SMeltery, which, though French, has a certain funky flavor. The M is too out there for me, though.
