Hi

I am working on a brochure with very narrow columns and our client doesn’t want the very long E-mail and URL addresses to be hyphanated. I can’t set them in a smaller size and they will obviously not shorten their addresses. Is there a miracle solution?

By the way I’ve seen in Dot dot dot magazine that they encapsulate URLs in < > symboles. Just like this <www.dot-dot-dot.nl>. Is this a typographic standard?

It’s not a typographic standard as such, but the way some mail programs show url’s — in the same way as underscoring. But I do think it’s an elegant solution to your problem. The only thing that could be bothersome is hyphenation: I think that’s undesirable (could be confusing!), so don’t use it and doublecheck all url’s in your text before going to print…
2005-08-23 12:26:22 • Bert Vanderveen

“…Just like this <www.dot-dot-dot.nl>. Is this a typographic standard?”

Some applications used the bracketing with greater/lesser than as a way to automate creating links from URLs in text. This is less of an issue now since most apps are smart enough to make links on the fly. In printed documents, the advantage is that you know the beginning and end. This does not help your problem when you have long URLs. The only solution is using one of the phonebook fonts (perhaps Telefont) which are designed for use at small sizes.
