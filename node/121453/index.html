To me this looks like a pre WW I sign with mini-serifs and line shadow emphasis like Martin Waite used in his revival style lettering under the name Horndon. Now digitized https://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/horndon/ Horndon’s letter-forms are quite different but you could find a sans with a similar letter-forms and give it the same treatment. The lettering is unusual in that the S is wider than O, about the same width as M. In other words, all the letters are wide except for O and M. One solution would be to take a font like Realist Wide Medium http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/martinplusfonts/realist-wide/ or Sweet Sans http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/sweet/sans/ and narrow the O and M.
