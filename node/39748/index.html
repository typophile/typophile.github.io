Paper for wine labels

December 4, 2007 • Frode Bo Helland

I’m looking for suitable paper to print wine labels for my grandfathers self made “BT Chateaux”. Any ideas?

That question would be most easily answered by a label printer, but it will vary based on how you intend to apply the labels. Nearly any kind of paper or plastic film can have adhesive applied to it, but those that already come that way are limited.

How many bottles is he trying to label? For more than a gross you’ll probably want to have a machine do it, but for less than that a few people could manage applying self-adhesive labels by hand. Some papers are more suited to high-speed/high-volume processes than others, and others require careful attention. You could even skip labels all together and have the markings silkscreened directly on to the glass.

What’s important to consider is the quantity of bottles, the price-point of the wine, and the image you want to project about it. Things will fall into place once you know that.

“Specialized papers?” Yes. Liquor labels, especially premium brands, use paper with higher ‘wet strength’. These are papers specially manufactured for the moist conditions in which liquor bottles are usually stored.
