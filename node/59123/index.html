June 17, 2009 • kristin

Is there a term that describes written speech that indicates overly enthusiastic or elongated pronunciation? For instance, a baseball announcer who transforms the word “strike” into several syllables and takes extra seconds to finish the word.

I am looking for samples of this in print and I can’t figure out how to search for the phenomenon since I can’t explain it in a few words or less.

Some words that come to mind are “strike” or “goal” or “excuse me” ala Steve Martin. Also various swear words.

I’m also wondering if there is a common method for indicating this in print. Is putting a hyphen between each character sufficient or does one need to double up the characters as I did in the title above? Does the word have to be in all caps or can lower case work? Is it important to try to add letters (stee-rike) that don’t exist in the original word in order to emphasize the mispronunciation?

Let me know what you think!

Kristin

Hyphens between letters can also indicate spelling out the word (though I guess the context would usually make clear which effect was intended).

Gotta envy Japanese for the dash used to lengthen vowels in Katakana. You can use multiple ones of these, too, if you want to get expressive.
