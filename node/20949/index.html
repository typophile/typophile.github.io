Setting up own company

June 26, 2006 • Matt Squire

Hi there. I am not sure if something like this has been posted before, I dont think so so here goes. I have been working as a graphic designer for around 7 years now and although the job I have is secure and brings in some good clients, due to the size of the company and the clients it has the creative part can be very frustrating. To cut a long story short, which I am sure many of you are aware of, projects can easily become more about the politics and strategic grounding behind a project which can sometimes lead to the creative part becomming less important. I am now thinking of trying to set up something of my own and although I know what this entails and what hard work, but worth while work it can be there is one thing that I am a little unsure of. I have always been employed by someone and although I have done many freelance projects along the way, most of my work has been done whilst employed. My question is, is when I create my folio for my future company is it ok to use the work I have done whilst employed, as long as I credit it to the company I was with at the time. Has anyone else been in this situation in the past whilst setting their own company up and if so how did you go about it? Matt
It is normal to sbe able how anything you have done in a portfolio provided you place your role in the work in context. So yeah; it sounds like no problem to me.

2006-06-26 21:39:51 • pattyfab
I work for both my own clients and as a subcontractor for advertising agencies. If I have a meeting with a potential client I show in addition of work for my own clients, some of the subcontracted work (unless forbidden, like with medical companies), and always state both my client and their client and what my part in the project was. I also try to avoid any conflict with my agency clients, i.e. I won't steal their customers. If you were to look for a new job, you would show work you've done whilst working for your current employer. I don't think setting up your own company is much different a situation.

2006-06-26 22:53:21 • ebensorkin
If the work has been produced and used by the client(s), then it is no longer under lock and key. Meaning it's safe to show publically without ruining any marketing plans or strategies. With that said, you should be okay to use work you've produced even if it's from another company you worked for. So long as you provide credits to the company and the creative/art director, you should probably be safe. Like others have said, oftentimes it's a good idea to consult a lawyer about these things. + Ryan Ford
