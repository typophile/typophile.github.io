Taken from http://diacritics.typo.cz Anunaasika History and examples of use Used in Sanskrit, the anunaasika is a dot above a letter. It is used by the devanāgarī script to represent vowel nasalization. When the letter is romanized, the anunaasika usually becomes a tilde. Tilde History and examples of use Tilde above the character softens n in Basque. In Spanish, the ñ is used as a separate letter roughly corresponding to the "ny" in "canyon". In Portuguese, it denotes nasality in a and o. Tilde above o was introduced into Estonian in 1818 by Otto Wilhem to denote pronunciation somewhere between ö and ō. In Vietnamese, the tilde (or dấu ngã) is used to specify a steep, glottalized, dipping and rising tone.
