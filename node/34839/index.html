Font proposals for newspaper headlines?

June 29, 2007 • jokjus

Hi, I'm redesigning a local newspaper here in Finland (circulation 8k, twice a week, tabloid, established in 19th century) and I'm searching for a headline font. My favorites so far are [[http://www.typography.com/catalog/whitneycondensed/index.html|Whitney condensed]] and [[http://www.fontshop.com/search/?q=amplitude|Amplitude]], although its big ink traps bother me. Properties for headline font are "humane" and "contemporary", but because this is a newspaper with long history and traditional image, it shouldn't be TOO contemporary... you know the story. Condensed version would be nice. If you have any further suggestions, I'd love to hear them. Thanks in advance. (for body text I've thought of Mercury Text, but that's only if I manage to convince paper to give up their Utopia...)
The headline choice is normally best made as an audience-pleasing occupation of the typographic space between the text face and the logo. Though I have no doubt that others may help you, it would be guessing for me to recommend anything without that knowledge, Cheers!

2007-06-29 13:10:51 • kuroneko
Christian Schwartz has actually done a version of Amplitude without the extreme trapping for use in newspaper headlines. You can find examples at his site: http://www.christianschwartz.com/amphead.shtml. The fonts are available through the Font Bureau Studio Library. It sounds like you're looking for a sans serif design. But if you're open to considering a serif face, you might also look at the Whitman Display series, which was originally designed for newspaper commissions. It is currently used by the Minneapolis Star Tribune, the Tacoma News Tribune, and the Idaho Statesman. The family has been recently expanded to 7 weights in 3 widths. I posted a few previews in this other thread on a magazine redesign. The fonts are not available in Retail release just yet (we're working on it). But you can contact the Font Bureau for licensing through their Studio Library. -- K.

2007-06-29 15:57:47 • jokjus
I'll suggest Shaker from Jeremy Tankard. I recently bought it for my newspaper's redesign as a caption/sidebar font. I have to say that I'm not 100% pleased with how it mixes with Farnham and Merucry, the other typefaces (I think the difference in x-height between them irritates me), but I really liked it when I tried it in headlines as a demo. The italic is especially nice, IMO; I used it for a temporary nameplate while our final one was in the works. Since you said no serifs, I'll interpret that as not including slab-serifs and point out a few modern ones: Dispatch (and its sans, Stainless), Soho, and Enclave.
