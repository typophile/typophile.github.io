Not to get all pendantic, but "old style lining figures" is a contradiction in terms. Old style figures are the ones that dip below the baseline; the ones with ascenders and descenders, if you will. There are still lots of Postscript fonts floating around with "OSF" tacked on to the name to indicate that they use that figure style. Lining figures are like the ones in your sample: all figures sit on the baseline, and the characters are a consistent height.
