McSweeney's column about typography just launched

September 25, 2009 • rosacordis

Hi everybody –

Long time lurker here. During the recent McSweeney’s column contest for their website, I pitched them on the idea for a column about typography, and how type design and designers have influenced writing and culture during the times they were working. To my delight, I was an official runner up, which means they’re running the column.

Although the target audience is people who have very little knowledge about type, I definitely wanted the type community to be aware of it, and hope that people will enjoy it.

The column is titled “Letters from the Hellbox,” and the first installment “Gutenberg and How Typography Is Like Music” went live today. You can read it here.

Every month I’ll look at a different period with a focus on historical context, education and designer. I hope you’ll take a look!

Best regards,

Martin McClellan
