The previous design firm chose Times New Roman due to accessibility and availability in word documents, website default, etc. I want to change it, so I’m just wondering what you guys think about the use of a serif VS a san serif font. Charles Leonard just quoted Duke Elligton earlier. “If it looks good, it is good.” You need enough difference between the grotesques and it’s all good.

For serif, almost anything goes. Since it’s a book, I don’t think the geometry of Neuzeit is very good (for body text I mean). But whatever you choose, please no ITC Garamond, Stone or Rotis. I’ve had enough of those in marketing books. ;^)

2007-01-09 16:59:29 • Grot Esqué

Modern/Romantic fonts like Bodoni, Didot, Walbaum, et al, are traditional companions for geometric sans-serifs. Many designers find these to be straining on the eye when used in long texts, however.

Neuzeit S has the small aperture and similar body structure to Realist types like Clarendon, Century, Century Schoolbook, and the like, so these might be possibilities as well.
