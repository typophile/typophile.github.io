Just checked my copy of los años del diseño: La década republicana by Enric Satué, and Franco-era typography seems to follow the trends prevalent in the rest of Europe at the time, meaning Art Deco styles in headlines and Futura (or its equivalents) in body text.

For the Twain, Nick Curtis' Rowan Oak. http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/nicksfonts/rowan-oak-nf/

The text of Canada Type’s specimen book is a story by Stephen Leacock (early 20th century Canadian humorist), which runs throughout, changing font as it goes.
