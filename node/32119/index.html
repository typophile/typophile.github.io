Lintotype’s Monticello, designed by C.H. Griffith in 1946, and revised by Matthew Carter while he was working at Linotype between 1965 and 1981.

http://www.linotype.com/55992/monticello-family.html

2007-03-09 22:31:16 • malbright

So much for that. :-) BTW, other (older) names for this design are: Binny & Ronaldson #1; and Oxford. Monticello is the contemporary revival. This design is used in Updike’s “Printing Types”, and has a unique “g” (the only other place I’ve seen anything similar is in Fountain’s Baskerville revival).
