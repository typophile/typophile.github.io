Hello again:

I checked with the foundry a few days back regarding Hoefler Text and Requiem Text for books. The representative did not know of any instance off hand. However, I did receive and examine the new Hoefler & Frere Jones type catalog.

Isn’t it funny how we form opinions (sometimes damning opinions) about type we see as online samples of repertoires or comprehensive pdf’s we downloaded from foundries, then to have that opinion totally change once we see it in print?

For instance, Verlag- not very pretty to me on screen (perhaps even ugly), looks much better in print. I’ll still take Neutraface tho :-) We see things much differently don’t we?

With regards to Hoefler Text (the swashy italics excepted), I see it as a face that is so neutral that you only focus on the meaning of the words, not their shapes (much in the same fashion as Minion). It also feels “literary” but I wonder: is it “literary” for magazines or “literary” for books? H&FJ catalog is printed on coated stock (kromecote?) - perhaps this has something to do with it.

2007-11-06 10:17:50 • SuperUltraFabulous

I just set a book in Hoefler Text (and Titling). It worked great; the letters look “black” and strong, and the page has a nice, old typographic feel, more “letter press” than digital, in some way. Besides the obviuos resemblance to Linotype Janson Text (Hoefler’s main influence), to my eye Hoefler Text has some kind of similitude to ITC New Baskerville - not the type design, but the way it looks, the way the letters and the ink fit the paper. Very different from Adobe Garamond, to say. What doesn’t seem to work, at least to my eye, is Italic in long reading: there’s a sequence of 4 pages set entirely in Italic and it looks too heavy to me. And an alternate Q cap Italic would be helpful - the default one is elegant but perhaps too baroque to some contemporary reader’s eye (by the way, I disagree with Mike: this font isn’t “neutral” at all; it’s very personal. Times New Roman in a book looks neutral :-)

Anyway, it would be interesting to see how Hoefler Titling, which is a much “finer” typeface, would work in small size. It looks nice even in 13 points, but I’m not sure how it would work in 11, or 8 points.
