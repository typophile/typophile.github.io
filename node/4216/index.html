As one might expect, the design of Gregory Maguire's Wicked: The Life and Times of the Wicked Witch of the West is quite playful and Oz-like.

In the hardcover and trade paperback editions, the text itself is set in Truesdell, but I've had a harder time than I expected identifying the face used for the chapter titling and cover design. Neither Identifont nor WhatTheFont came close, which surprises me, given the distinctive letterforms. Maybe they're too distinctive? FWIW, the "R" and "K" remind me of the New Yorker's iconographic logotype, and the "Y" is HTF Requiem-like.

Cheers,
Brent
