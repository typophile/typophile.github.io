Corisande

May 15, 2012 • inkexit

Kept seeing this font every time I go to the bank (M&T Bank). It’s so elegant and simplistic, yet everything seems to be shifted just where it needs to be. The extra thick arm of the lowercase r. The lower right hand quadrant of the lowercase a. Produced by Indentikal, out of London. I was surprised when I checked out the rest of their fonts that they seem to be overall a novelty font maker. I think they are really on to something here, so clean, smooth and modern, without really calling too much attention to itself. I am very curious to find out how everyone else here sees it.

[img:sites/default/files/old-images/corsiande_6072.png]

Actually, this stuff was more than a decade ago, and probably reached its saturation point in the Adobe Clean lowercase a, three years ago. We’ve had the whole neo-grotesk thing since then.

What I find notable about Corisande is that its most distinctive feature, the Didone feature of the lowercase a, is completely out of keeping with the structure of every other feature of the design. It’s a typeface entirely built around sharp connections of stems and bowls, except for this one instance in which the bowl flows out of the vertical.
