October 3, 2009 • Smitchell

I’m trying to write a script that checks the compatibility of glyphs before generating a MM font.

I wrote this which works for single glyphs

print lightFont[“A”].isCompatible(boldFont[“A”], True)

I really wanted to do it with a loop, and loop through the whole font.

I tired this, but it gives me a list saying all the glyphs are compatible but I know they are not.

for glyph in lightFont: print lightFont[“glyph”].isCompatible(boldFont[“glyph”], True)

print lightFont[“B”].isCompatible(boldFont[“B”], True)

========================

I changed the outline of the “B” and tested the compatibility after the loop and it tells me they are not compatible but the loops tells me they are

How can I loop through both fonts and test there compatibility ?

I would suggest to loop through the glyph names instead of the glyph objects: from robofab.world import OpenFont lightFont = OpenFont() boldFont = OpenFont()

print lightFont.info.fullName, “/”, boldFont.info.fullName

for glyphName in lightFont.keys():

lightGlyph = lightFont[‘glyphName’] boldGlyph = boldFont[‘glyphName’] print lightGlyph.isCompatible(boldGlyph, True)
