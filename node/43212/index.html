I spent around ten minutes in Photoshop transforming this stock photo (found via Google Image Search) into this.

Is that sort of what you had in mind? Here’s a quick summary of how I did it:

I converted the image to CYMK, then copied each CMYK channel into a separate new grayscale document.

For each of the four new documents, I added two 50%-gray layers and set them both to Overlay mode. Then I used Add Noise on each of the 50%-gray layers followed by Gaussian Blur. I used a different amount of Gaussian Blur on each of the two layers: a 1.5-pixel radius for the first one and 0.75 pixel for the second. The idea was to get two levels of grain: one coarse, and one finer.

Then I flattened all four of the CYMK gray documents and copied each one back into the appropriate channel in the original document.

I converted back the image to RGB, then added a Hue/Saturation layer to bring down the saturation. Finally, I added a Curves layer to boost the center of the red channel and depress the center of the blue channel.

Not knowing the source of the image makes it largely impossible to give one formula. You may also find one formula will give a certain result on a specific image and not another.
