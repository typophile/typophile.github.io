Galaxie Copernicus is a take on Plantin, a 16th-century genre, and Georgia is a Scotch Roman, a 19th-century genre. Plantin does have a large x-height compared to other designs of its era, but if you go into the details, there is very little similarity between the two genres and hence the two faces.

However, Georgia, as you noted, was designed for the screen, and Galaxie Copernicus was designed as a member of the Galaxie family that includes a grotesque (a sans serif subgenre), so those decisions probably affected the proportions, colour, and sturdiness of the designs. That could be said about a lot of other contemporary designs, though.

To answer your question in the title: Not much. They’re both serif typefaces. But Georgia is a Scotch Roman optimized for the screen, whereas Copernicus is a typeface stylisticly similar to Plantin, a French Old Style face.
