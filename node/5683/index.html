Another explanation is that sometimes when there is a very popular typeface, as this was for many decades, other foundries develop alternative versions with new names, and one or two slight differences to avoid the appearance of copying the type. I think that's a fair question. What we don't appreciate is 'can you tell me where I can get something like it for free?'

My suggestion is to go over to The Font Pool and pick the style category you are interested in. Then type in a word or two that you want to use. They will show you hundreds, if not thousands of fonts using those words (or letters), and you can pick one that way.

The reason I suggest that, instead of suggesting a font, is that 'clean and strong', 'interest', 'finish' and 'contemporary' are all very subjective terms. You know what you mean by them.

You can also do something similar at MyFonts.com if you register. You can set up your own sample phrase for the font showings.

Now, having said all that, I will say that what seems 'contemporary' these days is geometric fonts, because we are in a retro stage where Art Deco and Bauhaus looks are apparently 'in' again. Hoefler & Frere-Jones Gotham is used a lot, and Futura is timeless and has lots of weights. I'd start with one of those.
