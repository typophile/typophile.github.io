Raph, thanks for posting that link. The essay was quite interesting.

I note that Baja Fresh, a California-style Mexican restaurant chain, uses Neuland extensively on their website and in their signage. I don't think there is an African connection here, but the "stereotyping" described in that essay might be operating. If we replace African with "aboriginal culture" this might link to "Aztec" and thence link to "Mexico". It's a bit of a stretch, I admit. However, it might explain why the graphic design seems to work, even though it's quite jarring when one considers Neuland's history. I suspect most of Baja Fresh's customers are unaware of this history.
