The Crichton sans serif is a condensed grotesk of some kind, and there were hundreds of condensed sans faces in the old film type collections (in the Solotype Catalog with G-names). The closest current digital font I could find that matched the shapes of the G, R, M, C and (almost) the S was [[http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/preusstype/neue-steinschrift/|Neue Steinschrift]] from preussTYPE. It's a little heavier, but the letterforms are very close. - Mike Yanega
