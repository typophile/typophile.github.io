Printed portfolio tips for a recent grad?

February 24, 2011 • evilfansanfran

I have a nice .pdf I send around. Any tips for a nice way to print it, thats cost effective and doesn’t use sleeves?

I have 10 pages. Thanks in advance.

Get a decent inkjet printer and use a simple stitched binding. Make sure to not get an Epson printer if you need to do a lot of these as the paper feeds on Epson printers are finicky and not suitable for a lot of pages.

This is probably out of your price range (since you said “cost effective”) but I’m starting to see folks put their portfolios on iPads.

For printed pieces (a brochure or poster, for example) I’d rather have the actual printed piece to examine, but for showing electronic media (web pages, Powerpoints, PDFs, etc) an iPad works pretty well.

I did a horizontal 8.5x14 metal screwpost binder. It was about $60-70 I want to say but you can find similar stuff online if you’re looking for cheaper versions. It’s great if you need to switch/add stuff. Plus, it looks and feels cool.

Always a plus to take in actual printed samples if you have them for the interviewer to take a look at, especially if it’s something more involved than say, a poster.

Best of luck!
