2007-01-28 04:51:04 • Stephen Coles
If Bodoni is your display type, and you are looking for a legible body type, consider a Neoclassical type like Baskerville, or a Scotch Roman like Georgia or Bell. These would also be very scholarly choices in terms of their appearance, and they are very readable as well. Bulmer is another good choice that sort of straddles the boundary between the Neoclassical types like Baskerville and the Romantic types like Bodoni. I would steer clear of anything humanist (like Hoefler Text, any Garamond or Adobe Caslon) as they might clash with the high contrast and geometry of Bodoni. If you want to use a Bodoni for the body copy, ITC Bodoni or Berthold Bodoni Old Face would be good choices. David Thometz
