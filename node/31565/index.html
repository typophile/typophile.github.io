Jon, I studied for two years in Germany. German higher education is a different sort of animal. While there are a few graduate programs that are offered in English, German languages is still more or less something that cannot be gotten around. The entire time I was a student here, I kept hearing great things about Dutch schools, and many of my classmates were waiting for the first opportunity to run for the border. So there could be something to these recommendations. Studying in the UK as an American is, at least in comparison with the rest of Europe, insanely expensive. Nevertheless, the total cost per year is still a little less than going to a private University in the US (even with the current Pound–Dollar rate), if that helps. And you can use the same sort of loans.
