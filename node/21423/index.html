hello fellow typophiles

once again im asking for advice. for a one-day music festival, organized by young people and with the goal to bring nature conservancy to a broader (and younger) audience, i designed flyer and poster in one big rush. as you’d expect with such unexperienced organisators, everything was last minute. i used AG Super in pretty big sizes throughout flyer and poster. now i have to design a 28-page festival-magazine, printed black and white on “nature-friendly” paper - dont know which one exactly (its probably going to have a bit of a yellow touch).

i thought about tyfa by stormtype as typeface for text, but its very lifely and doesnt really fit what i imagined. any good idea? i dont want to go too conservative, for example i dont think minion would fit the audience. the page-size is pretty small to save money, so i’ll have to go small with type, too (would be 8/10 or something probably).

THANK YOU!

ps: i’d love to use akkurat for text and make a sans-only out of it, but alas i dont have the money to buy akkurat right now and i want to buy the whole family of it.

Try New Winchester. It screams “nature conservancy” to me, partly because of its softened, experimental nature.

And, perhaps more importantly, it will also set very well on your kind of paper.

2006-07-26 03:45:29 • Bobby Henderson

AG Super is my personal favorite default font, so I have been using it an awful lot for a few years now. I generally set it in letterspaced caps. This not only looks good quite large, but also at a text size as a contrast to the AG Light, which has all the typographic trimmings.

In the instances where it needs a different support type, I tend to choose the exact opposite of it in the form a script (like Edwardian or other formal script) or a swash italic (like Adobe Caslon). Using a normal Mom-and-Pop roman type generally produces a cold, stodgy look, so I go for the more exuberant, though equally rigid, old-school script or italic. Rounds it out, like Laurel and Hardy.

I hope I didn’t just give away too many secrets.
