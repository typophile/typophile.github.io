I am working on a mixed-type logotype and I was hoping for some feedback.

So, I was hoping for some comments on the spacing and the typefaces combinations (and perhaps other, more contemporary suggestions)? Which of the two versions would you go for?

Chiaroscuro properly refers to the innovation in painting whereby forms were represented as being primarily defined by light. Historically, artistically and aesthetically the term is inextricably wed to the Italian Renaissance. I would think that your serif font selection would be an opportunity to make that connection.
2008-01-16 17:58:35 • The Don Killuminati

I don’t think it is really Italian Renaissance. Early painters who experimented with chiaroscuro were mostly Mannerists and Caravaggio — who has widely considered as the one who mastered chiaroscuro into perfection — was an early Baroque painter.

While historically not quite perfect, the second logo (Bodoni and Nobel) in my opinion is a great example of how I would understand chiaroscuro in typographic terms — better than the first, because of the interesting two-way contrast: the weight increases while stroke modulation and design complexity decreases. That is more sophisticated that the first approach which really just says “little” vs “much” of everything.
