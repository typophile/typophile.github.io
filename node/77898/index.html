Suggestion of a Ligature to use in a Logo

January 10, 2011 • dvrader

I have a web design project for a new publishing company. One of the to do items is design a logo around the letters “ff”. Since it is a publishing company, I thought a ligature would be perfect.

Problem is I’m not super familiar with type. I’ve already spent way too much time searching through fonts.

Seeing as you are all experts, I thought you might be able to make suggestions and help narrow my search.

Thanks.

Most decent typefaces are going to have an ff ligature for you to work off of (or you can make a custom one easily enough). What kind of feeling are you going for with the mark? What kind of things does this publishing company do? That could be the difference between a really elegant italic or script ff and a bold sans serif ff.
2011-01-10 20:18:28 • Kazyole

The company publishes self-improvement books and some novels primarily in the online space. They are primarily online. I’ve been given creative license to develop the look and feel, so I wanted to try out a few different styles, but it will probably lean towards modern.

I’ve considered a script font with the crossbar curving into a circle around the ligature, a weathered typewriter font, or a beveled sans serif.

I was really hoping I could get some suggestions of fonts other people like to help me explore more possibilities.
