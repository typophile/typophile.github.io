Logo facelift

February 12, 2007 • carlherup

Hi everyone

I am a 3rd year graphic design student working on a project where I am redesigning some beer labels for a brewery. I want to modernize their labels, and I am in doubt if I should modernize their logo as well. I do not want make any major changes to it, but maybe give it a facelift, or a more modern up to date look.

Does anyone have any suggestions for how this could be done, or what I should be looking for, what makes the logo look outdated and could be changed? Or does the logo look good as it is?

Any suggestions or thoughts?

Thanks

Carl Herup

Experience is a highly valued trait in beer marketing, and a nostalgic looking logo that evokes the deep history of the brewery is highly desirable. Modernism for modernism’s sake is a bad idea for beer, except in rare cases for absolutely new brands and styles. There is, however, plenty of room for improvement in this logo here.

First off, it looks like it says “Restrooms” at first glance. There isn’t much you can do about this without convincing the company to change the name, but at least it evokes another idea associated with beer.

The tail of the R seems rather heavy and inelegant, but it should remain in some way so as to not disturb the brand too much. Gently curving and tapering the understroke might be enough. The rest of the letters might benefit by switching them to a more fluidly connected script font, rather than the italic currently in use.

Whatever you wind up doing, the key elements of the brand appear to be the big green R and diagonal stripe extending from it. You can probably get away with anything else, including reshaping those elements, so long as those things are included.
