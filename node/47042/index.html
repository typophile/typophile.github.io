For starters, I'd suggest using vector-based software (ie Illustrator) to design your logo—or at least to produce it. If you're logo is vector you'll have no issues separating it for film (for silkscreening) or blowing it up to go on a billboard. As for your old logo, I think the childish look has a lot to do with the bubbly letters, and the primary colours. Go ahead and use Gill Condensed and some neutral colours and you're already miles ahead of your old logo. Of course these are very basic suggestions... you should really consider who you're trying to appeal to and go from there. Best of luck! Eric
