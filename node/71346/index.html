here’s another sample of a seven that looks like (and is) an upside down ‘L’.

my theory here is that the guy making the mould for this concrete monument could find the form for the seven so he used an ‘L’, figuring no one would ever notice.

In north America, license plates are stamped to produce raised letters. If they are similarly made in the UK, then perhaps the person setting the number just rotated an ‘L’ die to make a seven.
