How do you ensure you're buying the "right" version of a font?

February 9, 2009 • hworkman

With the help of some knowledgeable typophiles, I recently identified a typeface that I need to purchase for a project. I want to make sure I buy the legitimate, licensed version but the problem is that it was identified under three different names. How do you determine which is the original and not a knockoff? In this particular case, the three contenders are named Shango, Bauer Text Initials, or Schneidler Text Initials. All three look nearly identical; one included alternate characters which makes me lean towards that version. In general, are there any guidelines to follow to make sure you’re buying the original typographer’s design?

Technically speaking, only the metal version of Schneidler’s font is the “original”, so any digital version you buy will be an interpretation of the original design. In most cases the originating foundry creates the most accurate digitization and pay royalties to the source, but that’s not always true. Your task as a consumer is to examine all your options carefully and see which fonts are of the highest quality, don’t cut corners, come from a foundry with a good reputation, etc.

If you link me to your options I can give my take. I know Shàngó, but haven’t heard of Bauer Text and Schneidler Text.
