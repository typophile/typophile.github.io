My problem is that all the poems are VERY different from one another and how I should best lay this out.

Line lengths are as follows (all in verse)

1 x 6 lines 1 x 8 lines 2 x 11 lines 2 x 13 lines 3 x 14 lines 3 x 15 lines 5 x 16 lines 2 x 17 lines 3 x 18 lines 3 x 19 lines 1 x 20 lines 2 x 21 lines 2 x 22 lines 4 x 24 lines 2 x 27 lines 3 x 28 lines 2 x 29 lines

Then there are also a few longer poems:

2 x 36 lines (both prose) 1 x 37 lines (verse) 1 x 46 lines (verse) 1 x 48 lines (verse) 1 x 99 lines (verse)

We are trying to give particular emphasis for each author (of which there are 38 in total), which means in reality they each need to start on a fresh page, rather than overflowing the poetry. I have been supplied with just 7 images.

2011-04-22 17:14:13 • Nick Shinn

I design quite a lot of poetry books, and your layout looks fine to me.

However, the longer poems running into the page numbers is problematic in my eyes. Perhaps you could choose for the more conventional location of the numbers; in the bottom left and right corners? Maybe it won’t look as harmonic, but at least you will have some breathing room for those poems that run a few lines longer than the rest. Another option is placing them in the middle on the side edges of the pages (i.e. in the left margin of the left page, and the right margin of the right page).

I’m not a big fan of setting poem titles in all caps. A lot of poets I design books for use all caps as well, apparently they like it, but I nearly always advise them against it. I do often set them in bold or medium, but usually at the same size as the poems. If you want to differentiate them more, you could try indenting the titles. But I guess using a sans serif can also work.

On a side note, in your pdf v10, hyphens are being used instead of en- or em-dashes.

2011-04-22 17:33:46 • Quincunx
Letting poems run onto a second page looks fine. I like your body type a lot. For titles, how about a bolder version of the style you use for author’s names, instead of all caps? Or if all caps, letterspace a little? Also, the subtitles or alternate titles should probably be in the same face as the actual titles, though the italic text face is very nice. On page 5 of your PDF, you probably want to use dashes — instead of hyphens -. I do think it;s far better to let a poem run onto a second page than let it run so close to the page number. For structured verse, break poems at stanza breaks, if you can.
2011-04-22 21:09:26 • Joshua Langman

Looks like the Sans Bold choice for poem titles isn’t that popular here, Katy. :-)

Here are some alternatives:

  1. If titles must be set in all caps, try to set them in the serif used in the poems as well, as others suggested, perhaps a bit spaced.

  2. Or you could try to set them in all small caps insted of all caps; they should look much less invasive on the page - perhaps increasing a bit the size (say, texts are set in 10, titles in small caps are set in 10,5 or 11, but do some prints of the page and have a look yourself).

  3. Finally, the most obviuos choice: set the titles exactly as the texts, uppercase and lowercase, again trying to increase them a bit (half or a point more than the poems).
