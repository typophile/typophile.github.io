There is a Photo-Lettering typeface called Pacella Decatur Biform 6 that looks exactly like this lettering. It comes in three heights and one italic, but I am not aware of a digital version (certainly not by that name). The catalog that shows this was printed in 1965, and PLINC was active through the 1980’s at least, until digital type replaced it for most uses.

Sorry Danny, the showings in these catalogs are only partial showings. It’s the big drawback they have. In some cases they may also have microscopic showings of the rest of the alphabets in the back of the book, but they are incredibly small. Even with a pretty strong magnifier, you can barely make out the features.

The other place where film types can be shown is in the Phil’s Photo book and in some of the Dover books by Dan X. Solo. I don’t think I have seen this in either of those places. The best I can offer is to scan the specimen they do have in this catalog.
