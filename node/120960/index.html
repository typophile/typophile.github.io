I can comment on classification. This style is usually called uncial, but the lettering is unusual in that it has with a Roman capital _A_ instead of a lower case or unicase _a_. I don't recall a digital match. BTW the bottom terminal on _S_ is very similar to the one used in Arrus BT https://www.myfonts.com/fonts/bitstream/arrus/ which is based on lettering by Richard Lipton, who produced various hand-lettered calligraphic alphabets. UPDATE: Other possible classifications; Irish, Celtic, Book of Kells, Medieval, calligraphic. Possible source: English photolettering supplier? Don

thanks so far… just a few thoughts: the cover is from the mid-sixties. (rec. '64) the high-time of (revival) uncials had long passed by then and i bet against a unique hand-lettering in an era when this stile was at best thought of as "highly eccentric". moreover - i got the distinct feeling that there has to be a full set of letters etc. out there because the whole impression is not as monolithic as it would had the design been created for "MESSIAH" alone. the bottom terminal on the 's' to me looks very art-nouveau. and so does its top companion! regarding the 'a': the erbar mediaeval has a similar alternate cap 'a' - its legs however do not meet in a top-junction. still i think a dutch or english origin likelier.
