Bringhurst's "The Elements of Typographic Style" is an excellent typographic resource. Unfortunately, as you're probably discovering, there is no concrete set of rules to typographic definitions or their usage. People seem to use what makes sense to them. So its best to carefully explain to others what you mean when you use certain terms that could have various meanings or even usages that vary by context.

"Contemporary" is a word often used to indicate a style, but taking it literally it means "at the same time as" (and here, an ending "us" is implied). So it seems odd to me to talk about types designed half a century or more ago as contemporary.

Agree with bemerx25, you'll find some descriptions to be erroneous in some places.
