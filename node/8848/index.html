Hi all,

I am looking to ID the typeface used in the intertitles from Fritz Lang's classic silent film "Metropolis" (1927). These shots are from the DVD of the recently restored edition (I did not capture them, only grabbed them from DVDbeaver.com); I don't know if they are identical to the face used in the original film.

Also, these shots don't highlight certain capitals (such as "T") that show some vestiges of blackletter styling.





As an aside, the restored DVD also uses Thesis (I think, or something like it) in the extra intertitles added to represent missing footage.

Additional images can be seen at <http://www.articles.dvdbeaver.com/film/DVDCompare7/metropolis2.htm>.

BTW, the quality of the restored film is exceptional.

Have you noticed the kerning? Some of the glyphs are (almost) touching each other and some even have a bit of overlap. Doing that with type would be impossible (or at least extremely hard to do).
