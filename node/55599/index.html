Skeletal Ape

March 6, 2009 • kt

Before

After

I decided to chose two separate pages in my sketchbook and combine them into one to make the page more interesting. I chose two entries that had elements that I liked but were too simple on their own. I wanted to do something with my ‘hand-drawn type’ page {skeletal} and my ‘favorite’ page {tape}.

My concept was a page that celebrated imperfections and truly encompassed the ‘hand-drawn’. I added color and took apart and re-pieced together sections to create the final outcome. It is still simple because I don’t like a busy page, but yet it is more interesting than the original.

I was inspired by the work of Stefan Sagmeister and his hand-drawn type and the postmodern era. Last semester, I created a type specimen book for the font Blockhead, and this page reminds me of it. I am always apprehensive in using my hand and this was something that was a stretch for me.
