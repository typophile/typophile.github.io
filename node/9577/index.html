Yes, I have just been hunting since you posted that. It turns out that 'Alison', which appears as freeware all over the web, is a modification of Dianna/Ballantines/Balladeer etc. The font files still have the orginal A and G included. Robert & Nancy Wall of Chameleon Graphics apparently re-drew those two letters and effectively created a new font. Those two letters are often the most distinctive, so that was a clever method to apparently create something new.
