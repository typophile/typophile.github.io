c1940 Italicised Script

August 16, 2014 • ipalindromei

Marked by the forward ascender loop and left axis crossbar of capital “F” and narrow aperture capital “S” (similar to the commercial Studebaker but with a longer shelf ).

Discovered on a postcard for the “Fairyland Service Station”, later “Cornerstone Service Station”, now a Starbuck’s, located caddy-corner to the entrance to Rock City, Lookout Mtn.

There’s no evidence the signage was never a part of the physical structure, to my knowledge, and was likely added by hand to the colorized photo used for the souvenir postcard bearing its name.

![](/images/old-images/postcard mystery font c1940_5442.jpg)

I checked fonts from the era and found none that are particularly close. If the lettering artist had wanted to base the sign – whether physical signage on the building or just lettering on a postcard – on a font, these are three that could have been used for inspiration. Signal, designed by W. Wege for Berthold in 1931 http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/berthold/signal-bq/ Elan, designed by Hans Möhring for Stempel in 1937 http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/berthold/signal-bq/ Kaufmann, designed by M.R. Kaufmann for ATF American Type Founders in 1936 http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/kaufmann/ Kaufmann was everywhere during the 1940s, while Signal and Elan were less common. Don
