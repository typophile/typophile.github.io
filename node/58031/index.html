Wine Identity - crit my Didot Sans

May 15, 2009 • waves

Hello there.

I’m working on a new identity for a boutique winery called Tenor. While the design is still pretty rough (colors, composition), I’m looking for feedback on the Tenor type specifically. Conceptually, I’ve settled on modifying Didot, e.g. modernizing it, which I think retains the classical look of Didot while the contrast of the letterforms references musical notes. The 5 lines are clearly a nod to the staff in sheet music. I know there’s been others who’ve experimented with sans’ing Didot (Bilak, etc). I’m curious to get your expert opinions, the ‘R’ in particular is a bit odd, and I’m thinking of redrawing the tail with a subtle curve rather than so flat.

I included another label which is 1:1, a very high end, low-volume specialty of Tenor. I drew the 1’s to match the Tenor type, this design would be entirely blind embossed.

Any help/feedback is much appreciated, thanks!
