2006-03-22 05:51:31 • Si_Daniels
What everybody in the many discussions around is missing is the fact that one doesn't always need a logo. When you're called Quark, you have an unusual name, short enough, well-known if not exactly well-respected in the business. Who says you need to abbreviate that into a Q? Why not customize that name into something unique? And then design a unique style around that to make the packaging, website et al stand out? We recognize Adobe products from their splash screens, packaging, advertising, because they have a style of their own. The motif invented for CS1 has been developed into a visual language which extended into CS2 and will extend into CS3 and beyond. One doesn't need the Adobe A logo to recognize the brand.
