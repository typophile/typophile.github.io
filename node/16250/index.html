Improving on the classics

November 15, 2005 • minardi

Came across this in an event guide. The “h” and “g” redesigns are personal favorites.

The lowercase characters are just Monotype Corsiva (a.k.a. the Zapf Chancery stand-in) rotated backwards a few degrees. It looks like the “ng” was rotated as a unit. The ampersand is Times New Roman. The two S’s appear to be Garamond, stretched a bit horizontally.

This is not what I would call fine typography. An interesting amateur effort, though. (Sorry for the dismissive tone, but that’s my honest opinion.)
