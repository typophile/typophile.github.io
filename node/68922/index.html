That’s a typographic error and mis-spelling. Fat Faces were a genre of ultra-heavy serifed type designs introduced in the early 19th century, arguably the first of the new commercial (job-printing for businesses) styles that evolved to meet marketing needs, rather than those of book-publishing.

It could be said that the original Egyptian (aka Antique) faces of the early 19th century had “flat” or unbracketed serifs. Also, the centre stem of cap E, for instance, in that genre, had no serif.
