>> does anyone know where i can find information on where Neuzit S was first used? The font is actualy called Neuzeit Grotesk ('New Age Grotesque') and was designed by Wilhelm Pischner for the Stempel foundry in 1928. It's an integral part of the ancestry of the popular DIN Schriften fonts. As for your question, well, Google is your pal: Linotype Font Lounge - http://www.linotype.com/7-540-7/wilhelmpischner.html Klingspor Museum - http://www.klingspor-museum.de/KlingsporKuenstler/Schriftdesigner/Pischner/WPischner.pdf History of the Stempel foundry - http://cgm.cs.mcgill.ca/~luc/stempel/stempelhistory.html
2003-11-16 14:56:54 • refusenik
When you say 'typographers', do you mean type designers? There is a book called 'Rookledge's International Handbook of type Designers - A Biographical Directory' by Ron Eason & Sarah Rookledge. There is another one called 'Twentieth Century Type Designers' by Sebastian Carter. Both would be excellent resources if you want to know about type designers. Of course, these are not 'autobiographical', because they were not written by the designers, but I presumed that maybe your assignment didn't require more than biographical information. Your other course of action is to use Designer links at MyFonts.com, and to use Google to search for biographical information about well-known designers.
