Edited: The De Vinne design is a revival of Elzevir, originally designed in the late 18th century, by Gustav Schroeder’s in 1893 for Central Type Foundry. It is a very bold display version of Elzevir Bold and became the most popular typeface of the 1890s. Central (and then ATF) added many styles to the family. By the early 20th century almost every foundry had versions of Gustav Schroeder’s design. Consider a Ruthenian (Ukrainian) DeVinne by Monotype in 1916 DeVinne went from the height of fashionable font taste before WWI, with many weights and variations, to unspeakably quaint in the Art Deco era. It disappeared from ATF catalogs after 1923. It has never returned to fashion. The 1928 Olympic numbers are a last gasp of a fading style. The name DeVinne is now used for another typeface design. With the exception of the Wooden Type version, revivals are sold under other names.
