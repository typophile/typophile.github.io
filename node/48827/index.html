This is from the IBM (now InfoPrint Solutions Company)AFP Core Interchange Fonts. You have an example of the GT series (Gothic Text). The specific name would be dependent on font size; for example GT10 would be Gothic Text 10 Point.

These fonts were created in the late 1970s by IBM for mainframe based printing. The GT series, particularly GT10, are probably amongst the single most used fonts of the last 30 years because of their widespread use in transactional documents such as billing. To my chagrin, I recognized this typeface immediately. This means that I am getting too old. Even the online copy of IBM’s old “About Face” font documentation is a bad scan, rather than a true electronic copy: http://publibfp.boulder.ibm.com/epubs/pdf/s4435165.pdf

I don’t know where to get a TT, PS, or OT version of this font, although people used to transform them all the time as PCs started taking over from mainframes.
