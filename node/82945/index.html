‘Dead’ is probably the wrong word here… It is my understanding that Sovremennaya Kyrillitsa (‘Modern Cyrillic’), dedicated to the tercentenary of Russian Civil Type, was a one-off event. I was on the jury, and I don’t remember hearing of any plans to turn it into a recurring affair (once every few years or so).

There was a similar competition held in Moscow ten years earlier, Kyrillitsa ’99. In their putting together Modern Cyrillic its organisers seemed to be encouraged and inspired by the success of that contest
