Font sample software

June 22, 2005 • kristin

I apologize if this has been discussed here already. I’m having a hard time thinking of the right words to use in a search. I have a sense that the typographic world must be discussing this somewhere, but I haven’t been able to track down any websites so far.

Here’s the situation: Target Corporation has licenses for around 1,000 fonts. There are about 700 creative types who have the access to this font library. Currently we have a couple of printed specimen books in the corporate library and one Type Specimen Book in PDF format that can be viewed over the network.

Recently, the Corporation is transferring basic resources to the corporate intranet site. Our type team needs to come up with a way to organize and display our type library that will be useful to the creative teams via this intranet. Here’s where I could use your ideas:

  1. Do any of you have experience with setting up web-based type specimen books?

  2. If you were able to view your company’s type library on-line, what kind of options would you like to see?

  3. Do you know of a foundry or font vendor website that does a particularly good job of displaying their type libraries that you can provide links to?

  4. Is there software available to create specimen books for the web?

I’d be happy to listen to your brainstorms or recommendations on people to talk to about this. I’ve got access to an internal tech team that is more knowledgeable than me. I wouldn’t mind giving them a challenge!

As basic as it is, I’ve always thought MyFonts does a good job on sample text. However, Veer has a pretty cool setup with the mouse-over feature for a quick flavor.

It can take a while depending how may fonts you have open. You can also customize by editing the script directly.
