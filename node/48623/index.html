MEISTERBRAND’ was done in the style of German/Austrian Art Nouveau lettering ala Künstler Grotesk, Larisch and (the one that came to mind right away for your purposes) Secession - all revivals from that period.

Interestingly, the very different Teutonia is, nevertheless, from the same period and style.

2008-08-21 00:39:18 • Chris Keegan

DEUTSCHER WEINBRAND doesn’t look quite like any type I can name. The A top serif is not very common, the serifs are generally rather long, and the letters appear wider than most text typefaces. The slightly squared look of the letters, and that W remind me of some of Hermann Zapf’s typefaces, and I think [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/adobe/melior/|Melior]] is closest, but it does not have this A.

Another typeface that it reminded me of is [[http://bowfinprintworks.com/SerifGuide/serifsamples/serif_a/antikva_margaret_full.gif|Antikva Margaret]], but this is only in general feel. The details are not close at all. (Sample from the Serif Font ID Guide)
