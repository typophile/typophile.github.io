The basic sans serif used by all the Asian typefaces on OS X

September 22, 2014 • jamesmillner

I know the font names that these characters appear in. The fonts are called Bangla MN Bold, Gurmukhi MN Bold, Kannada MN Bold, Malayalam MN Bold, Oriya MN Bold, Sinhala MN Bold — you get the idea. All these fonts share the same characters from ASCII 0 to 128 or so. Then they contain different characters depending on the name. I'd like to know what the original font for all these typefaces is. It's not quite Optima, but not quite anything else I know either. What's the nearest typeface to this style?
Even when they appear to be sans serif fonts, the roman glyphs of many of these non-Western typefaces turn out to be common serif faces with those serifs chopped off. I believe we are looking at a version of Times New Roman Bold or Extra Bold—minus its serifs. A sans serif that comes close is Intrinseca: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/aviation/intrinseca/
