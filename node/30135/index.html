2006-12-18 21:54:29 • seventy7

Have any of you ever had a problem with the letterforms being okay (not bitmapped as patty described above) but the order of the keystrokes changed?

In 2001 I did a 256-page large-format book, heavy to text. Thomson Shore was the printer and wanted it as PDF rather than native Quark files. Fine by me. I printed to Postscript and ran Distiller, and sent the job off. Didn’t bother to proofread the PDF because I had just had a complete read done on the latest set of corrected page proofs (those proofs printed from Quark).

When I got the printed books back, to my complete horror, on one page the text changed from – they’d hoped – to – they ‘d hoped. [Not sure how easy it is for you to see this on-screen, but instead of the first word having y-apostrophe-d, it was y-space-singleopenquote-d.]

I went back to check my native Quark file and it was fine.

This experience made me permanently distrustful of sending files off in PDF form without YET another round of proofing. Made me look careless as the typesetter, when in fact I had been scrupulously careful to have a complete professional read done before converting to PDF (in addition to prior proofreading work earlier in the project).

Has anyone else had this experience?
