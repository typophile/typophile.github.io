Bad Type

March 1, 2009 • erin callais

I was walking down the sidewalk at a Mardi Gras parade and this sign stopped me in my tracks. I apologize for the photo quality; it’s from my cell phone. This sign breaks the first four typographic guidelines:

1: “For optimum legibility, choose classical, time-tested typefaces with a proven track record." Comic Sans?!

2: “Be mindful not to use too many different typefaces at any one time." I count four.

3: “Avoid combining typefaces that are too similar in appearance." The “Chicken Sandwich” font is different from the Comic Sans used for “Vege-Burgers,” but it looks pretty similar.

4: “Text set in all capital letters severely retards reading. Use upper- and lower-case letters for optimum readability." There are some lowercase letters, but I think the design would still benefit from less uppers.

Here’s what this sign tells me:

  • There is still someone in America who knows where the apostrophe goes in the possessive of New Orleans, and who knows there's no apostrophe in the plural of Bloody Mary.
  • That same person knows how to use the serial comma.
  • Somewhere in New Orleans, there's a bar with a laundromat in it.
