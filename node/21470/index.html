Hi everybody,

I am quite a newbie with InDesign. Indesign Paragraph Composer is great but I have one problem with it. In the final stage of corrections of a book, I would like to prevent Indesign from composing the paragraph when I am only doing minor changes. I would like to have control over the composition in the final editing, as any automatic breaks, hyphenations etc. have to be checked by the proof-reader again. Especially in very long paragraphs even changing only one Letter from lower case to Capital can result in major recompositioning of the whole paragraph. This is beginning to anny me!

How do you handle this? Is there a way to switch to Single-Line Composer while keeping the composition created so far by the paragraph-Composer???

Any help of experienced Indesign-Users welcome

thanks, Axel

2006-07-26 15:31:34 • Thomas Phinney

Isolate the line you wish to edit by making it a separate paragraph. Do this by inserting a soft paragraph return, “shift-return”, at the end of the line, and at the end of the previous line. You don’t need to switch paragraph-composer mode for the line you’re working on.

A permutation of this technique would be to use regular paragraph returns, and change the justification style on the previous line, and the line you wish to edit, to “justify all lines”.

However, be aware that if the text is subsequently re-purposed, or significantly edited, the inserted paragraph returns will be potentially dangerous.
