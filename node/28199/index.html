2006-09-13 19:24:36 • Bert Vanderveen

In Quark you can add kerning pairs. You can also change the AFM file so you can have properly kerned font. Or in FontStudio open it up and use the the kerning part of the program (my favorite way). Try the kerning module for free from DTL, use Fontographer if you need to or FontLab. There are literally dozens of ways to custom kern your font. Before postscript, we used to output repro and kern whole fonts of the big 20 or so fonts so we would not have to kern twice, we got it close for 10 pt for text and 24 pt for headline fonts. Adobe was at Linotypes mercy in recieving the original PS fonts and they got what Typographers got, 200 or so kerning pairs, most not too nice. The customers did not care, they got freed of me billing them a couple of grand a month for galleys of type and type modifications. If you want I can send you kerning pairs from an AFM of a competiting font (URW Giltus) that has almost 1000 kerining pairs. You would have to adjust them somewhat, but I think they would do pretty good for you. I agree, many classic fonts are in need of better kerning. The best thing, though is you can do it yourself for a bulk of a font pretty quickly. The biggest issue becomes “OK I KERNED it, now all my previous work is going to be too tight because it will essentialy be kerned twice” Strike a good balance between perfect for one size and crap at other sizes. I guess that is the problem founderies have too, what looks good for my application, may be inappropriate for some one or everyone else. Gosh fonts are a mess.

Nice thing is that some designers are make better use of kerning, but some don’t. I think you could probably have a cottage industry just custom kerning for clients.
