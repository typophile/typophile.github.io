Comprehensive Historical Type Directory?

November 30, 2005 • Fisheye

I’ve been looking for a good directory of typefaces, categorized by Type Historical (or at least Art Historical) period, e.g. (Baroque, Neoclassical, etc). There are so many directories, guides and books, but all of them seem to be categorized by style (Blackletter, Serif, etc), not period. I have seen small historical guides, such as the four-pager in Bringhurst’s EOT, but none as comprehensive as I’d like. Has an authoritative historical directory managed to elude me?

Even better, perhaps there’s a list of all the authoritative type manuals and guides? I checked the Typowiki, but the book listing definitely leaves much to be desired at this point.

An Atlas of Typefaces by James Sutton and Alan Bartram is good if you can find it. It is a survey of current (as of the 1980s) typefaces presented historically, with historical examples.

Art of the Printed Book is pretty good, but is not about typefaces per se.
