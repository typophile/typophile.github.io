Page 303 of Bringhurst's Elements of Typographic Style calls {these} braces. "Braces are rarely required in text work, but they can function perfectly well as an extra and outer (or inner) set of parenthesis: { ( [ - ] ) }. In mathematics they are used to mark phrases and sets. In editing classical papyri, braces are often used to mark editorial deletings."
2009-12-02 04:47:33 • evanmacdonald
I use "square brackets" and "curly braces", to reduce potential confusion. In terms of usage in running text, I use the former to indicate something that can be included in the text or not; and the latter to mention something "meta" that's relevant beyond the textual content. And parentheses I use to mean something that is an aside to the main text (sort of a more casual or immediate version of asterisked or footnoted content). hhp
