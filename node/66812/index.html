I'm inclined to agree, although this sort of look is common enough in scrapbooking fonts. I found one free and two commercial fonts that might be close enough to use as substitutes, but not if you HAVE to match your sample. The freebie is Pizzadude's [[http://www.dafont.com/loveletters.font?nb_ppp=50&text=Isabella|Love Letters]]. The commercial fonts are [[http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/bagraphics/sugar-shack/?testdrive=dock%3Dfalse%26size%3D37%26w%3D720%26src%3Dcustom%26text%3DIsabella%26fg%3D000000%26bg%3Dffffff%26goodies%3Dfit%26seed%3D30|Sugar Shack]] by Bob Alonso,and [[http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/paratype/amore/?testdrive=dock%3Dfalse%26size%3D37%26w%3D720%26src%3Dcustom%26text%3DIsabella%26fg%3D000000%26bg%3Dffffff%26goodies%3Dfit%26seed%3D22|Amore]] from ParaType. If you study the Love Letters or Sugar Shack fonts, and imagine a few changes, like adding serifs, you can see how perhaps this font of yours might have been developed as a custom font 'inspired' by one of these others. - Mike Yanega
