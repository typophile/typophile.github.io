Gothic Vs Blackletter

February 18, 2008 • MishkaPeel

I've just started a typography project on Gothic (Blackletter) typefaces, and have noticed that some classifications of sans serif typefaces are also termed Gothic. Please can someone help in clarifying why these obviously different typefaces have the same terminology? Thanks
American san serifs are called Gothic, whereas European sans are called Grotesque. Gothic, when referring to blackletter, is kind of slang, for non-type people, probably referring to "Gothic art, a Medieval art movement." I pulled that from wikipedia: http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gothic There are more specifics, but that is the basic idea. I hope that helps. Also, check out http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blackletter for other ideas. I know wikipedia is not a scholarly resource, but it will give you some background.

In French, Blackletter types or scripts are indeed called "gothiques", referring to their medieval origins — it is no coincidence, then, that they were revived by Romantic artists, printers and typographers, who where equally fond of the Middle Ages, as opposed to the classicist bent of Enlightenment art (neo-classical) art and typography (Didot, Bodoni et al).
