2003-06-29 05:31:21 • hrant
To me, single-storey a's and g's in 'traditional' seriffed type look a bit strange, especially for revivals like Bembo. They are just not compatible with these early designs. The double-storey a and g are more of a humanist roman thing, where things were written slowly and carefully; the single-storey ones, on the other hand, came from the italic hand which were written a bit faster, without lifting the pen so much. I guess that's what distinguishes written and printed forms. I wonder if showing traditional seriffed faces like Bembo to kids with single-storey (schoolbook) forms might confuse them? I don't know...
