Designing grids—Inside out or outside in?

February 23, 2007 • blank

When designing grids, do you prefer to design a grid suited to the type and then add margins to create a page, or do you create a grid to fit a chosen page size?
For me, it makes more sense to work the grid with the final size.
2007-02-23 22:57:15 • Miss Tiffany
I agree - I create the grid based on the trim size and the various elements of the design and then style the type to suit the grid. I can’t really imagine why you’d do it the other way round.
2007-02-23 23:18:06 • pattyfab

its probably because im computer kid and learned designing on a computer, but i normaly just fiddle around till it fits my needs. i normaly know how complicated the grid has to be (depending on if its a simple flyer, a 80 page magazine or a white paper with survey results).

margins on the other hand are fitted to the needs of paper size, type of binding, type of publication. i just made a student project with 5mm margin on all sides, but thats because it had to be like that conceptual-wise.

the grid itself was really simple, 9x12 lines to get nearly square grid blocks. the paper size was 20x25cm, also because it fitted the needs (both production- and conceptual-wise).

im not sure how much this helps to answer your question, but i dont think there’s a golden way how to do it - sometimes i know exactly what i want and just try to find a grid fitting my needs, and sometimes i experiment until i find margins/a grid that fit the project.
