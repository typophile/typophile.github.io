Going to the Quarter...

January 20, 2009 • riko_shea

…Gonna take me a lot of pictures…

:)

[img:sites/default/files/old-images/Poboy_Truck_4468.jpg]

This is a truck that allegedly stalks my afternoon escort, Corey. According to Corey, it’s everywhere. I can think of far more grim followers… but we enjoyed reversing roles and sniping a picture from the roof of the parking garage we parked on.

[img:sites/default/files/old-images/Castillo_Restaurant_5166.jpg]

I’m mildly in love with the idea of using a building as a billboard. Sure, the drones inside don’t get the luxury of windows, but the outdoor drones get to revel in their edificial endorsement.

[img:sites/default/files/old-images/Sarlin_6279.jpg]

This reminds me of Sardi’s in New York. I suppose my obtrusive height brings me to empathize with the obnoxiously oversized (yet elegant and swan-like) S. It gives me an example to follow. I should idolize this letter. Sure, I might feel like Dorothy in Munchkinland sometimes… but lightning only finds the tallest trees worth getting acquainted with. That might not be the best analogy.

[img:sites/default/files/old-images/Everything_Shoppe_4299.jpg]

[img:sites/default/files/old-images/Walgreen_5644.jpg]

Two very different kinds of Everything Shoppes, and I’m absolutely wallowing in the difference in the signs. Walgreen’s looks to me perfectly 1950s, and I’m not sure why. The Everything Shoppe is just straightforwardly what it is, in the self-accepting, Peter Behrens let’s-not-lie-to-ourselves-you’re-not-interested-in-a-contrived-purpose-so-let’s-wallow-in-what-I’m-here-for sort of way. A pleasant way. I like it.

[img:sites/default/files/old-images/Papp_6720.jpg]

I really didn’t mean to get the sketchy black van in the shot, but Papp’s seems conducive to shady things. I love that the actual sign is gone, and just the weathered components are left behind. Ahhh.

[img:sites/default/files/old-images/Krystal_3483.jpg]

Can you say quintessential diner? Corey and I shared a shake. While it was listed on the menu as “MilkQuake,” I found that too embarrassing to say publicly and was altogether relieved when they knew what I was talking about when I said “A vanilla shake please.”

I have a few more left… but I’ll upload them shortly. Woohoo!
