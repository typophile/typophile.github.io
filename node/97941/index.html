http://www.oakknoll.com/detail.php?d_booknr=60132 Very interesting book, but the quality of reproductions is poor. Nineteenth Century Ornamented Types and Title pages (Faber & Faber 1938, 1951, 1976), By Nicolette Gray: http://www.ebay.com/itm/19th-Nineteenth-Century-Ornamented-Types-Nicolette-Gray-Victorian-typefaces-font-/110972571664?pt=Antiquarian_Books_UK&hash=item19d67b1010 Sure it’s expensive, but one thing you’re paying for is the fact that the reproductions are made from line blocks, size as. Google book: http://www.galleyrack.com/images/artifice/letters/press/noncomptype/typography/fry/fry-1816-google-bodleian--specimen-of-printing-types.pdf This really demonstrates the problem with reproductions, there is nowhere near enough resolution, never mind the ﬂatness of the image. I think you would be better to go to a rare-book library, they are likely to have a type specimen section.
