Well a TV/radio tuner card is the easiest way, but mine seems to be incapable of picking up Radio 4 at the moment. If all else fails I could record the program as an mpeg from the TV, then convert to a sound file.
Another way of recording:
Most decent sound cards, and even some crap ones, have a switch in the software control panel to set recording source between line-in, microphone, or 'what I hear'.
Free software such as wavelab lite will then record 'what you hear' to a wav file. Converting from wav to mp3 or CD audio shouldn't be a problem.

There are other ways, but I think these would be the most straightforward.

2004-12-02 18:50:16 • Richard Hards
Blag originated in cockney slang, where it meant to steal something. More recently it has been used in a general sense of 'nabbing something' whether legally or not. So one might say that one 'blagged the best seats in the cinema', for instance. It is also used to refer to talking someone into giving you something, e.g. 'I blagged my way into the nightclub'.

By the way, I've found the best way to blag BBC radio on Windows is Net Transport which will save streaming audio and video in native formats. Then you can convert to MP3 with something like the Boilsoft converter.
