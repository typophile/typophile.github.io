2003-12-09 18:22:01 • beejay
I can't say what Gill was thinking, but I can point out some design issues for which that might seem like a reasonable solution.

When a font weight gets that heavy, it is helpful to increase the x-height, allowing heavier horizontal strokes in letters with several horizontals such as a and e.

However, once you get a really large x-height, if the i dot is to maintain an appropriate relative weight, it ends up above the descender height! Something has to give. One solution might be to make the linear part of the i much shorter than the x-height. Having tried this, I think at the extremes (Gill Ultra/Kayo), it looks as goofy as the miniaturized dot, because the main part of the i ends up so far below the x-height. So it becomes a question of which aspect of the design has to "give."

My $.02, anyway.
