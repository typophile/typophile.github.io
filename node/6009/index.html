2004-08-10 16:54:02 • pablohoney77
This is actually a semi-serif... Good for you - we need those!

It reminds me a little bit of Costa (my fav Porchez font, finally released for retail sale - yay!) but it's certainly original nonetheless. I'm actually liking it a lot - but it needs work. The even tone is nice - it's an honest, but sensitive design. And the stroke terminals are great.

Lowercase:
- The "a" is obnoxious, sorry. It has the worst of Gill Sans, and that Jannon imbalance to boot.
- I don't think there's a good reason to give the "f" a descender.
- The "g" is nice - maybe give the head more size versus the tail - the glyph looks way too small compared to the rest.
- I think the "k" needs some of the bowing.
- I'm unsure about the "w".
- The "x" needs some of the bowing too, and maybe the "z" as well.

The caps are really nice.
- The "L" and "T" seem too wide.
- Consider bowing in the "K", "X", "Y" and "Z".
- The "S" needs more balance and polish.

Others:
- Interesting numerals. The "3" is too much though.
- I like the 3rd ampersand. A lot.
- Superb "at" sign.
- Flatten the UC accents.
- The acute and grave seem a hair too strong.

Spacing: it needs work, but with some tightening I think the wordspace you have is good here.

--

One thing about the pairing with the serif version though - I don't see them as compatible designs at all - certainly not sister types.
