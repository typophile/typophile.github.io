Hi all, I'm working on my own identity and decided to challenge myself with my first script signature. I initially drew this and then scanned and traced it. It directly translates to "In the wind" which is something I wanted to show in the typography. I have a few things in mind that I think need to be fixed: -I dont think the weight is consitent, I am wondering where I should thicken or thin, I think I should aim for thicker since it will be used as a logo. I think my first letters work better. -I'm not sure about the thickness of the l to the right, is my t too thin? etc, etc. I'd appreciate any input you can give me. I attached both .jpg and .swf files. Thanks for your time, Emilie Dans le vent signaturedanslevent1.swf (2.6 k)
i'll let others pipe in with a more technical critique, but i just have to say that i love the feeling of "spontenaiety." I think it goes very well with your meaning of "in the wind." I think you have a fairly good feel for your thicks and thins, just take a look at your letters and try to make sure that they have a (somewhat) consitent axis concerning your thicks and thins. Also it seems to me that the "Dans le" part is plenty heavy while the "vent" part is a bit lighter. However, I think i like the "vent" better than what precedes it. Anyhow, enough of my rambling… G'luck with it!
