This is an exercise using "inverted contrast" or "reverse contrast" that is found in a few fonts, but in less extreme form. Take a look at Signo [img:sites/default/files/old-images/SignoThin_OD_3536.gif]http://ilovetypography.com/2014/02/14/making-signo-fonts/ and http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/r-type/signo/ The best known reverse contrast font is Antique Olive [img:sites/default/files/old-images/AntiqueOliveLight_OD_4038.gif] http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/antique-olive/ It's a 19th century idea, used for many of the fonts we now call "Western" such as Adrian Frutiger's Westside Don[img:sites/default/files/old-images/Westside_OD_4083.gif] http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/westside/ Began with Caslon's Italian. More http://www.myfonts.com/users/r1eojck4vj/albums/558612/ and http://www.myfonts.com/search/tag%3A%22reverse+contrast%22/fonts/ A few more: ITC Zipper http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/itc/zipper/ Stretto http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/canadatype/stretto/ Don
