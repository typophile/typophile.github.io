The Ionic Fonts Opticon and Paragon

February 19, 2009 • Uli

I uploaded two documents about Ionic fonts, i.e. about "Ionic No. 5" and all the other similar fonts such as Aurora, Corona, Opticon, Paragon, Imperial, Textype, Excelsior, Clarion, Nimrod etc. etc. see http://www.sanskritweb.net/fontdocs/ionic2.pdf see http://www.sanskritweb.net/fontdocs/ionic.pdf I wrote that Opticon and Paragon have not yet been digitized. Is this correct? I know that nowadays the names of "Opticon" and "Paragon" are used for two completely different fonts: see http://www.dafont.com/opticon-one.font see http://www.fontcenter.de/P-PARAGONN.TTF-Paragon.html Therefore, it could be possible that the original Ionic fonts "Opticon" and "Paragon" were digitized under new names. I temporarily uploaded a scan from an old catalog showing these fonts: http://www.sanskritweb.net/temporary/opticon-paragon.jpg Any clues about digital versions of Opticon and Paragon?
