Orientalizing fonts

May 31, 2011 • jhupdes

I work at a university press. An author sent us her design (unsolicited) for her book cover. While this is annoying, we generally try to be somewhat accommodating if at all possible. We used her image, a painting of a Western woman in Asian dress, but changed her font to Broadband with brighter colors. She objected saying the font was “Orientalizing”. Do you think Broadband is orientalized and therefore offensive?
Broadband is strongly informed, to put it politely, by Neville Brody’s Insignia, which, if it has cultural allusions, would be neo-constructivist. That is its provenance; however, any style may be perceived as something other than its origins—look at what happened to Koch’s Neuland.
