Just a suggestion for the Type ID board...

March 25, 2008 • AndrewSipe

I was thinking, after noticing the 500th post titled "WHAT IS THIS?!" with a link to imageshack, that there must be a better way to do type ids. Of all the forums, I think the Type ID board gets the most workout during the day. I've seen an influx of fresh faces all asking the same question... "This is awesome, what is it? By the way I need to know immediately." I'm not trying to dispel the bad habits of people that didn't know other wise. I just think the forum could use a little more organization. Could save a few members some headaches. Perhaps the "open" approach towards the Type ID forum should be corralled. A form with instructions could be a more benefiting approach. 1. Title - please be specific, refrain from using "What is this font" or any variations. (think of poor Yves) 2. Image upload link - please don't post links to outside websites or image hosting, that may be blocked on some member's computers. 3. comments/information - What do you know about this font, where did you find it, is there specific thing you'd really like to know. 4. tags This is just a suggestion of course. I think it would help clear up some confusion, help speed up type IDs and help people search for IDs already made. I know that Typophile is a labor of love for some of the Punchcut crew and other Typophile members. I don't expect these changes to be made. Though it would be cool to see my suggestions implemented.
Andrew, many of your suggestions are addressed quite clearly in the FAQ (especially about giving your threads more specific titles) and in "How to post a sample or image". It seems lots of people can't be bothered to read the FAQ, even tho it says READ THIS FIRST. There was also a recent epidemic of people re-posting threads when they weren't ID'd quickly enough - one guy hadn't even attached a visual! I mean, we are not psychic - and I got reamed (by Furioustype for one, LOL) for chastising them.
