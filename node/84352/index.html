2011-08-05 23:41:11 • rob3rto

I’d suggest that you reconsider using “extremist” and the grenade. Though memorable they have a lot of negative connotations.

To a lesser degree, “freelance” can also have some negatives, for two reasons – 1) It’s vague. Are you a freelance screenwriter? Journalist? Programmer? 2) “Freelance” can conjure up an image of someone working out of their bedroom, which might not matter if you’re just working for other designers, but if you want to develop your own clients in the business world I think it’s better just to call yourself a designer.

2011-08-08 23:57:20 • timaarts

“Your logo should deliver a message. Always keep that in mind”

BUT…don’t read that as a literal rule. A logo can be literal, but more often than not, it makes sense for it to not be a literal representation but more of an emblem. It simply represents your company…not necessarily defines it. The logo represents the brand more so than it creates it.

I agree with many in here. I think it’s time to do some more brainstorming…start back at the name. Come up with some more ideas. See where that takes you.
