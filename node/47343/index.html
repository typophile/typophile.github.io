Help needed for a logo/font

July 12, 2008 • missserendipity

Hi everyone,

I came across this site by pure accident this morning while scouring the net for some inspiration for logo/font design. I am completely new to typography so please bear with me! I manage a new and up coming film director and what I am looking for is some help with a design and some ideas of what fonts we could use for his logo.

We have decided to go for a logo is the style of art deco as it is a style that seems quite synonymous with the film industry. However we are open to other ideas that might suit a logo for this purpose. We are looking for something that encompasses clean and simple, but classic with an edge if that is possible! I have approached a couple of companies and so far the results have not been too good. I have either had designs that are bland and boring- or ones that look like they have been nabbed from an old clip art album. First impressions are important and so we need this to be good- hence why I am out scouring for some ideas so that we get what we want!.

Thank you for reading. I look forward to your ideas!

Neha

“I have approached a couple of companies and so far the results have not been too good. I have either had designs that are bland and boring- or ones that look like they have been nabbed from an old clip art album.”

If you’re soliciting spec work, it’s no surprise that the results have been unsatisfactory. You get what you pay for.

You should find a few local design firms whose portfolios you like. Ask them to bid on your project and for your sake, be more specific in your brief. The description “clean and simple, modern/contemporary style, but classic with an edge” is internally inconsistent and frankly impossible to work with. You shouldn’t necessarily go for the lowest bidder. Finally, work with them and trust them.

I wish you luck.

Yeah Neha, Red is correct. Spec work is frowned on in this industry and considered to be [[http://www.aiga.org/content.cfm/position-spec-work|unethical.]]

A logo is not a font (but sometimes can be with altered letter-spacing etc). So looking for a font for a logo will not make the logo - the designer makes the logo. A similar analogy would be to assume that by buying an excellent 35mm motion camera you can make a great movie…

You get the idea.

A resource like [[www.dandad.org|D&AD]] in the UK should be able to help find a designer.
