Font suggestions for extensive body text

August 11, 2006 • typefreak

Hello typophiles, I am doing a set of books for an Indian artist who practised between 1940's to 1970's, recording the events of the country through its independence, and its people in strong b/w drawings and etchings. The books are heavy on text - the main one infact runs into 40,000 words. I am looking for body text fonts that may have a 'period' feel about them, while being very very readable. While Mrs Eames looks like a good choice, I am debating whether it is readable enough for this amount of text. Baskerville, Garamon and Veljovic are others that I am considering. While on the topic, what body sans serifs would you use for a book with such extensive text. Thanks in advance Typefreak
Janson Text is a nice one, very easy on the eye, and you could of course try good old Caslon.

While the era is Commonwealth, rather than Empire, I suspect that the British Monotype faces such as Imprint, Plantin, Perpetua, Times, and Gill would have been most prominent for text work in India at that time. And of course Baskerville--but you would want to use a digital version that gives a substantial impression. Plantin would be an interesting choice, it's strong.
