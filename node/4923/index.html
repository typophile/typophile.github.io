"What do your companies (of any size) use to standardize your art department and make sure everything gets done properly?"

A combination of personal experience and knowledge, a bit of stuff gleaned from other groups, and a generous dash of logic and a willingness to experiment.

Really, it's more about having a comprehensive view of what you want to do, and then writing it all down. Then, to make a guide, you distill it all to its core elements, with an eye towards the end-users (designers). I know this sounds like a lot of high-level talk, but it's true.

In terms of day-to-day job process, there are many models to choose from. A lot of it depends on whether you have/need a traffic manager (or 'controller') to manage the flow of jobs. Do you want your CD to be in the center (all jobs pass through them and are proofed by them) or do you want them to exercise a more casual autonomy (they monitor jobs at their leisure)?

What you're asking for is pretty big, and also tends to be specific to the people/ jobs/ environment. I can't go into too much detail (I'm at work right now), but I'd first break the project into A) style guide B) process guide C) roles and responsibilities D) Resources and capabilities. This might make it easier to understand and to manage.

I do not run my own company (I work at one and freelance), but the one I'm in did a major process overhaul and a bit of a rebrand recently, so I'm drawing from these experiences.
