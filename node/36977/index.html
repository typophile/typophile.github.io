  1. Problem solving

Type & composition (A person that shows knowledge of typehistory and can show me interesting and new ways of using type in their design will go a long way - Designers who have a strong sense for typography generally seem to also possess a strong sense of general form and composition - I’d rather have a type geek in my office than someone who is really strong with colour and has decent compositional skills)

  • Design and aesthetic prowess—the ability to compose
  • Knowledge of art history and an eye for fine art
  • Practical knowledge of typefaces and their uses
  • Word smithing skills
  • A good apreciation of literature and writing—type and typography are linguistic tools
  • Professionalism in dealing with clients
  • Amiable disposition—be well-adjusted and flexible
