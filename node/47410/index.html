2008-07-15 14:58:56 • aluminum

ya, what Al said. Solve for that good hourly rate that you can actually break down to your client on paper. No one likes to get a number quoted to them and feel like it was just pulled out of thin air. Stick by that hourly rate and chronicle every bit of work (i.e. sketches, computer-aided comps, even just simple ideation.) so you can show them exactly what you did. Having a well put together and organized job profile can go a long way. Sometimes clients don’t appreciate all of the little things that go into creating a solid design, so keep track of them and bill ‘em!

Also consider your prospective client’s profit margin. If they are economically “superior” and/or stand to receive considerable gains from your design work then factor that in too…it’s sink or swim nowadays!
