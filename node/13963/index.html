2005-07-20 08:37:47 • thierry blancpain

Sounds like it’s a custom design and you’re handing over all rights lock, stock and barrel to the game maker. In that case I think you should charge based on the effort you put in, based on your higher hourly rate. I’d pad the price a bit due to inevitable modifications the company will ask you to make.

Given the sad state of font (c) you might make the case to the customer that exclusivity is not really worth paying extra for. You might want to suggest that after a few years rights revert to you.

I suppose you might consider giving them a discount if you’re allowed to publicize your association with the game. This can be tricky - what if they mess with it prior to using it and you don’t want to be associated with the result. Also software makers often reserve the right not to use a font they license, in that case they’d be no publicity.

Hope this helps, if you’re in NYC please say hi.

Cheers, Si
