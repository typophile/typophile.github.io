You can run into postcript problems if you, for example, combined an Illustrator logo, a photo and some type in Indesign and tried to print it. At least that’s what used to happen to me. If you print from PS or Illy it will rip it for you. The problem is there is not a postcript interpreter in the inkjet so if you send it a job that requires it, you will get an error. I haven’t tried this for quite a while, but I remember Quark jobs blowing up on me with an early Epson. Acrobat may well rip the file for you as well, that’s just not something I have tried.

Did you read that link I posted? That addresses how to get to the printer settings. Also how to use any profiles you may have. Epson makes really good profiles for most of their papers, the only one that hasn’t worked well for me so far is Velvet Fine Art paper, I just downloaded a new one and have yet to try it. The Premium Lustre also needed a little tweaking but once you get it the results are outstanding. So are you saying you can print from a layout program (Quark or InDesign) and it looks bad? Or are you printing from Acrobat? The basic thing that is recommended is that you turn off color management in the printer and let the Adobe programs manage the color. I have had really good results using that. It also solved a problem of mine at work where InDesign would change the black plate behind .psd transparencies. I turned off color management in the printer and it looks great now. There was absolutely no problem in the files. Basically things were being double color managed (since everything you send from an Adobe program is color managed no matter how hard you try and turn it off) and that led to problems.
