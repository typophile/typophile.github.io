Relying on the availability of kerning is a tricky business.

The positive side of this is that people who have kerning off generally don't care about superb all-caps spacing anyway! And the "T" with the tight right side will "house" the lc better under it with no kerning. The negative is that you need a bunch of kerns to fix the funny spacing for sensitive users.

In the end I don't think what they did is a good idea. But I agree it's safer to leave it as is, otherwise you'd have to get a handle on their entire kerning scheme to adjust it, or redo the spacing yourself.

Probably the biggest one is where round/round adjacencies (like "oo") are made too tight. This is because straight/rounds and round/straights are more common linguistically, and making the round/rounds look right (without kerning) would make the (more-frequent) straight combos too loose. Kerns should then be used to loosen all the round/rounds.
