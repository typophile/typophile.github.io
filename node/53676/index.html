2009-01-19 12:51:06 • seml

It would be worth checking out [[http://www.allinlondon.co.uk/directory/1277/3340.php|Collinge & Clark]] in Bloomsbury. They have an excellent selection of old specimens and type books. They’re usually closed on weekends, but their schedule is a bit erratic, so you never know.

And it’s a shame you won’t be in town the following weekend, when the [[http://ephemera-society.org.uk|Ephemera Society]] has one of their big fairs. Those are an absolute treasure trove!

2009-01-19 14:50:14 • Kottis

Foyles are good for current publications and often have recently out-of-print titles but won’t have anything especially rare. They are a very big store with well-stocked departments on most subjects, and a very nice neon sign which I think dates from the 1930s.

[[http://www.maggs.com/|Maggs]] are a specialist antiquarian dealer.

Sadly I don’t think Zwemmers have any stores left in London.
