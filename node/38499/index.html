SKATEBOARD BEARING LOGO

October 27, 2007 • DENIM

This is a school project for an introductory type course. These are the rough logos for Quantum skateboard bearings. The logo should reflect the technological breakthroughs in bearing design that my pseudo company is making. The one on the left has some centering problems, but i am still happier with that one. I like the concept of the one on the right, but somethings off. Critiques PLEASE PLEASE PLEASE.

[img:sites/default/files/old-images/logo1-typofile_5107.jpg]

The “um” ligature does not work for me at all.

I like the mark on the left, although something seems off in the centre. Maybe it’s the way the white circle is just “punched out” of the ‘q’s. I’d like to see something that flows a little nicer in the centre of the symbol.

I’d also make the type in the left logo much larger - it looks too small in proportion to the darkness of the mark. Maybe bring the mark down to the left of the type.

Watch your curves in the right-hand logo where the base of the ‘q’ becomes the ‘b’.

2007-10-27 04:23:20 • Ratbaggy

In the left mark you’ve got a q form that looks much more like a 6 or a 9. Also, repeating and rotating a character has been done so often, that it looks like an obvious and uninteresting choice. (Sure, you could talk me into a bearing company being the most logical company where you could pursue that… but here it’s lost its spark.)

In the right mark, you’ve got a letterform with a traditional cut (the cuts where the circlular shape meets the stem of the q) mashed up with a modern cut (a b with a very uniform bowl). That’s why it’s not working, in addition to the other reason mentioned. Despite the problems, I think the one on the right has more potential to be interesting, because of its asymmetry. Just decide if it’s going to lose or hang on to recognizable and traditional letter shapes or whether it’s going to be more modern.

And Ratbaggy, you have the coolest avatar icon I’ve seen in a long time. Nice.
