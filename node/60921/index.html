José Mendoza y Almeida: ITC Mendoza Roman

August 12, 2009 • Sébastien Morlighem

Hi everyone, my name is Sébastien Morlighem. I’m a french teacher & researcher in history of typography & type design. I’m also the curator of the Bibliothèque typographique, a ‘work-in-progress’ book collection mainly dedicated to punchcutters & type designers for Ypsilon éditeur (http://www.ypsilonediteur.com, english website soon), who’ll make its debut in February 2010 with a (french-english) book about José Mendoza y Almeida, co-written with Martin Majoor & introduced by Jan Middendorp. There’ll be essays about the making of Pascal, Photina & ITC Mendoza Roman, as well as shorter pieces about less-known typefaces, all carefully documented & fully illustrated.

Apart of spreading the news (more about it In Mexico during the ATypI), I’m calling the Typophile community for help about ITC Mendoza Roman. I’m searching for any judgements/opinions/papers about this so uncanny face, especially around its time or release (1991). Many thanks in advance!

All the best Sébastien
