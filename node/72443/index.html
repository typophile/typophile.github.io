Hi Ken I get my paper samples directly from the paper manufacturer. Usually printers use their preferred stocks (bulk orders, good deals). But if you are after a certain stock it shouldn’t be a problem to get the printer to order it in. It will most likely increase the cost for the paper, depending on the quantity needed. If the printer can’t order it in, they will usually show you some similar paper stocks which will match your sample as close as possible.

Of course this doesn’t apply for any online printing facilities (vistaprint etc.).

I think best is if you meet with your printer and discuss your project, this will also help with any specs you have to follow, colour profiles etc.

Hope this helps.
