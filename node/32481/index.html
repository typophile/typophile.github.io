Actually, University Roman is neither a Victorian face nor a 70s face. It’s based on a face called Stunt Roman, from the Speedball Textbooks by Ross George, which appeared in several editions from the 1920s on. In the 13th edition (ca 1938, the earliest I have), it looked like this…

By the 20th edition (issued 1960; reprinted 1965 & 1972), a number of the quirks had been smoothed out, and it looked alot more like the face we’ve come to know as University Roman.

2007-03-23 17:50:45 • William Berkson

To get back on track a bit, Carter & Cone’s Monticello is a sort-of-scotch roman & much better rendered than the various PostScript Caledonia’s & Linotype Scotch Roman for setting text.

Princeton commissioned the digital Monticello (non-exclusive). I believe you can buy it from Linotype as well, but in the early days at least, it didn’t have the full character compliment that the fonts from Carter & Cone had. Besides, one should support the small foundries.

If if bothers you that Monticello is derived from a Binney & Ronaldson (i.e., American) typeface, then use one of the horrible PostScript Scotch romans – just remember that B&R were good thieves, so I’d say it is a “name thing.”
