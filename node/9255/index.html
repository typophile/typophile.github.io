It's good to see you've done some preliminary reading! The area of revivals is a very tricky field to negotiate, in the legal, ethical and even cultural realms. Some people Just Say No, and they have a point.

--

I for one know virtually nothing about any Baltic culture, so I can't tell if Palemonas has any merit in this respect. But I think that even if I did, it's hard to imagine it having any, since this is indeed close to Bembo (more below) and I've never heard anybody say Bembo had any Batlic -much less specifically Lithuanian- affinity! And anyway, it's very hard to imagine a functional text face having such a particular cultural angle. Some Eastern European fonts have that, but I think there has to be a lot of non-typographic cultural "support" to create such an enabling environment for this; does Lithuania have an overall distinctive graphic style, and one that can be expressed in a text font?

As for the closeness to Bembo, although the overall effect is slightly different -probably different enough- it does indeed seem that some of the outlines are really way too close... My guess is it was a direct derivation from digital Bembo, but I could be wrong. Personally, that wouldn't be so bad if the result was highly different (like where people take Garamond and Futura and blend them together - there's no way that font is going to be interchanged for either and affect sales or anything), but in this case I think you're hunch is right: the ethical limit line seems to have been crossed.

But you know what personally bothers me more: the bad craft. The neutered "f"*, and splotchy color in the italic, etc. Pretty messy, not professional. Well, at least the way I use the term.

* Was Palemonas perhaps derived from a Linotype cut of Bembo?
