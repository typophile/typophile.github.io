2014-10-07 21:44:03 • bojev
Given the date of the original and the manufacturing process, the original was most likely a custom die-punch typeface (they actually look like the sort of letters you'd draw with an architect's lettering guide). Here are a few contemporary digital fonts that may help you replicate the look: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/jnlevine/slim-james/ [you'd have to artificially expand it] http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/ef-typeshop/diamante/ http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/softmaker/diamante-serial/ http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/marksimonson/refrigerator-deluxe/ http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/fontbureau/fb-agency/ http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/processtype/stratum2/ http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/castletype/agency-gothic-ct/ http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/fort-foundry/industry-inc/ http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/fontfont/oxide-pro/ And, of course, there is the old standby, Eurostile (condensed): http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/adobe/eurostile/
