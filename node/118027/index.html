I agree with the never digitized idea. This from the heyday of photo-lettering when fonts were expanded, compressed and or clipped at will. Kevin’s suggestion of California Grotesk Black gives you a candidate to do the same with digital tools. Here is California Grotesk Black compressed so that the height width ratio matches your sample, done by compressing the width to 49%. But, as you can see the compressed version is not nearly bold enough. You need to start with a high contrast [aka thick and thin] grotesk that is a much heavier weight than California Grotesk Black. Must be an ultra black somewhere that you could compress. Don
