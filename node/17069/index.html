2006-01-12 19:14:20 • Hector Muñoz

Another way to categorize type trends would be by technology. New technologies and new design genres have always gone hand in hand.

For example, Baskerville’s letterforms, his smooth paper, extra black ink, and austere layouts. It was similar in a way to neoclassical architecture, such as Nash’s terraces, where the structural material (brick or stone) was covered with white stucco, to create the perfect surface – just as Baskerville had obliterated the wove. No trace of process or hint of human scale, a machine finish – the opposite of arts and crafts. So the physical style that is termed Neoclassical may just as well be called Mechanist or Scientism (another meaning for these words).

Right now, there’s the OpenType movement.

Speaking of Scientism (in the established sense), that’s another contemporary movement, headquartered at Microsoft.
