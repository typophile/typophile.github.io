Combining fonts, football, F1, fags (cigarettes) and beer…

This Telegraph article on western beers in Russia mentions Heineken’s Champions League advertising campaign that used a made up brand “ChapionsPlanet” to circumvent French laws on liquor advertising. The fictional brand uses the distinctive Heine font and colour scheme.

This kind of thing has been going on for years in Formula 1 (example) to avoid tobacco advertising bans. I’ve always thought this practice shows the power of typography, and would make for a great magazine article, coffee table book or undergraduate dissertation subject.

Does anyone have any interesting examples of this genre they’d care to share?
