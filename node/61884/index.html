InDesign, Scaling, & Bleeds

September 11, 2009 • evanbrog

I got a problemo.. I have tons and tons of flyers I need to create. Some might be professionally printed, with a bleed. Some might be printed manually on normal, desktop inkjets, and so they will not need a bleed. Is there a practical way to scale a document that I've created in InDesign so that it prints on a normal printer within its standard margins? I don't think it's practical to redesign every piece for two purposes, there are too many. Can I convert the fonts to outline in InDesign and scale the whole thing as a group, so that it will print on the inkjets? (I would, in this case, create a background under all the other elements at the final size necessary). Or export the bleed-version as a PDF, and scale it down 95% or so in Photoshop and re-save it? I'm not so concerned with a difference between the elements and their closeness to the edge between versions, just that everything looks good & crisp.
In my opinion, keep the document with your original dimensions in InDesign. And while exporting the PDF go to Mark and Bleed options and enable crop and bleed marks. So, when you print the document with manual settings you will automatically get bleeding margins on all the sides, which you can cut & implement later on. @evanbrog: Setup and finish the version with bleeds and print the final design to PDF with marks and bleeds. Open a copy of that PDF in Acrobat Pro and select 'Crop Pages' from the 'Document' menu. Select the option to show the 'trimbox'. This will automatically set the margins in cropping area. Click OK to crop. With the PDF now cropped to the trim size you can print from Acrobat with 'Page Scaling' set to 'Fit the Printable Area'. Any scaling that is required will be handled automatically.
