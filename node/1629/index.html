Logotype for a reverend(!)

July 28, 2002 • glutton

Had a neat opportunity to design the identity for the reverend who was officiant at my wedding. (Somewhat precipitously, as the judge didn't show – but that's another story.) The logotype is in Adobe Caslon Semibold, with italic swashes. I like how the R and T look, but something's funny with the C… any suggestions? Also, can anything think of an elegant serif typeface with swashes and ligatures that might look better? The feeling I'm gunning for is "elegant and dignified".
You might also consider Jonathan's eponymous Hoefler Text. All weights have a complete set of swashes, and you have the option of italic small caps with some great alternates as well. The swash 'C' is the same basic style as your Caslon example. You might try using a non-swash one instead, if you don't like the open, swoopy quality of the swash form. I personally prefer a lighter weight for "elegant and dignified." – Kent.
