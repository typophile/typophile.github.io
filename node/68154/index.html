Interestingly, I thought the Girard Script ‘r’ looks like a ‘v’, at least when it’s by itself, but I agree the script easy to read and the way it partially connects is nicely done. Their OpenType ligatures are usually well-implemented.

If you are still considering alternatives I like a couple of Victor De Castro’s handwritten fonts: [[http://www.unifonts.com/h_family/hudsonfam.htm|Hudson]], [[http://www.unifonts.com/h_family/hudsonserifam.htm|Hudson Serif]] and [[http://www.unifonts.com/a_family/arturisfam.htm|Arturis]]. One thing I like about these is they are not often seen (where House fonts can become trendy, like Neutraface and Chalet) and they seem to blend a casual quality with a professional lettering skill, not always an easy balance to find. I use Arturis and Hudson Serif a lot myself.
