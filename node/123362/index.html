That’s because it appears to be custom.

Closest commercial typeface is Eurostile (Extended or Extended Semibold should do the trick): http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/eurostile-next/

Agree twice with Kevin. Most probably exclusive and proprietary of Rolex and definitely very close to ‘[[http://www.myfonts.com/search/eurostile|Eurostile]] Extended/[[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/urw/microgramma|Microgramma]]’. From what I can see, 2 main differences but easily redoable:

  • /1: top-bar-less
  • /4: top with extended flat section Some more “Eurostile-Extended-like” clients for customization: [[http://www.houseind.com/fonts/housegothic23|House Gothic 23]] Extended, [[http://www.typography.com/fonts/forza/overview|Forza]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/t26/myers-sans|Myers Sans]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/typodermic/from-the-stars|From The Stars]], Latin counterpart of [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/arian|Arian]] (variation on Eurostile Extended), [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/cheapprofonts/bitsumishi-pro-v2|Bitsumishi]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/canadatype/miedinger|Miedinger]] (probably my choice here), [[http://haroldsfonts.com/portfolio/metro-df|Metro DF]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/flat-it/geom-graphic|Geom Graphic]] (nice too)
