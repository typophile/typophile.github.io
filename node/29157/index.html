Giggles: Build contrast. It's not that two sans are not allowed, but there needs to be enough difference and compatibility for the pairing to work. On limiting choice of typography in class: I do advocate this, but there's a side to me that rebels against it. There was a recent debate (and vote) within our program on teaching all the basics using only one typeface. Out of all the typefaces named, two were the front runners: Adobe Garamond, and Univers. Univers won out and, frankly, I was a bit horrified. One of my assignments involve setting a short story, and I just couldn't bear the idea of extensive text setting in Univers. Nothing against Univers, but... I have since tried to find ways to restrict type usage to the two presecribed ones to teach certain core formal principles, and then with other projects, allow students to open up to the vast sea, but demanding coherent considered reasons for their choices. One of the reasons why I ended up falling in love with typography as a student involved the seemingly endless permutations of letters.
