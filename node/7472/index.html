Akzidenz grotesque- with what serif face?

November 2, 2004 • dwdjmu

I am using Adobe Akzidenz Grotesque- light, roman, and bold, in a large format printed piece for a modern art museum. I have had a hard time pairing a serif face with the Akzidenz and wanted to see if anyone had any thoughts on this.

I'll be using the serif face in well-leaded columns of body copy, with headlines, subheads and captions, etc. in Akzidenz.

Usually I try to pair sans and serif faces of similar feel (color within blocks of text, similar letter constructions, etc.)- per Bringhurst's suggestions his Elements book.

Another option would be to lay the entire thing out in the sans serif face, but I want it to be super readable and classy at the same time. Not cold like some typical art museum publications.
Maybe a slab-serif, like Egyptian 505?
