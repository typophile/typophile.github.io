I guess you get what you pay for. Live & learn. In a fix, I went to a 'logo farm' for my production company. Now - there's time to play before a printing order and I can't help feeling that this could be 'more'. The font's Gothic No. 13 and the icon's a cityscape they came up with. It definitely makes for odd text positioning. Any and all input appreciated. I can take it. Thanks in advance - Michael [img:sites/default/files/old-images/FCSpost1.jpg]
First thing that caught my eye was the strange perspective on the plane where the "buildings" rise from. It's kind of weird. The idea of the buildings rising forming an F is nice, but I'm a bit bothered by the strong perspective. I'd make a kind of isometric grid, and just have the buildings rise without the high perspective. I hope I'm making sense here... it's hard to explain what I'm talking about. Tomorrow, I'll post a jpeg explaining what I mean. As for the type treatment, it's okay now, but it seems a tad cliché. Maybe have the "Fever City" line in a condensed font, and the "studio" line in a normal, or even extended font. Trade Gothic's font styles would indeed be excellent for that.
