Hi Paul,

I’ve been getting a mixed reaction about the typefaces treatment. Some who really like it, others who have found it distracting. So far they like it.

Unfortunately, there isn’t any more info on the name and it doesn’t appear to have any special meaning (just sounded cool, I guess). I’ve definitely tried the “hills” idea but also didn’t want to go with anything too obvious or cliched.

As it stands, the three humps (“hills” if you will) created by the “r” and “m” represent the three founders of the company. That info not obviously being apparent to anyone not in the know, but they seemed to like that idea so far. I’ve been trying to make the three “hills” concept more apparent in different ways, but it always comes off poorly (or at least so far).

As far as your scratched digital surface idea, though not bad (and any and all ideas are extremely appreciated right now), I’m envisioning it as a little more complexity in details than I believe to be appropriate. They’ve also made it apparent to me that the goals are something simplistic and iconic (they are looking for easy scalability, and detailed scratched digital surfaces are going to get funky when small).

Maybe an abstract symbol or shape (I’ve tried numerous abstract hill shapes… nothing seemed to work so far though)…

Anyway… I’m still at it, so should I come up with any more interesting mockups, I’ll be sure to post them.

Thanks again for your time/input!

If you won’t be able to make those hills readable, then the middle one should be red. But if you do make it, than you’ll have to make the first one red.

But. If you go in the direction of hills, simplify type. Don’t use those unfinished strokes, because you do need just one symbol in your logo, not more. Logos with more than one emphasized visual specialities tend to be confusing.

Bu beside all these. I like the hills approach much more.
