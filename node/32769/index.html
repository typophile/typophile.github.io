  1. the reviews are “official” typophile reviews. The reviewers are nominated (is that an english word?) by the editors (whoever they are).   2. Everyone gets to review, just make a new category under the forums folder. Pro: more democracy. Con: Everyone gets to write reviews, which would lead to a lot af debate about the quality of the reviews, the objectivity of the writers and so on.

i vote for number 1.

I agree with you Paul about how interesting these are. And if Typophile could foster them, and maybe collect references to outside reviews–typographica, John Berry, typographer.org, etc.–it would be great.

I think you are onto something in thinking that a wiki-like structure would be good. That is, you have the name of the typeface as the title, and then people can add comments. This would be enlightening to both graphic designers and types designers, I think.

2007-04-04 04:27:26 • William Berkson

The interactive, public resource for fonts, news and reviews Haven’t we already got that right here on typophile?

No, I would not say so. I’d say that this is a place where people usually discuss type in general. This includes currently just about every aspect but reviews. I don’t think that Typophile holds discussions constantly about type and humor, technical specs, software and hardware, type fashion, teaching, new releases and just about everything under the sun EXCEPT reviews. I suspect there is a reason.

“Official” reviews here might create a tension that would impede the Type Collaboration motto that fuels this hangout. This is somewhat of an Elysium for people in the industry, alongside other roles.

Nevertheless, I enjoy type reviews a great bit when I find them. As a designer of fonts, I like to learn what people appreciate or despise in others' work. As a type fanboy, I like to read the history, trivia and bios and enjoy contemplating a talented critic’s opinions. As a shopper, I would part with my money much more happily (read: more often) if I felt more informed beforehand. So there should be something offering this service.

Duncan says he is looking for something like Pitchfork for type. I glanced at the home page and thought, “yeah. That.” I proposed the Typosium project because the solution he wants doesn’t seem to exist yet. Could something else become that? Probably, but that doesnt mean it is now. I imagined content that is a type-oriented crossing of Consumer Reports and Spin magazines, and even refer to Typosium as a ‘zine at times.

This thread gives some background on the idea, and why it isn’t doing what Typophile could probably do better. The Manifesto explains things best, as that actually got such luxuries as a drafts and revisions. Right now I am preparing for a major product release, and there are several questions on the table for anyone interested in developing Typosium more. My attention will return to it shortly.
