Hmm. Verdana is one of the typefaces that comes with Windows. It is an odd choice for the subtitles in Mission Impossible 4, but to compare to Avatar - which used Papyrus - they would have to have used something truly bizarre. Like Hobo (a good typeface when used as intended) or Comic Sans.

But that’s an exaggeration. Intentionally using a “bad typeface” for subtitles is unlikely.

I’d suspect that using even a good typeface like Bodoni or Caslon or Times Roman is inadvisable for subtitles. A sans-serif typeface with a uniform stroke width seems to make very good sense as the thing to use against a variable background.

Helvetica, Univers… or Bell Gothic? What about Futura?

Or Kabel?

If it’s the uniform stroke width, and not the lack of serifs, that’s important, what about Stymie or Memphis?

Perhaps News Gothic is closer to what might be the most tasteful…
