Hi Miguel, I would not use Western Union, they have earned a bad rep because a lot of scammers are using their services. This might not be their fault, but then there are other services to consider, the biggest one being Paypal. You can set up your website to collect payments through Paypal and offer the fonts to download immediately after Payment has cleared, so you don't have the to send emails manually and people can get their fonts any time of the day. Paypal sure has it's problems too, but after coding some sites for clients, I have to say it's the best choice as of now. Cheers, The Giraffe

Hey Miguel, First off, I'm very excited to hear you are going to consolidate your designs under your own brand. In my opinion, this is long overdue. The service you describe sounds expensive to me. 10% of sales is pretty high, and the fact that they hold your money until they decide to send it to you, less their fee, is also undesirable. Have you looked into verisign's payflow pro? It's a bit more difficult to implement an SSL certificate, and a bit more expensive to set up a merchant account at your bank, but once you go through the initial expense and hassle, you get a much better system that you control, and you only lose a percent or two per sale to bank fees and credit card fees. But most importantly, you get access to your sales the day after they occur. That's pretty quick, and no one is holding your funds hostage. I agree that Paypal leaves a lot to be desired. But, there are a lot of casual web users that use Paypal extensively. You'll probably make more sales if you offer Paypal as an option. But, I wouldn't go with them exclusively. That said, I don't offer PP as an option at MT. Also, check out GoDaddy's SSL certificates. They're much cheaper than Verisign's solutions, but there are compromises in the service that make me slightly uneasy. However, depending on your needs, they might offer the best balance of performance and price.
