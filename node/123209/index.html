Can’t find a match but far from meaning it’s not a font. It looks to share earmarks of different geometric sans like [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/itc/avant-garde-gothic|Avant-Garde Gothic]] (/P, /O and /R) and [[http://www.myfonts.com/search/futura|Futura]] (pointy angles, /A, /N) with an ‘[[http://www.myfonts.com/search/tag%3A%22Art+Deco%22|art deco]] twist (low/high crossbar position, unbalanced counters, /A, /B) close to some [[http://www.houseind.com/fonts/neutraface|Neutraface]] but strangely on some letters only (/R, /E not impacted). Kerning looks also terrible, mainly the /TA part which could be the indicator of a freebie, a hand-lettering, an amateurish work or a mash-up. The closest I have at the moment is Billy Argel’s [[http://www.dafont.com/nova2.font|Nova]] which, part of /B, looks pretty close, including the awfully-kerned /TA pair. It’d probably be helpful to share more on your sample if you’re seeking a positive answer like the origin, creation date, brand… Alternatives: [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/corradine/emblema-headline|Emblema]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/marksimonson/mostra-nuova|Mostra Nuova]], [[http://www.typography.com/fonts/landmark/overview|Landmark]], Hanoded [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/hanoded/otago|Otago]] and [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/hanoded/kaikoura|Kaikoura]], M-B [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/m-b-creative/mb-deco|Deco]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/m-b-creative/mb-vinatage|Vinatage]] and [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/m-b-creative/mb-picture-house|Picture House]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/facetype/marlowe|Marlowe]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/redrooster/relish-pro|Relish]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/latinotype/uomo|Uomo]]
