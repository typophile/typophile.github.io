May 19, 2004 • kristin

Am I getting old or what? Yeah, It's rhetorical.

I seem to be finding a lot of examples of bad color choices with type. The latest example that drove me nuts was from the Minneapolis College of Art and Design catalog for continuing education classes. Dark yellow type on lilac. You can download the equally irritating pdf by clicking on the "Summer Course Catalog" link from this page:

http://www.mcad.edu/continue/fs-continue.htm

It reminds me of a local eatery called The French Meadow Bakery – their coffee menu is written in brick red chalk on a black chalkboard. I told a friend, "If I hadn't had any, I sure as heck wouldn't be able to order it from that!"
Uf! That's pretty bad. I did a vivid orange on a deep purple once … I wanted it to be jarring, though, and it was only the headline.

I printed your catalog out on the color xerox, and the orange died a little, so the print-out is almost readable.

The cool thing about this is that it makes me feel good about my designs.

I don't mind the color scheme. You can do things with RGB that you can't in print.

But the real weakness with the type at the bottom is this: It is set in Base, famously with "media specific" versions – and yet the face is not bitmapping correctly here, it is incorrectly used. THAT is poor typography (for instance, look at the "m" with uneven stem spacing).
