Coming at this very late in the game, but if SDMAC wishes information about type moulds please feel free to contact me at raymond.nelson.jr@gmail.com as I have quite a bit of relevant information. The photographs above are of Giet Instrument 48, from the collection of the Museum Plantin-Moretus. It is probably the oldest surviving typemould, based upon its structure and a tentative association with matrices dating from the late 15th century.
Guy Hutsebaut is experienced in hand casting, and knows that one does not need to shake the mould with every sort, but the shake is essential in many cases if one is to achieve a sharp face on the type being cast. It isn’t to remove air bubbles, but rather to drive the liquid metal into the ‘eye’ of the matrix before it solidifies, since the metal freezes in an instant.
