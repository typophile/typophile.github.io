2006-11-12 11:37:04 • poms
(see [[Fraktur]]) Fraktur was developed at the court of the Emperor Maximilian V, in Nuremberg, around 1517. A team of artists, including the calligrapher Johann Neudoerffer the Elder. and Albrecht Dürer were involved. I read somewhere that the ornate capital letters were inspired by the Emperor's own handwriting. The lowercase at least seems to have evolved out of Schwabacher. Certainly Schwabacher had a single-storey a. I think that uncial styles also used single-storey a's. But Carolingian miniscule, the basis for both blackletter and humanistic scripts (even though humanist resembles it much more closely ;-) ), used a double-storey a. Gerrit Noordzij, in LetterLetter, has his own counter-theory. He writes that the Nuremberg history was not how Fraktur came about at all. Rather, Fraktur was created by Burgundian artisans in what is now present-day Belgium during the 1400s. Maximilian's mother was Burgundian, and apparently, he favored this style all his life.
