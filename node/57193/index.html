Typographic Research

April 21, 2009 • Griffin

Hello,

I’m working on an online survey for my Typographic Research class. The survey is meant to show what characteristics people find in different typefaces and how it varies. I’m also planning on exploring the influence of different weights, letter-spacing, and line-length on peoples' opinions.

My question for you is what characteristics do you think are most important to include in the survey? I’m looking for about 12. It will be setup like this: each page will have one sample of type, below that image will be a set of checkboxes with the characteristics listed. The participant will be asked to check all that apply to the sample image.

I created a preliminary survey and handed it out to a few other students in the class– these are the “tags” I included, that I hope to improve upon: Strong, Exciting, Playful, Casual, Modern, Precise, Elegant, Boring, Distinct, Ugly, Old, and an option for Nothing.

My professor, Chuck Bigelow, suggested using the slang of college students to describe them, but I’m not sure how effective that would be. I’m also thinking I should make the tags more oppositional– each tag having a counter tag.

Any suggestions or comments are appreciated. Thank you very much.
