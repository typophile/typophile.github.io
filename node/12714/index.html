2005-05-29 11:33:02 • Bald Condensed

What, except the fact that it’s overused to the point of nausea, that its geometrical character shapes and the resulting word shapes are not reader-friendly at all, that it has totally outlived its relevance, that it gets specified because people mistake complacency and lack of imagination for “refinement” and “timeless beauty”

But those flaws all apply to Avant Garde, and would to Avenir if it were old enough to be overused. They are inherent in the design of all regular geometric sans. At least Futura has the sense to avoid Avant Garde’s absurdly large x-height.

Once you’ve decided to use something “Futura-like”, you’re basically assuming most of Futura’s flaws anyway, so you might as well go ahead and use it. If you have specific problems with Futura (like “I prefer two-storey ‘a’s”) then please voice them.

About Futura I quoute Hrant “It’s a feature, not a bug”

Of course Futura has a mediocre performance as a text face compared to most classical roman text faces (altough I read once a whole book set in Futura and it didn’t annoyed me as much as reading another book tightly set in Caslon 540).

Futura still has a lot of character which seems unchallenged today despite the various attempts to substitute it with some other “improved faces” like the boring Univers like Avenir.

I alsho think that the more classical structure of Futura (and it’s consequent elegance) is true to a geometric sans serif as much as to a greek or roman rustical inscription. Most of the other geometric sans serf are not elegant at all.

Too bad it’s overused but people is much less sensitive to this than us.
