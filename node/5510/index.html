Unless you're doing only web design, I would strongly advise you against looking at color examples on screen.

As Albers insisted, "color is the most relative medium". And when you're talking about web, make that times three. It varies way too much from monitor to monitor to be reliable enough. If you have a chance, do some paper stuff or look at exapmles on paper if possible.

2004-07-09 11:26:29 • as8
Pantone publishes some books that show cmyk colors next to pantone colors, and you can use them to convert from cmyk to pantone. Pantone colors really are better than CMYK of course, but some people don't trust the books completely, since it IS published by Pantone. (They want their colors to look better, and the cmyk mixes are usually duller than what is possible.) But the book's usually a good place to start, and then you can tweak the percentages. Or, they have books that show cmyk mixes in two, three, and four color combos, in something like 5% increments, and then match it up to a color swatch as best you can, and then still tweak the cmyk mix by test printing.
Or, ask your printer. They sometimes have their own mixes, and they know what works best with their printers, etc.
