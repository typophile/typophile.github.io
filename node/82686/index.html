What are the sales figures of type?

June 1, 2011 • Mr Dupont

Dear typophiles,

I feel the industry of type is very reluctant to give its sales figures. In book publishing it is often a known fact that such or such title has sold that many copies. I know the two industries are quite different (mostly material/digital, quick/very slow…), and of course typeface licenses aren’t as easy to count as books besause you have to take into account single licenses versus complete families, but still I’d be curious to know how many licenses have been sold of “best sellers” such as Meta, Gotham or Museo. How many font are the small foundries selling? How much does the industry of typography represent worldwide? etc.

I know typography isn’t the best place for money making, and it’s great that we’re all in it out of passion, but it’s no reason to be ignorant on the matter!

Does any of you have some elements of answer? Do you know where I could find documentation on the subject?

Many thanks!

I think stating how many books you’ve sold is far less revealing than how many fonts. Capitalism is inherently opaque.
