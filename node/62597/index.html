Robert Bringhurst says FBI and NATO should really be small caps. The idea is that they are part of a sentence and should blend in gracefully (like oldstyle numbers). But just like with numbers it might be "pretentious" as they put it on the H&FJ website. In one of their typefaces they offer a three-quarter set of figures as a solution to that.

If you have to use a face that has no small caps, I would recommend: 1. Drop the cap height down to just taller than x-height 2. Track it out a bit 3. Beef up the weight with the Stroke feature in your layout application--you can add reaaaaly small amounts. 4. Optional, increase horizontal scaling, say to 103 or 104%. This is my practice, but as James says, develop your own sensibility as to which of these techniques you employ, and exactly what values you assign to the variables.
