I guess you’re aware Ultramagnetic is a family member of Alternate Gothic (old Morris Benton Design).

So News Gothic, Franklin Gothic, Trade Gothic, Benton Sans – all those classic American Grots are stylisitcally harmonious.

For a serif face, I’m thinking something with a “press gain” look, e.g. my Paradigm Pro Light.

There are a lot of “sturdy/dark” text face recently published. Dolly was one of the first.

**

I used a demonstration with salt in my type design class for several years: pour salt in letter shape, photograph, make high contrast in Photoshop, import into FontLab, autotrace, et voilà, “Blur”.

2011-03-28 15:51:46 • Nick Shinn

For a similar/contrasting feel, maybe Slimbach (http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/itc/slimbach/alternate_cuts.html) or the more playful Arbiter (http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/berthold/arbiter-bq/). Or, as Nick noted, something which can give a “press gain” feel, so no thin or sharp serifs, but something slabby and without too much contrast.

By the way, I like the sample you posted :-) I asked about the type of cookbook just to better suggest something appropriate, given the peculiarity of Ultramagnetic in such context.
