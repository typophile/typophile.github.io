Gotham info....again

February 23, 2009 • cohoss

Any input from others would be greatly appreciated. I've finally decided to jump on the Gotham bandwagon and use it for an up coming project. However, whenever I buy a family of faces, I like to make sure I'll have future uses for them. Having said that, I was originally drawn to the regular width of Gotham and think it's perfect for a direct mail piece I'm designing. However, a lot of my other work is divvied up between brochures and newsletters and wondered if the Gotham Narrow family would give me more "bang for the buck." After printing some samples, I'm thinking some of the appeal of Gotham is lost in the narrow version of the font. So, I appreciate the text uses for narrow and would probably get more use out of it, but am torn between the two weights. Any thoughts? Thanks!
I just bought the " bundle" and I do a lot of magazines. I really love it. However it does set very wide and that is part of it's charm, but it does work well in publications. I don't miss the narrow myself.

I think if you license all widths of Gotham you don't need Gotham Narrow. I think having the regular width and the extra narrow width is good enough. But, on the other hand. If it is a choice between the regular width and narrow then you might like the narrow width because it maintains some of the roundness.
