November 4, 2013 • doneaton

Hello, I have been trying to id the font used by the US Army on their K ration boxes during WWII. This font also appears on other boxes, such as ammunition crates. While there is some slight variation to the fonts on original boxes I have seen, I have uploaded a typical box end. If it helps, the "KS" is 2" tall while the other letters are 3/4" tall. Although I classified this font as sans serif, the "S" and "2" do have serifs to distinguish them from "5" and "Z". Any help would be greatly appreciated. Thanks. Lettering. The military mind in wartime. An army interpretation of Bank Gothic. Everything is in heavy straight lines; no curves. Easy to create in a font. Bank Gothic is too light so I found something with a heavier weight to match the "KS." I adapted Liberator "a wartime inspired sans serif" by Ryan Clark from the Lost Type Co-op; http://www.losttype.com/font/?name=liberator All I did to it was to square up the angled corners and do some minor tinkering mainly to "2" and "3." To match the lighter weight of the other parts, I would use something intermediate in weight between Liberator and Bank Gothic. [img:sites/default/files/old-images/KSRations_4136.jpg] Don
