2015-03-16 14:25:30 • uzapucax

Just regarding the kerning, it’s a bit uneven. For example, look at the tightness of ESE in the first line, as opposed to the looseness of PRET in the second line.

As for a book on kerning, I’m not so sure a book would help. Good kerning is mostly a matter of seeing, then making those adjustments and compromises necessary to achieve the best possible distribution and balance of positive and negative spaces.

Have you seen the following online kerning game: http://type.method.ac. I don’t always agree with some of the designer’s solutions, but it’s good practice.

For what it’s worth, I like the poster.

2015-03-16 16:34:31 • Thylacine

I have to agree with Thylacine here, the kerning is still uneven. It’s a difficult thing to advise on because doing one thing has a flow on affect for the rest of the ‘word picture’. What you’re trying to achieve is optically balanced white space around each letter whilst maintaining whole word cohesion (if that makes sense! I think it’s more difficult to describe. than do) :)

Take D E S E O, I see it more as D ESE O. Kerning is not a science but IS all about the EYE (of which you appear to have a very good one, as this design as a whole attests)

Regarding the skull’s shadow, I’m not sure what program you’re using so again it’s difficult to advise, but I’m pretty sure you need to split the skull from it’s shadow somehow, so you have 2 separate objects.

Hope this helps
