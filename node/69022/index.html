2010-04-01 20:19:24 • Nick Job

Well, you said you thought it had echoes of Optima and I agree. To me it seems interesting how few stressed sans seem original. It’s hard to fault Optima – it’s been around a long time and it is beautifully elegant – but you would think more designers would tackle a new take on stressed sans faces. There must be more than I think, but offhand the only other similar style I can think of that I like a lot is Monotype’s [[http://www.fonts.com/FindFonts/Detail.htm?pid=243318&/cgi-bin/MsmGo.exe?grab_id=0&page_id=37056&query=STRAYHORN&SCOPE=Fonts|Strayhorn]] by Michael Harvey. I know it’s a sans version of Ellington, but it’s more than that in my opinion. Harvey shows wonderful skill with the letters, and I assume it comes from his stone cutting experience. At larger sizes (or in print), I think it’s a beautiful typeface.

Another really nice stressed sans by Michael Harvey is [[http://www.fonts.com/FindFonts/Detail.htm?pid=420264&/cgi-bin/MsmGo.exe?grab_id=0&page_id=12215&query=MENTOR%20SANS&SCOPE=Fonts|Mentor Sans]].

And another good one is [[http://fontbureau.com/fonts/Amira|Amira]], which has that angular energy of a lot of Cyrus Highsmith’s faces.

I like the heavier weights of both of these better than the light ones.

Doyald Young’s [[http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/doyald-young/young-finesse/|Finesse]] is a super thin variant on Optima which looks good very big.
