I was wondering if anybody has any information about the design or what has influenced the design of this phototype beauty. I was looking through this late 70s art book this morning that is set quite well in this typeface. I am thinking that I may want to break away from some of my usual suspects in type selection for my next project. I will try to provide a scan from this book once the scanning machine over here becomes available. Any history, factoids, opinions, criticisms, or even good type-pairings are well appreciated! Cheers, -David
So far I've only found two citings in print: Rookledge's International Directory of Type Designers, 1994: José Mendoza y Almedia French, 20th century Photina (1971) ...His best-known typeface is Photina, released by the Monotype Corporation in 1971. It was only their third typeface designed exclusively for filmsetting and was designed to work harmoniously with Adrian Frutiger's Univers series. Fine Print Magazine, 1979. GALLIARD, by Charles Bigelow ...In such a climate special note should therefore be taken of the designers and introducers of new text faces which use the photocomposition medium to best advantage. (edited out several designers)... José Mendoza's Photina (Monophoto, 1972) combines a calligraphic, Old-style letterform with the serifs and a contrast of a Modern. As a serifed face, it was designed to align and mate with the sans serif Univers.
