Hi there,

I’ve been designing a font for a while and it seems to be an on/off project. Mainly because I find more and more (wacky) ways of writing it, use it as a graphic element, etc. I’m a fan of optical illusions so I tried to get this font working as a hommage to Reutersvärd and Escher. Well, I managed to get quite a bit of impossible figures into it (it’s most obvious in the B), though not in all of them. My focus was the outline of the characters, I tried to still make them legible even when not filled with the ‘shading’. I’m happy with it now and actually a bit proud to have managed to squeeze a font in the most restricting three dimensional grid possible. Enjoy!

Here’s an image of the font ‘in action’. I’ve already used in on a couple of designs and got some good feedback from publishers. There’s just a wee bit of a technical issues to solve (won’t work in fontographer).

2008-11-30 06:18:52 • fatbob
The easiest way to make this work as a font is to rotate everything 30 degrees clockwise so that it can work on a horizontal baseline. When using the font, rotate the text box 30 degrees counterclockwise to get the proper effect.
