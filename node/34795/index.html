2007-06-28 00:29:29 • russ_mcmullin
This has already been explained in two or three other threads, but I think it is worth saying it again here. Typophile runs on slightly out-of-date, donated server space from Media Temple. Because Typophile is now serving up millions of pages per day, for thousands of users (based on current site statistics), we are experiencing slow load times. The past few days have been especially painful because of the upgrade. In contrast to what some may think, we are currently shopping around for a new hosting package that can support everyone's needs and make this site usable and enjoyable for all who visit. Unfortunately, this takes time and for a non-profit community whose contributers work mostly in their spare time, it might not happen as quickly as we'd like. It is frustrating for all of us, but we can see the light at the end of the tunnel and we hope you will too.

2007-06-28 00:53:11 • Ricardo Cordoba
There are a few factors that have influenced this change. The previous codebase looked at all of the unique visitors from the last 45 minutes. We reset this during the upgrade to 15 minutes, which reduces the number of guests more significantly than logged in users, since logged in users tend to visit the site more consistently. Probably the most significant difference, however, is that we curtailed a few unruly bots that were eating more than their fair share of page views. If you haven't noticed, the site performance has increased significantly since we told these bots to shoo.
