Like I said in [[http://typophile.com/node/49788|this other post]], about 15 years ago, I created for myself a phonetic alphabet in which all unnecessary or redundant characters were either omitted or re-assigned new sounds. I used the x to precede long vowels and did away with the silent e and all other letter formulas we use in the English language to indicate long vowel sounds. And I created a brand new letter to indicate the long bowel sound. The alphabet was first called Planetease, and then I changed it to Earthish. I even matched it up to Twinkle Twinkle (that's Sparkling Stella for the music snobs), like the real alphabet song. I showed it to my mom and she could actually read it without instruction. I thought I was a genius for a little while and it was a neat way to journal but then I quit using it.
