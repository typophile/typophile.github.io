Very low hyphens in a monument

April 23, 2008 • mili

This is the text on a monument ("Keisarinnan kivi") commemorating the Russian Empress Alexandra's visit in 1833. The hyphens in it are in an unusual place, never seen anything like this anywhere else. PÄÄ-KAUPUNGISSA (nowadays we write it together without hyphen) I suppose the months were treated like this to avoid repeating the word kuussa Touko-kuussa Kesä-kuussa Was this the way to deal with hyphens in 1830's, or is this an odd one out?
