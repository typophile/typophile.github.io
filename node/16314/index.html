It is more an issue of what it will be used with. The blue does not live in a vacuum. Also, how much of it? If your design is big solids, it may need to be different than if you are using the color in fine lines. You might post a GIF of it.

I also consider if the 4-color build of the PMS is reasonable. Very intense spot colors don’t seperate well too often. Often, you won’t have the luxury of a spot color–for instance ina magazine ad.

Take in count what Chris wrote. Also, for a printer blue color is the most difficult color to print in offset. What I could suggest is to choose your color in process as the basic reference if you plan to print the most of your document in 4 color process. If dont choose the PMS as the reference. An other way to choose a color is to work with the DS chart. Personaly, I find the blue color is easier to choose with DS and even match with PMS. That chart came with the PMS chart.

Watch out with blues if you are printing Reflex Blue and Rotomine Red have really bad drying times. If you have a cast coated stock they are almost impossible to work with. I would consult with your clients printer before finalizing it as “the color”.
2005-11-18 22:33:24 • Dan Weaver

The Royal Bank (Canada) has a really dark blue as a corporate color, and uses a spot color on upscale printed jobs.

But it also uses gold (4C approx. or special foil, ink, etc) to perk things up.

You will probably need a little bit of bright zing to go with the Dark blue, or the effect could be rather dreary and uninteresting.

I’m watching for the type, too. But while we wait, I can tell you I worked for a large company that used PMS 294 for its logo. The high quantity of Reflex Blue made it not only difficult to deal with in drying times, etc., but unreliable in terms of cosistency. The primary pigments in Reflex blue and rotamine red are prone to hue shifts under varying conditions, making matching across collateral very difficult, unless you’re printing it all at the same time at the same print shop.

On the other hand, things like this are the burden of the printer. You’re paying them to get it right.
