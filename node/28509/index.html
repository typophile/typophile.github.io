Read. There are dozens of great books (and magazines) on the subject and probably hundreds that aren’t as great. While you’re reading, pay attention to the way the books are set–it’s hard to find better typography than what you’ll see in good books on the subject.

It’s also good to look for bad typography and learn from the mistakes of others. You’ll find plenty of bad type online, cheap magazines are also generally overflowing with the stuff.

I’ve just had a look at ‘Exploring Typography’ by Tova Rabinowitz, (Publisher Thomson Delmar Learning, 2006. ISBN 1401815057) and I can recommend it as a general-purpose text (covers a lot of ground, including some layout) There is also the <a/href=“http://typophile.com/wiki/Triumvirate">Triumvirate to which I would add <a/href=“http://www.logofontandlettering.com/">Leslie Cabarga’s Logo Font and Lettering Bible.

But more than that, if you hold a graphic design degree already, go out to work. Preferably with some seasoned old timers who would like to mentor you in the finer aspects… trade knowledge beats book knowledge, but in typography they are sometimes the same thing.
