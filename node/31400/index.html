Logo for a small industrial park

February 13, 2007 • stw

The logo I have done is for an industrial park in a small village in Germany. The park consists of some larger companys like an engineering company or a window-builder but also a gym, chips chops and a children's playground. The industrial park would like to print some flyers in a few days when they have an open day! They dont want to make advertisement for themselves but for the companys which are in the park. But therefor they first need a logo. However they want to win some new customers/lessee too. I made a new font for their logo. It has rounded corners that looks friendly but not too childish and has large counters. For the icon I made a simplified depot which is almost similar to the german road sign for an industrial park. Any thoughts would be very helpful especially on the spacing :) Greeting: Steven
I would say that BO of Borchen is just about right in terms of kerning and that should act as the basis of the kerning for the rest, nearly every other pair needs to be a touch tighter. I wonder if the icon is too industrial (as opposed to retail), but I would amend the radii of the curves around the roof, maybe use the sharp corners on the inside and rouunded on the outside. Tim

I like the open cubes. I might expand on the cubes and make them multidimensional looking as expansive. I would make the type a different weight than the mark. You need some separation, make it bolder or lighter. Everything feels the same weight. Make priorities to your message. Ask yourself what is most important. Is it the graphic (I doubt it) is it the name (I think so) and the location (a throw away). Make each element stronger or less strong to stress the message.
