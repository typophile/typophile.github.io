For digital equivalents, looks like a mash-up of Kobalt (most of the letters) and Eagle Bold (C, S):

http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/itc/kobalt/std-bold/ http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/fontbureau/eagle/

It’s entirely possible that the original signmaker took his inspiration from older type samples (the original Eagle Bold was designed in 1933 by Morris Fuller Benton; Kobalt was created by Leslie Cabarga in 2007, but based on Art Deco typefaces from the same period as Eagle Bold).

EDITED: I note that the Dom Polski Centre is located in Australia http://dompolski.com.au/our-centre-3 What would a 1970s Australian designer use as inspiration for the DOM POLSKI CENTRE sign? Something from Poland? Something from Australia? One, as already mentioned, would be Eagle Bold drawn in 1933 for the National Recovery Association. Others could include the 1930s designs of Alessandro Butti for Nebiolo. Few are digitized. Some letterforms that are echoed in DOM POLSKI CENTRE are found in Semplicità. See Simplo http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/durotype/simplo/ and Semplicita http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/canadatype/semplicita-pro/ Don
