Readability of Optima Nova

May 10, 2007

Optima is certainly amongst the most readable (and used) sans serifs, but how readable do you guys really think it is? I mean for setting extended text, like say a book. Hermann Zapf’s “About alphabets” was originaly printed with the metal type Optima and that was quite readable. But the digital versions aren’t quite as strong. Except maybe Optima nova, which has a colour closer to the original metal type, but has anyone actually tested it out?

I think a monograph about Hermann Zapf is about the only place you would ever want to use Optima. Just a personal preference… Feels dated to me.

2007-05-10 20:09:01 • hrant
I find it very grey and bland in text. It’s got a consistent vertical stress and very wide shapes, and the basic weight, while appropriate for letterpress, looks too light and wispy in digital form. You could try the Medium weight of the new version, but that only solves the basic weight issue. Linotype has printed specimens of Optima Nova, which you could get to see the performance in text. I’ve seen the original digital Optima in text but at uncommonly large sizes. There are other alternatives, however….. ;)

Ok, this is our monthly bash Optima thread. Again, I don’t agree. The original is too grey in text, but is wonderfully elegant when well used in display–especially the caps. The Pascal is very impressive, but I don’t see it as an alternative to Optima, they are so different.

Tiepolo and Carl’s Beorcana seem to be more in the same space, but Beorcana is way better, IMHO.
