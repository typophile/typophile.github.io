Could you help me to find a mate for Futura?

February 18, 2009 • hbeier

Hey fellas!

It’s the first time I’m writing something around here, even though I’ve been following you for quite some time. And the reason I’m writing is that I really need some help.

I’m a graphic design student and currently an intern at the Marketing Department of the university I study in. I’ve been assigned to redesign their brand guidelines, since the ones they have aren’t that good.

The issue I’m struggling with is that their primary typeface is Futura, but they don’t have a secondary one. I want to define a serif typeface as secondary, you know, for the sake of contrast. This serif family would be used to set long texts, letters and other stuff like that. I am aware of Bringhurst’s suggestion – to use a modern one, like Bodoni – but this combination looks too vanguardistic to me, as they define themselves as a regional, social institution.

I was thinking in something more humanistic. Any suggestions on that? Oh, and it’s not possible to change Futura, since they already are well known for it.

I’d really appreciate any help!

Thanks in advance.

So far I’ve got ITC New Baskerville. Any thoughts?

I think the serif in the IKEA catalog is Century Schoolbook and I find it does go very well with Futura. It takes up some of the geometrical simplicity while providing a pleasant contrast in rhythm…

That being said, I’d stay away from looking just like IKEA. It’s such a well known contemporary design that people will notice and wonder why your university’s design looks like a furniture catalog.

Maybe Frutiger’s “Egyptienne F” would be something… It has a lot of simplicity is more geometric than Century Schoolbook… I think this could work, what do you think?
