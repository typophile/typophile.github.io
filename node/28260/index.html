Looking for typeface with Gaelic feel

September 15, 2006 • Jes

Hi everyone, I'm looking for a typeface that has Gaelic influences or a sort of Gaelic "feel," but is still pretty easily readable for a modern American audience. It's to set the excerpts of a poem that opens the chapters of a story in a literary magazine/journal; the poem is actually excerpts from the Carmina Gadelica (a body of traditional songs and chants from the Western islands of Scotland), first in the original language (anglicized, though; the author doesn't want it actually to be in Gaelic), then translated. I want the typeface to evoke the mood without being too extreme. I think a face like Eirinn Gaelic just requires too much work to read, even just for the five or six lines of a poem; the letter forms are too unfamiliar, and I think readers will end up just skipping it. I'm going to use Warnock (10/12) for the body of the story. Thanks!
How Gaelic do you want it to be? ITC Dyadis strikes me as ever-so-slightly Gaelic. ITC Forkbeard has traces of uncial which could be read as Gaelic. Or, you could search for Uncial, perhaps one of these?
