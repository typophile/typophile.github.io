“Un-authoritative” Serif Body Typeface Suggestions

March 1, 2015 • grubstreet

Hi all, I’m doing a branding project for my school’s library, and admins wanted a typeface that doesn’t evoke academia, or suggest anything about teaching, expertise, tradition, poise or authority. So Venetian and Garalde are immediately off the list. Also don’t want to use French modern or Scotch Roman (too delicate, too Vogue-like). This is a body typeface that will be paired by an either softened Neo-Grotesk or a humanist sans. I’m a bit out of ideas: what kind of serif performs well in long-form text while does not have too much calligraphic reminiscence to be considered classy? I proposed Lyon, which is already non-traditional enough (Noordzij style very obvious though) but still got rejected. Do I have to go with a slab?
I don’t know if you consider it a slab serif, but I think something like Chaparral could work.

Ok, I forgot this one. Here is a citation from A Backward Glance at Eighty by Charles Murdock
In my publications I followed a somewhat severe style of typography, especially priding myself on the possession of a complete series of genuine old-style faces cast in Philadelphia from moulds cut a hundred and seventy years ago. In these latter days a few bold men have tried to improve on this classic. One Ronaldson especially departed from the simplicity and dignity of the cut approved by Caxton, Aldus, and Elzévir, and substituted for the beautiful terminal of, say the capital T, two ridiculous curled points. I resented it passionately, and frequently remarked that a printer who would use Ronaldson old-style would not hesitate to eat his pie with a knife.
Given the specs, that would, I guess, justify using Ronaldson. By the way, Ronaldson was used for textbooks at least here in Quebec at the beginning of the twentieth century.
