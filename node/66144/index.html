2010-01-12 19:04:19 • Jongseong
And "orthogonal" is used in Hilbert space too, so in Quantum Mechanics one can say, for example, that a position eigenstate of a particle is orthogonal to a momentum eigenstate of a particle (the integral of the product of the two wavefunctions is zero), which means that Heisenberg's uncertainty principle applies directly in its simplest form to those two observables. From that usage, the figurative usage of "orthogonal" to mean independent or unrelated comes very naturally.
