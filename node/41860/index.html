The key to the luxury look is that you can afford to waste space. That may be why faces that are markedly condensed or expanded look upscale, because they force one to space them out (copious letterspacing and/or leading) and use them large so that they don't look crammed or fill in. Faces with a small x-height, like Mrs Eaves, look upscale because they too are not designed to cram in as much text as possible; book face proportions for a leisurely read. Albertus and Optima are elite because they're not your common or garden sans or serif.
