2010-04-20 20:22:35 • Thomas Phinney

The first revival of Jenson was by William Morris, which kick-started the historicist era of typography. That was the Golden Type, which really doesn’t look much like the original. Then it was revived by private presses, e.g. Bertram Goodhue’s Merrymount. Then for the largest foundry, ATF, by Morris Benton: Cloister. Then by Bruce Rogers: Centaur. Strangely, there was no large x-height ITC Jenson in the 1970s/80s, but there is ITC Legacy from the 1990s. Other digital Jensons today include Frere-Jones' Hightower, and my own Nicholas/Goodchild (with the large x-height). Benicia by Jim Parkinson, also based on the “Morrisonian” school of Jenson faces. I’m sure there are more, but I can’t think of them right now.

Strange but true: ATF published a type named Jenson, based on Morris' Golden Type, drawn by a fellow named Phinney.
