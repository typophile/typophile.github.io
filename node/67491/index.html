The graphic is linked to on http://cg.scs.carleton.ca/~luc/fontbooks.html It is from a book from 1921 on book lettering. Get used to the fact that this is, as it says, 16th c. calligraphy. Mind the two e's in "Sixteenth". You won't find it as a font unless someone fonted it (which would include "inventing" all the caps except for S and C). Jim Fordyce's Diamond Gothic (freeware) is similar, but wider. Manfred Klein's [[http://www.abstractfonts.com/font/14112|Dueres Minuskeln]] (according to Albrecht Dürer) are similar too. Dieter Steffmann issued a "Gothenburg Fraktur" which seems to be a completed form of a "Gothenburg" font from the "IMSI Masterfont collection" (mostly clones and pirated fonts, Gothenburg seems to be cloned from - sorry, thought I'd seen it somewhere else but couldn't find it any more).
