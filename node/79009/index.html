Hi Kent — and anybody else who’s interested, after heading down to St. Brides Library today, and going through a couple of books, I’m pretty sure that the Woolf’s used Caslon in their earlier hand-printed editions produced at the Hogarth press. Some text from one of the books (The handprinted books of Leonard and Virginia Woolf at the Hogarth Press, 1917-1932 / by Donna E. Rhein. ISBN: 0835716945);

[Just off Fleet Street they happened upon] “The Excelsior Printing Supply co., where they bought a small handpress, complete with instruction booklet and some Old Face type for £19. 5s. 5d.”

I assume Old Face type is meant in the broader sense of the word (classification) and having looked at the printed pieces (most of which are somewhat poor reproductions of the Title pages – not full text blocks) it seems to tally that it was Caslon or something very similar, I was focusing on the swash characters used (A, P and R), the inward curling second leg of the italic lowercase h and the numerals to identify the type.

Thanks again Kent! Apologies for the poor image quality!
