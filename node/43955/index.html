Also this CD case is non-standard. In that case, you've sort of answered your own question about getting it to fit into a standard rack. A survey of my collection for cardboard sleeve cases shows they are all just a light bristol board stock that match the height of a standard plastic case, and are otherwise square (with gluing tabs, of course). Have you looked at setting it up with a thicker spine to accommodate the brochure with a sleeve for the CD (open at either the spine or the fore edge)? Back in the good old days, Adobe used to ship out software in some really elegant all-cardboard constructions....
2008-04-07 15:01:50 • Linda Cunningham
One alternative, if you can spare some $$, is to buy a CD with a cardboard sleeve and then take it apart -- you'll get better measurements that way, if you're using a ruler. The first pressings of Cat Power's "You Are Free" had a cardboard sleeve like the one you want to make. It might be easier to find Sleater Kinney's "The Woods," though. Aimee Mann's "Bachelor No. 2" has just a half-pocket for the booklet, but you can use the measurements of the other half. Cheaper alternative: Sleeve City will send you a sample of their cardboard sleeves for just a penny. They also state that their pocket measures 5 in. x 4-7/8 in.
