2005-12-15 20:10:57 • karmachameleon
I like it but substitute the v symbol with a hand holding a daisy. The v symbol was used in that era but it was also used as the victory symbol by Churchill in WWII. The daisy was a pacifist symbol for peace. Now on the type "The would never be on a straight line It would follow the curve of the characters below. The h would drop and tuck under the t and get larger and the e would follow the curve and become larger (filling the space). These posters were hand drawn and they took liberties to fake spacing. The second l in gratefull looks like a t. Once again make Alive fill the shape and follow the curve. Destortion is a good thing for 60s a look.
