dear typophiles

in a specimen book by Bauer & Co, Stuttgart (1600 pages - wonderful!) i found a font called “Schmale runde Grotesk” (Condensed round Grotesk). i digized some glyphs from a photo of a copy, some lines arent vertical and so on… its just a first test.

i have two questions regarding this font.

  • the library wasnt able to date the book, but i’d like to know a circa date - so here the clues i have: on the bottom of each page, H. Berthold AG is noted beneath Bauer & Co - Bauer was bought 1897 by Berthold, as andreas mentions here. AG is shown beneath Royal-Grotesk, so i guess it was after 1908 (in the same post as linked above, andreas states that theinhardt was bought 1908). any idea?

  • is there a reason for the very thin connecting parts in a, d and n? this feature is found in a, b, d, h, n, m, p, q, u.

thanks for your help!

What about the upper-case letters ?——those are indentations placed at the intersection of two strokes or a departure from the intended shape of a stroke near the intersection of two strokes of a glyph to provide compensation for ink bleeding or plugging during printing or overexposure from a photographic process.
