2014-09-18 18:06:50 • fvilanakis

Unusual indeed, Kevin. apostrophe was a notorious masked man. A few years prior to establishing apostrophic labs, he angered all the major foundries by uploading thousands of commercial fonts onto a newsgroup. Referred to it himself back then as a “font orgy”. Never was there a bigger, louder, invective-filled discussion about copyright and piracy.

It turned out that he was Fredrick Nader, THE top custom font designer for big firms in Toronto!

apostrophic labs was sort of a font commune producing high-quality (technically, not design-wise in many cases) free fonts. apostrophe made a lot of them, but many others contributed and then apostrophe would help out with advice and technical fixes. They were the only ones putting out free multiple master fonts (remember those?).

Based on the number of times it’s been ID’d here and on MyFonts' forum, I suspect that, if Scriptina had been a commercial font, it would have been a huge money maker. Check out Mistress Script and the slew of alternates and ligatures in the Alt font.
