Art Deco Font

April 16, 2010 • Adventurous Putty

Hello everyone! First post by a typography amateur, so forgive me if I say anything absurd or sophomoric.

I recently took an interest to some of the fonts that people might have used in advertisements or monuments from the early and mid-20th Century, specifically those of the Art Deco aesthetic. Problem is, I haven’t been able to find any that suit my taste. I’m looking for something relatively light and unencumbered, but still really iconic – something like the [[http://sharetv.org/images/batman_the_animated_series-show.jpg|text]] in the logo of the old 90s Batman cartoon or the [[http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/e6/RocCt-LeeLawrie-Wisdom.jpg|words]] on the wall at Rockefeller Center. Actually, it’s funny about that last example, because I actually found a font called Lawrie somewhere once that almost seemed to suit my purposes, but I’ve never seen it around again.

Any suggestions? Perhaps somebody around here has made one like that, or knows someone who has.

The Batman one looks a bit like ITC’s Anna. Clicking the “Art Deco” link to the right of this font shows a lot more look-a-likes, plus lots of Broadway and ITC Kabel clones.
