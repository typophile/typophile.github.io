2005-02-03 07:16:59 • beejay
I think you mean lettering as opposed to drawing type. The process of drawing letters for type in the old days was more like making a blueprint for someone else to follow. If it was for direct reproduction, like for phototype headline fonts, the technical process was essentially the same as lettering except that the design of the letters was more rigorous owing to the fact that they had to be able to be assembled in any arbitrary order. Anyway, bj's suggestion looks like a good one in your case, but here are some other books you might want to look at (some are probably out of print, though): Speedball Textbook (used to be found at art supply stores–not sure if it still is) Lettering and Alphabets, by J. Albert Cavanagh (Dover Publications) Lettering for Advertising, by Mortimer Leach (Van Nostrand Reinhold) The Modification of Letterforms, by Stanley Hess (Art Direction Books) Lettering for Reproduction, by David Gates (Watson-Guptill Publications)
