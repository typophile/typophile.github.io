Overview of small typefoundries and their fonts?

August 18, 2006 • Sebastian Nagel

Dear Typophiles, I am wondering if there is a somewhat "complete" (ha-ha...) overview of small type foundries and their – often so excellent but hardly noticable – fonts, that makes it easier to find something "special" aside the beaten path of big foundries like linotype, fontfont, etc. Background story: I criticized the designs of a friend of mine because he always uses always the same, "boring" fonts. I mean, the most innovative thing he did was to use Myriad instead of Frutiger... But his arument was that it took him way too much time to find special fonts that would fit better because he first needs to know about the different foundries, look whether they have something useful for the job, make sure that it quality work (in design, extent, technical aspects), and often purchase is somewhat more difficult than with the well-known big foundries (because he is used to them). Does one really need to be an "insider" in fontdesign to know "small" things like (just for example) MacRhino-Fonts, Storm Type, etc.? Is there a book, website, etc. that is specialized on these foundries, getting an overview of independent works? Myfonts is okay, but there, the big players are present as well, which is okay, but makes things too vast. Of course I can propose some things to him sometimes, but I'm not omniscient, up-to-date, and I'm not a tell-me-the-perfect-font-answering-machine. Thanks for your help Sebastian
The two Indie Fonts books are very good, and are for sale in bookstores all over Europe, or online, of course ;-) Both Indie Fonts and Indie Fonts 2 are beautifully-bound type specimen books, more equisitely-designed than almost any catalogues made since the 1930s. They are sold at good prices, too, especially since they have been out of a little bit now.

2006-08-18 19:50:27 • Joe Pemberton
You could just connect him with 3-5 suppliers (could be designers, foundries, resellers) whose products are consistently high-quality, which have simple online purchasing options, whose designs are fresh, and which offer a good variety. Even if a few foundries get left out, it would be a good start for someone like your friend who is apparently cut off from the vibrant options available now. Quality of product (complete character sets, good digitization, expert spacing & kerning, robust, versatile families, reliable font performance, etc.) should be a primary criterion. Not every small foundry is able to provide this level of quality. Not to mention originality of design. Suggestions: Storm House H&F-J Village Feliciano FontBureau Device OurType Jim Parkinson Josh Darden Don't throw a huge list at him. If you picked 4 from this list, whose products don't overlap much in style, then it would be a big boost to his options, but not overwhelming. If several are reselling through fonts.com or myfonts, then all the easier. It might turn out to be a great intro for him to the wide world of indie fonts.
