[img:sites/default/files/old-images/GNR_logo_6091.jpg] The company logo appears to have two fonts based on the tail of the "R" and the variation in stroke appears. Uncertain if the ampersand has been manipulated or not, cannot find this symbol. Any assistance in identifying these fonts would be helpful. The logos the company have on file are low resolution.
The |G|| and |R| are Cloister (artificially condensed): http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/adobe/cloister/ The ampersand looks to be Sabon (also artificially condensed), but could also be Garamond Extra Narrow: www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/sabon/ http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/urw/garamond/ Sabon and Garamond are also likely candidates for the balance of the name and for |FINANCIAL GROUP, INC.|, but both have a capital |P| in which there is a gap between the bowl and the vertical stroke, and I’m not seeing that gap in your artwork (at least not in the main name).
