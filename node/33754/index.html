O.K here goes: I’m interested in designing meaningful communication with typography as its primary method of delivering that message. When we say meaningful its more to do with who we communicate on behalf of. Ideally we try and succeed 96.8% of the time to produce design for organisations that are not predominately led by profit. So guess that is more about the type of work I am drawn to from a client / commissioned point of view. The way I see it if you don’t design your work for clients and its not actual ‘living’ design you are not a designer. This to me is more like art, as what is design without a purpose? What am I interested in as an approach to the above, trying not to follow fashion or be influenced too much by the zeitgeist, ‘style not fashion’ is a bit of a mantra to me. I’m fascinated by the tone of voice / character specifying typography can create. I was playing today with using a serif ‘utopia’ on a community sport poster. It looked wrong but I was very interested in why it was wrong. this wrongness I like to play with but it tends to be where I depart from what I call serious design. As time wares on I care less and less about this. The Alan Fletcher exhibition reminded me you can have fun and play with type and language. I have been putting this into place recently with: Teachers Museum Project Dual Existence Film titles Artists catalogue - fur tongues Photography exhibition catalogue Community sport posters We also have a visual identity for a film production organisation. and have been specifically asked to be ‘playful’ So I’m addressing the logical and trying to release the playful me.

With regards to what discourse am I into? What a magnificent question: Not really sure what is on offer. I’m a neo-modernist / digital dada-ist / absurdist rationalist I’m really interested in people and exploring how design can enrich peoples lives. What are you into Claus? I am a bit stuck here…
