"quibuscumque"? Do you have a date for this? I heard it was not unusual for typesetters to cramp up a few letters when really pressed for space. In this case, I would guess the q bar is shorthand for 'qui' (with some imagination), and your guess of the '3' like glyph for 'que' could be right. My guess of the tilde as a suppressed 'm' comes from this phrase Unde facta fide de iure in hac p[ar]te requisita petit pars ista Jus et ius[ti]ciam in omnibus p[re]miss[is] et singulis et ea conc[er]nentibus quibuscu[m]q[ue] [[http://freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com/~malchist/mypage23.htm|found with "Google quibuscu"]] The extra large 'H' below would indicate this particular person cared about proper justification :-)
