Footnotes/Sidenotes/Endnotes

May 27, 2010 • vegfarandi

I’m designing a publication about Gender equality in Iceland for teenagers and young adults for the Ministry of Education and Culture. I’m having a debate how I should treat the endnotes in the document.

I much prefer having them close by where they can be easily found, and I do love having marginalia floating about to give life to the margin. But at the same time, most of them are book and internet references and a lot of them don’t look so great. Bringhurst advises us to use sidenotes whenever possible, but here I’m not sure. But I feel that endnotes are more disruptive to the reading of a text and a bunch of them in the endmatter make it look kind of ugly.

What are your thoughts?

I think sidenotes just work well for subtitles or captions. References works terribly with this format. And endnotes, although let the layout clean, are harder to read due to their distance to the main text. My choice would be footnotes.
2010-05-27 21:35:20 • Igor Freiberger

I’ve always preferred footnotes at the bottom of the page.

Unless URLs are short and memorable, they will not be used as links by print readers, so there is no practical reason to include alphanumeric gobbledegook. (I have come across research which had this as a finding, but can’t remember where.)

People will just google to find a website.

So I suppose clunky URLs could, indeed go in the back of the publication, where they will never be read, like legalese.
