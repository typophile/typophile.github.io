Oh! I see. Branding a store is entirely different from branding the products that fill it (though it is not unrelated).

Naturally you have to consider the sort of customers you’re seeking, how to differentiate your client from their competitors, and finally what would look good in many low-quality reproduction methods, from building signage to flexo print on plastic bags.

Copperplate Gothic certainly has an air of confidence about it, in a very conservative, bankerly kind of way. Not sporty at all, in my opinion. If your client’s heart is set on it, I suggest offering [[http://www.typodermic.com/32.html|Typodermic’s Biondi]] as an incermentally more modern alternative that will stand up to those low-quality reproduction processes much better.

not really sure if these are more acceptable.

Never acceptable to use the faux italicise or bold options, apart from the chance of it not printing properly, they are a distortion rather than the intended oblique.

While Futura is more sporty than Copperplate it isn’t that far away from a generic sports font and italic or oblique is regularly used as a metaphor for motion/sporting – it isn’t necessarily a bad thing to use an obvious style.
