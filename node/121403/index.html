Good find. So now we know this was a Photo-Lettering font designed by lettering artist W.M. Millstein.

House Industries now owns the Photo-Lettering archives and is s l o w l y releasing fonts from this source. Less than a hundred so far or out of perhaps out of over six thousand possible fonts. More info http://luc.devroye.org/fonts-51626.html

It might be quite a wait, unless you ask them to make you a custom font, which would probably be quite expensive https://www.photolettering.com/contact/

Photo-Lettering published only a “font in use phrase” at a scale that clearly showed font details. Apparently some of the later catalogs, which I have not seen, included an 8 point character set to supplement the larger scale phrase. With some careful interpolation based on the larger scale phrase an outside font maker could make a digitization that was a fairly close match.

Under intellectual property law, at least in the US, fontmakers are not blocked from making digital versions of old printed typefaces, unless they are the subject of a design patent, which currently has a term of 14 years, if I recall correctly. Millstein Guild was produced many years ago, so there is definitely no design patent in force. Digital outlines are protected as software and copying these into a new font is illegal font piracy. Font names are protected by copyright and or trademark, so fonts based on old typefaces must have different names.

If we are in luck a Typophile who has access to a Photo-Lettering catalog that includes the 8 point character set for Millstein Guild might post an image here.
