September 11, 2014 • Rique

Greetings, fellow type enthusiasts! A couple of years ago, I came across [[http://typophile.com/node/31682|a thread on Typophile]] about the “Spice Girls font”, a typeface I’ve been intrigued by ever since I first saw it in 1998. This font has been searched for by many, many people many, many times, but has never been found. The Typophile thread I mentioned did, however, yield a possible lead: the pre-digital typeface called Tempor. (Thanks, Mike F!) According to my (admittedly limited) research, a specimen of sorts was published in The Solotype Catalog of 4,147 Display Typefaces (on page 157 according to Solopedia – though I might be wildly off there). Unfortunately I am unable to obtain a copy of this book at the moment, so I ask: if any of you could please be so kind to upload a scan of this specimen of Tempor, I would sincerely appreciate it. It’s a mystery I would love to help solve. Oh, also, if anyone has any information on it, such as the name/s of its designer/s, foundry, known digitizations, I will love you forever! Thanks for reading. :)
Here’s the specimen in question. It’s definitely in the same vein. Dan X. Solo’s books were nearly comprehensive, he just wasn’t great about providing source information.

Just for the sake of completeness, and to corroborate what Rob (ralphsmouse) and Ryuk suggested, I will add that, according to MyFonts, the digital Europa Grotesque (2002?) is “based on [ATF’s] Europa Grotesque series circa 1960”. So there could have been an early (more or less legit) digitalisation of it, and maybe the horizontal spine of |S| was in the original (or, au contraire, was introduced to get away with the knock-off). Unfortunately, I’m unable to locate a pre-digital specimen (or any mention of it, for that matter: http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Type_Founders_%28list_of_types%29 ).

Full disclosure: I never had willingly (or knowingly) listen anything by the Spice Girls.

Looking back at the discussion, and by casual visual comparison of the Spice Girls sample posted by rob nolan, to the digital Europa Grotesque and California Grotesque displays at Myfonts, I see the width of the logo as being closer to California Grotesque than Europa Grotesque and, the weight as being somewhere between California Grotesque Bold and California Grotesque Black. Blend the two weights, edit the central portion of the S diagonal to make it horizontal, drop the center line on P, open the space between the terminals on C and you pretty well have what you want, what you really really want :) for the logo. A similar process might also work for the other letters shown in the sample. The track listing on the Spice Girls CD http://lonestarspice.com/singles/suyluscd.JPG does not match the logo or the subheadings text in the posted sample, but does seem to match the smaller sized text. Note the diagonal on S and sloped center strokes on s and a which are in the style of Europa. The appropriate lowercase for a font matching the logo is pure speculation. IMO the Tempor model is more appropriate than Europa for letters such as s and a. Don
