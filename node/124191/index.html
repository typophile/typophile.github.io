This brand was designed for The Principality of Liechtenstein by by Wollf Olins. It includes a crown [img:sites/default/files/old-images/LiechstensteinWithCrown_5189.png] and consists of rows of some of the stages in stages in a morph between a flower and a star[img:sites/default/files/old-images/LiechstensteinElementMorph_6054.png] The flower represents the agrarian roots of the Principality, the intermediate circle is the financial side and the star is industry. It seems there’s a lack of double row dotted fonts with a concentric placements of dots (I see an opportunity for type designers here). I think your best bet to achieve the result is to take an “engraving” font (either single line or two lines) and apply an appropriate brush to its paths. http://www.evilmadscientist.com/2011/hershey-text-an-inkscape-extension-for-engraving-fonts/
