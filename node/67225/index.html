Antonio di Bartolommeo Miscomini (c. 1445–1495) was a Bolognese punchcutter and printer who worked in Venice, Modena and above all Florence, where he cut roman and orthotic Greek types.

[[http://www.flickr.com/photos/bookhistorian/4187284898/|1482 Firenze - Antonius Mischomini, type 3,4]] cc-by History of the Book, Amsterdam ([[http://www.flickr.com/search/?q=Mischomini&w=43719536@N02|more on Flickr]])

I am uncertain if any revivals of his types exist.

Hermann Zapf’s Medici Script takes its name from the famous Florentine family.

While not particular to Florence, you might have a look at some script styles such as Adrian Frutiger’s Ondine, or a rotunda such as [[http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/san-marco/|San Marco]].

Apropos of your first examples of lettering styles: [[http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/urw/florentine/urw-t-regular/|URW Florentine]]
