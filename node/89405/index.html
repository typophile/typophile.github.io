Some strangeness in the world of font sizes

January 24, 2012 • jn22

While inspecting the various fonts in my MS Works, I have discovered that fonts vary in size. I do not understand this. If a very common font is selected, say Arial, and set at 12pt., the characters will be of a given size. When one selects other fonts, the sizes or heights may not correspond. I was under the naive impression that the pt. size defined the height, but this appears not to be the case. Any insight into this phenomenon would be appreciated.

In addition, is there an accepted convention about when one uses the term font instead of typestyle? The two seem to mean the same thing, but I find myself wondering which one to use in discussion.

Which particular dimension of a typeface do you think should be used to determine its size: cap height, x-height, or ascender height?

It’s a common misconception, jn22, that point size has some exact relationship to how a font looks in print. Details: http://www.thomasphinney.com/2011/03/point-size/

I’m not sure what “typestyle” would mean, but if you mean “font” vs “typeface” then I did a survey on that topic: http://www.thomasphinney.com/2009/04/font-terms-survey-results/

Suumary: There is no consensus, but the majority opinion is that in today’s world of digital typography “a typeface” means the general design, including all its styles, regardless of how it’s instantiated, while “a font” means a single style of a typeface, such as Myriad bold condensed italic, in a specific file format.

T
