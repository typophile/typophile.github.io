OK, Here’s what I think:

What you need to aim for is a balance between innerspace (space within— lightblue) and intraspace (space between — pink). Start with the largest of the innerspaces, in this case N or G, and then try to make the intraspaces equal to that. In your sample the S is way to close to the N. Once you have balanced everything you can either substract or add equal amounts of space between the glyphs, depending on what your aiming for (tension, or light colour, etc.).

The other line: Wordpace is excessive. Wordspace equal to the innerspace of N is adequate. The dot should be way closer to the O, and do not line it up with the topline, but let it stick out somewhat.

And try looking at this up-side-down or in a mirror. Hope this helps.

2008-12-01 08:55:20 • Sye

I’m with Nick on this one. There are competing factors in spacing. One is how far apart the closest blacks are. Another is visual equality between the letters. Another is balance between inner and outer. Your first goes for the first one. I think that is OK, if you want compactness, which you might want in a logo. The other way is what Bert said, but if you want to go that way I think you need the S still further away.

As Nick says, the joins on the N may be too heavy. Try slightly reducing the width of the verticals.

On the bottom line the OT looks too wide, but slightly tweaking the whole thing is probably needed for perfect balance. I don’t see why you need the period at all.
