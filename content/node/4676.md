  body: 'Well, in German, Typography can actually be writen one of many diffent ways.
    In his 1998 book, <i>Kurtstexte</i>, Kurt Weidemann writes the following about
    Typo and spelling &#40;please forgive me, this is both a translation and a mental
    summary&#41;: <BR> <BR><i>Tipografie</i> would refer to any kind of typography
    that involves someone typing, i.e., a compositor at his keyboard, or a DTPer,
    but not an author with Microsoft Word &#40;necessarially&#41;. <BR> <BR><i>Typographie</i>
    refers to the New Typography of the 1920s. Mostly because this is how Jan Tschichold
    spelled the word then. <BR> <BR><i>Typografie</i> is the &#34;in&#34; spelling
    of the word a the moment. It is also the correct spelling, according to <i>die
    neue deutsche Rechtschreibung</i>, I think. It implies the contemporary streamlinization
    of Typography, and is spelled just as it sounds &#40;at least, that is what a
    German would tell you!&#41;. <BR> <BR>On an other note, I recently made a poster
    for an architecture company in Frankfurt, in which I wrote out Architecture in
    11 different languages, including Turkish, Arabic, and Simplified Chinese. Unfortunately,
    in all of the European languages, the word <i>Architecture</i> looks about the
    same.  <IMG SRC="http://www.typophile.com/forums/clipart/sad.gif" ALT=":-&#40;"
