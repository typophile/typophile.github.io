2010-01-20 23:05:21 • eliason

I read her obituary this morning and played the album. “Heart Like a Wheel” is such a beautiful song, and their harmony so unique. Thought the cover design (1975) was interesting.

It wouldn’t have been too hard to draw the ampersand with a Rapidograph and circle template, to make the missing character. Other than that, the typography is fine and the spacing nicely done (although such super-tight setting hasn’t come back into fashion – yet). I don’t have a problem with the thickening at the joints, you get the same thing in Helvetica 25, for instance, even though there is some correction. Correction seems appropriate for a grotesque, but not for a geometric.

I was referring to hairline weights. AFAIK, the lightest Futura is the Extra Light by Scangraphics (although there were some even lighter typositor fonts), and it’s interesting to note that it has less correction than the Light. Futura, as generally rendered digitally, is not a pure geometric, i.e. its curves are not circles and arcs of circle. Also of note: Avant Garde Extra Light, with noticeable thickening at the joints.
