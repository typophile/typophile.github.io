March 29, 2007 • ill sans

Hi guys,

I’m currently preparing myself for building my own website (which still might take a while, mind you) & I was thinking about putting useful information on it for graphic designers in general. One thing I wanted to do, is put a RGB/CMYK/Pantone (& possibly RAL) conversion table on it & also a list of fonts which are known under several names. I’m thinking of retro fonts that have now been digitilized (eg. Jackson that is now available from Linotype as Laughin) & also fonts that are cloned and widely spread over the internet. I’ve often experienced (even at work) that we had font clones without even knowing it (eg. we had Arnold Bocklin, but also Horizon which -some kerning aside maybe- is basically the same). I had a few sites that mentioned font clones in my favourites list, but they no longer exist. So if anyone knows of any fonts with several names, please let me know, I’ll be sure to acknowledge you if I ever manage to get my site online ;-)

That could be an awfully long list. Are you planning on including the ripoffs or just renamed digital versions of metal types? I know Nick Curtis has reproduced a number of metal types with new names (eg. Congress/Filibuster).

You need to get your hands on

  1. The Bitstream Font Analogue (1992?) – which listed Bitstream fontnames against the respective standard industry names. This info may be on their website, but I’ve never found it. They tend to rely on the ‘family tree’ view on myfonts.com to provide names of equivalent faces from other foundries.

  2. A Manual of Comparative Typography by Benjamin Bauermeister, 1988, Van Nostrand Reinhold, N.Y. lists many alternative names from other photosetting suppliers for quite a few of the standards.

(Shameless plug!) some of the above information made it into the ‘see also’ sections of the individual font pages at my site about the so-called top 100. So for example, if you look for Akzidenz Grotesk there you’ll see ‘See also; Amsterdam Mercator, Bauer Venus, Stempel Reform Grotesk, Haas Normal Grotesk, Bauer Folio, Linotype Basic Commercial, BT Gothic 725. …and so on. In this particular listing I can say that only the last two are AG under other names from different foundries. Generally, I leave it to the viewer to decide which typefaces are simply close in design and which are rip-off.

I think Daniel Will Harris has something on this at his site too; try starting at http://www.will-harris.com/esperfonto/
