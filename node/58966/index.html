Friendly Rounded Sans fonts

June 12, 2009 • bendy

Hello Typophiles,

We’re having a new logo designed by a local agency and they’ve chosen to put our company name in a custom rounded sans. They said all the stuff you’d expect about a friendly, approachable, modern tone. I wasn’t immediately sold on the idea since many companies these days are opting for roundy sans serif fonts (like Xerox using FS Albert).

So I got to thinking well hey maybe they’re right to choose this kind of font, even if everyone else is trying to be personable and informal, why shouldn’t we too?

Anyway my two questions are:

1 What fonts would you recommend that are slightly rounded, with simple shapes and a one-storey a? The company name is IMA International and I think they’re going to set it with the ima in lowercase and the INTERNATIONAL in uppercase below it.

2 Am I over-worrying about choosing a style that seems to be everywhere or should I be happy that this kind of font does in fact represent our company values?

As always, I look forward to anyone’s suggestions.

Thanks :)

PS Can’t post an image of their home-made type currently but will try to get the pdf from them.

An identity that is too trendy will probably look dated in a couple of years, maybe Bryant with the alternate a fits your bill?
