body: "TypeCon2009 Update - 1/21/09 - Call for Submissions\r\n\r\n\r\nTypeCon2009:
  Rhythm\r\nJuly 14-19, 2009\r\nGrand Hyatt Atlanta at Buckhead\r\nAtlanta, Georgia\r\n\r\npresented
  by The Society of Typographic Aficionados (SOTA)\r\n\r\n-----------------------------------------------------------------\r\nCALL
  FOR SUBMISSIONS FOR PROGRAM CONTENT FOR TYPECON2009\r\n-----------------------------------------------------------------\r\n\r\nThe
  Society of Typographic Aficionados (SOTA) is currently reviewing proposals for contemporary
  and historical presentations and panel discussions, as well as focused hands-on
  workshops in both the digital and non-digital realms. Topics at TypeCon cover a
  broad range of interests, including typography, type design, font production, graphic
  design, new media, printing history, calligraphy and hand lettering, the book arts,
  advertising, type in motion, literacy, type sales and marketing, legal issues, type
  and design education, and other related areas.\r\n\r\nStandard programming formats
  are as follows:\r\n\r\nFull-length presentation - 40 minutes, including time for
  audience Q+A\r\n\r\nType in 20 presentation - 20 minutes, including time for audience
  Q+A (if any)\r\n\r\nType and Design Education Forum - see separate call below\r\n\r\nPanel
  discussion - a minimum of 3 panelists that represent a range of experience and expertise
  in a given topic. An experienced moderator is required for all panels. A moderator
  may also serve as a panelist where appropriate. Panel discussions range from 45-60
  minutes, depending on the topic.\r\n\r\nFull-day workshop - 7 hours (with break
  for lunch at midday)\r\n\r\nHalf-day workshop - 3.5 hours (no break)\r\n\r\nThe
  main program of presentations and panels will run Friday-Sunday, July 17-19. Pre-conference
  workshops will take place Wednesday-Thursday, July 15-16, with tours running throughout
  the week.\r\n\r\nYou may submit multiple proposals.\r\n\r\nIn order to consider
  your proposal, we will need the following:\r\n\r\n- presentation/workshop/tour title\r\n-
  a concise one-paragraph description of your topic (up to 100 words)\r\n- complete
  supporting materials for review - visuals, theses, articles, abstracts - the more
  details you provide, the better\r\n- your biography (100 words maximum)\r\n- speaking
  references (if you have not previously presented at TypeCon)\r\n- a print quality
  headshot in TIFF or JPEG format - must be a minimum of 300 dpi at 2 inches wide\r\n-
  any other material that supports your proposal\r\n\r\nPlease use PDF, RTF, or ASCII
  text for text. Compress all files in a single ZIP archive labeled with your name
  (example: TC09_LastNameFirstName). If your ZIP archive is larger than 20 mbs, please
  send a link for downloading instead of transmitting through email.\r\n\r\nEmail
  proposals to info@typesociety.org by Febuary 20, 2009. The programming committee
  will review all proposals for possible inclusion in the conference schedule.\r\n\r\n\r\n-----------------------------------------------------------------\r\nCALL
  FOR SPEAKERS FOR THE TYPECON2009 TYPE AND DESIGN EDUCATION FORUM\r\n-----------------------------------------------------------------\r\n\r\nThe
  Society of Typographic Aficionados (SOTA) will present its fourth Type and Design
  Education Forum as part of TypeCon2009. This special program will be held on Thursday,
  July 16, at the Grand Hyatt Atlanta in Buckhead. The forum will be devoted to addressing
  issues faced by type educators and the institutions they represent. The program
  will feature presentations in conjunction with breakout sessions dedicated to the
  open exchange of ideas and experiences.\r\n\r\nWe are looking for presenters in
  the following areas:\r\n\r\nAssignment Presentations\r\n15-minute presentations
  of effective and stimulating assignments that generate a passion for type:\r\n-
  Basic skill sets/foundations of type/typography\r\n- Basic lettering/letterform
  design\r\n- Type history/typographic history\r\n- Experimentation with type\r\n\r\nWe
  are looking for facilitators for:\r\n\r\nBreakout Sessions\r\nThere will be a general
  session consisting of small breakout discussions. We are looking for people to facilitate
  discussions on the following topics:\r\n- Curriculum development for junior/community
  colleges\r\n- Curriculum development for schools with 1\u20132 type classes\r\n-
  Curriculum development for design programs with 3\u20134 type classes\r\n- Curriculum
  development for liberal arts programs with 3\u20134 type classes\r\n- Teaching type/typography
  basics\r\n- Teaching letterform design\r\n- Font selection: how to teach students
  to do font explorations, and make appropriate choices\r\n- Strategies for more effective
  critiques\r\n- Teaching calligraphy as a means to developing an appreciation of
  letterforms\r\n- Experimentation in typography\r\n- Type in motion\r\n- Interactive/web
  type\r\n- Multi-lingual typography\r\n\r\nWe are considering adding a new component
  to the Education Forum this year - a series of hands-on workshops designed specifically
  for educators. Workshops will run approximately 3 hours, and should be geared toward
  smaller groups (15-25 participants per session). If you have an idea for a workshop
  for educators and would like to lead your peers, please submit a proposal with supporting
  documentation. We are open to all ideas for this exciting new opportunity.\r\n\r\nSubmissions\r\nProposals
  should contain your biography/resume/CV, your area(s) of interest/specialty, a description
  of your proposed presentation and/or breakout session, and a statement about why
  you\u2019d like to participate. Please include a speaking reference if you have
  not previously presented at a TypeCon event.\r\n\r\nPlease send all submissions
  to Ilene Strizver and Nancy Rorabaugh, SOTA Education Committee chairs, at education@typecon.com.
  Deadline for submissions is February 20, 2009.\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n-----------------------------------------------------------------\r\nABOUT
  TYPECON AND SOTA\r\n-----------------------------------------------------------------\r\nTypeCon2009
  is the annual conference of SOTA, an international non-profit organization dedicated
  to the promotion, study, and support of type design, typography, and the related
  arts. For more information about SOTA, please visit the website at http://www.typesociety.org.\r\n\r\nHave
  questions, need answers? Send us an email! mailto:info@typesociety.org.\r\n\r\nSee
  you in Atlanta!\r\n_________________________________________________________________\r\n\r\n"
