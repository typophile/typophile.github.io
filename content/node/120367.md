  body: '[[http://www.myfonts.com/search/brush+script|Brush Script]] came to mind
    first but mainly for /H and /C/u/t part. A decent solution to consider it as 2
    different letterings, a brush script for /H+/C/u/t and a different script for
    /a/n/d/i. FT [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/filmotype/keynote|Keynote]] might
    work well for the /a/n/d/i part but that''s a total different story for /H/C/u/t.
    Some more interesting ones: Filmotype [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/filmotype/lasalle|Lasalle]],
    [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/filmotype/filmotype-leader|Leader]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/filmotype/lucky|Lucky]]
    and <strong>[[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/filmotype/kitten|Kitten]]</strong>
    (I like this one even if too skinny), [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/mika-melvas/sanelma|Sanelma]],
    [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/typejockeys/sauber-script|Sauber]] (works well
    for /a/n/d/i), [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/sideshow/coffee-service|Coffee Service]],
