[[Indices]] : [[Terminology]] : [[Terminal]] : Ball Terminal

A circle-shaped device used to terminate a [[Stroke|stroke]], for example on the end of the arm on r, or the [[Tail|tail]] tip of y. Ball terminals are a prominent feature of so-called “modern roman” types which came into existence in the second half of the 18th century with the appearance of [[Didone|Didones]] and types used by the [[Fournier|Fourniers]] as well as [[Giambattista Bodoni]]. Ball terminals are typically drawn in a strict geometric form of a pure circle. Some types however, principally [[John Baskerville|Baskerville’s]] [[roman type]], feature organically-shaped ball terminals.

Ball Terminals join the stroke at an angle, as opposed to [[Lachrymal Terminal]]s which taper into the stroke.
