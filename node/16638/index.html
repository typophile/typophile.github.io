What strategy do you use to find example or test words for languages you don't speak/read? Supposing you want to test your font with a ő, or Ŋ, or ħ. How do you like to test these? I have started using google to find pages that use glyphs I am unfamilar with and I found this: http://www.websters-online-dictionary.org/definition/Maltese-english/ Which showed me ħamster ( hampster in english). But even if I do this for each glyph that is unfamiliar to me I suspect that I may miss atypical or challenging combinations of glyphs that I might know about if I spoke or read the langauge. And/or I may favor typical use in one language over the other. This site is a great references but it don't supply very much insight into context & use of glyphs http://www.eki.ee/letter/ Happily there is some terrific help from these two sites where Icelandic & Polish is concerned: http://briem.ismennt.is/ http://www.twardoch.com/download/polishhowto/intro.html But are there other sites? Does anybody have a stock of strategies, test words/phrases they might be willing to talk about & or share? Thanks!

Resurrecting this thread only to mention and bring up new discussion perhaps on unicode.org's availability of the UDHR as text for the oodles of languages for which it has been translated. Since most of the versions also have a PDF version available, you can test a font with the text version to compare with the "actual version" to test look for display issues, which might be more important for connected scripts (Arabic) or those who are not stored in the same order they are displayed (Indic). But, of course, it gives all of the text, but with slightly more assurances on proper encoding, since it is done by the Unicode Consortium. «El futuro es una línea tan fina que apenas nos damos cuenta de pintarla nosotros mismos». (La Luz Oscura, por Javier Guerrero)
