Most foundries doesn’t just do U&lc + numbers and italic U&lc + numbers. They are going to work on the whole family simultaneously. Nowadays, that includes at least all CE accents, plus some more stuff.

2005-06-27 05:54:03 • dan_reynolds

There’s a difference between finishing a font [enough] to sell to users, versus demonstrating type design affinity -via a font- to a prospective employer. In the latter case, completeness is relevant only to the extent that it facilitates evaluation of your skills. So for example instead of spending X days to finish the smallcaps, it’s smarter to spend part of that time doing some of the smallcaps and the rest of that time maybe showing good sensitivity towards extended-Latin accent placement. Now, you do want to totally finish the alphanumerics, and punctuation, and you do want to deliver finished base spacing, but you might forsake fully kerning UC-UC pairs for example, in favor of maybe showing your hinting or OpenType abilities, or making a prototype display/text cut of your base design, or maybe a smaller optical size, etc.

So the question -a really interesting and useful one- becomes: What are the discrete affinities that a type designer needs to have/show?
