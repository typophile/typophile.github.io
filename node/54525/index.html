I agree. It doesn’t look bad, but it doesn’t have the sort of distinctiveness yet that makes logos memorable, and makes them a cogent visualization —and voice— of the institutions they represent.

What I do when I start out with a new logo is try to find out what “tone” it should have, in what kind of a voice it should speak: loud or soft? Strong or smooth? Professional or fun? Like Frode said: What makes this church unique, what is its voice? Making a list of adjectives can help in defining the desired “feel” of the logo (and of the font used along with it).

BTW, I was wondering if this is some sort of group of three churches / religions, one large and two small, as symbolized by the three crosses? (I don’t see a church there, sorry :-| ) In a logo, everything has meaning; question every element, and leave only those in that need to be there.

Also, it would probably help to leave out color and gradients for now. Working in black & white only forces you to look for treatments that are original in a truly “graphic” way, and that also work in media/situations where no color can be used (the famous fax, inter alia); and once you work the color back in after you’ve found a formal solution that works, it will enhance it even more.
