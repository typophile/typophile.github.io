Sorry, the text is too small and low res to positively ID (the letters need to be at least 100 px tall and a decent resolution to run through visual database search). The |J| is pretty distinctive (first line), and resembles Life (released in 1965): http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/bitstream/life/

And you’re right, Nimbus is close, but not quite. In fact, it’s from the wrong era (1980s), and is actually just a clone of Times New Roman (but it was the closest hit on database searches). Given that your sample is from 1974, we’re dealing with a metal or phototypesetting version of Times Bold Condensed that may have been distinctive to the European market. And that might never have been digitized.

You can find a similar |f| in Madison Antiqua (originally release in 1965) and its Bitstream version, Century 725, and in Romana: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/madison-antiqua/ http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/bitstream/century-725/ http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/urw/romana/

There is a special German version of Times Roman with somewhat lighter capitals, but it doesn’t have a special J.

Looking on Identifont, I encountered [[http://www.identifont.com/show?GII|Le Monde Livre]] which, although similar, does not seem to be a match.
