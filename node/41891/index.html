I think the Wiki may be referring to changes in the digital font from the original metal version. I believe Herr Zapf did/does his work for the Linotype foundry, so there is no difference. "Palatino Linotype" is just the way the font file is named -- it is still Hermann Zapf's design. - Mike Yanega
2008-02-10 03:25:08 • bowfinpw
In the Linotype catalog there is a Palatino, which was the version sold by Adobe based on the Linotype design, and Palatino Linotype, which is the purely Linotype digitization of the same font (both based on the 1950 original metal version). There is also the 2005 Palatino Nova, which Zapf updated with Akira Kobiyashi. - Mike Yanega

Early digitizations took great care to minimize computerprocessing for the user. Hence the loss of subtle curves like in the stems of Optima (and probably a lot of other fonts…). Since processing power is no issue anymore, foundries are putting back the subtleties (and making more money of these workhorses as well). . . . Bert Vanderveen BNO
