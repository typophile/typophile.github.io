Looking for a left-hended stressed typeface

July 23, 2014 • Scalfin

Maybe this is the wrong place for it, but I’m wondering if anyone has ever seen a typeface where the oblique stress is ninety degrees different from where it is in most typefaces, running from top right to bottom left instead of top left to bottom right, thereby evoking a pen held with the left hand rather than the right. I’m looking for both serif and cursive typefaces to possibly be used in a wedding invitation to add a personal touch.
Having a look to fonts tagged ‘[[http://www.myfonts.com/search/tag%3Abackslant|backslant]]’ on MyFonts might be helpful. [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/kursivschrift|Kursivschrift]] Rueckwaerts and [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/roemisch|Roemisch]] Rueck are the one I know. Some Sudtipos might work too: [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/sudtipos/seashore-pro|Seashore]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/sudtipos/miss-packgope|Miss Packgope]] and [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/sudtipos/miss-blaker|Miss Blaker]]
