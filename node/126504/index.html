Smallest Size on Fax

July 20, 2017 • ellen shapiro

I’m an expert witness in a case involving legibility of type that comes through on faxes. FCC rules state that type in disclaimers and opt-out notices must be “clear and conspicuous.” We are trying to define that. In this particular case the type was set in 8 pt Univers 45 (light), but it came through almost 10% smaller – and blurry – making it very difficult to read in the fax. Are there any published guidelines that state the smallest a typeface should be to come through clearly and conspicuously in a fax? Thank you!

Hi Ellen, I worked for several years – among others with Albert-Jan Pool, who is also active here – on a DIN standard about readability. It’s not a binding rule but official recommendations including minimal requirements for font sizes, stroke thickness, width, design etc. to make type readable in different circumstances. We distinguish between recommendations for signage, consultational texts (like what you are talking about, fine print, captions, footnotes etc) and body copy for longer reading. It’s a German standard with the number DIN 1450, not translated into English or taken over into ISO (as far as I know) but maybe this could provide a trustworthy argument in court?
