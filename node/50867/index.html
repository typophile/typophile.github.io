Regarding Polish diacritics all but Ł and ł (uc and lc slashed L) are really well drawn, which is not common. Without Ł and ł (uc and lc slashed L) you cannot typeset texts in Polish language. There’s also historical and rare ligature combining to lc “łs” [[http://typografia.ogme.pl/images/stories/podstawy/rys_roz_2/Ligatury_stadardowe3.gif|link to example of ligature łł]]. BTW very interesting typeface.

2008-10-31 23:06:52 • Urquell

The typeface looks quite interesting. The important omitted glyphs are as follows: Idotaccent (uni0130) and dotlessi (uni0131) (Turkish, discussed [[http://typophile.com/node/13570|here]]) Tcommaaccent and tcommaaccent (without Unicode reference, needed for Romanian) Eng (uni014A) and eng (uni014B) (needed for rare languages like Sami and some African languages)

If becoming punctual, there are also 4 optional glyphs you should add, although in many typefaces they are not present - [[http://typophile.com/node/35221|Aringacute/aringacute]] and Oslashacute/oslashacute (although I’m too lazy to check the Unicode points for them).

Also from WGL4 maybe you should add kgreenlandic (uni0131) and napostrophe (uni0149), although I haven’t seen them in real use.

The matter of adding CE charset is discussed in more detail [[http://www.typophile.com/node/2764|here]].

Now about the quirks: I have some doubts regarding the ogonek placement in iogonek (Roman). Otherwise the diacritics placement looks ok to me.

2008-11-05 20:21:09 • Michael Wallner

Aringacute and Oslashacute are almost obsolete, although still are used in some Danish grammars, dictionaries and other linguistic texts, perhaps also in some poetry (quoting John Hudson from [[http://typophile.com/node/35221|here]]). kgreenlandic (aka kra)and napostrophe is almost extinct, but I still see them often implemented in many typefaces. They are described in this [[http://www.evertype.com/alphabets/greenlandic.pdf|PDF]] by Michael Everson. Implementing it possibly would require to add also a specific Greenlandic uppercase K, as described [[http://typophile.com/node/35971|here]].

About the lack of Unicode reference of Tcommaaccent the mistake is mine (in my old FontLab codepages I found only the glyph name, not Unicode values). Should have checked more thoroughly.

None of these glyphs are in heavy use; I mention them only because they still exist in [[http://www.microsoft.com/typography/otspec/WGL4.htm|WGL4 specifications]] (i.e. Microsoft cosider them still not totally extinct).
