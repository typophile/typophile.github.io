Toby,

there are several rules, which you can follow:

  1. look for good equipped typefaces, type families (who have sans & serif cuts)
  2. the mixed fonts should be different enough to be recognized for the untrained eye
  3. try to avoid mixing a sans with another sans, or a serif with another serif
  4. try to match fonts which have a almost similar x-height, so that a mixture in body copy doesn’t look to strange and fit harmonious
  5. look what good typographers do, because there are as many opinions, as designers
  6. use the search function here on typophile (could be the first rule)
  7. look for the links at the and of this thread: [[http://typophile.com/node/38882]]
