There is (was) a lot of hand work on that particular piece. In fact, if that was a piece for the United Nations in 1970, chances are that Lubalin, Rondthaler or Burns had a hand in it. In any event, it was not difficult to create and modify one-offs, in fact, quite easy for the likes of Tom Carnase and Tony DiSpigna: About 1969, designer Roger Ferriter and letterer Tom Carnase devised a wordmark for a line of hosiery to be called L’eggs. The same expressive quality in that mark appeared in Carnase’s titling face Busorama, a spinoff from a bus advertising promotion. Both Carnase and fellow lettering designer Tony DiSpigna became known for their exuberant scripts. From Peter Bain: Film Type, Friends of St Bride Printing Library, second annual conference.
