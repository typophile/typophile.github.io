Phonetic Terminals

November 6, 2009 • anapbm

Many characters in IPA, apparently, have the same terminals, but the terminals have different names. For example:

LATIN SMALL LETTER D WITH TAIL LATIN SMALL LETTER N WITH RETROFLEX HOOK

My question is: is there any difference in their aspect, proportions or design that should be considered in a new typography design? Or they are really the same with another name?

As far as I can tell, the proper name of the attachment is ‘hook’. ‘Tail’ is a description, just like “i-bar”, “h with stroke”, “turned h”, “small capital R”, “turned y”, and lots of others, rather than the mouthful “ulveolar front labial trill” they describe. Font creators are not that good in phonetics.

(On the other hand, I suspect the guys at IPA sometimes just make up characters as they write. “Anyone got dibs on a mirrored small capital upside down R? No? Okay, it’s mine!")

2009-11-06 21:05:37 • Theunis de Jong

You might be interested in [[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naming_conventions_of_the_International_Phonetic_Alphabet|this Wikipedia article on IPA naming conventions]]. The Unicode Consortium’s name for a given character or feature doesn’t always agree with the IPA’s name.

The “tail” and “hook” are indeed the same thing (the feature that indicates retroflexion); you will probably need at least two versions, one for letters like ɖ and ɳ where the tail is an extension of a stem or leg, and another for ʂ and ʐ where the tail is an appendage to a horizontal stroke. If you need more fonts with IPA characters for reference, you could also look at Gentium, Charis SIL, Doulos SIL, and Andika Basic, all available from [[http://scripts.sil.org/cms/scripts/page.php?site_id=nrsi&cat_id=FontDownloads|SIL]].

Nice to hear you’re putting IPA characters in your font!

They’re both used to represent [[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Retroflex|retroflex]] consonants in IPA.The old name of [[http://www.fileformat.info/info/unicode/char/0256/index.htm|ɖ]] was “LATIN SMALL LETTER D RETROFLEX HOOK”. I guess it was changed to “tail” to avoid confusion with [[http://www.fileformat.info/info/unicode/char/0257/index.htm|ɗ]], “LATIN SMALL LETTER D WITH HOOK”.

I would add that this seems to be a dispute between descriptions based on appearance (hook) and on function (retroflex). If the possibility exists, I’d always prefer a functional description, but I don’t run the place, and there’s a lot of history involved.

I know we love our computers, but I would strongly advise buying a used copy of Phonetic Symbol Guide by Pullum et al.
