2011-04-30 02:45:47 • dezcom

How dry transfer lettering is manufactured (serigraphy) is not the printing medium. The typography was done by rubbing onto paper. Sure, it was then photographed for film assembly, but that wasn’t typography either, and type imaged by any medium (e.g. letterpress) was also photographed for film assembly.

Similarly, it could be argued that a slightly earlier method (1930s) of typesetting is also a unique category—I refer to the assembly of letters pre-printed on transparent adhesive film. This product was still available in the 1980s, but I forget its name. One used a scalpel blade to cut a single “b” (for instance) from a group of b’s on their backing sheet, peel it off, and position it on the knock-out blue baseline one had drawn on the artboard.
