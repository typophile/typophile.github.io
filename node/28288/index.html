Why all the Slabs?

September 17, 2006 • MO ed.

I’m a violinist who is also a self-publisher, and I recently asked a friend of mine (a book designer) “What’s up with all the slab fonts everywhere?” He suggested that I contact this forum. So that’s what I’m doing.

It seems that I started to see them a few years back when Martha Stewart LIVING commissioned its slab, Archer. Ever since then, slabs seem to be popping up more and more. What’s it all about? Was Martha Stewart an instigator–THE instigator–in this? Or was she picking up on a trend she saw coming. Because slabs are very now.

In the present era of new genres such as deconstructed, techno, pixel, distressed, etc., and with the dominance of the sans serif, the subtle distinctions between historic serif genres (old style, transitional, didone) have become relatively unimportant, and the more extreme variants such as slabs have assumed greater typographic significance for art directors.

Archer may have been influential, due to MSL use. However, TheSerif (1994) was well used editorially before Archer, and Clarendon began to return to favor ten years ago. I designed an “old style' slab serif type, Bodoni Egyptian, in 1999, it seemed like a niche that needed filling.

2006-09-26 20:20:54 • hrant

Chapparal is probably the only slab that has optical sizes.

This begs the question of how a slab is defined:

  1. Squared-off structure.
  2. Thickness.

Many didones could be considered a slab according to 1. And many thick-serifed faces with wedge serifs (eg TheSerif} or bracketed serifs (eg Clarendon) fit definition 2.

Bauer and Berthold Bodoni at display size are very slabbish, whereas ITC Bodoni, never is, even the small text size set big. I have used the Bauer and Berthold in combo as an “optically-scaled” Bodoni.

I found that Scala was a good text face up to about 9 pt., then it started to get a bit clunky. Of course, that was 10 years ago, and my experience might be different now, what with different glasses, the march of fashion, and my own changing tastes and ways of setting type.
