2004-10-30 17:11:39 • dan_reynolds
Verdana is far more readable on screen, both in terms of letter shapes and spacing. Arial was a print font that was adapted for screen use. Verdana was designed for screen use from the ground up. With regards to letter shapes, Arial's relatively closed counters for shapes like a, c, and e make those letters less distinctive than they could be at typical on-screen text sizes. Try typing a sentence. Set it in 9 pt Arial and again right below in 9 pt Verdana. Copy those two lines, and do them in 10, 11 and 12 pt. View them on-screen at regular 100% zoom. Which text looks more readable to you across the range of sizes. Note: If you have a Mac, also use 14 and 16 pt. If you have Windows, also use 7 and 8 pt. This will help compensate for the different ppem ratios of the two OSes in normal applications. Cheers, T
