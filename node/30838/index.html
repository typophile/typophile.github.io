I really like the way the rounded serif of the angled right ascender closely echoes the counter space created in the angled right descender. I may just alter the character by hand to create a true equality in the shapes of the two. I don’t know if that helps you any. :-)
2007-01-24 03:23:32 • rfujiimoto

Your best chance to get that sort of symmetry is to look for fonts where the legs of the K meet on the stem, instead of on the upper leg, as in Baskerville. you will also need a full foot serif on the lower leg.

Examples: New Aster Bold Italic; Cushing Italic; Janson Italic and Glypha Italic (for a slab type). These are just examples. Having the legs roughly equal in weight also helps reinforce the symmetry of the counters, I think.
