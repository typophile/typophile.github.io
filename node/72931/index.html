This isn’t the most helpful sample - it would have been better served at its original size. However, I can tell this much: it’s almost certainly based on Trade Gothic or Alternate Gothic (based on the R and S), and the M is most likely an inverted W. It would appear to be a unique font, however, given that the Fs and Ss are the same size and position relative to the baseline. Looking at the darker pixels leads me to think that it’s a grungy treatment of either of these fonts, but DaFont, AbstractFonts and MyFonts reveal nothing - although there are plenty of alternatives that would achieve the same effect.
