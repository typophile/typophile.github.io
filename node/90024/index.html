2012-02-13 23:53:20 • David Vereschagin

With an imposition of 16 pages logic dictates that the ‘compensation’ should be n*x, where n is the paper thickness and x is the order of the spread in relation to the inner one.

Fun aside — any printer worth his/her salt will have impo-software that compensates automatically. Not something a designer has to worry about.

2012-02-14 14:09:49 • Bert Vanderveen

Or find a published book with similar trim size, page count, paper stock, and binding method and use it as a poor-man’s bound dummy.

Take a print-out of your sample page, trim off a little extra along the gutter margin, and insert the page so that the fore-edge is flush. Then note where your inside margin appears to fall. Adjust to taste. When it looks good, it is good.

2012-02-14 14:20:58 • kentlew
You have to talk to your printer about this (assuming you are having it printed, and this isn’t a one-off laser job). It will depend on how the job is printed, folded, and imposed. The rule that one printer gives you (or that you get on a discussion board) will be completely wrong for another printer using different equipment and methods.
