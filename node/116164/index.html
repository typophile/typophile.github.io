It looks like Dorovar Carolus by Casady & Greene [1988-91] According to Luc Devroye, the company is permanently out of business since July 2003 and the fonts are now probably in the public domain:

On July 3, 2003, Casady&Greene closed it doors permanently. However, one of its designers, Mike Wright, writes: I believe that all the fonts that were developed by the company are now in the public domain. Robin Casady and I are thinking of putting up a site with free downloads of all of the old C&G public domain fonts--mainly as a way of attracting Mac users to see iData 2.

http://luc.devroye.org/fonts-27509.html

The actual font used in your design could be a clone of Dorovar Carolus called [[http://www.findmyfont.com/index.php/fonts/font-preview?fset=Dafont-1&ffam=Carolingia%20-%20Regular&fid=4565451cb158af1f074941981c6b9cf8&fsize=60&text=Salon%20Styles%20By%20Glenda&wrap=2|Carolingia]] by William Boyd [available as a free font at Dafont.com]
