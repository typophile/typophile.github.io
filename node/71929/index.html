The typeface in this clipping is from the invitation to the opening of the Rice Institute in Houston, Texas. It was printed between 1911-1912.

The closest thing I’ve found is Plantin, but the distinctive D doesn’t match that or any other face I’ve ever seen…

Help, please?

Doubtless it’s a metal typeface that never got revived: the T is really clumsy, and the lack of overshoot on the C and S don’t help, either…

There were several different similar faces from different foundries. The original was a German face called Romanische Antiqua (1888). This looks like it was one of the imitations.
