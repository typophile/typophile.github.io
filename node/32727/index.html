What have Frutiger's typefaces been used in?

April 1, 2007 • Brett Stabler

Can anyone help me? I am trying to list as many different ways in which Adrian Frutigers typefaces have been used.(for a student project.) By “used” I mean adopted in brand identities, signage systems, etc. What i have so far includes; Avenir's use in the visual identity of Amsterdam and Univers's use in the Paris Metro, Montreal Metro and numerous airports. Any use will be of help but the quirkiest the better! Thank you
Frutiger (1975) was made specifically for signage at the Paris-Roissy Airport (for a reference refer to Bringhurst's The Elements of Typographic Style, p. 257). And according to wiki: [Frutiger] was commissioned in 1968 by the newly built Charles De Gaulle International Airport at Roissy, France, which needed a new directional sign system. Instead of using one of his previously designed typefaces like Univers, Frutiger chose to design a new one. The new typeface, originally called Roissy, was completed in 1975 and installed at the airport the same year.

It replicates Andys point about the Fruti airport usage, but part of the problem you face with this is that a) Frutiger is prolific – lots of examples from lots of typefaces b) many corporate brand identities may have used the 'unquirky' Frutiger faces as a starting point, but will have tweaked them in development and application c) Linotype doesn't appear to be trumpeting the 'quirky' aspects of Frutiger's work. I know that Meridien and Univers were used for corporate IDs for banks here (in NZ). Univers Condensed Thin and Ultra get used for film poster credit lines (as Stephen Coles has pointed out elsewhere on typophile).
