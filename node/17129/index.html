Calypso is also a very modernist font. Curved surfaces, “streamlined” shapes, ovals, eggs, etc. are very typical modernist design features. Think of: Saarinen, Courrèges, Noguchi, Moore, Expo ’58 in Brussels, Möbius Strip, and… oh, Barbarella.

Yeah it’s tacky, but bad taste is sooo trendy, ennit?

Since we’re discussing the ad in depth, here’s more of a formal critique.

  1. Inadequate product demonstration. It’s a missed opportunity to show how the tonal detail of the font is achieved: the ad square is the one place at Myfonts where there’s space to show detail – so it would have been good to show this sexy feature of the font up close, by putting one large glyph in the position presently occupied by the alphabet showing. So the viewer can get a better sense of the halftone scale in relation to the type height.

  2. Inconsistent use of colour. The all-white type is a bit too flat and cold for the saturated background and image. Some yellow would have been better, a big yellow C perhaps.

  3. Rhetorical mismatch of image and text. The image does not symbolize product features: She’s not dancing; the calypso is a dance, and the typeface dances – but the woman is in a static pose.
