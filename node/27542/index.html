Naming/sorting the family

August 9, 2006 • Goran Soderstrom

I’m a little confused on how to name / sort this font. The family consist of this:

Regular • Regular Italic • Regular Inline • Regular Inline Italic Bold • Bold Italic • Bold Inline • Bold Inline Italic Light • Light Italic • Light Inline • Light Inline Italic

Should I make three “head of families” like this:

Xxxxx Regular Xxxxx Bold Xxxxx Light

and then the variations

Xxxxx Regular – Italic – Inline – Inline Italic

Xxxxx Bold – Italic – Inline – Inline Italic

etc.

OR is it better to name the font with one “head of family name” so it becomes like this:

Xxxxxxx – Regular – Regular Italic – Regular Inline – Regular Inline Italic – Bold – Bold Italic … etc.

Thanks in advance for advices.

“Regular Italic” sounds confusing. The word “Regular” is normally omitted on the Regular weights. I’d name this way:

MyTypeface – Light – Light Italic – Light Inline – Light Italic Inline + – Regular – Italic – Inline – Inline Italic + – Bold – Bold Italic – Bold Inline – Bold Italic Inline

It’s a matter of taste. Maybe make the Inline variation a separate family? It look complete.

‘Regular Italic’ – this looks unfamiliar, but it makes sure that in applications which sort style names alphabetically, ‘Regular’ and ‘Regular Italic’ are next to each other, instead of ‘Italic’ somewhere on top of the list of styles, and Regular near the end. (This applies to the OT Style Name [ID17] only, not to Style Name [ID2] which is expected to be ‘Italic’!)
