Totally agree with Renko, most probably a custom job. Plus, really don’t know any Latin Tuscan Condensed such like this. As a sidenote, “Lionel” custom type and main woodmark differ also a bit: x-height, latin serif position (/L, /E, /N…). They surely share the same design backbone though, one derived from the other. They may have started creating main woodmark (possibly as described below) then extend this 5-letters job to an alphabet. If I have to reproduce it, I’d probably start from one of these following condensed sans, adding latin (triangular) serifs and median spurs: [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/flat-it/dharma-gothic|Dharma Gothic]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/flat-it/revolution-gothic|Revolution Gothic]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/flat-it/house-of-cards|House of Cards]] (serifs need to be tweaked), [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/flat-it/sheepman|Sheepman]] (possibly lacking a bit of height but latin serifs are already here, just needs to add median spurs), [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/woodentypefonts/teniers|Teniers]] (same work as House of Cards), [[http://www.letterheadfonts.com/fonts/prince.php|Prince]], [[http://www.letterheadfonts.com/fonts/bootcamp.php|Boot Camp]] Spurs, [[http://www.dafont.com/face-off-m54.font|Face Off M54]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/t26/tempus-gothic|Tempus Gothic]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/coniglio/nomad|Nomad]], [[http://www.letterheadfonts.com/fonts/unionthug.php|Union Thug]], [[http://www.typography.com/fonts/tungsten/overview|Tungsten]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/gatf/alfrere-sans|Alfrere Sans]], [[http://www.antreposhop.com/product/public-gothic-square|Public Gothic Square]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/a2/beckett|Beckett A2]]

Side spurs were a popular 19th century way to ornament a font. “Latin” fonts – where the serifs are triangular – were also popular. This particular combination of Latin serifs and spurs is unusual. To duplicate, I would follow the same procedure as Ryuk; choose a squared sans of similar weight and add Latin serifs and spurs. The closest squared serif that I was able to dig out is Geogrotesque Bold by Emtype. Don
