2004-02-04 15:19:26 • franz
Making Love looks like Parade Script from Panache Typography (FontHaus) that has been stretched taller. This is one of a series of brush lettering scripts that were film type that were digitized. They have found their way into a variety of font packs of widely varying quality. Panache has some of the better digitizations of these, but you might have had a version in another package that was a renamed version of Parade that was taller (to make it look different). These things happen, especially when you buy bargain font packages.
2004-02-04 15:29:40 • bowfinpw
Here are three simulations of 'Making Love'. The first is Parade Script from Panache (used to be available through Agfa when I got it).

The Second is 'Fairchild' from an Expresiv Fonts Brush Script Collection, which has at least half a dozen closely related styles that were digitizations of film types. In some cases the width/height/weight were varied and serifs were added or removed, round or straight, but with very similar letterforms underneath. (Page 92 in the Dover Solotype Catalog shows a flock of these phototypes, including 'Orlando', that I think resembles 'Fairchild'). The baselines also were shifted (called Bounce variations), and you can see some of that between Parade and Fairchild.

The third simulation is Parade Script with 'squooshing'. Using the special lenses with film type essentially gave many of the image manipulation capabilities we play with in our graphics programs, so you could have had any typeface squeezed or stretched to order.

I know this diverts us from your question, but I thought the permutations of the lettering artists' work that will live on gives an interesting perspective to see this aspect of our digital 'culture'.

2004-02-04 19:01:06 • easytheatre
Engela, you don't need a special version of a font to get tighter spacing. All graphics and layout programs allow you to change the spacing of any font using kerning. Check the manual for your program to find out how to do it. They also will allow you to shift characters up and down. Unless you are trying to do your graphics in a non-graphics program (Word, for instance) you should be able to get whatever effect you want.
