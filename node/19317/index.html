i don’t personally like it when people refer to designers as graphics people (or graphics design, or graphics/design)…it rubs me the wrong way because the only people who i’ve heard use it, usually don’t know much about this amazingly powerful and influential field.

the plural “graphics” sort of commoditizes what we do as if we are the makers of some sort of widget or something.

“i’ll get this to my graphics guy, and he’ll whip something up in no time (and not much cost)”

although i wish there were another term, graphic design (singular) is preferable in this case because it has more to do with visual communication than with simply producing “graphics”. graphic design is a profound practice that affects everyone’s lives, and it just bugs me that we don’t get the respect and prestige we deserve…

it probably isn’t that big of a deal to you, but i live and breath this stuff…

Hear hear. I for one am glad to have cut my teeth before the computer revolution. Because type was so much more expensive back then and mistakes/changes so costly, you were required to think much more conceptually from the outset, rather than just sitting down and messing around until you have something you like - or throwing more and more fonts at it until you have a huge mess on your hands.
