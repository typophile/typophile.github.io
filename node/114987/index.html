Lettering. The only font I know of that comes anywhere close to the big drop swoosh S is Vulpa, which has it as an alternate italic S. It also has an italic swash D as an alternate. Here they are together with the regular lower case [img:sites/default/files/old-images/VulpaItalicSwashRegLCreducedscale_4435.jpg] The lower case is shown at 80% to approximate the label. You could get the effect by using a smaller point size for the lower case than for the capitals. http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/schizotype/vulpa/ Don
