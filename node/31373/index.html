One lookup many rules vs. many lookups

I am developing an Arabic based font. As a type design I have stored dots separate form base glyph. The dots are joined to base glyph using “mark” feature.

The base glyph of beh(U+0628) may have about 6 different kind of dots: three (1dot, 2dots, 3dots) below the base glyph and three above the base glyph to make the shapes of beh(628), yeh(6CC), peh(67E) and noon(646), teh(62A) theh(62B) respectively.

Base glyph of seen(633) may have one kind of dot (3dots) above to make a shape of sheen(634)

Similarly base glyph of sad(635) may have one kind of dot (1dot) above to make a shape of dad(636)

Now I have two solutions for positioning these dots using “mark” feature.

Solution 1: One Lookup

Name “AboveDots” Position First———-Position Second BEH———————1dot SEEN——————–2dots SAD ——————–3dots

Solution 2: Three Lookups

Name “AboveDots for BEH” Position First Position Second BEH———————1dot ————————2dots ————————3dots

Name “AboveDots for SEEN” Position First———-Position Second SEEN——————–3dots

Name “AboveDots for SAD” Position First———-Position Second SAD———————1dot

In the 1st solution the combinations of SEEN – 1dot, SEEN – 2dots, SAD – 2dots and SAD – 3dots are not required. Will this be effect the efficiency?

In the 2nd solution the Uniscribe may need to consult 3 lookup; would you please comment on the tradeoff among above two solutions? Would you please suggest which will be more appropriate and efficient? Thanks

Best regards, Atif
