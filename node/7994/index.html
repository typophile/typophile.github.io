2004-12-05 17:57:03 • metadox
According to Mac McGrew in American Typefaces of the Twentieth Century, Comstock originated from the Inland typefoundry in 1902 and was later revived by ATF in 1957. It used to be commonly available from sources like VGC and rub-down type libraries.

I think it became obsolete (along with outline styles of fonts) because it's so easy to simulate this style with digital type and graphics software.

I presume the generic term derives from the typeface name.
