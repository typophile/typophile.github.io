2007-01-17 16:02:08 • bowfinpw

First used in 1906 in Waldmar Zachrisson’s “WZ:s boktryckerikskalender 1905-1906”. Only oine grade/size was ready by then. Took 2 years to make. Versiosn for Intertype and Typograph was also made. In Germany known as Genzsch Antikva.

Inspiration = Plantin

There’s almost half a chapter about this typeface.

According to this book “Nordische Antiqua” is a new cut made by Linotype in 1920, BUT not of the Nordisk Antikva, but of Deberny & Cie’s “Romain no 16” imported by Genzsch & Heyse in 1882 (and then they did some rework on it).'
