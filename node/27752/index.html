The Plough: English Pub Logotype Critique

August 18, 2006 • wormwood

The new owners of a 15th century English country pub and restaurant have asked me to rebrand it in a way that reflects both its obvious traditional qualities and the fresh contemporary atmosphere they hope to foster.

I’ve taken the brief quite literally with a kind of humanist? neo gothic script hybrid type treatment.

I’m not sure if i have succeeded, so your criticims would be greatly appreciated.

Give me your worst you crazy typoholics.

The layout is just a concept mockup using crapy photos from an old website.

I hope you can forgive the drop shadow on the big P :)

Looking good.

2006-08-20 11:48:34 • timd

Thanks for the comments and suggestions everyone.

Over 130 reads but only 7 comments? I hope that means it’s genereally OK. I guess more people would respond if there was something drastically or obviously wrong with it. Or maybe i’m just not very popular round here :)

If the client gives positive feedback i will refine it further and try to improve the compositon regarding the “At Stalisfield Green” line. I will probably modify some of the terminals? and serifs to reduce that “Harry Potter” vibe too.

Linda - The 2 images aren’t so much alternates, but more to show the client how either the traditional or trendy (blurgh!) qualities of the logo/pub could be accentuated when required. You can put the pub on your list as I have been assured both the beer and the food is real thing :)

Tim - I’m sort of glad you picked up on the claw-like and wrought iron qualities because i wanted the logotype to work as a bit of a visual metaphor for an actual old iron plough. But i can see what you’re saying. It was a concern of mine too. Unfortunately any gothic script/blackletter styling risks evoking horror film connotations. I’d hoped to go back to it’s roots and not the 70’s Hammer films. It’s a very fine line, which i may have crossed :(

Tim - Thanks for your suggested alternatives, but i think Wexford Oakley has overtones even more worrying than horror films and is just plain ugly. If the client has the same concerns as you about what i’ve done then i may consider the Sparrowhawk route. With some modification it could work well. Ta for that one.
