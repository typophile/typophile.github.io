Book font

January 7, 2008 • JCSalomon

This is from a book printed in the mid ’60s, on a typesetting machine that allowed for ligatures (I’ve seen some ‘ﬃ’s) but not kerning—even the italic is unkerned. My scanner doesn’t work under Linux, so I have a photo; if higher quality is needed for an ID I’ll scan it again.  Some features of the font I’ve noticed: • The leg of the Q' begins on the bottom-outside of the circle. • Quotes & apostrophes are smaller than the commas. • Four-headed W' with separate serifs. • The left leg of the M' is slightly slanted, the right leg is straight. • The square brackets []' have curved tops & bottoms and little something-or-others on their sides.  I’ll try whatthefont again after I get a high-quality scan; right now it’s just suggesting distressed fonts. —Joel [img:sites/default/files/old-images/Just_in_Time_6690.png]
