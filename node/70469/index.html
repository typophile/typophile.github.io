Hello @sendoushi I had same the problem like you, of course I forgotten its solution cause I solved it for myself in bef0re; but today I missed it again. So I tried to find a way to solve my problem. after thinking and test different ways I found it again.

If you open the image that you need for mapping in Fontlab in some applications like PS or everything else and copy the image to clipboard by selecting image(Ctrl+C), the image going to clipboard and then if you go to Fontlab studio and paste(Ctrl+V) buffered image; you get the result. also if it doesn’t have good view in Fontlab in graphical app first mix it(change its mode to Grayscale or Black&White)
