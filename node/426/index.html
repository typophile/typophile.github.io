2003-06-25 19:07:08 • arturo
"Chris, thank you, but JFP and others were sadly right on one point : the big E doesn't mean anything. And i don't want so much a meanless design, even if it's aesthetically nice." That's the crux of the matter though. If people recognise what you're trying to say, even if the design is meaningless through a design standpoint, you've done your job as a designer. You can have perfectly good design, playing by the rules and all that, that can be aestheticly pleasing, yet more often than not, it's the breaking of the rules that actually makes a design aesthetically pleasing. When I see your first logo, I see right away what it says, and I look at the way you emphasize the E and think it's a novel away of dealing with the situation. Then I look at your updated design, and see the same type of process going on, but say to myself "how boring". Before you decide, do this: Take both of your designs and show them to laypersons and see which design they like the best. That is the design you should stick with. You're not designing your logo for designers, you're designing it for the common person, and they want something that looks nice.
