Laboratory logo

February 4, 2008 • belisle

I've been trying to put together a memorable, simple, and relevant logo for a laboratory I'm setting up. I was here a while ago, where a particularly helpful comment prompted me to abandon the line of thought there. I'm back now with a new name and a new concept. Mostly this is just an exercise in developing a logo because it's fun. My lab won't be the only Nonlinear Dynamics Laboratory, so I figure that if nothing else, I can have the best branding. In particular, I'm trying to make a logo that can stand on its own without the long name. Here's the first idea I have, which is to add a recognizable nonlinearity to a logotype of the initials. [img:sites/default/files/old-images/nonlinear_4691.png] (Note to the curious: all of the gimmicks are relevant to the research that might go on in the lab, though some are more recognizable than others. The mockups are based on 1) a spiral vortex on a rotating disk, 2) a signal showing laminar-turbulent transition, and 3) "distributed roughness elements" (too esoteric to link) or Taylor vortices. I could also do something like the von Karman vortex street or the Rayleigh-Taylor instability because they're pretty and famous, but I'm less likely to do research into them and they're harder to get into illustrator.) The white-on-black version is promising, but I can't think of a way to complete the left side of the N. [img:sites/default/files/old-images/nonlinear_inverse_5071.png] Another possibility is the vortex without the NDL. I like this for its simplicity, but I'm concerned that the vortex isn't distinctive enough on its own. [img:sites/default/files/old-images/nonlinear_ifree_4590.png] Any comments or ideas?
