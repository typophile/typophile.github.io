Looking for an ID on the font used to spell out 'Clot Rat Pack' for this Nike varsity jacket. Many thanks in advance, Danny[img:sites/default/files/old-images/rat_pack_6425.jpg]
The bumps on the letters are called center spurs. This type of font could be called Spurred Roman. Very popular in the mid 19th century and often classified as Western or Wild West. I did not find an exact match. I found one in a heavier weight, called Cowboy Western, from Font Mesa: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/fontmesa/cowboy-western/ There are also some fonts in the Western category at dafont.com: Saddlebag by Dieter Steffmann, K22 Ambelyn by Toto, & Let Me Ride by Måns Grebäck. Don

H74 Warriors is a nice font for showing placement of spurs. But the base letterform shown in the sample image is a Roman with a unified base and top for the stems, rather than the split or bifurcated stems used in H74 Warriors. This type of letterform is in the group labelled Tuscan. There was a good discussion of the Tuscan Letterform four years ago at http://typophile.com/node/41566 And for people who read books there is a substantial chapter on the Tuscan letterform in Nicollete Grey's Nineteenth Century Ornamented Type. Don

2013-05-25 15:52:27 • franclee
If you are happy with Stahl's Tiffany -- a Tuscan with split stems rather than the flat ends used on the Nike jacket -- this simplifies your font search. However, if some of the comments I encountered in a web search are accurate, Stahl's Tiffany is popular with makers of T-shirts and other casual items but they find it difficult to purchase separately. This is not a major problem as the general design of spurred Tuscan fonts has been generic since the mid 19th century. It should not be too difficult to find a spurred Tuscan with low contrast and weight similar to the Nike font and Stahl's Tiffany for purchase, or even a free version with sufficient quality. A search of Myfonts using "Wild West" produced several spurred Tuscans that you might review: Hessian, Miss. Scarlett, URW Wood Type, Saloon Girl, Buckhorn, H74 Warriors [mentioned already by Ryuk], and Saloon. Dafont has similar free versions. Don
