Double click changes a node from corner to smooth and visa-versa. There is a problem with FontLab changing connections represented as smooth to corners--with the x instead of the o--when you re-open a glyph window (I think), or copy and paste a glyph (definite). As I understand it, this actually doesn't change anything with the font when you export it, as the different node types are indications for convenience of designers, and not part of the font's code. Still is it annoying, and hopefully they will fix it.

A node appearing yellow probably means that one or both of its BCPs is retracted and overlapping. Alt/Option-click to drag the BCP off the node. The "Correct Connections" command in the Contour menu should fix incorrect sharp connections and turn any erroneous x'es back to o's.

2007-11-02 00:03:53 • Quincunx
The node stayed yellow because its other BCP is still retracted. Alt/Option-click the node again and drag its second BCP down to the left. (Occasionally, Alt-dragging moves the node instead, leaving the BCP in place, I'm not sure why.) I have some nodes which have no BCP or only one. Which ideally should have another one "Yellow" nodes with retracted BCPs may look like they have one or zero BCPs, but the BCPs are there, in plain view, covering the node. Just Alt-drag them off. Alt/Option-dragging also works on sharp and tangent nodes. Alt-drag from the node to pull out a BCP, or Alt-drag from a straight line-segment to create BCPs from both adjacent nodes (convert to curve).
