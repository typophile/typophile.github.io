Recently I had the pleasure of perusing the first three
issues of TYPO magazine, a new Czech publication,
and I wanted to present a quick review of it.

http://www.magtypo.cz/

Overall it looks like the polished work one would expect from
a sophisticated European source. It does feel a little thin in
terms of page count with respect to its other attributes which
are more generous (like spread size, production quality, etc),
but the information is concentrated, without however feeling
crowded. And this thin-ness leaves tantalizing room for easily
scaling up the content. My only typographic complaint is that
the English text seems too recessed: both smaller and grayer,
somewhat hard to read.

On the other hand, it's great to have the text in both Czech
and English because the content of TYPO is certainly relevant
to much more than just Czech designers: many of the articles
are some of the best treatments of the given topic I've seen,
like the series on bitmap fonts in issue #3. Frankly, if you have
no interest in Eastern European type whatsoever even the
modest subscription price (6 Euro per issue) wouldn't actually
be worth it - but there's so much to learn form that part of the
world, it would be a shame to have such an isolationist stance.
The few articles not provided with English translations tend to
be technical discussions (like Mac OSX font managers) which
are easy to find in English elsewhere anyway.

Besides the bitmap fonts articles the two others that stood
out for me were an interesting -and in fact unorthodox- bit
of coverage about newspaper fonts (in issue #1), and a very
insightful article about the development of the Prague graphic
identity (in #2). In this article were presented viewpoints
and analysis from all the possible angles, making for a very
objective treatment, totally unlike the insincere whitewash
we've become used to suffering in the large design magazines.

TYPO also features some timely "news" items (like the Frothy
debacle, and that bancomicsans.com campaign), and last
but not least, it features Frantisek Storm… if you thought
his fonts were colorful, you should get a whif of his writing:
"Unlike the strictly rational Didot or elegant Bodoni, Walbaum
does not, at first sight, have features that would give the
fonts a brief adjective. However, it is definitely an outstanding
achievement, without any shadow of Walbaum's candymaking.
The expression is robust, as if seasoned with cow manure some-
where near Weimar, where Walbaum's type foundry was situated
in the years 1803-1839."

So, as Filip Blazek (the magazine's editor) would say: Czech it out!
