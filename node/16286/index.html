If it's realy constructivist you want & it's a logo I suggest you get in somne books & make your own! They are fairly simple to make - but probably less simple to make REALLY well. Stil, an original solution for type in a logo is always a little more ideal because it catches the eye better. If you want a super fast solution there is always 'constructovist' from P22 http://www.p22.com/products/constructivist.html [img:sites/default/files/old-images/Constructivistov_r1_c1.gif] The other thing is that so far you are giving me a swiss modern or post swiss feel not a constructivist one. They were big on wacky energtic angles and didn't go in for the degree of reductivism/simplicity you are using so far. Again, crack those books! And lastly, they were big on using human figures even if they were pretty abstracted Check this out http://www.a-website.org/design/pictohistory/pics/constructivist/kozlinskii.jpg But I like what you made too.
