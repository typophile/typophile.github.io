Strongest current directions are Upper Right and Bottom Left. The marks feel somewhwat unbalanced: try getting rid of the blocks behind the Upper Right logo, and kerning the N closer to the B. Also, this may be due to personal taste, but I find the exclamation point to be a tad cheesy (especially in a logo). If they're drawing Indie acts, the exclamation will on serve to compromise the audience's perception of the venue (and their ability to pull legitimate shows).

Hey Jeff, for what it's worth, I think it's a good concept, I think the blocky ones on the right are definitely stronger, the letter-press look is more obvious, the others have a bit more of the ransom note thing happening, ot as desirable ;) I have two suggestions: 1. integrate the B more, I just keep seeing the capital B, it's really dominant and everything else fades into a block. I think it's OK to lead with it but at the moment it seems to be doing its own thing. B square. 2. Very clean looking letters for letterpress... without going grunge crazy, I would imagine a bit of dirtying it up wouldn't go astray, nothing wrong with a few printing artifacts, it's a rock and roll club, subtlety isn't so much required. Finally, I'm not so sure about the 3dness of the letters, might be a little too busy for a logo, I hope these thoughts help, like I say, fair enough concept that will look good with just a bit more work cheers --N
