Just wondering if anyone knows either of these? They are definitely from 1910s, but anything similar is of interest. Thanks in advance.

The cover is set in Seven Line Grotesque, a typeface (the first sans serif with lowercase letters) designed by English typographer William Thorowgood for Fann Street Foundry in 1832. A digital version—Grotesque Regular—can be purchased from myfonts.com.

The body text appears to be Century Roman, designed by Linn Boyd Benton in 1894 for Theodore Low De Vinne, publisher of The Century Magazine. There are many digital variations of Century (again, see myfonts.com), but Century Expanded or Century 725 might be your best matches.
