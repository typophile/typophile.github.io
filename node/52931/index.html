Italian Rationalist architecture typefaces

December 23, 2008 • petjam

Hi everyone…

I’m designing a poster exhibition on the work of Cesare Cattaneo, an Italian Rationalist architect who worked in Como and Milan in the 1930s. The Rationalist school was led by Giuseppe Terragni; the Casa del Fascio is the signature building of the style.

I’m looking to the source material for design cues… and having trouble identifying contemporary analogues to the typefaces used in the publications of the time. Mostra could work for display, but it’s a bit too Art Deco - the rationalists were in line with the avant garde of Northern Europe (Bauhaus meets Corbu meets streamlined Italian industrial design).

I’m leaning toward Futura or Helv Neue…the former feels more Italian to me and the latter has a greater range of weights and styles (at least on my machine…).

Your two cents appreciated!

Kabel, Nobel, Neutraface, Verlag (H&FJ)?
