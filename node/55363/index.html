Fresh Catch of the Day - Saul Bass

March 1, 2009 • azucena

My favorite graphic designers of all time is Saul Bass. He is best know for his movie title sequences, but was also known for his graphic design, including movie posters and corporate identities. His work forms around solid colors, geometric layouts, montage, hand drawn type and dominant symbolism. At the time his work was extremely innovative and I find his design to be timeless and contemporary even today. His movie poster designs are simple and iconic, overwhelmingly powerful, eye catching and always evoke a feeling or mood captured to perfection. Bass reinvented the movie title as an art form and created them to be an introductory to the feel of the film and worked with some of the most notable directors ever, such as Hitchcock, Scorcese and Kubrick. I was exposed to a lot of his work while in taking film courses in school. My all time favorite and first known exposure to his work is the vertical bar sequence from Psycho, which was so visually striking it has always stuck in my mind (it also stuck in my mind because we analyzed psycho scene by scene for half a semester). It's movement was also inspirational for my type poster design from last semester in Typography I based on the typeface Interstate by Tobias Frere-Jones. He also created very strong logos, including those for the Girls Scouts of America, United Airlines, United Way and AT&T.
