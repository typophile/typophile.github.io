January 12, 2010 • kosal

What are some analogies that help typophobes better understand the art? I’ve heard these two.

Good type is like good lighting in a museum. It showcases the contents without being distracting or too noticeable.

Typography is like the voice talent in an ad. You don’t notice it when it’s professional and appropriate, but when it’s not, it just feels cheesy.
