Jenna,

There are a lot of conventions when it comes to metal band logos. A lot of them use Old English or Blackletter fonts. The more extreme genres (black metal, death metal, grindcore) tend to use hand-drawn ambigrams that are symmetrical and hard to read. The pointier and scarier, the better. Often (depending on the band’s style), occult symbols are worked into the logo (pentagrams for “O”’s, etc.). Do a Google search for “death metal logos”, and you’ll see what I mean.

I think this tradition started because metal was an underground style of music, with a D-I-Y attitude similar to Punk. A lot of bands designed their own logos, without any design training.

The “Dragonforce” logo belongs to a genre called “power Metal” that is generally less dark and scary.

Some of the more creative bands break these rules. But be aware that a lot of fans expect certain things from a band, just from the look of their logo.
