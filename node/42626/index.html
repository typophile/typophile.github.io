Antique Old Style No. 3

February 28, 2008 • Uli

At the Cambridge website is offered a KJV Bible http://www.cambridge.org/uk/bibles/kjv/standard.htm featuring a highly interesting old typeface called Antique Old Style No. 3, which has no descenders at all. More precisely, the descenders of f, g, j, p, q, y in Roman and Italic are sqeezed or crippled such that they do not (or almost not) go below the base line enabling extremely non-leaded typesetting. I uploaded a sample page here http://www.sanskritweb.net/temporary/luke.gif It seems to me that this KJV Bible edition, which is a reprint, was originally typeset using the foundry type or hot-metal type of Antique Old Style No. 3, which seems to be a modification of one of the various Bookman fonts. Question: Is this curious Antique Old Style No. 3 available as a digital font?
A good companion for Advertiser's Gothic.
