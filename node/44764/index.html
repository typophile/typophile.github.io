2008-05-20 01:13:32 • Ratbaggy
I second Paul's sentiment. There are appropriate contexts for a really obvious logomark, and a music publisher is not one of them. So many of the most successful marks are non sequitors - a little girl with an umbrella for salt, a peacock for a TV news station, a shell for a gas station, a ribbon for flour, an apple for computers, the list goes on and on and on. When the name is not obvious and/or the concept is hard to grasp, then some kind of explanatory or illustrative logomark is definitely called for, but a music publisher is hardly a difficult concept. Good luck! --- eeblet.com
