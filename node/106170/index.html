2013-09-09 18:21:44 • fvilanakis
Your software is indeed “impressive.” Softmaker’s font dates from 1995. Unless the piano labels are more recent, I suggest they were made from a font produced by a photo lettering company. They had very creative people who could use pre-digital editing techniques to completely transform font faces. I have no idea of the source used to make this design. Looks sort of like a Frankenstein of one or more fonts that started with ball terminals and underwent some slicing. As for the font name, “Impressive” is as good as any, since we have no idea as to whether this is a Softmaker rip of the work of another font maker or something they did themselves or who produced the photo lettering version. As you mention the Softmaker font differs on the numerals 1 & 6. It also lacks a 2/3. A strong argument that the Softmaker font is not the source of the labels, but is rather a font produced from the same origin as the labels. Don
