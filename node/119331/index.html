2014-09-09 14:49:17 • JanekZ

It’s a customized version of an early digital typeface called OPTI Papong, distributed by the OPTI CastCraft Company (1990-1991). The top of the A was changed from flat to pointed, and the U (which looks like a lowercase u in the original) was altered to remove a right vertical stem (no thickening required).

Information can be found here: http://luc.devroye.org/fonts-27506.html

2014-09-09 15:03:08 • kthomps5
OPTI did not create any new font designs, but in some cases they were the first to make digital versions of photo-lettering and metal fonts. As in this case the OPTI name usually was uninformative as to the name of the pre-digital font. In the predigital era Castcraft focused on copying existing fonts and releasing them under new names. All OPTI products are now font orphans and there could be legal issues if they are used in commercial projects. Don
