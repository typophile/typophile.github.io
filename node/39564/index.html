I am extremely happy to announce that Fontlab Ltd. is very close to releasing Photofont WebReady, a product that will allow users to convert outline fonts not only to Flash fonts for use with sIFR and similar solutions (that are employed for Flash testdrive capabilities) -- but also into something new, what we call "embedded web photofonts". Basically, Photofont WebReady will convert an outline font into a bitmap font of a chosen size and then put this bitmap font inside of a Flash file that will include ActionScript that will make this bitmap font work as if it was regular Flash text. Next week, we will publish a new version of the photofont.com website that will demostrate the technology in use. We hope that some folks who are afraid that fonts can be extracted from Flash files may consider switching to embedded web photofonts for preview purposes. I'd like to add that -- unlike some other solutions on the market -- Photofont WebReady will respect the embedding bits in OpenType and TrueType fonts, and will not allow a user to convert a font into an outline Flash font if the embedding bits don't allow that. Regards, Adam Twardoch Fontlab Ltd.

In the early 90's not many people I knew considered fonts as something you should buy for yourself, and even pre-press companies gave fonts away to their clients for free. Since then both legislation and public awareness have changed, but there are still a lot of people who are willing to share fonts and/or who are ignorant of the legal issues. Perhaps people should be made more aware of what's ok and what's not, but I'm afraid a lot of people just don't care.
