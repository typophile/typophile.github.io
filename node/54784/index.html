INTERNSHIPS: FUN OR REACH?

February 14, 2009 • fredcastle

I'm thinking of places I might want to intern. Small design studios. I have done some before, but this may be my last one before a real job. I'm torn between choosing places that I would have fun at and like the idea of it but fear it might be just easy and not push me as much to think differently, verse serious places who I respect and are super sophisticated. Do you think its better to intern at a place where you think you'd like to work in 10 years or intern someplace fun and good, but less serious?
I'm interning in school, too. I'd say there are pros and cons to both - if you intern at a place you want to work for 10 years, you have a greater chance of being able to get a job there when you graduate. On the other hand, this might be your last chance to try out that crazy place with the expectation that you aren't staying long. Although I have a question - why is "not serious" unacceptable if it is "good" at the same time? What do they do there? Why do you think it will be so easy?
