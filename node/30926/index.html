2007-01-27 23:19:54 • Grot Esqué

In general, the quality of your text justification will be directly proportional to the length of your measure. With a short measure, any typesetting application, Adobe Illustrator included, will be forced to add large amounts of word or letter spacing to justify your text. Assuming that this is not the issue at hand, here are a few of things you could try:

Advance Justification Options
Thankfully, even Illustrator offers some advanced options for text justification. You could try adjusting the minimum, maximum and desired settings for word, letter and glyph spacing.
Hyphenation
When enabled, hyphenation should improve the quality of text justification. Illustrator offers advanced options here as well.
Single-line vs. Every-line Composer
When the Single-line Composer is enabled, Illustrator will adjust word spacing on a per line basis. Contrastingly, the Every-line Composer will reflow all lines of text in an attempt to normalize word spacing and therefore improve justification.

All of these advance settings can be found in the additional options dialog of Illustrator’s Type»Paragraph window.
