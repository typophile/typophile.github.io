This looks like an embroidery font that does not seem to match any retail font. It seems to have been based on 19th century fonts styles ranging from "Antique" [Egyptian] to Clarendon. The serif treatment on _S_ seems to be the key to the source fonts. Perhaps the closest source might be Kinghorn 205 Med [img:sites/default/files/old-images/Kinghorn205MedAsHMNZS_4685.gif] http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/talbot/kinghorn-205/ which has strokes and serifs all of a roughly equal weight similar to the lettering in the Cap Tallie. There is also a condensed version. The reverse contrast -- top and bottom thicker than sides -- shown in the top of _S_ in the Cap Tallie might be a quirk of that the digital instructions for that letter. Also consider Antique Central Heavy [img:sites/default/files/old-images/AntiqueCentralHeavyAsHMNZS_5180.gif] http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/aboutype/antique-central/ if you want reverse contrast you could consider [img:sites/default/files/old-images/PTBarnumAsHMNZS_5815.gif] http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/bitstream/p-t-barnum/ which suggests the _S_ but the vertical strokes are too thin. The best match would be a Clarendon with strokes and serifs all of a roughly equal weight, which would differ from an Antique or Egyptian in that some of the terminals, such as those in _E_ and _L_, are tapered. Part way there is Patrick Griffins Clarendon Text Bold [img:sites/default/files/old-images/ClarendonTextBoldAsHMNZS_4966.gif] https://www.myfonts.com/fonts/canadatype/clarendon-text/ Don
