Erm…I don’t have much to contribute here and pop in occasionally to ask a question; I hope that’s okay…

I have to reproduce a brochure in another language; the english brochure has been supplied, and I’m puzzled over the choice of point sizes throughout. Would be grateful to know if someone here can detect any logic to this:

Type Point size/leading are as indicated across a six-panel brochure:

Panel 1: Subhead 9.65/11 Body: 9/11 Body: 7.5/10

Panel 2: Head: 23.152/15 Body: 9/10 Subhead: 13.891/15.569 Body: 8.489/10.804 Body: 8/9.5 Body: 7.5/9

Panel 3: Same as panel 2.

Panel 4: Same as panels 2 and 3, with a little 9.65/11 and 9/11 thrown in.

Panel 5: Head: 17 pt. Subhead: 14/auto Body: 9.25/auto

Panel 6: Head: 43.996/46.853

Otherwise it’s a well-designed brochure; am I just looking at sloppy typesetting?

Is the brochure for a circus?
