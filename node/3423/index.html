2004-03-19 19:39:22 • glutton
Yes, Nathan, that's the answer I was going for: Spiral, named after Blumenthal's small press. It was originally designed in Europe while Blumenthal was on a "sabbatical" of sorts, having closed down the press due to the Depression. The original 14-point font was cast for him by the Bauer foundry. On his way back to the States, Blumenthal showed samples to Morison, who then suggested that the face be adapted for Monotype. The Monotype version of the Spiral typeface was christened Emerson.
