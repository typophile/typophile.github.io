Booklet of personal LGBT stories

March 30, 2007 • Zach

I’ve got an interesting design project I’ve been asked to take on, and which I’d like to share with you all. It’s for a zine to be published at Gordon College, where I graduated from last year, and whose student newspaper I previously redesigned (with your much appreciated help – though like me you may be wounded to know that the current editorship was using Verdana for the text last I saw).

Background - Gordon is a generally hostile environment for LGBT people, due to its conservative Christian policies and culture. Most students (even some of the queer and questioning ones) feel that to be gay is sinful, and the administrators have in the past booted students even for pretty tame expressions of same-sex attraction, so virtually no one is out to more than their close friends. That may be slowly changing, but for now and the foreseeable future it’s a rough place to be.

Project - Amid this environment, there are usually a few progressive students trying to be a voice for change, and in that spirit two current students I know are trying to collect anonymous, personal stories from queer or questioning Gordonites and publish them together in a booklet. There will be about a dozen pieces, of various lengths. They’ve asked me to design it. It’s a little daunting, which is why I’m seeking advice from the big boys and girls.

I think this will be a milestone event, since it will be printed in fairly large numbers, and be probably the biggest act of public self-expression by queer students at Gordon ever, so I really really want to make it look really good.

So I’m trying to choose the fonts, and figure out what kind of feel would be most appropriate. To me it seems it should look personal, a little warm, even with a hint of humor, but still dignified and understated. Insofar as it’s a student project, and more like a magazine than a newspaper, and ultimately about LOVE*, the display could be a little bold, and if space permits I’ll probably make a couple illustrations (high contrast images of people perhaps). But these are people’s personal stories, a few of them rather painful, so the display and illustrations shouldn’t be exuberant to the point of irreverence.

The only fonts definitely on the shortlist so far are Comenia for text and Pelso for display, and I’m definitely still looking. Bello seems too out there, but for some reason I’m exploring going some distance in that direction. I might be thinking of Eric Sans for display if I hadn’t already used it for another project at Gordon last year. And it’s a touch too informal perhaps, but I’d be considering Charter for text if that wasn’t the school paper’s font (pre-Verdana anyway).

Oh, and we have a small budget so I’m hoping to stay under $200.

Comenia: Nice, humane, friendly and very readable. Pelso: Mechanical, thin, cold, awkward…. Can you get a nicer face for display? Maybe find another organic face in Storm’s catalog. Charter is very neutral and businesslike. Not at all bad for text (9 point in print), maybe something like Miller Display as headline face.
