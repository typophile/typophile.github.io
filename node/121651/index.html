About the Instagram reworking, I understand the need to smooth the logo down and to make it more a logo and less a word set in a nice font. But I think the result took away too much of the liveliness from the original. In this regard, for the g_r pair probably a more “twisted” curve would have helped (less tame loop for |g|, less wide counter for |r|, a bit like your Fertigo Script sample*). As for Authentica, it’s not the first time someone is called out for ripping off Wisdom Script (see http://typophile.com/node/93171). If you need to “improve” upon an existing script, at least do it with something which allows it (example: https://www.behance.net/gallery/Blenda-Script-Free-Font/13832171).

  • Although in Fertigo |g|’s loop could look a bit cramped, and the joining a bit thin.

On the question on which digital font is close to the revised Instagram logo, I’d say that the closer general style is the modulated-but-not-too-contrasted upright brushy cursive favoured by vintage advertising, sign painters and phototype.
