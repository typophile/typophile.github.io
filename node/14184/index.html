Lettering Artists

July 27, 2005 • willdean

I just got finished reading a thread in the type ID forum about comic book artists, illustrators, and lettering artists. I thought I’d post this offshoot here.

I think Rick Griffin is my all-time favorite illustrator/lettering artist. His figures are so abstract but fit so well within his poster compositions. Most of his work is ultra-psychodelic, but is just so tight for something hand rendered. His 3-D lettering is my favorite out of his whole collection of work.

Here’s a link to some of his work: http://www.myraltis.co.uk/rickgriffin/

Also, there is a book that I have in my library called “The Art of the Fillmore: The Poster Series 1966-1971” that is a great resource of incredible lettering. Makes me wish there was still this level of technical precision with hand-rendered type. Wouldn’t that be great?

Check out Delicious…

http://www.gigposters.com/designers.php?designer=15146

Gigposters.com is a treasure trove of lettering… don’t get lost.

In repeating what I wrote in the original thread that spawned this offshoot – Having spent many, many hours early in my career hand lettering headlines, and using lettering devices (Varigraph Headwriter, Leroy Pantograph) to create ads, I’ve always admired lettering artists who had such a facility and love for the craft.

I used to think that “doing things the old way” sharpened my sensitivity to character shapes and spacing, but in giving my younger son a brief tutorial on Photoshop and Quark, I was astounded how quickly his work flourished.

Yes, I’m old, but I’m back in style!
