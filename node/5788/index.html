Linotype's Old English Value Pack

July 27, 2004 • dan_reynolds

http://www.linotype.com/6-2162-6/oldenglishvaluepack.html What do you guys think of Linotype's latest value pack? Six representative "Old English" fonts at such a low package price. I was even more impressed after I compared it with an older value pack on Fonts.com (http://www.fonts.com/findfonts/detail.asp?nCo=AFMT&amp;pid=371688)

According to Martin Kotulla, SoftMaker is finally having an update on Blackletter typefaces, splitting up most Blackletter typefaces to include 2 layouts each: “Regular” (Original) and “DFR” (German Fraktur), but some Blackletter typefaces only have the original layout, no German Fraktur.

The following Blackletter typefaces in the layouts of “Regular” (Original) and “DFR” (German Fraktur) are:

• “Alte Schwabacher” - 2 styles • “C652 Blackletter” (Caslon Gotisch) - 2 styles • “E780 Blackletter” (Engravers Old English, ATF) - 4 styles (including Regular & Bold, each with 2 layouts) • “Fraktur” (Fette Fraktur) - 2 styles • “Gotisch” (Fette Gotisch) - 2 styles • “Goudy Text” - 4 styles (including Regular & Lombardic Caps, each with 2 layouts) • “J790 Blackletter” (Unger Fraktur) - 2 styles • “L750 Blackletter” (London Text) - 2 styles • “Marriage” (Mariage) - 2 styles • “Old Blackletter” (Old English Text, Monotype) - 2 styles • “P790 Blackletter” (Poppl-Fraktur) - 2 styles • “Rundgotisch” (Weiss Rundgotisch) - 2 styles • “W650 Blackletter” (Walbaum Fraktur) - 2 styles • “W722 Blackletter” (Wilhelm-Klingspor Gotisch) - 2 styles • “Z690 Blackletter” (Zentenar Fraktur) - 4 styles (including Regular & Bold, each with 2 layouts)

The following “original layout”-only Blackletter typefaces are:

• “American Text” • “F692 Blackletter” (Fette Fraktur) • “G820 Blackletter” (Gregorian) • “Gothic” (Weiss Gotisch) • “Light Text” (Linotype Text/Linotext) • “Ottoman” (Old English Text, Monotype) • “P650 Blackletter” (Paladin)

And yet, exclusive Blackletter typeface designs include most with two layouts (Regular & Dfr), and some with the layout of “original” only, no German Fraktur. These fonts are finally appearing on two of SoftMaker’s premium-quality typeface collections - “MegaFont XXL 3.0” for home users, and “infiniType 4” for designers - both being released around May 27, 2014.

(one last disclosure, I did do some writing about the old englishness of old english types that will be released in later marketing materials from LL. But this is not a marketing ploy). Until last week, I thought that Old English was a total misnomer, something that ignorant font-buyers used when searching on the internet. Well, I was wrong! Old English referes primarially to historized texturas design in England during the victorian era. Texturas were introduced into England during the 1400s. And, as everyone here probably knows, I LOVE TEXTURAS! The victorians loved them too, but thought them out of date and illegibile, it seems. So they "modernized" them. This is classicism. Mariage (in the Old English Pack) is a revival of Morris Fuller Benton's Wedding, originally from 1901 and thoroughly Old English. So almost anything "historicized" and "textura-inspired" can be vaguely Old English, whether it was designed by an Englishman in England or not. Blackmoor is from Quay, and supposedly based on something old and english. Notre Dame and WK Gotisch are not english at all but are texturas, and WKG is very historicized, just in a beautiful germanic manner by Koch instead of Benton's or the Victorian-style. Notre Dame is not as historicized, but it is an interesting revival of pre-printing texturas. So I think that it is vaguely appropriate, too. Monotype's packet has more pure old englishes in it, maybe, but it also has a Fraktur, which I can't see as being Old English at all.
