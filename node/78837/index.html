Combining Chaparral with Avenir

February 7, 2011 • splice

Hey there all,

I’m a new-to-Typophile print designer of the campus magazine at my uni. It’s 1623cm (~6.39 in) and there’s plenty of content every month, so the space I can work with is really very limited.

A recent idea of mine is to pair up Avenir and Chaparral Pro in this magazine. The former for titles, the latter for body text.

What’s your opinion of this combination? It would be great to hear what you think as I’m in no way an expert on print design. The way I see it, this solid slab serif provides good readability (which is a key factor for me here) and Avenir complements it really well, with the diverse weights and whatnot.

I would go to three columns and less leading. That seems more like a magazine, less bookish/brochurey. Also, never run body text across a halftone, even if faded. You might want to consider adding some lighter weights in the heads (possibly of Chaparral) to spice up the visual interest.
