Hi everyone. I am working on a project and am trying to find out the name of the typeface or typefaces used in the Filson logo. I have attached it here and would appreciate any help in identifying exactly what it is or something close. Appreciate any time and assistance in advance!!
_FILSON_ looks widened and perhaps boldened and perhaps has other changes. _SINCE 1897_ might be close to a font. Closest match I found so far is Goudy Catalog [img:sites/default/files/old-images/GoudyCatalogAsSince1897_4331.gif] https://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/goudy-catalogue/ An also ran is Stratford [img:sites/default/files/old-images/StratfordAsSince1897_6347.gif] https://www.myfonts.com/fonts/ef/stratford/ Using Stratford Medium might work as a base for making _FILSON_ [img:sites/default/files/old-images/StratfordMediumAsFILSON_5708.gif] if it is widened 130% [img:sites/default/files/old-images/StratfordMediumWidth130pctAsFILSON_4235.jpg] Don

Hoefler Text Bold, stretched and squashed, would also make a good basis for recreating the look of |FILSON| (you’d also have to refine the serifs): http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/apple/hoefler-text/ Or use Hoefler Titling Bold, which has finer serifs: http://www.typography.com/fonts/hoefler-titling/styles/ [img:sites/default/files/old-images/HoeflerTitling_4664.png]
