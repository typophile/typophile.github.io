Matching Egyptians to SansSerifs

June 4, 2008 • scmccarthy22

Requesting some advice on matching Egyptian typefaces with sans serifs. The project in question is a newspaper, and the sans serif used is H&FJ’s Knockout. The Egyptian typeface will be the third typeface in the paper’s type family. My question is, how close to the sans serif characters should the Egyptian characters be? I’ve seen pubications where the cut of the Egyptian is completely different from that of the sans serif, and others (such as Esquire) who’s Egyptian-SansSerif relationship is dead on (Stag and Stag Sans)…

I’ve included a link to two options I’m currently thinking about (Knockout with Soho and Knockout with Serifa). My initial feeling is that Serifa has more of the Knockout “feel,” but individually, I like Soho MUCH better (plus, it has more weights than Serifa).

Thoughts???

http://img382.imageshack.us/img382/2549/serifspecimeniw5.jpg

The reason Serifa works better with Knockout is because it has a similar old-school stroke contrast. Soho has a very contemporary, almost monolinear stroke. I think Serifa is a much better fit. How many weights do you really need? Try setting a few headlines and pages with Serifa and see if it does the job. You might also consider Frutiger’s other slab Glypha which is essentially the same design but more condensed with a larger x-height.

I don’t suppose—given your preference for Soho—that you could sub in another sans serif for Knockout? Seb Lester just released his newest type family: Soho Gothic, a deliciously contemporary sans serif companion for Soho. Actually, I like it personally much more than Soho itself, and given a choice between the two, would choose it.
