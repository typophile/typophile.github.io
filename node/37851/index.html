On a general and somewhat related note – not to diminish the quality of Letterhead Fonts, because certain types of faces and especially scripts do require lots of kerning because of their very nature – the amount of kerning pairs is not automatically a reference for the quality of a typeface. One could argue that a font with an impressive amount of kerning pairs is a font that was poorly spaced to begin with. :^) This statement isn't by any means related to Letterhead Fonts in particular, just a general remark for educational purposes aimed at our new users.
