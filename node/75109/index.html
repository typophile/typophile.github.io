Dispelling a wanted poster with typeface

October 12, 2010 • dtaylor

I’m trying to establish whether an image of a wanted poster is real or not. To me, the image looks like someone just put some wordart over a picture. But, to be sure, I was wondering if someone could identify the font. This way I can confirm that it is, in fact, not of the 1862 period but a modern creation. [[http://www.fsst.org/images/little_crow_wanted_poster.jpg|The wanted poster is here.]] The image is cited as a “Little Crow Wanted Poster submitted by the Santee Sioux Tribe of Nebraska”. While I’d hate to question the tribe about it, I’m thinking the WANTED text above his head is from a computer. If someone could identify the font and the year it was made (or as close as possible) I’d greatly appreciate it.

I’m guessing the typeface is Engravers MT but you’re the experts. Thanks in advance.

~Dave

It does look like Engravers, but it looks like a digital addition. The fact that the “A” tucks under the “W” tells me this wasn’t achieved with metal or wood type. It was also uncommon to see type printed over a photo, as opposed to outside the photo area, due to production limitations. The biggest giveaway to me is simply that the blurriness of the “WANTED” type doesn’t match that of the type on the bottom.
