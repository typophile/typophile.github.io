Dear Typophiles; I'm producing a small booklet with text samples and it'll be printed in Swedish and English. I have, however, no clue what kind of text to use in the english version. I don't want to use Lorem Ipsum, and the quick brown fox… is a bit too short. What would you choose, which are the standard sample texts in english? Regards,
Fredrik, I never use Lorem ipsum, it has too many characters that aren't used in common english language. What I use for my comps is a part of a chapter of Alice in Wonderland. Mad tea party. I would advise you to investigate snippets from stories that might be in the public domain.

I've heard it said that Lorem ipsum is used because it tends to make text look good. The theory being that the letterforms evolved for letter frequencies characteristic of Latin. If so, it might have value for presentation. Since your purpose is publication, however, I think you should look for a passage that suits your fancy in some classic public domain text, a chidren's book or a classic of travel writing, for example. Take it as an opportunity to put some of your own personality into the project.
