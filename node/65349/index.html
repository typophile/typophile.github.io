Hi there,

I’m a digital media student and have just started my own company just so I can freelance and do some work on along side my studies. I used to be an interface developer so mostly what I do is just “PSD to XHTML” or “PSD to Wordpress” work.

For the name of my company I’m using Calgary script. I wanted something that looked handmade as all my work is bespoke and handcrafted, and not using templates or dreamweaver etc.

When I worked at a large agency, I only recall using Arial, Georiga, Tahoma and Verdana, the boring web-safe fonts. I’ve never been involved in print…

So my question is, should I have the same font for print (letterheads, invoices) as for the website? Or can I choose something more exiting for print?

Should I have a mix of serif and sans serif on the website? What could go well with Calgary script?

Any advise or links to relevant articles etc woukd be very much appreciated=)

Thanks, Linda

I’m not sure “more exciting” is the proper way to go, but yes, you definitely want to explore non-web fonts for printed materials. You can then use an image of the logo on the website, until we finally get our act together to allow all fonts to be used effectively on the web.

I would suggest a open, humanist sans serif with large x-height to complement Calgary Script.

Don
