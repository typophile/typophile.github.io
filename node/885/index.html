2003-08-17 17:25:18 • hrant
Depends. One major factor is the scope, or scale.

((speaking generally)
If you had a competition among a small, tight-knit online community,
or among a group of friends, with the
winner getting a pat on the back, then the question of
whether to include staff submissions wouldn't be an issue.
The overriding factor would be, Let's have some fun.

When you include big-name judges and cash and prizes,
when you get exposure at major design portals,
then you widen the scope and heighten the expectations.

So Jared and Joe now have to weigh
the joy of submitting and the competitive desire to WIN
against the perception of wrongdoing should one of them win.

That's a tough call, but it's easy for outsiders.

Last year, this issue was discussed, if only briefly.
Because I was going to be involved in producing the
Tshirts, I renounced
my eligibility. It can't hurt to err on the side of caution.

At the same time, I can see where J&J are coming from.
Some of the initial designs were lacking, and Joe's pride
kicked in + they conceived this online type oasis,
and they spend money out of pocket to make it hum.
It must be weird not to be able to submit to your own contest.

(you could still create stuff, submit stuff, post stuff, get feedback
and just refrain from sending your stuff to the judges)

?

anyway, I would hope that we don't suck all the fun
out of this before it even starts.
