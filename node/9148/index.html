>the computer people ignored "squeeze," the slight spreading of ink when the lead type hits the paper

Like many people who don't experiment much with typesetting (especially book designers), Mr Tufte expects digital versions of the well known faces to duplicate the effect of letterpress.

In the first place, this is not necessarily what the digital foundries set out to do. Secondly, it's not easy, and reviving a classic in such a manner may not be as good an idea as making a new face, eg Scala or Charter, to tackle the problem of "digital to offset".

Thirdly, if the complainants took a little more time to research the topic (or employ someone to do same, or post on Typophile) they would discover that there are many less-known versions of the classics which address the "good as letterpress" problem, eg Founders Caslon, MvB Verdigris, and several Shinn typefaces to boot. "Jenson" has been revised at least a dozen times: surely book publishers can find one of the digital versions that is compatible with their needs. Sorry, no quick answers, this IS rocket science, after all.

BTW, the magnificent Monotype Bembo was their second attempt at the Griffo font, Poliphilus was the first, and they were pretty happy with it for a while.
