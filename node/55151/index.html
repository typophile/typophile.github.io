Geometric Oldstyle?

February 24, 2009 • Dan Gayle

This was from a thread about a serifed match for Avant Garde, but I think it's a relevant question on its own: Is there a such thing as a geometric oldstyle? Of course, we have examples like Albers, etc., but oldstyles are typically associated with their humanist touch. What about a geometric oldstyle with less of the humanism and more of the geometry, but without the increases in contrast associated with the move away from humanism and into rationalism and the engraving tool? Don't think of it as Futura with serifs (shudder), but think of it as an oldstyle typeface with the same skeletal structure as Futura or Avant Garde. If this typeface existed, what would it look like? I have a picture in my head, but not enough time to rough up an idea.
[[http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/itc-lubalin-graph/|Lubalin Graph]] is pretty much a slab serif version of Avant Garde. I wouldn't call it old style though.
