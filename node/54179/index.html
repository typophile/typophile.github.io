In no specific order...

January 30, 2009 • CaliTraina

When first assigned to post examples of work from three design periods in the 20th century, the first to come to mind was, my favorite, pop art. Pop art, beginning in the United States in the late 1960s, in my opinion is just plain fun. I love the use of irony in a lot of pieces, not mention the vibrant colors and a direct reflection of the pop culture happening at that time.

I’m sure everyone can guess the name and artist of this piece: That’s right, Its Campbell’s Soup I by Andy Warhol in 1968

Jackson Pollock came to mind next. A lot of his work falls under the Abstract Expressionist period. This was happening in the late 40s, early 50s in the United States. Abstract Expressionism is described as “an American post–World War II art movement,” and Jackson Pollock played a big role in this movement.

This is Male And Female by Jackson Pollock in 1942. You should check out this website. You too can be Jackson Pollock. You’ll see what I mean. http://www.jacksonpollock.org/

The last period I’m going to talk about is Surrealism, another favorite of mine. This movement, beginning in the early 20s, featured unexpected juxtaposition as well as the element of surprise. Surrealism was not limited to just painting and design, but also literature, film, and music.

This is my favorite by Max Ernst called The Elephant Celebes done in 1921.
