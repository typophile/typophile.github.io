2015-03-28 18:22:28 • riccard0
General rule: aim for a certain number of words per line. Look around and you'll notice that newspapers go for less words per line than books on serious matters, fashion magazines mostly aren't concerned about this issue at all because their art directors consider text to be subordinate and a hindrance to their creative expression, book publishers pump up short texts to be big books by doing bad things to text sizes, websites are still in the dark ages, and that serious graphic designers are in a minority when it comes to facilitating easy reading. In other words: experiment and see what works. For you, but mostly for the reader.
2015-03-28 19:19:46 • Bert Vanderveen
A factor is the age of the readers, as eyesight gets worse as we age. Unless this restaurant is just for teenagers, make sure the type is large enough for older folks. If there's any doubt, print a test menu and ask a few retired folks if they can read it. Do it in the restaurant at night so it's a realistic test.
