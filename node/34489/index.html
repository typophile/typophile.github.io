Resume for Interior Designer

June 15, 2007 • AndrewSipe

I'm not nearly finished, but I'm starting to feel hesitant about this design. The resume is for my sister, she's an interior designer with a few years in the business. I wanted to create an inviting design that has a clean modern look without being too overwhelming. As a male, designing for a female doesn't come as natural. I could really use some ideas for the section headers/dividers (Experience, Education, Skills/Computer Proficiency and Accomplishments). I originally was thinking a design similar to a folder tab, inverted. The fonts I'm using are: Whitman, Whitney and Caecilia. I know there's not much there, but every time I add something it seems really odd. Also, the order of things seems weird. She wants here Experience first, Education second and all the other filler, third.
I’d shorten the line length of that column on the right a wee bit (maybe an inch).
