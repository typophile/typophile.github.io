Couple of points: • The pen angles on the d and q are different from the rest of the font; did you just mirror the p? • The k is just odd but almost works.  (What does it look like in the source text?) • The s is too light in the main stroke of the letter. • The t is very awkward; note the height of the cross-bar and the rounding-off of the bottom of the stem in the original. • Where the original o has full-width stroke at either end of the stroke, yours just looks like somebody bit into the letter. • The v is a bit too wide, the y and the right half of the w much more so.  (Yes, I see that they're all the same width; they shouldn't be.) On the other hand… • Good job on replacing the teardrop dot on the i, though the height and shape could be improved.  (Maybe a slightly curve-y teardrop, almost a comma, a bit higher up? • Love the m.  This font needs more such flourishes—why'd you drop the dangling bit on the r? —Joel
