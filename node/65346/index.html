The Bujardet Freres font is not the same as your sample. See the R – it’s not the same. [edit: neither is the N] I Looked in the Solotype Catalog since often these revivals can be found there, and the closest I saw was one called Tuscan Dotted (p. 36), but it was not identical. The R did have a similar leg, unlike the Bujardet font. I think if you searched on Tuscan, or Victorian, or 1800s, you would probably see what’s available in styles of this vintage.

Yes, I think that would often be found in the styles I suggested searching for. The way the terminals split is one characteristic of the Tuscan styles, which you might also find with the other search terms. Solotype, HiH and a few other foundries, seem to specialize in more antique styles from the late 19th century. That is the time period I think you want to look at.
