EDITED: Looks like an outdoor sign. My guess is that it's not a font as such, but rather sign-maker's lettering. The effect could be duplicated with a fairly heavy, monoline, boxy or gas-pipe style wide sans. For example Valfle Extended [img:sites/default/files/old-images/ValfleExtended_MHS_5668.gif] http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/suitcase/vafle/ I trust a kindly typophile could recommend something that is a better match. For the _M_ and _H_ paste on small Clarendon style serifs. As for the the _S_ the paste could consist of the serif and terminal treatment from any of several "wild west" wood type designs. Good choices include Jeff Levine's Saddle Tramp JNL [img:sites/default/files/old-images/SaddleTrampJNL_S_5551.gif] http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/jnlevine/saddle-tramp/ Equally suitable, Blackoak [img:sites/default/files/old-images/Blackoak_S_6709.gif] already suggested by Kevin. In both of these cases some tinkering would be needed to get the terminal to match. Don
