In terms of production don't have bleeds on both the top and the bottom. You push the cost of printing up, that is if you are going to print. I would review your design based on what the industry is doing. Your design is light and airy like a boutique selling clothing but is that the image you want to present to your target audience. Do they want arty design or bold, productive, safe qualities. Its not a bad design but maybe not the right one for your industry.

2007-05-28 01:22:10 • Miguel Sousa
The back of the cow this way IS intentional, within the dairy industry, where cow "type" is pushed, a level (like a ruler) back is pushed as ideal, regardless of how unrealistic this might seem. I did use a picture of a 'show cow' for guidelines when doing the logo, I think I actually used a picture for an underlay to get it going. I believe that I might have actually shortened the back to make it fit better with the sign. http://www.comestar.qc.ca/lautamie.html This link shows an example of what you see typically in photographed show cows. So there you can see, flat back, and long proportions lend to the 'Dairy' character of the cow.
