[img:sites/default/files/old-images/KCMasterpieceLabelTypophile112896SM_3664.jpg] U.S. federal trademark registration for this design was filed on September 20, 2011 http://www.trademarkia.com/kc-masterpiece-85427596.html I believe this is lettering/artwork and not based on a single font. In some ways the letters are similar to a sans with Clarendon style serifs added to the base and top of the vertical strokes. Check for example the letter-forms and terminals for C, c and e in Euro Sans Cond Bold http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/profonts/euro-sans-pro/ There are also similar features with some serif fonts, such as the lobe on r Jakob Erbar's Candida, as suggested by Fivos. Also, Walbaum or Sirba http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/type-together/sirba/ for the top terminal on the s. The diamond dot on the i is a feature of some Fraktur designs. Don
