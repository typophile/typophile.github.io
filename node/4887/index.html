Get Bitstream's Type Odyessy instead.

Their "Swiss" is a very good Helvetica, and you get a lot more stuff than on the Linotype CDs (and in a larger range of weights), plus a nice range of more recent stuff.

Off the top of my head, versions of Futura and Franklin Gothic aren't included with Bitstream. If you need these, you may like to consider Linotype. I got both sets (LT and bitstream).

Yves

I'd agree with you but I really don't know what "really stand out" entails for you. Stand out to whom?

Personally, and I know this goes against the grain of current practice, but I'd rather have less rather than more fonts, or at least, a trusted few well thought out, technically proficient fonts, whose look I also happen to like, and whose text setting capability saves me a lot of time and money (by forgoing adjustments to letterform combinations, etc.).

So my recommendation would not be to go the CD route, as you also suggest, but for perhaps different reasons. In writing about the selection of metal types, Lewis Allen, in his book Printing with the Handpress, suggested selection after careful study, as "inferior tools corrode the spirit." Always kind of favored that remark.
