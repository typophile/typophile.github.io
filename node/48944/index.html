Your other Unknown type listed above was "Gruppe VII - Antiqua-Varianten - Unbekannte 2" and that looks like a typeface that was the inspiration for such faces as Trooper Roman, Bernase Roman, Romana and Hawthorn. In fact, De Vinne No. 11 also resembles this quite closely, with the biggest differences being the A, R and W. In the Jaspert description for De Vinne, it mentions that it was probably related to the Schelter & Geisecke "Romanisch". It further says that it was a basis for all "Romanisch, Romisch and Romaans" series produced in Europe. The somewhat confusing description also refers to "French Old Style" as "the basis for the Schelter & Giesecke re-cutting in 1889." Mac McGrew's book also shows a 1910 Linotype face called "Berlin Antique" that was "derived from a German source". Somewhere in all those related variations seems to be your typeface. - Mike Yanega
