Interstate -vs- FHWA

July 29, 2005 • hrant

Yesterday I spent some more time than usual on the freeways, driving more casually than usual (so deliberative driving, not immersive :-) and something hit me*: I saw a “p” with its shear at the bottom in the opposite direction as Interstate. In know that Interstate is touted as a “cleaned-up” version of the FHWA design (with this particular difference striking me as chirographic in motivation - and actually less effective than the original) and now I’ve become quite interested in analysing the differences between the two. I think such analysis would expose interesting clues as to how a designer thinks. If TFJ himself would elaborate, that would of course rule.

Is it possible that the “p” that sparked this whole thread was in fact a “q” applied backwards? I’ve seen a sign that used an upside down “m” instead of a “w”. Anyway, all the sheared stems in FHWA are cut southwest-to-northeast. Does Interstate do that differently with some letters?

By the way, I also like the new Clearview fonts for the most part; I’m not so enthusiastic about the numerals, however. I thought the FHWA numerals were quite effective, and some of the Clearview numerals look too much alike – specifically, the free strokes of the 6 and 9 curve back towards the loops more than in FHWA, making them less distinct from 8. I also miss the inflection in the middle of the curved stroke of the 2, but that may be more a matter of personal taste than function.
