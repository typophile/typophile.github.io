It's funny how one's perspective might change the headline. I guess Nick's "Quark still No. 1 for Canadian magazines" makes sense if your default assumption was that InDesign has dethroned Quark in the installed base. Looking deeper at the numbers, of the 46% of the market that was on QXP 4 and 5, and moved, 15/46 went to newer versions of Quark, and 27/46 went to InDesign, meaning that of Canadian magazines implementing newer DTP software in those three years, over 60% moved from Quark to InDesign rather than upgrading QuarkXPress. As a magazine AD at a major Canadian publication, I think it'll be interesting to see these survey results in 2007. We switched to InDesign about two years ago, but many of our sister publications have continued with Quark. The reason? InDesign's hardware requirements. Moving 200+ staff from OS9 iMacs running Quark 4 to OSX capable hardware has proven to be a multi-hundred-thousand dollar investment. Still, over the past two years, computers have been upgraded piecemeal, and now any publication that has the means has moved to InDesign. I think the plan is to have all 15 of our magazines over to InDesign by the end of the 2006 calendar year. I'm not sure if anyone's reading these stats as a lack of willingness to move to InDesign -- it's quite the opposite. Anyone here who's not using InDesign is chomping at the bit to rid themselves of Quark.

Yes, I know lots about the next version of InDesign, and no, I can't say anything. I got the original magazine article, and it was quite interesting. Their headline for the software part of the survey results? "Adobe, Microsoft, Quark are the 3 Kings." The survey had 67 responses from production managers responsible for 171 magazines altogether. Statistics Canada says there are 2383 magazines published in Canada, so that's about 7% of the total magazines. If it were a ransome sample, that would be enough to be pretty representative, but of course it's not. Hard to say just how representative it is. Some of the other stats from the same survey were interesting. For example... 2003: 10% on OS X, 48% more planned to adopt within 12 months. 2006: 82% on OS X, 11% more plan to adopt within 12 months. Ad files submission: 2003: 42% app file, 6% "Wacko & other," 30% PDF (28% X-1a, 2% regular), 11% PostScript/EPS, 8% TIFF-IT, 2% film 2006: 17% app file, 19% "Wacko & other," 40% PDF (18% X-1a, 22% regular), 14% PostScript/EPS, 7% TIFF-IT, 3% film "Wacko & other" is a category that includes taking PowerPoint, Word and other odd (for publishing) file formats. I was surprised to see the growth there, but I guess it means that people are being more responsive to customer demands. Interesting to see the 25% decrease in app file usage but only a 10% increase in PDF. Proofing takes many forms, but the single most common approach? None, at 23% (up from 18%). Cheers, T
