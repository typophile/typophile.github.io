Some more basic typesetting (copy setting) advice:

Nowadays, most text designs are “optimally” space for basic readability, so changing tracking to the extremes plus or minus will affect one’s reading experience.

If you have a general rule of spacing ALL typefaces at the SAME tracking (like all 12 pt. copy at -5 tracking in Quark) you will also have readability issues. Each typeface has different characteristics that you should take into consideration before universally tracking everything.

It doesn’t matter what it looks like on the screen. It’s the final printed piece that needs to be reviewed, evaluated and adjusted if necessary. So in reality, you can’t make arbitrary spacing decisions on the screen without knowing how it looks in print.

At least you should learn somethings about type even though your supervisor instructs you to the contrary.
