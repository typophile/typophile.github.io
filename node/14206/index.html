2005-08-03 18:37:25 • oldnick

This is a realy interesting thread. I’m probably ot going to be answering Marc’s question either (sorry Mark). I have been using sassoon for kids multimedia. I wanted to know about peoples thoughts on the essentials too:

x-height, point size, relative weight etc.

Also, more about actually setting the type, kerning, leading etc.

I guess the key really is maximum ledgibility. We have been using voice over for everything and supporting that with text on screen.

We have three point sizes for three age ranges: prep/foundation: 30pt sasoon sans grade 1-2: 26pt sasoon sans grade 3-4: 22pt sasoon sans grade 4-5: 18pt sasoon sans

These also had various kerning and leading setting.

We tried out some stuff and instinctively went for quite a bit of leading, up to 6pts for the younger kids.

There was a fair amount of discussion about ambiguity of letters in various fonts: a preference for a straight tail on the nine(sorry about the terminoloy…), as opposed to the upside down 6 was a good example of this. We looked at heaps of fun fonts, but for the younger kids, didn’t have enough differntiation between characters like 9. A capital I with ligatures was also necessary.

mrfussy, yes, totally agree with your comment, “letter identification first” I guess even if you’re not setting out to teach reading or writing, then it will still probably go a long way if what you’re doing can do that as well.

I think also that what is said on “kidstype.com” is a really important, in terms of what we were doing:

Teacher opinion, generally, favours sans serif typefaces because of the simplicity of the letter shapes

But children encounter a wide range of letterforms in their day-to-day environment

Publishers tend to listen to teachers because they influence the book-buying policy in schools

Children are seldom asked what they think about the typefaces in the books they read

I may have answered my own question here, but wanted other peoples feedback.
