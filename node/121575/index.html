Updated: Martina, which dates from the early 1990s, is apparently based on the photo-lettering era Filmotype Hudson. Some letters in Martina like E and perhaps A were alt letters in the Filmotype Hudson filmstrips. The alt E is found in the digital Filmotype Hudson as an alt E, but it does not include an alt A like the one in Martina. The Martina A is either an alt A that was in the filmstrips and was missed in the Filmotype digitization, or was created by editing a when the digital Martina was made. Don

Again, I am not clear as to whether there are legitimacy issues. Further Update: I checked both digital Martina and FontSite digital Hudson. The Digital outlines, node positions etc. are virtually identical so one is a clone of the other. Identifont states a 1992 copyright for Hudson by The Font Company. Internal information on the Hudson font now being sold by FontSite claims a copyright of 1996. Internal information on Martina claims copyright 1990-1993 FontBank, Inc. I have no idea which dates reflect reality and who copied whom. Don
