A Short History of the Printed Word
by Warren Chappell, Robert Bringhurst (Contributor) Publisher: Hartley & Marks; 2nd edition (April 2000) Paperback: 288 pages ISBN: 0881791547 Price: $24.95  Buy It! Reviewed by Paul Stanley: The title is only partly right. Short this certainly is, relative to the ground it covers. But it is hardly really a history — more a sort of chronology with comments. It does engage to some extent with technical developments, but it doesn't attempt any real cultural or economic analysis. Overall the tone is antiquarian rather than historical, and Bringhurst's sententious style grows, to my mind at least, tiresome here. Although it does cover territory other than books, it does not do so thoroughly; its focus is very much on high typographical culture. It can be read with some pleasure by those who are already interested in printing. But I am underwhelmed by it. There surely is a fascinating history of the "printed word" to be written; but I don't think this is it.

(Fritz Park)An education! Taking the reader from the birth of the printing press through the evolution of type from Gothic through to Roman.
