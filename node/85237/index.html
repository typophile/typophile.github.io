2011-09-09 04:53:07 • kerrytron

> I’m not sure if this was Lew’s decision or FontShop’s, but I’m sure most users would prefer to have fewer, more feature-rich fonts.

The conversation has moved on, but I thought I’d clarify just for the record.

Yes, Whitman is not the best choice for those on a tight budget. I agree, the way FontShop presents the package is potentially misleading.

Note: it’s not so much that the fonts were “broken out,” as that they’ve never been combined together.

The “8 fonts for $140” is a byproduct of these fonts being almost ten years old. At the time they were released, getting a choice of either OsF or LF as default with the U&lc and getting both versions of the fonts for a single license fee was a big deal. Now, not so much.

The OpenType fonts of Whitman are pretty much just straight conversions of the PostScript fonts. In that respect, they’re akin to the Adobe Std paradigm.

Just because a font is OT format doesn’t mean it will have every bell and whistle. I don’t think that either FontShop or Font Bureau claims that these fonts are “full-featured.”

I will mention, however, that an undocumented aspect of the current Whitman OT fonts is the fact that each variation has both figure sets, both accessible via OT features, but just different defaults. So, if you generally want OsF in text, you can select the OsF font; but then if you need an instance of LF, you can just apply the OT feature, instead of changing to the LF font. And vice versa.

But no, small caps have not been integrated yet. And so are counted as separately priced fonts.

I’m not excusing the fact that the fonts are out of date. Just explaining.
