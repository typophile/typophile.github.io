2010-04-07 20:27:32 • Si_Daniels

@Tim

Internet Explorer does not support style linking in connection with @font-face.

This is incorrect, yes. font-style and font-weight are supported quite well. But in a different way than is proposed in the CSS3 spec. (In short, certain data must be congruent with the style and weight values in the @font-face declarations. Frankly, in some ways it is a more sure-footed system than what exists in the CSS3 proposal.)

As the co-creator of EOTFAST I’ve had a bit of leg up on this because I was able to create compressed EOT files very quickly and easily and therefore test quickly and easily. See the documentation for EOTFAST for more details on this. In fact, I’m proposing to deliver a paper on preparing fonts for IE 6, 7, and 8 at Atypi in Dublin later this year.

Also, here is a test page with a complete font-family declared and, in IE, you’ll see the regular, italic, bold, and bold italic faces appearing just as they should. A more complicated page, featuring a wide variety of fonts in different weights and styles is here: Ten Great Free Fonts Cross Browser. (Informal notes on what was done to prepare the fonts are at the bottom of the page.)

Tip: There seems to be much blind ideological hatred of Microsoft and Internet Explorer. There is also a point of view that would like to see widespread use of @font-face postponed for as long as possible. Unfortunately, in an effort to drive both agendas, it’s gotten to the point where misleading statements like, “IE does not support style linking in connection with @font-face” are being made even by those who know it’s inaccurate.
