You don’t use kerning in a monospace font, because it defeats the purpose.

Proportions: an x-height of 50% of ascender is very small, especially for a design like this. So I would shoot for very wide forms; maybe start with a totally circular lowercase letter “oh” and see how much narrower you’d like to make it, working with the rest of the lc in parallel. A generous width will also help the unusually tall caps and numerals (which I presume also need to be at cap/ascender height) not to look too narrow/cramped.

BTW:

  • What about the descenders?
  • The “t” usually doesn’t [need to] go to full ascender height; and especially in a font with a very small x-height that will look very idiosyncratic.
