What advice do you have for a designer trying to embark in designing a new typeface?

1: Get hold of a broadnibbed pen and start drawing! Essential for understanding the fundamentals in type. 2: Print out typefaces you like really huge and study them. 3: Take a look at this: http://typophile.com/courses/type110

Guess there are tons of other stuff to learn from.

Think up a concept. Study lots of existing, good fonts that share some of the characteristics you want yours to have (I personally prefer doing this directly in FontLab). I’ve found this even more insightful than the “Designing Type” book; YMMV.

Also, I’ve got to agree what Jonathan said about posting stuff here for critique. Vastly helpful. Btw, it doesn’t hurt to start really early: I’ve gotten great input even on really wonky first sketches.
