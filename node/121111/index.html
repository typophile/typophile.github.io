Thanks to those who have offer suggestions and help so far. The biggest issue I am running into is that these were either wood, metal, or hot-metal typefaces that were then printed on cheap newsprint. Some of them have been digitised, but not 100% EXACTLY like the original versions.

So far, this is what I have found:

SARONNI FAVOLOSO Actual Typeface: Metropol designed by Aldo Novarese, released by Nebiolo in 1967 Best Digital Equivalent: Press Gothic Pro - http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/canadatype/press-gothic/pro/

MOSER Actual Typeface: Metal Helvetica Black? Best Digital Equivalent: Neue Haas Grotesk Pro Display 95 Black - http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/neue-haas-grotesk/pro-display-95-black/

FANTASICO Actual Typeface: Condensed metal Helvetica? (this was the most difficult to match digitally) Best Digital Equivalent: Helvetica Neue 87 Cond Heavy - http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/neue-helvetica/helvetica-87-heavy-condensed/

HA BATTUTO DI 343 METRI Actual Typeface: Hot-metal Helvetica Condensed Bold Italic? Best Digital Equivalent: Helvetica Pro Bold Condensed Oblique - http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/helvetica/pro-bold-condensed-oblique/

Any additional suggestions or help is greatly appreciated.
