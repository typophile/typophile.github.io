ITC Berkeley goes pro

June 7, 2006 • Addison Hall

I’ve recently been hired by two new clients that both require me to use Berkeley Old Style (who’d of thunk it?) so I visit itcfonts.com and lo-and-behold, there’s Berkeley Pro fresh out this month! I know Tony Stan gets ripped here every now and then (mainly for ITC Garamond) but this looks nice if you prefer a more sedate Goudy font. Has anyone else tried this out?

I’ve never really liked Berkeley, I wouldn’t really consider it sedate, especially with the victorian crossbar on the e. I had a client once who’s identity guidelines required Berkeley Old Style set at 85% width with -40 tracking. It was beyond painful to read. By contrast, the regualar setting of started to look really nice. They also used Avenir with 115% width. Anyway, the “Pro” version just looks like they bundled it up into opentype format.

Does Tony Stan remind anyone else of Iron Man?

2006-06-12 16:25:03 • Addison Hall

I have seen Berkeley used very successfully.

I think more ‘sedate’ is accurate, and that can be good or bad depending on what you want. If you want something with that Goudy warmth but less assertive–with the slanted ‘e’–then Berkeley is good. It is well drawn and sets very smoothly.

By the way, the slanted crossbar is not Victorian, but Renaissance–Jenson.
