2007-04-20 17:49:38 • non

A photopolymer plate is a thin, hard plastic plate mounted on a thin metal backing where the stuff to be printed is raised. A special machine makes the plates, from regular old film output (or even directly/digitally I assume). It can cost as little as 50 cents per square inch. But you do need a thick metal back to raise the plate to type-high, and those aren’t very cheap (although many letterpress outfits will have some handy). I printed my business cards this way: http://typophile.com/node/21340

2007-04-20 18:30:50 • hrant
Polymer plates also come with adhesive backing which sticks to a base plate, so you’d make your digital file, send the PDF to a platemaker (like, for example, Boxcar), buy a base plate (Hrant’s right, they ain’t cheap), then stick the polymer plate to the base and start printing. Anything you can do on a computer you can do with a polymer plate (with varying levels of result; however, polymer doesn’t do transitions well, so I’d steer clear of photogrpahs or vectors with gradients, etc.).
2007-04-20 18:41:23 • jason

And for plates with no adhesive on the back you have two choices: spray-on adhesive (3M makes a good one apparently), or a high-magnetism Bunting base, which is really expensive. BTW the degree to which you need any adhesive at all (the point is to prevent drift down the press every time you roll) depends on: how small your plate is; and how important (vertical) registration is. For larger pieces in one color and loose margins you’re generally OK sans I think.

non

Yes, pretty much anything you can create digitally in a page layout program (um, in this day and age, maybe I should say in any Adobe program—actually anything PostScript) can be reproduced via the letterpress process. So it doesn’t matter if you have altered the tracking. Your file would be made, initially, into a film negative, which is then processed as a photopolymer plate. When mounted type high on a base, it can be printed letterpress.

Prices have not changed significantly from the 1990s but do vary slightly from processor to processor. I’m a processor so take that as you may. Of course, you do need a press to print this stuff. And, as mentioned, a base.
