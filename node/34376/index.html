It depends on what the “setting” is done on.

When we were developing ClearviewHwy we found that White letters on Dark backgrounds (what you all call negative, but what is called Positive Contrast) required broader spacing, but a lighter weight letter. The Black letters on Light backgrounds (what you call positive, but what is called Negative Contrast) the design needed to be bolder and more tightly spaced. This has everything to do with the retro-reflective material that modern guide signs are made with.

So the question remains, what environment are the “Negative” and “Positive” settings created for? 4-color printing? Retro-reflective sheeting? Back-Lit illumination? Video? Each situation will require a different solution.
