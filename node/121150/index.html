The photolettering/filmtype firms had first class lettering artists who redrew many of the classic fonts. Sometimes as formal fonts, but more often as not they were custom projects for record labels, book covers etc. Most of these creations have not been digitized. Circa 1970 was at the peak of this process. Don

2014-11-08 23:08:00 • kthomps5
Aurea looks like a knock-off of Nova Augustea (1951), by Alessandro Butti and Aldo Novarese for Nebiolo, inspired by Roman inscriptions. Unfortunately, the only legit digital version available is the all-caps handtooled Augustea Open (http://www.myfonts.com/search/name%3A%28augustea+open%29). The font in the sample looks like someone took Nova Augustea and placed Schneidler fishtail serifs. A few images of Nova Augustea’s specimen: http://www.aiap.it/cdpg/?ID=3767&IDsubarea=169&IDsez=188 A recent thread with several pointers: http://typophile.com/node/117980
