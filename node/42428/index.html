The whole page was done in lithography, which means that the separate colourplates were drawn on lithographic stones. Lettering was mostly done by hand, using samples or from heart. In this case a special technique appears to have been used, where type was printed on special paper and offset on the stone. These typesamples could have been done/printed esp for the purpose, or words were comped out of sample sheets. As you have probably noticed some of the glyphs in your image are reversed (upside down, like the two s’es in Passiflora). For the inspiration/source you’ll have to look into contemporary type (not the nicest period imho).

. . . Bert Vanderveen BNO
