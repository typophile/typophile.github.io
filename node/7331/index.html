Most important vs. Most importantly

October 25, 2004 • ginaleen

I'm new to this forum, but as a proofreader I often have clarification questions.

So, can anyone tell me the reason behind putting in the ly, or leaving it out? Opinions differ around here. Thank you!!
It's a question of context. Important is an adjective, importantly an adverb.

"Of all the reasons for war, the most important is…." (important is an adjective modifying reasons)

"Why did we go to war? Most importantly, we went because the President said…." (importantly is an adverb, modifying went)

Note that in the second sentence it would not be unusual to leave out the verb phrase "we went," yet it would still be implied.

Leastways, that's my understanding. But it's been many years since I made my living as an editor.

Gina, as Tom has explained, it depends whether what is 'most important' is a noun or a verb. At the beginning of a sentence, it depends what follows, e.g.

'Most importantly, we should CONSIDER [verb] whether the phrase applies to a noun or to a verb.'

'Most important, in our consideration, is the QUESTION [noun] whether the phrase applies to a noun or to a verb.'
