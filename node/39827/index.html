Help: Companion Roman for Futura?

December 6, 2007 • LeslieB22

Dear Typophiles, has anyone a suggestion for a roman that'd be a good text face companion for Futura? I'm designing a flyer / magazine ad and later a website for a music competition from a non-profit. The competition and the organisation are focused on an unusual Modern composer - active from the 30's through the 70's, who flouted the atonal trends of the time to compose music that was melodic and harmonic yet very fresh, clear and modern-sounding. When I heard the music I thought of Futura - modern yet (some think, Bringhurst does at least) humanist. So that's what I've been using for this client for past couple years. But these days I'm not as into Futura as when I used to be able to print it in lead (my college print shop had some nice chunky sizes that looked really great) and I'd like to round it out with a text face. But I can't figure out how to match or complement those round, wide shapes and long ascenders - help! Thanks, Leslie
Maybe it's because I saw them paired together quite a bit in the '80s, but I think of Baskerville when it comes to serif companions for Futura. Baskerville's round shapes, low x-height (in the non-ITC versions) and long ascenders/descenders make it a decent match. Mrs. Eaves (a Baskerville derivative) might work, too.
