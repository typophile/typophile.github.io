engraver's caps slab serif

June 27, 2013 • Terrence Chouinard

Anyone out there with a clue as to what this Engravers Roman-ish slab serif face is? Western Railway is close candidate, but no cigar: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/jnlevine/western-railway/

Note the generally triangular serifs. This resembles a 19th century style then called Latin. Not certain if there is an exact digital match, but if there is one, our typophiles will probably find it for you. This is pretty close, a font from the Connor’s Sons catalog of 1888 digitized as Ysleta by Nick Curtis. In this font the Latinate serifs are clipped in an angled style called Detroit or Mansard – a refinement that may or may not be present in your samples. This version of the Latinate style was also known as Aetna or Painter’s Gothic. Don
