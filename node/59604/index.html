Here - yes, in general - no, a good third of people prefers #2.

By the way, I started using http://feedbackarmy.com and it is quite useful for gathering the feedback from random people. Basically you submit a URL, phrase some questions, pay them a fee and they post your request on Amazon’s Mechanical Turk. If you haven’t seen Turk before, [[https://www.mturk.com/mturk/welcome|have a look]]. The most clever side project that came out of Amazon to date. Answers do vary in quality obviously, but there is an option of rejecting the answer if it is not good or off the subject.

The most popular remark against #2 was “too much white space”, especially on larger monitors with high resolution .. which is something that I would’ve never picked up on my own as my monitor is not of that kind. All in all, pre-release feedback rules.

Thanks a lot guys, your answers helped a lot. We are starting public beta on Monday. If you feel like dropping by, do drop by :)
