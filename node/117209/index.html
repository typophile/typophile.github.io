Late 19th or early 20th century artwork in the form of a trademark. The most interesting features are the median spurs, the terminals on S and the leg on the R. Median spurs were usually used on Tuscan, i.e. split or fishtail terminal fonts, rather than ones with flat terminals. A font in the latter style was digitized by Dan X. Solo under the name Cavalcade. Unfortunately the only legitimate way to get this font is by purchase of a new or used copy of “24 Circus Display Fonts (Dover Electronic Display Fonts) (CD-ROM and Book)” Previous discussion of a median spur non-Tuscan font http://typophile.com/node/116784 The trademark could be expanded to make an attractive font, pehraps with plain and rimmed variations. Don
