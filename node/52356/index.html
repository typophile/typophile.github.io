See that little spot under the arrow where the second hump of m comes into the shoulder of the first, and there’s a tiny bit of a curve there? In most faces this would be straight all the way up but I can’t really do that here. I can’t decide if it looks hideous and amateurish or if it’s a cute little quirk. So I decided to take this route: [img:sites/default/files/old-images/mmmm_5563.png] i.e. making the first 'hump' without a smooth curve like the right one, so that it joins more naturally. Maybe you can try something similar?
2008-12-04 17:01:46 • Quincunx
I wonder if just giving a little more curve to the "straight" parts of the hump might help. As it is, with the way it intersects with the first hump, optically the curve almost turns inside out and looks like an S-curve coming out of the top of the first hump. Of course, what the other letters look like will establish how much this will stick out. Your h and m humps look (for lack of a better term) a bit architectural (like [[http://www.fontshop.com/search/?q=tekton&x=0&y=0|Tekton]] etc.), but your a has round curves that are quite different. If the smoother and more symmetrical curve at the top of the a is part of this font's aesthetic, one might be left wondering why the same curve wasn't used to make more conformist humps in the other letters.
