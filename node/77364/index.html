The Unit System of Foundry Type

December 19, 2010 • quadibloc

Having noted, from an ATF catalog, some time ago that 3-to-em and 5-to-em spaces, particularly, for many type sizes were not actually at their nominal sizes, but instead at even multiples of 1/4 or 1/2 point, I had wondered if this were true even for regular foundry typefaces, and not merely the “Self-Spacing” or “Typotabular” faces.

I recently had the good fortune to have a look at the classic and celebrated work, “Typographical Printing-Surfaces”, which has a wealth of information on many subjects related to typography. There is interesting information there about typesetting in some languages other than English, and about automatic typesetting machines other than Monotype and Linotype - which are also covered, as is a modification of the Linotype machine to cause it to cast individual types like a Monotype.

It gave a table of the widths of the characters in two 12-point foundry fonts. All the widths except one were multiples of 1/4 point, the other being a multiple of 1/8 point. It seemed to me that there was a tendency to favor multiples of 3/4 point, which at first I took to be the unit of the system.

That book is usefully available on [[http://books.google.com/books?id=cmjPAAAAMAAJ&dq=typographical+printing+surfaces&source=gbs_navlinks_s|Google Books]]. Were you looking at tables 7 & 8 (pp. 72 & 73)?
