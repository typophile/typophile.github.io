Q4: Did the profession, compared to former days, change? If yes, how and what makes you see that in practice? Yes, we got computers, which meant that the subspecialties of graphic design such as typesetting and mechanical production got absorbed into the job of "graphic designer". The old ways required you to sketch and conceive of your design in advance of committing to it. The computer is more intuitive and process-oriented; you can tweak and massage a design until it feels right and fits your parameters. I wouldn't go back, but I'm glad I learned the old methods. Q5: Nowadays everyone’s in the possession of photo editing software, word-art, dtp-software. Users think they are designers fast, although they have no or little knowledge of the design process. Do you see something of that ‘anyone is a designer principle’ in, for example the way your clients brief you or give you freedom? Everyone thought they were designers before the computer too. The biggest difference I find is that now they think you can work much faster, and expect things to happen in the blink of an eye. Q8: Can anyone (in the possession of a computer) become a graphic designer / call himself a (graphic) designer? Anyone can become a graphic designer and call him/herself that. Not everyone can become a good graphic designer. Q9: Do you call yourself graphic designer? If no, what do you call yourself / what term do you use? Yup.
