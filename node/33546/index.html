Secondary Typeface Needed to Complement Farnham

May 6, 2007 • Reed Reibstein

I'm looking for a typeface for my school newspaper to complement Farnham, which we use for headlines, and Mercury, used for body text. I redesigned the newspaper over the summer and have been using Myriad Pro for sidebars and captions and Chaparral Pro for "nuts and bolts" (all the text not tied to any particular story). Both faces have served me well, but now that the redesign has had some time to breathe, I want to get something that really works with the design. What I'm looking for: I want a typeface that can be used for everything other than headlines and body text: pullout quotes, the masthead, captions, jumplines, etc. Because we have a very tiny staff that, while willing to learn, does not have much in the way of training in graphic design, I'd like the typeface to be extremely typographically interesting; it should not recede, but rather pop, to make up for potentially uninteresting layouts. I chose Farnham for this reason; it's strong angularity and serifs are extremely forceful and compelling. Mildly contradictorily, the fonts should be clean -- nothing incredibly distracting to the reader. Finally and obviously, the typeface needs to work well with Farnham and Mercury, as well as within the confines of a newspaper. After some research, I came up with three very strong contenders: Amplitude, Shaker, and John Sans. Amplitude has the requisite character, and Christian Schwartz himself says that it works well with Farnham. Shaker shares Amplitude's and Farnham's angularity, but is a bit subtler (although not too much so). John Sans is a softer typeface, which adds a nice touch of contrast, is based on a transitional model like Farnham is, and has letterforms that are funky enough for me. So, first, does anyone know which of those three I listed will work especially well/fail miserably? And second, are there any that I should definitely consider as well? Thanks a ton for any assistance. Below is a page from our last issue for a bit of context: [img:sites/default/files/old-images/April Front Final Small_6580.jpg]
I'd look for something with a little 19th Century sturdiness and character: FF Hydra, FF Bau, Maple (Process Type Foundry), Champion (H&FJ), and the new Parry Grotesque (OurType) would all play well with your serifs.

2007-05-08 22:23:45 • Reed Reibstein
As usual, I spent too long debating every little merit of each typeface and ended up coming right back to where I started: I finally bought Shaker as the secondary sans for the newspaper. (EDIT: The same thing happened when buying Farnham and Mercury in the first place: they were among my first choices, but I vacillated for over a month. In the future, I'll try to have more faith in my instincts.) Tiffany's suggestion got me thinking that Amplitude was too similar to Farnham and would feel monotonous, and John Sans and Parisine Plus didn't appeal to the other staffers (not newsy enough). So it came down to Neutraface and Shaker. After comparing them, I felt that the geometric Art Deco feel of Neutraface wasn't quite right for the publication, so Shaker it is. So far, I've gotten a good response from my colleagues, and I'm really looking forward to implementing it in the next issue. Thanks again to everyone who chimed in; I really appreciate your suggestions and concerns.
