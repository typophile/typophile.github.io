2005-07-18 23:25:46 • f0rk

Overall, I like the hierarchy. Get rid of the two word spaces after a period. And make sure you never use them again. It’s a bad typewriter-era habit.

I agree with Nick about the all small caps. Using the regular caps to highlight the company’s products doesn’t feel consistent enough to me. It looks like an error to my eye. If you have to differentiate between generic and the company’s ingredients, I’d go with something that’s a variation applied thoroughly to the type. For this reason, small caps may not work for the ingredients (though otherwise I like it). A roman/italic contrast might be more appropriate. Roman/Bold might be suitable but the bold might throw the hierarchy of the page off. Or maybe I’m talking out of my ass. It might be subtle and obvious enough to get the job done.

As for the type size, try standing at your kitchen counter with a vegetable in one hand and a knife in the other, then turn your head and try to read the ingredients laying on the counter hands-free. But my gut tells me the smaller proportions of Garamond probably make 8 pt an iffy bet.
