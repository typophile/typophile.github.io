with no images in the manuscript itself

I would think that some photos of your wife’s injuries and her recover would be very appropriate and would add to the book’s impact, especially if placed near the text describing those events.

for both print publishing …[and] eBook

Make sure that the portfolios of the designers you talk to include examples of both print books and ebooks that they’ve designed. Ask for references so you can contact some of their previous clients to ask how the jobs went. Be sure your contract includes at least a general timeframe.

And be aware that most design jobs go through several rounds of revisions, which may be additional charges unless the original estimate includes a certain number of rounds of revisions.

That’s where a “graphic designer” could be appropriate.

with text & no pictures, how much back & forth can there be about the interior design?

Well I’m hoping the author considers using some pictures, as I’ve seen them used effectively in some books which have related themes. Entirely up to him, of course.

But even without pictures, the amount of work involved can vary depending on the author’s wishes. I’ve been involved with self-published book projects where the author asked me to set the entire book (this is for print, not an ebook) because he wanted professional help with things like proper punctuation (he had hyphens and dashes mixed up, for example), ellipses (he inadventently used several different styles), widows and orphans, smart quotes, proper use of italics, good line breaks and page breaks, etc.

It gave his book a more professional look, and he was happy to pay for that service. Many authors would choose to save some money by doing all that themselves, of course.
