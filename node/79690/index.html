Characters per line - a question

March 4, 2011 • katij

Dear Typophiles, I am designing a magazine (text set in 2 columns) and using Minion Pro at 9pt. I know the general rule when setting text columns is 39 characters per line in order to achieve readability, but I also know it depends on the font and language. InDesign's info panel tells me my lines are 53 characters long. When I print it out I find it quite easy to read, but maybe it's just me. I also would like to keep the 2 columns setting, because I think it suits the theme of the magazine well (short philosophical texts written in an understandable manner). What do you think? Is it still OK? Do I need to write more information? Thank You, k.
The "general rule" is only a guide - good design depends more on what works and if it reads easily it is working.

unless you want to drive old people away from your magazine -- which is sometimes not the worst thing for you to be doing, but you should do it with full intention, not just accidentally -- you shouldn't use 9-point type for anything people should read. and making it 11-point type will mean fewer characters per inch. -bowerbird p.s. some convenience stores broadcast a high-pitched sound which only youngsters can hear, so they won't loiter at the store. it all boils down to a strategy of targeting the audience you want.
