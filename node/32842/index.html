Suggestions for a text-weight face?

April 6, 2007 • mlyn

I was recently hired into a creative placement agency as a junior graphic designer. Their awesome brand has been poorly managed for a while, and I’m plugging holes right and left.

The display face for advertising and corporate material is Trade Gothic, primarily medium and bold no. 2. We need an alternative for body text. So far they have been using Verdana. Happily, a designer working within the company (in a non-designer capacity) knows how wrong this is and suggested that we switch.

Her suggestion was Arial. I immediately said “How about Helvetica?” But I know Helvetica has its oddities too, from the Humanist R to the inconsistent tails. I’m wondering if there is something even better to suggest. I’ve been surfing the internet all afternoon looking for some good opinions.

Necessities: sans serif, any and all media but especially print, text size; available for corporate use license without spending a fortune. The audience is a design-groovy company that represents designers, so it should be functional and interesting, but not ostentatious.
