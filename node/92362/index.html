Glyph components - L.C.D.

April 22, 2012 • inkexit

There was a text on type design, Pretty sure I read it in PDF format, though not sure if it came from a book, or from a website, or a presentation or what.. And in this text was a picture of all the shapes you need to construct the basic latin alphabet, upper and lower case. Can’t remember every shape that was there, but it wasn’t more than a dozen or so. I remember it had the bowl shape, a straight line shape, tittle shapes, etc… The idea was, these are the only shapes you need. Of course this would result in a very homogenized font with no difference in shape between the b, d, p, and q, for example, but nevertheless, the shapes presented were the lowest common denominator for constructing a complete set of latin glyphs.

Anybody familiar with what I’m talking about? I have no idea where I saw this now and would like to read through it again and become more familiar with these “pieces”

Sounds like Gerard Unger’s contribution to FUSE back in the nineties—a font composed of letter elements.
