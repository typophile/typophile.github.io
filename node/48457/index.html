2008-08-19 21:04:00 • Theunis de Jong
In traditional chinese, punctuation and characters share the same width, because this makes things easier for setting text in both ways. In simplified chinese, the width of punctuation varies, because it's only set in one way, and they are left-aligned. There is no dash in the picture....it's "one". An em-dash is twice as long as that, and there is no en-dash. Character pairs... ;-) There are some hilarious jokes derived from incorrectly paired characters, but no it doesn't really matter that much. I'm not an expert on mixing chinese and english together, but I usually just decide leading based on chinese and justify english later on. I try to avoid mixing them up mostly, because english words breaks up the "rhythm", in other words it interrupts the reading.
