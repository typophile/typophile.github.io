2. When was Gill Sans digitized?

You mean, when was Gill Sans first digitized? Most likely in the mid-nineteen-seventies during the early years of Dr. Peter Karow’s development and expansion of the [[IKARUS]] type digitizing system. Do you need hard dates?

Fonts were digitized even BEFORE high-end digital type-imagesetters were available. The IKARUS system was used initially for CAD/CAM expansion of type families by interpolating weights.

By the late 1970s, the first digital typesetting equipment came into production, and vector/spline outlines were used for font data. This was all before Macintosh was even a “seed” in Job’s eye!

Gill Sans has a history prior to the drawings he did for Monotype dating from the period he worked with Edward Johnston, which, he acknowledged, influenced the forms that he introduced into his sans face. According to Fiona MacCarthy’s biography the first recognisable samples of Gill Sans was used in hand painted signs at Capel-y-ffin and later 1926 (or 1927) for Douglas Cleverdon’s shopfront, the uppercase drawings for Monotype were dated 1927 and lowercase 1928. Morison apparently commissioned the work in 1927. Tim
