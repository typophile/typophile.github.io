Trademarks in Logotypes

August 25, 2005 • James Provost

I’ve recently started doing design work for financial advisors, on top of the ninetofive at a commercial signage shop/digital printer’s. I’ve done logo design at the day job in the past, but obviously the clientele there (mostly of the Mom & Pop’s Fish & Chips variety) isn’t half as concerned with protecting their marks as the financial advisors.

I was wondering how to approach placing Trademark and Registered Trademark symbols tastefully on logos. Do I want them to ‘nest’ with the letterforms and blend in, or seperate them and leave them hovering like an insect? What about the size relationship? Naturally a logo must be printed at different sizes, does the trademark remain a constant size or should it resize too?

Insight appreciated.

I say make it as small as possible for it to still be legible. Unobtrusive is the key.

2005-08-26 06:29:01 • hrant

There is really no rule. As a rule of thumb, the TM* should be as non obtrusive as possible. Our firm will occasionally develop several different optical variants of a mark, intended for different size applications. A feature of these variants normally incorporates different sizes of the TM or (R) to match the intended reproduction size of the mark.

Go with a placement that works with the mark. Normally it follows the mark, and is most commonly located at the top (although recent trends have seen this changing).

*I am assuming you will mostly be working with a TM, not a registered mark.

Also please note that the ® symbol can only be used if the logotype has in fact been registered with the U.S. Patent Office. The TM, on the other hand, does not require registration but offers only common-law protection to the mark.
