Making handwriting into a font

November 9, 2010 • Jean Paul Beumer

Today we advised a client to digitise his own handwriting so he can use it as part of his corporate identity. He likes the idea very much. I know of software and sites like yourfonts.com where you can upload your handwritten glyphs so they make a font out of it. It's under $10, not even a large amount when the result is awful. Have any of you had any experience with this site? My main worry is that yourfonts.com creates a font solely made out of single characters, not out of sentences which will flow much more. I would like to do it myself, but perhaps using the yourfonts.com-font is a good starting point? Suppose I do it myself, what should I charge? Any ideas on how much time this will take?
Some of these services don't allow commercial use of the font, so read the small print.

A lot depends on how cursive the writing is. These days, a good cursive handwriting font is a huge undertaking, due to the amount of OpenType contextualization that is now employed. However, if the writing is more like printing, with discrete letters, then it's a lot easier to make. Perhaps you could suggest your client writes in more of that style?
