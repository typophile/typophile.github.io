2007-03-01 03:29:30 • Linda Cunningham

Eben, what you say is absolutely true; ILL is a great resource and maybe not as widely known or utilised as it should be. Depending on where you are, you may find that local academic and specialist libraries (like St. Brides) will also allow you to apply as a patron/borrower.

I know that certain academic libraries form consortia, which allows them to both pool resources and extend their customer bases – my university for example, has reciprocal lending arrangements with several other local university libraries and the national library. Very much a case of ‘…if we haven’t got it on our shelves, we certainly know someone who will…’

My librarian recently used a consortium scheme called Borrow Direct (possibly only available to librarians?) to lend me, free of charge, a copy of John Dreyfus' ‘Into Print’ – a book I would have remained ignorant of without her interest and ability. Needless to say, it’s fascinating!
