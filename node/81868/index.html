With GREP, you could search for \s*-\s* and replace that by – (use spaces instead of the underscores). This will match any number of spaces (including zero) and any kind of spaces (including non-breaking and thin ones etc.), before and after the hyphen.

One of the built-in GREP searches is “Multiple Spaces to Single Space”. It’s better than just searching for “space space”, because it’ll also take care of situations where there are more than 2 spaces.

I use it routinely on documents to eliminate multiple spaces.
