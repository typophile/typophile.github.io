It would help if you had given some idea as to the date of the book i.e. 1910s, 1920s, 1950s, 1990s etc. This might be a font for which a digital version has not been made. It would also help if you had posted a larger scale image with some “earmark” letters such as g, Q, K, R, A, M. The posted image is, however, sufficient to make a suggestion or two. I note its low contrast and, using my imaginary microscope, I believe I might be seeing large rounded serifs dished at the midpoint. This type of font is sometimes described as being in an English vernacular style. So as a digital substitute I could perhaps recommend Plantagenet by Ross Mills of Trio Typeworks. The lowercase a is two story rather than single, but the flavor of Plantagenet is the same as your sample. [img:sites/default/files/old-images/PlantagenetInUse_4234.jpg] Don
