2011-07-15 07:06:29 • PabloImpallari

Dynamic fonts (no Flash) is certainly the best option. Typekit is fine, but you make sure you pick something that also renders well under the worst conditions: Win XP. Many resellers have browser samples.

For good text type, a place to start is the masterful Webtype. There’s also Fontfont, Typotheque’s Fedra Screen, Ascender fonts and Adobe, along with [[http://typedia.com/blog/post/cure-for-the-common-webfont-part-2-alternatives-to-georgia/|this list]] and [[http://typedia.com/blog/post/cure-for-the-common-webfont-part-1-alternatives-to-arial-and-helvetica/|this]].

For display, the market is bigger, as the type designer doesn’t need to make such a huge effort to render the fonts acceptable at larger sizes.
