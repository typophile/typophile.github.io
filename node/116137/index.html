As usual, a client that just says feelings and nothing more concrete than what I am looking for.

Can anyone suggest? I am more leaning towards san serif typefaces since it is a tagline of sorts (can’t say the tagline really, but something that in service with joy.)

“Happy” is pretty subjective but I suppose it has about the same effect as cute, or rounded, and avoiding a sterile mechanical style like Helvetica. For a sans try Yummo http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/flat-it/yummo/ But why restrict your choices to a sans? For example check out Laura Worthington’s fat hybrid _Ganache _ http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/laura-worthington/ganache/ Or fairy tales handwritten roman letters like Besley Hand http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/juraj-chrastina/besley-hand/ Think outside the box. BTW “happy” and “serious” seem to be incompatible as font criteria. But I can see “happy” and well designed as a great combination. Don

Strange – why does he want this to be “serious”? My only guess is he doesn’t want it to look retarded-happy, like maybe this: http://media.chick-fil-a.com/Media/Img/catalog/Pressroom/ImageLibrary/logos/Chick-fil-A-logo-sm.jpg?download=true

In my view the main things that make a font friendly/happy are weight, width and a large x-height; it can have serifs, as long as their not delicate; and some controlled irregularity might help here too.

So what about this? http://typographica.org/typeface-reviews/swagg/
