2009-08-26 13:31:26 • SavillesLoveChild

I feel that your approach is correct, by trying to find something unique about the area to incorporate into the logo. The directions you’ve been exploring seem to be more fitting of a coffee distribution company, a bit more corporate, more refined, and to me they lack personality. I think of a cafe to be inviting, somewhere you want to have a cup of coffee.

You’ve mentioned that your client rejected the first idea, because it was too modern. Although, maybe you should revisit that idea. Ask yourself what would that logo look like if it took on a different personality. Use that logo as a starting point and move away from it, change the colors, thin out the line, strip away the pieces, take them apart, and then reassemble them. Think of those elements as your palette.

Regarding color, blue isn’t a color closely associated with food and drink. It does not inspire people to eat. Research has shown that blue decreases one’s appetite.

I also agree with “aluminum” —using the logotype on the bathroom doors is diluting the strength of the identity. You wouldn’t want to brand bathroom doors with the same emphasis as the logo that’s representing food product.
