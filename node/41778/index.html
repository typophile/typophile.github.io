Let’s face the facts here; Whether you like it or not, good old fashioned Direct Mail (that’s ‘junk mail’ to the average consumer) works and is here to stay for the foreseeable future. Given that this is such a tried and tested approach to hooking new business, why oh why do SO many companies treat it like the advertising equivalent of a drunken scribble on a pub napkin?

I had a flyer through my front door today offering a ‘third world clothing collection service’. You know the one; you give your old stuff to this company and they sell it on for a profit based upon the pretense of philanthropy. Well, this particular flyer was possible the single worst example of copywriting I have ever seen. Unforgivable grammatical errors, duplicated statements and spelling mistakes to embarrass the average six year old.

Not that I would have been conned into giving them my belongings anyway, but this just made their hole even deeper. I really hope someone got fired for that. I know that some people in adland have a somewhat supercilious attitude to DM and feel it beneath them, but it really is, in my opinion, a powerful tool in the salesman’s arsenal. Ogilvy said it fifty years ago and it still holds true today.

Direct Marketers, please, for the sake of my sanity, have at least a little pride in your work. Of course this does not apply to everyone in the sector; as this rather extravagant example from BMW proves. Genius: http://www.adverbox.com/mini-5/
