2014-11-20 17:29:09 • JamesM

After more research, I still believe it is likely a forms ruler, possibly intended for use by programmers. I came to this conclusion because older dot-matrix printers and some modern printers have the ability to print at 17 cpi. All the other measurements, possibly including the metric one, would be useful for the same reason because they are all standard cpi measurements. I think the ruler shown in the image may be fairly old and possibly was a proprietary ruler.

The following list is from a Panasonic printer setup manual which shows all of its various cpi capabilities. Pica (10 cpi): 80 cpl Elite (12 cpi): 96 cpl Micron (15 cpi): 120 cpl Compressed (17 cpi): 137 cpl Elite compressed (20 cpi): 160 cpl Pica elongated (5 cpi): 40 cpl Elite elongated (6 cpi): 48 cpl Micron elongated (7.5 cpi): 60 cpl Compressed elongated (8.5 cpi): 68 cpl Elite compressed elongated (10 cpi): 80 cpl

If anyone has a better idea that involves 17-to-the-inch measurements I think we would all love to hear about it.
