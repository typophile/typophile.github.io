Best practices when making text/test documents for font testing

October 17, 2007 • ebensorkin

I recently posted a text file* to help people who want to quickly get a sense of how their font looks in a variety of languages. But one of the people using it says they can’t see all the glyphs. The format I uses windows linebreaks and Unicode 8 no BOM. I could have used just Unicode 8 or Unicode 16, or Unicode 16, No BOM, or Unicode 16 Little Endian, or Unicode 16 Little Endian, No BOM.

Any idea what line break type is going to be most compatible - and perhaps more importantly what Unicode encoding is best & why?

The most important thing is not to share this as TXT, since the application will need to guess the encoding or even worse: use the default. Make it an RTF and choose the encoding and everything will be fine.
