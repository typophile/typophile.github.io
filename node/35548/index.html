Research Magazine Redesign: serif suggestions to join Bliss?

July 30, 2007 • bDIN

I'm working on a university research magazine redesign, and we're struggling to agree on a new serif face for feature story body copy. We've chosen Jeremy Tankard's Bliss as our sans serif, which we'll use for departmental body copy and sidebars. Any suggestions for a contemporary serif face that would be a good companion to Bliss? It needs to be easy to read, yet it would be nice if it also works as an occasional headline treatment. I like Tankard's Kingfisher and Enschedé's Lexicon (too pricey though). http://www.typography.net/type/kingfisher.htm http://www.teff.nl/fonts/lexicon/lexicon.html Also Anziano might be a possibility. http://www.fountain.nu/catalogue/anziano.asp We've been test driving Whitman, but the editor would like to see other options. http://www.fontbureau.com/fonts/Whitman Here's an example of how we're using Bliss in the front of the book.
