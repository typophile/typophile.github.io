TrondAslak from HelloTypo

I don’t know if they still let people in there, but I visited, some seven years ago, the Enschedé Museum in Haarlem; just a short ride from Amsterdam.

I had a chance to hold the original punches of Henri Didot from 1827. The size was Non Plus Ultra, which is roughly 2 or 3 points. And I got to hold the “Evangile selon St. Matthieu”, a book the size of a matchbox, set with that very type. And you could actually read the text! And there is loads and loads more stuff there.

When I went there, I found an e-mail of the curator and set the date and time. No photography allowed, since the printworks where the museum is in the basement of still prints money.

Hope this helps.
