Is the typeface you're selecting to be used in creating the logo, or in setting the text for the promotional material? I think that selecting a typeface and then designing the logo around it is going about things backwards. Logo design is more than just selecting an appropriate typeface for the event/client/occasion. A logo could contain illustration, photography or graphic symbols, and the design could be about the arrangement of these elements as much as about the elements themselves. I think a better design process is to think about some ideas for the logo, then select a typeface that works with your logo design ideas. Sorry if that sounds like not much help, but sometimes it's better to just go back to scribbling in the sketchbook, then shackle yourself to your font menu. Jason C
