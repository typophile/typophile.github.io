I have a simple vector tee shirt design with four colors already chosen from illustrator, but I need to find the PMS number to send to the printer.

Alright, so first of all PMS just means pantone, correct? Whats the difference between PMS color and Swatch #?

Whats the easiest way to match the colors I already have? Photoshop?

Thanks!!!!!

PMS is short for Pantone Matching System, but is often used interchangeably with “Pantone” to refer to colors in their library. The PMS number is the swatch number that corresponds to the color from the Pantone library. Unless you have a pantone book to flip through (which I am guessing you probably dont) the only “automatic” way to do it is to use the color picker in Photoshop. Enter the CMYK values into the picker, and click on “libraries”. That will give you what Photoshop estimates is the closest match. However, this can sometimes not be very accurate.

In the future, if you know you are going to need Pantone colors, I recommend picking your colors from the Pantone swatch library in Illustrator to begin with. It is much easier to convert to CMYK from Pantone than the other way around. Beware however that different programs “estimate” CMYK values for Pantone colors differently (especially if you are using Quark). So make sure that you proof the colors with either a match print or a Pantone book.

With Photoshop, it is going to be raster depending on how your graphic is created, you would then need to save it as duo, tri or more depending on how many colours and this can be more hassle than it is worth. If however it is a image and full colour (cmyk), then you do not need to worry about spot colours or Pantone, you ned to make sure your resolution and colour settings are correct as per the printers specifications.
