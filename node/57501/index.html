Affordable Slab Serif Recommendation

April 29, 2009 • mikebara

I am in search of a slab serif typeface that meets the below criteria. So far, I’ve come up empty. Any suggestions would be greatly appreciated.

1.) Suitable for extended text (papers, possibly a short book).

2.) Reasonably clear as a screen font under Windows w/ Cleartype

3.) Available in at least 3 styles (reg, bold, ital) and ideally 4 (add bold ital).

4.) Here’s the tough one - priced at $20 or less per style.

Were price not an object, I’d probably go with Mokka or Skolar. Typefaces resembling those, at my price point, would be great.

Thanks, Mike

No italics, but beautiful: Silica.

Not exactly within your budget, but pretty close, is the selection of Chaparral fonts offered in Adobe Type Basics Opentype Edition;, and these come with good glyph coverage, such as smallcaps and OSFs.
