Curious stamp on WW2 Nazi document

November 7, 2013 • Type-face nut

Hello people.

I wonder if you can help me with a query regarding an inkstamp which was on the front page of a key surviving Holocaust document.

The ink stamp is taken from the front page of the notorious “Jager report,” a document listing Jewish persons and others executed in 1941 in Lithiuania. It was compiled by SS-Standartenfuhrer Karl Jager who was the head of Einsatzkommando 3 attached to murder squad, Einsatzgruppen A for submission to Heydrich in Berlin.

http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jager_Report

http://www.nizkor.org/hweb/orgs/german/einsatzgruppen/esg/jager.html

Both make for gruesome reading.

But is this a custom type face made for the stamp? I have been told that it is a Celtic uncial font. Or more specifically, “Cry Uncial”, which I doubt highly.

Can anyone here think if they know of any other examples like this one dating to the 1920’s or 1930’s or early 40’s that matches this style? Or was the type-face really just custom made for the stamp?

Geheime Reichssache, translates as “secret Reich business”

Thank you for any light you can shed upon this curious type-face.

Type-face nut.

Curious indeed. I checked through Jaspert’s Enclopedia of Typefaces, which is generally very good in coverage of German typefaces of from the 1920s to WW II, but drew a blank. For what it is worth, the letters are simplified Roman italic, not script, and have nothing to do with Unical letters. So I suspect it’s hand lettering made into a stamp. Might be in an alphabet book for draftsmen, showcard makers etc. Don

You’re welcome Chris. Good finds Ryuk. So we can probably say that the designer of the stamp drew on a popular lettering style in Germany in the late 1890s. This same style was more or less imitated in the Bauer/Berthold typeface that HiH used as the basis for Regina Cursiv. Similarly for Joey Maul’s Spring # 7. To reproduce the stamp I would use Spring #7 and modify to match. Now that the discussion has prompted my memory, I do recall seeing this type of lettering on some of the old postcards in my [unsorted] digital collection of old images. Might even be able to find a few. Don
