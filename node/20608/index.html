2006-06-12 03:58:17 • ben millen
I work with other designers and different design firms and I've noticed this myself--but it varies from firm to firm (and city to city) and it might have something to do with experience. When I was at Chermayeff & Geismar (47 years in the design business), it hardly ever happened--those guys knew what they wanted and were never afraid to experiment--one project would be Garamond, another in DIN, while we're were doing a CI in Charlotte. One designer there did go through an Interstate phase, though. But at newer firms with younger designers, or outside NYC, I've noticed that not only will a designer stick with a typestyle over several projects, but everyone else in the studio will start using it (or similiar), too. Actually makes my job easier.:)

2006-06-17 19:08:09 • Bobby Henderson
Agreed. I find myself doing this and as Michelemiller suggests, im my case its to do with experience. I mostly typeset lightweight trade ads for newsprint (so i guess it doesnt matter!) and find myself relying on a fairly solid set of 2-plate process colours, and more often than not i'll set body copy in gill or officina sans with Univers or Gill Compressed, or maybe DIN for headings and Chaparral Italic for leadlines and accents. I'm sure this will change over time, as I become exposed to more styles and experiment more. But for the meantime I think its okay so long as I'm aware. Maybe good typefaces are like like good wine, in that you dont start to appreciate the finer points of its characteristics until you've had the same (good) one a few times?
2006-06-19 09:51:23 • JonnySchneider
I think typefaces are a bit like clothing. Some kinds of clothing, like jeans, go with a lot of stuff. You can wear tennis shoes, loafers, boots, sandals or just go barefoot while wearing jeans. Pleated slacks aren't quite so versatile. The same kind of thing occurs with typefaces. Some faces can be safely used with many other kinds of fonts without drawing reactions like, "dude, what were you thinking that day?" It's just like that clothing comment, "you're not going to wear that shirt with those slacks, are you?" I think playing it safe is the biggest source of one repeating himself. Of course, one may just fall in love with a particular typeface and just have to use on all sorts of stuff.
