Type for children aged 6 to 10

February 27, 2009 • Winand

Hello, Would it be possible to recommend me some types (reading text) that are most suitable for children aged 6 to 10? Things I should pay special attention to regarding this target group? Thx in advance, Winand
Since young minds don't have the experience of viewing and cataloging various typefaces and their nuances in their hard drive (brain), I would keep it simple, i.e., Avante Garde, News Gothic, Futura; faces that can be read easily and quickly. I would also stay away from extended or condensed, as well.

Is it concentrated voluminous copy or just spotty text along with illustrations? If it is not long and tedious I designed Peanut for kids about two years ago and I have seen it used in a number of books for first thru fourth grades. I think it is fun, open, young; and I tried to keep within the confines of the traditional Roman. [img:sites/default/files/old-images/peanut_4135.png] If this is too juvenile then maybe something like Palatino Sans. Michael
