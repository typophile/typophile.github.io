EDITED According to Meggs’ History of Graphic Design Behrens used this cover to explore formal geometric design motifs with modular sans-serif characters based on a square. Here is a larger scale image of the lettering portion of the cover [img:sites/default/files/old-images/BehrensCoverSquareSansDetail_5891.jpg] So what we need is a very boxy wide font with a fairly heavy weight. Nothing really works. Sackers Square Gothic and Bank Gothic are about right for the squarish shapes but are too light. http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/mti/sackers-square-gothic/ Bank Sans Caps Bold [img:sites/default/files/old-images/BankSansCapsBoldUC_4946.gif] http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/ef/bank-sans-caps-ef/ is closer. For a better match the font could be edited. For _A_ flip the _U_ and add a crossbar. Edit the arms on _K_. Trim off the top and bottom tips on _S_. Reduce the height of the vertex on _W_. Extend the serif on _G_ almost to the lhs stroke. For an almost exact match use a parallel line stroke to thicken the letters. Don
