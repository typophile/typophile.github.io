2004-06-15 04:17:53 • Grant Hutchinson
FontFonts did quite well, in what was mainly a national (pre-Internet) direct-mail marketing era. They had good branding, well designed printed specimens, plus Fuse and the FontBook. And the FontShop stores. Their classics are Scala, Meta, Dax, & Quadraat. DIN could be included, although that was designed in the 1930s originally. Maybe classic: Fontesque (still selling well). More ephemeral: Blur, Trixie, Dot Matrix, Mambo, & the "Instant Types".
