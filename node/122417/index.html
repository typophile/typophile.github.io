1930's (or prior) Geometric Sans Serif

January 7, 2015 • obsidianstudio

I’m trying to ID the following typeface, implemented in the sign (attached). I know that this photo was taken around the 1930’s, in Albuquerque, New Mexico. It’s a geometric sans serif, that I am assuming was not made specifically for this sign.

I’ve so far ruled out: Futura (top lobe of the “B” needs to stack over the bottom) Gill Sans (counters on the “B” are too tall) Kabel Akzidenz (“O” is not circular enough) Erbar

Any thoughts?

I am assuming was not made specifically for this sign.

Perhaps not, but most certainly it was made for signs, so it’s improbable you can find a perfect match among typefaces made for print. Your best bet is finding a good approximation in a modern recreation which imitates the look. It shouldn’t be difficult, given the recent popularity of geometric vintage-looking offerings (a few examples: https://www.myfonts.com/newsletters/sp/201501.html).
