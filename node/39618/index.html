November 30, 2007 • Kwesi Amuti

Now I know this question will either be ignored, taken down or the answer might refer back to either Joshua Darden or some thread about the guy who made the font named “Soul” or even the thread about Intertypographics in the 70’s….but I’m still going to ask since the question begs to be asked again and again by the folks I work with, deal with and moreover tell I’m designing type….

The Question:

Where are the African American Type designers?

Why do I ask? I ask because I am one…Ghanaian-American to be exact and I was wondering if there were any other African / African-American type designers out here…or is just me and Joshua…if so that is indeed sad…

Thank you for reading and I appreciate any comments, criticism and even the hate mail,

Sasabonsam from ATL

There have been several threads on Typophile with a similar question: where are the female type designers? It’s good for us to find out the answers to these questions, and to encourage people from margins and hidden communities to achieve and to share, and I think that’s happening all over the world now. I don’t think there is any cause, fault, or cultural constraint at work. I can certainly see the skewed racial and gender distribution among type designers, but the type world is so very small, and populated by autodidacts and lifelong enthusiasts, I don’t see a substantial connection between race, gender, or any other factor, and type design. I don’t think type designers are really that similar, once you investigate their motivations, approach and methods. Type designers come from such different backgrounds, it’s hard to see real stereotypes (Simon’s comical exaggeration notwithstanding). I think Darrel is right that the larger graphic Design world is a place to inquire about these issues.
