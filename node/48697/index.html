formatting Word docs for later use in InDesign

August 22, 2008 • Delia Floor

I am have a hard time finding detailed information on making style sets in Word 2007. The on-line tutorials are too basic. I want to not only use styles, but set up fairly fool-proof templates for others to use during a two-year institutional self-study process.

The issue I’m looking at is how to establish standardized formats that can be used to unify information from many different sources (8 schools/colleges; perhaps 135 academic units). Many reports will need to be kept in both print and electronic form for the 2010 accreditation team to reference. We’ll ultimately need to import a series of Word documents into InDesign to produce the final self-study report. My understanding is that the cleaner and more consistent the Word documents are, the fewer problems arise in InDesign. One of the big issues will be to maintain a consistent hierarchy of information with headings, footnotes, numbered lists, tables with data, etc.

I’d love to find references (book, blog, website, whatever) that would help me anticipate problems in the process, particularly moving information from Word to InDesign.

Thanks very much for your help.

I just finished a project which had me importing a large number of Word files into inDesign, and from my experience, what I found most efficient was to have my styles defined in inDesign already and just import the text without formatting (ctrl+shift+v), which I added as necessary. Might just have been my specific situation, but I’d still tend to think that’d be the way I’d wanna do things, just because Word style formatting can be tricky if you’re not paying attention.

Peace, JT

My understanding is that the cleaner and more consistent the Word documents are, the fewer problems arise in InDesign.

Something that often goes awry is automatic numbering of headings (and perhaps of lists as well). For some reason ID imports it dead wrong – resetting the numbering and stuff like that.

You can kill off any of the automatic stuff by selecting “Unlink Fields” – unfortunately, I don’t remember where to find it in Word! I’ve had a Word Macro for ages, that first asserts “Track changes” is off, then accepts all changes (!), unlinks fields and removes hyperlinks – that saves on the number of blue underlined texts to unformat. Only then I dare to put it into ID (and the next set of scripts go over it once again).
