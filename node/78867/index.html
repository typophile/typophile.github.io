February 8, 2011 • Joma

I was thinking of pairing [[http://www.typography.com/fonts/font_inside.php?wipID=19&productLineID=100008|Gotham Narrow]] and [[http://klim.co.nz/unitslab_info.php|Unit Slab]]together for a magazine. Stylish but serious. I may also use them in print for a book that accompanies the magazine's branding. Two questions: 1) Are these a good match? 2) If you had to pick a simple typeface (serif) for the book's body text, what would you choose? Thanks!
1. Yes 2. Unit Slab, with ample leading, expanded 10% horizontally. There is a prejudice against horizontal scaling, but the effect varies from type to type, and IMO a face such as Unit Slab is not adversely affected by it; let your eyes be the judge.
