“You can not give a gift of something you do not own.”

Can you lease something you yourself did not pay rights for? I believe Bookman was an ATF release and itself based on earlier faces that trace back near a century. Did Bitstream, ITC, Jukebox, lease their versions from ATF? I’m guessing likely not. Foundries have a grand history of theft themselves.

There was a time, not so very long ago in the scheme of things, that when you bought type, yes indeed, you owned it, and you could do whatever you wanted with it (except, of course, reproduce it as your own).

EULAs are a brilliant legal manipulation to cover up the industry’s inability to protect its own product. Digital type certainly did not start out this way.
