(I posted a lengthy reply which disappeared when I posted it. I must have forgotten to do something so I am doing this indeed again. If this follows my previous posting then assume that I stutter when I type…) The logo in this thread is the work of Rick Griffin. The works that appeared in Rolling Stone Magazine were drawn on deadline which meant I at best I had three days to finish them. Fred Woodward hired me to mimic the works of Rick Griffin and Jim Parkinson; my versions are designed renderings and not digital tracings (please note the lopsided "o"s in Parkinson's logos and the symetrical "o"s in mine). The logo drawn for Vincent Winter's "Book of Rock Lists" is another example of this mimicry. None of these logos exist in the form of a font. You can view them at the following URLs: http://ortiz-lopez.com/Plate04.html http://ortiz-lopez.com/LogosInkRSLogoBk.html http://ortiz-lopez.com/LogosInkRSSpecials.html Here are the covers: http://ortiz-lopez.com/RS25Cov.html http://ortiz-lopez.com/Tina.html http://ortiz-lopez.com/RS50s.html This is the Vincent Winter piece: http://ortiz-lopez.com/InkBkOfRkLsts.html The four fonts drawn for Major League Baseball Properties were designed and rendered from a few samples sent to me to base my work upon. There was nothing to scan and the type is original. My favorite is the Boston Red Sox font: http://ortiz-lopez.com/OLP06.html http://ortiz-lopez.com/OLP12.html http://ortiz-lopez.com/OLP16.html http://ortiz-lopez.com/OLP17.html I draw what I am hired to draw and frequently I am hired because I can deliver finished art in a fraction of the time the artist I am imitating can deliver.
