Looking for the name of this font with a typical A Anyone?

According to [[http://www.bastillebastille.com/music/|their website]], the font is called “bastillefont”. As many of this kind of job, it is deeply rooted in [[http://www.myfonts.com/search/futura|Futura]]. This one looks mainly like a redrawn/roughen/hand-shaped version of it (see printscreen below). As you’re mainly after this kind of /A (which could easily be done starting from /A, removing middle crossbar and “closing” the resulting triangle): [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/hvdfonts/diamonds/|Diamonds]], [[http://26plus-zeichen.de/en/fonts/tj-evolette-a/|Evolette A]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/matchandkerosene/lightyears|Lightyears]], many ones at [[http://www.tendollarfonts.com/|TenDollarFonts]] (TWOFACED, Echelon, Rathe, Parqa, Fonecian, Finding 57, Harf 77, Labieno, Positano, Marina, Alumia…) More inspiration: [[http://experimentalsans.tumblr.com/|I Am So Tired With Your Experimental Sans]]
