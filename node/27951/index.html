2006-09-01 14:39:34 • Maurice Meilleur
It is not that easy actually. It certainly is easier if you have drawn all the glyphs already for say an expert set and multi-lingual sets. by adding a few hundred glyphs, you have multiplied the kerning requirements quite a bit. You have to adjust all the fontmetrics as well. If you have lots of combining to do with diacritics, your job is even bigger. There is nothing to prevent people from making OTF fonts from T1 or ttf fonts as long as the EULA permits it but that is a big if. Thieves and pirates probably do what you said but they are probably more likely to just steal a font and redestribute it to avoid all the work. My theory is that thieves are thieves because they are too lazy to do the work themselves. Foundries may have thousands of fonts in their catalogs as well as new ones coming out. Foundries these days don't have big staffs so they have to prioritize what to do first. It may sound simple but try it. You will be surprised how much work is involved after all the outlines are drawn and done. ChrisL
