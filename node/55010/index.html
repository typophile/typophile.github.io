Why care about type?

February 19, 2009 • rwiedeman

I've been through a couple of the introductory lessons, but still need something even more basic: Why care about type? I'm not being facetious. I sense there are reasons, both rational and emotional, that the way a word looks, matters -- but that's only intuitive for me. Then, what I'd love to see is a lesson or essay describing the principles: How to make basic choices for HTML/Flash vs print, animated vs static, etc. I know sans-serif generally is easier to read on a screen, and serif is easier to read on paper (in terms of human factors/reading speed); that too many fonts is called "ransom noting"; that people read fastest at about 12 words per line -- but that's where my decision-making knowledge ends. Maybe that's too low-brow for Typophile, but I hope not. :) I enjoy this stuff. I just don't know what to do with it.
"Why care about type?" Clarity of communication. As for the rest, there are a lot of rules of thumb. A lot of them aren't necessarily based on terribly tangible research, though.
