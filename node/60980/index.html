Squooshing is much more problematic than stretching, which IMO is not an issue. In the early days of DTP, I would stretch Adobe Garamond to 150% in Quark (for text), and it looked great. Really! Diagonals are the sticking point, so square faces without diagonals may be compressed lots without apparent distortion. But no, I haven't designed any faces with squooshability as a parameter.

2009-08-14 18:57:47 • nina
“biologically” seems a lot more “normal” than “determinations” Yes. I'm trying to reconstruct my design process. I think that I was using the variety of stretching to "massage" text blocks to fit the layout, and the variety of stretching, and type styles, served to disguise that "cheating". For instance, the really wide drop cap was done to optimize the justification of the first paragraph. And the three squared-off items were stretched to fit the space they're in, with nice margins. ...in a very 1989 sort of way... Yes, it was supposed to look new, advertising a new piece of scientific equipment, and stretched type signified new at the time, the DTP meme. Also the spectroscope itself was long.
