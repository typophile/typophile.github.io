2009-03-25 22:06:35 • brockfrench

The way I would do that is in Illustrator.

1: Type your word/line of text.

2: Create Outlines.

3: Do an “Offset Path” under objects. Play around with this until you get the right weight around your text.

4: Take the background shadow element you just created, copy it, and paste it in back, moving it down and to the right, or whichever way you want. This is going to define how far out your shows will go.

5: Go to “Blend>Blend Options,” choose “Number of Steps,” and put in something pretty high, like 100. Select the shadow piece in back of your text, and the one you moved down and to the right, and go to “Blend>Make Blend.” If it’s too stepped, undo it and go back to blend options, setting the value higher.

6: Use the Pathfinder tool to combine the shadow into one simple piece.

That’s at least how I would do it. There are several other ways you could go about it, but that one is the quickest. Sorry if all the functions are not exactly the right name – I’m not looking at Illustrator right now – but it should give you a general idea of what to play with.

Glad that worked out for you! One thing that can make it a lot smoother is once you’ve flattened the background shadow pieces into one shape, select all the points except for the far left and far right (or highest and lowest, depending on what kind of shadow you created), hit backspace, and then do an Apple+J “Join” command. Do this step for both sides with a lot of extra points. This gets rid of any of the stepping from the blending process, but if you used enough steps, it won’t be visible. It will create a very large file, though, if you leave the steps.

2009-03-27 18:36:09 • Plaidjaney

To make an inner shadow, convert the text to outlines, then:

  1. Copy the letters (shapes)
  2. Paste in place (command-F) to make a copy of the letters directly on top
  3. Offset the copy in the direction and distance you want the shadow to go
  4. Make the color of the original letters darker (the color you want the shadow to be)
  5. Select both the copy and the original and apply the Pathfinder “Minus Front” operation – this is your shadow
  6. Do a paste behind (command-B) to paste the original letters behind the shadow (the original letters should still be on the clipboard)
