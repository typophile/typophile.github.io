One of the best ways to get a large distressed type with ease is to print out the type on a3 paper and then go to town on the paper its self. Just crunch it up rub it against walls etc… then flatten the paper and scan it back into photoshop to do the rest!

I’ve done this and seen it been done to great effects without it looking fake!

2008-07-20 01:18:56 • robbiefa
In the ‘Frank’ family I did for Precision Type ages ago there was a dot-distressed version of Franklin Gothic bold (extra?) condensed that I never released because it would have had to be blown up to several hundred points to have the dots be visible so it was impossible to show in the catalogue. (This was a fairly automatic process thanks to a macro Yuri Yarmola kindly wrote for me for in Fontlab 2.5 language.) I very much like the suggestion of crinkling paper – imagine the different effects one could get depending on the texture of the paper. Another thing that that can work nicely is multiple re-xeroxing. Combine that with multiple re-crinkling and you could be getting some nice effects.
