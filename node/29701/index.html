If you want to know what Nick Curtis' inspiration was, you could e-mail him and ask. His address is shown on the page I linked to, above. My guess for the origin of square counters wouldn't be sign painting--I'm a sign painter, and I'm not often tempted to do square counters. It's much easier to paint curves than perfectly straight lines, let me tell you! My guess is that the square counters come from calligraphy, specifically from blackletter faces, where broad, flat pen nibs create a squared-off look. Skylar
2006-11-25 17:31:57 • skyfein
Sander de Voogt, who sometimes makes an appearance here, maintains an archive of info about Nick's fonts. In the case of Day Poster Black, it is, "An extrabold utilitarian font based on the work of 30s lettering artist Harold Holland Day" and the source was Day's 1931 book Modern Brush Lettering.
