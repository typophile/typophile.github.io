Alphabetical culture clash.

November 18, 2009 • HijackYourLife

Hallo everyone. I have an interesting question.

I am designing a photo book with lots of photographers contributions from different countries. It is a dutch publisher, but the book is in english only.

One section contains one contribution per photographer, and it is in alphabetical order according to the names of the photographer.

There are a few of them which have last names starting with W, and one of them is Wåhlstrand.

My dutch colleauges sorted it as if the å was an a, but the photographer is swedish, and å comes almost lasst in the swedish alpahabet. While it is not in the dutch and english alphabets at all.

So my qusetion is, should I treat it as an å or an a? The book is in english, the photographer is swedish, the publisher is dutch. How do I treat an å in english alphabetica order?

Hi Kalle If you scroll down to 1.2.2 here http://www.evertype.com/alphabets/ you can find the alphabets of the euro languages in their native alphabetical order. Since the book is in English, then the order should be in the English alphabetical order: http://www.evertype.com/alphabets/english.pdf
