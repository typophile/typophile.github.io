2011-02-13 20:34:25 • JamesM

Make it project-based.

Change minor requirements a few times in the middle of each project.

Include a round of feedback where changes are required without any rational reasoning.

Start each project with a brief. Have the students right a de-brief after hearing about the context of the project.

Have them present their concept BEFORE they begin designing.

Attach a grade to their professionalism during the process.

Have them report their time as they work and include it with project documentation. Give them a budget (of time) to stay within.

Every project should be presented formally, in person, with a slide-based presentation.

Attach a grade to how well they matched their original concept and target audience.

Teach them to edit a portfolio to only their best work.

If you can manage to do that, regardless of the technical details of the program, they will come out expecting real life, rather than lab-conditions.
