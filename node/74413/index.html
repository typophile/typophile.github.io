2010-09-21 18:00:49 • Lex Kominek
The images are degraded (through faxing, duplication or scanning) so that it would be very difficult (maybe impossible) to tell which version it is from things like the serifs, which are virtually indistinguishable between Times (Linotype/Adobe) and Times New Roman (Monotype/Microsoft) anyway. However, the "P" in the word "Please" and the spacing in certain words ("look") suggest that this may be Times (Linotype/Adobe). Comparison on MyFonts.com. That said, it would be difficult to say what year this document was created just from the typeface. Both versions have been around as digital fonts since at least the eighties and the shapes of the letters have probably changed very little (if at all) since then.
