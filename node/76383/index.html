The primary reason to avoid fine lines lies with the method for foil stamping, which is done with both heat and pressure: too fine a line may cut the cloth…

2010-11-16 17:17:35 • oldnick

And too small a negative space may not clear of material.

In addition to Nick’s caution about cutting the cloth, in general fine lines and details cannot be reliably held in stamped foil. In my experience, it is like extreme ink gain. The smaller you go, the less detail you can hold.

You should definitely talk to your binder about their parameters and any recommendations.

Yes, yes and no.

The second “yes” in my experience is a maybe. There are presses out there that can stamp both covers and spine at once. A larger plate and press will cost more to set-up; for smaller runs it may be more economical to have the front and back stamped separately.
