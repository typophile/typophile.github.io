I'm wondering if anyone knows of a utility/program/script that will examine a font's inventory and list out glyphs vis-a-vis codepoints (whichever coding it may use)? I'm noticing a lot on non-unicode fonts seem to have a lot of redundant glyphs scattered throughout the whole of the inventory and sometimes I can't quite tell if a font will be comprehensive and replete with everything I'm looking for... this is especially the case with legacy non-latin fonts that assign glyphs to , , , etc, without necessarily having a systematic correspondence... thoughts? advice?
TTX? (Produces an XML formatted text of almost everything in TTFs and OTFs. If it's not in there, it's probably not important.) For just a quick-look on a font's contents on Windows, I wrote a little program myself: SeeFont. It shows all glyphs, with Unicode points where available, and can sort by Glyph index or Unicode. (I intended it to evolve into a complete OpenType examine tool but stopped developing when OTLMaster came out :-( ... and I also switched over to Mac.)
