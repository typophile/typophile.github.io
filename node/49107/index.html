Form with the word "Walnut" used alot

September 5, 2008 • mica

We have an internal form that I am redesigning. This is a work order that lists instructions for building a custom guitar. This is laser printed on 11x17 smooth white cardstock that is folded in half. Most of the text is 12pt, with a few headlines. The form is a text file and is basically a list with bold headings.

The purpose is to clearly communicate the customer’s wishes to the craftsmen that are building the guitar. After the guitar is made, the work order with its construction notes from the builders goes into a filing cabinet and can hold related documents to the build for future reference.

I’ve tried a few fonts that I have in my library, but I haven’t been happy with any yet. RTF Amethyst makes a funny looking “Walnut” and I use that word on about half the forms. Puyfoulhoux’s Classica looks great in text, but these little groups of words make the “p” and “b” look sparse and hard to read. I’ve been the most happy with Simonson’s Grad, but the underline doesn’t connect, and I use a “____ __ /__ /__” for someone to initial and date the work completed.

So now I’m shopping again. I looked at Parkinson’s Sutro and Stone’s Serif. I’ve always wanted a reason to buy Lew’s Whitman. Poppl-Pontifex BQ might work. I’m leaning toward a serif, but the right sans would move me. I could just use Times that we’ve had on the form since I got an HPIIID printer with it on the cartridge, but I’m hoping that by selecting just the right font, the form will be easier to read and that nobody even notices the font.

Any suggestions for nominations for my shopping list?
