Nina, The comparison of scanned images is not the best way to check a printer for use in type design. You can see general printing ability and rendering of pixel images but type design needs to see how a printer pours pixels into a bezier outline–how does it choose to deal with rounding, how much hinting affects the output, how it interprets PostScript, how much toner spread it generates, etc.. But this doesn’t enable us to account for different kinds of degradation. A better way to think about the relationship of typefaces as output to typefaces as source would be a scattershot projection, in which the typeface as source is in the middle, and individual examples of output surround it, closer or further away depending on quality of fidelity and radially arranged in terms of type of degradation.
