Please critique international festival logo design!

December 2, 2008 • dinazina

Hi everyone, I'm in awe of the the accumulated typography skill, knowledge and taste assembled on this board, so I'm asking sorta nervously for you to critique a design. I'm entering a competition to desgn a logo for a nearby neighborhood's summer festival. Here is the challenge, as it appeared in my email: ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~ "White Center's "Jubilee Days" is looking to update its identity for 2009 to more reflect its community. White Center is the most diverse community in the greater Seattle area, having 80 cultures, speaking 50 different languages. I would like to see that spoken to in the logo. What is needed on the logo- Name: White Center Jubilee Days Theme: Living the dream Date: July 19th & 20th 2009 The logo will be used on T-shirts, banners, buttons, and all related printed material. I am accepting black and white roughs to start with. I will then select 3 finalists and ask for full color versions. As an artist myself I realize the amount for work that goes into creating an identity...." ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~ White Center is anything but White, as the man says. The present community leadership is looking to turn the gritty and tough identity into friendly international pride. The theme "Living the dream" refers to Martin Luther King's "I have a dream" speech. It sure is not the "American Dream," as this hood has probably the lowest per capita income in the area. Below are some previous Jubilee logos - to give you an idea how truly bad were the graphics before someone realized this kind of thing wouldn't improve their image. [img:sites/default/files/old-images/JD-buttons-s_6206.jpg] Looks like generic Balloon and Brush Script, doesn't it? Here is my black and white design: [img:sites/default/files/old-images/jubilee-logo-solo_4626.gif] Here is how it might look on a tee shirt: [img:sites/default/files/old-images/jubilee-logo-bw-tee_5752.gif] I wanted to represent typographically the many cultures in this area, especially Thai, Cambodian, Vietnamese, Latino, Pacific Islander, Native American, and Arabic as well as African-American. I felt the type needed to be "foreign-looking" and eccentric, yet still quite legible at a distance and to someone who has learned English as a second language. Critique away... 1.The image in the center represents a Native American "sky basket" bronze sculpture on a tall pole at the gateway to this hood, just dedicated last year. 2. The words "White Center" and the date are in a font identified only as "Dingaling Bold" in my folder. I have no idea where it came from or its true identity - please help me out here. Obviously I tweaked the font to make the "Thai" circles, but the W is the original W, although it resembles a Russian "sha". Because the phrase has no descenders, I thought an arch would be attractive. 3. The Words "Jubilee Days" are in Nick's font "Yo Quiero Taquitos," but I reversed the descender on the Y to resemble a Cambodian character. 4. The words "Living the Dream" are in a font called "Jive Talk". THANKS in advance!!
Nothing? Nothing? Hmm. Usually Typophiles are so opinionated. I can only surmise: 1. The design sucks so bad you don't know where to start, and don't wanna hurt my feelings by telling me to delete it and start from scratch or 2. The design is so brilliant no one can add a single suggestion for improvement, and y'all are jealous or 3. My choice of fonts offends your professional sensibilities or or or I don't know. ~ Dina

Perhaps the words "White Center" and "Jubilee Days" should form a circle around the tagline which would help incorporate the tagline into the design.
