Silkscreening is the same as screen printing. Basically large sheets of fine screen fabric stretched over a frame. Using photo emulsion you can burn and block out patterns into the screen which then, using a squeegee, you push ink through the empty spaces to make a print. It’s fairly easy to do yourself, but you need a super intense light to expose the emulsion. I found a video of someone printing his already prepared screen.

http://youtube.com/watch?v=qCZe9BWxCu0

I’m not sure if regular printing houses do this, but there are probably studios in your town that will. Also you could hit up art schools and find a printmaking student to do it for you. This is how a lot of hand-printed t-shirts are made as well. I’ve used this technique for type in books before and it works really well. And like I said the ink comes in all sorts of colours like flouresents and glow in the dark. You can also print white.

Look up “silkscreen book” on flickr for tons of images of small run books other people have done.

2008-01-03 19:45:47 • Koppa

I’d look into screen printing too…you could print the pages as a signature (ie, multiple pages on one large sheet) then trim and bind.

That said, the cost for both screen printing and letterpress is typically the set-up. So, both aren’t really designed for runs of 3 or so.

One last option…back in the day (a decade+ ago in art school) one could by little sheets of custom color. They looked like foils. You’d place it on top of your laserprinted paper and apply heat to it, the heat would fuse the color to the toner in the paper. It was a way to emulate spot colors and foil embossing.

that might work if your use of color is in small amounts.

To clarify why I think letterpress is ideal for runs of 3 (as opposed to offset)…most commercial printers are not going to want to print a run of 3. There are lots of letterpress printers who would be happy to print a run of 3.

Another very modern option is the digital press that many small business printers have these days. I think the limitation here, however, is that spot inks are not possible. I could be wrong, but I’m pretty sure you’re limited to the 4-color process with the digital printing technology.
