There are indeed. There is plenty of room for artistic interpretation; I’m puzzled why one would think otherwise. We are talking about a writing system with a rich history of [[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_script_styles|script styles]].

As for the relationships of line and weight, that depends on specific styles, just as the treatment is different for the Latin alphabet depending on whether the style is inscriptional, old-style, or modern.

On this point of complexity, Chinese characters have a lot of built-in redundancy. Most characters are built from combinations of components called radicals, so there’s plenty of leeway for stylistic variation. It allows characters to be readable even when they seem to be too small, blurry, or stylized to pick out individual strokes.
