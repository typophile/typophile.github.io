2007-08-19 08:27:31 • Christian Barca

I’m perplexed how even the largest internet corporations don’t have proper favicons for their websites. Look at those of eBay, AbeBooks, TechCrunch or even Apple. Those designers don’t seem to know how the surrounding background behind the logo should be knocked out using a transparent layer in .png file format.

Yes, they do know that – they aren’t really a bunch of amateurs. It’s you who don’t seem to know there are many old browsers still in use today which have no support for transparent PNG and partly only incomplete support for transparent GIF. Therefore, instead of having their favicon look nice on some browsers and broken on others, they’d rather have it look acceptable on all browsers – it’s a compatibility issue.

A PR principle of big corps: You can’t win much, but you can loose a lot. Try not to loose, and you’re doing good work.
