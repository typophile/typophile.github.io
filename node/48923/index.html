I have invented a design brief for the font I am to design this fall. It is intended as a kind of composite of the characters, features, and applications of some of the prominent humanist sans faces of the 20th century, synopsized in [[http://www.typophile.com/node/48663|this blog entry]]. So I am “commissioning” myself to come up with a font that fulfills these intentions:

REQUIRED FEATURES: a) - no serifs b) - low or very low contrast (experienced as monoline or close to it) c) - upright d) - readable enough to be suitable for middling-length texts (e.g. captions, sidebars, pullquotes and other blurbs) e) - workable in sizes 11-36 point f) - neutral in character enough to be suitable for a wide variety of texts (without a strong impression of a particular time, place, or emotion) g) - strikes a balance between constructed and written forms

a), b), and c) simply set this out as a typical sans font.

g), of course, is where the creative challenge begins - what features will help the font maintain crisp regularity, and which will give it human warmth?

And d), e), and f) are the constraints which will focus the design. I suppose the positive approach would be to see d), e), and f) as helpful factors that will guide suitable answers to the big g) challenge.
