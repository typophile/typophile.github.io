No, you don't want a bitmap font editor because most OSes and applications no longer support any bitmap font formats. (I say this despite being VP of the company with the only serious bitmap font editor app.) I don't think bitmap fonts will work in either Photoshop Mac or Word 2008 Mac. Plus, at such large sizes, bitmap fonts are a highly inefficient way of storing your shapes. Although some font programs, including ours at FontLab, offer automatic spacing as an option, any decently skilled human can do a better job. Of retail available apps, DTL FontMaster probably has the best auto-spacing. Ours is okay. (Similar issues with auto-kerning.) Igino Marini's iKern service (you don't buy the app, he runs it for you) is probably the best option for automatic spacing and kerning of a single font, but it depends on your budget. http://ikern.com/k1/ Our own FontLab Studio would meet you needs. It allows you to create ligatures, to bring in an image to place in the background, and does auto kerning and auto spacing (although a skilled designer can do better, for sure). I don't think your version of Word supports automatic ligatures, and I'm not sure about Photoshop Elements. Word 2010 and full Photoshop do, however. You can accomplish what you describe in getting the long s on the "1" key in either of two ways: - make a font that "lies" about its encoding. So put a glyph that looks like a long s in the slot for the one. You probably want to consider vectorizing your bitmap images. Type 3.2 font editor ([[http://cr8software.net/type.html]]) has a nifty auto-tracer that can do this.
