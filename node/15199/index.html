2005-09-18 12:34:24 • Dan Weaver
You might even try a long poem such as T.S. Eliot's "The Love Song of J. Alfred Prufock," or a series of shorter, concise poems like hiakus or something. If it's portfolio work, you probably don't have to choose something in public domain, but the comment above is a good one. If you plan to reproduce the work, publish it on the internet or sell the work in any way, be very careful of copyright laws. You might read 10 myths about copyright explained by Brad Templeton.
