Great difficulty finding best page layout

June 10, 2010 • Mother Goose

I'm writing my Ph.D. thesis (in maths), and I'm having a hell of a time trying to find the best page layout. The thesis will be printed on A4 paper, double-sided and in book form. Normal font is Adobe Caslon Pro at 11/13.6 (the 13.6pt leading will be changed to something else later...this is just what LaTeX uses). At 11pt, that's roughly 128pt for the size of the alphabet. The textblock is currently set at around 30 picas, which is about 80 characters/line. I've tried various page layout templates (from the memoir package of LaTeX), including the medieval layout and Binghurt's ISO layout, but I've been unsatisfied with all of them (their textblock widths are too large and heights are too small) Basically, I'm looking for something with a smaller textblock, but still preserving a decent amount of height. Can someone suggest a better page layout? See the attached file for a sample of what I have. The file has a lot of text. The second file has more maths, which is more typical.
I believe some universities have specific format guidelines for these things. Does your university?

2010-06-10 17:25:12 • Mother Goose
This isn't bad. First off: I'd definitely lose the Zapfino. Frankly, it looks out of place – like a desperate attempt to instill some dramatic aesthetics on something that's so smart that I'd think it should be allowed to look mostly serious. :-) Also: This would benefit from more leading, also because your line length is a bit long (something that's very hard to avoid on A4 size paper). More leading can compensate for that, to a degree, to make reading more effortless. Try giving it more air, like 15 or maybe even 16. See what happens. If this takes up too much space maybe you can give your text blocks a little more height, and/or decrease point size a tiny bit.
