it is hard to believe that you actually googled anything, first hit for ' typeface Tudor Black':

[[http://www.oakknoll.com/bookexcerpt.php?booknr=72642]]

EDWARD PHILIP PRINCE was born in 1846, in Kennington, south London, where his father was a schoolmaster. At the age of sixteen he was apprenticed to the well-known punch-cutter Frederick Tarrant, and continued to work for Tarrant for many years. In about 1878, Tarrant and Prince cut the punches for the typeface Tudor Black, based on a 15th century model, for Miller & Richard.
