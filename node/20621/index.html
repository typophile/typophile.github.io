June 12, 2006 • skfields

I originally designed a piece with this as an accent font: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/johann-sparkling-itc/

and the client thought it was too hard to read.

So I found this one, http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/p22/cezanne-regular/ and my boss said “I think if they found the other font hard to read, they’ll find this one even harder to read. I think you’re looking for a somewhat similar script that is not as slanted. This one is slanting in all directions and it’s funky enough that I think it loses the old-style character. That’s just subjective stuff. But I do know they would find this even harder to read.”

I also tried http://www.dafont.com/search.php?q=jefferson and http://www.dafont.com/search.php?q=tagettes Both “not it”

Any suggestions?

PS It’s for a foundation in honor of someone who lived maybe 1920-2000. the client wants a little nostalgia, but readable.
