2010-04-19 06:50:03 • WeAreLoa
Not sure I fully understand your question. Are you concerned about a project falling behind schedule while you're waiting for feedback? Or just annoyed that you rushed to meet their deadline and now they're acting like the deadline wasn't so important after all? When a project has a tight deadline I'll remind the client when I drop off layouts that I must have their feedback by a certain date if we're going to stay on schedule. If they miss that deadline, I remind them. I've found most clients to be appreciative of such reminders as long as they are done politely. It's important that discussions of schedule be in writing (email, for example) so you've got a written record if they try to blame you later for the project falling behind schedule. On the other hand, if you're mainly just annoyed that you worked hard to meet a deadline and suddenly the deadline is starting to look rather arbitrary, well, I agree that it's a frustrating situation. Not much you can do about a client who is thoughtless except decide not to work for them again if you're really ticked off about it. But personally I can put up with a lot of annoying behavior as long as they pay my invoices on time.
