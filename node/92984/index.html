You’re in luck. Two books came out recently, both bilingual:

  • “Roger Excoffon et la fonderie Olive” by Chamaret, Gineste and Morlighem.
  • “Roger Excoffon - Le gentleman de la typographie” by David Rault (plus various contributors).

The second one (translated by moi) is more personal, so if you have to choose only one (why not get both?) I guess in your case go for the first. That said, the personal dimension is never really separable from the end-results; if one wants to find out what made Excoffon tick, and his work click, then it’s useful to know things like how he treated his friends, the effects of his military service, and everything else.
