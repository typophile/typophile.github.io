2006-11-11 04:12:52 • abayarde
Check out "Bembo's Zoo" - a book and a site: http://www.bemboszoo.com/ Also see traditional Arabic calligraphy, which sometimes uses long -and legible- phrases (often from the Qur'aan) to form pictures of various things (including animals). The book "Arabic Script" by Gabriel Mandel Khan has great examples. Lastly, our Chris Lozos has a penchant for making pictures (such as his own face) out of letters. hhp
2006-11-11 04:21:49 • hrant
Also of note are designs with repeating letters (usually in a circular arrangement) to form patterns, arguably the most famous being Licko's Hypnopaedia: http://emigre.com/EF.php?fid=98 And then there's a site that dynamically renders snowflakes (although with 8 sides instead of 6) out of phrases that the user types in: http://www.typeflake.com/ hhp
