Designing type digitally or at least translating hand drawn types to digital compositions would seem to be an essential part of study. Are there masters degrees in this? I certainly do want focus on studio work first and foremost. Is this a common practice? I see many programs where a masters in all research into books. Becoming an educator at this point in my life does not seem to be a worthwhile goal for me. I want to design. For me, design should be the focus of the research.

I thank you for your contribution to this dialogue.

Research is a major component in any Master’s program at a university. However, a good program should also involve a lot of practical work as well.

Don’t look only at Master’s program (there is a good one in Reading, UK). You might find what you want in a college or extension program. Colleges tend to be more hands on, and ignore the research aspects that universities find so necessary.
