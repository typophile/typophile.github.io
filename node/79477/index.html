Punctuation in The Maltese Falcon

February 26, 2011 • JamesM

I was watching the classic 1941 film noir “The Maltese Falcon” and was a little puzzled by the punctuation in the opening scroll (if that’s the right term). In one place they used 3 consecutive en dashes, in another place 5; something you’d rarely see today.

Was that a punctuation style that was common on those days, or was it just an idiosyncrasy of that movie’s writer?

It was done by the lettering artist to facilitate justification—ultimately, he wanted to get “Maltese Falcon” unbroken and minimize the shortness of the final line.
