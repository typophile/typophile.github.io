I don't know the source of your quotes, but the best bets are either Adrian Frutiger or Gerrit Noordzij. If you look into their works you will find interesting discussions of the the balance of black and white, form and counterform. The discussions go back to end of the 19th century, with the introduction of East Asian ideas about the balance of dark and light, known as ‘nóng dàn’ in Chinese or [[notan]] in Japanese.
2007-04-24 14:22:41 • William Berkson
If you can find a copy, I think either edition of Hermann Zapf's Manuale Typographicum would be an ideal source for you. My edition (1968, MIT Press) has 100 pages. Each page contains one or more important thoughts about typography, type design, printing, books, culture, language. Some pages have three or four quotes. There are quotes comparing the form of typography to that of architecture, or music, in addition to the expected things such as, "With 26 soldiers of lead I shall conquer the world." What is most impressive is that Zapf has printed the quotations in the original language of the author (!) and provides a translation into English in the back of the book. I assume the other edition (late 1950s?) is the same general idea, although I have not seen it in person. I do not know if either of these was ever reprinted in a subsequent (offset, thus cheaper) edition. The original editions were very carefully made in multiple colors, printed letterpress, so they are rare at this point and you would be likely to find them only in the special-collections section of a university library, or in a private collection.

Ria, your effort has a lot of merit, but there's a big problem: virtually all expressions of form-counterform (better called notan) in type design are nothing but lip-service. This is especially true of those who believe that chirography (loosely meaning calligraphy) should be a central force in type design; they tend to say nice things about notan, but in practice work to demote it. As my father is fond of saying: You can call you daughter George, but that won't make her a boy. Really the only type designer who's said anything worthwhile about notan is Bloemsma: study his work and thoughts - you will derive a singular benefit. hhp
