ttfautohint only adds truetype hinting, emulating the autohint used in FreeType. However TrueType hints are (for the most part) only used on windows systems, so they don’t effect the way that PDFs are rendered.

2014-10-06 17:36:03 • Martin Silvertant

Hinting, whether of TrueType or PostScript outlines, affects most non-Apple rendering environments, including those from Microsoft, Adobe, and often FreeType*.

With “Adobe” you can include the rendering in Adobe Reader, Acrobat, InDesign, Photoshop and Illustrator—even on Mac OS.

  • FreeType can be set to ignore hints in the fonts like Apple does, autohinting on the fly. Indeed, the TTFautohint hinting is based on the autohinting created for FreeType. But I gather many FreeType-based environments choose to respect the hinting in the font.
