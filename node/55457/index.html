March 3, 2009 • Jserrano

Can anyone please, please give me some direction as to where I can find information on the history of paragraph forms? For example, when text began to be set as flush left rag right and vice versa? I could really use some help! Thanks a million

Although I’m not sure it covers this specific issue, [[http://www.amazon.com/Meggs-History-Graphic-Design-Philip/dp/0471699020|History of Graphic Design]] by Phillip Meggs is a standard and could be a starting point.

Flush left/rag right would not be any more difficult than full justification whether in a manuscript or a printed document. I would imagine the flush right/ragged left came about with the invention of movable type. Lines set with movable type on a press bed can easily (relatively) be moved from left to right justification whereas a calligrapher would have had a difficult time knowing where to begin the lines of handwriting in order to end them at the same point.
