The thing with the Euro is that as handed down by the grey suited ( I assume) Euro folks it was supposed to be like the sign for estimated (0x212E), which is to say it’s supposed to be unchangable/unmodifiable. The other ( official shape ) way it draws way too much attention to itself.

numerals and currency symbols exactly twice the width of the blank space

A good idea if it doesn’t compromise the proportions of those symbols, and that’s true for the dollar and sterling symbol. But for the Euro in a normally proportioned type, in comparison with lining figures, it makes it look as if it has been swiped from the condensed version of the face. (Bearing in mind that lining figures already have a condensed aspect in relation to the alphabetic characters.) In the sample shown, the poor emaciated Euro looks more like a mark than a real character, and that would be even further demonstrated if alphabetic characters were shown. But perhaps that’s what it deserves.

None of this would bother the modern OpenType megafont designer, whose design solution would be to provide alternate Stylistic Sets of tabular and proportional monetary symbols :-)
