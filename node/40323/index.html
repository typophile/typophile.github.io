Oldstyle miniscule a

December 26, 2007 • Dan Gayle

I have fallen in love with typefaces with the scribally influenced miniscule a’s. They’re not wimpy or weak at all, and the way they’re squared off makes them seem rugged and virile to my eyes.

These types of lc a’s seem to be prevalent particularly in [[garalde]] and [[venetian]] [[oldstyle]] typefaces, but a few modern serifed fonts have used them as well. This seems to be a particular favorite form of [[Robert Slimbach]], as quite a few of his typefaces have this particular style of a.

[[Nicholas Jenson]] types LTC Metropolitan Pro ([[Centaur]]) [[ITC Legacy Serif]] [[Adobe Jenson Pro]] Claude [[Garamond]] types [[Adobe Garamond Premier Pro]] [[Stempel Garamond]] 20th Century types [[ITC Mendoza Roman]] [[Adobe Minion Pro]] [[Adobe Arno Pro]] [[Madrigal Antikva]] Does anyone know anything else about these styles of miniscule a’s? And if you have any favorite types with this style of lc a that I’m missing, please post a 72x72 pixel sample. I’d love to see it.

A few Emigre fonts have similar a’s. Malaga definitely, and Tribune too, I think. Then there’s Xavier Dupre’s Absara. Maybe even Weiss would qualify.
