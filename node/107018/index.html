Unfortunately, can’t spot a perfect match for this one. Closest I have (including 4-line shade effect) is [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/parkinson/balboa-shaded|Balboa Shaded]] by Jim Parkinson but /S is a bit different. More ‘Sans Serif Shaded’ with a totally off /S: [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/hihretrofonts/thorowgood-sans|Thorowgood Sans]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/pizzadude/ultinoid|Ultinoid]], [[http://www.moorstation.org/typoasis/designers/steffmann/samples/s/sans_serif.htm|Sans Serif Shaded]] I guess the best way to reproduce it perfectly might be to consider it as a “condensed woodtype sans-serif with a 4-line shadow affect applied”. Some ‘condensed woodtype sans-serif’ you may find interesting: [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/lettering-inc/directors-gothic|Directors Gothic]] (/S a bit off), [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/jnlevine/classroom|Classroom JNL]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/chank/blinc-pak|Goshen]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/parkinson/balboa|Balboa]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/ephemera/toronto-gothic|Toronto Gothic]]/[[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/redrooster/railroad-gothic-rr|Railroad Gothic RR]] (shamefully vertically scaling it…)
