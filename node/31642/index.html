I was a teenage C64 charset designer

February 21, 2007 • fredo

After reading a previous post about Student type designers I felt inspired – or in need of ego compensation – to google into my own past. And there it was. My main occupation was designing logos and charsets as was the word in the day (1987/88).

Usually it was single colour pixel charsets, but my friend programmed a back drop editor for me so we could design multi colour letters as well.

We attended swap parties, sat up all night drinking jolt cola and designing ‘graphix’, trying to socialize (not a common skill then) and most importantly, worked hard on not being a lamer. It was an intense period, probably not lasting more than a year or so.

My aliases were Fred and Banshee, and the last group me and my friends were active in was Agile (we did however NOT have anything to do with those credit card frauds).

Only recently did I connect those C64 experiences (sans the smelly feet) with my current occupation, as a designer of visual identities. The next thing that comes to mind is that a lot of people from that generation should be in our line of business as well.

Were You there? Get yourself a C64 emulator and check those demos, read those cheesy scrolls and enjoy some Rob Hubbard!
