Mysterious Masonic Font from 1919

July 20, 2017 • RogerC

Hello, Found this used as headings in a Masonic book from the 1917-1919 timeframe. Among other oddities, note the two different lowercase 'e' forms. Tried a number of the usual automated font identification sites and they were pretty confused. Any pointers towards something that looks anything like this would be very appreciated!
Known as Gutenberg in MacKellar, Smiths & Jordan 1892 specimen book: https://archive.org/stream/specimensofprint00mackrich#page/n233/mode/2up/search/Gutenberg [page number 267, or page 234/448 on the nav bar) Revived as a digital typeface by the same name by Dan X. Solo, but no longer commercially available. Sign DNA’s Madelyn is a fairly faithful recreation: http://signdna.com/shop/index.php?main_page=advanced_search_result&keyword=Madelyn%20DNA&search_in_description=1&categories_id=1&inc_subcat=1&manufacturers_id=&pfrom=&pto=&dfrom=&dto=&x=0&y=0 Gutenberg MF is another digital revival: http://www.dafont.com/guttenberg-mf.font?text=Thehg&psize=l
