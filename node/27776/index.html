2006-08-24 00:27:03 • Miguel Sousa

Regardless of venue, the Americans make up the majority of attendees to all ATypI conferences. Because of this fact, several years ago, ATypI was entertaining the notion of holding the conference in North America, every other year.

The stated policy beginning in the mid-90s was to try to have a North American conference one in four years. But this reflected the North American membership levels, not the North American conference attendance. I’m afraid your comment about Americans being ‘the majority of attendees to all ATypI conferences’ isn’t true, James. As representatives from a single nation, they are often the largest group, but they are not the majority overall. Furthermore, and problematically for ATypI, our conferences in North America are never as well attended as our conferences in Europe. San Francisco in 1994 was a small conference. Vancouver in 2003 had only 260 attendees including speakers (against an average of 350-400 attendees at European conferences in recent years). My memory of Boston in 1999 was that it was larger than San Francisco or Vancouver, but perhaps that was in part due to the relative proximity to Europe. In North America, there is much more competition from other design conferences and recently from Typecon. At the Prague ATypI, some of our board members met with SOTA board members to discuss this, and from our perspective the question is very much ‘How best can ATypI continue to mount events in North America?’ i.e. it is very much something that we want to do. We’re overdue for a North American conferences, and although we have not confirmed the location yet, I’m pretty confident that you can expect one in the near future. I’m very willing to look at the dates for such a conference in the context of what will work best for the majority of our members from the Americas.

2006-08-24 00:47:57 • John Hudson

how you would spend two or three days in Lisbon as a tourist.

First day, Lisbon In addition to the classics, Monastery of the Jerónimos and Tower of Belém, I’d walk around downtown and the old neighborhoods, always trying to avoid the big malls and stores that you can find anywhere in the world. (Hopefully we’ll have a late-Summer sunny week). I’d also visit the new part of the city, Parque das Nações, which was refurbished from top to bottom to held Expo 98.

Second day, Sintra (20Km West of Lisbon) Take the commute train and get off at the end of the line; if you took the right train, you’ll be in Sintra. (You’ll also notice the difference in the air’s quality, and its humidity :^). This is definitely a must see!! The Cultural Landscape of Sintra was classified as World Heritage by Unesco in 1995. There you can visit the old Villa, the Palácio da Vila (right downtown), the Mourish Castle, the Palácio da Pena, and the Quinta da Regaleira, which holds many secrets of Masonic and Templar rituals. Along the way you’ll find other places of interest, like the City Hall, and the Moorish Fountain, for example.

Third day If you feel adventurous enough, hop on a train heading North (start from Oriente Station), and visit Tomar (another World Heritage site), Fátima’s Sanctuary, Coimbra, or Oporto. Portugal is a fairly small country, so a train ride to Oporto (the most Northern city I mentioned) would take only about 3 hours.

Are we talking about the conference programme or the pre-conference TypeTech Forum content?
