Proper way to display measurements in ranges?

September 8, 2010 • kohme

Is there a right or a wrong way to diplay measurments in a range? if so what is proper?

1"–10" or 1–10" 1mm–10mm or 1–10mm

Also should there be a space between the number and “mm”?

1–10 mm or 1–10mm?

“1–10 mm”.

The en-dash is used in its “range” context, and the space between value and unit is mandatory per SI specifications.

Am exception is degrees Celsius: “10–20°C”, no space (and it’s not really an exception, because degrees Celsius are not an SI unit).

(I have to admit I don’t know the rules for negative numbers; −10–−5°C looks weird, with alternating minus and en-dash. Fortunately, values in mm are usually not in the negative range.)
