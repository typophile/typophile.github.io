Non-fascist Blackletter suggestions?

June 19, 2008 • stefpos

Hello all, Being that I often search these forums for advice and it's always been helpful, I have decided to take the plunge and actually post a query for once, so here goes: I am designing a cover for a book relating to the history of WWII, Germany in particular. I used Tannenberg Fett and DIN as my main typefaces, because I liked the modernity of hard edges and I thought the sharp edges seemed to work well to represent the time period and the aesthetic theme of the cover, in that the design slowly becomes more and more hard-edged and angular, as the book describes germany from before the war to during the war and I wanted to represent this shift visually...(sorry I can't show images at the moment, but I'll just try to describe what I was going for) However, while the design was approved, and no one thinks that the design has Nazi overtones, the author has had reservations about the use of Tannenburg, so I have to change the typeface to something else. After research into the rise of the Schaftsteiffelgrotesk type of letterforms, I can see his point. So: Does anyone have any suggestions about a German typeface, pre-WWII, that doesn't seem to convey "Nazi" all the while conveying both "German" and "hard-edged"? Is there a modern blackletter that would be suitable? Or, is there another, German, non-blackletter typeface that fits the bill? I have found Potsdam, but I am concerned it is too similar to Tannenburg. http://moorstation.org/typoasis/blackletter/htm/potsdam.htm And I have found this logo, which I like the look of as well, but I am not too sure where I can find German typefaces like this, or if this is again too close to Tannenburg: http://www.flickr.com/photos/70153157@N00/2296495586/ I don't want to glorify the Third Reich, obviously, but I want to find something that communicates the period and culture, yet has a minimal, geometric feel, and I am not sure where to go. Thanks Stef
