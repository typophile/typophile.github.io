Our local zoo sells elephant poop-paper. For the record, these papers are likely much more archival than tree-based (as the plant matter used has much longer fibers). When made into paper, you're really not keeping the 'poop'...just the semi-digested fibers. Ruminants are really ineffecient animals in terms of digestion...most of what goes into them comes back out.
2006-10-02 19:10:39 • aluminum
The real concern is not the plant fibres per se -- it's whatever else that gets trapped in the fibres, as even a good washing isn't going to get rid of everything. For example, the USDA (http://www.aphis.usda.gov/ppq/permits/plantproducts/soil.html) has some pretty picky rules about importing soil (and the constituents thereof) and even if what is brought into the country isn't bad (hello, Dutch elm disease) for the environment, it may well alter the chemistry, especially pH, of the paper. Your heffalumps probably produce great paper -- ours produce "Zoo Doo" garden nutrient. :-) Linda
