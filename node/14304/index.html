2005-08-02 12:24:15 • eomine

Some cursive font makers (for example Signature Software) map the alternate cursive forms to upper ASCII characters (eg all those accented characters) they then use a macro to switch these alternates into a passage of text. Apart from being a bit of a hack, the text is not searchable or cut and paste-able, and if you switch fonts becomes gibberish. Another reason why OpenType is a better bet.

2005-08-02 14:40:30 • Si_Daniels
The Cursive handwriting font requires individual characters to have multiple beginning and ending strokes depending on the character pair combination used in a word. Around 1992 I designed and developed the typefaces for Zaner-Bloser Publishing who had the original drawing. Because the character set was limited I developed several font and a colleague designed a custom application to switch fonts on the fly. I assume this application is very similar. Your right Si, OpenType would have made the project more interactive.
