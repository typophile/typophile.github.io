…another part of this post is seeing if people either agree with my style staying in graphic design or would i fit better in a different category. Four years in art school will leave you looking back at everything you did before and shaking your head. Styles and attitudes change, and you’ll never know what happens. I started as an abstract painter, switched to digital media design, and finally ended up falling in love with print and type design. The important thing to do is choose a school where you can experiment with everything and where the fine art and design departments overlap. Take a look at the work of seniors and recent grads—if it looks like most of the work was done entirely with a computer, that means you be stuck doing most of your work on the computer, and trust me, you’ll miss out on a lot. And think about big schools so that you don’t miss out on important classes because of scheduling conflicts or course cancellations due to low enrollment.

2008-08-22 19:29:05 • iffy
How much money you make typically depends on what you're designing. If you design album covers and concert posters, you're most likely going to make a fraction of what someone designing annual reports for the medical industry is making. Of course, most people would find the former more fun than the latter. Where I live, designers right out of school would make somewhere between 30K and 45K a year, with most people somewhere in the middle. If you need more money than that, going down the motion graphic and/or interactive path might be what you want to do -- a lot of people bill $60-100/hr after a few years in these industries. If you live in rural Mississippi, those numbers would be much lower, though. The most important thing, though, is to not listen to "experts" too much, whether they're random people on the internet or teachers at your school. I had a million people tell me "you have to get into web," or "you should do more illustration," etc. while I was in school, and I took them very seriously for a long time. If you love doing something, you can figure out a way to make money with it.
