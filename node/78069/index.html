2011-01-16 11:34:58 • ReneRutten
There are also a lot of people producing calligraphic/drawn pieces inspired by copperplate writing with tons of flourishes just now. It’s everywhere. Enough already? In UK Seb Lester has much prominence and produces lovely work (much ripped off). Alison Carmichael is another one. She has collaborated with Sean Freeman on other projects so could well have drawn the original lettering for him for the ‘cream’ piece. If it’s a case of finding good script typefaces then that is a problem. Translation into type kills the life force! Much weedy and kitch. Commercial Script (Morris Fuller Benton 1906) is a pretty good model and robust too if that’s what you want and is a kind of ‘if it ain’t broke don’t fix it’ choice. Matthew Carter’s Shelley has his excellence but is a lighter weight. Doyald Young sells a really beautiful one from his own site, but it’s a lighter weight too. Similarly, Ale Paul at Sudtipos has gourgeous Burgues with a vast array of alternates with various swashes. (his Piel and Adios are not copperplate but very impressive). I’d like to know of more myself so let me know if you find something fab. Nothing beats integrity of writing/drawing here though does it? and work of the modern ‘greats’ like Doyald Young or Jean Larcher.
