Font choice for a book of poems

June 1, 2005 • Bouch

I'm looking for some opinion on a choice of font for a book of poems. The mood of the book is somewhat despondent; poems of death, holocaust, graveyards, historical references to gruesome battles and struggles of faith. I'm thinking along the lines of Vendetta. Minion is too generic, Warnock is too happy. Fairfield is nice but it feels too much like old literature. I guess I want something with a just bit of an edge to it, but not so much as to distract from the reading. It's a book of poems, not a novel, so pure function isn't so important. Tell me if you think Vendetta, or someting like it might work. Any other suggestions are welcome! Thanks
I don't think it matters if its all about the content, but I would look at Emigre's Dalliance Script as long as the poems aren't too long. Its elegant and might provide an edge to the presentation.

But I'm also getting this feeling that you need two distinct voices: something coarse but well-proportioned like Vendetta; but also something clinical and austere, like a Didone, an old-fashioned one, somewhat archaic, maybe turn-of-the-century French. As G W Ovink said*, "Bodoni is an admirable letter for a death notice!" The trick though would be choose two fonts that both contrast and match. * Actually, that's a collage of two of his phrases. hhp
