Typography Insturctors: A Modest Proposal

April 10, 2011 • oldnick

I am currently—and rather slowly—in the process of updating the entire Nick’s Fonts library of fonts in anticipation of packaging them in thematic collections and one whole-shebang package. The entire collection represents twelve years of work, with 530+ font families and over 600 fonts. In order to make the packages more navigable for end users, I would very much like to produce accompanying documents classifying the fonts according to style, weight, historical period, attitude and the like. Unfortunately, the exigencies of production (as it were) leave me with little time (or energy) to do the necessary heavy lifting.

So…it occurred to me that there might be, somewhere in Typophile land, an instructor of typography at the collegiate level who might be able to assist me in this endeavor. This is what I propose:

  • I will provide you and your students with my entire collection of fonts, fully licensed for use in the classroom.
  • I will donate to your program type specimen books from my collection, to include the ATF 1923 book, the 1934 ATF book, Barnhart Brothers & Spindler’s Catalog #25, and a number of vintage lettering chapbooks.

What I would like in return, at a minimum, would be spreadsheets covering the classification I outlined, plus any other classifications which we mutually agree might be relevant. And I am certainly willing to consider other options which might allow your students to flex their creative muscles in ways that would also add value to my retail packages.

If you are such an instructor and interested, please drop me a line a himself [at] nicksfonts [dot] com, and we can talk turkey…or grease…

So, no response because…

  • No typography instructors hereabouts?
  • It's a stupid assignment?
  • It's way too much work for over $5,000 full retail value in fonts and reference materials?
  • No one takes me seriously anyhow?

2011-04-15 11:47:07 • ben_archer

Thanks to all who have replied. Let me add a little more information about what I think I’m looking for.

My goal is to create some kind of tool which will make navigating my font collection easier, in terms of locating the kind of font one is looking for, and being more user-friendly than a simple PDF catalog. However, I don’t have any preconceived notions as to what such a tool might look like. Thus, part of the project could include asking students to devise a classification system which is meaningful to them as potential font users/buyers. If they could come up with a widget or an interface to make navigation easier, so much the better, but it’s not a required element.

So, the font navigation database is what I would like as a minimum: any suggestions to expand the scope of the project—specimen sheet design, perhaps?—would be eagerly entertained, as would be options for offering students incentives/rewards for work beyond the main scope of the project.

I am not under any formal time constraints or deadlines: I still have some fine-tuning to do myself. But, obviously, the sooner the project gets done, the sooner it can be an important value-added component to the font collection as a stand-alone retail package. I hope this info makes things a little clearer…
