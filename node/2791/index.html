Another very good book is TF Mitchell's Writing Arabic. This book focuses almost exclusively on the ruq'ah script, the most common daily handwriting style found in the Middle East, but it is the only text I've come across, apart from Thomas Milo's essay in Language Culture Type, that treats connectivity of the Arabic script as something other than collections of 'ligatures'. John B's recommendation of The splendor of Islamic calligraphy is a good one, which I'll second. Its catalogue of styles is not as complete nor as nuanced as that in The art of the pen, i.e. it misses a number of significant sub-styles, but it has many good examples of fine work. Perhaps a academic library near you has a copy of The art of the pen?
