Billboard Typography - Readability From A Distance?

September 21, 2007 • jimsbee

Does anyone know where I can find some online reference for environmental typography, specifically what general type sizes are required for reading at certain distances?

I need to design a billboard and I’m not sure how small I can go with the type, yet still have it be readable from say 50 yards.

Thanks!

In this thread: [[http://www.typophile.com/node/36418]] James Montalbano quoted; “As to size, I believe ADA guidelines specify 1 inch of cap height for every 50 ft of viewing distance.(I know this is what FHWA has built their spec on).”

I also remember using this guideline back in my days in the sign business. You might want to use a size larger than that formula provides, since a billboard needs to be read from a moving vehicle, which is considerably more difficult than reading while standing still.

I just did 11 billboards for a campaign in New Jersey. 15 words. They range from 14' x 36' (signage companies always do the dimensions backwards - at least for me) to 16' x 60'.

I would estimate that the main letters (a band name in Franklin Gothic Condensed or Extra Condensed) are 8' to 14' tall (depending on the board size).

The other words are probably around 2' to 5' tall.

They are done to scale in Illustrator (1/48 to 1/12 scale depending on the vendor) so you can get there with point size. I have done them full size and then you have to set the type and scale it - 1200 points - I believe that’s the limit in our software - is not large enough.
