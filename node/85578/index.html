Text font, possibly Linotype, circa 1966

September 18, 2011 • sampotts

Hello all,

This is from Pantheon paperback edition of Hopscotch by Julio Cortázar, published in 1966. I cannot for the life of me, etc etc. The designers at Knopf guessed it was Primer, and most likely set in metal. My scan is from a reprint, so allow for thickening of everything. You can still see some of the remnants of a medium-to-high contrast face.

I seem to be unable to attach or upload, so please go here to see the scan: http://www.sampottsinc.com/H/hopscotch_text_face_F.jpg

I’ve highlighted some of the things that make this unusual (to me at least): the Century-like tail of the R with old-style figures, an unkerned f and a longish and quite vertical tail to the y. The italic is wide and certainly not of the Century style, and the single-story italic g throws me.

My best guess is that it’s derived from a Zapf face, perhaps some rounder, more classical kind of Melior. Or something that was an imitation of Caledonia, maybe (if it’s odd, it might be Dwiggins, as they say).

Thanks!

Sam

The closest match I can see is: Century751 SeBd BT [Semi Bold] - Copyright 1990-1999 Bitstream Inc. http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/bitstream/century-751/semi-bold/characters.html Is a bit lighter than your sample, but maybe there is also a Bold version.

EDIT: Maybe this one is a closer match: Century751 No2 BT [Bold] http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/bitstream/century-751/no-2-bold/

..and for the italic: Century751 SeBd BT [Semi Bold Italic] - Copyright 1990-1999 Bitstream Inc. http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/bitstream/century-751/semi-bold-italic/characters.html

Century751 No2 BT [Bold Italic] - Copyright 1990-1999 Bitstream Inc. http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/bitstream/century-751/no-2-bold-italic/characters.html

An interesting detail is that both italic typefaces have the single story italic g while the regular version is not!
