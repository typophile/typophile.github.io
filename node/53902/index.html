The signage of New Orleans is a great reflection of the creativity of the city as a whole. Many things in the city are family owned and run. These places are local favorites and play a big role in the city’s culture. Dewitt’s Fruit and Vegetable Shed is a perfect example of this.

Another way that New Orleans signage resembles the city’s personality is through the use of nostalgic colors and symbols that resonate with locals as well as visitors. The Mardi Gras colors, purple, green and gold, are present in the Jacques-Imo’s sign as well as a fleur de lis. These things remind people of New Orleans and the restaurant itself continues to do so with it’s incredible food.

Lastly, the Post-Katrina New Orleans is flourishing by opening up new businesses, reopening old businesses and sprucing up everything in between. Tipitina’s has been rocking this logo for a long time but their use of large and well constructed signs make it look even more professional.

These are just three examples of the different ways that the New Orleans signs portray the flavors of the city and its people.
