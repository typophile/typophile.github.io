Once a client gets involved at that level and begins to pick n mix your designs instead of letting you be the designer they employed in the first place then you’re probably just gonna have to sacrifice the job on the altar of their ego.

Any time spent trying to now make the logo better in your eyes will be wasted because the client is unlikely to appreciate or even notice the difference.

Don’t worry if it offends because the offence is no longer yours.

You may be able to pursuade them to choose just one of the original fonts if you tell them that other designers on a respected type design forum said the ‘h’ stands out like a sore thumb :)

The fleck on the ‘h’ just looks like a dirty mark especially when it’s a characteristic that’s not in the other letters. It will look even more like a dirty mark at small sizes.
