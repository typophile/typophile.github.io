CS3 > CS4 compatibility.

October 13, 2008 • Pedro Peixoto

Hello folks.

Our design department is with some doubts about CS3 to CS4 compatibility. We work mainly with InDesign, so I’d like to ask you guys if I can just save a document in .indd from CS3 and open it in CS4 right away. And of course saving it in CS4 and opening it in CS3.

I realize this may be a hard question since the software ain’t out yet, but someone could have read about this somewhere else.

If you happen to know any other compatibility issues between these two, don’t hesitate on telling me.

Thank you.

Up until now the policy has been that documents are upward compatible (eg you can open a CS3-doc in CS4), but NOT downwards (eg you can not directly open a CS4-doc in CS3, but have to export a file in the IDX-format to open it in the lower version, one step at a time — from CS4 to CS3 works, but not to CS2 — you have to re-export in CS3). Chances are that the new version works likewise. It has to do with new features being introduced in new versions that will not translate well to the older ones.

I may be not correct, but this was what I understood from the demo I attended.

. . . Bert Vanderveen BNO
