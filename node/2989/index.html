What exactly does it mean for type to be "fussy"? I've seen the term used in Typophile discussions as well as other sites. Any other terms I should be aware of? Thanks, Addison
Good question. I myself don't hear it too often (fortunately), although recently on SpeakUp (which I rarely frequent, only because I have to draw the line somewhere :-) somebody was defending the use of Times because it "wasn't fussy". Which is pretty funny because Times is strongly self-conscious - in a way it's really a display face. I don't think you'll get a consensus, just a vague feeling. To me it means "detailed" in a self-conscious way. Minimalists like their bread dry, and they tend to confuse the power of expression with fussiness. A more focused term might be "mannered", which is actually used in a sufficiently coherent way by a number of designers. hhp

2004-02-05 21:23:33 • Miss Tiffany
I did call Minion fussy on these boards. Then I got Minion Pro, and found it not fussy. I just now took the trouble to try to compare the letter forms large. Minion Pro slightly thickens the serifs at the tips. I think that did it. To me the original was a little too calligraphic in its finish. Minion Pro is also very subtly refitted, but you can definitely notice the difference when you set text in both as I have - Minion Pro is more compact, and the color is better. Fussy to me is decorative elements that don't harmonize with the overall design. In this case, I think it was the sharper serifs (same length) that were to me fussy. I first got on these boards to ask about Minion, and felt pretty happy later when I found out that Slimbach had corrected just what I had felt was wrong, but couldn't put my finger on. I think that the Victorian period had a lot of fussy design, but this may be more true of the later Victorian periond - I don't know enough of the history. I also said I find the serifs in Hoefer's Requium fussy - apparently I'm the very distinct minority. I am not against ornament, but I think it is hard to bring off well. When it is brought off well it is breathtaking, a tour de force. Probably it is out of place in text type.

2004-02-06 23:45:18 • matteson
It would be interesting to compare those books printed in Minion and Minion Pro, if anyone knows. I have compared the outlines of Minion Pro vs Minion minion pro vs minion.pdf (438.3 k) at 300 pt, set with Illustrator CS. I find it quite interesting, having seen the effect on the set type, and knowing that Slimbach is a master who here had another crack at improving the type for fit and color, a number of years later. You will notice that the serifs and thins are noticeably thickened, and that the o is not only thicker, but narrower. The overall effect when set is that the type is sturdier and sets narrower. The new Pro regular now sets nearly as compact as the old display weight, which was lighter than the old regular.
