Kerning just one pair in InDesign

March 29, 2006 • mili

I am freelancing at an agency that does a lot of supermarket ads. They are in a process of changing from Quark to InDesing CS, and came accross a small problem. When typing prices without cents they use ,- (50,-) kerned so, that the hyphen is flush left with the full stop. Font used is Helvetica Neue Heavy Condensed. When there are cents, they use superscript. Is it possible to define the .- pair so, that it would behave the same way every time, regardess the size of the font, while the kerning of numbers would stay normal? And if it's possible, how do I do it?

The easy way to kern is place your curser between the characters and option left arrow key to tighten the kerning pair. Option right arrow key to loosen the kerning pairs. It also works if you highlight the whole word and option left arrow key to tighten the word and you get the rest
