I am looking for magazines designed for the disabled.

September 17, 2007 • blank

This weekend I came across ADDitude, a magazine for individuals with attention deficit disorder and their families. The design was remarkable—it was extremely attractive and attention-getting visually, but had a well-developed—and followed—hierarchy that was clearly designed to make it easy on readers with ADD. This got me wondering—are there more magazines out there designed for readers with visual, reading, or learning disabilities?

My son is Autistic but none of the material I have seenin this area ahs been particulary well or poorly designed. I am glad to see there are some people who have considered well their audience. Can you post a scan or a link, James?

2007-09-19 14:17:19 • joeclark

I’ve got a profound hearing loss and therefore have a disability. I’ve got no issues with the word “disability”. However if people asked me the question, “are you disabled”, I’d simply answer “no” purely for political or social reasons. There are people who equate disability with “crippled” or someone with an inability to achieve anything substantial at all. This is a complete nonsense. A disability is simply an obstacle in life, if you can’t hear, see, taste, feel or walk, you gotta work around the problem like anything else, that’s all. It’s disappointing sometimes how people perceive disabilities but it’s improving all the time with increased understanding and also support for braille, captioning etc.

I’m all for magazines about disabilities.
