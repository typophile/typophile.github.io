Hi, Bogdan.

Is there a rule that says that in a logotype, all letters should be spaced equally?

I don’t think so. But if someone told you this, perhaps they were refering to optical compensation – the letters should appear to be balanced or spaced equally.

Your third and fourth examples don’t work because there is equal distance. But in your first example, I think you have too much space after the second L… And you may have too little space on either side of the U (but too much in the second example). Here’s a suggestion: print out your logo, hang it on a wall, and stand back some distance to look at it. Then, squint your eyes a bit. This will help you to see where you need to adjust the kerning.
