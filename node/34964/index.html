When you describe the letters as »clumsy« you are judging it in zoomed view on screen, don’t you? You should only judge them when printed in a small sizes. You will notice that it usually helps to have a bolder stroke and less contrast. So making the text sizes bolder is a good start. You may try differnt settings for X and Y and you will need make adjustments, because you will loose some details like ink traps.

Stefan,

Simply: Print several samples of text at several small sizes, such as 6, 8, 9, 10, 12 point. Then, look at them. Go for a bike ride, play soccer, sleep, wake up and return to the samples, and again, look at them. See them not as the designer but as a disinterested reader. Can you read them? Compare those samples with a variety of books and magazines. What is easiest to read, what is difficult? Once you have seen the effect of your type at small sizes, you will know what to do.

General rules: light typefaces, and typefaces set at very small point sizes are not comfortable to read. The smaller you set the type, the stronger the forms should be.

If you want to use automatic tools, FontLab lets you add different amounts of weight to verticals and horizontals at the same time. For example, you can add 50 units to the main stems and only 20 units to the horizontals. This does not give finished results, but it can be useful.
