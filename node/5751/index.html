2004-07-24 08:45:42 • Giampa
Nick, Hrant, Thomas and Gerald all in the same place. It's one
big happy love fest. I sat by Nick during the entertaining, but
tediously long Linotype awards (seven honorable mentions for
crying out loud). You'll be glad to know that Nick speaks his
mind in person as much as he does online. (Kudos Nick for
being true to your brand. =) Jared and I are deejaying the
Saturday night party. I can't wait to see Nick kick it to some
house jams.

I took the Ultrafonts class, Designing Type for Mobile Devices. It
was co-hosted by Ultrafonts cohort, Hrant. I had no idea he was
working with Ultrafonts, but it's not at all surprising. Their
presentation was great, very hands on. I've never met Hrant in
person. He was as helpful as he usually is in the critique forum.

I'm having a great, great time. For me, TypeCon is as much about
people as it is about type and that's the way it should be. It was
great to finally connect a face with Randy Jones and to see Grant
Hutchinson and his classic Chuck Taylors.

Regarding the Linotype awards. There were a couple scripts
that are really great. But the one that blew me away was not
type, but symbols. The husband and wife collaboration of Karin
and Peter Huschka, he an advertising art director and she a
textile designer yielded Picture Yourself with some truly
great forms that are interchangable, meant to be layered and
formed into patterns. The black and white samples don't do it
justice. Imagine it in bamboo and celery greens, layered and
overlayed. Great.

A word about Interrobang. It's much improved over last year's.
Nice job Stewf. (Proof that paper matters!)

As far as meatspace vs. online goes, when you talk to someone in person, you have much less opportunity to be misunderstood because you not only get the words, you also get body language, facial expression, tone of voice, etc., and it's much harder to forget another person's humanity when they're standing right in front of you.
