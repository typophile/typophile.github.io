naji,

i suppose you are testing your font on mac osx. unfortunately osx relies on aat tables to render arabic on the system level. opentye support only exists (and even that is poor) for latin and japanese.

however, this applies only to system applications - textedit and safari for example. in fact i don’t know of other applications which support aat - presumably that’s the reason no one but apple uses this technology. your fonts should be fine in adobe me applications on mac.

naji,

AFAIK you can have opentype and aat tables in the same font if you really want to support arabic rendering on a system level on both platforms. however, i have no idea about the extra workload this involves.

the illustrator crash could have many reasons. but generally you should be able to insert every glyph through the glyph palette - i’ve used this with arabic in illustrator cs2. you don’t get correct rendering, but have to choose the appropriate form of your letter manually.

you can find tools for aat on the mac homepage: http://developer.apple.com/textfonts/Tools/index.html

2006-08-04 12:53:04 • titus n.

Naji:

  1. You can’t have OpenType and AAT tables in the same font, as since Apple started implementing OpenType support the two will conflict if both are present. Use seperate OT/AAT font files to get around this.
