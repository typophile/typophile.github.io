Unfortunately, the vast majority of those logos clearly weren’t designed with any concern for readability, or the ability to be reproduced cleanly in a variety of media.

Since the red cross is such a well-known symbol, I saw it as a red cross, rather than a “T”, which made the logo read as Red Ruck Foundation. Even though you’ve broken apart the cross so it doesn’t look exactly like the Red Cross logo, I think if the RTF used your logo (or any of the others with a red cross in it), they’d be getting some unwanted attention from the Red Cross’s lawyers.

The stencil font you used also doesn’t help readability. The “d” kind of reads as “cl”

You’ve got a good start, but there are a few things you could do to refine it that would really improve it:

• You’ve got three different fonts, and unrelated ones at that. See if you could limit it to one or two. If you need to use multiple fonts, you’re safer using ones that contrast with each other (rather than three different sans serifs) or are from the same family (like different weights and/or widths of Franklin Gothic, or Helvetica, etc.)

• Nothing really aligns. The bottom of the horizontal bar is a little lower than the bottom of the “r” in “red”, and the bottom of the vertical bar is a little lower than the “r” in “ruck”

• maybe try making the horizontal bar the same thickness as the height of the “r” and the horizontal bar a little wider than that.

• What if “foundation” had less spacing, and went from the left edge of the vertical red bar to the right edge of “k”, and the tag line was the same width

The idea behind the foundation is great, and I would think they’d want something simple and iconic that didn’t rely on English text to be understandable.
