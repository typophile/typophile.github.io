“The only reason Apple isn’t supporting Flash is because, by doing so, they will sell wayyyy less Apps in the AppStore”

Apple seems to be taking a brunt of the blame in this ‘debate’. I think it’s rather telling that it wasn’t until this past release of CS5 that Adobe made native Cocoa apps.

Apple is a forward thinking company and I’d agree with Steve that if you rely on the 3rd party IDEs, your developers will always be several steps behind what Apple is doing. At that point, you’d not differentiating your platform very well (which has always been Apple’s primary business model).

Does that piss of a chunk of developers out there who like the idea of right once deploy in multiple devices? Absolutely. But there are so many dedicated Apple developers that I think they’re not too worried about that.

“I, personally, hardly ever see Flash crash so I don’t know where they got that.”

They get it from more than one person’s first-hand personal experiences.

“other than you may have been losing (very little) money”

Again, they want to fully manage and preserve the user experience. I think there’s room for arguments of anti-competitiveness, for sure, but from a basic user experience POV, Apple isn’t stupid.
