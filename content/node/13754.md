body: "<strong>[[Indices]] : [[Education]] : The San Francisco Center for the Book</strong>\r\n\r\nSince
  it's opening in 1996, <a href=\"http://www.sfcb.org/index.php\">The San Francisco
  Center for the Book</a> is dedicated to providing educational resources in the book
  arts. The center offers workshops and exhibitions for varying degrees of expertise,
  welcoming just about anyone with an interest in books-arts or related crafts, from
  newcomers to experienced practitioners. Some of it's 300 <a href=\"http://www.sfcb.org/php/classes.php\">workshops</a>
  offered include year-long and introductory courses covering topics like traditional
  bookbinding methods and advanced printing techniques. In addition to the many workshops
  and <a href=\"http://www.sfcb.org/html/exhibitions.html\">exhibitions</a>, the Center
  offers free events for the public and some that are designed specifically to reach
  out to Bay area communities, such as hands-on demonstrations for local schools and
  organizations.\r\n\r\nThe SFCB also serves as a venue for other book-arts organizations
  in the Bay Area, such as  [[The Pacific Center for the Book Arts]] and [[The Hand
  Bookbinders of California.]]\r\n\r\nPast Exhibitions include:\r\n\r\n\u2022 <a href=\"http://www.sfcb.org/html/past-hanni.html\">Swiss
  Impressions: Romano H\xE4nni and the Art of Metal Type</a>\r\n\u2022 <a href=\"http://www.sfcb.org/html/past-cuba.html\">Journey
  to the Source: Handmade Books from Cuba</a>\r\n\u2022 <a href=\"http://www.sfcb.org/html/past-massin.html\">Massin
  in Continuo: A Dictionary</a>"
