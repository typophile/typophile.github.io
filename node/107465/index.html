Don’t know it but here are some you may like: [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/itc/lubalin-graph|Lubalin Graph ITC]] (possibly the closest in term of overall design approach, main difference being top unserifed /A), [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/mti/rockwell|Rockwell]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/northernblock/teletex|Teletex]] (artificially emboldened by adding a thick outline stroke might work?), [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/synthview/novecento-slab|Novocento Slab]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/artill-typs/sketch-block|Sketch Block]] (keeping only outlines), [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/intellecta/figgins-brute|Figgins Brute]] (very different mainly because of /R but I like its vintage feeling), [[http://www.myfonts.com/search/Stymie|Stymie]] (see Teletex), [[http://www.fontsquirrel.com/fonts/arvo|Arvo]], [[http://www.fontsquirrel.com/fonts/rokkitt|Rokkitt]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/search/Kettering|Kettering]] 105 and 205

Slab serif lettering perhaps inspired by Rockwell, designed by Frank Hinman Pierpoint in 1934 for Monotype as an updating of Inland’s Litho Antique of 1910. Rockwell’s G matches the lettering, but the leg on R has only a half serif. http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/mti/rockwell/ Don
