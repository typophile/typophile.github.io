online or not online?

November 6, 2006 • missgiggles

in what forms do you think designers should have their CV in? should it be online only or should it be paper based too? what other creative formats could you have them in? please help. i need ideas.

Your online version should be able to be easily downloaded and printed, like a PDF. You should also include a link on where to download Acrobat Reader just in case the person doesn’t have it on their computer.

If you have an online portfolio, again you should have a downloadable PDF for people to print if they want to. It doesn’t have to include every sample that your online portfolio has, but it should be 5-7 pages to give them a good idea of the type of work you do.

2006-11-06 22:16:09 • adnix

When I was trying to locate a design job earlier this year, nobody would even look at anything (portfolio/resume) that wasn’t softcopy, and then it had to be in PDF or MS-Word format. So I put my portfolio of crappy student work online, and send out my resume as a PDF.

Keep in mind that you should never send people an editable version of your CV/resume. Although it probably doesn’t happen often in design, it’s not uncommon for recruiting firms to alter your resume to make a you look better to a potential employer.
