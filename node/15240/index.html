Climbing out of the design rut...

September 20, 2005 • Duckworth

Hi all! I was just listening to a [[Typeradio]] Podcast - Typophile's own BaldCondensed talked about using typefaces you personally dislike (or aren't particularly keen on) - forcing yourself to use them - consequently keeping your design work fresh... Does everyone else have their own method to keeping/maintaining a fresh approach to their design work? It hit home with me because I have become selective in my old age (28) which means that I've got a set of favourite typefaces I use, I'm set in my ways with grids, and I've just realised (I couldn't quite put my finger on it before) that I'm not striving to push myself further... Thanks Yves, I'd stayed away from the fonts I didn't like - and people are going to give me stick for this - serif faces have pretty much fallen right out of my font palette! I'm going to try and stick a few in my next project and try and appreciate them a bit more! Si
I guess it can be like ffishing lures. There are a few in the tackle box you've had great bites on, and you find yourself reaching for them again and again. That's not bad in and of itself, though. Certain typefaces just have a resonance with certain designers, and it's natural, just like having favorite musicians, painters, etc. But the real FUN starts when you stumble upon something new or overlooked and see the beauty in it. To that, I'd say, try a new font. Experiment with a bold you've never had much use for before. It's fun, and helps you stretch. But in the end, understand the "right" type for a particular piece (which you can only determine with a broad exposure to faces) -- not using something just BECAUSE you don't like it or think it doesn't work.
