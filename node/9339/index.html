Random all-digraph pseudotext

February 21, 2005 • raph

I offer for your edification the following 1000-word randomly generated pseudotext that contains all lowercase two-letter digraphs.

I took some care to make it vaguely resemble English text. The word lengths are drawn from a gamma(2, 2) distribution, and the target digraph frequencies were chosen to be somewhere between completely random and real English word frequencies.

You'll note a number of real words in there (lest, tump, oral, tore, tide, kale, rover). I'm especially fond of some of the pseudo-words that popped up: larther, licanor, houndelo, beronck, olopaki, thash, lexvac, fline, asmatechonal, hammyle, cimapyperus, thakemlo, enhragics, ugalve, jalyray, tsmonsur, epidet, hexiest, timigashinid, and cobis. Some of these really deserve to be words, in my opinion, while others would make a perfectly fine trademark.

Font designers are of course free to use this text to make sure that all same-case kern pairs are covered. There are a few other directions this could go, including adding caps, periods and commas to fake sentence structure, and further tweaking of the probability distributions to avoid such obvious nonwords as uhmwhwnmrf and rwvwxria.

Another possible direction is to combine automatic digraph generation with human editing, to make a plausible-sounding lorem ipsum with full digraph coverage.
