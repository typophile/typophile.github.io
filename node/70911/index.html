I agree, Slogan is your best bet. The extra loop in the ‘g’ is not necessarily a signature style of Matheis, but a relic of German Kurrentschrift, see this lettering chart on Nina’s Flickr pages. So is the stemless ‘d’.

Of course! This form of ‘d’ is not unique to Kurrent; for example, it can also be found in the French Ronde and typefaces inspired by it, like Gando, Fling, or even Cochin Italic. For 1940’s Germany, Kurrentschrift is just a much more obvious source of influence.
