2010-08-25 17:12:57 • Frode Bo Helland

Why not?

The symbols aren’t letters or numbers or any other textual character. They’re images. Try this: set anything in Zapf Dingbats. Then italicize the line. Nothing happens. The font is designed not to be italicized. I tried it in InDesign. I had to apply a manual skew to the Zapf characters.

When image glyphs are slanted, they seem to be tilted in perspective, not just skewed. I don’t see lines of type that way, unless the line tapers in some way (like the Superman nameplate on the comic strip or the narration to the first Star Wars movie).

2010-08-26 10:22:47 • Frode Bo Helland

2706: Telephone receiver. 213B: Fax sign. This is supposed to be an alphabetic glyph that says “FAX” but I think function is more pertinent than form.

For the rest, PUA. I am not sure whether it is legally acceptable to imitate the PUA codepoints used by Microsoft’s Wingdings, which uses F02A for an envelope and F03A for a computer. The “glyph order” is supposedly patented. It’s probably not worth it anyway since it’s still just PUA.
