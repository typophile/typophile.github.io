question about italic periods

September 16, 2009 • smithre

Hi. Just new to Typophile. I am transcribing some printed materials published in the American West (Illinois, Missouri, etc.) in the 1830s and 1840s. Our transcription style requires us to identify whether puncutation is roman or italic. It is of course quite easy to see the difference on most punctuation marks–except for the period. In something printed from my computer on a laser printer, I can see whether a period is italic or roman, but it is impossible to tell from old printed materials, when periods were not really uniform anyway (b/c, e.g., the type was old). So instead, I am trying to figure out if the printers would have even owned an italic period. Was there such a thing back then? And if so, would it have been common or rare for a printer to actually own and use such a piece of type? Can any of you recommend something in print that discusses matters like this?
While I’m at it, I should ask another question too. Some of the em dashes in the printed materials I am transcribing appear to have been formed by two smaller dashes, likely en dashes. They were probably meant to look like a single dash, but there is a hairspace between them, perhaps because the ink didn’t get applied properly. Did printers often make em dashes by combinining two en dashes, or would they have done this only when they ran out of em dashes?
