Poetry setting is definitely a different animal than most other typography.

I think that you have done a nice job.

One possible criticism: You haven’t had any long lines that exceed your margins. If you do, you may want to consider a) moving your left margin further left, and b) consider how you would handle those issues.

It’s just me, but I would move the left margin anyway. The first page looks good, but some of the longer lines on the other pages look a little unbalanced to me.

Only real problem I see: The first page uses vertical dots between the title and the text. The other pages don’t have this device.

2006-12-05 20:57:27 • dsgoen
Are the periods after the roman numbering really necessary? There’s a big chunk of space separating the numbers and the text, so the periods are redundant. Also, try working a little color—maybe just the titles and possibly section numbers in red—those poems lean toward melancholic, so a little warmth might do the pages some good. If the final version is going to include pages numbers, add those now so you don’t have to redesign around them later.
2006-12-05 21:06:44 • blank

My pet peeve every since Bringhurst enlightened me is the inclusion of the Th ligature along with the normal ones (ff, fl, fi, etc.) in most of Adobe’s pro fonts. If you like it, keep it in, but I find it distracting (see the title of “The Diner” for an example of where it crops up). The only way I know of correcting it without removing the other ligs is to select the Th’s and uncheck “Ligatures” in the character palette.

Other than that, I agree with the others – very nice work!
