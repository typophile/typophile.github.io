June 9, 2008 • cubanica

I’ve designed a family with six styles…Regular, Italic, Medium, Medium Italic, Bold, Bold Italic. The overall family name is Playoff Serif. The problem is the order that CS places them:

Italic Regular Medium Medium Italic Bold Bold Italic

Of course, Regular needs to come first, not Italic. I’ve read numerous documents on naming, etc. and have tried a bunch of different solutions with no luck. I feel like Muhammed Ali in the George Foreman fight. He lost te first couple of rounds…

You might be over-estimating the Power of CS. A quick glance to my InDesign shows that some fonts are placed in the regular (convenient) order; others are not.

A second look, however, reveals that the convenient order only happens with fonts that just have ‘Normal’ (i.c. “Regular”, “Medium”), ‘Italic’ (“Oblique”, “Slanted”), ‘Bold’ and ‘Bold Italic’ styles. Every font with styles other than these appear in alphabetic order, which is, of course, also some sort of convention. Adobe’s flagship fonts Minion Pro and Myriad Pro don’t have “it”, so obviously they don’t know themselves how to sort their own fonts!

It seems that for standard fonts the bitfields “Bold” and “Italic” are used – you can set these somewhere in your Font Info, but adding that one in-between weight “Medium” messes the system up, and CS reverts to alphabetics. Which makes at least some sense – which one is ‘darker’: Medium or Regular? How about (scanning my font list) “Allegro Script” and “Andante Script”? “Regular” and “Dfr”? “Solid” and “Inline”? “Italic” and “Oldstyle”?

The only one that makes sense is “Univers” :-D
