... the true nature of the ogonek has been revealed to me! It is not a something completely unique to Polish; there is one other language which appends something to its letters that genuinely looks like an ogonek in exactly the way that the cedilla doesn't. It is of polytonic Greek that I speak, and the iota subscript. Since it is in origin a subscript letter, while the ogonek is in origin simply a differentiating mark, like the cedilla, the two marks aren't really the "same" mark in terms of their origin. But they are both hooks pointing and sloped in the same direction, so at least each one tends to support understanding how to draw the other one properly, whereas subscript commas and cedillas tempt people to replace the ogonek by things that don't look like an ogonek at all.

they are both hooks pointing and sloped in the same direction An observation which sticks on the surface and helps very litle. The iota subscr. IS NOT A HOOK. It is *a iota*. What points to the right on iota glyphs is *a serif*. DON’T mix up iota subscriptum with the ogonek. Don’t mix it up with anything else. The both have literally nothing in common.
