Serif companion to Neutraface?

March 17, 2005 • adnix

I am working on a proposal that my company is putting together. It's a huge binder filled with a lot of financial info as well as photographs and architectural renderings of our stores.

We are using Neutraface as the display face, but I want to use a serif that complements Neutraface and is legible, hopefully making long financial pages less dull.

Suggestions?
Neutraface has those tall, spindly ascenders, which makes it a little hard to match. I'd suggest an antiqua like Koch Antiqua or Eva SG, but those are too overtly calligraphic and achieve much of their effects through low x-height rather than tall ascenders. Also, it's too spindly for use as a text setting.

Filosofia has a similar amount of personality as Neutraface, and is roughly the same age.

David I might suggest something like Vendetta or Tribute, even if others laugh. Neutra is so tough to match you are better off finding another serif that is more old style than not. I'd also suggest, if you are going to use all caps, either use small caps instead with some letterspacing or at least all caps with some letterspacing maybe 2 pts less than you think you should.
