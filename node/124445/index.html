Can ageing fonts and logos look better than the original?

April 8, 2015 • printsome

I always encounter various fonts and logos that have become outdated and haven’t been upgraded and sometimes they even look better! With the amount of designs and fonts out there, companies sometimes forget about their old ones. [[http://www.printsome.com/blog/2015/5-worn-vintage-brand-logos-look-better-original/|This post]] shows some examples of logos being able to capture that vintage look without really trying! What do you think are some other fonts and logos that manage to do that?

Reading the linked article quickly you might get the impression that those logos are still being used, for Coke she says “that same old typography”, but they’ve been updated.

The Coke logo looks similar to the original one but has been updated at least 10 times. The Levi logo is different. The Ford logo retains a similar script but is now within an oval shape.

A vintage look can be effective sometimes, especially if an old-fashioned look is marketable, or if the logo is iconic, but in many cases the logos have been updated in subtle ways.
