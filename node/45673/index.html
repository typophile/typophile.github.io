Elegant and formal with distinctive touches

May 23, 2008 • cumaso

I am looking for an elegant and timeless feeling typeface that has a distinctive feel. It will be set at a fairly small size (8-18 pt) and won’t be used for any large bodies of text. It will probably be a serif, but there might be a sans that could also work.

My first choice now is Requiem, but I am looking for some alternatives that have a more unique look. (For example I really like the cap Y in Requiem.) I am also looking at Mason from Emigre, though it may be a little too quirky.

Any suggestions of something along those lines would be much appreciated. Thanks.

I just discovered Requiem myself, and I have to say that I’m quit impressed with it.

What are your thoughts on Brioso Pro (Opticals and Poster)? I find the face to be lovely, elegant, and when I look at it, I see the Carolingian Renaissance on up through the ages. To me it seems staid enough for business correspondence, but has an elegance suitable for poetry or invitations, or scholarly works.
