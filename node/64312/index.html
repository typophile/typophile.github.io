The issue: The less you charge, the less you are valued

November 16, 2009 • Dan Gayle

Here’s the issue: The less you charge, the less you are valued The more you charge, the more you are listened to.

I remember a designer Bruce Hale telling a story about working for a non-profit for very little money, but getting very little respect or cooperation in return. But when working on a six digit project it’s “Yes sir, Mr. Hale. Would you like coffee with that Mr. Hale? How high again did you ask us to jump?”

I’ve seen this time and again, whether it be web design, a custom font project, book or brochure. Where, financially or otherwise, do you draw the line?

You draw the line where you have the clout to do so. Paul Rand once told us the story of working on the Ford Identity project. He said the Ford PR guys first flew him up to Detroit to see the glitzy airbrush takes on a new logo they had done to show him their “ideas” for a logo. It was a big bash with spotlights and fanfares. Randy said, “I told them to take me back to the airport and call me when they were serious about the identity project.”

Paul Rand could get away with that, you and I would have been dumped at the bus station.
