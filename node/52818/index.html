2008-12-20 02:29:31 • jazzhustler

It’s interesting how all of the characters in the first line lack any of the “special” design elements so abundant in the second line. My first impression was that two different fonts had been used, but upon closer examination it’s clear it’s the same font throughout. Had I been designing this sign I would have been concerned about this – I don’t like things that make viewers pause for no real reason – on the other hand, in this situation the line break couldn’t be better – it would be much worse if there were words containing only non-special characters intermixed on the same lines.

The “o"s look too small (no doubt because they do not overlap the x-height), and of course the letterspacing is atrocious (especially around the “a” in “General”).

Sorry if I’m being too picky for some, but, hey, what better place to be picky than on Typophiles? I couldn’t ask for better company.            ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~          When going from A to Z,          I often end up At Oz.

That’s the luck of the draw. It happens, and in a very real sense it shows the font used could have been better integrated so that no single letter or letters stand out from the rest. The N for example could so easily have been designed in architectural fashion to match the majority of characters on the second line. Same with the A.

What irks me about that font is the density of P, R, W and M compared to the other characters. They stand out like dog’s balls. Font rating: one star.
