How Your Business May Benefit from Custom Software

Custom Software, also known as bespoke or tailor-made software, is developed for a single customer and can accommodate that customer’s expectations, specifications and preferences. It is considered to be very expensive, but that is not always the case. Commercial software that can be purchased off the shelf requires high up front license fees and many of those systems need to be updated every couple of years. Sometimes the advantages of custom software development lifecycle is worth the cost. If your business is unique, the commercial stuff may not suit your needs enough to be effective. There are many things to consider.

When thinking about Software Development for your business, finances need to be considered. Look at the cost, the benefits and if upgrades are going to be needed in the next year or two. Consider the time to market for the custom software over the commercial products. Also look at the size of implementation because commercial software comes with many standardized business processes and reporting. This can mean savings, efficiency and increased productivity to international companies. If that is the case for your company then the commercial software may work well for you.

Should you decide to go with customization, find a Software Company that can suit all your software needs. If your software is developed using stage by stage processes, the beginning phases will involve many departments. You want to be sure the company you hire has the capacity to include marketing, general management, research and development and engineering departments to get the project operating smoothly. There will be hidden dangers and “bug” to be worked out and they will need to be able to deliver the custom software you need for your business. Ask questions and contact some previous clients of you can and get their input before hiring any company. You will be investing a lot of money into your business, so take the time to find out with whom you will be working.

Businesses use custom software to be more competitive, improve response times, streamline processes to increase efficiency, and match existing ways of operation. They also use it to keep specific information about clients, designs or track trends to help them develop new products or new systems of service delivery. It can offer a distinct edge over other businesses in your industry or in your particular location, depending on the nature of your business. You know your finances and the direction you want you business to move toward in the future. Look at all the factors and decide which type of software is best for you.
