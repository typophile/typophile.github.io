The Eszett (»germandoubles«) is replaced by two »S« in uppercase texts. So you can simply make a glyph of two »S«, but you should not leave it blank. About the missing special characters: It's always a good idea to design complete codepages. Take a look at: http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Windows-1252 http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mac_Roman If you use FontLab, it's very easy: Just select the codepage and fill all grey characters with your designs. So you will have support for all the languages mentioned on the Wiki pages above. Ralf
