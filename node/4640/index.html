GERMAN WWI typeface

May 6, 2004 • 2030

I'm doing titles for a film set in World War I. Are there any good typefaces that approximate plane signage from the period? (Blackletter/gothic ones?) Thanks.
I have no idea what German Airplanes looked like during the first world war. However, I can think of a couple of typefaces for you to spec. First off is Fette Fraktur, from Linotype. Fette Fraktur is a "typical" victorian-era fraktur. Such typefaces were still in wide-spread use throughout Germany during the first World War. Second should be akzidenz grotesk. This can be bought through Berthold or Adobe, at least. This is not a blackletter face, but it was used throughout Germany at the time. Lastly, I would consider almost any typeface designed by Rudolf Koch. Most of his typefaces were designed after the first world war, but I would guess that this anacronysm could be forgiven. Koch also fought in the first world war (as did most other famous german designers from that time).

Chris, please dont use Fraktur or Blackletter for titling. And please dont use Kochs types. Koch was a very brave man, made his types for the holy word - so it would work for films like Luther. But if you want extend the clichee of the ugly German - Fette Fraktur & Co. will do this job. ;-) In this time Fraktur was not so popular (especially for scientific things and advertising)- it became more popular in contrast to Bauhaus and Futura later. fonts match the timeline: - De Vinne Latin http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/bitstream/de-vinne/ - Clarendon http://www.identifont.com/show?2SA - Akzidenz Grotesk, right You will find the above types in pre WWI Berthold specimens. Also Ehmcke Antiqua - now marked as Carlton matches the time (published by Flinsch Foundry 1908 and very common till WWI for titlings) http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/carlton/ - some bold oldstyle Egyptienne, Grotesk or Antiqua designs with the typical flavor of this time work too, but I miss the right ones in the digital age. for plane signatures only, catch some historical sources of the german planes. Search for MANFRED VON RICHTHOFEN / RED BARON or FOKKER. BTW: I think the iron cross was used only - no types.
