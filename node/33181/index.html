Tschichold and dictionaries

April 19, 2007 • blank

Did Jan Tschichold ever design a dictionary, or any similar reference book? I’m trying to find an example to compare with his work at Penguin books. I don’t care how obscure such a book might be—it’s worth a day at the Library of Congress.

James, while living in Basel and working for Birkhäuser Verlag, Tschichold did a lot of very ordinary and humble jobs such as the design for phone books and address books – jobs so normal and unobtrusive that hardly anyone would notice that the master had his hands on it. Later on, as you probably know, he worked in house for the chemical company »Ciba Geigy«, where he designed dozens of reference and advertising works. I don’t think that any of these works will be found in the States, but you’ll get an idea by looking at his »Ausgewählte Aufsätze über Fragen der Gestalt des Buches und der Typographie« as well as his »Erfreuliche Drucksachen durch gute Typographie«. I don’t know whether they have already been translated. It somehow a relief to see that even the master did not work on Shakespeare or the likes every day.

Best wishes Florian
