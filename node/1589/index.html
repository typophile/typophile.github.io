2003-10-12 14:28:07 • .00
I think there was a discussion here a while back about what typeface was used for the Microsoft logo. Obviously, they use ITC Franklin Gothic extensively in the corporate identity, but if you look closely at the logo, you will see that it is not Franklin Gothic (ITC or otherwise). All the obvious Franklin Gothic details (including Benguiat's "keyhole") are absent. In fact, it looks as much like Helvetica as Franklin Gothic, but not exactly like either. It seems likely that it started out as one of those two, but was redrawn and modified.
