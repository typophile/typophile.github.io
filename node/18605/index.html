2006-03-16 18:02:28 • timd
Renard is undoubtedly beautiful. As a lawyer, I'm sure I don't have to tell you to check TEFF's EULA. Their fonts are expensive, and their EULA has some unusual restrictions: "The Licensee shall be entitled to install the fonts mentioned in Article 2 of this Agreement and to use these fonts to layout documents and/or for word processing purposes on no more than one computer and one output device belonging to the Licensee." —Many EULAs allow 5 devices. "Without prior written permission from TEFF licensee shall refrain from putting documents, files or data in which the fonts/data mentioned in Article 2 of this Agreement are embedded, such as .pdf files, at third-party's disposal and refrain from making documents, files or data in which the fonts/data mentioned in Article 2 of this Agreement are embedded available to any third-party." —Many EULAs allow PDFs for print and preview. Personally, these conditions would make me think twice.
