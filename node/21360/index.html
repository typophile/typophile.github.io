How sweet is that?...

July 18, 2006 • Miguel Sousa

There’s something pure about good type design which attracts without being needy or loud. This could go some way to explaining Erik Spiekermann’s early involvement with hot metal. “I was given a small printing press (a desktop platen) by a neighbour when I was 12,” he says. Although this kind of gift might not go down well with a typical youngster today, this was the late 1950s and the young Spiekermann was besotted. “I taught myself to set type and print,” he says. It’s not so much the fact of his enjoyment but its purpose which distinguishes this episode, as he explains: “I used it mainly to print envelopes for letters to prospective girlfriends.” in Computer Arts Projects – Issue 86, July 2006 – page 23 http://www.computerarts.co.uk/about_us/latest_issue/computer_arts_projects_issue_86

I couldn’t stop smiling when I read that paragraph :)

This issue also contains a section with type tips and advices from several Typophile regulars.
