2005-01-30 18:56:21 • bowfinpw
Yeah, I agree it fits the same "bones".

I don't know of any close alternatives, but Font Bureau's Eagle or Neville Brody's Typeface 6 and 7 come to mind. There's an old metal face called Vogue which would be similar, too. I don't know any digital versions, except VF Sans which James Montalbano did for Vanity Fair is similar.

Incidentally, Glaser originally called his typeface "Neo Futura" since it was, according to him, based on Futura. I'd say it was a loose interpretation. I suspect they changed the name when it was released to the public. Also, his original design had three weights. In addition to the two we know (light and bold) there was an even lighter weight. This info was culled from the big Milton Glaser Graphic Design book which was published in the late seventies. One last note: There were no "non-stencil" versions of this face mentioned.
