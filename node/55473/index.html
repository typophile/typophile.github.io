[img:sites/default/files/old-images/pic_sagmeister-s1_6413.jpg] So I just recently read Stefan Sagmeister’s book Things I Have Learned in My Life So Far. I really fell in love with the the whole concept of the book. One of my favorite pieces in the book was the piece by Marian Bantjes. I love the subtlety of the work and the curvilinear lines she used. I know that it’s legibility is questionable, but I don’t think that it is necessary in this piece. I love how fragile it seems. With just a single blow or brush of the hand it could be gone. The lack of permanence creates a delicate tension with the viewer.

[img:sites/default/files/old-images/pic_sagmeister-s6_4519.jpg] Here is another version that can be found on her website.It is definitely easier to read, however lacks the delicate aspects of the previous one. However I love this one because I think the type created in the sugar is really interesting and has a retro quality to it.
