Hello again Ted,

I would encourage you to research logos of conservation groups and find ones that you like. Then post links to them so that we can see what you like, and then offer advice on how you can develop your own idea. Be warned, however: if you want help with a logo design, then you will have to learn to be an “ideas person.” We can’t help you if you’re unable to come up with an idea that can be criticized and shaped by others' opinions. Like I said before, by demonstrating a willingness to learn, people may be able to offer advice and direction on how you can gain the skills and perspectives to design your own logo.

I’m getting a sense, however, that when you say, “Help with Logo Design,” you may actually mean that you want other people to come up with an idea for a logo and implement it for you. In this case, you’re effectively asking us to design a logo for you for free. If you want pro bono work for your organization, then just come out and ask for it. Notice, however, that people get paid for design work, and if you are not willing to hire a designer, then you must be willing to accept that there may or may not be people who are willing to do work for you for free.

Once you’ve clarified your intentions and what you’re willing and/or able to contribute to the development of your organization’s logo, then this will help us in how we can respond to your needs and expectations.

Yours, Nick

– Nick Hladek
