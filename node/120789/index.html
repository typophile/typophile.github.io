October 23, 2014 • PointDexter

Hi there, this isnt really a font ID request or at least I don’t mean it to be! Its more of a history question. I am doing a design project for a restaurant in Paris and am intrigued by the S in the original 1920s signage. The rest of the type is very close to Neutraface, with the E inverted! I think the architect may have been a fan of Richard Neutra but am wondering if anyone knows the origin of the style of the S or seen similar examples of it on other signage. I would like to add some editorial about it to the menu and blog.

the architect may have been a fan of Richard Neutra

More likely they both were influenced by the style of their era.
