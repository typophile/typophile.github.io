I am designing and animating the speech Robert Kennedy gave in Indianapolis the night Martin Luther King was assassinated. The speech is somewhat impromptu and very moving.

You can read it and hear it at: http://www.americanrhetoric.com/speeches/robertkennedyonmartinlutherking.html

I would like to hear some opinions on what typeface comes to mind when you hear this speech. I’m looking for a somewhat sad serif that captures this mood. I’d be interested in hearing what everybody has to say. Thanks!

I’ll go with Benguiat (Book) — the serifs are reminiscent of knife tips, rose thorns, danger…

The reason:

“it’s perhaps well to ask what kind of a nation we are and what direction we want to move in. you can be filled with bitterness, and with hatred, and a desire for revenge.

We can move in that direction as a country, in greater polarization – black people amongst blacks, and white amongst whites, filled with hatred toward one another.”

2005-09-22 23:54:25 • hrant

I truly feel that using different treatments would work best on several levels.

For one, changing typefaces and size, shooting static text, panning segments, etc., would do a lot to relieve the tedium of reading straight text, no matter how compelling the words.

Consider how Pablo Ferro mixed several modes of reading text, and how the reader focus is maintained is the opening credits of To Die For (viewable as QuickTime movie).

I know the subject matter and film mood is all together different, but before you watch the clip TURN OFF the soundtrack. That will allow you to see how Pablo works the images on the screen. Also, you will know what mixing methods can mean as you get towards the middle of the clip.

I won’t reveal anymore since the clip will “speak” for itself. It would require more work than just using title text, but who said doing great work was easy.

2005-09-23 03:39:41 • Norbert Florendo

Are you doing only text and the voice? If so, I like Hrant’s and Norbert’s idea of changing typefaces for different parts. I’m afraid that the ‘dagger’ faces, like Hrant’s might be too much, except maybe for a few words like ‘violence’, etc.

You might use a newspaper face with some stuff as headlines and other as text. David Berlow’s new Truth face might be just the thing. You can see his motion intro at the Fontbureau.com site. There are two motion intros to new typefaces, so if you get the other one, just refresh. So you could do the heavy weights for ‘headline’ information, the lighter weights for other parts, and a classic titling face for the poetry and the more poetic parts.

If you are going to used photos also, then probably the type will have to take a back seat, except maybe for the poetic passages which you could do in all type.

This sounds like quite a challenge but it could be really wonderful–great idea. Let us see the result!
