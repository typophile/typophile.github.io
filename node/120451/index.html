Blackletter-esque display type on cookie dough packaging

October 12, 2014 • Birdseeding

Spotted this type on a package of gingerbread dough at the supoermarket today. May well be proprietary – it’s meant to evoke the kind of folksy blackletter-esque lettering that’s found in [[http://www.nordiskamuseet.se/sites/default/files/styles/adaptive/public/public/amnen/mobler-boende/dalmalning_0198927.jpg?itok=aNthJBGR|traditional rural painting in Sweden]] (almost rotunda-like, except on the other extreme of Europe) , and I’m not sure whether that’s a commercially viable style. But on the off-chance it’s available, anyone have any leads?

Very likely hand-lettered.

A little editing (on the P and p), and Linotype Dala comes close: http://www.fonts.com/font/linotype/linotype-dala/text

Edited: This round-script Fraktur lettering looks warm and inviting compared to the choppy forms of standard Fraktur. Interesting options for uppercase letters include the flowing letterforms of some of the letters in OPTI’s Modern Blackletter I suspect that this is one of the relatively few fonts that OPTI independently digitized. OPTI is a defunct operation and its fonts are now orphans. Deiter Steffmann pirated the font and called it Fraktur Moderne. Look closely at the lowercase and you will see that behind the flowing curves of the script are the broken stroke letter-forms of the classic Frakturs such as Wittenberger Fraktur http://www.myfonts.com/search/Wittenberger+Fraktur/fonts/ Neue Luthersche Fraktur http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/ef/neue-luthersche-fraktur/ http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/efscangraphic/neue-luthersche-fraktur-sb/ Walbaum Fraktur http://www.myfonts.com/search/Walbaum+Fraktur/fonts/ Justus Fraktur http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/ef/justus-fraktur/ Don
