RGage. ‘M’ is not bad either. It depends a little on whether you want glyphs that help to identify the typeface, or rather glyphs that convey the typical look of the typeface. For example, characters like ‘Q’, ‘Y’ or ‘&’ often are very suitable for distinguishing one typeface from another, but are not so common in real life text like, say, ‘n’, ‘i’ or ‘t’. You also will want to include both characters with ascenders and descenders. Typically, letters occur within words. That’s why I wouldn’t sort by case, but mix: ‘Mag Ref’ or ‘Red Gag’ (instead of ‘MR aefg’ or ‘GR adeg’).
