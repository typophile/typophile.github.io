1930s New York State Education Department roadside signage

April 13, 2014 • cucamonga

These signs have been produced from the 1930s onwards, or were at least most active in the 30s. Seen throughout the state of New York. There are two faces present. The larger one being a bit more unusual than the smaller face – note the somewhat odd shape of the ‘O’ and the ‘U’ in ‘Hudson’. News Gothic seems like a close but not perfect match for the smaller face – but that’s as close as I’m getting to figuring it out.

For the big one, you may like an emboldened [[http://www.findmyfont.com/index.php/fonts/font-preview?fset=Dafont-1&ffam=Dev%20Gothic%20-%20Regular&fid=fe07d6b10d546b0ee402a6349d11313c&fstyle=b&fsize=60&text=HENRY%20HUDSON&fit=1|Dev Gothic]] (a dafont font by anonymous designer) or [[http://www.findmyfont.com/index.php/fonts/font-preview?fset=Nick-Curtis&ffam=Route%2066%20NF%20-%20Regular&fid=1c64dbcf2a2f3adf80d60ba612063993&fsize=60&text=HENRY%20HUDSON&fit=1|Route 66 NF]] by Nick Curtis

As for the small one, if you exclude /E /1 and probably /R I think a [[http://www.findmyfont.com/index.php/fonts/font-preview?fset=Linotype&ffam=Helvetica%20Narrow%20S%20-%20Bold&fid=1a86bc7147a942f96ea0297484f1f1dc&fsize=36&text=LANDED%20HERE%20SEPT.19%201609%20INDIANS%20WHO%20ENCAMPED%20CALLED%20KINDERHOOK&wrap=2|Helvetica Narrow (Bold)]] would make a good substitute
