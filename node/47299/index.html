Book that explains how every last detail of some project was chosen

July 11, 2008 • Thomas Levine

I’ve read a few books of case studies that show the process of the development of the designs, but I’ve never found one so extremely detailed as I would like. Ideally, I want a stream-of-consciousness account of the designers thoughts through the entire design process. Of course, writing that down isn’t at all practical, so I’m not expecting anything close.

I want something that explains why every little decision was made: Why was the type not three picas to the left? Why the bold weight rather than the medium or heavy? Why are the words capitalized? Why Adobe Garamond rather than Stempel Garamond? How were these exact shades of green and orange chosen?

You’re looking for The History Channel ; not a book.
