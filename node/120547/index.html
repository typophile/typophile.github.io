To make the 4 use L in Handel Gothic Bold. Rotate L 90 degrees, and mirror. Shift up until old lhs side is at level of crossbar for 4. Clip or extend top of new lhs side until it lines up with the other lettes. Paste an I for the vertical. Make thickness adjustments to match your logo. Don

As I’ve said on this board multiple times in the past, logotypes are rarely created by a designer simply picking a typeface and setting the type—they almost always involve customization (sometimes mild, often extreme).

Other fonts with similar letterforms and a numeral 4 closer to your sample: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/fontdiner/dairyland/ http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/paragraph/paragraph/ [only good for the numerals; doesn’t include capitals]

Most logos are created in vector format (the format preferred for printing), which makes them infinitely scalable for multiple uses, from letterheads to billboards and trade show graphics. Converting them to fixed raster sizes for use on the web is relatively simple; not so much the other way around.

But, if you’ve been limping along with a vector file that was auto-traced from a printed original, the fonts listed here and above should help you recreate a cleaner file. Just don’t expect it to be a quick and simple process.
