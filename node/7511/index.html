2004-11-05 12:54:05 • magnus_gaarde
Yves, I don't want to be picky either, but in the interests of strict accuracy that is a slanted Barmeno, not Sari. The one rather clear distinction between those two fonts is that Barmeno has a straight-sided M (like the sample). The M in FF Sari has legs that are splayed. Sari does have an 'italic' according to the FontFont catalog, and Barmeno does not, so that is the other main difference I am aware of.

2004-11-08 06:43:03 • smarks
The main reason I knew about the M is that it is one of the 7 key letters in my Sans Serif Font ID Guide. The two characteristics I note are whether the legs are vertical or splayed, and whether the crotch (vertex) of the M reaches the baseline or not. This gives four possible M variations.

Another difference is that the tail on the R is very slightly curved in Sari, while it is completely straight in Barmeno. The R is another of my key letters, with four variations based on the position and shape of the tail.

So Barmeno has Style Codes of 31/37, while Sari is classified as either 31/38, or 31/62, based on whether you take the slight curve into account or not.

Pardon me for dwelling on this, as I realize I may be the only one who cares about how I classify Sans Serif fonts.
