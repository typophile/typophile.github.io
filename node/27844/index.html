2006-08-23 00:29:40 • Mike F

Mac McGrew’s book ‘American Metal Typefaces of the Twentieth Century’ shows this as Goudy Cursive too. Says it was designed by him for ATF in 1916, as an expansion of Goudy Italic. The sample in the book appears to show that some of the letters in your posted sample (A, K and N) were alternate characters in the typeface.

Goudy Cursive was meant to combine with either Goudy Oldstyle Italic or Goudy 38 Italic. (Goudy 38 is also called Goudy Oldstyle Light) I digitized this as Goudy Oldstyle Cursive. The Lanston version of Goudy Oldstyle and Oldstyle Bold have all the alternates in the Pro versions of the italic fonts.
