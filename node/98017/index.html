French Chic

November 11, 2012 • ellewms

I came across this photo online and have been dying to know what the font is that reads “French Chic”. It looks quite similar to Carisma Light, but I know some characters have either been modified or this is custom lettered… or another font altogether! Please help.
If I clearly remember, this sample comes from the Russian issue of Harper’s Bazaar Magazine. Previously asked [[http://www.typophile.com/node/68068|here]] and [[http://typophile.com/node/91407|here]] but still unsolved. As most of fashion magazines, it could be a bespoke typeface exclusively designed for the magazine. From one of the above page, you could get various suggestions for “[[http://typophile.com/node/67084|ultra thin constrated sans]]” in the same style as your sample. My selection: [[http://e-daylight.jp/design/fonts/type/s/stark-debonair.html|Stark Debonair]] (digitized and freely available as [[http://www.subflux.com/Fonts/FontsRecre_Retro.htm|Retro Super Skinny]]), [[http://e-daylight.jp/design/fonts/revival/penny-bee.html|Penny Bee]] (never digitized), [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/flat-it/cotoris/|Cotoris]] (closest /R I have), [[http://www.fontsquirrel.com/fonts/COM4t-Fine-Regular|Fine]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/reserves/vanitas/|Vanitas]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/andrijtype/seaside/|Seaside]], [[|Sang Bleu]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/t26/minerva/|Minerva]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/k-type/gill-new-antique/|Gill New Antique]] (could be a good one to customize), [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/typeinnovations/contax-sans/|Contax Sans]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/green-type/patriciana/|Patriciana]]
