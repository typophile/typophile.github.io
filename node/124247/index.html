Looks like another old gravestone. Again not a font, but rather the stonecutter’s idea of good lettering. The stonecutter was probably familiar with wooden type grotesque typefaces such as the one revived as Times Gothic Bold https://www.myfonts.com/fonts/woodentypefonts/times-gothic/ The S has flared angled terminals as were found in various circa 1900 fonts such as Inland Edwards http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/nicksfonts/inland-edwards-nf/ and Valjean http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/solotype/valjean/ Agree with Ryuk that the G is similar to Eagle.
