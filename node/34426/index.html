Looking for Professional Opinions

June 13, 2007 • Tkhines

5ive by 5ive Design is a company that I recently created. I plan to cater primarily to military affiliated organizations and universities. Five by Five is military jargon (WW II) meaning “loud and clear”. My goal with the attached wordmark (logo) was to create something that is firm in tradition (balance and symmetry) but comes across as contemporary and modern.

I am looking to get some professional feedback on this wordmark and any input or suggestions that anyone may have. Thank you for submitting any comments.

I feel most people will read, “SIVE BY SIVE.” Plus, this solution is not unique. We’ve all seen it before: 3hree, SE7EN, etc. Using numerals in place of the first letter of the word doesn’t necessarily say anything about your design company. At least nothing which I can discern.

I also see your choice of typeface as somewhat tech-inspired. I don’t quite get “firm in tradition,” especially since the mark is less than perfectly readable. The weight of the face seems a bit weak to be considered firm in tradition.

This is only one opinion and I’m trying only to be helpful so please consider my remarks as food for thought. Somebody else may come along and say you’re spot-on.
