Well. How “classic” are you talking? If you’re looking for something distinctly or historically “London,” then Baskerville or Eric Gill. Although I can’t stand Gill’s lowercase letters, I love the uppercase bold.

What’s the application? The main consideration is to choose a transitional classification or at least a face like Sabon with minimal bracketing.

A few faces within the Garamond or Jenson LINEAGE would really suffice even though products of the Italian Renaissance exported to France. Just watch the bracketing and stress.

Any other thoughts here?
