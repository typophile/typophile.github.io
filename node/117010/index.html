How to know if a font set is legit

June 3, 2014 • BrannigansLaw

I have a fairly large font collection that has grown over the years. Most of the fonts that I have were purchased by me or an agency. However, some have been traded between me and other designers (gasp). I have come to realize that some of these font sets are not legit.

Apparently, hackers are rebuilding fonts from stolen sources such as .TTFs, WOFFs, EOTs and PDFs. In these cases, the kerning is not set properly and many glyphs are often missing. What is the best way to check to see if a font set is legit?

Holding a clear and compliant license trail is a good start. If you are unable to verify that the font data you hold conforms to the license then you can confirm with the foundry or designer in question. If the license you hold is for an agency you own, have owned or previously worked for, then the license is only valid for the agency but again, some foundries or designers may be willing to transfer those rights directly to you if all the paperwork is in order.

Yep, it’s very common, especially among younger designers who are used to trading videos and MP3s. Most designers I’ve known save a copy of every font they have access to. Like you said, it’s just the reality of the situation.

Employers and clients sometimes add to the problem by providing illegal copies of fonts and refusing to reimburse if the designer wants to buy them legally.
