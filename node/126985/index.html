Typefaces in the 1870s weren’t named the way they are now—they were often given a general style designation followed by a size descipriptor (given that they existed as metal or wood type). To recreate the look, try Franklin Gothic Condensed for J.F. Glidden Wire Fences: https://www.myfonts.com/fonts/itc/franklin-gothic/ For the blackletter, that exact style doesn’t appear to have survived into the digital age. Similar: https://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/mariage/ https://www.myfonts.com/fonts/adobe/linotext/ https://www.myfonts.com/search/Courant/fonts/ Still working on the patent number....
