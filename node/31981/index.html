Address block question

March 5, 2007 • Mister M

I’m designing a biz card and just had a question concerning the address block. The client has a PO Box and my question is; Is it correct to set ‘PO’ in small caps and set the word ‘box’ with a capital ‘B’ or should I set the entire word in small caps. Also, when setting an e-mail address on a biz card, is it necessary to type ‘e-mail’ in front of it? Is it something that is obvious or will it be confused with a web address? I personally would like to do without the ‘e-mail’ title for simplicity’s sake.

Welcome any and all input you may have. Thanks in advance.

IM

my feeling: no rules, whatever looks good in context, less is preferable.

the po box question depends on context, styles, weights, overall block layout, any other uses of smallcaps ? does it stand out ? how many pieces of contact info need to appear, etc.

i try to keep some sort of rhythm to the whole thing, with consistent weights, prefixes, labels (or not) so it all holds together.

i prefer to leave off the word “e-mail” as it’s usually self explanatory, as are phone numbers, but on occaision you have a list of several contact options:

office:
cell:
fax: e-mail:

in which case consistency suggests giving them all the same status, but you’ve got to balance simpicity with clarity. if one piece of info stands out as odd from the rest, there’s probably a better way.

2007-03-05 14:49:55 • timd

Use small caps if the letters are meant to be read as letters, not as words. So in this case, small caps PO, u&lc Box works.

As to business cards, simplicity is best. The less text you have, the easier it is to find what you are looking for without wading through it. People look at business cards specifically to find your email address, so they probably can identify it at first sight without a label.
