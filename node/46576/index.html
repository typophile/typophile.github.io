Baskerville Variation? (larger x-height, etc)

June 19, 2008 • bwhitla

Hi all.. i'm working on a logotype, and Baskerville (Monotype cut) really has the perfect letterforms for my situation. The problem is, i want to build something that feels more open, and where the x-height of the letters is taller, and the shapes of the characters generally closer to square, rather than thin rectangle. Same goes for the caps, I wish they were a bit wider - more square in shape. I'm wary of redrawing it on my own and bastardising Baskerville (see attached image for a quick mockup of what i'm thinking). Also, I'd like to use the same typeface for subheads of the logo (think: Entire Catalogue, Recruitment Services Division), and the client will be need to be able to set these on their own.. so a pre-existing typeface would be perfect. I really feel like there must be a face out there that fills this niche, but i'm not sure what it is. thoughts? [img:sites/default/files/old-images/Picture-3_6681.gif]
It has probably already occurred to you, but ITC Baskerville (aka "Linotype New Baskerville") has a larger X-height, and is closer to your illegitimate offspring. You could check that, or maybe the Baskerville BE (Berthold-Adobe). There are a number of B'villes from Storm, too, but I've never used them.

There is something else that might fit the bill: Henderson Serif, a Baskerville interpretation by Jean François Porchez. It's a proprietary typeface, though, and never will be made available to the general public. So this addendum to the thread won't help Ben at all (in addition to being a few weeks too late), but I'll still think the typeface is worth a mention.
