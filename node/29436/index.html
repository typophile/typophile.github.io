its amazing how the Eygiptian inscriptions have the effect like its embossed on the stone and the Roman inscriptions are debbossed into the stone. why is this? is it because of the method of carving that may have been done or am i just asking something that is out of this world and has np answer? :)
Both the Egyptians and the romans often carved into stone, but Roman letters (at least on the Trajan Column) are cut with a v-cut. I think that most ancient Egptian hieroglyphs were more scooped out than carved… the cut would look more rectangular tha v-shapped.
