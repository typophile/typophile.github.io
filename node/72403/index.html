The apps that are “crudely adapted from the iPhone” are actually just iPhone apps, which the iPad is able to run. Look for apps that are designed for the iPad. You can view these separately in the iTunes store by clicking the “iPad” tab at the top of the Apps page.

Some apps are “universal” apps that look different depending on whether you run them on the iPhone or iPad (taking advantage of the extra room on the iPad).

With other apps, there are separate iPhone and iPad versions.

If, when you launch the app on an iPad, it’s displayed at iPhone size and has that little “1x” button in the corner, then it’s not designed specifically for the iPad, i.e., it’s an iPhone app.
