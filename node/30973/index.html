An interesting side light on Gotham came up in the TDC ‘lettering tour’ of lower Manhattan the past fall.

Paul Shaw, who led the tour for the TDC, mentioned that there was reason why the similar wide sans which inspired Frere-Jones were all over Manhattan: there were in fact books of alphabets for architects, which were also used by sign painters. In other words, there was a very influential source for alphabets that was not type. These were the starting point for the inscriptions and signage. Frere-Jones saw these varied examples, and creatively developed his own version in digital type.

Part of Gotham’s success–like that of Interstate, no doubt–is that its forms were already familiar to Americans because of the old, common, and influential source. And, of course, Frere-Jones beautifully executed the ‘revival’.
