2007-04-29 22:06:03 • pattyfab

In the early 70s I worked at Charrette, the big art supply store in Cambridge and Boston. In the late 60s and early 70s they still serviced mostly architects. At that time we were the biggest dealer of Letraset in the world.

The reason I bring this up is that Clarendon was a huge favorite with architects and so was Helvetica. Both were early mainstays in Letraset’s line. So in the same way that the Laserwriter font set made Palatino far more prominent than it might have been otherwise, I think Letraset had a lot to do with the heavy usage of Clarendon among architects, at least back then.

(Now that press-on lettering is a fossil from the past, I doubt they use Clarendon much anymore – they’ve undoubtedly migrated to Comic Sans.)
