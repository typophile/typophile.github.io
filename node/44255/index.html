Question regarding Movie Credits

April 15, 2008 • Sara Jeanne

I'm hoping some of you might have some movie credit advice you'd be willing to impart. Essentially, I'd like to know what a typical point size for crawling credits might be, and how it might transfer to the big screen. I've seen some nasty typographic accidents in local film and I'd like to avoid making these mistakes. A colleague I'm working with is concerned that if I make the text too small, viewers on the web/youtube may not be able to read the credits and general motion type - however, I'm worried the text will look clumsy or horsey when viewed on a larger screen. I know this seems to be a case of poor planning - I know we should have taken into account the multiple contexts but this doesn't really work with our time and budget constraints. I tried to explain to the director that when we scale things up in print we have to readjust the type and imagery in regards to the new format, etc., but I don't think I'm getting my point across and I need to find a happy medium here. Help for a first-timer?
The minimum legible point size of a font is directly proportional to the reading distance. I can tell you the exact numbers when I get home and check a book. I don't know typical movie font sizes.

A bit late... This recommendation is probably based more on things like signs than movie credits, but it should give you an idea. The text should be the sum of 1/600 of the viewing distance and 7.2pt (good vision and lighting) to 21.6pt (bad eyesight or lighting). If that doesn't look right, tell me, and I'll check in more depth; I slightly simplified what I read.
