Ogonek as a block: acceptable or awful

August 21, 2009 • blank

A while ago someone posted some photos of signs and maholes in Poland. The ogoneks were sometimes drawn as a horizontal rectangle or a small square positioned where the hook would normally be. I suspect that this was either the product or foreigners or was done to save vertical space. Would such a design ever acceptable with sans types, or is it just wrong?
Usually it is wrong. It is acceptable in machine-like typefaces or linking to 20-30’s avant-garde tradition. Look at FF DIN. Because until nowadays many bad fonts used in Poland don’t have polish diactrics, people add them “by hand”, with various effects - using bars, commas, triangles… ;)

In the bottom line you can see Aogonek and Eogonek which are acceptable in the context, although they look weird and too small*. (The top line contains also interesting, flipped Nacute).

*) Making accents too tiny can cause some grammar/language problems. For example: in spoken polish, spelling the final ę as an e, sounds quite loutish. So a text containing almost invisible eogonek would often create a real ornery feeling.
