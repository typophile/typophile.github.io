New Maya Language

July 19, 2008 • fridalarios

Here are some spreads of my new 'New Maya Language' book. This book compiles my previous works in a simple easy to understand format. The New Maya Language is a redesign and recomposition of certain original Maya script ideographs that communicate concepts and even sentences. It parallels the principle of the Chinese-concept script where primary root or Lego-like pictographs can be combined to generate compound pictographs that signify a more complex idea (Chizlet, C.). The ‘Maya Language’ section of the book introduces a basic understanding of how the Maya writing system works. The second section tells the story 'Joya de Cerén' UNESCO World Heritage Archaeological Site in El Salvador in Central America through 23 New Maya Language glyphs. Spread by spread the reader is introduced to each one of them and how they were formulated. For example, ‘Stone’ + ‘Fire’ combined equal the ‘Lavastone’ New Maya Language ideograph. Finally, the ‘Applications’ section shows real life examples of how the New Maya Language can be taken forward to other forms of visual communication, such as brand identities.
