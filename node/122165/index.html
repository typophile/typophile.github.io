This is not a font - it would be unique to the type of machine that stamped these numbers. Hoefler has a series of [[http://www.typography.com/fonts/numbers/styles/|Numbers]] fonts based on similar types of numbers, but none are an exact match. - Lex

Sometimes called "crash numbers" the numbers are used in machines that rotate a digit every time the device is used to stamp a number. Note the monospace treatment where all numbers more or less fill the width, even the one. If you are interested in similar letter-forms from a font, consider Prata designed by Ivan Petrov for Cyreal [img:sites/default/files/old-images/PrataNumbers_6483.jpg] which is available as a Google font http://www.google.com/fonts/specimen/Prata Don
