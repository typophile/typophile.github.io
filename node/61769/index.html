I think you’ve got a great start and the leaf and green certainly communicates ‘nature’ and ‘growth’. The ultimate challenge will be in the quick viewer thinking this is an environmental or agriculture company. Blue is popular in banking because of the psychology of color and it being tied with ‘trust’ and ‘integrity’.

Super Technical: In the top logo the stem is on the left which makes the leaf appear to go down and to the right. Not sure you want to visually imply that for a financial company. ;)

Also the reversed logotype and tagline in that option is way too small.

One tip if you haven’t done this… turn off the computer and grab a pencil and paper. Sketch out a bunch of different directions this can go and then eliminate the weakest concepts until you’re left with 2-3 solid solutions.

Beautiful work so far!

If you are going to use a leaf (which i like, it breaks the mold of finance i think) direction is definitely important. Like the person before me says, the line of the leaf stem seems to be going downward.

Id flip the leaf horizontally, so the stem is gradually going upwards. This could represent even more growth, or moving forward.

Also, I agree that using a green leaf may initially cause viewers to expect an environmental company. But again, I like the idea of a leaf. There could be a few possible fixes for this, such as changing the color of the leaf. Making it a non traditional color could possibly disconnect it from the environmental image while still maintaining the strong leaf symbol. If that makes any sense. I hope it does.

As for the wordmark, I love the way it ties into everything. I think the text works well. Not completely organic, but it does seem somewhat friendly and free-flowing, while still maintaining the structure you might want as a financial group.
