When I think about New Orleans in a visual sense, the first word that comes to my mind is charming. New Orleans is filled with buildings, gardens, statues, murals, and signs that are both creative and visually pleasing. This blog gives a few examples of the various types of signage found in the city and shows how they vary in design but are still effective as identification markers.

Out of the four pictures, I think that the sign marking the Carousel Gardens in City Park is probably the most native New Orleans in design. The wrought iron decor is definitely a staple of New Orleans and is found all over the city. Both the Botanical Gardens and the New Orleans Museum of Art signs are fairly plain but extremely substantial. They are easily readable and probably can withstand New Orleans weather. The Boh Bros sign is creative in that in resembles a setting or rising sun, depending on how you interpret it. The combination of the thick and thin sans serif lettering is very eye-catching and shows the hierarchy of the text effectively. Although all of these signs differ in looks, they all are appropriately designed for the function they are meant to fill.
