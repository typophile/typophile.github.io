as Beejay sais, it´s about finding the right balance between neutrality against the content/the brands, and a personal expression for the brand itself, BrandMinute.

When you are talking about fonts like Vista sans, frutiger or Gotham etc. I assume that it does not need to be a browser safe font. I would either go with a classic sans, with a familiar, quite neutral expression, but with a bit of personal expression. Something from the Franklin gothic family is one suggestion. The other way I think is to find a Display serif font that has the characteristics of Georgia, but with a more detailed outline for text in bigger sizes in headlines etc.

I don’t have much to add to the suggestions so far. What’s missing for me in this conversation is some more context. I’d like to hear more about the primary criteria for the wordmark – “better presenting the mission of the web site”.

What then is the mission of the web site? Or perhaps more to the point when considering typefaces for a simple wordmark: what are you trying to convey about your own BrandMinute brand? – Give us some adjectives, some characteristics that you’re trying to convey.

What is it about the faces you’ve mentioned that appeals to you, speaks to you, or represents some aspect of what you’re trying to communicate? That way, we have some guidelines for suggesting alternatives.

“Integrates well with search engine-friendly Georgia” is a pretty broad target and, really, only a secondary criteria.
