Which font is this?
Don’t recognize it off hand. Also can’t say if this is modern logo making or from an old printed text. Depending on the date of your sample it could be a digital or photolettering font. Looks sort of like a simplified “Old English” – with a Roman upper case with a textura lower case. Old English dates back to about 1760 when it was called Caslon Black. The closest I have seen to your image is an alphabet for house letters http://www.accurateimageinc.com/Plastics/F_OldEnglishSet.aspx also http://www.accurateimageinc.com/Cut%20Metals/CutOldEnglish.aspx Not available as a digital font. The closest digital I found is New Old English http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/k-type/new-old-english/ If you are lucky another font detective may be able to find a match, or at least something closer.
