There’s probably more than one ways to go about this, but generally it doesn’t look very nice.

• I used a line height (leading) that would work with the largest point size. • Then I typed in everything in a smaller point sized without changing the line height, changed the size of the words decided and used baseline shift to center them on the line. • I picked a typeface with old style numerals to avoid them sticking out. • % is to tall, but I’ll leave that to you to figure out. • I used the bold weight with small text to match the words weight better.

Your design premise is flawed.

If you want to do mix and match, you should stick to the same type size, and vary type style, or completely “eyeball” much shorter sentences–where realistically the best technique is to put each word (or small group of two or three words) in a separate box and drag and size.
