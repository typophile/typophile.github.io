This is one of the brush scripts from old film types. The digitization of it I have is called 'Klakson', and the only difference I see is the tails on your sample's letters are slightly longer. We get these posted now and then, and they are all quite similar. You might be able to find this, as it comes from an old Expresiv Font collection no longer sold, and such fonts have a way of getting out on the web. - Mike Yanega

2007-01-19 06:09:54 • Mike F
The Solotype Catalog (pp. 104-5) shows Hunter Script and two other weights of the same basic script called Jester and Hindu. The A is characteristic in all three. The Expresiv Brush Font collection says the font names and files were copyright 1994 by MacArtist, Inc., but obviously they were digitizations of existing film types. - Mike Yanega
