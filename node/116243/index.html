The original typeface is an Art Nouveau one shown in the Petzendorfer book from Dover Books. The typeface name was “Pretorian” from the London firm of P. M. Shanks & Sons.

Intellecta Design has a font based on this called [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/intellecta/pretoria-gross/?refby=bowfin|Pretoria Gross]], which includes various ornamental treatments using the letters, as well as the plain black and outline versions of the typeface.

Another is called [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/dtptypes/pretorian-dt/?refby=bowfin|Pretorian DT]] by DTP Types. It has 4 versions.

FontBank had a version called “Vivian”, and Serials (or whatever they are called now) had a family called “Pretoria”.
