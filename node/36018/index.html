It’s a pretty centered, safe, immobile design. You want to think especially about two things: the center of attention (where a reader’s eye will go as soon as he/she sees the ad) and the hierarchy (What information is most important? The website, the company name, or the company’s description? What’s slightly less? etc.). The most attractive element seems to be the logo, so try to make good use of it. Off the top of my head, you could try making it big, nearly the size of the page or even bigger, bleeding it off the page. That might make your ad more memorable. But you need to also think about legibility, since this is such a small ad, so make sure that everything is readable at actual size.

You should also consider integrating the text and image. Right now, they’re pretty separate, caused in part by the big separation between elements and the big black bars at the top and bottom. See if you can make everything feel like it’s part of a whole – again, my quick thought would be to tilt the website name to the angle of one side of the mountain and to run the company description below it for the same width as the logo.

Really, what I’m saying is that you may want to take some time to play around with what you have. If you don’t need to put it in this very second, try sketching every layout and idea that comes to mind. You’ll likely come up with something that, if not brilliant, will be a marked improvement. But I’d recommend you keep Palatino Sans – it fits the company’s image nicely. Maybe really flaunt it – have fun with the font. :-) And if you have time, post the final result – I’d love to see it.

P.S. I don’t want to presume that you haven’t read some of these, but you might like to take a look at the books listed in the Typophile Library. I haven’t read most of them, but I can attest to the great insights and design in Paula Scher’s Make It Bigger.

Okay, here goes.

A website address just can’t be the most important thing on this ad, but you have placed it at the top left, where your key selling proposition should be. What you need up there is something that will catch the eye of the reader, and make them continue to read. I suggest “Custom Greeting Cards” or something similar.

The logo is a nice one, but again, will it make people buy? a photo of the product is more likely to do so. Then your sales pitch (which will have to be modified if you take the heading out from it). Finally your contact information. (The www stuff can go down here.)

Perhaps it will work the way it is, but if it does it will be the exception.
