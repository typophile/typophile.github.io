There is some evidence that suggests that there are higher rates of dyslexia in countries that have poor spelling to sound correspondences, than in countries with a strong relationship between spelling and sounds. If this is true, than switching to a phonetic alphabet would reduce the occurrence of dyslexia. Of course cultures are reluctant to change their orthographies, so a phonetic teaching alphabet is a transition alphabet to help a child learn to read with regular spelling to sound correspondences before transitioning to the less regular orthography.
