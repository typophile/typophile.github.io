2010-10-26 03:20:39 • Chris Keegan
I would think you’ll need to do some weight adjustments, e.g. since /M/’s strokes are all at or closer to vertical (the direction that doesn’t get thinned with the squooshing) it winds up too heavy to my eye.
2010-10-26 04:01:36 • eliason
Squishing it wrecks the horizontal/vertical weight relationships in most letters and screws up the counter balance in F, R, and A. So…just don’t do this.
2010-10-26 04:05:52 • blank
Well, I don’t know the legality of this, but you could try interpolating it with a bolder weight after squishing it, to get the horizontals right, and then tweak all the issues.
2010-10-26 05:03:02 • brianskywalker
It needs some fixing but I think it will look good. Couldn’t you cut some of the horizontals from the original version to repair the squashed ones? You wouldn’t lose the horizontal/vertical relationship if you reduce the height of each letter individually using groups of points, rather than squashing the complete word.
