Looking for novel text recommendations

July 8, 2010 • JButts

I’m currently slated to tackle my first book design and am looking for recommendations for interior typefaces. It is a fiction novel focused on teenagers playing baseball that has a strong medieval undertone.

I’ve got a couple in mind and pulled others from internet searches but was curious what insights the Typophile community might have. For chapter headers I’m looking towards something with a nice set of alternate glyphs – Adobe Caslon Pro being a good starting point – so something that might complement that type of character set well.

What do you guys think?

Instead of Adobe Caslon you could use as well Williams Caslon, which is a very well done reinterpretation of Caslon. It is also the Featured Face right now. [[http://www.fontbureau.com/fonts/WilliamsCaslonText/characters/|Williams Caslon]]. For headers you could use [[http://www.fontbureau.com/fonts/BigCaslon/|Big Caslon]] then or Sumner Stone [[http://www.stonetypefoundry.com/arepooverview.html|Arepo]].

Anyhow depends on how far the medievalness of your book is, have a look at Andron font, which is a fantastic font for everything where you need historical characters/signs/glyphs. [[http://www.signographie.de/cms/front_content.php?idart=69&changelang=2|Andron]]. Or the free font focused on medieval, but it only has a roman face, no italics, no bold: [[http://scholarsfonts.net/cardofnt.html|Cardo]].
