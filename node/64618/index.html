I’m working with an architecture firm on a restoration project of an old B&O railroad shed. There is a series of numbers painted on brick to indicate the different tracks. As part of the restoration process, we want to repaint the numbers in a typeface as close to the original as possible. I’m aware that the typeface, being from the 1800’s, might not be digitized, but I’m sure there is something that comes very close.

I’ve attached photos of the numbers. Let me know if you would like a hi-res version of any of them.

Paint over the photo numbers with black in PhotoShop - select the outline of number form with the magic wand tool - make selection into a path - make points and move to Illustrator - make full size template, stencil, etc. You now have a guide to restore rather than to replace the numbers. Or you could make a font from the outline points in Fontographer, FontLab etc.
