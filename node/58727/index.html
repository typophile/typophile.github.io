I just can't get past the fight that is being fought between the 'T' (the stem) and the rest of the tree (leaves. Why not one color?). They both are fighting for attention, they both don't get what the deserve. Besides that the stem is very wide and bold, the hands - however - are tiny. I don't like this. The hands are lost. 2009-06-05 14:32:57 • bobbybobo
The colors are harmonious an a have a soft natural feel. The relationships of the images and text leave me confused. The work is "Take," but the hand is in an asking position, as in, "Please give me something," as opposed to, "Please take something (a burden, I'm guessing) from me." The tree and "taking" makes me think of the giving tree which again creates a conflict. As a symbol, the tree is a source not a receiver. With the hands on it, it seems like the tree needs something. A radial arrangement of the hands suggests a group of people coming together. But I'm not sure why there are three groups of people, or why these groups are on the tree.
