2004-02-08 15:11:20 • andreas
> Does a studio have to pay for type face usage like for art (paintings) in movies?

Normally, not. Paintings are art, while typefaces are commercial products. Usually, font licenses do not specify additional royalties for a particular way of use. Also, it is unlikely that licensing models involving royalties would work at all.

In case of successful movies, type designers usually profit because movie posters (or popular book covers, for that matter) often make a typeface popular, so it becomes commercially successful. So movie titles are sort of free advertising for a typeface.
