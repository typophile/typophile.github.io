InDesign: Reining in optical margin adjustment

July 5, 2010 • nina

In a justified setting, when there is a dash at the beginning or end of a line, InDesign optical margin adjustment is out of control. It puts like half the dash into the margin. Now with a dash at the beginning of a line, I usually insert a soft line break before it and then a hair space, which the dash can then be kerned against to pull it in (out of the margin). But, any tricks on what to do with a dash at the end of the line? I can’t seem to find a good way to pull those in. Any white space I add after them is not in fact added to the line in terms of pushing the dash in. Im still on CS4 in case it matters. Thanks!

2010-07-05 20:58:15 • blank

I actually like the affect of the optical margin alignment, as it loosens the text a little, and gives more flexibility.

And you can control it to some extent. When you go to to type>story it gives you an option of setting the type size, which will adjust how far the hyphen sticks into the margin. There is also on the fly-out from the paragraph menu an option to ignore optical margin, so you can turn it off as you like.
