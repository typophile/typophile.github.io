I'm sorry to hear that Apple employs such poor technicians at their 'Genius Bar'. There are at least two solutions for the problem that allow users to salvage their data -- and that without taking the screwdriver in your hand. One is to attach an external Firewire drive to the Mac, boot it from the system DVD, install Mac OS X on the external Firewire drive, boot from it and the copy all the data from the internal hard drive to the external one. The other one is to hook up the Mac to another Mac using a Firewire cable and boot your Mac in the target mode (pressing the T key while booting). This will allow the other Mac to recognize your Mac's internal hard drive as an external drive, so you'll be able to copy all your data from your Mac to the other one. These solutions are known since Classic times, and I'm quite familiar with them, being a recent Mac switcher myself (the main reason I switched to Mac OS X was Windows Vista). If the 'Mac geniuses' at the Apple store did not suggest any of those simple solutions, well, shame on them. Adam
