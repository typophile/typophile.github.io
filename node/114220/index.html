Silent film font - very similar to Silica Regular and Sancoale Slab Ext, with different serifs

February 16, 2014 • spitespecific

This is a font from a silent film, Why Change Your Wife (1920) by Cecil B. DeMille. It was used as the intertitle font

The inter-titles were hand lettered. I don’t think there is an exact font match for this one. Somewhat different, but a modern digital font that captures some of the flavor of inter-titles of the era, Meyer Two http://www.fontbureau.com/fonts/MeyerTwo/ Also LHF Silent Movie http://www.letterheadfonts.com/fonts/silentmovie.php ITC Photoplay, by Nick Curtis, http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/itc/photoplay-itc/
