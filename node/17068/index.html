I don't love Eidetic. But I try to put my personal tastes aside when it comes to judging if a font is texty or not. Tribute: I agree, it's a text face. Note that I put a question mark after "still only" - basically because I haven't been following Emigre recently. I remember when Eidetic came out I thought it was their first real text face - so I stand by that. Tribute's "g" was suspect to me when I first saw it, but then I saw the issue of the magazine where it was used, and realized it's fine. So I'm sorry, I forgot to remember Tribute. Vendetta: Out of context I agree that it can be a text face. But it has a "fatal" problem in that respect: its central feature -the facetness- can only really click with optical masters. Lacking optical masters, that feature basically kills it as a well-rounded text font: if you set it at more than one size, it becomes more than one face. Excoffon knew this, which is why -even with the availability of the pantograph- he decided to make Mistral's roughness inversely proportional to size. The others: no. hhp
