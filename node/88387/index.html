Yes, I recognize that as a four-character idiom*. I'm a little rusty, but I'll give it a shot. Literal: The warm spring weather brings forth flower blossoms. Also used to convey something like... "A good opportunity presents itself", often used in the context of work. *Four-character idioms, or chéngyǔ (Traditional Chinese: 成語; Simplified Chinese: 成语, literally "to become (part of) the language") are widely used in Classical Chinese, a literary form used in the Chinese written language from antiquity until 1919, and are still commonly used in Vernacular writing today.
