Advice needed.

June 2, 2010 • Claire Bibio

Dear all, I’m a third year design student and Will have an interview with a design agency in 3 days. It’s my first time and I would appreciate if more experienced ones could give their advice on thing that I should have on my mind and do on the day of interview. For example the dress code, how many works should I present, should I do a screen presentation, put thing on a cd for them and etc.

Thank you!

The last interview I went to, I was so nervous that I downed a whole bottle of Rescue Remedy before going in. Just be polite, smile, have questions ready about the company - express interest in what they do, with examples. Whatever way you show your work, make sure it’s clear and works properly (have back-up if need be). Don’t show anything you’re not proud of, and can’t defend under expert scrutiny. Be prepared for tricky questions such as: how long do you think you’d need to be working here till you’d be making a contribution to the company?, how much do you think you deserve to be paid?, how will you adapt to very tight deadlines for sometimes dull projects, after the relatively easy creative freedom of college?

Just dress like a smart version of your everyday self (not for a wedding but not for screenprinting). Wear something you really like and feel comfortable in. Good luck :)

Another important point: if you don’t know something, admit it. Ignorance is curable, but lying about your ignorance will not score points, and could sink your prospects. The best response to “do you know how to [such-and-such]” is: “No, I don’t, but I’m looking forward to learning about it.” This would also include the above-mentioned question about “how much you deserve to be paid.” A good answer: “I don’t know: you’re the experts, and I’m new to all of this. But I’m sure that, if you offer me this job, the pay will be fair.”
