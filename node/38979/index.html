2007-11-13 02:09:32 • NewGuy

Hello folks.

I wish I could have stuck my nose into this thread a bit sooner. I was just waiting for a bit of the dust from last week’s news to settle.

Yes, everything is indeed good.

As an addendum to the clarifications by Nick, Stephen and Ricardo (thank you all), I would like to reinforce the fact that Veer does have a large in-house stock photography production and art department. We are currently building out four brands of photography: Fancy [1], Solus [2], Dear [3], and the Latin-American focused Somos [4].

[1] http://www.veer.com/fancy/ [2] http://www.veer.com/solus/ [3] http://www.veer.com/dear/ [4] http://www.veer.com/somos/

As for typeface development, I am but the sole type designer (of sorts) and technician on staff. However, we work very closely with Jason Walcott on adding new faces to our Jukebox [5] collection. Likewise, Alejandro Paul, Stephen Rapp, Christian Robertson and others are all significant contributors to the exclusive typefaces in our Umbrella [6] library. This year, we acquired the Mean Tangerine [7] foundry for its solid lineup of pixel fonts, and are working with Tyler Young, continuing to build out this great collection. Finally, we just soft-launched Cabinet [8], an embryonic new collection of text and titling faces - Stefan Hattenbach of Macrhino is producing exclusive families for us in this context.

[5] http://www.veer.com/jukebox/ [6] http://www.veer.com/umbrella/ [7] http://www.veer.com/meantangerine/ [8] http://www.veer.com/cabinet/

See, we’ve been busy.
