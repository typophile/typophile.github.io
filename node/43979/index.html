I'm conducting an independent study this quarter on "process" in type, i.e. procedural approaches to the creation/manipulation of typefaces, letter forms, typographic imagery etc ... I'm trying to compile a list of people who've touched upon this. Here's who I have so far. Does this ring any other bells for anyone? 2008-04-08 20:41:28 • russellm
Thanks for including me in your research and for all the images/links! Here's some others that I've run across (thanks to Lindsay Ballant for some new ones) for your use: Letterror is sort of the granddaddy of programmatic type (though I'm sure someone's going to object to that). Specifically, [[http://www.letterror.com/foundry/beowolf/index.html|Beowolf]] has randomized rough edges. And the letterforms for [[http://www.letterror.com/portfolio/twin/index.html|'Twin']] are affected by the weather in the Twin Cities. Another older fella ;) is [[http://www.pcho.net|Peter Cho]], with [[http://www.typotopo.com/|Typo Topo]] You should definitely check out [[http://www.scriptographer.com/Gallery/|Scriptographer's Gallery]], featuring the work of [[http://www.jonathanpuckey.com/|Jonathan Puckey]] & [[http://www.scratchdisk.com/|Jürg Lehni]]. Eye magazine did a feature about it a while back. David Reinfurt created an interesting feature for the design of Yale's architectural 'zine. He asked various designers to take a customized version of Hevetica Neue and make a new typeface from it using some form of alteration. Two examples of computational results were created by [[http://www.projectprojects.com/#H_HELV_NEUE|Project Projects]] & [[http://www.syntheticautomatic.com/activity.html#helvetica|Apirat Infahsaeng]]. Former classmate Joshua Walton's [[http://babel.conflux.org/|Babel type]] generator. [[http://joshua.conflux.org/portfolio/workImages/babel2.jpg|Example 1]] [[http://joshua.conflux.org/portfolio/workImages/babel3.jpg|Example 2]] [[http://www.ortho-type.it|3d type created in Processing]]. More processing by [[http://www.caligraft.com|Ricard Marxer]]. Still not so sure about Hudson-Powell's [[http://www.responsivetype.com/|Responsive Type]]. Robotic type in Nikita Pashenkov's [[http://acg.media.mit.edu/people/nik/projects_n.cgi?alphabot&screenshots|Alphabot]] agency:collective's [[http://www.agencycollective.com/projects.php?view=type&id=container|Container Steganography]]. While not typeface work, these German Students have using [[http://www.looksgood.de/log/2007/07/27/diploma-posters-indesign-scripting/|scripting in InDesign]] to do some new methods of layout. That's what I've got at my fingertips, hope it helps! -- JK
