Type ID: exhibit signage @ Art Institute of Chicago

June 2, 2008 • awelchel

This beautiful typeface was used in the signage & accompanying brochure for the exhibition [[http://www.artic.edu/aic/exhibitions/exhibition/figuration|“Figuration in Contemporary Design”]] at the Art Institute of Chicago. Photography was sternly discouraged, but an understanding security guard allowed me a couple furtive shots of the typography. Any help identifying this lovely font? Thanks very much!

2008-06-03 13:09:03 • Florian Hardwig

This is a new one to me. The ’s' is very odd, with two kinds of serifs. Swash Romans are pretty rare too, and none that I know of match this.

The closest I can think of is Thierry Puyfoulhoux’s large and varied [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/presencetypo/classica/|Classica]] family which includes a Gallic version with the Venetian style e, and the Prestige subfamily that has all kinds of swashes and ligatures. There is a swash W like the one shown, only it is reversed.

This might be another time when writing to the Museum might get an answer.
