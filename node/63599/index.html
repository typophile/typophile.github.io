Is it wrong to design just one cut?

October 28, 2009 • Dan Gayle

Every time I pick up Fontlab and work on a type, I realize that even if I finish this one and it’s awesome, I still have to create the italic, bold, and bold italic also, and I get depressed.

For things like scripts or upright italics, one variant is fine, but what about other typefaces? Is it wrong to not want to do them? In the end am I doing my type a disservice because less people will consider the single cut as being “complete”?

I guess it depends on what it’s intended for. If it could possibly be used as a text face, then I think you’d want at least italic, and probably bold and bold italic. If it veers more towards a display face, I think you’re fine (although additional weights are always good, and thankfully, they’re a heck of a lot easier to do than italic).

Dan, In the past 5 years, I have started and am still working on 17 different type families. A few have just one weight and some have 10, some have italics and some not. I finally gave up and just released one stinking font just to relieve the pressure of the whole damn mess. My advice is just put one out and add to it later. The longer you wait, the harder it gets to let go. I have nearly finished seven variations of that font now and three versions of another. I hope I can persevere and get them out on the market soon. Life is not easy and I should know better after all these years.
