Checked out Mac programs. Played games. Lent it to a friend (and never got it back) after next purchase. 1989/1990: (ish) Second hand Acorn Archimedes A3000. Double disk drive, but still no hard drive. Learned ARM assembler. Incredibly powerful machine -- learned to program for PCs through its emulator. Still have it, but no monitor to hook it up to ... 1991: Self-built 486 PC. Learned 8086 assembler and C. Bought a virtual reality helmet and learned to write programs for it. A new dimension to graphics! Traded in for next purchase. ..2009: Various PCs, all Frankenstein-wise containing salvaged parts from previous PCs. Ransacked items travelled down the family to dad, brothers, sisters, until (presumably) old beyond measure and scrapped. Learned C++ and wrote all kinds of programs for Windows, with various success. 2009: Dawn of a new age: while writing, I'm still oogling this shiny new 21.5" iMac. Learning XCode as we speak. Price-wise I don't appear to have learned something; always shelling out near-top prices for machines that are advertised to be very nearly "top of the bill" for no more than 6 months. since nobody else has said "osborne" yet, let me be the first. i loved that machine... worked on lots of p.c. machinery after that. and for the last decade or more, macs only. (a handful of them, and 2 iphones as well.) my machines are always tight, and run well. i don't tolerate any bloat, or random crashes. i've also found that memory, hard-drive size, and monitor size are crucially important, even to the point that the machine-type is dwarfed. while doing statistical work in graduate school for a professor who was consulting for xerox, i came across the alto/bravo machines, with a portrait monitor, and became fixated on it. all i wanted was the ability to see a full page of a document at a reasonable size, which is certainly not an unreasonable request, is it? i worked a shit job once, for far too long, just because it let me work on a portrait monitor... year after year after year, i waited, patiently, but it seemed like the monitor manufacturers were bound and determined to stall things out with monitors that gave not-quite-a-full-page. finally, about 5 years back, i got a 23-inch cinema monitor, where i can now see _two_ pages of a document at a reasonable text-size. it's really nice to finally get something that you have wanted for two decades, let me tell you... add to that a terabyte drive sitting on my desk, and things are pretty good for me right now... i could use some more memory in this macbook, especially since today's browsers are bloatware (and unavoidable, since the web is now required), but other than that, i'm not complaining too much. looking back, there's only one machine that i wish i would have bought -- one of the amigas that were video-capable way back in the old-days, years before other machines caught up to 'em (if they actually have). being skilled in video capture and manipulation just might have pointed me in a slightly different direction. -bowerbird
