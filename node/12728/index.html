2005-05-28 06:25:55 • hrant
I switched from analog photography to digital almost all the way. My old EOS and my Mamiya and Rollei are gathering dust these days, not too much of a shame about the Eos, but the other too.. oh well. I work with a 20D at the moment and have so far almost everytime upgraded whenever the next semi-pro-EOS digital cam came out, as there always were significant improvements. I'd love to have the 1D, but it is just to expensive to upgrade every two years, I feel... and as I'm not a pro-phtographer, I just don't need it all that much. And hey, most of all, of course, I'd love to have a H1D by Hasselblad :-D. My workflow is that I shoot either directly with the Eos (using the Canon L-lenses) or tethered with BreezeSys' DSLR Remote Pro, which makes studio photography much more easy. I don't use photoshop to convert RAW (or even the awful original Canon Tools) but the superb CaptureOne, the only real tool for doing a bit of darkroom work on the files, and fast as lightning. The final editing of the 16bit Tiff happens, of course, in the shop, mostly with the aid of AutoEye and nik ProPhoto, but some other filters as well. I think that digital photography saves you a lot of time in situ, but on the other hand you have to invest more time in the digital darkroom to get the results you want. This, however, to me personally was a liberating step. I never liked hovering round the photo lab and paying loads of money until I finally got the results on paper. It's much more natural for me, being a kid of the computer-design-age, to do what I did to «normal» photos anyway, that is work them over digitally. Only now, my basic ingredients are much better, as there isn't any scanner involved anymore. I almost always workon images I get, even if they are from external pro photographers, and I also do so on my own photos. In many ways this has been as liberating as doing music on the computer. I can't hardly play a song on a real piano, but with constructing music on a PC, stacking sequences and elements... which is rather like designing music, I think... I actually do have a lot of fun (whenever I get around to it, which is almost NEVER these last two years :-(). Oh, and for «on the road» I found myself getting a 5 MP Sony T3. Nice, small package, very well designed, fast reaction, but alas it is so small that most images are somewhat blurry... and the camera needs a flash too often, I think. HD Schellnack
