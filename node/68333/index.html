Hi Dösi,

in addition to Dan’s article (which omits the Kurrent genre, the script equivalent of Gebrochene Schriften – I suppose deliberately, as it never was significant in typography), there’s also the classification that [[Judith Schalansky]] uses in her Fraktur Mon Amour – basically a similar approach, with a finer differentiation of experimental (display) styles, see this overview PDF [3.8mb] by Fontblog.de.

As you speak German, see also the TypoWiki, [[Wolfgang Beinert]]’s Typolexikon, and this article by [[Bernhard Schnelle]]. Offline reading recommendation: Fraktur by [[Albert Kapr]].

a guide out there that breaks up blackletter forms to it’s very basic elements
