WPVI logo font

March 19, 2015 • Daniel Bent

![](/images/old-images/WPVI logo font_3800.png)

Does anybody know what is the font that WPVI used? The WPVI “6” logo currently uses the font.

It appears to be some 1960s reinterpretation of an old woodtype Latin: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/mti/latin/

If it survived into the digital age, I haven’t found it….

The numbers 3 and 6 are a lightweight version of a design cut by John F. Cumming in 1898 as Binner. Here they are, together with a bonus of 5, 2 and 9 in the same style http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/ef/binner/ Don
2015-03-20 08:55:02 • donshottype
The letters are an edited version of De Vinne, which was available in many widths and weights circa 1900. The bowl on P and the terminals on S are very distinctive. The serif style is a match. Here is a digital rendering by Jeff Levine of one of the wider versions as Publication JNL http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/jnlevine/publication/ The TV station logos add serifs to the bottom of V and W and clip spurs from the top of T and F and the bottom of L. Make these edits, narrow the width, and Publication JNL would look the same to most readers. Don
