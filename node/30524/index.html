2007-01-11 16:18:06 • Bert Vanderveen

As a designer for a Government Contractor, I can emphatically state that anything we (designers) do is an improvement over what’s already in place. I would also encourage making as many visual improvements until someone says something. I’m constantly tweaking forms and factsheets that were created by some number cruncher using powerpoint.

It brings up an interesting side question: Should I feel ethically wrong if I tweak a non trademarked logo?

I’m constantly working with Government and Military logos that are either exist as small raster images, or as a horribly outdated and poorly created vector EPS. On more than one occasion, I’ve had to re-vectorize one or more of these logos for a project. I’d take the opportunity to then “tweaked” them, typography and all.
