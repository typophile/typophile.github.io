2009-05-19 14:50:36 • Frode Bo Helland

The classic standard of beauty says that you need unity, variety, and balance. I think the same is true in type design. Unity can become boring, as James said, but variety can become visual noise that doesn’t make visual sense as a design. The excellent critiques you got above address the problem of the design not looking coherent. The challenge I think is to have unity of vision that includes lively variety in the execution.

You need a design idea that is rich enough to get both unity and variety, pulled off with balance. This is difficult to bring off, which is why type designs that are really new and great are not so many.

Two interesting recent examples of those who have brought it off are: Christian Schwartz’s [[http://fontbureau.com/fonts/Amplitude|Amplitude]], where he has ‘traps’ all over the place, but changes them significantly with the position and character; and Matthew Carter’s [[http://fontbureau.com/fonts/Rocky|Rocky]], where, as I note [[http://new.typographica.org/2009/typeface-reviews/rocky/|here]], the ‘spikes’ on different letters are all different, but fit together harmoniously.

Added: I also find it helpful in checking for consistency to look not at curves, but at weights, or widths of strokes. Look at the weights of joints, branches, verticals, horizontals, shoulders, terminals, etc. The goal here is not uniformity, though there is a certain amount of that, but coherence of the design, even ‘color’ and a nice ‘dance’ of characters across the line. To me, getting the curves pretty comes after getting the weights to work, and the widths of counters and spacing. But that may just be my thing.

This has been covered some, and in some cases is hard to see in a gif of this size:

g: feels a little boxy in the tail for the roundness in the other letters. Perhaps less weight in the tail join to eye (as in the a). Tail needs more weight on the right. Looks like the top pt could slide right a touch. Could be a hair narrow.

a: a little weighty in the terminal. Bowl, inner and outer curves are too disparate. Outer is boxy, inner is round. Angle on the tail is maybe a little much, and too weighty.

e: crossbar is too thick. Compare to a. Also, a has very even counter spaces. Consider equalizing the e also. For example taking the weight out of the crossbar only from the top. Might be a hair narrow.

o: could be a hair wide.

n: wide. Not sure about the taper on the stem. Feels week to me. Might need to be more deliberate - eg. a curve, or perhaps flaring the outside too. Then again, could be fine.

s: seems a bit narrow.

in general, i’d consider shaving the horizontals a bit. o looks ok, but g.eye a.top s.top look a little hair heavy.

This looks like one of the funky grotesques en vogue now: eg Maple and National. So don’t regularize too much. I think especially the forms and proportions/widths should stay unique. Color aberations should be fixed IMO.
