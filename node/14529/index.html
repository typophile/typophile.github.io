Text appears heavier in print after conversion to outlines (Illustrator CS2)

August 11, 2005 • timfm

Anybody else see this? I'm working with Tarzana Narrow at 7, 9, and 11pt. When I convert to outlines and print, the text appears considerably heavier than if just printed as text. How will this affect the final printing on press? Do most printers usually ask for text outlines? Or can they be delivered postscript? Any insight for this screen specialist is appreciated. -t
This is normal, and it's due to the difference in rendering methods between text and objects, the latter coming out an average of 1/2 a pixel fatter around the edges. On a laser printer, this is pretty obvious; but the good news is that on an imagesetter it's only really visible for really small type, like less than 8 point. hhp

Joe, I'm not confident it won't be a problem on printer ripped files. Ask for a proof, perhaps before the job goes into the printer's workflow. Also try making a print optimized pdf with the outlines and non-outlined letters side by side. Then blow up the pdf in your pdf viewr to the max. This might tell you something. Outlines destroy hinting and are slightly heavier. So they are best avoided but it's not always possible. PDFs coming out of InDesign for example often have CID font encoding which some older RIPs cannot handle. Most of the problems I have with this is with newspapers. In that case I will export an EPS and then Distill. Talking to the printer is the best advice here. Get a proof.
