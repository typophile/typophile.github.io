Pointed serif, similar to Serpentine

December 1, 2016 • WinterDesign

I’m trying to identify this sample – I am confident it originated from a font as it was created by a printer. They wouldn’t have gone to such lengths to create custom letterforms for this small job. Unfortunately the printers are no longer trading (Defasio, Bradford on Avon, near Bath).

The sample can be viewed here: http://www.winterdesign.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2016/11/IMG_1910.jpg

If this can be identified I’ll be so pleased!

Thanks in advance.

My copy of Rookledge’s International Type Finder shows a font called “Tamil” that looks very close to this. Sadly any attempts to Google this are swamped by results about new fonts using the Tamil script, rather than actually CALLED that. Grr. I agree with Dave’s ID of Tamil as the typeface that was used. It shows up in the Solotype Catalog (p. 158), where is is shown in even weights. Tamil Medium looks about the same. Even though the Solotype Catalog doesn’t show the whole typeface (notice I am not calling it a font), his “Sans Serif Display Alphabets” book does show the complete font and I’d say it’s a match, even the quirky U and S. The book only shows the Light and Black weights, but that convinced me.

I don’t know if this has been digitized, although my notes show Expresiv Fonts had a font called Tamago that was Tamil Black.
