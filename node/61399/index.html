R.I.P. Will Powers

August 27, 2009 • charles ellertson

Below was posted to the AAUP design list:

It is with deep sorrow that we let you know that Will Powers, MHS Press Design & Production Manager, passed away yesterday (Tuesday) afternoon. He was vacationing at a family cabin in Canada with his wife when he unexpectedly had a massive heart attack.

Will went by the user name Powers on Typophile. Before becoming a designer, he was a printer. Not a monitor-watcher, but an old-fashion printer. I will miss Will and his ironical sense of humor.

My sincerest condolences to the family.

We can see his words here: http://typophile.com/user/7891/track

2009-08-27 06:45:29 • Sye

I’m stunned and saddened. As kind and generous in the handful of times I got to meet with him in the past few years as he was in posts here.

Early last October, Will wrote me an email with two items. First was that he had decided the beautiful two-volume Deberny & Peignot 1927 specimen he had dug out of his treasure trove and loaned to me for my Face the Nation exhibition needn’t be returned; he was offering it to my university’s library as a gift.

The second item was that somehow he had arranged to commandeer the 6:30 am slot at a small local radio station the following Monday, and he invited me to listen is as he would be playing Ray Charles’s early jazz recordings. I listened; it was fantastic.

Another memory that makes me smile is Kent Lew at Buffalo TypeCon announcing to convention-goers that Will had left but wanted everyone to hear his advice to place the lead Ludlow slugs he’d given out as souvenirs of his workshop/presentation in an easily accessible location within luggage lest they raise the eyebrows of airport security.

We’ve lost a knowledgeable resource and a generous friend.

2009-08-27 10:39:26 • gaultney

I am shocked and saddened this morning to learn this terrible news!

Will and I first met in 2003 at TypeCon in Minneapolis. Since then he has become a good friend as well as an esteemed colleague. I remember sitting down to that first beer together and the invigorating, wide-ranging conversation, discovering all our many shared interests.

In addition to our love of type and typography, the history and the craft, Will and I shared an interest in birds and wildlife. We would periodically send reports to each other of the local changes of season – Will: the flights of snow geese and pelicans up the central flyway or the first kinglet in April; or me: a fallout of spring warblers or a bittern “singing” from a nearby swamp.

Last year he invited me to be on the jury to select book interiors for the AAUP’s annual competition. We had a wonderful time talking about books and hanging out in NYC, where the judging took place. Later, in July, at the AAUP annual conference in Montreal, I fondly recall skipping out on the Saturday morning sessions together to spend 4 hours tramping around the Parc du Mont Royal instead, investigating the local birds and enjoying the trails.

I was looking forward to hearing tales of this last month’s grand tour around the Rockies and back across the Canadian plains to the cabin in Ontario. Will had confided in me that after his return, he was planning to ease into retirement from the press. He was looking forward to time for personal projects.

This sudden and permanent retirement is not what any of us had in mind. He will be sorely missed.

May he rest in peace.

– Kent.
