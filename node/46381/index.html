Hockey Night in Canada Theme Song

June 13, 2008 • russellm

For forty years the CBC, (the Canadian Broadcasting Corporation) had used the same distinctive theme song. The contract with it’s composer was due for renewal. She and her management firm were asking more than the CBC, a public broadcaster was willing to pay. In steps CTV, a private television network and pays $3,000,000 for complete ownership with the intention of using it as the theme for their Wednesday night hockey show.

The theme has become iconic in Canada - If you don’t know the tune, you probably are not a Canadian. It’s so strongly identified with the CBC’s Hockey Night in Canada that when the possibility came up that CBC could lose the rights to it, that was THE headline story in this country for at least a week, pushing aside war, murder and mayhem. We’re talking music here, but really it was a question of branding. As in, “who owns this thing and what does ownership mean?” It’s been around so long people feel a sense of ownership towards the tune. can it really belong to the people who paid for the rights, or is it even truly “ownable” ?

Perhaps an analogy would be if Michelin’s fat-tire-guy went to work for Goodyear he’d still be selling Michelin tires for years to come and there would be nothing Goodyear could do about it. How long before people watching Hockey on CTV will hear the theme and not think, “Oh yeah - CBC Hockey Night in Canada - Saturday - Must watch…” ?

Lose a song, gain a font…
