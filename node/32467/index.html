March 22, 2007 • Don MacDonald

Here's the opening spread from Machiavelli's 1506 Decemnale (first edition.) The typeface looks to be a Jenson or a close relative thereof. I'm looking for a font that matches the look of this letterpressed text. Fifteen36 looks very promising, but I am concerned that there's no bold, italic, or small caps. I'd prefer to buy a font family that would give me the most flexibility. P22's Mayflower is a nice looking font, but I'm fairly certain I will go to hell for using a typeface from 1610 in a book about Machiavelli (1469-1527.) Plus it's not Venetian, Florentine or anything else. Any suggestions for a nice antiqued Roman font from Machiavelli's era? Thanks, Don
P22 William Morris Set or LTC Jenson might be a good place to start. William Morris idealized the look of the books from this period and did his utmost to create a nice dark color on the page with his types.
