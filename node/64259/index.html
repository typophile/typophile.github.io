2009-11-18 21:09:34 • cpaluch04
If the core of the label is alternative bands, this logo maybe doesn’t fit the target. It feels very much about connectivity, parts fitting together, positive and negative space, which are all great things, but something that I would expect more for a non-profit or a corporate client. Of course alternative can mean a million things (and you could debate if it ever meant anything in the 90s). I’m not suggesting you should do market research on alternative labels – I love the K shield (http://www.kpunk.com/), but doing something similar would be cheap and lazy. Naive hand-drawn stuff has been done to death, particularly in the indie/alternative world, but in the 80s I’d imagine their logo seemed pretty innovative and subversive. So with this logo, maybe you make it look extremely corporate, contrasting with the fact that it’s a tiny record label. I would just go back and ask her what kind of things she wants the label to represent, and go from there.
