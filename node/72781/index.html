Actually, Kozuka Mincho Pro does include a full set of italics that correspond to its proportional Latin glyphs. They are at CIDs 9444 through 9737, and are made accessible through the ‘ital’ GSUB feature, which is supported by InDesign.

2010-08-05 00:10:14 • Exhibit_A

Unfortunately, unless an application supports the ‘ital’ GSUB feature, or allows access to unencoded glyphs, the italic glyphs cannot be used. I doubt that iWorks Pages supports either.

Also, while the Latin glyphs in Kozuka Mincho, including the italic forms, are Adobe Serif, they are based on specific multiple master weights and widths, and are also scaled to be slightly larger.

2010-08-05 02:38:02 • lunde
Pages (and other Apple apps like TextEdit) have a pretty decent OpenType support: Open the font palette (Format > Font > Show Fonts — ⌘T). Open the fly-out menu that is hidden beneath the gearwheel icon, and you will find ‘Typography…’ and ‘Characters…’. The former gives you access to the OpenType features, incl. Kozuka Mincho’s italics. Via the latter, you can manually insert glyphs, incl. unencoded ones.
