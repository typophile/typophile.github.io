Hi, I’m working on a book which includes Turkish text, to be set in Neutraface. Although House Industries tells me that Neutra supports Turkish (http://www.houseind.com/fonts/neutraface/fontfeatures), it seems the Fonts are missing the S-cedilla (ş). As I’m not speaking/reading Turkish, I’m a bit confused. Any thoughts? Thanks in advance! V
I was a bit confused by this a while back too, and it seems we're not alone. Take my findings with a grain of salt, but some countries/languages use a comma accent beneath the S, while others insist on a true cedilla. Others are seemingly ambivalent — regarding it as a matter of style or preference. Font designers have been all over the place with it. Here's an explanation and a solution, of sorts: http://diacritics.typo.cz/index.php?id=9
