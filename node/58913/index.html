Irish and dotaccents

June 10, 2009 • blank

According the the Diacritics Project, “The dot accent is occasionally used in Irish…”, and I have noticed that occasionally is not often enough for many foundries to create these characters. Is this because they just don’t want to support Irish, or because “occasionally used in Irish…” means that they’re only going to see use in the footnotes of books by Morgan Llewellyn and Seamus Heavy?
There are two orthographies for writing Irish. The older orthography uses the dot accent to indicate lenited (weakened) consonants, in the newer orthography an h is used after the consonant to indicate lenition so the dot is not needed. This means that there is a standard way to write modern Irish that does not require dots, so they are not strictly necessary. However, both orthographies have official status in Ireland -- the constitution, for example, has an official text in both orthographies -- and for reproducing older documents the dotted consonants are needed.
