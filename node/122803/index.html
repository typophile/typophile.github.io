Thank you. This is indeed an exact match. According to the note for the OPTI font it was “Originally a phototype by Filmotype.” Unless there was a version with the same digital outlines by another fontmaker before OPTI produced OPTI Racer, this is presumably an original digital creation by OPTI. Does anyone know of a matching digital font before the OPTI font? I only ask because of of the notorious reputation of OPTI/Castcraft as a forger of unauthorized clones of digital fonts. A relatively small proportion of its fonts were original digital creations. Don
2015-01-25 13:08:54 • donshottype

Not much help, but I can confirm from Filmotype catalog showings that this was originally Filmotype Racer. I know of no other digital versions.

Stuart Sandler bought the rights to the Filmotype collection in 2006 and has since been having several fontmakers create digital versions for sale. No Racer yet, though.

Mike, looking at the example of Racer, we have a design that Oxford University Press choose as the best way of expressing its title theme. I don’t know whether OUP used the OPTI digital or a Filmotype analog source.

I find the Racer typeface design superior to Fashion Didot for this purpose.

Anyone trying something similar for a commercial project today would be stuck in the bind of using a digital font from OPTI – an orphan foundry where there is no way of obtaining appropriate intellectual property permissions – or making the font from scratch.

I do hope that Stuart Sandler’s digitization plan has Racer in the next batch.
