Font Shui

July 28, 2008 • andrewzahn

After many years in school and in the professional world, I have “collected” a ton of fonts. So I’ve decided to do some house cleaning. I’ve deleted about 75 fonts already. Part of me would really like to rebuild my type library from scratch, keeping only the obvious ones: Garamond, Univers, Bodoni, Clarendon, etc… you know, the “standards.” A font starter kit if you will. I came to this realization after spending way too much time clicking through suitcase looking for that perfect typeface: Arial (no), Beachhouse (never), Catfish Script Pro (what the hell does “pro” mean anyway? Is there a Catfish Amateur out there with dreams if making it to the big leagues?), and on and on and on. So…Hi, my name is Andy, and I’ve become out of touch with fonts. I need help rebuilding my type library with the classics and some soon to be classics. Does anyone else know what I mean or am I just getting old?

I’d add Caslon, Meta, Mrs. Eaves, and Minion…

The problem with deleting ones like Arial is that people send me stuff in Arial all the time and I wouldn’t be able to see it correctly if I deleted it. Granted, I prefer it to comic sans, but wouldn’t it be nice if it could just disappear for a while (forever).

2008-07-28 18:45:39 • jessi.long

I think your goal is admirable, but I would caution you not to actually delete the fonts you don’t want to use, in case you find someday you actually do need them. I’d recommend a font manager, such as Linotype’s free Font Explorer or <a href=http://www.extensis.com/en/products/font_management/index.jsp;jsessionid=RCMXCP3OUNLCVLAQAAUARSQ>Extensis Suitcase, so that you can just activate and make use of the fonts you want.

And I wouldn’t get rid of what I assume is actually Caflisch Script Pro; I happen to like it. The Pro means that it is one of Adobe’s OpenType fonts with extra features, which in this case I believe means contextual alternates to make it flow better.
