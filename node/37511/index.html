Italicized prepositions in titles

September 25, 2007 • JCSalomon

I’ve just begun to notice this, but some book titles italicize words (such as prepositions) that are not capitalized. The first time I noticed it was in Robert Bringhurst’s “The Elements of Typographic Style”, then on the cover of a Dick Francis novel.

Is this an accepted style? I mean, Bringhurst uses it, but he doesn’t mention it in the book that I’ve found.

2007-09-26 00:01:13 • pattyfab
It’s merely a design convention, used primarily because “of” looks more interesting italicized (as does “the” in many cases). On the other hand, book titles as a whole should be italicized rather than put in quotes. If “of-italicization” were a rule, the title would look like The Elements of Typographic Style…which looks pretty dumb.
2007-09-26 01:07:01 • oldnick
Title typography on book covers and title pages often uses many (hopefully not all at the same time) devices such as bold, all caps, italics, different fonts for different words, different colours for different words, etc. for design purposes and emphasis. But when cited in normal text, the convention is all italic in lower case, with first letters of appropriate words in uppercase according to established style rules; all other typographic treatments are dropped.
