None of those end in "-ing". "Designing" is a gerundive, a verbal form used as a different part of speech. That is the point of Christopher's double entendre, to recontextualize "designing", something which designers do every day, in a different light. Several of those other words carry double meanings, e.g. artiste, which is used to describe "a member of the company" (e.g. ballet), as well as perjoratively, but there is no "arting" or "musicing". Well, not much. I have also occasionally come across "fonting".
