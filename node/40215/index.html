How to shift glyphs down in FontLab

December 19, 2007 • juandelacruz

I’ve been googling to find a way to shift a certain glyph downwards in FontLab commands in generating glyphs but couldn’t find any. I’m trying to place the commands in alias.dat something like unixxxx L+^circumflex (which shifts circumflex upwards), is there any way I could shift the circumflex down in alias.dat? would really appreciate the help…

It is possible to shift glyphs upwards by the [caps-height — x-height] distance: L+^circumflex and it is possible to enforce that the second component stays on the baseline: L+~circumflex. It is not possible to shift the component below the baseline, though. To do that, you should employ anchors.

Regards, Adam

If you are looking for a good way to place diacritics more precise, I believe the best way is to always make two or three versions of them. Two if you dont have small caps in your font, and three if you have.

For instance, circumflex, circumflex.small*, circumflex.cap

By doing so you can place them so they fit their respective base character better. The circumflex.cap goes to the capital letters etc.

You can then adjust your alias.dat file to match this, for instance:

Acircumflex A+circumflex.cap acircumflex a+circumflex acircumflex.small a.small+circumflex.small*

This could also be .sc, .smcp or whatever glyph naming routine you have.
