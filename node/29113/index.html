Dress Boston typeface

October 27, 2006 • cre8

Hello! I am a graphic design student, and am always distracted by everything typographic around me. There is a store in Boston called Dress, which I spotted while walking one day. I was drawn to the store, as their sign is pure type. it is understated, and very elegent I feel. I went inside to ask for a business card, which really impressed me as well. The logo of the store stumped me though. It appears to me as if the designer has modifed the typeface, but I just wanted to be sure. I just thought it may interesting to share. Does anyone have any ideas? I was playing around with different typefaces in Illustrator, and it almost seems as if they modified Filosofia to me. The bowl of the d, counterform of the e, and the space under the ear of the r seem to be closest to Filosofia (for what i have for typefaces). Justin
It doesn't look like Filosofia to me, although the 'd' and 'r' seen to be very similar. I think the 'e' and especially the 's' are different, though. I'm afraid those letters are not very distinctive -- even some of the Century fonts seems pretty close, and match much better on the 's' -- so I think it will be hard to ID this with only these letters. Do they use the same typeface in other ways, where we could see more letters from the alphabet? - Mike Yanega
