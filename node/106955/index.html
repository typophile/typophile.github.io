2013-10-22 23:10:11 • Nick Shinn

Probably not. FuturaND was published by Neufville Digital and your Futura Std is likely produced by Adobe. Although I think Futura is licensed from Neufville by Adobe, internal settings of the fonts from the two different foundries are different in several respects which may be causing the problem you see.

If you are using InDesign or Quark XPress you may be able to fix the problem for the entire document by changing the app settings for the first baseline.

If you’ve already applied paragraph styles to your text, I’d use Nick’s method of adjusting the baseline shift in the style settings. But if you previously set up the document without style sheets, be sure to proof it carefully afterwards in case there are unintended consequences to applying new, global styles. There shouldn’t be if you do it right, but check to be sure.

Your original suggestion of moving the text boxes a bit could also work; you wouldn’t need to do it on each page, just the text boxes on the master pages (assuming you haven’t broken the links to them). Again, proof carefully afterwards since even small changes can cause text to reflow.

And be sure to work with a copy of the file, so you can easily revert if needed.
