2003-08-21 16:39:39 • kentlew
> narrower overall Kent, I've come to the -admittedly counterintuitive, and somewhat tentative- conclusion that narrowness is an illusional virtue in a news face. When you take into account the frequent linebreaks in news setting (and possible a negative effect in apparent size), it turns out that it might in fact backfire*. So the choice of width in a news face has more to do with the atmosphere you want to convey than economy. I'm talking about body here, not headings, where horizontal economy is of obvious benefit. * Does this jive with what the FB experts have told you? Also, when you pull in the extenders you automatically create a perception of width. When I was deriving Patria from Harrier, I ended up with a much more squat and masculine design than I expected, or even originally wanted. I'm lucky I ended up liking it like that! :-) In the case of Whitman, it depends on the atmosphere you want to convey. If you want it wide/homey (which to me fits well) rather than narrow/elegant, chances are just pulling in the extenders is enough. hhp

>the "stateliness" conveyed by its narrowness Actually, Worldwide might be a bit too condensed to work with Gotham, which is quite squat and wide looking. However, it could work with Gotham Condensed. But really, it's tough to know till you actually start playing around. There are so many typographic parameters that a designer can fiddle with these days in news design – a huge difference from the rigid restrictions of the pre-digital era. A few years ago, North American web presses went narrower, causing all kinds of design problems for news designers, who generally responded by going to narrower faces. So there is definitely a "semi-condensed" look to the genre – but of course we don't know where Zach is is heading in relation to the trend.
