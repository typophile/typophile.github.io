Original or a copy

June 12, 2011 • Novak

Hi, I have found a very interesting case which might be interesting. There is a free font which is based I gues on one of Berthold’s fonts. One of graphic designers from Poland is claiming to design a new typeface as his Ph. D. thesis.[[http://translate.google.pl/translate?js=n&prev=_t&hl=pl&ie=UTF-8&layout=2&eotf=1&sl=pl&tl=en&u=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.polskalitera.pl%2F&act=url]] Looking at the project closely it seems to be a modified copy of Berthold’s font - Laudatio BQ Light designed by Friedrich Poppl.

There is a big discussion on Polish graphic designes blog. [[http://translate.google.pl/translate?js=n&prev=_t&hl=pl&ie=UTF-8&layout=2&eotf=1&sl=pl&tl=en&u=http%3A%2F%2Fstgu.pl%2F2011%2F05%2Fapolonia-czy-arogancja%2F&act=url]] Is it original or a copy? What is your opinion?

There are some clear differences between the two, and in many cases I’m not sure I don’t prefer Apolonia’s treatment, so I certainly wouldn’t simply dismiss it as a copy. To what extent inspiration was drawn from Laudatio I’m not sure, nor am I familiar with the particular university’s precise requirements around original work in a PhD, but I certainly see it as a different design with merit which goes beyond Laudatio.
