Thrice Objectified

January 23, 2009 • Natecox

Objectified is the latest documentary from director/producer Gary Hustwit (Helvetica). The film, which investigates the field of industrial design, is set to premier in March at Austin’s South by Southwest Film Festival (with preview screenings in San Francisco, Chicago, New York, and London TBA). As with Helvetica before it, Objectified has its very own website with blog, shop, and most recently, trailer. With all these different features have come three very different type treatments for the film’s title.

The first is a modified Helvetica Ultra Compressed in two shades of green used for the masthead of the website, designed by Jason Santa Maria.

The second is an “objects logo” constructed from letterforms of iconic product silhouettes (Ive’s click wheel for Apple, Rams’ pocket radio for Braun, etc.) designed by Michael C. Place of Build. The logo is used on T-shirts and posters in the online store.

Finally there are the titles from the trailer set in gray on gray Akzidenz Grotesk, presumably by Hustwit himself. View the trailer below:
