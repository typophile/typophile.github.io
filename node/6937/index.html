2004-10-01 17:10:58 • Chris Rugen
What a lot of excellent advice! The only bit I might well add is a reinforcement of this thought (and others like it):

if yer not doing anything interesting at work, make projects for yourself. design yourself a logo for your personal studio, design yourself a website, a business card, some stationery, etc.

To make such self-assigned work as useful as possible, I would try to base that work on subject matter with which you are already familiar. Reasons:
1) Familiarity with the subject might make more research unnecessary… less research, more time to design.
2) For those subjects about which you have pre-existing knowledge, you might be able to generate more genuine enthusiasm… more enthusiasm, better quality product.
3) To those who have knowledge about a particular subject, factual mistakes might be more noticable (and, therefor, more likely to be corrected), than to one who is new to the subject.

Some examples: cd packaging for a musician or group that appeals to you; advertisement or poster for a sport or activity that you enjoy; promotional material for a charity that you support.

I hope that this small addition to the other great ideas is helpful.
