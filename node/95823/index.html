The New York Times Covers The Complete Engraver

August 25, 2012 • nancy sharon collins

The New York Times covers Nancy Sharon Collins’ [[http://tmagazine.blogs.nytimes.com/2012/08/24/the-complete-engraver/|The Complete Engraver]], her book about all things engraved (though its written as if both MOMA and Clinique are active clients—which they are not—correction forthcoming … ).

[[http://www.kickstarter.com/projects/1872144378/engraving-for-nerds-the-book-tour|Help]] support the book tour (2 days remaining), it underwrites a four city, east coast book tour, commencing in September when [[http://www.amazon.com/The-Complete-Engraver-Monograms-Stationery/dp/1616890673|The Complete Engraver]] hits book shelves.

Pictured, above, is an engraved specimen of both JMC Engraver and Feldman Engraver, these companion revivals are based on engraving styles which were re-engineered and expanded into modern, OpenType fonts by Terrance Weinzierl, Monotype Imaging, and are available [[http://blog.fonts.com/2012/07/24/the-complete-engraver/|FREE]].
