Hi boys et girls: [img:sites/default/files/old-images/Screen Shot 2015-04-08 at 4_6428.10.36 PM.png] I’m not sure how one renders this French word with title case: Lalique hommage à l’homme Is this correct? Lalique Hommage à l’Homme ...with the H capitalized in both instances? or Lalique Hommage à L’Homme ...should the L be capilalized too? Lalique uses a dumb apostrophe in the word “l’homme”, I assume that using ’ is correct, rather than something else like `. Comments much appreciated. Mike Diaz :-)
I was always taught that only the first word, or if the first word is an article, then the first noun and any adjectives that proceed it are capitalized, so |Lalique hommage à l’homme| would be considered correct. However, there are differing schools of thought on this: http://french.about.com/library/writing/bl-capitalizationoftitles.htm

It would be < Lalique Hommage à l’Homme > It is the name of a product, so you generally capitalize the nouns Lalique, Hommage ('homage') and Homme ('man') in this case. À ('to') and l’ ('the') would never be capitalized. Bonne chance !

Well, if you go on Lalique's own site, http://www.lalique-parfums.com/fr/hommage-lhomme-0, in the left-hand column, you can read “Hommage à l’homme” with only Hommage capitalized. Of course Lalique needs to be capitalized. For me, in French, that would be either “Lalique – Hommage à l’homme” or “Hommage à l’homme de Lalique”. The rules for French capitalization can be found on Wikipedia Usage des majuscules en français. Some are rather involved. If it were to be in an English context, then I would do like Dan said.

I have noticed that many foreign languages don't have "Title Case" in the sense we have it in English. One can see evidence of this in how people posting songs to YouTube treat their titles. Not having title case, of course, is useful in an international context because it's hard to recognize definite and indefinite articles, never mind prepositions, in every language under the Sun.

For French, if you follow the "traditional rules", you need to know your adjective and your adverbs because if they occur before the first noun, they are to be capitalized. Examples from the Wiki link above Le Grand Meaulnes Les Très Riches Heures du duc de Berry Les Cinq Dernières Minutes but Les Liaisons dangereuses because the adjective is after, not before the first noun.
