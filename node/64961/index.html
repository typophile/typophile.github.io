2009-12-03 04:49:17 • Miro

I think my biggest criticism is still your lack of grid.

Start with a document that is something like this:

Then use it! Check out this article on grids and spend a little time on this website.

A poster that uses type somewhat similarly can be seen here.

Make invisible boxes that fit together with even spacing for all your words and use things like tracking and size (and condensed or bold or extended fonts) to fill those boxes. Not exactly like the image below, but you kind of get the idea..

Don’t be afraid to break your grid! That is a great way to create interest or focal point.

Oh, and if your instructors have not taught you about grids, be sure to ask for it! Grids are like rules.. and rules are meant to be broken. But you have to know the rules before you can break them.

I hope that is helpful!

2009-12-05 05:25:31 • shoesoffnrun

I still feel it’s not coherent enough and the composition as a whole is too patchy. I think there is a lack of real focus points in the design. It is not clear which information is the most important, and has to be read first.

I think you’ve made it pretty hard for yourself working with different typefaces, weights, directions etc. It lacks focus and a clear idea in my opinion.

What can be done to make this work? I think you have to make a pretty radical change. You are working with at least four different typefaces at the moment, which don’t really work together IMO. They all have very different characteristics, so this makes it hard to create coherence. I would cut down the amount of typefaces to a maximum of two. Find two that complement each other, rather than bite eachother. You can still work with different weights, but I would try working with different weights from the same family.

Now you’ve combined very light with very fat weights, this could be interesting, but looks messy and slightly arbitrary at the moment. You could give different weights to different kinds of information, but be more consistent, and try to find a balance between fat and light.

To make your life a lot easier I would throw out the direction changes, it overcomplicates the design and is hard to read. Also, limit the amount of information on the strictly neccesary, I think there is too much going on right now. Find out which information has 1st priority, which 2nd, 3rd etc. and build you design up accordingly.

Grids are nice things, but they only will help you find solutions, in helping you organise and balance your content. The hard work still has to be done by you though.

Keep us informed on your progress. good luck!

2009-12-06 04:59:50 • MrMoto

Yeah, it still has a lot of problems, one being the pinball issue. I think you’re still doing too much, with sizes and weights and overall composition. It just looks messy, and unless that’s what you we’re going for, you really have to change quite a lot. It still needs to be simplified a LOT. It’s very difficult too pull off a composition that works in this way, so make your life easier and the piece better by doing less. This may sound odd and boring, but I feel it’s the only way forward. As this is a piece of communication, you want it to communicate, clear and simple.

I would start with an empty document, as working on the same document all the time can lead to a kind of blindness. Start afresh and start putting in one element at the time, starting with the most important parts. Try to get one element to the point that it looks and feels good, then start adding other elements. I would keep the secondary information simple in their design, without fancy type, size and weight changes.

As for the typefaces, I would really advise you to pick a different combination, as these two don’t work together. If you want to work with the lighter geometric typeface, I would put another geometric typeface next to it, like futura, avant garde, serif gothic. Or if you want to go for contrast I would advise you to work with Bodoni. If, on the other hand, you want to work with the Helvetica you’ve used, I would just make everything helvetica, so throw out the geometrical typeface.

I hope this helps.
