Hello Everybody, I found this typeface around my University (Bauhaus, Weimar). Anyone has an idea what the name of it is?

This was originally part of TeX “Concrete” fonts family designed by Donald Knuth for a book called “Concrete Mathematics”: http://www.tex.ac.uk/cgi-bin/texfaq2html?label=concrete

They are later converted to .TTF and .OTF format and you can find it as e.g.: CMU Concrete (BoldItalic Extended) - Converted by Andrey V. Panov from TeX fonts or Computer Modern Concrete (Italic)

There is also a similar Google Web Font but it requires expansion and modification to match your sample as it is generated by tracing a MetaFont bitmap font: [[http://www.findmyfont.com/index.php/fonts/font-preview?fset=Google-Web-Fonts%20%2F%20%28Freemium%29&ffam=jsMath-cmti10%20-%20cmti10%20Italic&fid=bdffeaca019f2dc4e584d651948d86a0&fsize=60&text=work%20Raun%20304&wrap=2|jsMath-cmti10 (Italic)]] (c) says: “Generated from MetaFont bitmap by mftrace 1.0.33, http://www.cs.uu.nl/~hanwen/mftrace/"
