Buccardi as book text font?

November 24, 2009 • YaldahPub

I’ve seen Buccardi recommended a few times here and it is exactly the look that I wanted for a fun children’s early chapter book (ages 6-10) written from a dog’s point of view. However, my only concern is that it might be slightly too funky to be readable for a short chapter book. A very non-scientific study of a handful of first-graders showed that they can read it just fine, so maybe it’s just my resistance to the tried-and-true Garamonds.

I was also considering, as runner-ups, both Magellan and Adobe Caslon, though Buccardi is still hands-down my favorite for this project. Also, I’m thinking that with Buccardi, I could use it as well for the title, headings, etc., whereas the other two wouldn’t cut it for a title font.

I need a second (third, fourth…) opinion from folks who are even more experienced at this than I am. What do you think?

Thanks!

I hadn’t seen Buccardi before - pretty cool. Its proportions, color and spacing are good - and those are central to readability. And some funkiness in the letterforms might actually help readability! Go for it.

BTW, for a titling companion I might recommend one of these: http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/fontfont/ff-oneleigh/ http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/efscangraphic/vendome-sh/bold/
