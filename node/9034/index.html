I think there was too much 'artistic license' being taken with whatever letters the artist was copying from. It seems the bottoms of the M were made to go that way because of the contours of the body, for the purposes of design, rather than to be a faithful rendering of a typeface. I've done enough hand-lettering to know how quickly the medium dictates the lettering you use.

In other words, I don't think this is a font now, even if it was 'inspired' by one.
