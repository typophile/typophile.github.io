How to distinquish Mac vs PC Truetype?

June 2, 2006 • TypeSupport

I’ve got a load of TT fonts on file. Most are Mac, but a few are Windows format. Is there a way to easily distinquish between the two formats? Ideally, I’d love a utility(, script or something) to process a folder of font files and separate them into Mac and PC folders. Short of that, I’d take a report, or even a way to manually determine by checking individual files.

Thanks…

For the most part, you can probably tell the Windows fonts from the Mac fonts just by the “.ttf” extension. Windows TrueType fonts always have this extension. On OS X, the Mac fonts will be listed as “Font Suitcase” in the Finder and will have the letters “FFIL” on the icon. In OS 9 and earlier, the icon will look like a little suitcase. If you copy the Mac fonts to a PC, the files will be empty and the fonts will not be useable since Mac font suitcases have a file structure that Windows does not recognize.
