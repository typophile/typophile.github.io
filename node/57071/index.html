2009-04-16 21:04:50 • Dan Gayle

Dan - yes, Nina was developed as a UI font and was used in Windows Mobile smartphones (Windows Mobile now uses Segoe, Microsoft’s branding and UI font family). It can be considered part of the Tahoma and Verdana family. As you may know Verdana was designed along with Georgia for use with web pages (as the sans serif & serif fonts), and Tahoma was designed for the UI. Nina was designed to address the UI requirements on smaller screens such as phones, where every pixel counts, and where legibility and readability are even more critical. Hope this fills in the gaps from the above links.
