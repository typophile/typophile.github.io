2007-12-02 13:45:14 • Don McCahill
I’ve dug up a piece I did in 1989, suitably Industria-l. It’s a 16-page 5 1/2 x 8 1/2 booklet, black + spot colour. As you can see, the three Brody fonts came as a package on diskette. I used Industria and Insignia, but Arcadia was too elegant for this job. On the cover, I had fun playing around with dropshadows, horizontal scaling, and a piece of software (can’t remember its name) that distorted type, but not easy to control. So I combined that with a flag I drew in Illustrator 88. It’s a two-colour piece, because in those days, 4-colour was too expensive for a lot of jobs. Dropshadows were difficult on my Mac Plus, which was B&W, no greyscale, so it was hard to tell which text box was on top, and sometimes it was the dropshadow. I stretched Insignia, somehow it seemed to contrast better with Industria that way. For text, I shied away from using the Brody faces, went with Franklin Gothic, Futura, and Century Schoolbook. I also didn’t use the Industria lower case. Too arty, I guess. It’s sad to compare the environmental committment of that time (the Brundtland Commission, etc.) with today, because in between, it went missing.
