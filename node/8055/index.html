>>how common is direct to plate printing? For what kinds of printing?>>

Almost all our work is for University Presses, which usually means short-run printing -- 1000 to 3000 copies is the typical print run. I'd say that about half the books we set are printed direct to plate -- some shops don't use CTP at all, some use it for web presses but not sheet-fed presses, and some use it for everything. But you know that as the number of people in the stripping room falls, sooner or later, stripping up flats will be a thing of the past.

Another factor -- I'd say 75% of the books we do have at least some halftones. We always place "live art" in the PDF. That means that even if the printer is running out a negative from the PDF (instead of making the plate directly), they still can't play any tricks to fattten up the type, because the dotgain in the halftones would be too great.

All this stuff is ripped at 2400 dpi or more for either negative or plate; another reason type gets thin.

Best I can do to answer your question.
