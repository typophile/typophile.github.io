2008-05-23 17:39:30 • blank

My gut response was very similar to James’s. For the four years that I’ve been designing seriously and have had Meridien, I have been a huge fan of it. I have certainly enjoyed pairing it with Frutiger, and admittedly hold some pride in the fact that I know it makes a good pairing even though they aren’t branded as a Serif/Sans combo. So, it saddens me to see Meridien updated and renamed to follow the current trend for Serif/Sans super-families.

But, the logic of people like Miss Tiffany seems to prevail in the end. I’m sure many people in the industry had similar gut responses when italics were added to fonts like Centaur, which were not originally designed with italics but had well known italic counterparts. Now we don’t hesitate to use Centaur Italic instead of Arrighi.

Personally, I would have been more fond of carrying on the Meridien name, but as long as good type is proliferated and proper credit (and payment) is given where it belongs I have no problem with what the name is.

The job is always about the content, not about the type designer or graphic designer. When looking for pairings, let the text be your teacher and your eyes be your judge. It is hard for a type designer to anticipate all needs of all text. Type designers do their best either on purpose as in a superfamily or by accident when 2 different families happen to suit the job together perfectly. The graphic designer is the vigilant watchmen who does his best to represent the text or content in a suitable way. At least now choices are not limited by the library of the compositor you were forced to choose.
