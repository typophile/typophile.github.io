Art Deco-like geometric font, 1940s, G that looks like a C with a downwards spur.

September 17, 2013 • Locoluis

Greetings.

This is a font that flourished mostly around 1940, looking similar to contemporary Italian Art Deco poster lettering.

I think I saw it elsewhere when I was a kid, but nowadays I can only find this particular one in the façade of two buildings designed by the Chilean architect Juan Martínez Gutiérrez.

ESCUELA DE DERECHO UNIVERSIDAD DE CHILE [[http://congresocivil.cl/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/ejempo-940x340.jpg]]

ESCUELA MILITAR DEL GENERAL BERNARDO O’HIGGINS [[http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6426/2444/1600/Escuela_Militar.0.jpg]]

Common glyphs between both pictures: ACDEHILRSU. The N letter has a different shape in each picture.

Also, the first one has V, while the second one has BGMOT.

The following is the awful result of me trying to reproduce the shapes of the letters.

The shape of the letters E, G, M, R, S (and the N in the second picture) are the most distinctive traits of this font.

In particular, the G looks odd, like a C with a downwards spur, resembling a sickle.

Please help me find a similar font to this one. A hard task, since there are thousands of Art Deco-like geometric fonts.

You say that some letters differ between the buildings. Perhaps the architect did not use a single source for the lettering. Various fonts have letters similar to your sample. For example the S is close to the S in Aase, designed by A. Bihari for VGC in 1977. Image source: Bowfin Printworks Bauhaus Guide. There is a digital version under by Image club Graphics 1992. Don’t know of a legitimate source to obtain it. Don
