I’m in school (not design-related - in a social science) and sometimes need to hand in papers that are “double-spaced, in Times New Roman or similiar font, at about 12 point.”

For various reasons I am looking to use something different. I’ve seen two typefaces I like a lot that are not too expensive – Publico and Guardian Egyptian. Utopia, Iowan and Charter seems to be good options too.

2010-06-25 10:12:55 • Queneau

You can go for Palatino, Georgia, Century (Schoolbook), these are on most Systems, although on windows you might need to look for the Palatino clone Book Antiqua. If you are on a mac the Hoefler Text is related to the Garamond tradition and a nice font, although it has Old Style Numbers.

If you use MS office you can go with Constantia.

Free fonts consider Gentium, Liberation, Libertine, Bergamo, Deja Vu Serif you can find on [[http://www.fontsquirrel.com/fonts/list/style/Serif|fontsquirrel.com]]. Have a look at the progress of [[http://aldusleaf.org/|Crimson Text]].

Utopia and Minion in an older version are free for LaTeX, I am not sure about the licence here, but at least they can be found in some latex font directory, google will help you here.

I would not recommend to go for something to warm or to progressive. Academics certainly benefits from a departure of TNR, but it should not be a noticeable font so to speak.

Classical text faces include Janson Text, Sabon, (Adobe) Garamond, (Adobe or Williams) Caslon, Baskerville (Original), Minion among others. It does not need to be this conservative, they are just options. Guardian is a fine one too.

@forrest

Double spacing and Times New Roman are examples of many of the anachronisms inherent to the world of academia. It makes little sense for most printed (not for publishing) academic work, and makes even less sense if it is handed in PDF format to be read (more often than not, one assumes) on-screen.

Anachronisms aside, if the requirement is to hand the work in using as a PDF document, you should ideally be looking for a typeface which is designed to have extremely good screen legibility (a really well hinted font). I personally find that Bitstream’s Iowan Pro and Charter Pro fit the bill, while remaining affordable (remember, you probably need only 2~3 weights for average academic work). Of these two Iowan would probably be easier on the eyes for extended text.

You may also want to consider [[http://www.linotype.com/137452/trumpmediaevaloffice-family.html?PHPSESSID=27b8480e3ecf6291aea7780bb0295b82|Trump Mediaeval Office]], which was re-designed for office communication and to be a TNR replacement. It is also a superbly well hinted font, specifically designed for on-screen reading.
