2003-11-24 22:50:08 • Dan Weaver
I'm far from being naive, especially since I have been a professional graphic designer for 25 years, but I'm really stunned that so many designers invested so much time and energy for the CHANCE at so little in compensation.

I'm no longer surprised when businesses (of any size) opt to put so little investment into the primary branding or identity element on which they rely for the promotion and customer recognition of their business venture in the marketplace. After all, in the 1970's Nike did originally pay only $35 for their logo - which Phil Knight accepted with some reluctance due to a looming print deadline. At least the company made it up to the designer years later with the gift of a good sum of stock and a day in her honor.

At the end of his career designer Paul Rand would take on one identity project a year for the fee of $100,000. I think that's a great fee goal for any designer - instead of bidding to see just how inexpensively (i.e. cheaply) one can produce such a project.
