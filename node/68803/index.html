Maybe I’m a little confused on the meaning of collateral, but I see this terminology a lot.

All selection of print items for a client is print collateral?

What other ways are there to say it?

The term has been used primarily by advertising agencies, whose main work appears in media (newspapers, magazine, TV and radio, billboards, etc.). Collateral items do not generally appear in media, but are standalone pieces. So, brochures are collateral; ads in a magazine or newspaper are not.
