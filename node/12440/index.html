Poynter Old Style

May 15, 2005 • Falak Shah

O, how this typeface, Poynter Old Style, has been haunting me. It is ubiquitous — everywhere you see, it is there; magazines, newspapers and books. It is everywhere. But the problem is single-users cannot purchase it for personal use. Why do they do that? Anyone else obsessed with Poynter?

They do that because they can charge more for the fonts; they sell less copies that way, but it makes sense considering the paucity of the retail channel, not to mention its greater risk of piracy.
