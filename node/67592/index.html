Lineto’s Le Corbusier is indeed drawn in the style of a »Didone« or Modernist/Romantic letter.

The last two date from 1924–26 and 1930, respectively; a period when Le Corbusier was active.

Other Romantic faces would include Didot and Walbaum.

However, you specifically mentioned that you are looking for a sans serif. Here the task is more difficult, but it is not inconceivable to pair a Romantic Modernist serif with a Geometric Modernist sans serif such as Futura (c. 1925) or even [[http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/adobe/memphis/|Memphis]] (c. 1929).

Better still, [[http://www.bertholdtypes.com/bq_library/AkzidenzGrotesk.html|Akzidenz Grotesk]] traces its geneology to the letterforms of Walbaum, and you may have an easier time harmonizing it with Le Corbusier.
