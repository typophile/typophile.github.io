EULA Data Collection: Which allow modifications and which do not.

January 11, 2007 • Miss Tiffany

Maybe you've read my first attempt at dissecting a EULA, The Case for User-friendly EULAs. Within the article there is a chart which Nathan Matteson and myself compiled. This chart was the first stab* and I'd really like to, someday, make a more complete version. For today I'm only interested in foundries which do or do not allow modifications. If they do allow modification how do they define it? Also, if you can provide a link directly to their EULA or at least a PDF (please make note if it is a PDF). Example(s): Foundry: P22 Modifications: Not Allowed Foundry: Adobe Modifications: Allowed Stipulation: Format conversion only, Non-commercial or in-house only. *First stab meaning there are errors so please don't follow it like scripture.
Here's my paragraph on modification...
  • Restrictions. You may not modify this font data with intent to resell or redistribute such data without Nick's Fonts's prior written consent. Additionally, you may not adapt, translate, reverse engineer, decompile, disassemble or create derivative works based on the Nick's Fonts font data without Nick's Fonts's prior written consent; you may, however, make modifications if said modifications are intended solely for your personal use or for use within your company, if your company has purchased this license. You may not sell PFRs, sIFRs or EOTs created with this font data to a third party.
