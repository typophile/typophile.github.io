The oft-used square glyph at the end of articles?

May 31, 2009 • Kiwi

I’ve been catching up on some typography, but one thing has eluded me; what is the glyph a lot of magazines tend to end their articles with?

Most tend to use a square, while some spruce it up with their own inventions, but from a typographer’s point of view, what exactly are they? A quirky idea or an ancient typographic convention?

FWIW, our publishing company calls them “end bugs.” We use everything from a simple square to a miniature version of the magazine’s or client’s logo.

Regarding the term “tombstone”:

“Tombstone” in letterpress news and magazine work was also used as a term for an advertisement that appeared regularly in issue after issue, with no changes. The form would be put away as each issue was killed, and hauled out for the next. After much repeated use the type might be re-set, but exactly as it looked earlier.
