No match at the moment, not even a name. I firstly thought as COS is a sub-brand of H&M, it could have been [[http://www.google.com/search?hl=en&source=hp&q=site:typophile.com/node+hm+ampersand|Ampersand]] (sample [[http://www.typesample.com/samples/Y9ehZ|here]]). HM Ampersand is very close to [[http://playtype.com/index.php?q=node/139|Du Nord]] designed by Jonas Hecksher. Reminds me a bit of [[http://commercialtype.com/typefaces/graphik|Graphik]] too. My guess would be (a very common usage with Scandinavian companies/designs), they may have commissioned a Northern Europe foundry/design firm like [[http://playtype.com|Playtype]], [[http://www.a2-type.co.uk|A2-Type]], [[https://monokrom.no|Monokrom]], [[http://www.schick-toikka.com|Schick Toikka]] or maybe Swiss (Lineto, Optimo, Grilli Type, Milieu Grotesque…) but I’m surely missing a lot. Found also 2 more ones, somehow similar: [[http://www.newrailalphabet.co.uk|New Rail Alphabet]] and [[http://www.a2-type.co.uk/html/regular_light.html|Regular]] both designed by A2-Type (their designers have also worked for Playtype).

Shame... Colophon... I even checked their website but only 'Aperçu' with the assumption it couldn't have been one of their typefaces as they almost all look a bit odd/quirky/hipster-ish (like Radim Pesko's) and your sample was not. My 2 cent now. I'm obsessed by typefaces but this one not. To me (but doesn't want to sound blase), it's "just" another re-interpretation of Helvetica (like Ampersand). Earmarks have been erased (/R leg, /a counter...) and faux-monospace treatment has been applied. For the rest, Graphik looks far better to my eyes.
