Another typography book advice for a student

July 29, 2009 • Bezier Abuser

Hello! I'm a student from Italy, who is developing a passion for typography. I've read many books on graphic design, and i recently bought an italian-only book: Caratteri e comunicazione visiva (Characters and visual communication) that officially introduced me into the vast world of type. I feel i can appreciate typography a lot more than i did before, but this book left me with lots of questions. I work as a freelance graphic designer, and I'm recently making a logo for an attorney and consultant studio: they wanted it simple, and we opted for a serif type-based logo. Before reading this book i wouldn't care about the chosen typeface, i would have used the first font that looked nice, let's say Cambria. But now i care more than i could think before. The problem is that this book teached me nothing about how to choose a typeface! And that's why i'm looking for another book to buy and read as soon as possible. I know that there isn't a magic formula, and i think i should train my sensibility towards typography more than looking for guidelines... but I have to start somewhere, haven't I? So I'm here, asking for your help, looking for books and ideas. P.S. from the title i would choose "Thinking with type" but I'm not 100% sure.
I will always recommend starting with 'Elements of Typographic Style', which has good information on how to approach font selection.
