Tattoo Typefaces

May 31, 2009 • Ed_Aranda

I need opinions on a good timeless typeface for a few lines of poetry that will be tattooed on a friend. Since this will be permanent, I’m treating the responsibility of choosing the typeface rather seriously. Here are the lines:

But fiction flaunts its grave deception, Flies its flags of grand illusion, Cast a glimpse of truth across my eyes, Then blames me for not anticipating What foreign ideas have danced across My very being ever since page one …

I know this could go a number of ways in terms of mood and personality. I’m currently considering a few Blackletter style typefaces for a more traditional tattoo feel - namely a free font (I know, yikes!) called “Cloister” from DaFont.com. But I am also considering Minion Pro Semi-Bold and Trajan Pro Bold. I like Trajan for this, except that all caps is a horrible idea for something with the expressive, musical quality of poetry.

I am probably looking for a serif font with a timeless appeal that has just enough “style” to make it stand out from the typical classic serif fonts. But I would love to hear any of your ideas or suggestions, regardless of whether they fit within these parameters.

Thanks! Ed

2009-05-31 07:26:09 • Chris Dean

If I was you I’d try not to agonize over picking a digital typeface and laying out your sample on screen. I think a more fruitful approach would be to find a good tattooist who knows / specializes in lettering, explaining what kind of “feel” you’re after, and have them work from your ideas. Skin is not paper is not the screen. And there are people who specialize in skin.

And whatever you do: that’s a lot of text; be sure you don’t go too small with it, especially if you’re considering a somewhat detailed (such as a seriffed) face.
