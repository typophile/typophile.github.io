Determining Regular and Bold Weights

August 23, 2010 • phrostbyte64

My latest project is to create regular and bold weights for an existing poster font. The completed family will be san serif for headline and display usage. I’ve compared weights of several san serif typefaces and there doesn’t seem to be any real solid rules concerning these weights.

So, how does one determine the weight of the regular verses bold weights?

A good place to start is by considering the ratio of stem width to space in “n”.

Some designers also consider stem width as a proportion of vertical height.

No formula will replace common sense and a good eye…< But is always good to know… Formula: Stem width of the letter “n” (10 pt Didot): ultra thin <0.2 thin 0.2-0.3 regular 0.3-0.4 semi bold 0.4-0.6 bold 0.6-0.85 ultra bold >0.85 (in milimeters)

2010-08-23 17:51:15 • JanekZ
I find it useful to keep ADA guidelines in mind when working out weights. No need to feel overly constrained by them but, 1:10 stroke width:cap height is the lightest allowable weight and 1:5 is the boldest. These work out to a light/thin and a medium/demibold.
