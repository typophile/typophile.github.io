Which typefaces do you find most readable (easy on the eye / legible) for novels?

April 16, 2010 • vgunn

I picked up one of my daughter’s independent reader novel and thumbed through the pages (Dark Hills Divide, Scholastic Press). After trudging by way through a couple of dense books, my eyes rejoiced! It was so airy and easy to read (not sure what the typeface is).

To my question, I am wondering which typefaces in novels you find the most readable.

There are scores of text faces that, properly set, make a novel or any other book easy to read. I would not be surprised that if in the books you found “dense”, the problem was not the choice of typeface, but typesetting and layout problems: text too small, not enough leading, lines too long, margins too narrow, etc. Maybe the quality of printing was bad as well.
