2007-03-18 23:20:23 • Miss Tiffany

Am I normal? i doubt it. there’s no such thing as “normal.”

maybe i have it all wrong, but to me , the term [[humanist]] refers to letters that are influenced by [[ductal logic]], whether they’re [[sans serif|sans]] or [[serif|serifed type]]. in [[calligraphy]], the [[humanist minuscule]] is a descendant of the [[carolingian minuscule]]. The humanist model of calligraphy is what influenced the earliest [[Roman]] ([[whiteletter]]) types, particularly those of [[Nicholas Jenson]]. The [[Vox-ATypI classification]] system uses the term humanist to refer to the [[Venetian]] model of type.

2007-03-18 23:42:13 • paul d hunt
Doesn’t sound wrong to me, Paul. But I rarely see “humanist” bandied about in regard to serifs (with the exception of typefaces inspired by Italian Renaissance type), maybe because many more serifs are “humanist” by your ductal-logic definition than are sans- and slab-serifs. I’d guess that this comes from the early slab-serifs and sans-serifs not being too concerned with ductus as far as I know until Johnston and Gill in the early 20th c. and Frutiger in 1968. So to say “humanist” doesn’t separate a group out as much for serifs as it does for other type genres.
