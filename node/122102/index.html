Pickworth Old Style by Red Rooster

December 19, 2014 • donshottype

Red Rooster has a font that it calls Pickworth Old Style Larger scale character set attached. More info http://www.identifont.com/show?2DOO When we look at the glyphs we see the multitude of nodes that is characteristic of auto-traced letters. I appreciate that the original might have been a “rugged” design, that today we would call antique, grunge or distressed. I don’t recognize the font by its original name from about a century ago. My searches for the term “Pickworth” online and in my few reference books turns up nothing identical except the Red Rooster digital font. I know of a fair number of similar fonts, such as Post – digitized in a slick version by Nick Curtis as Saturday Morning Toast https://www.myfonts.com/fonts/nicksfonts/saturday-morning-toast/ but I am trying track down this particular design. I would welcome any suggestions as to the old time font name and its source.

Thanks. Don

Previous discussion of similar “rugged” fonts http://typophile.com/node/115257 Unfortunately no match to Red Rooster’s Pickworth. Don

Dieter Steffmann has a version called Antique No. 14: http://moorstation.org/typoasis/designers/steffmann/samples/a/antique14.htm

Nick Curtis has a cleaner version called Snappy Patter: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/nicksfonts/snappy-patter-nf/

Pete Dako also created a version in 1991 called Speedball (available as shareware from numerous sources).

All are evidently based on a typeface called Antique No. 14 featured in Dan X. Solo’s “Rustic and Rough-Hewn Alphabets” book.
