I believe that if the URL is on an external (or maybe even an internal page) and it links to a valid page on typophile.com Google will follow and index that URL. This explains our fontmagazine.com address appearing in Google. The solution is to disable domain “masing” and make sure all subdomains are redirected to your main domain.

Thomas, thanks for that link. I don’t think this practice is a good idea. Again and again, the search results are cluttered with duplicate content: one specific wiki page or one profile page gets listed over and over again with changing subdomain ‘prefixes’ – that’s annoying.

And are those URIs supposed to stay? I mean, when people who are unaware of these subtleties want to cross-reference threads, look up the address via Google & Co and then insert some ‘rainbow snot birthday’ – what will happen to these links in the future?

To me, it sounded quite plausible what Jeff Croft contributed to Roger Johansson’s recent article ‘Make sure your website works with or without www’: There should always, always, always be only one URI for any given resource. […] Canonical URIs are good. Multiple URIs for the same resource are bad. Period.
