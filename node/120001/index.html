Reviewing the Ability of the Printer

With hydrographics printing today, consumers can achieve a textured pattern on objects that could have become a major challenge for providers years before. This trend provides printers with the ability today to present consumers with mass produced art in which they can place on objects such as their automobiles, motorcycle, and golf carts. While the disassembly of the chosen object is challenging, the process needed to present the consumer with the high quality pattern isn’t.

The Steps for Hydrodipping

After the chosen object is disassembled, the printer will package it to protect all section in which they do not want to pattern to appear. The primer is applied after any bumps or damage is addressed. If the printer doesn’t perform detailed body work on these products, the consumer should acquire these services before approaching their selected printer.

The next step in the process for the commercial hydrodipping company is to apply the base coat, which represents the primary color in the overall design. The printer ensures that this coating is precise and evenly distributed. The printer will also apply an adehesive film with this base coat to allow the ink to conform to the object more effectively.

The Final Step

The printer will place the ink film into the hydrodipping pool to allow it to dissolve. As it dissolves, the ink is balanced throughout the water in the correct pattern to ensure an effective application. This process allows the three dimensional design to come to life. The printer will inspect the object properly to ensure that the coating is adequate and doesn’t present the consumer with flaws. As this ink dries, further inspections are made to ensure the highest level of quality.

After all the ink dries completely, the printer applies a clear coat to each part in which the pattern was applied. This adds a protective covering that will prevent flaws through wear and tear. It is performed in a high quality manner to ensure that there isn’t any bubbling on the surface.
