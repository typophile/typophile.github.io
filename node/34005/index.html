Printing problems with a PDF

May 24, 2007 • Ramiro Espinoza

I have a weird problem with a PDF. I made a specimen with InDesign featuring a typeface I made. When I print the PDF from Acrobat all the letters are overlapped on top of each other. Scanned page: http://www.re-type.com/notaweblog/images/print-caca.jpg The PDF look perfect on screen and if I print from InDesign there is no problem. The embedding permits of the fonts are set to 'everything is allowed'. Apparently the problem is with inkjet printers (only with this font). Laser printers seem to print the specimen with no problem. Thanks for any feedback.
Have you checked to make sure your drivers for your inkjet printer are up to date? It seems like a tracking issue with the print head. The signals from the computer aren't translating properly to the printer and that's usually a driver issue.

I think it has more to do with the file, but most likely the embedded font. It almost looks like if the sidebearings and spaces have disappeared or something. I don't think it's the printer itself, if the tracking of the printhead was off, the horizontal lines would not print either? Also the glyphs seem to be printed non-distorted, just overlapped. The fact that it only happens with inkjets, probably has something to do with postscript. Have you already tried converting the font to outlines, and print again? I know it is not a solution, but it will let you know if the error is in the font.
