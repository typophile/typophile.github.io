2007-05-06 03:52:00 • david h

The small type in the 3qs example is Cheltenham Old Style and Cheltenham Wide (don’t know of a digital version). The large type is a typical wood type grotesque. The word “CANADIAN” is Copperplate Gothic.

Some of the small type near the top in the 3DU sample is DeVinne. The only digital version I could find is this one, but it’s apparently based on a large wood type cut, not the small metal cut in your sample. I’m pretty sure the rest of the small type is an old metal font called Grasset. “A.R.R.L.” is Copperplate Gothic.

Regarding the last one, I’m also the one who identified it for you on the [T-D] list. This is about as close as you’re going to get to the original metal Parsons. There doesn’t appear to be any ITC-style design modernization.
