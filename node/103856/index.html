It’s a variation on the famous & by Caslon, so it looks very familiar: Ampersands are the place for creative play by font makers, so it could well be from a font – if so, probably a high contrast thick & thin. It would take some time to go through the candidates to check for a precise match. The & symbol is also a popular art project. Perhaps one of the cognoscenti with a good memory could pronounce on this one. BTW, ampersands have been discussed before in typophile forums. You might enjoy browsing through: http://typophile.com/search/node/ampersand Don
