If you can find a copy, this book is worth getting: Venetian Printing Nicolas Jenson and the Rise of the Roman Letterform by M Lowery. It serves double-duty as a thoughtful history and a specimen of George Abram's Venetian type (George also contributed an essay). The text is in English and Danish.

Unfortunately, copies online appear to be selling for $250 or more. I found my copy by chance in a second-hand bookshop on Evanston, Illinois, for a lot less than that.

I like George's Augereau (Garamond) and Venetian types. They're both very clean, elegant book faces.
