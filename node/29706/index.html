If you want something a little more academic, you might want to look at some of Daniel Kahneman’s work: he was a co-winner of the 2002 Nobel Prize for Economics. Not specifically “marketing,” but as a psychologist, he looks at how human behaviour rationalizes economic outcomes.

IMO, one of the neatest thing about his work isn’t just that (surprise, surprise!) human beings aren’t rational, but how they aren’t rational, and how that impacts judgements and subsequent actions. Not exactly a light read, but pretty interesting stuff.
