Looks like a version of the circa 1900 DeVinne design. Not to be confused with current digital fonts sold under the name DeVinne, which are a different design. The DeVinne font was very popular and many widths and weights were produced. For a digital variation of condensed De Vinne see Hawthorne: http://www.identifont.com/similar?6EF A regular width version of DeVinne was recently digitized by Jeff Levine as Publication: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/jnlevine/publication/ Also De Vinne from wooden type fonts: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/woodentypefonts/de-vinne/ The De Vinne typeface has been discussed many times before at typophile, including: http://typophile.com/node/104584 Don
