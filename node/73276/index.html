August 17, 2010 • Frans

As the topic subject suggests, the closest matches I could find for this font were Bradley Pro by Profonts and Fyne Fish by Nick Curtis. Now, I would be inclined to say that the latter would be the best match, but the typeface used in this logo (which belongs to the gothic industrial band Lestat) was used by them since the early 90s, and both Bradley Pro and Fyne Fish NF were designed and released in the noughties.

[img:sites/default/files/old-images/Lestat-band-logo_5532.jpg]

Here is the cover of their album Grave Desires which was released in the early 90s.

[img:sites/default/files/old-images/lestat - Cópia_4388.jpg]

Does ANYBODY have ANY idea what this font’s name is?

Bradley is an old typeface, the aforementioned are just newer digitizations of it (there are others too). Quote: »BRADLEY is an 1895 typeface credited to Joseph Warren Phinney (1848-1934). It was based on lettering by Henry William Bradley (1869-1962) for a Christmas cover of Harper’s Bazaar magazine. Bradley was the basis for logotypes such as Firestone and Ketel One, and has also been widely used by Disney on theme park signage.«
