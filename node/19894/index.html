New logo sports event

May 12, 2006 • Ali Haan

Hi typophiles,

I am in the organizing committee of the Dutch Indoorrowing Championships (in Dutch: NK Indoorroeien). For those who have never heard of indoorrowing, please don’t laugh. Since the beginning of the event 16 years ago, the number of participating sporters has grown every year (1400 last edition). The event takes place in December in Amsterdam.

At the moment a friend and I are redesigning the house style of the event. This also means designing a new logo. The current logo exists of the word “NK Indoorroeien” set in Helvetica Neue. I think this logo is poorly outdated and doesn’t fit the event (anymore).

I know how hard it is to design a good logo. We have been busy drawing for a long time now and it’s time to make some final decisions. We decided that the logo will exist of an abstract drawing of a rower and the word “NK Indoorroeien”. I need your opinion on the last part, the word “NK Indoorroeien”, which is a tough word to deal with. First of all the word is quite long. Second, there seems to be a problem with the two r’s, because each r belongs to another part of the word.

The keywords for the event are: professional, dynamic, fun. My last thought was to keep the idea of the old logo, but to use a different font. I tried a lot of different fonts, but I really liked John Sans, because it is both tight and friendly. I really like the endings of the “K” and “r”. It is almost like a splash of water.

What do you think?

John Sans has the qualities you were looking for and the different weights split up the words, you might need to kern the lighter one a bit tighter. However without the accompanying illustration it’s hard to guess if the logo as a whole is going to work. You could explore using two colours or tints to break the words, would it be wrong to break it into two lines with indoor and roeien aligning on the left with NK outside the alignment. NK indoor NK roeien The second NK is just to align the two words, I tried word spaces but they seem not to be permitted.
