Originally designed in 1894 by Linn Boyd Benton, Century had countless of versions and variations for every type of print technology (starting with Century Expanded by Morris Fuller Benton) and that not counting the ones with different names (like Benton Modern cited by Don). They differing in details like sharpness of serifs (Century expanded) or shape of part of numbers (Century Old Style), and hoping that someone at some point digitised the exact same cut (optical size and all) used for these pre-digital numbers is a leap of faith. Finding it, based on such a poor sample is very unlikely. Anyway, here are the numerals of all the typefaces named Century on the biggest font retailer there is: http://myfonts.us/td-D4K6rB
