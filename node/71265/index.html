2010-06-12 00:10:52 • Tomi from Suomi
Bank Gothic is, as its name suggests, a conservative style of sans serif. Even though published in 1930, widely interpreted as an art deco face and oft compared with the genuinely modernist City, it is a late 19th century throwback style, as attested by the narrow centre stroke of E, the diagonally chamfered terminals of the “rounded” letters, and its lack of a lower case. So a good text face to complete an authentic retro look would be another Benton revival of a vertically stressed “geometric” style, Bodoni, or one of his Centuries.
