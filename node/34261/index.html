Font Sugestion: performance type

June 6, 2007

I’ve stumbled upon an interesting brief: typesetting a translation of Alain Ehrenberg’s Le Culte de la Performance to portuguese. My inicial thoughts are making the whole thing as high performance as a book can get* (within out budget constraints, of course). Except that brings up the obvious problem of what typeface (or combination of typefaces) to use. What would you guys sugest? (lets not get into a readability/legibility discussion though)

Should I go for a sans that screams ’20s modernism? or should I go for a good serif (arguably more readable, therefore better performance?) that we’re all used to seeing in books everywhere? I’m having trouble deciding, perhaps one of you can swing my decision either way.

It’d be around 260 pages; so not too long, but not too short either.

  • Though I could be swayed the opposite direction and go for as low-performance as we can get to emphasize the books content.

Unless it’s mostly illustrations, that’s too long for a sans.
