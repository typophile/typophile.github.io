Contact Information Layout

January 29, 2008 • AndrewSipe

I'm working on a piece that has multiple levels of contact information. The largest that I've come across is set up this way: Name* - Bold Title* - Small Caps Bold Program Title - Medium Building/Department Address Address Address Phone Number* Internal Phone Number* Fax Number* Internal Fax Number* Email* Web Address *Most common contact information. Structurally, what would be a good way to layout this out. I've used tables in the past to align all the phone numbers and email address, where I'd use the titles "phone", "dsn" (internal phone), "fax" , "email" would be in the right column and the numbers and address in the left column. I tend to Bold the Name and Small Caps Bold the Title everything else is Medium or Normal weights. Also, what's the norm for laying out phone numbers and emails. I tend to used the (000) 000-0000 and emails are always lowercase user@site.com. Is there any tracking standards? I hope that isn't too confusing. Thanks.
Are we talking business cards here? That is a lot of lines, and I would work out some way to minimize them. Can you get the address down to two lines ... one for the street related bits, and the other for city, state, code, country, etc. Phone numbers can also be placed two or more per line. As for whether to align or otherwise, that is a design decision, not something you want to standardize. Sometimes it will work best aligning the second phone number on a line to something, other times, not so much. As for phone numbers, I tend to use your example, but many designers are now omitting the brackets and/or hyphens. I have seen center dots replacing the hyphens as well.
