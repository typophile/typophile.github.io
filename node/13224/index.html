Dot accent is a quite common diacritical mark in some European languages. It is used in Maltese (over ‘c’, ‘g’, ‘z’), Lithuanian (over ‘e’), Polish (over ‘z’) and Turkish (they distinct between i/İ — ı/I — i with and without dot). And actually, the dot as a diacritical mark can be found in every language using Latin script over ‘i’. More at Project Diacritics

Catalan (u/c & l/c I and U for diaeresis and u/c & l/c L for dot) and Turkish (u/c & l/c O and U for diaeresis and u/c I for dot) use both but not on the same character and Hungarian uses the diaeresis (umlaut) and double acute (Hungarian umlaut).
