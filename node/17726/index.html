nick cottrell

The low-end HP printers are perfectly adequate as office printers. As long as you don’t expect to do anything more than check spacing, overall color and alignment of your typefaces, it will work fine. Just don’t expect the printer to accurately reflect the subtleties of your outlines: you’ll need a Postscript printer for that, and you’re going to have to pay eight to ten times as much to get a true Postscript printer.

2006-02-06 18:31:22 • istitch

Actually, I have an HP 1200, and I think it does better than you’d expect for such a low price. For one, It seems to have true 1200 dpi resolution, which is pretty important for type. For two, it has a lot less gain than other lasers I’ve tried. I think the 1020 has the same engine, but it’s hard to be sure.

Laser vs inkjet is harder to quantify. Both distort type considerably, but inkjets have gotten a lot better in recent years. One real advantage of lasers is that they work on plain papers. To proof type on an inkjet, you have to use glossy. It also helps to set a very high resolution and unidirectional printing in the driver (both of which slow down the printing quite a bit).

I am very pleased with my Epson 2200. In the letter-size category, the R800 ($400 MSRP) has similar specs. I wouldn’t be surprised if the R220 ($99 MSRP) produces adequate results as well. It has a bigger drop size (3 pl vs 1.5 for the R800) and dye based inks (so they’ll fade), but the low end Epsons are pretty amazing for their price.

Probably the single biggest thing you have to worry about is gain. If you have a high-gain printer and design your type to look good there, then it will be really weak and anemic when printed offset.

2006-02-06 18:39:55 • raph

LJ 1020 for proofing type: a clear no!

The datasheet PDF – just google the for the printer model and select the hp site – says: “Resolution: 1200 dpi effective output quality” which has a footnote: “600 x 600 dpi with HP REt and FastRes 1200 technology”. Which is not real 1200 dpi.

Always be cautious with notions like “1200 dpi quality”, regardless of brand. Same with Xerox or IBM.

I bought a LJ 1320 recently which gives good results at little costs. True 1200 dpi and double side printing which I haven’t tried yet. (But my impression is that the printing of the 1200 was a bit finer. And the quality of the 2100 is still unmatched.)
