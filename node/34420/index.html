That’s a normal Roman brickstamp; looks first century AD to me. Ancient Roman lettering doesn’t necessarily have proper serifs unless it was carved in stone, in the monumental style, by a professional inscription cutter, between maybe 100 BC and AD 400. Stamp and die cutters used all sorts of stroke terminations, including the traditional sans serif forms.
