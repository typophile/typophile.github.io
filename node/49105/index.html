Math operators in italic fonts

September 5, 2008 • Randy

Discussion of math symbols is all the rage. I happen to be working on the italics for a non-math font. Meaning it's not used for setting complex math formulas, but for casual usage (inline and tables) it does include designed symbols from the Mac Roman math set. The font includes oldstyle, lining, and smallcap numerals in tabular and proportional variants with corresponding operators. In the italic, should I italicize the math symbols for casual usage defined above? If so, are there ones that shouldn't get italicized? Thanks!
As a guy with a math degree, I can state with confidence that italic math symbols are pretty much useless, and a waste of your time to draw and tweak. For all "normal" mathematics, there is no reason to set operator symbols in italic.
2008-09-05 02:55:35 • JBlock
I now slant ALL the math operators in my italic fonts, on the principal that these are, as Randy says, non-math fonts. That way, if perchance the characters are used in italic text -- as, for instance, the plus sign may be in company names "... + Associates" -- they don't look wrong. Or if a tid-bit such as E=mc² is mentioned, then the equals sign looks appropriate. If compositors wish to use these fonts for simple equations etc., they will know exactly where they stand, and that all the upright operators are in the roman fonts. Historically, italic fonts were used with roman figures. Now that italic figures are available, why stop there?

Fantastic. I can honestly say there is zero chance this will be used to set a math text. Another reason to include them is for opentype equivalency.
