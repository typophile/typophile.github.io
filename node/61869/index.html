Logo for an online yellow pages kind of website

September 10, 2009 • mikeb7

hi guys!

I’m currently designing a logo for a small company which produces both software as well as hardware (terminals) for a yellow pages kind of service called “city exhibition” (translated) which they sell to small cities and townships.

The solution I came up with focuses on the information and location aspect of the product (i for information and the arrow kind of thing for the location) However it still incorporates the aspects of towns or cities, although be it subtle. The arrow is shaped to reminiscence of coat of arms and the dot on the i is shaped like a house to represent the idea of small businesses (main customers) adding their data to the service.

I would love to hear some feedback on this thing (especially on typo and size relations to the symbol)

I didn’t get a house from the tittle (i-dot), but an arrow pointing upwards, which conflicts with the overall arrow pointing downwards. That would be my main critique point – conflicting movements/directions. Oh and, the 2 fonts don’t work together ideally – they’re almost the same, but not quite (or are they supposed to be the same? What’s up with the “S"s then?). Also they might be just a hint too techno-ish*, but maybe that’s what you want. Other than that I agree it’s coming along quite nicely.

  • Just a word of caution that this “cold”/angular look seems to be on its way out; and I’d guess you don’t want this to look dated in a year or two. But maybe others would disagree.
