PDF-ervescence

August 24, 2010 • Nick Job

I don’t know about you but I’ve downloaded some pretty fantastic pdfs in the short time that computers have been a way of life.

It is a source of disappointment that Hoefler & Frere-Jones, among others, don’t make samples available in pdf format. I understand why they don’t (there are enough threads on this forum about intellectual property theft etc.) but I find some of the pdfs offered on some websites border on sublime perfection.

Clearly there are berzillions of pdfs out there but, if you had to, what would you say were your favourite type-related pdf downloads and, if it needs stating (which I very much doubt), why?

Let me kick off proceedings with with this beauty from the United Kingdom’s Department of Transport.

After my last release I decided to stop producing big PDF specimens because very few people download them at all, so they have little impact on font sales. It makes more sense to put my effort into the little 720x360 pixel specimens because that’s what get people to buy a font. Other type designers download the PDFs, but they don’t buy type!
