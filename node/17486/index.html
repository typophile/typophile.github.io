Letraset Serif Gothic Heavy Vs I.T.C Serfic Gothic Heavy

January 24, 2006 • Nietzsche

Hi,

I’m working a project where a client wanted to use Serif Gothic Heavy. After creating a few text samples, the client said they font was different than what they wanted- particularly the “E” and “W.”

Going through my old letraset catalogs, I did notice that Serif Gothic Heavy has an alternate “E” shaped like a horseshoe, and the “N” and “W” were different from the characters in the I.T.C Serif Gothic family.

Is there any version of Serif Gothic Heavy that is true to the letraset font?

If I’m not mistaken, I don’t think there is a digital version of Serif Gothic with the alternate characters. Given the fact that ITC has recently released ITC Avant Garde Gothic and ITC Bookman with all the extra characters, it seems possible they may do the same with some of the others like Serif Gothic.
2006-01-24 05:31:31 • Mark Simonson

Last night after reading your post, I checked out the old 20 lb. ITC black specimen book to review all alternate characters for Serif Gothic Heavy. What Mark said is true about ITC releasing AGG with all characters, but I think it would be a long while before they do anything with Serif Gothic (it’s not seen as much as their other more popular faces).

The big question is what do you need it for? A logo wordmark or for a display headline? You may have a few options if it is for a logo, but less if they intend to use it consistently for display heads. Let us know, maybe we can come up with more suggestions for you.
