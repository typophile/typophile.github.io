Redesign font ideas?

July 23, 2007 • LHait

We are looking for a new font for our consumer parenting magazine. We currently use Spira as our primary serif body font but dislike that it looks too hand-cut and has an overall folksy feeling. What we are looking for is a cleaner and more open font, but still based in classic typography. It is also important for it to have a fairly extensive family and includes beautiful ligatures. We love the look of Sterling, by Hoefler & Frere-Jones in the AIGA Design Archive, but it is unavailable for purchase. Could anyone suggest a similar font? The ones we have found and are considering thus far are Whitman, Miller, and Eason. Thanks so much in advance!

Perpetua has certain similarities to Sterling. Indeed, Sterling may have used Perpetua as an inspiration. Sterling looks more ‘refined’ than Perpetua IMO. It is gorgeous.

I love Perpetua but I have to caution you against it if you’re using InDesign. The optical and metric kerning pairs are pretty dodgy, especially in the Italic. Since InD doesn’t have the kerning tables option (grrrr!) you’d have to go in and tweak the font itself. I just used it for a book and had to do a lot of manual kerning, quite time consuming.

I have the Adobe Perpetua from 1998 - it’s possible the Linotype version is better - unless they’re the same.

Sterling is pretty - it’s reminding me of something else - tip of my tongue… Whitman’s great too.

Scaling is an issue for Perpetua. It gets a bit thin at small sizes. So it may be worthwhile, if you’re after the effect of finesse, to opt for a typeface that has some size-specific variants. That gives you more flexibility in your layouts.
