FONT FOR OLD MUNICIPAL SAN FRANCISCO RAILWAY LOGO

November 16, 2014 • bfingeroth

Hi,

Can anyone identify the font (or recommend something similar if its hand drawn) in this logo for the San Francisco Municipal Railway?

Thanks.

It most certainly is hand drawn. If an existing typeface was the model, someone could come up with a match. In the meantime, looking for “railway” at MyFonts, brings some interesting results. Perhaps unsurprisingly, Western Railway (http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/jnlevine/western-railway/) has a similar flavour to the words (it needs some condensing for “Municipal”), while something from Greater Albion Typefounders (http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/gatf/birmingham-new-street/ http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/gatf/great-bromwich/) could give you the |F|.

Lettering circa 1900. The winged M, heavy letters and big serifs have some similarity to Heirloom Artcraft Black http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/baselinefonts/heirloom-artcraft/ compare to a slick lighter version of Artcraft http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/ascender/artcraft-pro/ Don
2014-11-16 11:10:25 • donshottype
Some of the serifs, and especially the |A|s have a Latin shape (http://www.myfonts.com/search/latin/), which, I think, make them optically better suited to be placed on an arc.
