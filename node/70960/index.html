2010-07-08 14:37:26 • RosaRosa
Does it seem to anyone else that the R may have been re-drawn? It looks almost like the leg runs up through the counter, just a bit. The E seems to be the main letter ruling out Stymie, and Rockwell and Memphis have the right E, but neither has a matching R, even though Rockwell's is closer. The width of the E could be due to using condensed letters mixed with normal width, because the Œ glyph looks normal width. Another thought that occurs to me is that the French foundry may have 'adapted' Rockwell and altered some letters to avoid the appearance of completely copying the typeface. After all, wasn't Memphis just the slightly modified Linotype copy of Rockwell? Even Stymie was supposed to have been based on an early Rockwell version, according to Mac McGrew's book. - Mike Yanega
2010-07-08 17:27:39 • bowfinpw
Hmm, reading the entries in Jaspert's book, seems to indicate that the first version of Memphis was Stempel's in 1929, which pre-dates Monotype's Rockwell in 1934. Linotype's Memphis was 1935-6, so it's a bit ambiguous, maybe. Another possibility is Beton, which Jaspert says was released by Bauer in 1931-36. Beton has the right E and has the same R as one of two in the typeface. Shown on p.23 of 1991 reprint edition of "Encyclopaedia of Type Faces" - Mike Yanega
