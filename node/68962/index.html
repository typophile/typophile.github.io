It's "faux-Thai" because, obviously, it's not a "real" Thai font :-) (See wikipedia.org/Thai_script for what that looks like, or google "thai script" images.) The idea is similar to faux Chinese (those awful restaurant chopsticks fonts) and faux Arabic (and of these, I've seen a couple very good ones!): use the general shapes of another script to form Latin characters with. First time I see a Thai one; done very well!
