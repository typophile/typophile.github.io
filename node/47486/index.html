Eben, perhaps what Sii hints at is that its best to define your brief before you pick software configurations. Since you won’t “officially” (hehe…) define your brief at Reading until November/December, there is no need to lock yourself into to anything now. It isn’t a good idea to try to “start early” on your project anyway, as things may change once you hit the ground in the department and see all of the resources available.

Besides, who knows what technological changes might come to the field between now and whenever you need to implement what you need to implement?

I licensed Vista and Office while I was writing my first essay over the Christmas break. I would have waited longer, but there was no other way to typeset Indic fonts properly on my Mac (which was the topic of my essay!). I didn’t use my PC partition again until June/July, when it was time to produce the font in VOLT.

But if I had done an Arabic font instead of a Devanagari one, I might have:

  1. licensed a copy of InDesign ME instead of InDesign
  2. started in VOLT earlier, since I could have properly tested my font then on my Mac side in InDesign ME along the way (instead of on Windows in Word 2007). This wasn’t an option for me, since there is no “InDesign South Asia” or what have you.

Paul also did a Devanagari, and he has XP. The version of VOLT that you need in order to write for the latest Indic shaping engine works on XP, too. I guess you’d just have to test on Vista as well as in Office 2007 on XP… you could ask him right now at the conference.

As a Mac User who has always worked on secondary PC as well, I must add that I don’t understand this “oh no, let’s stay with XP” business from Windows users at all. Vista is a better OS, at least every way that I look at it. Which—I must say—is from a type POV. That is the future, and I don’t want to go back to previous OS problems.
