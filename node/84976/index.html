Maybe one of the Adobe guys can explain it in more detail, but I believe you could theoretically put more than 256 glyphs in a Type1 font, but since the "typical Western encodings“ were based on 8 bit, those additional glyphs would not be accessible to apps (like Freehand) expecting those 8 bit encodings. So especially Western fonts only used 256 glyphs until the format died. And just for the record: If you want to compare Type1 to OpenType, don't forget that most of the credit should go to the TrueType format: http://opentype.info/blog/2010/07/31/opentype-myths-explained/
