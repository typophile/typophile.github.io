Large Fry's: A work in progress

February 13, 2008 • blank

I use ITC New Baskerville as a titling/display face quite often at work. New Baskerville is gorgeous, but the hairlines get too big at larger sizes, so I’m working on a Baskerville for large sizes. I am working from a scan of the 48-point Stephenson Blake Fry’s Baskerville presented on page 34 of The Western Heritage of Type Design, under the assumption that the type was cast with new matrices made from the original punches—I’ll check up on that one after graduation. Because I’m still figuring the whole font-creation thing out, after the glyphs are drawn I am comparing them to Bitstream’s very fabulous Fry’s to spot my mistakes.

2008-02-13 05:51:04 • ebensorkin
Most of the verticals are straight—All the Fry’s designs are like that—but there are a few subtle curves left that I’m working in. I blew some stuff up and checked out the top serifs and they’re a little off, but not too much; I think that the issue is that my hairlines are a little too narrow, which is making everything else look that much thicker. I also need to bump my cap height a little bit, right now it’s still too close to being proportioned for text.
