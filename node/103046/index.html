Hi Folks, This old movie title type is eluding me. The closest I found was Nick Curtis' version of the Saturday Evening Post type that he called "Saturday Morning Toast". It's at least from a typeface older than the movie, but it's not a great match. The rounded tops of the R and B should be good earmarks, but this may never have been digitized. I looked in the McGrew book, skimmed the Solotype Catalog, and even looked in a Photo-Lettering catalog. Anyone have any ideas? Thanks, - Mike[img:sites/default/files/old-images/RebelGirls_6137.gif]
The tops of the R and B are similar to [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/mti/perpetua/?refby=bowfin|Perpetua]], but that's almost all that seems similar. I think that's why I thought this looked familiar at first, but clearly not the same type. - Mike

The uppercase largely matches a typeface called Book Extra Bold Italic, appearing in the Quick Brown Fox Co. filmtype catalog. It looks like a variant of Bookman.

It’s LSC Book, which used to be part of the ITC library. There’s no authorized digital version as far as I know. Now, LSC Book wasn’t released until around 1970, so you might think that would rule it out. However, the film was originally titled Cuban Rebel Girls when it was released. In a later reissue of the film, the title was changed to Assault of the Rebel Girls. I don’t have a date for that, but judging by the type it was after 1970. (More info here and here.)
