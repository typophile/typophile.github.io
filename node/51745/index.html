In copy-heavy newsprint design, I often find indented paragraphs for body copy are a visual relief. However the last publication we redesigned, the editor vetoed them. I wouldn’t use them for the intro paragraphs though, or the subheads - that’s a bit of overkill IMHO. Watch out for the too heavily condensed type too, that can be difficult to read.
2008-11-17 00:03:59 • Petar Belic

Paragraph indents serve as a visual clue for the beginning of a new paragraph WHEN NO OTHER CLUES ARE PRESENT. Thus, leading paragraphs, isolated blocks, etc do not require, and should not have, indentations; beneath subheads, no indentation.

“make Millions and Make Change!” appears to be a title; itals?

I have no way of telling if these pages all belong to the same publication. If they do, please be consistent with end-of-article signatures. The diamond in the first page is OK, but if you don’t use it throughout, you’ll find readers wondering where the end of article is.

I agree about the condensed face.

Why “Web developers,” but “website”?

Run the text past an editor once more: “As a buyer…, understanding…” is flapping around. “understanding” is not the “buyer.”
