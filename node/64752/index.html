November 26, 2009 • Peter Ainsworth

This is a book from the Central Saint Martins Archive. As i say in the title it is a book by Jonas Kortens. Titled Incunabula and Old books and was printed in 1743.

The current archive lecturer didn’t know much about this book other then the details i have given here. Despite its poor condition its really beautiful and really worth a look. If anybody can translate any of the content that would be much appreciated or any information at all about the blackletter type would also be helpful.

If not just enjoy these images of a beautiful old book. (more images here http://www.flickr.com/photos/iiwekei/sets/72157622880237506/)

This is a description of a travel to the Holy Land and its neighbors. And this is a rouhg line by line translation of the tilepage:

Jonas Kortens' Former bookseller at Altona (= now part of Hamburg, Germany) Travel To the former Holy But now For seventeen hundred years Under the curse lying Land Also to Egypt, Mount Lebanon Syria and Mesopotamia Written as seen by himself And In this second Edition With two supplements Augmented Published by the author Halle Printed by Joh. Christian Grunert, 1743
