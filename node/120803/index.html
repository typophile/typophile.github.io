2014-10-24 13:50:38 • donshottype
Looking closely at the numerals I see a double-decker Tuscan with curls. Blunt sans serif at one end of the section and Tuscan fishtail split end terminating on one side with a curl at the other end; or an angled Ionic column effect from having two curls, with the curl on one side flipped up, at the other end. The two sections are stacked. Could help in tracking down substitutes. Variations: Don
2014-10-24 15:14:41 • donshottype

Found many Tuscan with the same “fishtails” ornaments but most of the time, they come with no digits and don’t have these snail/spiral/ionic serifs/finials/terminals. Most of the time, they’re tagged/referenced as ‘[[http://www.myfonts.com/search/tag%3Acircus|circus]]’ and sometimes the fishtail decorated part is available separately so maybe transplanted to ionic serifs one? Half fishtail, half ionic but 100% Franken-font. Makes sense? Another one, quite nice is [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/typerepublic/carmen-fiesta|Carmen Fiesta]] using the [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/typerepublic/carmen-fiesta/regular/glyphs.html#glyphs/563337/638|alternate digits]] (a bit disappointing though considering the rest of the letters).

Some references: [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/fontmesa/apple-pie|Apple Pie]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/fontmesa/gold-standard|Gold Standard]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/flat-it/grandes-vacances|Grandes Vacances]]

For the game of making a patch up font, Carmen Fiesta and portions of Gold Standard have some tweekable stuff. Unfortunately, in a case like this its more effective to create the right modules from scratch and assemble the numbers from them. The same modules could be used to build an alphabet starting with the basic letter-forms of a high contrast font, such as a heavy Bodoni.
BTW here are a couple of split segment fonts that catch a little of the flavor of the design Grandes Vacances http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/flat-it/grandes-vacances/ and Mademoiselle http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/flat-it/mademoiselle/ Don
