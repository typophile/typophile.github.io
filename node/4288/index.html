Custom Art Company

April 14, 2004 • dylan

I need your feedback on this logo, as I'm about to take Custom Art Company from a fun little side gig to a full time business. I have done one short print run with this logo, but I'm willing to make changes. I'm about to begin designing a new website and make a second print run for design promotion.

The Custom wordmark carries a somewhat heavy burden: I design for print and web, illustrate, and, like you, have this fetish for typography, particularly hand-lettered type. I like the wordmark as a solution, and I want to convey a mark that is strong, not too flashy, and shaped a little out of the ordinary. Swelling with pride, potential, but not bloated. I wanted something that instantly said, "This is not created from a stock typeface (or one that's instantly recognizable)". At the same time, I wanted a refined, clean, ordered finish on the thing.

So this is where the logo stands currently (plain, with full name, and with URL). As I mentioned, I do have some equity in it, but not much at this point. Thoughts? Thanks, everyone.

2004-04-16 17:56:20 • andrew_baker
Dylan-

I'm glad you're running this through the gamit even after you've done a short print run.

One of the biggest strengths of this mark is the hand lettering; you're able to achieve a definite sense of independence. I don't think the letterforms swell too much. In fact, I think you're on to something unique by juxtaposing crisp edges with somewhat organic forms. Every curved line seems to be balanced by a straight one. I would definitely not warp it all or angle it all. Having both is what balances it and makes it custom.

Keeping with the theme of independence, "CUSTOM" by itself seems the natural choice. Its not as descriptive verbally, but I believe it conveys the boldest iconic message of the three. Alone, it should work at smaller sizes and in more contexts.

On the other hand, you're still left with the dilemna of not explaining enough about your company through the name. However, I think the mark is strong enough that it would be worth your time to find creative design solutions to design your way out of this problem. Perhaps the design promotion and website could be designed in such a way to strategically identify Custom Art Co's various services.

Custom Art Co., Custom Art Company, or simply Custom? Consistency in branding yourself through naming could be dicey. Audibly, Custom Art Co. has the best ring to it. I would guess that Custom Art Company is what will be on the books, and then CUSTOM is most interesting graphically by itself.

Since your website is www.customartco.com, I would think you'd want your customers to think of your company by that name. Perhaps you could have "Custom Art Co" written out in addition to your "CUSTOM" logo. "Custom Art Co" could shy away from being a logo element and become part of the introductory text of your design promotions and website.

Overall, I'm very impressed by the mark "CUSTOM" makes. It'd be interesting to see it scaled down, scaled up, reversed, and applied to a couple of promo peices.
