>> The idea of recognizing words by "word shape" seems so silly to me That's only because he hasn't studied reading. It surprised me too when I found out, but an open mind and much research and introspection leads directly to the conclusion that the parallel letterwise model simply can't be a true representation of reading, in the full sense of the word. In Thessaloniki this year Peter Enneson and I both explained -in very different ways- why the letterwise model is deficient. Which doesn't mean the word-shape model is perfect, in fact it's pretty dumb. The key is boumas, which are clusters of letters that are recognized as wholes due to their frequency and notan distinctiveness; a bouma can be a word, and it can also be a single letter. Soon enough I will write an essay putting the letterwise model in its place - firmly in the fovea, which is less than half of reading. >> I am fluent in Mandarin Chinese, and can read >> and write it quite well – even when I dream. http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/health/3618060.stm hhp
