Booklet - which fonts go well together? Which font for continious text?

December 27, 2007 • AtomicGarden

Hey everyone.

My communication design course at art university wants every student to create a booklet which is supposed to accompany an ad campaign for a chosen subject. Each and everyone of us needs to present our work early next year.

I have NO idea for an effective ad campaign (newspapers) yet… ;-) However… I thought - why not start with my booklet then, as I did some profound research concerning my topic.

I like the typefaces which were created for the relaunch of the UKs daily “The Guardian” a lot. I would have loved to choose them for lead-ins and headlines in my presentation as well. Another typeface I love (and which was also awarded by the TDC) is “Greta”. However, due to Christmas and other heavy expenses and my life as a poor design student, ;-) I had to settle for Lucida Std which I found on my Mac and thought of it as a good alternative for headlines and bigger sized lead-ins…

I think about combining it with one of the following typefaces (for continuous text): Bliss Pro, Vista Sans or Dax… I’ve attached a screen shot of those three, with Vista Sans being my favorite at the moment, for the simple reason that I feel it might be more readable (in small print) although it’s “f” and “l” collide for example.

What do you think - I feel the two typefaces might go well together, I’m no type specialist, so I might be completely wrong as well - that’s why I ask you guys :-)

Thanks a lot & happy new year, Jan
