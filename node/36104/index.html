Is Design School Worth It - Part Deux

August 14, 2007 • kooltopia

Just today I read an article regarding the worth of design school.

I am currently looking at several design schools and their graphic / communication design departments.

One school in particular will end up costing $80,000. Most of which will need to be covered by loans. At 34 years of age, this potential burden causes me anxiety, even though I’d love to attend this school and know the school’s reputation will help open doors.

I think what was missing in the previous forum was an answer to the question: Does a design school education and the job opportunities available in graphic design warrant being in debt for many many years?

Any feedback would be greatly appreciated.

I’ve said this before but no, I don’t think so, especially not for a grown-up, who is perhaps changing careers.

I’d try to get some sort of a job in the field. You’ll learn much more that way, even tho you’ll probably have to work your way up to being a designer.

Does a design school education and the job opportunities available in graphic design warrant being in debt for many many years?

From what I can tell there are a lot more design school graduates than there are good design jobs in desirable locations, so no. As Patty said, one does not need a design degree to become a designer. 2007-08-14 22:52:20 • Robert Trogman

At the agency I worked at, one of my jobs was to evaulate and hire young production artists, graphic designers and art directors. Really the only 2 things I thought about were, the Book, and the Personality. I think if your book is very convincing, and you’re not a wienie, you’ll do ok—with or without design school.

I wouldn’t have learned enough to get my first design/AD jobs without getting through the Vis Comm program at the Alberta College of Art and Design though. Just remember, there’s a big difference between learning TOOLS, and learning aesthetics and the principles behind using design to communicate. Sometimes the only way to that superb portfolio is the long way. Learning and doing.

Maybe you shouldn’t try to rely on this schools' rep to get you hired. Maybe THAT’s not worth it.
