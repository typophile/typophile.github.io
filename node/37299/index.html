If you have connectors on either side of a character, this is one way to do it…

FontLab will display the coordinates when you click on a node; if you set both sidebearings to zero, and the numbers for the vertical coordinates for the tops and bottoms of the connectors match (top-to-top and bottom-to-bottom), then the glyphs will join. You may have to fiddle with the bezier handles on these nodes to get the angles right (so that the letters seem to flow into each other) but, if you keep the same vertical coordinates for all connecting letters, at least they WILL line up. If, perchance, you want you terminal strokes to slant (rather than be perpendicular to the baseline, as in this example), set the sidebearings to zero, write down the numbers for the left-hand connector first, flip the character horizontally, match up the numbers, flip again, then adjust the sidebearings (subtract half of the horizontal difference between the top and bottom from each side).

This may not be the best way to do it, but it works for me…
