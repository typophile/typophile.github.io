If you plan to use Mac OS X, FontLab is the way to go as Fontographer (FOG) for Mac works only with Mac OS 9. (Not sure if FOG works with latest versions of Windows.) If you want to buy software from a developer committed to developing the app, go with FontLab. The new support offering from Support and More is promising, but it doesn't represent a commitment to enhancing the app itself. That said, Fontographer will be cheaper to acquire and there are lots of savvy, helpful users and good tutorials around. But, if you're going to start out and learn something, why not invest in learning something that has a more predictable future (i.e. FontLab)?

If you work on Windows, you can take advantage of two free tools from Microsoft: the Font Validator, which is very helpful at catching problems with fonts, and VOLT, which adds OT features to TT fonts. VOLT can do a few things with OT that Fontlab and the Adobe FDK can't. If you are planning fonts with large character sets (i.e., for multilingual use), be aware that current versions of Word can access only 256 characters in a T1 or CFF-flavored OT font. I don't know if this has been fixed in the new version of Office that is currently available in beta. Fontlab also has much better Unicode support than Fog, which is important if you want to support languages other than English and other western European ones. FL also includes the FontAudit feature, which can help you prevent problems. It's been a long time since I used Fog but I don't recall anything similar. David

Much of the opinions given on this subject here and elsewhere appear to focus on interface issues. It seems that those who are used to FOG find FLab an uphill struggle as they come to terms with a new interface and ways of working. I believe it to be primarily a case of people being comfortable with what they are used to - just as many of us will find alternatives to Photoshop bizarre to deal with, and indeed how PC users feel initially when they move to Mac (or the other way round of course). Thats not to say that FLabs interface does not need improvement, but it is hardly suprising that a package with so many more advanced technical features than FOG will suffer in comparison to its rival. The important thing to remember here is that FLab is a developing package and will get better. Can anyone claim this of FOG? Go with FLab, dont get intimidated by the interface, and invest in the future. There is little point learning FOG anymore. OpenType forever!
