Why is wood type so popular again? In just a few short months, its everywhere. Is it this poker tourney or is Jesse James haunting the typophiles' minds?
Discuss.
It must be a reaction to the cold, mechanical mainstream of contemporary western society. Same goes for so called "vernacular" type, where badly crafted letters are seen to have charm.

I think warmth and imperfection can be good things, but the way they're coveted these days seems like an escape, not a true valuation of those virtues. People who now cherish wood type still can't live without their chrome gadgets.
