A couple of initial thoughts for you. Your grid is going to be quite flexible for you and work to your advantage in allowing for creative and interesting compositions, however I do think this could be adjusted slightly. I think you could allow for slightly more room in the outside margins – don’t forget we have thumbs and a generous space for mine is always appreciated. Does the grid allow for or require space for page markers and general navigation indicators? For example have you developed a system for section marking, page numbering etc? If this is to be a requirement, now or in the future, perhaps either the top or bottom margin will benefit form extra space also. Looking at this grid it actually allows for 4 col not just 2? You do not always have to confine yourself to only two col. Experiment with combinations of wide and thin column where appropriate. Does your grid indicate a specified height at which article headings, sub heads, and intro paragraphs fall? Dictating this may allow for more consistency from article to article, issue to issue. Have you considered a method for pull quotes, and their usage? For example size, typeface choice, emphasis etc. What is your chosen serif for body copy at this stage? Hope these ideas help. Good luck. Best. -nic

2006-04-04 12:47:45 • Joseph Yap
I prefer the Minion choice, but a few points: Your indent seems excessive especially when you have a line space I think you could bring it back to an em (the height of your leading). I would try to make the body copy sit on a baseline grid, so that when you break it with a picture or pull quote the two columns still align because you have a narrow gutter it is more noticeable. The pull quotes should probably be ranged left and ragged right with as few hyphens (if any) as you can get away with and should either really intrude into a column or not at all (your Minion pdf is really too small an intrusion) the space at top at bottom should ideally appear the same, try to keep at least three lines together when breaking a paragraph with either a column break or pull quote – you need to be flexible with your grid. Depending on the number of pages you could steal some space from the centre of your grid to add to the outside margins. Where you mention creep (the loss of up to a quarter of your margin during trimming) you should also note this will affect any sidebar section markers, although you could apply them to the top or bottom of the page. Hope some of this helps. Tim
2006-04-04 15:10:57 • timd
Hi, Susana, I agree with some of the comments already posted here. I think that you are off to a good start by having used a grid to organize the page layout, but maybe it could use some adjusting. As juki says, at 1/8 of an inch your gutter is too narrow to keep the columns separated visually. The gutter should be at least 1 pica wide (12 points, or 0.166 inches), just to be on the safe side. If you modify the gutter width, you may also want to consider modifying the amount of columns within the page grid. May I suggest having an uneven number of columns rather than an even number? This might give you more flexibility to play around with different column widths (for example, you could have two wide columns for the main text and a thinner column reserved for photo captions, but otherwise kept blank -- this would give your pages some much-needed white space, as well). Last but not least, using a grid does not mean that you have to fill up every last space with text... Things might start to look "boxy." Try using a left-only justification (i.e., ragged right), as timd suggests, even for the main body copy, and compare that with the current justified spread. Hope this is helpful!
