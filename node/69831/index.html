Tools to help figuring out Subtable Overflow?

April 25, 2010 • nina

Kern table (subtable) overflow situation: Finally having figured out which exact subtable is overflowing, and that removing pairs of lesser importance in that same subtable does indeed remedy the problem, and therefore being happy, but also getting a bit tired of staring at text files, I have to ask: Is there any tool that visualizes* subtables, highlights which ones have problems, and maybe even gives you an interface that lists the pairs in that subtable and lets you preview and (if you need to) remove them? Is anything like that maybe even planned for the next FontLab version? For instance, it might be useful if it was possible to group/filter the kern pair list (the thing on the right in the metrics/kerning window) by subtables, as in «show only pairs in current subtable», or even «show only pairs in problematic subtables». (* By «visualize» I don't necessarily mean pretty infographics, but any way really that would present the subtable information in a more digestible manner than the word «subtable;» hidden somewhere in a humongous text file; collapsible tables, interfaces, even a colored marker….)
I am interested in what your kern feature looked like before & after. I'll contact you off typophile.

Nina, have you seen the "My Kern Runneth Over" talk by Ben Kiel. It is here: http://letterror.blip.tv/file/1911431/ Kiel goes into various ways to deal with subtable overflow. What worked for my font was exporting it in Metrics Machine with the "make subtables" option checked. Then I didn't have to do trial and error deleting of kerns.

2010-04-29 14:23:44 • dezcom
William, thanks for the pointer. Ben Kiel's talk was where I started. I found the "you have to delete kerning and there's no way to know how much" route frustrating; so I rolled back the kerning to the last compiling version, gradually added the missing kerning back in, and then when it overflowed I checked which portion of the kern feature the pair in question (which had just made it overflow) was in. Deleting a pair of lesser importance within the same section (between the previous and the next "subtable;" marker) allowed me to compile again. But this actually/possibly doesn't seem to work ad infinitum. So I've pretty much solved the problem in my case (and I would be curious if this is in fact the way this works!), but it's good to know about the Metrics Machine option for the future. Cheers.
