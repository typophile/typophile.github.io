Ideas for body text font for Book/brochure

August 24, 2011 • S1lvermoth

I’ve been designing flyers, posters and brochures for a company for a while and they have now asked me to design a lifestyle guide book/ brochure. The book is to be fun and flirty but easily approachable and aimed at men and women of a fairly broad age range (18 - 55 year olds) It is a handbag size guide (think the small version of a fashion magazine) so space is at a premium.

The book will be split into chapters and within each chapter include 2 distinct page types:

  1. Pages which include various product images and reviews of that product (the brochure bit)
  2. Pages of lifestyle features and guides (the book bit)

I have never before had to deal with such large areas of body text (The features and guides) and this is what is giving me nightmares! I am in 2 minds as whether to use a serif font (so it is warm, readable and inviting) or a sans serif font (for it clean crisp lines, although I am scared this will end up looking like a text book!)

I need to decide on a font (or group of fonts) that will fulfil my needs and seem to be going round in circles so some fresh suggestions from you guys would be much appreciated!

I would take the opportunity to consider the typography of small caps and old style figures in body text. Also the integration of italic and bold into running text. Bringhurst’s style guide is a good reference. Pick fonts that you like the look of in sc&osf, and would like to work with. For both, spacing and height vis à vis x-height are significant.

(Some faces with a large x-height and short capitals may not require small caps.)
