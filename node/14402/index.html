Teekay? Tk? Stet?

August 5, 2005 • jmeier

My creative director and I just had a discussion about where the terms Teekay and Stet originally came from. I know this is more of an editorial question but since most of us deal with these words way too often, I thought I’d give this list a try. She was telling me that Stet and TK originated in Germany. But I’m German and I really don’t know what those terms would translate to. Do you?

Thanks!

Jana

“TK” stands for To Come (not sure why not TC, but that probably seemed too ambiguous or easy to overlook). It has been in use for the 40-some years I have worked in publishing (at least), and I suspect it is American.

Like the use of “lede” for the lead paragraph in a (usually) newspaper story, it may also be a sort of insider tradition.

“Stet” — the writer’s defense against overly zealous copy editors — is even older, and I believe it comes from Latin.

–Kathleen
