On the matter, but not entirely a good answer: Graphic design relates to rythm in the same way as music does. In the middle of a 4/4 beat there might be a break defying all rules, but it always returns to the rythm again. In the type world, you might set the text 11/15 and the headlines in a completely different size/leading, but you make sure space above and below gets you back into rythm again.

I, myself, practically solves this by aligning text to the baseline, and placing headlines manually. Sometimes the leading is a double baseline step, sometimes I use three baselines to cover two lines of display.

2008-12-26 15:27:39 • Frode Bo Helland
Use multiples of your body text line height to achieve this. E.g. if you have p set to 12/18pt you could use two imaginary 18pt baselines (36pt total) for h1 with 14/18pt with 36-14=22pt margin (distribute those 22pt to your liking between margin-top and margin-bottom). For a complete example see [[http://www.alistapart.com/articles/settingtypeontheweb|this article]] (via [[http://daringfireball.net/linked/2007/04/10/baseline-grid|daringfireball.net]]).
