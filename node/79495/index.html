Kent Lew lecture: 14 April, Minneapolis

February 27, 2011 • eliason

Minnesota-area typophiles, save the date! Esteemed [[http://www.kentlew.com/|designer]] (and [[http://typophile.com/user/1215|Typophile regular]]) Kent Lew will be delivering a lecture at [[http://www.mnbookarts.org/|Minnesota Center for Book Arts]] at 6pm on Thursday, 14 April. TYPEFACE DESIGN AS A DIALOGUE WITH HISTORY Reflecting on notions such as allusion, citation, transcription, revival and homage, type designer Kent Lew will weave a narrative of type history's recurrent efforts to marry past and present convincingly and to infuse old forms with new life. The lecture is free and open to the public. A reception will precede the lecture at 5:30. The event is sponsored by the [[http://www.stthomas.edu/|University of St. Thomas]] (and generously hosted by MCBA). Hope to see some of you there!

Theme and Variations: Typeface Design as a Dialogue with History Frederic Goudy famously quipped, “The old fellows stole all our best ideas.” What does it mean to be innovative in a realm (typeface design) where the essential forms (the shapes of our alphabet’s letters) have been more or less fixed for centuries? As a creative professional engaged in such a circumscribed craft, type designer Kent Lew will examine his field’s self-referential proclivities within a broader historical and cultural context, drawing parallels with other artistic disciplines, including music and painting. Reflecting on notions such as allusion, citation, transcription, revival, and homage, he will weave a narrative of type history’s recurrent efforts to convincingly marry past and present and to infuse old forms with new life.
