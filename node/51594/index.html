some years ago i read (perhaps in a book about paul rand, but i’m not sure) of a celebration at which a designer’s students presented him with a bent ruler. The bent ruler represented what he had taught them: they should believe their eyes, not the ruler’s measurements, e.g. in aligning one edge of lines of text some letters must overhang to look right.

i’m writing an article in which i’d like to cite that bent-ruler award, but, after searching through many books, i still haven’t found it.

does anyone recall it, or something similar?
