2005-08-17 12:08:24 • dan_reynolds
> original copy of Edward Johnston's Writing and Illuminating and Lettering (with diagrams and illustrations by the author and Noel Rooke). Yes, a great book and an essential manual for calligraphers. I picked up my copy just after college. First published in 1906 by Sir Isaac Pitman and Sons Ltd. My version is the 1969 reprint by Pitman Publishing Corp., New York. Another Pitman book I've used is Roman Lettering by L.C. Evetts (original printing 1938, my version reprint 1960). It has a long subtitle -- "A Study of the Letters of the Inscription at the Base of the Trajan Column, with an Outline of the History of Lettering in Britain." My personal favorite is just a tiny little book: A Book of Scripts, by Alfred Fairbank. Originally published in 1949 by The King Penguin Books, my version revised and reprinted 1960. This was our textbook used in Paul Standard's calligraphy class at The Cooper Union.
