I'm in the process of recreating this 1938 Zenith radio dial entirely in Photoshop and I want to be as accurate as possible. Can anyone tell me the typefaces used on this dial? Your help is much appreciated, as always. http://www.tuberadioland.com/images/zenith12-s-267_2_dial.jpg Thanks in advance! Justin
I did some searching for the numerals and found nothing that I thought would be close enough for your project, if you really want to replicate the lettering. Huxley Vertical was quite similar, but not identical. The 4 was open and the 8 was more symmetrical. Agency Gothic is too angular. Raleigh Gothic is also fairly close, but the 1 has serifs, and at least the metal type version was heavier weight. Eden Light was a serif typeface, but its numerals show few serifs, and might be a possibility for you. Eden has been digitized, as have the others I mentioned. Maybe with a bit of tweaking you could make one of these work. The basic sans lettering should be fairly easy to replicate, so i didn't focus on that one. - Mike Yanega
