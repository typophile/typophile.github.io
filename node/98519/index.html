My theory is that designology tends towards type, as it provides objectively manageable markers for learned discussion about culture, history, economics, politics and technology. The mash-up of post-modernism, for instance, is well demonstrated by architecture, of course, but also by types such as Fudoni and Dead History (now in the MoMA collection). And of course, everyone wants to know about Nazi fonts. Basing designology on type is an alternative to the Great Man narrative. And now, scientists want to get in on the act, with their measurements of readability as a key to brain function. Kevin Larson has written for Eye.
