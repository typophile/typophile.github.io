Because contemporary art is not about material technique, which is mere making.

On the subject of typography, while text in art has a certain gallery presence, it always looks like shit. Either artists do their own ham-fisted daubs (e.g. Ed Ruscha, Ben Eine) – distress being the limit of their technical facility, or they appropriate dreary default fonts (eg. Lawrence Weiner). And then there is poor Barbara Kruger, more type-cast than Massimo Vignelli.

Recently, when MOMA curated the “@” symbol, it did in fact credit several foundries (if not designers) responsible for specific @’s, but there was no discussion about the various shapes and forms of the symbol.

Do curators and critics have the vocabulary to discuss technique? Is it relevant?
