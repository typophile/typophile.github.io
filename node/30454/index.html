Just a point for reference. Both the entities mentioned above need a semicolon at the end. An entity without the semicolon works in IE, and perhaps in other browsers, but is incorrect and may not work correctly in all cases. An entity is defined as starting with the ampersand and ending with a semicolon.

2007-01-09 16:49:39 • Miss Tiffany

If you were on an old-fashion typewriter (manual or electric) and you needed to type a 1-M dash for your manuscript – you did not have an M-dash to use. So it became common practice for the typist to use two hyphens to show the extreme pause.

However, when this same document made it the “professional” typeshop, it was automatically changed to the 1-M dash it was meant to be.

Hope that clears things up a bit.

Also, don’t forget one line under a word or sentence was for emphasis - the typewriter could do that fine…but they didn’t have the italic counterpart that the “professional” used.

There are lots of instances from typewriter to typesetting we can go through just like this.
