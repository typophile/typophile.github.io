A 19th century serif style called Detroit or mansard. The lowercase of Outstanding by “bobistheowl” is in the ballpark for CRUZAN [img:sites/default/files/old-images/OutstandingLCAsCRUZAN_3633.jpg] http://www.dafont.com/outstanding.font?text=cruzan&psize=l More info by Luc Devroye http://luc.devroye.org/bobistheowl.html Outstanding is based on Big Vintage Letters by Big Letters Org http://bigletters.org/big-decorative-vintage-letters.html With some digging it might be possible to find the original 19th century source. Note that the Outstanding font has awkward sizing. Resize the glyphs to about 40% and they work much better. Relatively minor editing of Outstanding could produce a pretty close facsimile of CRUZAN. Don

Found the 19th century source of the letters used to make Outstanding. It’s Cameo, patented in 1874 and sold by Mackellar Simiths & Jordan. There is also a digital version by “Character” at abfonts http://abfonts.freehostia.com/ Open the page, follow the link in the third paragraph to list of all fonts, page down to CameoAntique. Don
