First step is to distort the letter’s edge, and in this case keeping the distortion in the same ‘style’ as with the desired font manipulation. So I chose to use a brush of shaggy carpet. I used Arial Rounded and stoked it 3px(?) maybe 2px - I forget. Rasterized the text and merged it with the Layer Style. Then I hit it with the Eraser brush of shaggy carpet.

Once I had the edge to where I like it (heavy handed and hurried as my sample is), I duped the layer several times (3) and Gaussian blurred the middle one, and merged all.

Then I grabbed a standard Stipple brush and began to texture as shown. I used three grey tones and I think I used about three different course sizes going from less course to heavy course - background to foreground in my texture layers.

One interesting track mark in the Mr.X image is shown in the up-scaled edge image. A selection of the letter seemed to have been made and contacted (something like 2px or so), then inversed. This ‘edge’ was then darkened and nudged. And, the Stipplin' and the darkened edge seem to suggest a slight light source direction going on (?)…

Anyways, my hurried attempt is heavy handed sorry about that. A finer Stipple brush should have been used, and the darkened edge is also to thick, and I Gaussian blurred my type too much.

I always crit myself - sick basterd that I am.

Oh ya I forgot to mention that there seems to be some Stipplin' going on out side of the type which also varies in coarseness.

Good luck, hope that helps.
