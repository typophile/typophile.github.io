The same things that make it bad to most people who hang around here - just wait until the morning (unless the right Oceanians happen to be paying attention). The weights are imbalanced: between horizontals & verticals (the latter have to be thicker to appear the same), between straights & rounds (look how light the “r” looks), and between caps & lc (the former need to be thicker to look heavy enough). Also, the straight-curve joins exhibit the classic problem (called the “bone effect” by some) of looking unsmooth (compare yours to the original) because there has to be a slight shift away from pure geometric parts.

It is in fact an interesting paradox in type design that the “simpler” it has to look the more complex it actually has to be! Take a close look at Futura for example.

All this should serve not to embarrass you (you’re not a type designer [yet] so it’s OK) but to reveal some of the darkness in this black art of ours. :-)

hhp

2011-07-04 05:49:57 • hrant

Also, there’s something called overshoot: round (and pointy) bits have to overshoot an alignment line to actually appear to align there; so in the “a” for example look how the little stem part seems to go lower than the circle - the circle has to be made to go lower in order to appear to line up (and the amount to overshoot depends on how large the type will be viewed).

BTW, the eye in your “e” is much bigger than the original’s.

Lastly, spacing: it seems you’ve replicated the original’s, but the “Da” is too tight even in the original.
