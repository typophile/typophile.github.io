Great list, Miguel! I want to add that all German examples for ‘fk’ and ‘ffk’ (except for Kafka) would be ligatures across word boundaries of compound words. Traditionally, these are to be avoided.

See also this thread: oodles of poodles.

And: I assume your list is automatically compiled, from the interwebs? There are a few words that are very rare, and others that don’t really exist in standard language, imho: begrifft, besofft, vergafftes, verknifft, versofft. If you google for them, you won’t find more than a few dozens hits, most of them from word lists like this one. Of course, that is of negligible, for kerning and drawing ligatures.
