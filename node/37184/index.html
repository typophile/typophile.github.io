General-purpose typeface for personal use?

September 17, 2007 • jacobh

For a while now, I’ve been using David Manthey’s free version of Caslon (called [[http://www.orbitals.com/self/ligature/ligature.htm|Wyld]]) as a general-purpose font for all of my word-processing (e.g. letters, essays etc.). Unfortunately, the number of characters which it includes is probably a bit too few to be practical (especially as there are no lining numerals) and it doesn’t work particularly well in LaTeX (which I use for most of my word-processing).

I therefore think it might be time for me to buy a professional typeface to replace it and was wondering if anyone had any suggestions of something that might be suitable? I quite like the Caslon and Baskeville families and was thinking along those lines but I am a bit bewildered by the range of options. My main constraint is the price– I don’t think I could really justify spending $100 on Bitstream’s Baskerville, just for personal use! I was hoping to get away with spending around $50 (or, preferably a bit less). My main requirements are simply that it looks nice and that it can be made to work with LaTeX (that should be no problem, I would hope). I also quite like using the “ct” ligature, but I can probably live without that, if necessary!

Apologies if I’ve said anything that sounds particularly silly; although I’m interested in typography, my knowledge is not great!

Best wishes

Jacob

2007-09-17 18:12:16 • Reed Reibstein

If you like Baskerville, Storm’s Baskerville 10 Pro is great and I believe can be had for less than $100 for the roman and italic if you don’t need the Cyrillic (just a bit more with). You can buy it in Euros on his site–check exchange rates to see if that’s a better deal than through Myfonts. Baskerville 10 is meant for text sizes. His regular Baskerville is a little finer.

I’ve never used it, but Lars Bergquist’s Baskerville 1757 also looks good and is only $79 (Fountain).

I’m not a big Caslon fan, but Adobe Caslon is nice and you can get the roman and italic in Opentype for less than a hundred.

I second Gary on this one: If you like Caslon and Baskerville (Baroque typefaces), then Storm is your man. Also look at his Jannon, Regent, Antique, and Anselm Serif (if you want to try something amazing, but a little out there).
