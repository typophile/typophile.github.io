2006-05-04 12:45:54 • BERGMAN
When deciding on a Cyrillic to nicely match a Latin, the one thing I would recommend (not least because virtually nobody else will) is to end up with a setup where the Cyrillic "x-height" is slightly larger than the Latin x-height. This is anti-conventional, but to me critical (at least in running text, although not in display) since it helps equalize the apparent sizes (because Cyrillic has far fewer extenders). One problem though is that you generally can't simply use a difference in point sizes (within most any "canned" Latin+Cyrillic family) to do this, since the color balance will shift. (Yet another reason to wish MM were still alive.) -- Danya: killer logo. hhp

2006-05-05 16:52:17 • Nick Shinn
where the Cyrillic “x-height” is slightly larger than the Latin x-height ... since it helps equalize the apparent sizes (because Cyrillic has far fewer extenders) I did some tests of this theory (with a sans face) and it does seem to work, but only when the typeface has a small x-height in general. Otherwise, the Cyrillic tends to look very "cappy" and extender-less in comparison with the Latin. But as there are not any typefaces like this available, ones that have a large x-height seem to minimize the "extender gap" between the two scripts, as the extenders don't play such a strong role as they do in faces with a small x-height.
