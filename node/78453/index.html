January 27, 2011 • The Troubadour

Hi everyone, I'm pleased to be here, I love design, grammar, and font. I'm a copy-editor by trade, but with a flare for aesthetics, I hope. I work at BC Children's Hospital. For those who don't know, a quick Google Image search will reveal that most of their logos are designed to look like they're written by five year olds with Crayola products. That's not a slight, that's their actual intent. Crayons. Their letterhead is made to look like it's written in crayon. The signs of the departments outside my door has the same crayon motif. Now I've just inherited the job of updating this page: http://www.bcchildrens.ca/Services/ClinicalDiagnosticFamilyServices/SocialWork/Forfamilies/Accommodation.htm Something occurs to me; and I realize that saying so may or may not make me an instant pariah in this community, but I'm willing to make the bold statement to risk your contempt; I may have actually discovered an appropriate place for Comic Sans. It goes along with their disarming appearance and child's script motif. Is it even arguable that I should keep it that way, or will even the layman's sentiments about this font ruin the credibility of the information below? Anyways, thanks everyone, and I hope to become a prolific part of this community.
Comic Sans is easy to read, clear and friendly. The hate on it is over the top. For some uses, it's not bad at all.
