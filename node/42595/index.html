Twentieth Century v. Futura

February 28, 2008 • keoxa

I run a fine-press studio, and we have several typefaces based on Paul Renner's Futura. Both Twentieth Century and Spartan—where can I find studies on the histories of these faces? Just HOW much of them is directly related to Futura and how much is mere well-intentioned emulation? All this to say, I'm considering completely RE-NAMING the faces Futura? We have some sizes of Futura, some of Spartan, and some Twentieth Century. It would be nice to homogenize them, but I only want to do that if the others truly are imitations of Renner's design. Just how different are they?
In the case of Spartan (Linotype version), the main differences are the lowercase 'a', which had both a one-story and two-story design; the left quote marks were available either rotated 180° or mirrored from the right hand variety, and the question mark is of a more usual design, instead of the backward 'S' design in the original. The ATF version of Spartan is the same, except it has only the one-story 'a' and only mirrored quote marks.

2008-02-28 18:56:30 • keoxa
McGrew describes the differences. They are not many. He does not consider them significant. You might have to decide for yourself. If you compared the same sizes of all three, you might notice the differences. But mixing one size of Spartan on a page with a different size of Twentieth Century, for example, would probably not show up any appreciable variances. Especially if you consider that there are sometimes subtle differences between different sizes of the same nominal typeface in metal anyway. Nevertheless, if it was me, I'd probably keep the different drawers labeled according to the different fonts to retain the info about the original provenance, but perhaps arrange the drawers in the same cabinet acknowledging that the different sizes can essentially stand in for each other. -- K.
