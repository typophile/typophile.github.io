Hi I have an assignment to redesign the logo of Snapple (drink). The brief is to reclaim the quirkiness of the Snapple attitude - it is a drink that doesn't overpromise "unthink it's just a drink". - it is the antithesis of drinks that promise life-changing experiences. But it is laid back but fun, unusual, natural.

I have not designed logo's before. Are there any typefaces/advice anyone can recommend? Also, what do you think is the best software for logo design.


please ignore that landscape above the logo ! :D
Adobe Illustrator (or vector based drawing programme) is the best software to use. While designing you have to think of all the media it might be applied to and you may need to change to suit your medium (for example web, large and small printing, paper type, embossing &c &c). There are many things to consider, choice/number of colours that can be faithfully reproduced in different media
2005-01-17 14:53:59 • timd
Make sure your logo does three things.
1. Is relevant to what the company stands for.
2. Is original. Make sure it stand out from the company's competitors.
3. Is impactful. People should take notice of the logo when they see it.

A great site to check out is www.identityworks.com. The theories that are presented here are quite sound.

Good luck!

2005-01-24 16:20:56 • aluminum
I don't want to stand-up for the teacher, but sometimes, in the real world, your clients won't give you much more to go on. You could take such a short brief as allowing you more freedom.

What do you see as quirky? Who do you think the target market it? Could it be you? What do you think is quirky? Make some lists. Write down all of your thoughts and then start doodling/sketching based on those thoughts.
