[[http://www.linotype.com/2276/cisalpin.html|Cisalpin: The ideal typeface for cartography]] The Swiss designer/typographer Felix Arnold designed Cisalpin during the late 1990s, after he had challenged himself to create a contemporary typeface that could be used for cartographic uses. Arnold came to the subject of cartographic typefaces after analyzing many maps and atlases, and discovering that there was no standard typeface for these types of documents.

Would you consider commissioning something new? A map typeface to be used online at small sizes have very specific requirements. You need large apertures, big x-height, simplified features, clear contrast between various pieces of information and possibly condensed styles, maybe a set of symbols, and all of it has to be hinted to render clearly across all modern browsers.
