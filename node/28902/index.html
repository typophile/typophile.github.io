The proportions and spacing of hyphens are more critical than the plainness of their shape. Here’s a thought (for OT font developers), and it could also be applied to the spacing of punctuation: Have two identical-glyph hyphen characters, but kern them differently. That way you can use one for regular combinations, and the other for problem combinations such as Y-T, in the case feature perhaps.
