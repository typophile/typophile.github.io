I once (1991) used a lot of digital Aldus, and re-kerned it in Quark. I found that combinations such as s-a and p-o, etc., needed opening up. That relieved the tightness which you spoke of. The problem was that the bowls were too narrow and squarish to be fitted so tight. Palatino was the corporate face I had to work with. Aldus was my cheat for text, I used Palatino for Bold, and display. If I were doing the same job now, and had free rein, I would use a contemporary face. Seria, perhaps, has a similar cut and proportion.
