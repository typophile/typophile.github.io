You might try try some record labels, your style has a certain mtv meets ikea chic to it. it will sell fake indie pop.

This is not harshly critical, just please try and be mature - realizing that greater designers than you may have ‘missed’ your navigation interface not because they’re dumb or inobservant, but that you may have wandered into the ‘mystery meat’ realm and are still under the impression that it is somehow chic outside the fine arts to be functionally obscure. I would use this as feedback, roll up my sleeves and get in there and improve it.

The exploitation of labour by yourself to illustrate the imperections in outsourced processes is again, like andrea dworkin making a pornographic picture to illustrate some obscure feminist point about pornography. Instead of using a plotter or printer with clever random seeds, you used live human labour, how innovative. What social conscience. Pharaoh turned down forklifts too. I think it serves as a more poignant critique of expensive, hyped design schools and the socioeconomic status of their student bodies.

Keep trying though. Just cause I’m a cranky old sextagenarian shouldn’t let me influence you. Perhaps the newfangled way is yours. Try and remember that your giant flash downloads, take just enough time on a 56k modem to make my tea… about twelve minutes.

I’m just jealous. There is a spark in the work.

2005-08-03 22:18:05 • Dan Weaver

Tara, Your plight is normal for anyone starting out in design. Go to design events hosted by AIGA TDC, ADC. Just meet people in the field. You have a better shot at a job if you get an intro from someone. Freelance through an agency (Aquent) to make contacts. Business is tough. Your first job won’t be a dream job. Put stuff in your portfolio that shows you can be used right away (production savvy work–tables, charts, layout, etc.

It isn’t easy but stick with it.
