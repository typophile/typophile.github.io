Clear Numerals on Screen?

October 21, 2011 • jaredxrandall

I’m looking into a good font family for clear numerals to display on screen. (project is an engineering instrument - will need to display words & numerals on screen ranging from 9-20px height @ ~150ppi - embedded fonts ok, not web/browser specific). I see a lot of discussion on the web about readability of crappy web browser fonts, readability of body text in print, Clearview for road signs - but haven’t found any great resources for numbers and scientific data. Does anyone know of any good typography resources where folks have rated/discussed readability of numbers? Any personal preferences or recommendations? Currently using Frutiger based on variety/weights, and it’s doing well, just thought I’d try to find out more before moving too much further along in the process. Thanks all for any help or advice!
Unfortunately, as far as I know, there haven't been any truly scientific studies on the readability or legibility of numbers. Studies on typefaces designed for optimal readability / legibility, like Clearview or the ClearType typefaces would probably work well enough. Frutiger too probably is well enough. Though it doesn't really answer your query, you might take a look at "The Relative Legibility of Modern and Old Style Numbers" by Miles A Tinker. It was a scientific study in the 1930s comparing Lining and Text figures. Might be of interest.
