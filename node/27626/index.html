Looking for some background information on Morris FB's Clearface Gothic

August 12, 2006 • judah

Does anyone know the history of this typeface. Aside from brief bio’s of Fuller Benton (usually in connection to ATF) there doesn’t seem to be a lot of information out there about Clearface Gothic.

Hi Judah

According to Jaspert’s Enclopedia of Typefaces it was first produced by ATF in 1907, but became a stock face of many foundries subsequently - Stephenson Blake, Lulow, Mergenthaler Linotype, Lanston Monotype, Intertype. So it was ubiquitous, to say the least.

The most info I’ve ever seen on this face in a single place would be in ATF’s 1923 Specimen Book - see if you can hunt down a copy - there’ll be about 4 or 5 pages of very handsome specimen setting and some background blurb about legibility testing, schoolbooks and the like…

Of course it was later the basis for ITC Clearface - but I suspect that it was a victim of the ITC house-style mangling when it got recut.
