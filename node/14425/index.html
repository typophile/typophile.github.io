Type as wall art

August 6, 2005 • Kent Wang

I’m trying to decorate my room and offices and I think prints of type samples would be really great to hang on the walls. Either all the letters, a pangram, or some mixture of the two laid out attractively. Has this already been done? Where can I buy one? Or maybe I can get one of my artists to draw one up for me. I’ve never commissioned art before, it would be pretty cool!

I think I’d like to get a print of Palatino and maybe get Zapf to sign it. Do you think he would be up for that? Or Futura and get Wes Anderson, who uses it a lot in his films, to sign it.

I have always dreamed of having a very nice studio with huge prints of classic jewels such as a page from Aldu’s Hypnerotomachia Poliphili, Bodoni’s Manuale Tipográfico, Jenson’s Eusebius, etc…

2005-08-07 01:22:08 • ebensorkin

Hmmm… yeah, good luck with that. But seriously…

I have been collecting typographic posters for years. I can tell you signed specimens are rare. Although, if you see [[Hermann Zapf|Zapf]] in a public setting he will probably sign something for you, as long as it is an acceptable reproduction of his work. For that matter, I think most type designers would do the same.

Something else to think about, many a typographic ephemera are only to be found at the [[conferences|typographic conferences]], where may [[designers|type designers]] can be spotted. sometimes type [[foundries]] sell posters that they have designed as specimens. I have acquired [[House Industries|House]] and [[Hoefler]] posters this way.

Also, you can look for original/historic poster art… can be a bit pricey but a great way to decorate.
