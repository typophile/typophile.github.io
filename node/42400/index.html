Logo Critique : Microspective Multimedia

February 23, 2008 • microspective

So, I’m about to overhaul my business identity. The business, Microspective Multimedia, develops small-business identity (my largest draw is artists and complementary health practitioners), plus multifarious print, web, audio, and photo services.

I’m tired of hearing, "‘Microsp… Mediawhat?’ What a mouthful!", so I’m shortening the name simply to Microspective. Even though the “multimedia” descriptor has been omitted, I figured Google, Flickr, Amazon, etc. don’t include their service in their name, so a simplification seemed in order.

Anyway, I’ve found it difficult to be objective about redesigning my own business identity, so I’d like to solicit your knowledge and experience for opinions on kerning, proportion, and anything else that you find objectionable (or favorable) about this logo.

Thanks in advance for your comments!

Regards, Ray

“Microspective” is a cool word, and right now none of these seem to take advantage of its aural or visual qualities. It has two i’s, and a descender near the middle. I’d like to see you play with that, instead of using a mark that refers to “perspective” but is otherwise meaningless. I’d play with the letter ideas on paper before thinking about what font or color to use.

Good luck! It’s hard doing your own business identity - I’ve been taking almost a year to work on my own.
