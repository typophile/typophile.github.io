Greater Albion Releases Bonning. 40 percent Introductory Discount

April 2, 2009 • Rasendyll

Greater Albion Typefounders has just released Bonning on [[http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/gatf/bonning/ | Myfonts.Com]]. Bonning is a Roman face full of the spirit of the 1920s.

It was inspired by a (real)estate agent’s For Sale board seen in an old sepia photograph from that era and combines visual flair and period with good clear legibility.

A range of Opentype features including alternate forms, old style numbers and fractions, as well as discretionary and standard ligatures are included. Three weights are offered, including a shadowed black form are offered, all in a choice of three widths. It’s the ideal face for signage with a period feel, as well as posters, headings and feature paragraphs.

A full (and fully licensed) version of Bonning Wide Bold can be downloaded completely free of charge from [[http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/gatf/bonning/ | Myfonts.Com]] . The restof the family can be purchased there.

In the meantime Greater Albion’s [[http://gatf.wordpress.com | blog]] has some examples of the Bonning family in use.
