2005-08-26 18:03:26 • deafmetal
Héctor - Like other Scangraphic fonts, Unica comes in two versions with varying spacing. From the Scangraphic Fonts page at FontShop: Scangraphic offers an extensive range of fonts, all available in two special Scangraphic design formats called “Bodytext” and “Headline”. Scangraphic Bodytext (“SB”) faces are ideal for text in small point sizes. The hairlines and serifs of the Bodytext faces are thicker than normal hairlines and serifs to make them more legible in fine print. In addition to this, they have a special ink-traps incorporated in the font design to prevent blurring and loss of print quality. The Scangraphic Headline (“SH”) faces are made for text in large point sizes. Scangraphic optimized the Headline fonts specifically for this purpose, for example, by narrowing the spacing of the fonts and re-designing the umlauts in order to make them work better aesthetically with the overall design.

Héctor, I feel your pain. About once every 2 months I have to deal with an RFP that specifies "Arial 12 pt". What's worse, it will also have a page limit, and the engineers I work with consider that a challenge to cram as many words on a page as possible. The result is Arial set 12/12, on a 48 pica line. Makes me cringe, but I get a paycheck every 2 weeks. I think one option is to use arial, but use it well. Some very good typography has been done w/ grotesks. The fact that some really ugly typography has been done w/ arial shouldn't stop you from using it. Another option is to do a work-man job on a arial version, then do an excellent job on an alternate version w/ a different typeface. Use this as an opportunity to educate someone as to the joys of typography. Jay
