That's a more focused -but also pretty challenging- question.

When looking for Arabicized Latin fonts* most people choose stuff that's caricature, like FF Falafel. Maybe because anything deeper requires an understanding of the roots of letterforms (and not just the roots of Latin). In the display realm this can actually work (sometimes**) but in the text realm -which is all about subtlety- it can't. In the text realm you have to shoot for a mere "atmosphere" of Arabic. To me a really good way to do this is to use a Latin font with reverse contrast, like certain designs by Excoffon or Bloemsma. The latter's Balance for example has a lot of potential here. There's also a font (I forget the name) that was shown on Typophile - it was by a South American designer - which combines reverse contrast with that "fauve" feeling typical of Latin and Eastern European culture. I can track it down if you like.

* BTW, check out the Arabicized Cyrillic font in this post:
http://www.typophile.com/forums/messages/30/49453.html

** Perhaps the best example is Mo by www.toko.ne, which can be seen in the current issue of Spatium magazine.
