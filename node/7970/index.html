2004-12-03 14:03:55 • geraintf
Ken,
This is some good work. As a first comment I would make sure that you do not use double word spaces after a full point (or in any other place) this will improve the colour of your type, for certain combinations . W and . T it is worth kerning the space to help. As pointed out justified type can be a bit of a trial, for example, in your first (and to a lesser extent in the second) the second line of the righthand column is a bit spacy, if you are in a position to rewrite/have rewritten to suit that might help, or there is no reason that the two columns couldn't be different widths. To aid readers and potential clients I would try to get all three elements of the date in the same line. In the second layout the leading in the righthand column is not the same as that of the left not being the same results in an ugly step. To get picky, if you have a full point or comma at the end of a line in justified text it can make a step in the righthand vertical margin, an easy way to deal with this is to insert a white (or the same colour as the background) full point/comma at the end of each line to create hanging punctuation (remember that you have done this when it comes to changing/updating a piece of text). I might consider moving the logo so that the t of Connect aligned with the righthand margin of the body copy.

Ken, it's cool that you are looking through other ads and comparing justification and making observations. That attention to detail will really help you out if this is something you continue to do.

If you don't mind me jumping in late, I can add some suggestions to Tim & Daniel's already very helpful ones.

With Vito & Jack, the line length is much too long. 113 characters is almost twice the "maximum" for optimal readability. The two-column layouts are much more pleasing and won't overwhelm your readers. People (who read correctly) read by grouping up phrases from line to line, but this is impossible with long line lengths. Try aiming for around 45-66 characters, includings spaces.

I think it's really nice that you haven't added space between lines AND indented new paragraphs. Usually people do that all the time, and it's unnecessary. Congrats! (and thank you.)

Proforma has a set of small caps, and since you are using all caps with FREE and AMPS, it would be extra nice to set these in small caps.

For Jack, should the headline end with a period?

For Aynsley1 (the first one), you might also want to hang the punctuation in the photo descriptions after the names. Since the captions are shorter, some people might purposefully leave them the way you have them. Your line count (or depth) shouldn't vary by just one or two. When you are lining things up, you either want to have them align perfectly, or create enough of a difference that it makes a healthy contrast.

For the two column layouts, it looks like you have matched the space between columns with the space between paragraphs. You might want to consider bringing the columns a little bit closer. This will make them feel more unified as a section of copy. Now, there's also something called rivers. This is where white space runs up through a paragraph. Subconsciously they can be distracting, and they kill the color for paragraphs. It can happen when the same word falls in the same position on two consecutive lines (the first aynsley ad, second column, the word "her") or it can occur randomly just because. If you spot them, you can try to reword sentences. It happens more in justified settings because sentences stretch out to fit, creating more space, and it might be another reason Daniel is suggesting left-aligned ragged right.

If it's possible, the dates would look better if you can keep from breaking them up. Might not be possible, but if you are able to rewrite things, it would flow much better. Tim mentioned this previously.

Stock photography is really tricky. In the future, look for pictures with eye contact and not a lot of background clutter. The two photos with eye contact are so much more dynamic, and even makes it seem like these people are actually telling you their story. In the second Aynsley photo, the cluttered desk makes her look disorganized and the red cup draws too much attention. In a directed photo, you would have been able to remove it and put meaningful objects in the background.

This is just something I noticed, and I'm not trying to say anything, but it's all white people. It seems like the border might be a little more controlled than you think! Just teasing. Some diversity might help though.

Lastly, in the tagline, do you need the word "Because?" Might want to just have "Borders were made for crossing."

Hope you found these comments useful! Your revisions so far are looking good.
