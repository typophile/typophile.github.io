JOSH DARDEN'S CORUNDUM

September 8, 2006 • mondoB

What do we think of Joshua Darden's new serif family Corundum, his re-working of Fournier? I remember trying to sell him on doing this in two conversations some time ago, so I'm delighted to see he bit. Though there are lots of subtle differences versus Monotype Fournier, as there would have to be (including his tail on the cap Q that just isn't salacious enough!), nevertheless the Fournier feel and overall look are there. I do wonder, however, whether Josh considered incorporating the Tall Caps--in Monotype you had a choice of two kinds of caps, and I remember when Penguin Books used Fournier, it was always with Tall Caps, a type choice I grew up quite fascinated by... http://www.joshuadarden.com/shop/corundumtext.php
I've been waiting for a digital "re-creation" of Fournier for some time and what little I've seen of Corundum I like a lot. Incidentally, I seem to recall that the tall capitals were part of Monotype's original hot metal version, and it was Francis Meynell of the Nonesuch Press who asked them for a version with shorter capitals.
2006-09-08 13:57:58 • estienne
It's an attractive font but I don't think I'd use it as a substitute for Fournier. It's heavier for one thing, and the italics lack the character of Fournier's - they are tighter, have a higher x-height, and are at less of a slant. Fournier's italics are much of why I use it, they are so beautiful and distinctive. But it does look quite complete (altho where are the fractions???), and looks solve the HUGE Fournier caps/smallcaps problem.
2006-09-08 14:33:14 • pattyfab
Joshua Darden's Corundum types are not based on Monotype's Fournier typeface, but are based on Pierre-Simon Fournier's actual work. Joshua owns some Fournier specimens which he used as reference when developing Corundum.

One tends to do what is requested by clients, and use that as a barometer of what is viable, therefore I suspect that no publication has commissioned them to fill out that particular family. H&FJ are no slackers in creating mega families, so they have certainly not absconded that responsibility.
