NaturaRoman ... a Requiem knock-off

July 24, 2007 • williamnapier

Recently, I started a new job at a firm using a font called NaturaRoman for its text.

I have since learned that NaturaRoman (although I have it in ttf and Mac PS 1) does not technically exist. Someone created it from Requiem by Hoefler.

So, I have a problem. Obviously, I cannot use the ttf file for production work. And I am working on a Windows OS machine, so the Mac PS 1 version I have won’t work either.

So, I need a Windows PS 1 version of NaturaRoman, Italic, what have you …

Or, I could purchase Requiem. Not a problem. But I am unclear whether or not the 3 weights Hoefler mentions on www.typography.com (Fine, Display and Text) translate to what I’m used to as Roman, Italic, Bold, Bold Italic, Small Caps, Swashes, etc.

Regardless, I am looking at font substitution issues. I can deal with those but what I can’t deal with is the thought of sending a True Type font (even with outlines which might shift my InDesign layout when preparing for RIP.)

Any suggestions? Anyone familiar with NaturaRoman? Under the info on a Mac OS X I have found that the Nature Conservancy produced via a license from Hoefler … what the heck is that?

Kills me how people can rip off font designers …

The Nature Conservancy publishes a magazine (by the same name) that uses Requiem. Looks like they, or the folks at Iridium who did the redesign commissioned a custom version of Requiem.

That doesn’t explain how it landed in your lap, though…

Hi William,

Natura is indeed a variant of Requiem, which was made for The Nature Conservancy. We (and they) have since replaced it with Requiem proper, as well as some new variations of Requiem that eagle-eyed typophiles might notice at http://www.nature.org.

If you’re using the Natura fonts, just let us know and we can arrange a license for you. We’ll throw in Requiem as well, since I think you’ll like it better (The Nature Conservancy did!) Feel free to e-mail me offlist, or contact Romeo Ruz at my office for speedier service, at ruz at typography.com.

By the way, I hope that while any of you are scoping out the typography on The Nature Consevancy’s site, you’ll take a moment to learn about the organization. They’re fighting the good fight for our environment, and they’re one of the many non-profit organizations that H&FJ is especially proud to be working with.

Thanks!
