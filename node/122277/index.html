Not necessarily. Keep in mind that we are dealing with the pre-digital era here (I’m assuming the plate dates from the 1970s, given the information supplied by the original poster), and other weights of the face do not have that angled terminal on the F: [img:sites/default/files/old-images/MTGrotesque_5758.png] I displayed the bold because the overall letter forms seemed to be the best match, but given that it is a logo it may well be a mix of several weights, or a heavier weight, that has been customized. If you think there is a better candidate that fits the historical time frame, by all means post it. The person seeking the ID already stated that there are two fonts he needed help identifying, that’s why I posted the suggestion of Univers for the balance of the text. Neue Haus Grotesque might also work for the smaller text: http://www.fontbureau.com/nhg/ But all of this is provisional without a larger, higher-resolution sample....
