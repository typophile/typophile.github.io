Separating words and phrases

May 26, 2005 • popovich

I am setting a large list consisting of city names and addresses (for a magazine ad). Some cities have up to 4 addresses, some have just one address. Everything should be centered (! not my “idea”), and when it is - there is just this whole list of addresses and no place for the product at all. Pretty bad, taking into consideration it is a product in a luxury segment of goods.

So I was thinking, maybe there is a way it can be centered but separated somehow different? A middle dot is the only thing which comes into my mind. What else would you use?

Cheers, Alexey

You could use a bullet or some embellishment or ornament or a line, or an ornate line.
Of course space can be used. Or you could alternate styles to separate them (bold/light, roman/italic. Color can be used too.

Cheers!

Can you choose any fonts? I suggest convincing the client to set up the list as one paragraph using a script font like Kuensler two bold for the name of the City and Centaur for the address info.The script font will serve as an obvious indication that a new name&info is beginning. These two fonts look classy when used together. Plus the main focus is the product and not 3/4 of a page used up by space for lines and lines of typed addresses.
