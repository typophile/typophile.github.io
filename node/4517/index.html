The corp did consider InDesign and got lots of pressure from below to make the switch. Oddly enough, and as a huge surprise to our tech team, Quark actually sent a rep to headquarters to answer questions and push the upgrade.

The work around for the type problem is to work off-network, open the Quark 4 file and view each and every page (including master pages), THEN save the file, thus transforming the document to a Quark 6 file. Anything else will force the type outside the text boxes and into some kind of Twilight Zone.

I'm fascinated that the act of viewing a page will save the type. It's as if each Quark 4 page wants me to say a personal goodbye before it gets absorbed into the Quark 6 (aka Borg) universe.

It's the kind of problem that would be fun to track down if I had tons of time and didn't have circulars and print ads and posters and brochures to produce on a deadline.

I just can't imagine why displaying a document page on a computer screen somehow prevents the bug. For ex: we tried viewing different pages of a multiple page document and saving it in Quark 6. Each time, whichever page or pages we had not displayed was/were corrupted.

Does that stir any ideas among the more technically inclined typophiles? I'm mystified.
