AIGA and GAG.

March 7, 2005 • queso

When I was first in school for design I remember finding the Pricing & Ethical Guidelines book and found it very intriguing. I also found it cool that there was a organized union for Designers, and they could help you get a lawyer if you ever felt ripped off. (nice insurance for those of us in school weary of becoming a freelancer). I never did join but was something I wanted to do once I graduated school. Anyway in the middle of here I transfered to Portland Oregon. The head of design of my new school is an AIGA member(an organization that is a lot more potent it seems than GAG) and he stresses that AIGA is very important to join, just for the contacts alone. The only impact I feel they have had in my community is a bunch of guest speakers (and I am not complaining). On the surface level they seem to offer a lot of the same stuff that GAG does. So I am a little confused. Who does what? If they both do the same thing who should I join? Should I even join at all? A while back I was talking to a friend of mine who is also the head of the AIGA student group, and I asked him… "What does AIGA do?" "pfft no one really knows". I graduate really soon and am confused. I was hoping I could get some good testimonials on here. Thanks for any information.
After not attending a single AIGA function for 5 years, I gave up my membership. It is better for young people as a place to meet. Our local chapter does a lot of TGIFing at bars. It is definitely a 30s and younger thing. If you are new in your area, it might be something to join for a year or two.

"he stresses that AIGA is very important to join, just for the contacts alone" He's 100% right about that. AIGA's main benefit to any member is that it's a good networking tool. The other benefits are all debatable. Some folks find a lot to get out of the AGIA, some folks don't. I was in it throughout school and a handful of years afterwords before finally bowing out. It's not a union of any sort. More of a club.
