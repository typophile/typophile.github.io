There are too many threads to keep up with, but again I will say WE HAVE A PLAN. :)

Everyone calm down and be patient. Gabi, the community is having a difficult time with this because suddenly the forums are being bombarded by unstructured threads which lack the information necessary to allow people to interact with the content and you (the author). This isn’t necessarily the fault of the students, and part of the problem might be the underlying architecture of the site and the way in which we handle and surface content. Blogs have never been an integral part of Typophile and not so long ago, only paying members of the site used them. And really, no one used them until we opened it up to everyone.

Surfacing blogs is a new thing here. It has become very popular in a short amount of time and is now causing us to re-think how the system needs to function, and how people use them. There isn’t anything wrong with the way in which your class is utilizing this feature, as a matter of fact it is quite interesting and proposes some new opportunities. But it will require organization to do this in a more engaging way.

No one here don’t dislikes students, and I apologize if our nastier community members are making your experiences at Typophile unpleasant. We are communicating with the instructors involved and will be proposing ideas to help solve some of these problems and promote collaboration and dialogue instead of negative criticism.

If you have any direct complaints or want more information, feel free to contact me directly. zara [at] punchcut [dot] com
