Prensa for Books?

May 6, 2010 • nina

I've been considering Prensa for a couple of projects – mainly wondering about books, nonfiction if possible. Has anyone used it, or seen it used, in such a context and can share some insight (and pix :-)? The examples I've seen of it in use are mainly editorial design, or more display-oriented. Some people appear to think it's too distracting for books, but I wonder if that should be true. Of course it looks snazzy up large, but it seems to read very crisply to me too. (I'm a new convert in terms of the Electra "g" – I used to think it's too distracting, but then I read a couple of books set in Electra and have found it really isn't. And Prensa seems to apply the same principle to more glyphs, in a way, so I wonder.) Also, what is the deal with the "Book" weight being lighter than the Regular? I like it in terms of the less-strong contrast, but is it a little light? Curious for your insights/opinions.
I like the "distraction". Such snazziness does a good job of parsing the rich qualities of letterpress into the bland medium of offset.
2010-05-06 20:38:09 • Nick Shinn
Nina, the “Book” designation is just a weight declaration, not a specific recommendation of that particular weight for books, per se. (As far as I know, anyway. I never asked Cyrus.) FB has historically used Book when there’s a big range and something falls between Light and Regular. However, the practice is being phased out, because having both a Book and a Regular weight can cause problems in InDesign menus. (Fixed for existing families.) If you’d like to see a PDF of both in text settings closer to your target, contact me offline with some sample text and specs — line-measure, leading, approx size(s) — and I’ll see what I can set up for you.
