Matching so few letters of a sans serif typeface that has been reproduced in thread is a fool’s errand, but here goes….

If by “the bottom right corner of the ‘R’ does not protude outwards” you mean that the leg of the |R| ends perpendicular to the baseline, not with a little curve, that may just have been too fine a detail for the production method to reproduce. It is exceedingly rare to find that type of curved-leg |R| in a sans serif without that detail.

Helvetica Neue 77 Cond Bold should do the trick: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/neue-helvetica/

Pragmatica Condensed Bold is also an option: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/paratype/pragmatica/

Also amusing to get this in response to my visual search using your sample: http://www.dafont.com/search.php?q=Diffuse-&text=THEANSWER
