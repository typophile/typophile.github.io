The old FontBank font [[http://www.buyfonts.com/value_for_money/talon.htm|Talon Bold]] is a version which appears to differ only in the stress of the ‘O’ and ‘o’, the latter being an alternate form in the version shown in the Phil’s Photo book, as is the g and y of your sample. Mike’s choice of the word ‘legitimate’ makes me hesitate, but I don’t know that just because the name Trooper was avoided, that this might not have been a legal ‘revival’ of a non-digital typeface. For that matter, MyFonts is selling [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/ef-typeshop/toledo/bold/|Toledo Bold]], plus a number of other weights. These TypeShop fonts are from the Brendel Studio (a.k.a. Quick Brown Fox), if I am not mistaken, which has often been a source of re-named revivals of pre-digital designs. I would rather not say much about the ‘legitimate’ status of these, as it still is a gray area in my mind.
