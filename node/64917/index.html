It doesn’t look like any grid is in use, and a grid is paramount with this kind of aesthetic. Weights of fonts are all so close, that nothing stands out.. there is no sense of hierarchy.

Here is what I would do if I were you:

  1. Create a grid (something that is flexible enough to allow for plenty of modularity).

  2. Pay attention to spacing. Keep all the spacing even (or at least visually balanced)

  3. Use variety in weight, and add color LAST. Color is often a false sense of hierarchy. If it doesn’t work in B&W, it won’t in color.

  4. TEST your hierarchy by taking a screen shot, putting it in photoshop and adding a Gaussian blur. You should be able to pick out your 1st 2nd and 3rd levels, even though you may not be able to read them.

Maybe also try adding some solid or dotted rules to cut up the space. And don’t be afraid of white space.

Hope that was mean enough ;)
