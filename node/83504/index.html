hip-but-not-too-hip newspaper headline/subheadline font

July 5, 2011 • veekee

Hello everyone,

Being the lone in-house graphic designer in the back corner of a robust Canadian university student newspaper, I’ve been tasked with revamping things after ten years of a very classic, stuffy look (not my design). Our redesign committee has created a personality for our ‘new’ paper: ‘that hip-to-it senior year undergrad who’s totally in the know but is your peer and isn’t talking down at you’ ‘the first person you call if you need to know something a little off the beaten path’, etc etc. Why the newspaper is a ‘he’ is beyond me!

I am stuck on headline/subheadline options. We’d like the design to stick for a while, so while we chose a punchy Emigre font for the flag, the headline and subheadlines should be reasonably devoid of catchy quirks that will date them very quickly (so something like Amplitude or Malaga are sadly nixed). I’m really stuck! I still want some hipness in the fonts! Help!

Choices made so far: Fairplex - flag face Tisa - Body face (this is not set in stone)

It would be nice to have a mix of sans/slab between the heads and subheads.

Working option for subheadline: Whitney

Possible options for headline: News706 Flama Tiempos Dagny Gotham Condensed

…but none of them are quite cutting it. I just don’t want to be forced to choose something that makes us look like we’re taking ourselves too seriously….we’re not The Onion but we are also certainly not the NYTimes. Your assistance is appreciated! -vikki

I like the fact that you are considering contemporary fonts. Anything with any popularity is no longer hip, so why not choose choose something new that you would really like to work with (in the kind of layouts that you would like to do) as long as it hasn’t been overexposed? Then, your layouts should make the difference. Lots of white space. Avoid symmetry. (The layout equivalent, in newspapers, of not overexposed.) Trust your taste, and that should do it.
