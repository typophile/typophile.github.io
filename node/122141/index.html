EDITED: Good find Dick. Some further research shows that Büro Destruct created the font BD Calamares [i.e. squid] based on the lettering on a record cover named Allez Servette released by M-Records in 1977, which a team song [or songs] for a Swiss soccer team http://45football.com/index.php?id=38

Note that the lettering is all lower case. The letters in alpendr which differ from BD Calamares are the ones not included in the Allez Servette, i.e. d, n and p. Obviously Büro Destruct modelled its version of d, and p on the other letters shown on the record cover, which use curved tunnels to reach the inner circles. Büro Destruct did, however, use the straight tunnel used in v which to create an n that is somewhat similar to the n that they did not have available as a model.

I believe that M-Records used the same dry transfer lettering, or photo-lettering, that was used to make the word or partial word alpendr. Perhaps this word is derived from alpine? Together with the choice of these letters for a Swiss soccer team, this suggests that the creator of the rub-on or photo lettering might have been a Swiss company, perhaps included in this listing http://issuu.com/csisson/docs/u_lc_vol.20-2/43

Does anyone have any suggestions as to the mystery maker?
