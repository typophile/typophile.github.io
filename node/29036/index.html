Just another thought here, from someone who's been in the signage business a long time. Helvetica is an easy typeface to reproduce by hand. In the days when that's how things were done it was therefore a favourite with sign painters and silkscreen shops, where copy was often either hand drawn at the drawing table or laid out with Letraset, projected onto a wall traced, straightened up and hand cut in Amberlith or vinyl.
