Last Night in Twisted River

April 27, 2010 • John Lyttle

I’m reading John Irving’s novel, Last Night in Twisted River and enjoying it quite a bit. The Canadian cover is beautiful, thanks largely to the photo by Irving’s son, Everett Iriving.

The type appears to be [[http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/agfa/sackers-light-classic-roman/sackers-light-classic-roman/|Sackers Light Classic Roman]]. And the thing that my eye keeps going to is the space after the R in “IRVING” – is it kerned oddly? Is that because of the metrics that Monotype assigned, or did a designer kern it that way for a good reason? I keep staring at it, trying to see the rationale.

Perhaps folks here, many of whom have extensive knowledge of kerning, would like to comment?

Incidentally, the American version of the cover doesn’t grab me the same way. It must be the “warm tones elicit warm responses” phenomenon. Or maybe it’s the typography.
