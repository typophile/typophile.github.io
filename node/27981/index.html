Unless I’m mistaken, it looks from the Amazon UK picture that the white on the sides is not part of the cover, so the ‘H’, which Chris finds a redeeming device, isn’t there. Ouch.

Ok, I’m game. Here are my commandments of Typography (pinched from others):

  1. Thy type shall be legible. Obedience here. Ok, but now downhill.

  2. Thy visual hierarchy shall express the conceptual relationships of the author. Violation. The idea of the book is a duality: Those who represent classic type wisdom and obey the rules, including Tschichold and Spiekermann, verses type ‘heretics’ who break the rules.

Here there are random sized bits of brown and white, no relation. A way to do this would be to split the cover in half, eg horizontally or diagonally, with one side black on white and the other black with white knocked out, or red and black or another two colors. The border could look like torn paper, indicating visually the opposition and disruption. I don’t know if this particular concept would work, but it would be a. more visually arresting and b. reflect the idea of the book. Both a plus.

2006-09-06 00:24:10 • William Berkson
  1. Thy placement of type will have a pleasing relation to the space it occupies.

Violation.

Aside from being centered, the type seems to have no visual logic. Does the shape of the cross relate to the top brown block? No. Does the Cross plus the text relate? No. Why do we have a short line, long line then small short line, followed by a new color with lines all different lengths again? Why the color block again at the bottom? I at any rate can’t see any rhyme or reason.

Part of the problem here is the two titles and subtitles of the book. The full thing according to Amazon: The Ten Commandments of Typography/Type Heresy: Breaking the Ten Commandments of Typography

Now having two titles and subtitles is breaking the rules. In my opinion this breaking can be ok because it has a compensating benefit: it is an arresting, interesting opposition. But the cover doesn’t have the whole two titles, so the whole thing becomes a muddle. The point is, if you are going to break rules, you need to make a virtue of it. It has to have a strong point. Here the point is just lost because the two titles are not even put on the cover!

But–back to placement of text–as it is there is no rhyme or reason to the placement. And that is what looks most weak in the layout.

  1. Thou shalt keep logically related words visually together.

Violation.

The important concepts are: ‘Ten Commandments’ and ‘Typography’.

So the designer breaks up the words ‘Ten’ and ‘Commandments’!

The other key concept is “Type Heresy”, which as Erik Spiekermann points out are separated now by a double word space or something.

  1. Thy typeface shall be well suited to the subject matter.

Ok if the top title were in Sabon (Tschichold) or Meta (Spiekermann) that would make sense. But it’s not, it seems. Since the subject is actually type, I don’t understand why the avoidance. Also is the ‘Type Heresy’ one of Branbrook’s fonts? Maybe but I couldn’t spot it checking. Why not use Mason or one of his other wonderfully disturbing faces?

Also why put ‘Heresy’ in black letter and Ten Commandments in Roman? If you are playing on the association of Church and Blackletter, then both should be in blackletter. The two titles should also be massaged so they are parallel. That is a failing of the author or publisher, but the designer could ask. Then perhaps you could have ‘Ten Commandments’ twice in black letter, and ‘Heresy’ in Mason.

  1. Space and size thy lines, word spaces and letter spaces in a way that suits their function and purpose.

Violations.

Why is the top title smaller than the bottom title? Why is the second subtitle in a different font again? Why is the second time the author mentioned smaller than the first?

I could go on, but I’m now tired of the game.

As others have said: if this was trying to break rules in a funny way, it doesn’t succeed. If it wasn’t trying to break rules, well it does, all over the place.

2006-09-07 18:33:10 • jlt

Hello Everyone, I Hope everyone is well. I’m the author of the book you’ve all been discussing, so I thought who best to answer all your questions than myself.

Firstly, I would like to say please don’t judge the book off this picture, Originally this picture was created for the sole purpose of showing the printers where the foil was intended to go and was produced incredibly quickly (hence the spelling mistake, no it wasn’t a stunt!) This picture has now been used on pretty much everything, which I wouldn’t say I’m over the moon about really, but thats one of those things, Very well spotted though!

Regards the line break on the cover, I considered the title to be too long to occupy one line and ideally I wanted to keep the ‘ten commandments of typography’ part together. If you think thats odd then thats fair enough, I respect your opinion and if we all had the same opinions regarding typography the world would be a very dull place, so I do accept that.

Regarding the comment about never buying it, that again is totally fine, I’m often just as guilty of making decisions on a book purely by the cover, but I personally think its a bit harsh to make the comments you have without actually viewing the content. Judging by the fact that Matthew Carter, Jeff Keedy and Jonathan Barnbrook have all seen the content and were happy to be associated with it, plus the review in this months Grafik magazine it can’t be that bad.

I don’t want to go on too much but I think the Type Heresy section, needs clearing up too. The book is a flip format book, so the image you can see is only the Commandments cover, I know its a bit hard to understand with just that cover image, the white section is essentially a belly band, its alot easier to understand when you actually have the book in front of you.

I hope I don’t come across like I’m having a rant because I’m not, I just wanted to get my case across! Maybe I can arrange to post a copy out to one of you guys and then make a judgement, Its not strictly buying it so you wouldn’t be going back on your word! Plus if you still think its crap then I will hold my hands up and respect your decision!

Sorry to ramble on but I hope to hear back from you all soon
