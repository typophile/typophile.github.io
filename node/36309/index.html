Just discovered this forum. Very nice. I’m hoping you can help me generate some new ideas on a project I’m working on.

I’ve been asked to create an icon to accompany a logo still in progress. I want the icon to be simple and to suggest being “in IT.”

Ideally, the icon would read, simply, “IT,” but upon 2nd read you notice that in the negative space, you can see the word “in.” That’s the ideal I guess. But ultimately I just want the word “IN” to “play” with the word “IT.” The Sun Microchip icon that looks like a chip but upon 2nd read, you see that it spells “sun” is another way this could work.

“IT” should be u/c and the overall look should be “techy.” But no other restrictions. It’s been a real challenge to get this to work. Any ideas?

I and T really don’t offer a lot of flexibility in their negative space when used in combination, so other approaches might be warranted. Nonetheless, here are two options, one pretty obvious and the other rather inscrutable (and lowercase, too)…
