After a bit of detective work I figured out that this is a new typeface called BellSlim designed this year by Canadian type designer [[http://www.ianbrignellletteringdesign.com|Ian Brignell]]. You can read a small bit about him [[http://villatype.blogspot.com/2008/04/type-club-review-part-2-ian-brignell.html|here]]. There is no reference to BellSlim anywhere online as far as I can gather but here is a link to the [[http://www.bell.ca/web/flash/bellSlim.swf|flash route file]] that the Bell website is using to render the font with (the same sIFR system typophile uses). The specific name of the font being used is BellSlim Regular which might suggest that there are more weights in existence.

Well, my dad's worked for Bell Canada for longer than I've been alive and I've seen quite a few versions of their identity throughout my life (yes, we have some Bell antiques around the house too) I will say that for a company whose main focus is telecommunications, except for the logo just before this re-brand (the spinning-head logo as it was affectionately known), Bell has always had good design in their print materials. This logo is a modern take on their logo from the 1970s, which was just the word Bell in a bold Sans. I don't watch any old-school television (internet television like revision3.com all the way!) so I regretfully haven't seen any of the TV ads, and am bewildered as to why they have chosen to embolden random letters on their website, but I'm sure it makes sense to somebody somewhere. I think this re-brand is needed to freshen up the corporate image a little bit, the other one was easily datable as 1990s, and this will obviously be somewhat dated in the future, but I think it has much much more potential and staying power than the one that preceded it.
