Probably not for sale to the public, if it is actually a font and not just lettering. Used in Shana Galen's Jewels of the Ton romance series. Three so far: [img:sites/default/files/old-images/WhenYouGiveADukeADiamond_5503.jpg][img:sites/default/files/old-images/IfYouGiveARakeARuby_4215.jpg][img:sites/default/files/old-images/SapphiresAreAEarlsBestFriend_6534.jpg] The terminals are sometimes called foxtails and are found in fonts such as Droid Serif http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/ascender/droid-serif-pro/. Note the variation in some of the letters from book to book. The wavy cross bar on the E and H is usually called a tilde and is found in various fonts as discussed in http://typophile.com/node/110669 Don
