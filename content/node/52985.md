body: "Open up FontCreator.\r\n\r\nInsert 4 characters, named Koppa and Sampi.\r\n\r\nClick
  OK.\r\n\r\nNow these 4 glyphs appear correctly.\r\n[img:sites/default/files/old-images/polytonic-1_6128.png]\r\n\r\nOr
  insert 8 glyphs.\r\n\r\nCopy \u0150 and \u0151 to uni03D8 and uni03D9.\r\n\r\nDraw
  the rectangle in uni03D8 and in uni03D9.\r\n\r\nCreate the kai symbol by clicking
  Add Composite Glyph Member.\r\n\r\nClick the small letter, 'k'.\r\n\r\nMake the
  glyph simple, then resize the k to a greenlandic kra.\r\n\r\n<b>Creating the greek
  Koppa</b>\r\n\r\nRight click the glyph, then select properties.\r\n\r\nClick the
  Mappings tab.\r\n[img:sites/default/files/old-images/polytonic-5_5448.png]\r\n\r\nMake
  sure that the Koppa was mapped to Greek letter Koppa.\r\n\r\nClick the OK button
  when finished.\r\n\r\nCreate the small Koppa.\r\n[img:sites/default/files/old-images/polytonic-2_6112.png]\r\n\r\nRemove
  the existent point.\r\n[img:sites/default/files/old-images/polytonic-3_4868.png]\r\n\r\nAfter
  removing the existent point, the greek Koppa will look like this:\r\n[img:sites/default/files/old-images/polytonic-4_6076.png]\r\n\r\nCreate
  the capital greek Koppa by copying the greek small letter koppa to the greek letter
  Koppa.\r\n\r\n<strong>Making the greek Sampi</strong>\r\n\r\nOpen 2 glyphs, namely
  the Greek Sampi.\r\n[img:sites/default/files/old-images/polytonic-6_6185.png]\r\n\r\nDraw
  up a rectangle.\r\n\r\nSkew the rectangle.\r\n\r\nInsert 2 rectangles.\r\n\r\nEdit
  the points with Point Mode.\r\n\r\nClick Get Union of Contours.\r\n\r\nGet rid of
  the existent intersecting circle that you created.\r\n[img:sites/default/files/old-images/polytonic-7_4867.png]\r\n\r\nCreate
  2 rectangles, edit the points, then select all of the small greek Sampi.\r\n[img:sites/default/files/old-images/polytonic-8_4387.png]\r\n\r\nSelect
  Get union of contours.\r\n\r\nInsert the lunate sigmas and the yot letter.\r\n[img:sites/default/files/old-images/polytonic-9_6209.png]\r\n\r\nClick
  the OK button.\r\n\r\nClick the 3 glyphs, then click Complete Composites.\r\n\r\nKern
  the greek sampi with the Kerning menu from Kerning, New, click 2 Greek sampis, OK,
  then enter a value of kerning.\r\n\r\nClick the OK button when done.\r\n\r\nMap
  the glyph, \u03B1 to the letter: Greek small letter alpha. Enter alpha, which is
  a greek postscript name. Double-click the blank glyph, called 'alpha'.\r\n[img:sites/default/files/old-images/alpha-1_4059.png]\r\n\r\nGet
  rid of the existent point at the greek small alpha:\r\n[img:sites/default/files/old-images/alpha-2_5441.png]\r\n\r\nThis
  finished greek small alpha would look like this:\r\n[img:sites/default/files/old-images/alpha-3_3868.png]\r\n\r\nAdd
  the greek iota.\r\n\r\nClick the capital greek Iota, then select complete composites.\r\n\r\nClick
  the letter, \u0131, then copy it to iota.\r\n\r\nClick the insert characters icon,
  and an window will pop up.\r\n\r\nInsert this to Add these characters and/or character
  ranges. For example: 8364, $FB50 - $FBFF, $FFFC:\r\n$1FBD, $1FBE, $1FC0-$1FCF, $1FDD-$1FDF,
  $1FED-$1FEF, $1FFD, $1FFE\r\n\r\nClick the OK button when done.\r\n\r\nFor children:\r\nAdd
  12 glyphs with Insert -> Glyphs.\r\n\r\nAnd map all the 12 glyphs to:\r\n1FBD Greek
  koronis\r\n1FBE Greek prosgegrammeni\r\n1FC0 Greek perispomeni\r\n1FC1 Greek dialytika
  and perispomeni\r\n1FCD Greek psili and varia\r\n1FCE Greek psili and oxia\r\n1FCF
  Greek psili and perispomeni\r\n1FDD Greek dasia and varia\r\n1FDE Greek dasia and
  oxia\r\n1FDF Greek dasia and perispomeni\r\n1FFD Greek oxia\r\n1FFE Greek dasia\r\n201F
  Double high-reversed 9 quotation mark\r\n\r\nAfter done, click OK, then click complete
  composites.\r\n\r\nCopy the glyph '\u0148' to eta.\r\n\r\nMake the eta glyph by
  deleting the caron, then making the glyph a modified n.\r\n\r\nYou've created an
  greek alpha, an greek eta, an greek epsilon and an greek iota.\r\n\r\n<strong>Now,
  here are the glyphs you inserted for Vietnamese, named A caron, I caron, O caron,
  U caron, U diaeresis macron, U diaeresis acute, U diaeresis caron and U diaeresis
  grave:</strong>\r\n(img:vietnam-accents_3758.png)\r\n\r\nEnjoy the new 14 glyphs.\r\n[img:sites/default/files/old-images/polytonic-10_5267.png]"
