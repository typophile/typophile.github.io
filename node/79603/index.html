Letterpress Simulator for iPad

March 2, 2011 • JamesM

I just read that a letterpress simulator called LetterMpress is being developed. It will be released first for iPads. You’ll place and arrange type on a press bed, lock the type, ink the type, and print. You can print your design or export to other applications.

Apparently the developer is trying to raise development funds through KickStarter. (I have no connection to this project; I just thought it looked interesting.)

More info and video demo: http://tinyurl.com/4tm2ke9

I have trouble wrapping my head around this. If it exports an image, then it will be useless for actually creating letterpress prints, unless it’s a high-resolution, un-antialiased image that will be used to make a custom die, in which case the process really isn’t any different from using any other software, cuteness aside. Right?

It says something about eventually wanting to acquire metal and wood type so people can actually create prints, but does that mean the program will email finished designs to a mothership and the mothership will ship finished prints to the user? In that case, I’d personally rather just go to my local print shop.

Can someone help me understand this?
