I'm in Japan

July 24, 2005 • raph

So, I’m, uh, in Japan for a week. Is there, uh, anything typographically interesting I should see when I’m here? I realize that this is high on the list of things not to say on Typophile, but hey, wouldn’t be the first time.

I bought a lavishly produced magazine called “Design Note 2” that has a section on fonts. I didn’t find any books of particular interest, but that’s just the bookstore nearest the hotel (I’m staying near Shinjuku station). Any suggestions?

I’m amazed by the amount of branding and corporate identity that’s in English. At one mall we passed on the train, every single sign was in English (one also had the name in a much smaller Japanese font). Sans is really big here - I’d estimate that over half the English signage I’ve seen is either in Helvetica or Univers (or a fair copy thereof). Eurostyle is also surprisingly popular. Just in terms of the typographic environment, it feels like a throwback to the ’70s. I was expecting something hipper, more now. I was also surprised at how mediocre the Roman typography tends to be, especially in fit and spacing. I’m sure the Japanese will feel the same about me if I try and produce Japanese typography - it’s gotta be an issue of familiarity with the native ways, as I love Japanese design and visual art in general.
