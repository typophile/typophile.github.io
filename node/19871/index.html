Tombstone Typography

May 11, 2006 • PBJameson

Have you ever thought about designing your own tombstone? A morbid idea, perhaps, but something a true typophile should certainly consider as it may be the most everlasting piece you design—a sort of last business card in stone. It’s not something I necessarily thought about until nearly two years ago when I had to design one for a family member. I didn’t want to go with the standard designs and type options the monument company provided, so I asked if I could design it myself. In case you’re interested, it only requires an Illustrator/EPS file much like one you would prepare for a vinyl letter cutting machine. Everything has to be converted to outlines and you have to keep in mind how the machine works. I started by doing some research in the cemetery into which the tombstone I was doing was going to be placed. Attached are some photo that served as my primary source of reference.

Well, once you’re dead, the only thing that counts is what other people think of your life. So maybe it’s best to get people who knew you to design your tombstone? The problem, of course, is the risk of them setting it in Comic Sans.
