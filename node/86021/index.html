October 2, 2011 • fritzthefox

I’m having trouble matching this typeface. (see linked photo) Apologies for quality of photo, but it’s the best I have. I am looking to match the lettering on the engine nacelle where it reads “The Boys of Syracuse” (If you zoom in, there is a fair amount of detail) I’ve been through all my blackletter fonts, but nothing comes close. The letter “T” seems to be the toughest to match. It is possible that this lettering was hand painted (these planes were painted before digital typesetting was widespread), so I may never find a perfect match, but if I can get close enough that a little hand-tweaking to the letterforms will put me there, I would be happy.

You can find photo at [[http://www.boomandzoomgraphics.com/downloads/photo_50264.jpg]]

Somewhat off subject, but the lettering actually says The Boys FROM Syracuse, which is a reference to the title of a somewhat silly but immensely amusing motion picture from the 1940s…
