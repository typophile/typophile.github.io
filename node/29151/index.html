2006-10-29 17:59:49 • Alessandro Segalini

And, technically speaking, you can’t copyright a logo; so, the © (opt.-g) symbol wouldn’t apply next to a logo. You can add the ™ (opt.-2) trademark symbol, though.* However, you should only use the ® (opt.-r) registered-trademark symbol when your mark has been registered with the U.S. Patent and Trademark Office or its equivalent in your jurisdiction.

* in the U.S., anyway. Your milage may vary elsewhere in the world.
