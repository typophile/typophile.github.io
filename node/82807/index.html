converted to a TinyURL

I’m not sure how long TInyURL (and similar services) guarantee that their links will work, so I’d be hesitant to put them in a printed document if it’s going to be in use for a while.

But I agree with your general point that long URLs can be a problem in non-clickable text. For that reason I generally try to avoid printing URLs when I can, and if I must use them I try to make them as short as possible.

For example, instead of saying “go to http://www.company.com/support/support.html" say something like “go to the support section of www.company.com”.

Won’t a very low underline collide with the next line?

TinyURL only “translates” the link, so it will work as long as: the target is still around; TinyURL is still around! Some other services actually keep a copy of your target (which might sometimes be illegal I guess) so the first requirement is gone, but AFAIK they require some form of “commitment” (like money :-). You could always host a reference link on a site you control; more work, but at least the link will still be around as long as you want it. Or as long as the Internet is around. :-)

From the Associated Press style guide:

• When a phrase refers to a month, day and year, set off the year with commas. (e.g. Aug. 20, 1964, was the day they had all been waiting for.)

• When a phrase lists only a month and year, do not separate the month and the year with commas. (e.g. February 1980 was his best month.)
