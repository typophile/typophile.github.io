the essence of letters?

November 12, 2008 • vosse

Hi all, I’m a graphic design student and i’m interested in the way how letters are experienced. This started when I got asked by an artist (also a student) how we as designers experience the interrelation of type and image in a design. Do we supplement image with type? Or do we treat image and type equally?

Personally I experience type as an image. As a graphic designer, I play with composition and I ‘have’ to see type merely formal. Now I would like to know how other designers experience (personally) the essence of letters. Do you look at these from a purely functional point of view — a tool to communicate? Or do you relate them as images on their own?

Try research about Mr. Gérard Mermoz.
2008-11-12 14:49:56 • Alessandro Segalini
Both! The intention of letterforms is to communicate visually. The forms themselves have meaning, but at the same time, they should be legible, otherwise they have no function or meaning. The same concept applies to images, as images should also have meaning. Type is just as important as images, because they both have form, and they are both manipulated in different sizes, shapes, colours, structure, etc. The beauty of good design is to make it all work together.

I vacilate between seeing words and seeing the shapes or counterforms of individual letters. Once I started designing fonts things really started to change, because I’ve got this massive list of problems with my fonts floating around in my head, and sometimes I’m just walking along and BOOM I GET GOBSMACKED by letters that solve some problem I didn’t figure out on my own (or didn’t even know was a problem!). And I’m slowly shifting into seeing the white spaces on their own, because in some of the best type those white spaces are a hell of a lot better looking and more interesting than the black.
