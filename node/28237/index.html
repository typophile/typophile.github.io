2006-09-17 23:48:00 • hrant

hrant, I found the book! It took a while because I couldn’t exactly remember which one it was.

It is called:

“130 alphabets & other signs”. It’s from Redstone Press, London, 1991. Edited by Julian Rothenstein and Mel Gooding. ISBN: 1870003268 (hardback) ISBN: 1870003500 (paperback, the one I had)

And for good measure, the text on the back says: “ALPHABETS AND OTHER SIGNS brings together 130 rare and unusual alphabets and a wealth of printers' signs, monograms, logos and trademarks. Containing original essays, pages from ARTS ET MÉTIERS GRAPHIQUES, the great pre-war French magazine of printing, and many other surprises, it is a uniquely unconventional collection of fascinating visual material. Designed also for practical and creative use, it will be an invaluable resource book for artists, poets, typographers and other professionals of the letter, word and sign.

Well, that was basically it. I think the typeface has been identified. Digitized it’s probably Wilma, the woodtype was called Railroad I think.
