Ideal font stacks for web

February 27, 2009 • Dan Gayle

I've seen a lot of rubbish out there about font stacks for web use, so I wanted to see if we could come up with some better ones. It's a matter up for much debate, but I'd like to see what we can come up with. Here is my contribution. Feel free to rip it to shreds, and add/delete. Apologies in advance to Linux users. "Humanist Sans": font-family: "Gill Sans", "Gill Sans MT", "Gill Sans Std", GoudySans, "ITC Goudy Sans Std", "Myriad Pro", "Myriad Web", "Myriad Std", "Bitstream Vera Sans", Verdana, sans-serif; "Oldstyle": font-family: "Adobe Jenson Pro", "Hoefler Text", "Centaur", "Centaur MT", "Garamond Premier Pro", "Stempel Garamond", "Adobe Garamond Pro", "Adobe Garamond", Garamond, Georgia, serif; "Geometric Sans": <<- My worst one. Needs help. font-family: "Futura", "Century Gothic, "Tw Cen MT", "Gill Sans", "Gill Sans MT", "Gill Sans Std", "URW Gothic L", sans-serif;
The only fonts that guarantee cross browser compatibility are Georgia for serif, Verdana for sans. Times New Roman, and Helvetica could be good secondaries. Great article discussing increasingly compatible system fonts.
