Hi Regina! [Ignore Claus, he’s just making fun of a certain misspelled word ;-)]

For [[Carol Twombly]], consult the wiki. And check out this thread.

Here are a couple of quotes on Chaparral, from typophiles. It seems to be quite a polarising face. I, for one, like it very much (using it for my stationery & stuff).

I keep trying to use it and it always seems to fall short. I can’t articulate why. — Patty

Chaparral […] could end up as a standard children’s font for me. — Malte

Chaparral is hard to use well, for sure. — Will

Chaparral is probably the only slab that has optical sizes. I do have to say that it’s really versatile, though it’s not exactly a Scala-kind-of-workhorse. — Bram

What ONE typeface would you have rather not exist? Chaparral. Ok, you beat me up for this one. — Thomas

[[Elements of Typographic Style|Bringhurst]] does mention Chaparral in his list of types. And since he mentions so relatively few types, that means he gave it his seal of approval. — Reed (Bringhurst’s The Solid Form of Language is set in Chaparral.)

Plus: some unisonous thoughts about pairing: Auto is sort of a Chaparral Sans. — Stewf The informal shapes of Chaparral will probably play real nice with Auto. — Carl
