2008-06-15 18:51:11 • Frode Bo Helland
Being bang in the middle of that demographic, I would second the idea of a slab serif. Legibility is an issue for those 40+, so try to avoid text type with too fine details. Sure, you can go with a larger sized serif face (or sans), but that cuts down on the amount of copy per page, and makes things look banal. So a sturdy text face would be good, and a slab serif would be considered more sporty than say a Clarendon. Bodoni Egyptian is mine.

I’m afraid I don’t think any of those have enough punch and snap for magazine headlines, except perhaps for the Black weights. Also, they have all been a bit too well-used in corporate communications.

I just tried out Clan, which I got a few weights of with the Font Stars CD, and it looks quite interesting, contemporary shapes, and with a massive x-height. These settings are with Bodoni Egyptian at 9 on 10. I would work out a strategy, for instance use Clan Extra Bold u&lc, with Clan Book all Caps – but no Book u&lc settings (that style has bad contrast with Bodoni E).
