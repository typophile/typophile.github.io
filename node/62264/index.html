2009-09-22 15:58:38 • Mark Simonson
There are hundreds if not thousands. But the line is not very clear between "knock-off" and other forms of "similar," and things can get weird and complicated. For example, 20th Century was a knockoff of Futura, but was redrawn to match the widths (and look?) of ITC Avant Garde and the result was renamed Century Gothic. Monotype Corsiva was drawn from the beginning to match the widths of ITC Zapf Chancery, yet the letterforms are sufficiently distinct that I don't think of it as an imitation or knock-off. Book Antiqua is a straight-up knock-off of Palatino, but well done, and the same companies (Microsoft & Monotype) also now have the original.... Corel used to have hundreds of knock-off fonts under "similar" names. They changed to licensing from the Bitstream and URW libraries for all but a few. Bitstream has lots of designs that were originally unlicensed but well-executed, but often involving the original type designers, just not the original foundries... and then they got an arrangement with Linotype as well. That's not a comprehensive list, and then there are cases which are more disputed.... It's a pretty wacky world out there. Cheers, T

[img:sites/default/files/old-images/gothamavenir_5719.gif] I would never describe Gotham as a knock-off, but the similarities between Gotham and Avenir are undeniable. I think of them as two strong typefaces aiming at near targets, from different angles, that happen to resolve with similar solutions. But the two behave quite differently in use. And many would consider Gotham superior to Avenir (a debate for another time). If we're examining visual similarity, it's one thing, but if we're examining the designer's intent, that's another. With sans serif typefaces, I often find the similarities stronger than the differences. Most of the differences don't really come out between similar faces until you start typesetting and designing layouts with them, testing their unique capabilities and tolerances. There's an endless stream of Frutiger-like sans typefaces out there, but I'm not sure they could/should all be considered Frutiger knock-offs.
