2004-06-27 12:02:15 • glutton
It would be amusing to build up a foundry pattern out of wood cut-out letters and the sort of herringbone background pattern and take it to an iron foundry to have one cast made. I once had a press part made in iron, and the prices was surprisingly cheap. The part was a simple Colts Armory platen, about 15"x21"x2.5". The cost of the iron casting was around $125.

You would spend the lion's share of the budget in making the wood foundry pattern, and a lot of time with a rotary power brush distressing and aging the casting. Not an approach for the timid, but a valid one, and the results would be puzzling to all of your contemporaries; wondering how you Photoshopped it! On the other hand, you could go at it in Photoshop, and ignore my attempts at making trouble.

Personally, I'd go for the pattern and casting.

James, here's an attempt … (i'm a sucker for these requests).

1. clone tool to strip away the SNL and
leave just the manhole cover
2. set type (trade gothic, double bold)
3. cheat with some alien skin outer bevel
4. then alien skin inner bevel
5. use the dodge/burn tool and the
clone tool to clean up a bit.

if I was actually doing this for a project,
i'd try harder to get the angles of the shadows right
and to make the highlight and color
match a bit better.

2004-06-28 14:20:53 • glutton
Here's what I came up with

1) Healing brush the old type away
2) Set Type in average color of the surroundings
3) Apply a bevel and contour (messed with the settings at bit) <– I was actually suprised at how good this was alone at approximating the effect. I thought I'd need expensive plugins or something.
4) Applied a drop shadow.

Certainly not perfect, but I'd pocket the $2k I shaved on the photoshoot and buy a dual G5. That will be my nice souvenir!
