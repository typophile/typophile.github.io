Inflections--what evil lies within?

January 30, 2007 • William Berkson

FontLab's Font Auditor supposedly flags "inflections". The manual says that "it is better to replace such curves with two curves." The accompanying picture shows an 's' curve like this: [img:sites/default/files/old-images/S curve_4013.gif] I wrote "supposedly flags" because I don't really see them flagged by Font Audit, though that option is checked in the 'options' window. (Extrema without points are flagged, but that it seems is another matter.) Now it is convenient to use 'S' curves since that is what you can do with a Bezier curve that you can't do with a quadratic spline. What harm is done by inflections? Is it a question of functioning of the font? Of aesthetics? What is the issue here, if any?
From the dusty bowels of my memory, I seem to recall that it is only a hinting issue and that it may be difficult to hint such curves properly. Once again my warning--the deepest dark corners of Chris's mind may not recall as acurately as they should :-) That being said. I am surprised that FontAudit did not flag the above curve for missing extremma. If you draw such a curve rotated so that there are no missing extremma points, you have the normal middle section of an "s" situation. I often use this technique when drawing an s. I may later tweek it a tad by adiing a midpoint for more control but generally get as close as I can without the midpoint. ChrisL
