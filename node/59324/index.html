Debate Club logo

June 24, 2009 • reinis

Hello, I’m working on a logo proposal for a debate club I’m a member of. The bottom line reads “Debate Club of the University of Latvia”. The speech bubbles point in opposing directions and represent a debate having two sides. It’s a pretty simple “riddle” and speech bubbles are overused in design, but I think their use is justified here. I’d be very pleased to hear more critiques of this logo, since it hasn’t been accepted by all members of our club yet, and I’m looking for possible improvements.

I like the idea. I think the bottom 2 work best, since they can be dressed up with color, yet are still able to be reproduced in 1 color. The full name underneath reads quite small to me, but it looks like it will be difficult to go any larger without using another line. I’ll think about it and let you know if there’s anything else that could be improved. Thanks for sharing.

2009-06-24 09:17:33 • apankrat

Well, number 5 is looking a bit tacky, to me, along with three and two, because of the use of contours… just feels amateur-like. I’d definitely go with number one. If people can’t agree on the colours – which I think work out just fine, actually, and I’m always partial towards black and white –, you can always change those, but I think it’ll work out best in the end. Oh, and I’d push the K just a tiny little bit closer to the D, or maybe pull the D towards the K and away from the U just a bit, too. Or maybe just pull D and K together away from the U. Kerning is always a hit and miss thing, right? One other thing I would try is using small-caps instead of regular caps; these look a bit too tall and caps really weren’t made to be used together. They make it harder to read, I think, and look a bit disproportionate.

About “VIDEO”; you probably shouldn’t space it out like that, although it does make it reminiscent of other logos for video stuff. The whole logo for video looks classic (or “old”), so if that’s not what you’re going for you might want to change it.

So all in all, I think you’re definitely on the right track with number one.

About the text below… that’s a tough one. You might want to try putting it in front of the logo, you know, bigger, in two lines or even in four lines, since the right part of the K is leaving some white space open which could be seen as unbalancing for the whole thing.
