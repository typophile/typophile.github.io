2010-11-09 20:43:45 • charles ellertson

There will probably be many instances of this sort where someone will not be willing to re-enter the equations and will have to resort to the sort of solution you’ve settled on. I might point out for others, though, that the recommendation to utilize Word was predicated on one actually creating the equations in Word, and then porting these into InDesign. So what you have ended up doing, so far as I can tell, is just add extra unnecessary steps to the procedure—if you are scanning the images there is little reason to make those scans into PDFs, rather you could just place the images directly into the InD file. The principal reason to make PDFs from a Word-typeset equation is to have an equation that is not of fixed resolution and therefore is better suited for print purposes and is scalable. This, incidentally, is an issue using plugins which rely on plopping images into the InD source, although the ability to have an active, editable linked equation is important in many workflows.

Aside from the sort of situation you ended up in, there are several solutions to choose from. Most do rely on creating the equation in Word, and then exporting some object (image or otherwise) back to InDesign. InMath allows for the creation of equations directly in InDesign using arbitrary fonts (that is, unlike many proprietary systems, is not reliant on its own set of custom fonts). This approach has its weaknesses, as do all solutions at some level—but generally could be summed up as not having all the refinements one might want that are available to a purpose-built Math font and layout system. It would probably be acceptable for many situations, and in many regards may not be any worse then other options—it all depends on what compromises to quality you are willing to make.

For the sake of those who might want to take advantage of the native equation editing features present in Word 2007 & Word 2010 (Windows-only), then I have made up [[http://www.tiro.com/Articles/Word_to_InD_Math.pdf|this document]] which describes one simple solution for the porting of Word-made equations to an InDesign source.
