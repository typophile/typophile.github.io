Column Gutter Width

January 28, 2008 • AndrewSipe

I’m working on a factsheet design and was curious if there’s a formula for figuring out [[column]] [[gutter]] widths.

The factsheet will be a single sided 8.5" by 11" sheet. The [[margin]]s will either be .25" or .35" on the right and left with a similar top margin and a .5 bottom margin. though, I might just go .5" all around, it’s still up in the air.

I’ve decided to go with a 3 column layout, using 10/12 pt serifed type (probably Karmina).

Hopefully someone can enlighten me. Thanks.

You need to make the gutter between columns wider than a space, of course. 1 pica (1/6 of an inch) is about the minimum I would go with.

On a factsheet, keep in mind that it may get put into a binder, which calls for a 3/4 to 1 inch margin on the binding edge.

2008-01-28 16:54:45 • AndrewSipe
History? I don’t think so. In the old newspapers, a rule was put between columns, and I think they were about 4-6 points thick (not the rule, which was generally a half point or hairline, but the metal strip it sat on). When newspapers removed to a more open layout, with larger type sizes, often fewer and wider columns, the rules were removed and gutters were widened to give the visual separation between columns that used to be created by the rule (aka vertical line).
