Suggest a display face for higher education web site.

January 3, 2008 • ctcliff

Hi - I'm designing a web site that will serve educators and students. I'm trying to decide on a display face and I thought you nice folks might offer your suggestions. Here's what I'm trying to accomplish: - The site is going to serve a wide age group, so it has to be regal enough for crusty old professors w/o being too dull or pretentious for students. I'd like to try to capture some of the romance of higher education while keeping a sense of humor about it (closer to "Animal House" than the New England Journal of Medicine). - The site itself is going to have a dynamic identity that will accommodate the name and colors of a particular institution. Therefore, the typography will need to be unique enough to create a cohesive identity, w/o completely swallowing up the institution's psuedo-identity. - It's going to be web-based, so most of a page's text is going to be in some system font. I haven't decided on serifs or not, but most likely either Georgia or Arial/Helvetica. I've just started looking around, so maybe you all can help me get started. I like what they're doing over at the new Space Collective, but I think that display face might be too playful. Any suggestions?? Thanks in advance!
> I haven’t decided on serifs or not Decide on sans. Serifs are harder to read online for most people. The only thing to deal with is that sans generally have larger x-heights, and therefore should be presented at 80% the default size. (Do not specify points in your CSS, and then people using all browsers will be able to change to a larger size if their eyes require it.) You can use graphics or sIFR to make the headings and subhead distinctive with nice fonts.
