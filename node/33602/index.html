2007-05-09 21:48:55 • wormwood

Advice: Pick your battles and check your ego at the door. Not everything you design will end up in your portfolio. Clients will d**k you around and muck up your designs and ask you to do things you think are awful, but they’re the ones with the money. You have to decide which ones are worth the fight, and when it’s best to just give them what they want. You are not in charge, unfortunately, as long as they are paying you.

There are a lot of talented designers out there and if you have too much attitude the clients will turn elsewhere.

2007-05-09 22:28:17 • pattyfab

Here’s another one: have a contract or some sort of written agreement before you start working on a job. Include some sort of kill fee (usually a percentage of the final fee) in case you start the job and you & the client find you can’t work together.

I try to break down contracts into the various components of the job, both because it tends not to freak the client out as much as a bottom line total and also in case the scope of the job changes - it’s more clear what you are owed for work you’ve done.
