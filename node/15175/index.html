Previously: http://typophile.com/node/14378 By perceived popular demand (over 1300 reads/visitors for part 1) here's a second (or third if you count the Perpetua one embedded in the Matrix thread) challenge: Some people don't mind, some people* might actually like it, but over the years I've heard complaints about the terminal of the "r" in Stone Serif, in an otherwise commendable design. I highly doubt it might cause reading stutters like the "g" in Matrix, but calling that terminal shape "sickly, cloying" (as I've done previously) is just brutal honesty in my book. It's so ridiculous that even in their own "On Stone" specimen book they make fun of it - from page 46: http://themicrofoundry.com/other/Stone-r.gif _ And you thought I was just being mean... * Souvenir/phototype/TNT/mushy/70s types? Other glyphs ("a", "c", "f", "y") have that basic terminal shape too, but it is in the "r" that it rears its annoying little head (quite literally it seems) most prominently. Ergo: if you can fix that, the others are easy. And while you're at it, and especially if you go ahead and mod the other four glyphs with that most affronting appendage, I might suggest you try to fix the bowl of the "a" too. Please. Two final things: - Since this is more of a finish thing and not a structure thing like Matrix was, we'll need to see polished forms, large. - The ethical caveats from before still stand of course: don't use a swiped font - work from a rendering/scan if you have to; and certainly don't redistribute any cannibalized outlines. May the flogging stick be with you.™ -- Later on in this thread I think we might try a Stone Informal challenge as well. hhp
