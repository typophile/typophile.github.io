2007-01-16 16:56:13 • Si_Daniels

Actually, the use of “type font” may make sense here, to distinguish “font”, as physical or digital instance, from “type face”, which is a design capable of being expressed in various media by various hands. The idea being wrestled with, is that a particular font is a dateable iteration of a typeface design.

The problem is that in popular usage, “font” has come to mean the same as “typeface”, losing the distinction between the two. So while we may think “type” is a redundant prefix to “font”, that’s not so for the writer here, who may be attempting to re-establish the distinction.
