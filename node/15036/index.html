From what I can tell from the BIG ITC Book of 1980, Caslon 223 is a display cut with really fine hairline strokes for the thins. The overall shape of the characters don’t differ too much from Caslon 224 which had been modified by Ed Benguiat in 1982 for text (why???).

In both cases the bowl of the lowercase “e” isn’t as small as the provided sample. Here’s a really bad sample of Caslon 223 found at Datascan UK.

One thing to notice is that the tittle of 224 is much closer to the sample than the 223 tittle (display face).
