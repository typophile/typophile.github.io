Canada is a bad choice. In Quebec, the most bilingual of the bilingual provinces, English is not treated equally. French must be larger and (I think) to the right or above. In the other bilingual provinces (Ontario, New Brunswick, Manitoba, Saskatchewan, I think) English is the dominant language although it gets fair treatment with French.

Our federal government tends to try to make the department names similar in both languages, and if not, shows them side by side (order can be exchanged, depending on the predominant language where the sign will be). See http://www.tc.gc.ca/en/menu.htm and http://www.tc.gc.ca/fr/menu.htm
