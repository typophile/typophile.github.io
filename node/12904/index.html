Acording to Lupton (2004), in metal type, a kerned letter extends past the lead slug that supports it, allowing two letteres to sit more closely together. Does it mean that the eye of the letter could go beyond the lateral sidebearings? Bola http://planeta.terra.com.br/arte/bola/bala/
Bola, Creio que seja possível fazê-lo, mas a peça resultante seria tão fraca que provavelmente não suportaria o uso. Além disso, um kern tão grande implicaria na necessidade da letra seguinte ter um espaço "livre" (branco) grande. Nesse caso uma melhor alternativa seria produzir uma ligatura. I believe it is possible to do that, but the resulting piece would be so weak it probably would not support being used. Besides, such a big "kern" would imply the need for the following letter to have a wide free (white-) space. In this case a better alternative would be to produce a ligature.

Eduardo, Kerning between two faces or a ligature could be used in the most common cases, as between the "A" and the "V". Acording to Lupton, it seems that kerning was applied with moving types.
