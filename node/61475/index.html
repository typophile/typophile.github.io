2009-08-29 21:31:50 • SuperUltraFabulous
It’s certainly Palatino. It’s possible that the type was set in Monotype, and the malformed ascender on the f is what they used to call a broken letter, a place where the metal of the type got dented in handling. Until the 1970s, most books were set on the Linotype or Monotype, and the printing plates were made with negatives shot from repro proofs. This process didn’t disappear in the US until well into the 1980s, and may have hung on a bit longer in the UK.
