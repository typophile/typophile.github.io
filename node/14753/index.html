Cyrillic De question

August 22, 2005 • Chris Washer

Hi, The roman typefaces I am familiar with have mostly handwritten origins (save for the 2-storey 'a' and the binocular 'g', I assume). I am confused by the cyrillic 'De' character. It looks pretty awkward to write. What's the deal? The rest of the characters look simple enough. - Chris
even the 2-storey ‘a’ and the binocular ‘g’ have handwritten origins. take a look at the [[carolingian minuscule]] and you'll see both in a calligraphic hand. the same is true for [[cyrillic]] letters: they were all based on handwritten models. It is true that some were tweaked to become the cyrillic alphabet we are used to seeing today, but even the old [[poluustav]] models correlate quite strongly with the forms that are used today. To better understand where these letter forms are coming from, i'd recomment [[Vladimir Yefimov]]'s article on "[[Civil Type]] and Kis Cyrillic," which can be found in [[Language Culture Type]].
