September 5, 2007 • rondomingue

I’m working on illustrating and designing a Tarot deck.

http://rondomingue.com/2007/09/04/temperance/

I want to add the title of the card at bottom. However, Its debatable since some decks don’t have them. The Tarot of Marseilles does have titles and my set is loosely patterned after it. I like the French decks so I like the typography to match the style. I chose Baskerville initially but I’m not certain of it. Can anyone offer some insight or suggestions what I should do typographically?

Excellllent. Some suggestions:

Storm Jannon. Based on an antique French model, excellent update by Frantisek Storm.

Christian Gothic. No, it’s not released, but I’d be happy to let you work with a beta version if you felt it was right.

Della Robbia. It’s based on an Italian model rather than French, but has the right flavor of antiquity and matches the low stroke contrast but sharp features of the card you showed.

Beaufort by Nick Shinn. A beautiful modern update to the same genre as Della Robbia, and by a designer who’s still breathing.

Most of all, have fun! Go with whatever works best visually with your artwork.

Oddly, tho' not really authentic for a French deck (perhaps one based on the Swiss, however), I like the suggestion of the Della Robbia. Look, carefully whether you’ll want a boldface to make an impression with the illustrations on the Major Arcana. Brings up a good question: Are you fully illustrating the minor Arcana–i.e, the Ace of Cups, Nine of Pentacles, etc.–as many decks do? If you’ve thought of just using the number and suit for the Minor, I’d suggest having a look at the Crowley deck.

Good luck with this. There are many, many variations. It takes a stout heart, I think, to wade in to a design with such a long history of different versions going back so many years.
