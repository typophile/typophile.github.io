DIN Unusable?

February 2, 2010 • bstrom

Hey, I was reading the story of FF DIN from Albert-Jan Pool and he had this to say: Erik Spiekermann saw the old DIN typeface on the market in two weights and he noticed that several designers began using it. And he thought: “why is this happening?” These were leading designers, he saw them going on and he thought: “Hm, we should do something about it. Why do they use this unusable typeface? Let’s ask someone who can make something useful out of it. Then it might work even better.” My question is, What, exactly, is Spikermann talking about, and why does he consider the old DIN typeface to be unusable?. I own DIN 1451 (Mittelschrift and Engschrift), as well as 30640 (light and condensed) from Adobe's Font Folio. Are these the weights in question?
Well, when you commission the redesign of an existing typeface it is highly recommended to declare its predecessor as unusable ;) From a contemporary typographer’s / type designer’s POV, there are some readability issues with the original DIN 1451 typefaces, e.g. its contrast, the clumsy forms, etc. Did you also read the articles linked at the bottom of the page? Really, it’s all in there, especially here: http://www.magwerk.com/mag.php?magazine=encore&language=en&issue=15&page=76 That said, even nowadays, with all the “new” versions of DIN around, a lot of designers still prefer the old, clumsy one for posters, headlines etc.
