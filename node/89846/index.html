Hagel (serif typeface)

February 7, 2012 • Martin Silvertant

Hagel is my latest typeface. It's still in an early development stage and right now I'm focusing more on my grotesque 'Celcius'. Now and then though I work on Hagel. The concept is to develop a fairly condensed, clean and modern serif with some classical elements. Hagel v.6: http://www.fichier-pdf.fr/2012/02/12/hagel-v6/hagel-v6.pdf Hagel v.7: http://www.fichier-pdf.fr/2012/02/12/hagel-v7/hagel-v7.pdf Hagel v.8: http://www.fichier-pdf.fr/2012/02/12/hagel-v8/hagel-v8.pdf Hagel v.9: http://www.fichier-pdf.fr/2012/02/12/hagel-v9/hagel-v9.pdf Hagel v.11: http://www.fichier-pdf.fr/2012/02/12/hagel-v11/hagel-v11.pdf
The eye of the italic /e/ should slope down more near the stem I think. Also, the direction of Hagel is not quite clear yet but I quite like to adapt a few Garamond elements. What are your thoughts about an italic /h/ like in Garamond with an inward serif on its leg? I want to keep the construction modern though. I also might have to make the italic h/n slightly wider.
