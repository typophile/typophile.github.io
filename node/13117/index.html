June 16, 2005 â€¢ oddjobboy

Oh type gurus, might you be so good as to offer your suggestions for an application which I might employ to search our voluminous company font archive for bitmap and font outlines with matching FOND IDs?

Back in the day, Font Reserve used to be the bees knees for extracting such information but these days it misbehaves terribly (at least on my system). Suitcase doesn’t offer the option of searching for FOND ID, and neither does FontAgent Pro.

I have recently employed Morrison SoftDesign’s Font Doctor to bring order to our collections, but it doesn’t pay much attention to the differences between fonts of the same name from different foundries, opting to simply keep one copy of a font of a particular name. I am left thinking if only Font Doctor could build its archive in a similarly robust fashion to the Font Reserve of yore (ie: same-named fonts neatly stowed in folders named after their foundry), then I would be sure that my bitmaps paired with my printer fonts.

As it is, I’m left chasing around my Mac for fonts with matching IDs to better ensure compatibilty. (Please tell me I’m doing right in this respect?!)

Keeping abreast of the licensing of our company font assets is proving a vast undertaking, so if you feel a particular application really cuts the mustard when it comes to building accurate font collections, I’d be extremely inerested to hear about it!
