Overall it looks nice! I prefer the horizontal composition on the top right. I agree with Michael's comments on the icons. However I quite like the existing colours. Since you've got fairly self explanatory pictograms, I wonder if it's okay even if a colour doesn't readily connote the respective idea. I'm not sure whether you need the vertical rule between the symbol and the type. Also do you realy need the period marks after the 'I' and the 'L'. I think not. I think you should adjust the alignment between the name and descriptor visually and not let the software do it. That way the 'P' of the Plumbing would not align to the left edge of the serif of the 'I', but rather to vertical stroke of the 'I'. Similarly, the 'S' in Gas would align to the vertical stroke of the 'Y'.

On the top left example in the link you provided: No, no, no! Plumbing is below the house and water always finds the lowest place, so the symbol for plumbing should be on the bottom, not the top. The sun is in the sky, solar panels on the roof, and lighting on the ceiling, so that symbol should always be on top. I recommend that you use an arrangement based upon a hexagonal tiling, with solar on the top, plumbing on the bottom, and heat and gas in the middle. Beyond a basis in hexagonal tiling, I feel you should follow the surface tension lead even further (hexagonal tiling is the most perimeter efficient arrangement, and so it the most surface-area efficient two dimensional arrangement), and base the shapes on how soap bubbles actually fit together (soap bubbles always for the most efficient arrangement), as seen on the left here: [[http://www.nature.com/embor/journal/v5/n11/images/7400274-f2.jpg]] Rotating it would form equally valid arrangements (especially by 90 degrees), and I'm only showing this orientation because it is what I could find.
