I’m trying to find a font to use in everyday: emails, text and documents for a Live performing business bringing a new type of Immersive audio visual live shows.

I’m searching for a futuristic font with square lines to transmit the following messages: Futuristic, forward thinking, cutting edge, fresh, next generation.

Doing a quick search; I came up with the following fonts (see attached image)

A- Anita semi square B- Capella extra condensed C- Nextwave condensed D- Nano Low E- Radio space F- 5th Agent

Which one do you think could suit best ?

Have you got an advice for a better font ?

Disclaimer: I’m an old dude and clearly not in your target market.

I am a graphic designer, though, and based on my experience, nobody–not even cutting-edge, next-generation people–likes to read whole sentences or paragraphs in all caps, so I’d advise against B, C, and D.

E and F are fine for a logo, but too hard to read for a whole sentence or paragraph. But maybe that’s the effect you’re aiming for: something that looks like a secret code that only the cool kids can read, and their parents (me) can’t read.

emails, text

Won’t work. Emails and texts use fonts the recipients have on their computers/tablets/phones. If you use an unusual font which those folks don’t have, it will appear in a different font on their devices.

An alternative for email might be to send the message as a graphic or PDF to preserve the fonts and appearance. Keep in mind that some folks (mostly on cell phones or tablets using data plans) don’t download graphics automatically so they won’t go over their bandwidth allotment, which may reduce readership.

Another option is to include a link to a web page.
