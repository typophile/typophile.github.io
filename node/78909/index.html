From what I recall of these style-faking processes, first you have to skew or slant the type, usually 10° though it looks like more in your example, then overstrike it four times, that is copy and paste the same text over its self in the same place, offsetting by a small amount each time such that the strokes thicken without creating new voids among the intersections, if that makes any sense. This of course is easier to do in a drawing program like Adobe Illustrator, but for a 300 page document it might be more practical to draw up your own custom derivative fonts (strictly speaking about getting the job done, not dealing with the messy font licensing hazards involved, or even considering your deadline. I do not envy your situation). Somebody please come up with better advice than mine?!
