in the lower part of the s as it meets with the backbone of the d it looks like a stylized ‘P’. logos with just letters are tricky and since you admittedly dabble, do you have access to illustrator? you can get creative and show off your skills and have your logo be one of the things you’re must proud of. i’d say use a serif font instead, the lowercase letters would be more recognizable and you’d have the benefit of the serifs to work with the interlacing of the letters.

i’d recommend also letting the letters sit on the same baseline, the current logo looks topheavy to the right, like it could topple over.

a wispy font also runs the risk of being to thin it could be unreadable if you print it small enough and/or on a porous stock or labels.

hope it helps and keep us in the loop on your progress.
