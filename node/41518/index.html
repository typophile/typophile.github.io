Do you have a license that permits this sort of conversion? Except for Adobe’s, few retail font licenses do. Or is it a shareware or freeware font?

Sounds like TransType Pro didn’t do as good a job as one might like. Assuming it is legal to do so, you should first try to redo the conversion in TransType Pro, modifying the menu names along the way. If TransType doesn’t allow the degree of fiddling you need, you might have to go to FontLab Studio or some other higher-end program.

Well, if it’s a typical retail font, you need a license to use the font at all. When you say “I was given a .pfb and .pfm” that sounds rather like somebody just supplied you with the font for the task. In many cases that wouldn’t be legal, any more than them supplying you with a copy of Photoshop to do a project would be legal, unless they specifically bought a license for you.

But then again there are shareware and freeware fonts, and it’s possible that whoever gave you the font had a license that permitted it.
