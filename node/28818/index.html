2006-10-16 05:17:30 • dave bailey

You will need thicker strokes on the U’s, C and B. I would also look at a narrower B and broader Fs and Ts. You might also add some overshoot on the curved letters. This might give you some direction for the S middle stroke, think of it as drawing it with a broad nib. http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/urw/britannic/
