You don't need to worry about the "micro grid", i.e. the unit grid, in FontLab Studio. All the points automatically snap to integer coordinates, as this is a requirement of the font formats. In Fontographer, you need to manually invoke the "Align Points to Grid" command, otherwise the results of rounding may be a bit unpredictable. In addition to the unit grid, FontLab Studio offers a "macro grid" every 100 units, or whatever you choose it to be. This is useful for drawing pixelfonts (outline fonts where "staircase outlines" simulate pixel appearance) or other sorts of highly regularized designs. What IS important in FontLab Studio for better screen appearance is that after you have drawn your typeface, you choose Tools / Action / Hints and Guidelines / Autohint (and enable the "Apply to entire font" option), and then go to File / Font Info / Hinting Settings, and click on the "Auto zones" button, and on the next page, you click on the "Auto stems" button). Performing these three simple steps will ensure much better screen appearance of the font than not doing it. A.
