Experimental Hinglish Script

November 12, 2009 • rohini

Hinglish is what happens when people mix Hindi and English while conversing and chatting.

  • Hindi words are substituted for English words in sentences
  • English words are used in Hindi sentences
  • Phrases are added from either language for emphasis
  • combination words are morphed, by conjugation and verbification of nouns

This trend has grown significantly and examples are seen in India in

  • International branding - coke, pepsi, McDonalds etc
  • Chatting, conversations, radio, TV, soaps, Movies, Coomericials, Posters…

So my experiment questions

  • If a combination can happen on a sentence and word level
  • is it possible to push the envelope at a character level

James - this idea is different than what you have linked above. In the samples Rohini have posted, the base consonats are Latin, but the vowel signs (matras) are from devanagari. Here, accents (matras) has not been used just for the style sake - they actually change the meaning of the words, just like they do in Hindi.

And in order to understand this, one must know Hindi! :)

The one shortcoming I see in this kind of Hinglish is that “aa” matra can look like an [English] “i” at a quick glance. So, for instance, Banini instead of Banana (which I would have transcribed as Banaana, personally, without the final “aa”).

There are other potential confusions between scripts, especially with handwriting – like Mango, where your M looks a little like Devanagari “la” and your G looks a little like “da”. I don’t know if this is more or less likely to be a problem for someone coming more from the Hindi side.

But fun!
