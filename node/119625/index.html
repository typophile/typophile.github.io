Murray Hill Gothic by Photo-Lettering (1940's)

September 17, 2014 • ralphsmouse

Any of you guys have any samples of Murray Hill Gothic? Ed Rondthaler talks about it in his book Life With Letters.

The family had several weights and they were all based on Railroad Gothic, but had a proper lowercase. It got a lot of use in the 40’s and 50’s, from what I can tell.

That would be Murray Hill, a handlettered gothic which originated at Photo-Lettering in New York back in those days. The word “gothic” was not a part of the name, so it would be confusing to many due to the script font of the same name by ATF. It must have been very popular due to the wide range they show it in.

In their index it is listed as follows: Murray Hill, 9 weights, 4 widths Murray Hill Open, 7 varieties Murray Hill Shaded, 11 varieties Murray Hill Stencil, 3 varieties

If you can find a Photo-Lettering Alphabet Thesaurus Volume 2 specimen book somewhere, you will be able to see samples of it. The alphabet showings in the back of the book are not complete and are quite small. You might also contact House Industries about it since they bought the entire Photo-Lettering library. They may have already issued the fonts in digital form under another name.
