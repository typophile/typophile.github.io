Groovin is noticeably “1960s”. Seymour Chwast’s Artone logo (1964) is iconic for this “fat bottom” style. The font is probably named after the Young Rascals song (1967). However, although bell-bottom trousers/pants were invented in the 60s, their heyday was the early 70s.
2008-01-24 17:55:02 • Nick Shinn

“Groovin” was a very 60s term that came out of the Haight hippie vernacular. The lettering was best seen in work known as Family Dog http://www.wolfgangsvault.com/se/family-dog-series/FD.html

The early 70s was actually what most people remember as 60s look. The Beatles Rubber Soul album shows a glimpse of that fat bottom look. There was a great Art Nouveau influence as well.
