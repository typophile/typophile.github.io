The question I would have to ask first is this. Are you planning to copy an existing font (dingbat)? That is where intellectual property rights comes into play.

2004-06-30 07:11:24 • Giampa
Could be a bug of some sort in our applications. But Kathy said it affected all the Creative Suite applications, so it's more than just an InDesign glitch.

In my experience, these sorts of things are usually font bugs. Overwhelming majority of the time, in fact. Sometimes they are bugs that don't affect other applications because they don't use the fonts in the same way.

For example, this month I ran into a problem where some Type 1 fonts from a major second-tier font vendor were behaving improperly in the CS apps for a major New York/national paper trying to deploy the Creative Suite. Turned out the problem was that they had messed up in the PostScript FullName, where they had left out the style as part of the name and made the FullName just equal the family name. Turns out that the PostScript spec itself says the style should be part of the FullName, and our apps were relying on it as part of making consistent cross-platform menu names for Type 1 fonts.

Anyway, just goes to show that there are obscure things about how fonts are supposed to be made, but often these are easy to fix.
