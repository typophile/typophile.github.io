  valign=top>\r\n\r\nby Alastair Johnston\r\nPublisher: Oak Knoll Books; (October
  2000)  \r\nPaperback: 220 pages\r\nISBN: 1584560096 \r\nPrice: $39.25&nbsp;&nbsp;<b><A
  HREF=\"http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1584560096/typophile-20\">Buy It!</a></b>\r\n\r\n<b>Reviewed
  by Poltroon:</b>\r\nThis book started out to show how Dadaism and concrete poetry
  were foreshadowed in the anonymous nonsense texts of type specimen books in the
  nineteenth century and grew into a much larger cultural study of the reading habits,
  politics and amusements of that era. The type specimens (many of which are illustrated)
  are a springboard to a wider discussion of many topics from hermits to the alcoholism
  of printers. Most of the fun occurs in the footnotes (there is even a fake footnote
  that can be deciphered as a reference to PALE FIRE by Nabokov). There are checklists
  of rare specimens in public & private collections in Britiain & the US which can
  be consulted & very useful family trees showing how the holdings of various typefounders
  passed from hand to hand. In addition new light is shed on the earliest American
  wood types cut for display in the 1830s as well as some interesting stuff on the
  graphic appeal of large type.<br>\r\n\r\n<b><i>Do you own this book? Add your review!</i></b>\r\n\r\n</td>\r\n<td
