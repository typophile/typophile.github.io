About a twelve years ago, I wrote a translation of Ugo Foscolo's* poem 'Dei Sepolcri' ('On Tombs'), a kind of meandering neo-classical meditation on the meaning of mortuary monuments and memorials. And as a result I've often thought, since, about what I would like for a gravestone. Not so much the design per se (other than that it would be handcarved, probably in welsh slate: no router'd granite for me, thank you!), but about what I would like written there. I currently favour Domine dilexi decorem domus tu
