Let’s talk about about Plantin’s Reale Romaine ( Also known as Van den Keere’s Paragon Roman )

I found it today on page 253 of “16th century printing types” by H. D. L. Vervliet, 1968.

According to Jan Middendorp in Dutch Type it has inspired Fred Smeijers ( Arnhem?), And Tobias Frere Jones' Poyntner Oldstyle, and Frank van Blokland.

I would love to see the face in use somewhere. I have one book listed: Rariorum aliquot stirpium historia, by C Clusius in 1576. Do you know of others? What techniques or resources would you use to find others?

Thanks!

Frank says that it is DTL Van de Keere. Hrant says the Fred font design is Renard. And Marina Garone Gravier suggested these books:

  • Harry Carter, “Plantin’s Types and Their Maker’s, Gedenkboek der Plantin Dagen, Antwerp, 1955.
  • Typographcia Plantiniana. II Early Inventories of Punches, Matrices, and Moulds, in the Plantin-Moretus Archives, De Gulden Passer, 37e. Jaargeng 1959.
  • Harry Carter, “The Types of Christopher Plantin” The Library, vol. II, 1956

In Counterpunch, Fred Smeijers states that it was Van den Keere’s 2-line Double Pica Roman (or Gros Canon) that inspired Renard, not the Paragon Roman. But yes, Renard was inspired by Van den Keere, not Arnhem. (Arnhem traces its roots to Romain du Roi and neo-classical styles, not Dutch oldstyle.)

– K.

I have a copy of Vervliet & Carter’s Type Specimen Facsimiles 1618, which includes the Plantin Folio Specimen c. 1585. (Got this at an ATypI auction almost a decade ago.)

There is a showing of a Reale Romaine, and the annotation explains: “One of the best Romans cut by Hendrik van den Keere. He cut it for Plantin in 1575. [citation] … No use outside the Plantinian office is known to us.”

Reale seems to be between Ascendonica and Parangonne in size, approx. 21 pts.
