Ted Passinisi: Australian Designer

October 11, 2006 • Graham Rendoth

I am a graphic designer in Sydney, Australia. Currently doing research on Edward (Ted) Passanissi, a little-known Australian designer in the 50s-80s. Apparently during 1956/57 he studied in the Netherlands at 'Lettergieterij Amsterdam' (Amsterdam Type Foundry). * Can anyone advise if this well known foundry was also a 'school' and maybe ran a 1, 2 or 3 year course, or perhaps Ted could have been something like an 'apprentice' or possibly an 'assistant'. * Was 'Lettergieterij Amsterdam' more than a type foundry? * I have found reference that Henk Gianotten may have been there at this time, and perhaps he or some other person could possibly have met Ted. Any suggestions? Thanks. Graham
Henk Gianotten began in 1963 at Lettergieterij Amsterdam v/h N. Tetterode, or ‘Tetterode’ as it was called when it merged into the Bührmann–Tetterode concern in that same year. So if Passanissi interned (my guess) at LA in 1956/57 they probably did not meet… Tetterode wasn’t just a foundry. It sold machinery to the printing industry, eg typesetting equipment, presses etc. It does so even now. Some history can be found here: http://www.buhrmann.com/eng/about_buhrmann/history.aspx (english) Dutch website of Tetterode’s: http://www.tetterode.nl/ Henk Gianotten can be reached through henk [at] gianotten.com
