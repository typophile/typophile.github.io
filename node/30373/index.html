Your course is graphic design I assume?

Start by thinking about what interests you; make a list of topics and consider also how you might manifest these into exciting personal portfolio pieces. I have seen students take a desperate stab in the dark at choosing an FMP topic, only to realise that they have bitten off more than they can chew or that they soon grow tired of a subject that they initially thought would be exciting. Don’t forget that this project needs to sustain your interest for around nine (?) weeks. What type of (typographic) projects have you found most intersting and enjoyable so far?

“I know its silly to be this worried about a FMP at this level” No, it’s not silly, I suspect that you are preparing a portfolio for HE or employment. You’re right to be concerned about the outcome.

Above all, you need to discuss this at length with your tutors as soon as possible.

Karl,

“its abit experimental and might be a little to complex in my opinion tyring to explain it to people but i think it has creative potential.”

Agreed, I think the project has potential, though the brief needs to be tighter. Just a couple of questions:

Firstly, these “a2 posters promoting diverse music being brodcasted by digital radio”.

Who is the typical listener?

Is this a new station or a station trying to draw in a new group of listeners?

How diverse is the music? You mention Jayz (of whom I know little) but let’s say the music played is truly diverse, like world music for example. Then the poster might be markedly different to that with an R’n’B angle.

How important is it to promote the digital aspect? Is it digital radio that’s the focus of attention or a specific broadcasting station?

I don’t understand the ‘love/hate’ concept. How and why did you arrive at that?

Is there any other copy?

I think you need to consider exactly what it is that you want your posters to do. Keep distilling the ideas until the focus is that much clearer. Simplify.

Finally, have you discussed these ideas with your tutors? They should be helping you and they will be marking your work. Utilise them.

Perhaps post your initial draft ideas on Typophile’s critique section.
