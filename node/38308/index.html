Arabesques article by Ilene Strizver. Arabesque according to Wikipedia. I suppose that BF stands for "Bold Face." Comiccraft's Mild Mannered is a font that has alternate characters for each uppercase letter... Notice, for example, how there are two slightly different versions of the capital S in the word "TRUTHINESS"... Alternate characters are especially useful in a handwriting or handlettering font. OpenType technology allows for many alternate characters, among other things. The terms "Arabic figure" and "Arabic number" probably refer to the same thing... although I am more familiar with the term Arabic numerals. [EDIT] Ack, Thomas beat me to the punch. :-)
