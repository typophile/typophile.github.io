Welcome to the dark, alluring world of "lo-fi" type. :-) > our phone directory I think the Helvetica thing is pretty dumb, but the splicing-in of those Eras (?) numerals is very interesting (if stylistically discordant). Although the slight slant is bad for lo-res rendering, and the color is probably too light*, the structures are smart, like in the "6"/"9" - it's certainly better than any grot numerals, where the "3", "6", "8" and "9" typically all converge. * What's the point size? It's always good to scan up a pica rule alongside this stuff. BTW, in a phone book font narrowness is critical, so something like Adsans wouldn't work. Retina would be great, although the traps might turn out slightly too strong - which they actually do for Retina's target usage, the Wall Street Journal stock listings - my guess is that Tobias F-J overdesigned them on purpose, so they could use Retina to good effect in lower-grade papers too. But I'd probably never get confirmation of that… :-/ http://www.typophile.com/forums/messages/30/1083.html http://www.typophile.com/forums/messages/72/11013.html hhp

Hello. I really like Retina, the letters when enlarged have sections cut out - I believe to reduce bleeding when printed. See this link for samples of it used larger than the small type and you'll see… It was used in the Smithonians Cooper Hewitt's National Design Triennial 2003 http://www.ndm.si.edu/EXHIBITIONS/TRIENNIAL/content/HOEFLER.html Also, I believe that the title "National Design Triennial" is set in Retina and for those of you that have the annual's book, look it up and check for me? Or you can try, BELL CENTENNIAL. Created by Matthew Carter for the phone company Bell Telephone, it also has a nice ability to print at small sizes. It has similar anti-bleed charactertistics of Retina, and may be slightly easier to purchase/find. http://www.linotype.com/121/bellcentennial-family.html#more Hope this helps? sm
