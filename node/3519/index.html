Digital type flea market finds

March 9, 2004 • addison

I suppose I qualify as a type hobbyist since I'm not self-employed. I purchase for personal use and tinkering mostly, with the occasional freelance gig. Because I don't have big budgets to throw around, I look for inexpensive fonts to satisfy my cravings. For example, two of my favorites are shown below: Monteverdi and Montrachet from Fountain (by Lars Bergquist) and Antique Modern from the Storm Type Foundry.



To me, finding these fonts was like discovering a treasure in a flea market – and Mr. Bergquist and Mr. Storm are no amateurs, so I feel these are beautifully crafted products.

I guess my point is there are great fonts for $100 and there are great fonts for $1,000 (and up!). I'd like to hear more background on the smaller foundries and lone practitioners of type design who produce these treasures – or just see what kinds of discussions this prompts.

I read comments on Storm's work fairly frequently (which I agree is amazing), but I hear few or no comments on designers like Mr. Bergquist and Mario Feliciano, or the digitizations of Goudy's fonts by Gerald Giampa. Does a lower price make a font any less well-crafted?

For instance, Goudy and Van Krimpen are both historic figures in type design. DTL Haarlemmer, a Van Krimpen revival, is $1,620. Lanston's revival of Goudy's Kennerley type is $185. Robert Bringhurst called Lanston Kennerley an "excellent digital version." Any thoughts? (I am most certainly not bashing DTL – their work is beautiful and probably worth every penny.)
