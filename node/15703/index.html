2005-10-21 04:08:30 • Hector Muñoz
>In those magazine articles you write, do you rely heavily on empiricism? I don't have a research budget, so really all that's objectively available to "prove" that a trend is happening is information on font sales from a few distributors. The AIGA "top 50 books" named the typefaces. That's a competition and would tell you more about the judges' taste, perhaps, but interesting. I don't see why it wouldn't be possible to do a statistical survey of type use. In Canada one could start with the book Canadian Advertising Rates and Data, which lists audited circulation of publications. Then you'd go to a magazine store and check the top 100 and see what their text types were. Same thing for newspapers. Book Sales: pretty straightforward too. Harry Potter and DaVinci Code would give you the "most read" fonts. Berkeley Old Style in Harry Potter, innit? So "most read" is not necessarily "most used font " or "most licenses sold". On the internet, it would perhaps be possible to devise a crawler that sought out the most visited sites and checked what fonts they used. I suspect that the most read faces, measured in characters multiplied by readers, would be internet "core" fonts. It would be interesting to compare that with newspaper text faces.
