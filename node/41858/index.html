(such a newbie, I accidentally had posted this in the sans critique section. Thanks to the people who originally responded) Hi all, I have seriously considered designing a sans typeface. I’m looking to see if anyone knows some great texts or other resources for designing said type. I was looking at both Gotham and Avenir as inspiration, but wanted to make a face that was unique as well. Aside from two typefaces i designed while I was a student, i don’t feel as if i actually know many rules of thumb that create a good typeface. I had a tendency to try and force all my letters (aside from lc m,i) into a monospaced width, and tried to force everything into a neat little system. It came out looking something like the bastard child of DIN Engschrift and Franklin Gothic, with Modula as the surrogate mother. The more I looked up close at Gotham, Avenir, and others like them, the more I wanted to try and make a serious typeface of professional quality. I know it takes years for that, but i may as well start while I’m young. Some points I’m thinking of (in order of importance): Proportions and ratios of individual letters compared to others Italics (obliques) non-alphanumeric characters Achieving 3-4 weights (maybe this comes after I get a single weight down) Condensed Smallcaps Also, what typog program would be recommended? This Adobe whore doesn't know what the standard/favorite is. Any resources or books would be much obliged. Best, Andy
