High resolution scans or specimens of Neo-Grotesque

December 29, 2007 • eleanai

According to the British Standards 2961:1967 Typeface Classification the Lineale Grotesque are “type faces with nineteenth-century origins. There is some contrast in thickness of strokes. They have squareness of curve, and curling close-set jaws. The R usually has a curled leg and the G is spurred. The ends of the curved strokes are usually horizontal.” The examples given are: SB Grotesque No. 6, Condensed Sans No. 7, Headline. Does anyone know where I can find high resolution scans or specimens of these three typefaces? Also I would like to have a few more examples of ‘Lineale Grotesque’. According to Mitja Miklavcic dissertation ‘Neo-Grotesque Sans Serifs of the 1950s and the 1960s: “The British Standards 2961:1967 Typeface Classification suggests that the grotesque faces have the ends of the curved strokes cut horizontally, while the neo-grotesques are cut oblique – usually it’s the opposite.” Does anyone have any views about that?
