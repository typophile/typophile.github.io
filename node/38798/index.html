2007-11-05 06:30:43 • Alessandro Segalini
What guidelines have you been given regarding the content of your paper? I can imagine such a paper possibly including: * a discussion of the development of the typeface * comments from the typeface's designer * reactions from people other than the designer, both praise and criticism * a discussion of uses to which the font has historically been put; are there usage trends associated with the font (was it extremely popular in, say, the 1970s? has it been growing in popularity since its introduction? etc.)? Are there particularly famous (or infamous) applications of the font? * an analysis of the font's characteristics * personal reflections on the font: why you chose it (though if you really chose it because there is a lot of "information on it," you should come up with some other explanation); how it supports or detracts from the idea your poster is trying to convey; etc. What does your instructor want you to get out of this assignment? What do you want to get out of the assignment? If what you really want is a sans serif font with a substantial literature, I'd try Optima, Helvetica, or Futura (disclaimer: with the exception of Zapf's book About Alphabets, I don't know what info is out there regarding any of these fonts. If, however, you ever need to write a paper on indigenous language dictionaries, let me know and I'll send you a decent bibliography). But maybe a more satisfying approach to this assignment would be to choose a font (or a handful of fonts) that would work well for your poster and ask for help finding resources related to that font/those fonts. Either way, good luck with your project.
