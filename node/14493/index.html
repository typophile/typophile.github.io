It's ok to do work based on already existing fonts, IBM logo is based in Berthold's City font for example. Many logos are based on modified versions of already existing fonts and then there are many hand drawn logos too. It is up to you to decide if a font face is appropriate for a work or a new lettering needs to be done. 2005-08-10 04:50:05 • Norbert Florendo
Erica, welcome! Yes, it's not "usual" to design a complete font... But it's a unique challenge with special rewards, and you seem to already have an important ingredient: you think it's fascinating! Using a free/cheap font for a logo: that's actually safer than using such fonts for text! Because you have a chance to look closely at the beziers and fix stuff as needed; for text a font has to be adequate out of the box. To be fair though, not all free/cheap fonts suck. And some reputable foundries even give out quality free fonts, sort of as bait. BTW, I've always wanted to learn swordfighting. There are different styles I guess; to me the stuff at the Olympics is least interesting, because it has neither romanticism nor spirit; I prefer what might be called "Conan-style", but most of all I would love to learn to properly use a wakizashi. The katana seems too... loud. I actually have a Japanese sword set (although it's stored half a world away now) but the most action it's seen was against a side of ham. One parallel between swordfighting and making type I guess is the level of concentration required: putting your life on the line and making a good quality font cannot be done casually (even though few type designers rely on type to make a living). hhp
