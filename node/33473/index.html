Bodoni and Didot fonts were incredibly popular for business use in the mid 1980s to early 1990s. Unless you’re doing fashion work, where a Didot or Bodoni can still work, a Bodoni identity is going to look very, very dated. Your other choices-Ziggurat and Souvenir—also speak much more to the past than to the present. What sort of company is this that you feel it needs to have an identity with a period look?

"Dated" is probably just the handiest term they could think of - even if it's not totally accurate there's something to it: Bodoni looks like some regal gandfather, aloof and old. In fact it's almost dead. As G W Ovink put it: "Bodoni is an admirable letter for a death notice!" Didot will have the same problem. Souvenir is just retarded looking. I might recommend Mrs Eaves, as long as you won't be using it for anything over a line here and there, since it needs manual [re]spacing to look decent. Or you could try a Baskerville. Or maybe The Foundry's Wilson. hhp
