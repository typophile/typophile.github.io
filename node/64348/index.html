Hrant, my point is that there are two ways to approach Latin-ness. Either through authenticity (Trajan) or through popular (mis)perception (Caesar's Palace). IMO, some of the Shaw/Boge types would be good for "Latin", e.g. Donatello, Ghiberti, Beata. Based on Italian Renaissance inscriptions, they combine the authentic classic proportions of Latin antiquity with a slightly hewn effect, to satisfy the popular conception of something a bit rustic. A clean sans to complement such faces could be Futura, with its Classically proportioned capitals.
