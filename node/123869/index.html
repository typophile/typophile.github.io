Need help figuring out what this font is. It seems to be the standard for most leather stamp number kits. I couldn't imagine it being anything fancy. I feel like it's right under my nose and could even be a default typeface. [img:sites/default/files/old-images/4904-00-L_4589.JPG]
Leatherworking and metalworking are closely related, so it most likely originated from something like Engravers Gothic or Sackers Gothic (but isn't a match to either). Like the type used in many mechanical/hand processes, it may not have a digital equivalent. Closest digital typeface I found was Gross (you’d have to substitute a capital |I| for the |1|): http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/words-pictures/gross/ Then again, you could do the same substitution with Helvetica Extended and come close as well.
