2007-10-22 12:40:10 • dan_reynolds
You aren't really saying what you are looking for. Great quality, cheapness, black & white only, etc. When I needed a good replacement for my imagesetter years ago, because a few clients still liked pasteups - I couldn't find anything better than the 1200 dpi black & white printer from HP. I bought it a special paper, and those blacks were solid and crisp enough for a large camera that still shot for film to plates to pick up clearly. When I first looked for a color laser printer, they were almost all equal and not of very good quality. Two years ago, I splurged and bought a 1200 dpi Okidata (spent a fortune). I find it expense to own (you must buy 4 individual drums and 4 individual toners) but the quality is excellent. I tend to print out many an 11x17 poster for local shows with it -- and even the professional printers cannot get over the quality of the machine. (One even said it was as good as what came off his company's press, and he was in shock that it done off of someone's small business machine.) Laser printers have changed so much over the years - it is one area where the technology has paid off. Back in 1989, you could buy an Apple Laser printer with a whole 300 dpi for $2700. Today, you can go to your local Staples/Office Depot shop, and pick up a superior machine for under $200. Don't forget to look at all your expenses. I have a small HP color laser. It does a nice job - allows me to use different DPI which does save on toner - but the toner is expensive and doesn't last that long. Sorry to be rambling, but it really seems you have a lot more homework to do, before anyone can give you a suggestion for what it is you are looking for.
