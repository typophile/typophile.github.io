Minimum tilde

November 25, 2008 • .00

I’m involved with a custom font project that is being created out of a grid of circles. Seven tall and five wide. I have an accent space of two circles deep by five wide. What would be the minimum shape of a tilde need to be to be recognized by its readers?

Would the attached work or or is there a simpler form I could use?

Unless Hungarian would be involved (double-acute) you can just delete the two vertically stacked dots in the middle (and make the rest abut). In practice though what’s under the tilde might determine the “parity”. In fact in some of my bitmap fonts the shape of the tilde varies, depending.

What do the other accents look like?

In space saving contexts (such as metro LED signs and indeed in handwriting) Spanish-speakers simply write a bar over the letter, macron-style. I’m not sure if there’s a language that distinguishes between macron and tilde over the same letter (though someone will of course correct me if there is).

If you are destined to go with the two line shaping, I’d just kill the middle dot on the second line, which actually makes it a bit old fashioned with the left wave being a bit sharper (and shorter horizontally) than the right wave (again, in Spanish contexts, other langauge’s MMV).

Spanish and Portuguese both have both, although the dieresis will no longer be used in the coming months.
