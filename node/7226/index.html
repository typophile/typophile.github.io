We printed 20 000 back in 1998, so there must be lots of FontBooks gathering dust on shelves with the wrong people somewhere. I never use ebay myself for fear of getting addicted, but a call there surely must yield results?

And re: Nick:
the whole book is produced from a database with as little manual interference as possible, apart from me having to proofread 1300 pages. Inserting film generated from outside data would simply make the process way too complicated. The database is being redone, a mammoth task, and that is one reason why we're late with a new edition. And it is also a mammoth task financing the beast. We may print only 5000 for the first run, but it still takes years to sell them, so there's a lot of capital tied up, which these days isn't easy to come by.

Since we have Mr. Spiekermann's attention (thanks, btw) let me throw in another idea: how about an upgradeable form of binding? 3 ring or similar?

I believe I have three editions of the FontBook, the first one being mostly a FF catalogue I believe, and this implies a lot of repetition.
