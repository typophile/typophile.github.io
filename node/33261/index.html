Aligning dollar signs in data tables

April 24, 2007 • blank

I am currently setting some financial data, and I want the dollar sign to stay in the same position all the way down a row within a table as it appears in the Five-Year Selected Financial Data section of a 10k report. How do I do this in Indesign?
Well, if U+2007 & U+2008 (figure space, & comma/period space) have been correctly filled out in your font, you could use them to pad between the numbers & the dollar signs, while aligning on the decimal. I’m told this is very old school, & there are better ways, I just don’t know them. I guess because it is so old-school, most fonts don’t seem to include them, though. The other way I can think of is to use an extra tab stop for the dollar sign.
2007-04-24 03:45:09 • charles ellertson

Super-special InDesign way: 1) Highlight the column of numbers. 2) Open the Tabs palette (Window>Type and Tables>Tabs). 3) Click on the ruler inside the Tabs palette to create a tab stop. 4) Click on the 4th of the arrows at the top of the Tabs palette (the down arrow with the decimal point next to it). 5) Under “Align on,” type “$” (no quotation marks). 6) Drag the tab stop on the Tabs palette to wherever you want to align the numbers.

It may also be helpful to making the numbers tabular figures so that they align as well. That should be accessible from the character palette flyout menu: OpenType>Tabular Lining or Tabular Oldstyle.

Not exactly, Charles, assuming that I’m understanding you correctly. The tab stop method above can only align to a single character, so either the $ or the decimal point (or comma). It will line up all the numbers along the chosen “Align to” character; the beauty is that different number of digits from number to number doesn’t matter. You could use it for whatever character: a “2” or a “d” if you so choose.
