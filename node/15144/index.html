2005-09-15 21:46:43 • ChuckGroth
It’s very possible the sign maker modified an existing font specifically for this AERO sign. If it was made in the '30s or '40s (and it looks like it was), it's highly unlikely that the sign-maker would have worked from "an existing font." Sign-making was separate tradition from fonts and typefounding, although there were influence in both directions. It's only been since the advent of digital fonts and vinyl cutting machines that the two worlds have merged (to the detriment of the sign-making art, in my opinion).
