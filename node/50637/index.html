That there is an alternate Greek theta that got dropped in the wrong slot. Luckily, you could replace it with a Latin B. The worse problem is that э and є are two different Cyrillic letters. I would guess that if you contact the creator about these two mistakes, he would probably be glad to correct them and replace the font, because it's something that can affect future buyers and he would want to know about it.
2008-10-18 10:45:25 • cerulean
The glyph sample you show doesn't show all the letters of the Russian alphabet, and instead has several archaic letters (Ѣ, Ѵ, IA, Ѳ) and a couple of Ukrainian letters (Є, І). Some of those archaic letters have questionable designs in Priva pro. For example, Ѣ looks very strange considering it is supposed to be a capital letter. Also, I'm not sure how acceptable a Ѵ that looks like a V is. There's also something funny going on with the outline of the Х as well. I would strongly recommend that you run the typeface choice by typographically-trained Russian eyes. As wonderful as Dino dos Santos's Latin typeface designs are, these Cyrillics don't look really professional.
