hi,

this is the engraving lady and yes, this is engraved, probably by hand.

its not a font. do you have the plate (or die) or just the print.

there are 2 categories for this kind of font available: script and calligraphic (calligraphy.)

the “lines” are either the shadow cast within the “cut” or the guide lines scribed first. neither of which will help you.

if your task is to replicate this, best and (historically “correct” according to the market) is to re-draw it in illustrator. it will be lovely. and maybe you can use the lesson learned for someothing else.

Offhand, the only font I know that has these kinds of fills is [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/canadatype/treasury/|Treasury]], a revival of an old MacKellar, Smiths & Jordan 1875 typeface by the same name, by Canada Type. Patrick Griffin and Rebecca Alaccari made a very ornate script with lots of variations that can be used, including two styles of fills.
