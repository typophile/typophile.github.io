Mystery historic font

September 22, 2017 • KevinEberle

Greetings from Charleston, SC! A resident was working on a historic house in Charleston and unearthed a stone marker with the name DAVIS carved on it. The owner is unaware of any Davis family connections to the house. The stone looks vaguely like some sort of a memorial, but maybe not a personal grave marker. Perhaps a marker for a family’s plot? The look of the lettering is very artistic. It has a general 1910s look, but I’m no expert on lettering. I would love to try to pinpoint the general age of the marker to help with identifying its use. It’s only five letters, so it might be too small a sample, but it is unique enough that, if it was even a well-defined set and not just a one-off design, it might be identifiable. Any thoughts?

The style of lettering is Lombardic (aka versal), and has been around for centuries (as in since the Middle Ages and the time of illuminated manuscripts).

Because of that, the style of lettering alone won’t help much with pinpointing the date of the marker.

Alphabets used for stone carving prior to the age of digital type often diverged from those used for printing, but similar printing type styles appear in type specimens from the early through the late 19th century.
