Wanting to improve my typography

August 30, 2007 • Wine Label Designer

I design wine labels and some are very nice but I stink at typography. I am often blown away by my competitions use of type. I am not talking about designing type or reworking it. I am talking about what fonts to select and interesting compositions. I don't understand how they consistently bang out great design. Often their type choices are clean and classic. Any tips on how I can improve so I can remain competitive. Books, web sites references. How do kick butt designers do it?
There is a list of books here: http://typophile.com/node/12157 Recommended for your purpose are: Tally of Types, A -Morison, Stanley. David R. Godine, Publisher, 1999. Anatomy of a Typeface -Lawson, Alexander. David R. Godine Pub., 1990 Treasury of Alphabets & Lettering: A Source Book- Tschichold, Jan. W.W. Norton & Co., 1995 Letters of Credit: A View of Type Design -Tracy, Walter. David R. Godine, Publisher, 1986 Elements of Typographic Style -Bringhurst, Robert. Hartley & Marks, Vancouver, 1997. Elements... is the more general of this selection, and the others take over where this one ends. Are you looking for info on scripts as well, then have a look in the main list. Enjoy the journey!
