Finding projects that have trouble displaying quantitative information

April 25, 2009 • Thomas Levine

I realized a few weeks ago that I like to display quantitative information visually more than I like finding the quantitative information to display.

Fortunately, I have access to decades of theses from a large research university from which I can take interesting information. I’ll probably start presenting the findings of some of these in a monthly student art magazine.

But, considering that I’m at a large research university, I figure that some current research units here could use me. Most could probably use help with cleaning up details of the generic types of graphs and tables, but I’m really interested in more complicated, unconventional information, and groups with simpler information probably wouldn’t want my help anyway if the changes would be so small.

Does anyone have ideas about how I may find groups that regularly have trouble presenting quantitative data visually or whether such groups even exist? I suspect certain disciplines may be more prone to this trouble than others.

Please excuse me if this is too obvious or not relevant: It seems to me that Edward Tufte has identified quite a few groups/types that have trouble displaying their data:
