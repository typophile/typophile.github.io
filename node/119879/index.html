Belvedere - What went wrong?

September 25, 2014 • Keith Tricker

I released this font family on myfonts a couple of weeks ago expecting it might be, if not a runaway success, at least moderately popular. To date, though, not so much as a single sale. Is this just a fashion thing, are we still in the silly season, or is the work seriously flawed? I spent two or three months drawing it, so it’s a bit galling that it’s met, to date, with total apathy! Your comments, suggestions and critiques are welcome. I attach a small sample, but you can view the full family at: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/studio-k/belvedere/ [img:sites/default/files/old-images/Belvedere Sampler copy_5901.jpg]

When you make a font or family you really hope everything will go well sales-wise, but you should not expect it to happen – that’s kind of like buying a lottery ticket with your last dollar thinking for sure it’s going to be a winner. Just because you really, really like a design doesn’t mean others will.

Even in the days of foundry type, ATF’s biggest worry when introducing a new font in the various sizes was that they might end up with 150 linear feet of type inventory on the shelf that no one wanted.

Don’t let non-sales get you down though; keep on making fonts if that’s your thing. Just don’t depend on font sales supporting your lifestyle because it’s not likely to happen.
