If you take Burgues and give it a negative skew (slant the letters back toward vertical, but not too far), you’ve got a pretty great match for the lowercase in the sample. The h and k variations could have been adapted from the th or tth glyphs, and the g and C wouldn’t be too hard to edit to get the finished results (just refine the terminal strokes).

The L actually looks a bit like the L in another Sudtipos typeface—Feel Script. Marry some of its attributes with Burgues, and you’re not too far off: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/sudtipos/feel-script/
