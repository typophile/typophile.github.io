2010-09-23 20:51:43 • 5star

not a single suggestion concerning the logo itself?

I realize you’re just looking for a quickie solution, but you’re going about this the wrong way.

You don’t design a logo with a big promotional photo behind it. You first design a logo by itself; one that will work well on your letterhead, business card, etc, and that will work in black-and-white as well as color, and one that will work well in tiny sizes, and most importantly which does a good job of representing your business.

Once you’ve designed the logo, then you can start thinking about applying it to web banners.

So put the photo aside and give your logo some careful thought and post some logo ideas by themselves, and I’m sure you’ll get some advice.

2010-09-24 08:30:44 • Bunny3000

I think people are genuinely trying to help you with good branding. Here are some perhaps provocative questions for you. No need to answer them here, but you will probably have to answer them as you develop your business:

“I don’t really want to invest too much energy into this” Why not invest some time and money into something as essential to the success of your business as branding?

“it won’t be used anywhere other than the website header” Why even have a website when it doesn’t relate to any other part of your business brand?

“I’d really appreciate it if you could put the higher standards aside for a moment” Why would you approach your business this way, do you want to communicate that sentiment to your customers?

Waiting until you make enough to invest in a good identity and branding solution is like a computer programmer waiting to get a computer until they are a successful programmer. In other words, you will likely never get there until you invest in consistent and effective communication with your potential customers. That could be a logo, or it could be word of mouth, but you need to focus on the plan for communicating the value and benefits of your business to your customers. Copying graphics is a waste of time that you could be spending working out the foundation of the business you are trying to create. I do hope your business is a big success, and wish you all the best as you move forward with this challenging task.
