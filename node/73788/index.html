September 2, 2010 • iamwill

Hi Typophiles I am looking for a typophile to work with me to design a logo for my photography web site (currently being re-vamped). I am a London-based photographer and am looking for a logo inspired by old-fashioned camera manufacturers. If you're interested in talking further, please get in touch, giving me an idea of your rates for this type of work, and an indication of when you would be able to work on this. Many thanks! Will
Will, I took a quick look at your site; you've done some beautiful work! > a logo inspired by old-fashioned > camera manufacturers. I'm a graphic designer and I'd suggest that when working with whoever you hire, you suggest that as one possible direction but also let the designer know that you're open to considering other approaches, too. I've found over the years that the logo a client likes best is often totally different than what they had initially expected. A retro look based on old camera manufacturers, as you suggested, might work, but on the other hand your work has a contemporary look to me, and I imagine you use modern equipment, so you'd need to be careful that your logo didn't create the wrong impression. Since "panoramic photographer" is mentioned prominently on your home page, a logo design with panoramic aspects is an approach I'd suggest considering. Perhaps a business card with panoramic proportions, too.

> Yes. But honestly, it happens the other way round, too. I agree. It's important for the designer and client to go through the design process with an open mind. You look objectively at the situation and goals, you do research, you come up with concepts, you critique and modify them (sometimes repeatedly), etc. At the end of the process, it may turn out that the designer's initial idea -- or the client's initial idea -- turns out to be the right solution. But sometimes the process takes you to a solution that neither of you expected.
