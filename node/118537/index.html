2014-08-07 21:41:01 • fvilanakis
As an experiment I rounded Meet Me in Brooklyn while trying to respect the general average of the outlines Some letters, like O ended up being close to AVOCADO ON, while others while having a similar thick and thin character, were different in emphasis. In my opinion AVOCADO ON was hand lettered using a casual thick and thin technique and the similarities are due to convergent evolution on similar shapes that the lettering artist of this sign and the fontmaker of Meet Me in Brooklyn considered effective in catching a casual unstructured and spontaneous mood. A rounded version of Meet Me in Brooklyn would be very suitable as a source for making a full alphabet in the general style of AVOCADO ON. This type of font works well when the letters are angled slightly. A good trick is to angle the letters one way for the upper case and in the opposite direction for the lower case. Generate a couple of extra fonts with the letters at different sizes and a some variation in detail. For output, mix the fonts and cases, and you are about as close to informal lettering as you can get within the limits of digital font making. Don
