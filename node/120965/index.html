Recommendations for displaying concise type specimens

November 1, 2014 • alfredxing

I’m working on a website redesign on my webfonts project, Brick, and I wanted to get some opinions on how best to display the font specimens in the font catalogue. I probably can’t load the actual fonts, since there’s a lot of fonts and they’re all quite large, so I’m looking at SVG or raster image solutions. I like the Wikipedia style (example), but I’m open to other suggestions as well.

The specimen should also be small enough so that the user can quickly browse through the list of fonts. Optimally, the specimen would show off as much of the font as possible (letters, unique features, etc.) while providing a good sense of what the font would look like when used practically. Thinking of this, what word (any roman language) or combination of letters would be best to show off these features?

Thanks!

Most foundries and distributors have their own choice, from the minimal (e.g. FontFont’s “Rag”), to the more complex (e.g. FontSquirrel’s typefaces’ pages), but most of the time they show a list. I like the card idea, and Wikipedia’s style isn’t that bad. A similar implementation (which suffer, to me, from excessive differences in style among the cards) is shown in TypeCache’s clusters: http://typecache.com/font-clusters/01

Anyway, early questions to answer would be:

  • one card design fits all? (for example, Google Fonts presents titling and text typefaces differently)
  • one card content fits all? (Wikipedia’s cards have unique content for each typeface, as the list views of several sellers)
  • related to the above, will the name of the typeface be part of the card?
  • how many styles will the card contain? (if the answer is "one", then how to present families? How about layered fonts?)

Just a couple of observations. I think you can (should?) move a bit further away from the Wikipedia format. For one I would move the name of the typeface above the card. That would leave space for more content. Also content could be more varied: for example, lowercase |a| is displayed three times, there’s a complete lowercase, but just four capitals and just one diacritic (which could disappear if a different word is chosen).
