Jaquizaca – a jaunty typeface with a serious attitude

September 27, 2013 • Kristians Sics

I created [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/lamatas/jaquizaca/|Jaquizaca]] in 2001 to make [[http://vimeo.com/74241282|inter-titles for a TV programme]]. After twelve years it was revised and updated with ligatures, contextual alternates and other OpenType features. It supports all the European languages using Latin alphabet. In 2004 I draw [[http://origin.myfonts.com/s/aw/original/242/0/124175.pdf|a snakes’n’ladders board]] to illustrate a magazine article on graphic design process. Now, with a little help of my friends, I translated it into [[http://origin.myfonts.com/s/aw/original/242/0/124178.pdf|English]], [[http://origin.myfonts.com/s/aw/original/242/0/124177.pdf|Spanish]] and [[http://origin.myfonts.com/s/aw/original/242/0/124176.pdf|German]].
Not something I am likely to use in my work, but it is very nice still. Congratulations!
