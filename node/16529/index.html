2005-12-06 00:47:30 • Stay Youth
I'm not sure that adding colour and images serves the meaning of the quote. It seems to me Massey is saying that the written line manages, in black and white simplicity, to provide the richness of ideas and thought. To take "colourful thought" literally distracts from the more complex way it works in the quote. Perhaps I'm off. I would suggest an expression which is -- not simple, but -- more focused on the lines of the letters and text to make the quote *do* what it says. Perhaps use colour in the actual letter shapes, rather than around it. Or if you want images, make them lighter, more of a background -- secondary to the text. Kristina
