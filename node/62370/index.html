2009-09-29 16:11:55 • russellm

[stock logos] they’re not always going to be the wrong way to go."

Yes they are, in terms of what a logo is supposed to be, what it’s supposed to do, and when it’s needed and when it isn’t. If Larry the Plumber is going to get that sort of logo, maybe he doesn’t need a logo. And that sort of logo doesn’t do the job that a real logo does, anyway. It doesn’t set him apart; it doesn’t make him memorable; it doesn’t emphasize his specific special strengths. It just says that he’s a plumber. But to communicate that, he doesn’t need to licence a “little drawing of a pipe and a wrench”. Except maybe if his target audience is “people who don’t know what a plumber is”. :-
It’d be better if he thought instead about what his invoices look like. Or that he mails out a special Season’s Greetings card to his clients every year. Or even if he decides that when he visits clients who have kids, he brings a special popsicle for them, and the same one every time. That has more to do with what design and branding – in the broadest possible sense of these words – can do for him than an interchangable little clip-art logo. Yes the world is full of perverse commodifications such as “stock logos”. But that doesn’t mean we all have to play. Or should, even.
