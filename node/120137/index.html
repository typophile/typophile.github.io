Seeking Roman Face to Fit a Particular Situation

October 2, 2014 • homeboy

I need a regular proportionally spaced roman typeface (not decorative) that when used to spell Black Institute on two lines flush left yields the most amount of space between the length of Black and the length of Institute. In the sample, because the word Institute has a number of narrow letters, that remaining space (A) is greater relative to Institute (B) using Courier rather than Times Roman. For instance, I’m thinking (hoping) there might be a face with the cap I and the lower case T designed somewhat wider that normal – without the face looking monospaced.
I would suggest that using negative tracking on BLACK and positive on INSTITUTE plus letter width adjustments on INSTITUTE in a program like InDesign will get you a result without hunting for another face
