With only 3 letters and one number to go by I can only offer some general suggestions to assist in creative recreation of the rest of the alphabet. The concave vertical strokes are a feature of the old wooden types loosely grouped as Antique Tuscan. If you reduce the serifs these might help: Assay http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/solotype/assay/ Winnetka JNL http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/jnlevine/winnetka/ Minnesota http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/solotype/minnesota/ For other features such as the open top 4 look at fonts such as Eingraviert [which also has a good assortment of what to look for in 19th century heavy letter-forms] http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/intellecta/eingraviert/ Shady Lady NF http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/nicksfonts/shady-lady-nf/ Also: Gunsmoke http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/fontmesa/gunsmoke/ And the western theme at dafont http://www.dafont.com/theme.php?cat=106&fpp=100 BTW I have seen letters somewhat similar to your image, but not digitized.
