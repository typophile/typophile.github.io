Sabon with yogh etc...?

March 11, 2010 • dtw

Can anybody confirm whether there are versions of Sabon available that have yogh in? The “Monotype Sabon Pro Regular in OpenType Pro format” on the Linotype site has only seven characters from the Latin Extended-B code block, none of which are ȝ. Our setters are working on a publication in Sabon, and we’ve just realized it’s going to need to be able to set Old/Middle English. So probably eths and thorns and wynns and all that sort of stuff at some stage, but yogh in the immediate term. If all else fails, which of the free “big” fonts d’you reckon is closest? I’ve tested a stretch of Sabon with Gentium yoghs and thorns in it, and it doesn’t look too bad…
[img:sites/default/files/old-images/andron_yogh_4304.jpg] . You are likely to find something useful [[http://www.mufi.info/fonts/|on this page]]. Look closely, those glyphs get not always mastered well. If you need the yogh et al. in more than just Regular, look [[http://www.signographie.de/cms/front_content.php?idart=215&changelang=2|there]].
