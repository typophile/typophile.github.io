The more I thought about the original post, the more obvious it became to me that I have no predisposition to purchasing particular fonts because of who made them or their country of origin.

I'll site Cottingly as one of my alltime favorite typefaces and its designer hails from the UK.

See! Simple as that! It's also hard to ignore a successful typeface like Fontesque from our pal Nick Shinn up in Canada. It's become an incredibly popular display face in the US.

I guess a great typeface is a great typeface and I'd personally be reluctant to go out of my way to find a "local" homerolled version of something that already is exactly what I want.

That's my opinion when it comes to purchasing type. Regarding other products I purchase, I think I may have a bias due to regionality, relationship to my personal background, etc. - less 'legitimate' reasons and more 'gut' reasons.

Hopefully I'm not going too far into a tangent, but I think my purchase decisions are based mostly on historical purchase experiences, sense of value for amount paid, packaging/ambiance, and cost.

Say I'm an Art Director in Toronoto and I want to capture the regional flavor. Chances are I'd want to use a face that reflects that flavor and more than likely it'd be a face developed locally.
