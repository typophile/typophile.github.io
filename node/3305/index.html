I've recently been trying to prepare a presentation detailing the differences between fonts. My problem is that as a student I know very little about fonts myself and I've had difficulty finding any difinitive distictions. My aim is to take categories such as Serif and Sans Serif and break them down into sub categories such as Slab, Neo, Geometric, Humanist. I also hope to be able to describe their most distictive features i.e. Humanist = anti goemetric, anti perpendicular, slightly bent tips and so on. And to the question: Is there a trusted comprehensive source for detailed information on fonts? All advice welcomed and greatly appreciated, Thanks, Kanebo
There are the utmost basic primers: The Mac/PC is not a Typewriter by Robin Williams The Elements of Typographic Style by Robert Bringhurst Stop Stealing Sheep An Find Out How Type Works by Erik Spiekermann & E. M. Ginger These are good starting points. There are many many more of course. See the Typophile Book Reviews. Jon
