Well, of course I have my own single-decker g for those occasions. And the narrowness is not a problem as long as you track properly.

2004-12-02 18:56:30 • hrant
So one could quite happily set a phone book with Meta, without any customisation (bar 'g') and it will perform as well, if not better than most phone book types?

It was designed for phone books et al. Never really tried and tested, but i have a few illustrations from 1985, showing Helvetica (actually: Holsatia from Hell), electronically condensed, and an early version of Meta, uncondensed. Very unrefined, but we could set narrower columns, get more columns on the page and save around 8% content. And that was without any customisation. But the German Post Office decided to stick with Helvetica and its bastard sisters until 3 years ago, when they joined the Frutiger bandwaggon.
