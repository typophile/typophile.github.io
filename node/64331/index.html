Have a look at the condensed styles of Cyrus Highsmith's [[http://www.fontbureau.com/fonts/Antenna|Antenna]]. They have a fresh look, and the angularity of Highsmith's style seems to work particularly well in condensed styles. I was very impressed with how well the different weights and styles of [[http://www.fontbureau.com/fonts/BentonSans|Benton Sans]] worked together in Armin Vit's catalog for this year's TypeCon. This design was worked on first by Tobias Frere-Jones and then Highsmith. It is originally based on Trade Gothic, but worth a look.
2009-11-17 13:35:54 • William Berkson
Actually, Bill, the Benton Gothic/Benton Sans series was based more on Morris Fuller Benton's various sans serif series for ATF (including News Gothic, Franklin Gothic, and Lightline Gothic); hence the name. Trade Gothic was Jackson Burke's variation on the theme, designed for Linotype a couple decades after Benton's.
