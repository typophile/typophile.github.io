2006-05-25 15:34:12 • hrant
A 610,000 unit em seems excessive, even for the reasons you describe. For instance, given an output device with 3000 dpi resolution (typical high resolution), a 10-inch tall character would be rasterized to no more than 30,000 pixels, less than 1/20th of the available resolution in your font. You would not reach a one-to-one ratio until the character was over 20 inches tall. Go ahead and use 610,000 if you can, but 30,000 will probably be enough, and well within FontLab’s limits. I seriously doubt whether the difference will be visible, or even measurable, on typical output devices.

2006-05-26 00:10:19 • Mikhail Kovalev

The UPM size in the OpenType/TrueType specification is limited to 65,535. That is a limitation of the format. However, point coordinates in FontLab Studio are also limited to 65,635, which is a limitation of our application. Therefore, it’s impractical to work even at a UPM size of anything close to 65,535. However, I pretty much feel that a UPM size of 10,000 should be OK for you. If not, try 20,000 or 30,000.

A.
