In order to bridge the gap between machine and mind in AI, we perhaps need to use our imagination and model extrasensory phenomena. We need to model polymental structures with less of a definite gap between them, thus obviating the need to bridge that gap. After all machines can already read each other’s minds, why not let them? The Turing test is a milestone, but someday we are going to arrive at a definition of AI which also includes creativity and humour. How would a collective view creativity? As a shared resource? It may be interesting to attempt models incorporating a great many simpler, even dedicated elements but with these extrasensory sorts of boundaries.
