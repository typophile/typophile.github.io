Help Identify a technical name for a part of a letter

April 20, 2010 • jdfcreative

A technical question on type anatomy: What do you think the gap on any open counter is called.

For example, on a lowercase ‘n’, this would be the space between the interior facing serifs, or on the sanserif the interior-facing side of each vertical stroke.

Is this space called a ‘gap’ on an open counter, or is it an ‘aperture’ (such as found on a lowercase two story ‘a’), or is it something entirely different? I have attached two images for reference.

Yes, this is a question of semantics, but, I haven’t found a definitive (or more authoritative) answer to settle what that thing is called. Thoughts?

thanks in advance.

–jdf

“Aperture” sounds right when specifically leading into an interior area. A “gap” is more general; for instance, the gap in the center of the broken vertical bar would not be an aperture.
