Thoughts on Brothers Font by Emigre?

January 10, 2015 • jvankriedt

Perhaps this has been posted on before, but I’m not entirely sure. I notice that often fonts get picked on for overuse (see Archer) yet somehow I notice that “Brothers” by Emigre gets used more and more and no one seems to notice. To quote the great Mugatu “I feel like I’m taking crazy pills over here!!!”

I understand that it is a very nice looking font that has become immensely popular even though it is over 15 years old. What bothers me is that it seems to be taking advantage of the fact that a client might not notice that their Microbrew label looks EXACTLY LIKE that other microbrew label. I even read a piece in a well-regarded logo book where a design firm claimed to find the “Logotype” from researching old shipping crates, when it is clearly BROTHERS!!!

Would love to get others thoughts on this….should I just give up and put Brothers on everything?

Given that John Downer, the designer of Brothers, is a sign painter by trade (with undergraduate and graduate degrees in fine art), I’d guess that he brought that hand-made aesthetic to his design for Brothers. That doesn’t rule out the possibility that his references included type found on old shipping crates, and that your microbrew logo designers worked from similar sources.

If you can provide the name of the brewery (or the logo book), I’d love to compare it to Brothers.

But you are quite right about the ubiquity of the style. I see it everywhere these days. Much like the “hipster” logo style that involves some form of crossed line elements and “rustic” type, the steampunk/woodcut/hand-carved aesthetic of Brothers (and typefaces like it) has become shorthand for “authentic.”

All to entice young professionals with too few frames of reference and too much disposable income to spend three times the going rate for a basic cast iron frying pan (a la Best Made Company).

Resist the urge to resort to shorthand…..

2015-01-16 21:03:43 • JamesM

I’m the designer of the Fidelis Co. Brand. Of course the typeface used is Brothers! I simply wasn’t asked which typeface it was in the interview. The spirit I used Brothers in was indeed inspired by typography on antique shipping crates.

Brothers is a strong display typeface, especially when used to convey a specific visual language. It was perfect for this particular client and business, at the particular time that the mark was designed. I agree that this style of typography has recently exploded into ubiquity, and I love that Brothers is a 15+ year old typeface enjoying a newfound popularity.

As the owner of an all women-run design studio, I’m highly enjoying the investigation into Brother’s connotations of “socially constructed as tediously, single-mindedly masculine in contemporary society” etc. I don’t disagree. Those connotations work perfectly with the business this mark represents.
