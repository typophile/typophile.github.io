2009-10-06 18:53:09 • poms

I’m not sure that a font can go into that particular emotion. The words have to do it, and even then, words alone are often misread when sarcasm is intended. A tone of voice has to be used. I can’t count the number of apologies I have seen online where sarcasm has been misread as honest reaction, with bad results. That is why the HTML <sarcasm> tag was invented.

Italics could do the trick, if the reader has been conditioned to know that. Offhand I can’t think of anyway to convey the emotion seamlessly.

2009-10-06 20:01:22 • Don McCahill
