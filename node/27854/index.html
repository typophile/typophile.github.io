In In Design, take a text box, change the font to Minion Pro, type ‘st’, ‘fi’ or something like that. Then, from the fly out menu (the small triangle button) check ‘Ligatures’ and if you wish, from the Opentype submenu, check Discretionary Ligatures. Um, hope this helps.

2006-08-23 15:07:48 • Grot Esqué

Search for “ligature” in the InDesign help facility, and read “To apply ligatures to letter pairs”: In InDesign the character palette has a fly-out menu accessed from the little arrow in a circle on the top right: click on “Ligatures” in that menu.

Search for “ligature” in the Illustrator help facility, and read “To use ligatures and contextual alternates”: In Illustrator, chose “Ligatures” from the similar fly-out menu in the Opentype palette.

Of course, you won’t get any ligatures if the font designer didn’t put them in the font, but most fonts have at least “fi” and “fl”.
