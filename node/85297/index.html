Suggestions for a text face midway between book and magazine?

September 8, 2011 • Frode Bo Helland

I’m redesigning a journal with two sections: the first rather magazine-y (three columns, plenty of images, dense), the second somewhat bookish (two columns, more leading, calm and sober). Weights/styles: regular and italic, and perhaps also a medium. Grades would sure come in handy with the difference in density between the two sections, but I’d like your suggestions even if it doesn’t have them. I’m looking for a humanist serif with a contemporary feel to it – more chunky (I guess) than a typical book face, but not all slabby and stuff. Suggestions? I have some options listed, but I want to keep an open mind.
Not sure if it fits exactly but I’m keen to explore what Kris Sowersby’s Tiempos Text could be used for…IIRC the concept was to re-explore Plantin from a newsy/restrained angle, and it’s definitely contemporary.

2011-09-09 01:52:32 • Nick Shinn
My [[http://www.fontbureau.com/fonts/WilliamsCaslonText/|Williams Caslon Text]] has been used for text in both magazines and books. You could eg use it at 10 point in narrower magazine columns and at 11 point in wider book columns. I’ve also seen it recently used well at 10 point in a small paperback, like the old Penguin pocket books (column measure 19 picas). The bold weight is really a semi-bold, if that’s what you’re looking for. You would want something else in display; it’s been paired with Caslon 540 and Big Caslon, as well as Knockout and Archer.

2011-09-09 06:21:40 • Trevor Baum

The Janson/Kis typeface is one of the most beautiful old-style faces, at least Tschichold thought this way. Interview Magazine is using it for example, and it is a commonly used book typeface. The regularly used version is Janson Text, however recently a complete rework of the typeface was released, called Kis Antiqua Now.

Kis Antiqua Now has 4 weights and two display cuts. The reinterpretation turned out great in my opinion, the capitals are now in a more contemporary size relation compared to the lower case characters. On the page it looks great, not as monotonous as some recent typefaces. http://www.fonts4ever.com/portrait_typeface.php?language=en&id=5
