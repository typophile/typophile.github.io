Jon, you have hit on the two major knocks on MasterJuggler.

MJs error checking can and should be disabled in prefs. It is so out of date, and really gives false 'problem' answers that majority of the time (as do most font checkers of this sort). While many of the reported 'errors' are possible in a certain set of circumstances, very few of these occur anymore. Including this 'feature' is an error that Alsoft has not rectified (by simply getting rid of).

The sample scrolling is simply too slow to be useful as you have discovered, and can be easily accomplished through other shareware programs. The good news is that MJ happily works at the same time as other font apps are activated (and this is as true in OS 9 as in OS X).

Those two items are the major downside, the upside is that it is a great and rock solid font activation/deactivation tool that doesn't mess around with the OS or your programs - and consequently does not need updated all the time as many others do.
