2004-09-03 00:24:54 • dan_reynolds
dan, currently i'm a working designer with an actual title of marketing designer at modern luxury publishing co. they do CS magazine here in chicago and a whole host of others in the Cali and in Texas with new titles launching. it's expanding like crazy and that equals fun because it's lots of random work as they don't have the total staff they need so it's always a variety of design and layout.

before that i was a practicing freelancer in the chicago area...i was doing the usual stationary stuff but really had fun doing music venue posters. did that for about 2 years and am still doing it on the side when time permits. it's a great way to stay creative as most of my clients trusted my judgement over time.

before THAT i was at school at ringling school of art and design in sarasota florida on the gulf coast (i think the gulf coast still exists?) their i was immersed in complete experimentation in anything i could get my hands on. mostly type, as my typographic teacher 4th year was edwin utermohlen who studied under ed fella. the guy was pure inspiration and fun as hell to grab a beer with from time to time.

so in just about everything i do, it's my type i pay most attention to. for the most part when a logo project were to come up i'd work a hand drawn face up the would accompany a logo or mark...i just hated copping out and using something on the computer that sorta fit but didnt alot of the time. it just felt too easy and since i drew the mark in the first place it only made sense. since detail was of the essence, i just got really good over 2 years of doing detailed type sketches which lead to my ambitions of making typefaces...but it stems further back but i'm out of breath here...
