Alternatives to common screen typefaces?

May 2, 2009 • mikebara

Most - but not all - of what I write stays on-screen, whether in a web page or a PDF. Because of this, I tend to favor faces that look particularly crisp on-screen but also look reasonably good when printed. Unfortunately, many of my favorites for the printed page - particularly Chaparral Pro and Arno Pro - look very disappointing on-screen - at least they do in Word 2007 for Windows, with ClearType tuned as much as possible. (See the attached image, taken from Word 07, to see what I mean.)

It seems that there are very few typefaces that can handle this - outside of Cambria and Constantia, I’m not able to find much of anything that works really well for setting large blocks of text. (Georgia doesn’t really work for me in print).

Any suggestions would be greatly appreciated.

Mike

ClearType in most Windows apps is limited to TrueType font rendering, i.e. it does not apply to PostScript CFF flavour OpenType fonts such as Chaparral Pro and Arno Pro.

Microsoft’s Windows Presentation Format and the new DirectWrite apply ClearType to both TTF and CFF fonts, but there are no major apps built for these platforms yet.
