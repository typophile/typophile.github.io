Present Std, Friedrich K. Sallwey - Any other examples?

September 8, 2006 • Riss Wiese

Hi all,

I have a client who has asked that we use a particular typeface - Present Std - in their website design, as it is used in the slogan section of their logo.

Their actual logo was created by another (unknown) designer, and in the interest of consistency across mediums (media?) I’d like to retain as many elements as possible. The main body has been created out of Trogman’s Handel Gothic (albeit with adjusted proportions), with the slogan created out of Present Std by Sallwey (similarly adjusted).

I would like to suggest to them to retain the Handel typeface for their logo, but perhaps refine the face used for their slogan. I don’t feel it (Present Std) is appropriate for their business (selling cars), or for the web. I also don’t feel that the two typefaces are particularly complementary.

So, my question is:

  1. does anyone know of any well known examples of logos or identities (or other) that use Present Std so I can review the typeface in practice, (or direct me to a resource that can help) and;

  2. do you have any suggestions for an alternative, but not too dissimilar font, in order that we don’t disrupt their current branding, given that they have previously invested heavily in print advertisements and signage with these two typefaces in the logo?

Thanking you in advance!

Riss
