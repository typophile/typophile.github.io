I agree will Bill ( actually is that how you prefer to be refered to or not? I have seen other people do it & I am apeing them I suppose but I would prefer to know how you feel about it... ) that increasing screen rez & the current font tech is pretty acceptable *if* we were going to have it across devices. However, I think low rez may last a long time and regardless of how long low rez will or won't last there is a great deal to be said for creating an OS architecture on MS, OSX, Linux and portable OS' that is aware of and can switch to font data which is size specific. It would be amazing if that kind of tagging could be added to OpenType. Size specific design is the ideal in print obviously and I think it aught to be clear that it is definitely the ideal on screen too. If that font in question is pixel matched as DB suggests or not is to my mind a secondary issue. That is not to say it's not a worthwhile or unimportant one. Not at all. But it assumes a stability of rez or a specificity of device which may or may not always be relevant. That said It is probably possible to make good looking fonts that are pixel matched at a given PPEM which also look good when not pixel matched on devices with higher PPEMs. But no matter the strategy the best looking font is always going to be the one which matches it's context the best. That it might be robust outside of that context it is a great secondary gain. The great thing about DB model as opposed to TT+hinting or Hrant's favorite pixel centric model is that it has far more room for that secondary gain to occur in.
