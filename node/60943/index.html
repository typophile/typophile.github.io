2009-08-14 00:24:35 • Ricardo Cordoba

Hi There:

Yes, of course! There are so many excellent typefaces and founderies that are currently out of my reach.

OurType, DTL, Font Bureau Studio Library, Teff and probably Typonine.

But I always try to cross-shop at different foundries to see who has the best quality and the best value for the genre/style I’m looking for. So I guess I really don’t have uninformed impulsive buys. I’m so tired of buying really cheap fonts that have lousy character sets and metrics!

Text faces for books is one category that seems to be growing exponentially each year. There is so much choice because there is so much competition. So if I have to save for it, it will a long time before I buy it.

Sometimes there’s a face with no satisfying analogues. Big Caslon Italic is one of those fonts. There’s nothing like it in the world. It’s a $100 a style. I’ve bought a few. If it was something else—no way!

There is a recent thread on the University of Reading graduates in which I was planning on making a few comments on, but I will make them here. If you thought the concept of the “house style” died or only applies to oil and canvas then you would be wrong. Take a look at much of the output from University of Reading and KABK. Variations on themes (for Reading it seams to be the extremely syncopated and faceted text faces) and undeclared “homages” to their typographic heros (Veronika Burian and Underware must be annoyed)

But its not all bad tho… this gives us graphic designers options—options at different price points. I use this for instance.

Take a look at Greta: http://www.typotheque.com/fonts/greta_text

Then take a look at Acon: http://www.mysterywesterntheory.com/holger/index.php?/project/acon/

They just might (hopefully) be at different price points too (Acon, I assume is still in development). So if you need a Greta for books Acon maybe for you.

Price is isn’t the only thing that holds me back from the spur of the moment purchase—it’s licensing too: http://typophile.com/node/51784

Thanks for bringing up this topic,
