“…teams such as the Pittsburgh Steelers”

Besides the military uses mentioned, industrial uses of stencils were quite prominent and most likely predated military usage. I grew up in Pittsburgh in the 40s and 50s. I had summer jobs in mills and factories. Stencils were used to mark material for easy retrieval in a warehouse. They were an efficient way to mark numerous crates over and over on all sides so that they can be read from any vantage point. Since

Pittsburgh was above all an industrial town, It makes sense that the Steelers would follow the lead of the steel industry and give their logo an industrial look.

ChrisL

BTW, This is the Steelers 75th anniversary year so that should give you a point in time to look at. In the 1930s Pittsburgh was known as the smokey city and steel capital of the world. Heavy wooden crates of steel products were common and all stenciled. The United States Steel Corporation also had a stencil logo and the symbol of the Steelers is taken directly from the old Steel Industry symbol.
