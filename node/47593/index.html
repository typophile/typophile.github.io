Some of the bigger metros are actually somewhat dull (although well done) if in part because their use by larger numbers of people and sheer number of sations starts to impede originality. For example, comparing the Madrid and the Lisbon metro, the Madrid one uses Helvetica almost religiously, has gray or blue backgrounds for everything except the line colors, and is obviously designed to be efficient and easy to understand but very much in the background. The Lisbon one, with only four lines, has a much lighter feel to it, using more whites, curves, and a much more modern sans. Are the campuses separated by fields? Or essentially distinct universities under one name? I would think you could make some interesting design decisions based on that. «El futuro es una línea tan fina que apenas nos damos cuenta de pintarla nosotros mismos». (La Luz Oscura, por Javier Guerrero)
