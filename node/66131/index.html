Hi there, this is a (bad quality) reproduction of an old tax announcement I have found in a hotel hall the other day, in Brescia, Italy (pics taken with the mobile phone camera). It was printed in Verona in 1817; what stroke me at first was the fact that it seems to be set in Walbaum, a German typeface produced in the early years of XIX century, instead of, say, Bodoni, which should have been a more natural choice. But in 1817 Verona was under the government of the Austrian empire, so it seems likely that Walbaum punches have reached the “royal” typography which printed the announcement.
