I think having a fixed set/sequence of characters: 1) helps the organizer(s). 2) promotes strategizing (like Kent said). I don't think there should be teams - there should be a qualification round (maybe show a couple of complete 8-glyph sets to everybody, get them to write and hand in their answers, and the first 8 corrent respondents qualify), and then have a pairwise elimination tree, with quarter and semi finals, and a final. As for closed/open book, one big advantage of meatspace versus virtual interaction is that you can prevent people from checking references, so I think you should take advantage of that. – BTW, here's another option: instead of asking "how many glyphs do you need to identify the font", show the glyphs slowly (like 5 seconds each) and the first correct respondent wins. If the first respondent is incorrect, the other guy has until the time limit to answer correctly, otherwise there's a rematch. Audience members shouting out answers will be hung by their finials. hhp
