Jeremiah—to the best of my knowledge and experience working at branding and branding communications—no legal requirement exists for a logo or any form of branding to use the TM mark provided the owner of the brand / logo makes use of it to identify their products / services / business / trade. The TM mark amounts to an assertion of the branding / logo as the brand used in trade by its owner. As noted on a similar recent thread, the only legal stipulation is that the owner use the brand on their stuff and engage in trade with that stuff and a customer or two. By contrast (R) or ® cannot be used unless the brand owner has registered their logo(s) and branding with the trademark /patents office. Designers may include the TM mark if they want to, as a nod to professionalism, either by request of the commissioning client, or voluntarily, but there's no responsibility for designers to do so per se. It's optional. j a m e s

Go to [[http://www.uspto.gov/main/faq/index.html]] Click on the Trademarks tab What is the difference between TM and the R within the circle ®? Use of the TM and SM symbols may be governed by local, state, or foreign laws and the laws of a pertinent jurisdiction to identify the marks that a party claims rights to. The federal registration symbol, the R enclosed within a circle, may be used once the mark is actually registered in the USPTO. Even though an application is pending, the registration symbol may not be used before the mark has actually become registered. The federal registration symbol should only be used on goods or services that are the subject of the federal trademark registration. PLEASE NOTE: Several foreign countries use the letter R enclosed within a circle to indicate that a mark is registered in that country. Use of the symbol by the holder of a foreign registration may be proper.
