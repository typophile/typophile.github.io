¶ Caslon is the last of the Old Style. Caslon’s Roman was influenced by the Dutch Old Style of the XVII th century: see the the bi-directional serifs of the capital “T”. Caslon is a Baroque and late Old Style.

¶ Baskerville was a neoclassical transitional of the mid-XVIII th century which was considered as a difficult to read typeface back then because it was too strict, contrasted and geometrical. Baskerville’s trademark lies in the opened loop of the lowercase “g”. Take a look at the capital “C” which look like a horseshoe because the two serifs are outside. The tail of the capital “Q” is typical of the neoclassical typefaces. The serifs of the capital “T” are vertical.
