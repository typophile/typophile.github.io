This is a start of a family with some calligraphic tendencies. I tried to bring serif elements to this font; the angles and counter forms are closer to serif type, even though this is very much a sans serid font. [img:sites/default/files/old-images/Tip_4636.png] Here's a pdf:
This has a lot of potential, Tomi! Make sure you keep those counters as open as possible in the heavier weights to avoid some pitfalls from your earlier families. - Some glyphs are missing the stroke modulation that's more obvious in those with curves. This seems fixable on the 's', but I don't know what to tell you on the 'k','v','w','x'. Maybe some very subtle, straight lined tapering? See Amira! - I like it's liveliness, but I think the 'g' is leaning a little too far to the left. Maybe move that bottom bowl a bit. - The italic needs a lot of work. I think there is too much evidence of automatic slanting, but someone with more experience can explain why.
