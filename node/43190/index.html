How to propose font for an identity system

March 15, 2008 • alec

Hi All, I’m currently in the midst of building an identity system for a client at the moment and I’ve managed to find a suitable font which is FF Clan Pro for the system.

For the logotype I’ve managed to vector trace a snapshot of the font as a place holder but now I’m at the stage where I’m visualising all the applications and collaterals and it’s tough not having the font. I really want to sell this font to the client so they can use it across all their communication, including touchscreen etc.

How does one go about visualising a project like this without the font Thanks

You raise a good point. The answer is it not always that easy.

  1. The best solution is that the budget is expansive enough to allow the purchase of font(s) in the development process.

  2. Another solution is to contact the foundry and secure the font on a trail basis. (some foundaries understandably won’t do this).

  3. A third solution is to substitute a similar font in its place and sell the client a paper print out of the font style you want to use. This approach is often flawed as a lot of clients will say why am I having to pay or an extra font when the one you have used looks perfectly OK.

  4. My preferred solution is to factor in font purchases when I quote up front for the job, not giving the client the option. If you think about it, even if you were to use the fonts that come ‘free’ with, for example the Creative Suite, you have had to pay for them indirectly when you purchased the software. It makes business sense to pass on the cost of font licensing in the same way you would pass on the cost of laser prints, writing CD-R’s etc etc…

I totally agree with belleisle… from my experience it’s better to raise your fee to cover typeface purchases and factor it in when you work out the initial budget.

Or else people do tend to question it and you end up being forced to limit your choices. As designers we don’t have a lot of expenses once we have all the equipment – so treat it as you would treat anything else you outsource (photography, illustration…).

If there’s no way you can raise the initial funds – consider billing half up front.

But in this case you can only really either just go for it and get back the cost in your bill, or try and see if the foundry will let you have a trial version.

Village also do a thing where if you purchase one weight (pretty affordable) you can go back and purchase the family at the discount package price.
