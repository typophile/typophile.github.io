Getting into kerning/metrics

December 16, 2008 • kosal

Hello! Now that I’m fairly happy with some of my glyph drawings, I want to give more love to the sidebearings. How does everyone here start? Are there certain key words that guide you in tackling the varied shape combinations? Maybe I should focus on the core characters like o, m, b and build from there. Any tips?

I do a quick pass with the Walter Tracy method and then start looking at shape problems that require changing the letters. After that I go through words (I have a massive preview file with strings of words for every letter) and random letter combinations. Once those look good on screen I move to Emil Ruder’s word sets on paper, and then I jump to pages that rotate through chunks of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights. Then I start kerning (although when I did a revival of Eagle the letter shapes were so weird I started kerning early on).

After it all looks good in English I go back and add diacritics, kern any weird stuff they do, and then start rechecking the Declaration in a series of languages. Giving special attention to Icelandic, Finnish, and Slavic languages is an easy way to spot letter combination that are really rare in English and Romance languages.

Of course, I’m still pretty new to this, so don’t assume that I know what I’m doing!
