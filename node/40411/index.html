Hi Tim, you might wanna have a look at [[Font Starter Packs]].

H&FJ Verlag definitely is worth the money. Do you need all the weights? I’d choose a 10 weights bundle; those $199 will pay off soon. (Hey, the Dollar is cheap! :°)

Foundry Sans isn’t a bad font either, but I personally would rather go with Syntax. That’s a true classic, to me. You can pick single weights in OpenType format for as little as $29.

What will you use the fonts for? All your choices are sans-serif. I don’t say you can’t have more than two sans, hell no; or that Meta and Foundry Sans are in any way similar, but you should be aware that right now you’re covering only a certain corner of the type universe.

Univers 47 What I like about Univers (and what makes/made it outstanding) is its systematic approach: a multitude of weights and widths, all meticulously concerted … so, it feels a little bit odd to pick just one weight out of this system. (Then, why not?) F

Oh, and my first commercially licensed font was Charter, for a university project.
