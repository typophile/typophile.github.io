Does anyone know if any of these Lettres Ornees versions been digitized? It seems they are from wood/metal type-era…

I know version A was digitized by someone but it seems to have been based from a low resolution scanned source as I can see the details are very rough…

There are a few digitalisation of the genre, mostly “free fonts”, usually not of the best quality, as you already noticed. http://moorstation.org/typoasis/designers/steffmann/samples/l/lettres.htm http://moorstation.org/typoasis/designers/westwind/exotica.htm (I can’t find an oblique version)

Looking at past threads (http://typophile.com/node/67020 http://typophile.com/node/42149 http://typophile.com/node/73447 http://www.typophile.com/node/106599) it seems that one still can acquire it in metal and there are at least two commercial releases:
http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/celebrity/parisian-ornamentals/ http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/wiescherdesign/fleurons-initials/ (this one is a less textured version) The fact that they’re commercial doesn’t automatically guarantee a better quality.

With the recent popularity of chromatic fonts and the efforts to fill the gaps in the Dan X. Solo’s collections, there could be some new offering somewhere too.

2014-12-13 16:17:14 • bojev
Too many points is the problem with making any of these elaborate ornamented fonts. About half a decade ago I took a stab at making a well engineered version of Lettres Ornées with the minimum number of points. I got as far as doing part of the letter E [img:sites/default/files/old-images/LettresOrnées1_6253.jpg] [img:sites/default/files/old-images/LettresOrnées3_6382.jpg] At the rate I was going it would have taken an incredible amount of time to do the full alphabet! So I threw in the towel. A project for somebody with lots of time on their hands! Which probably explains why we are stuck with mega-point digital versions that sometimes seize up during viewing and fail in printing. Don
