Sharon, if you want New England, you really should look more into Font Bureau (www.fontbureau.com Boston), or anything from Tobias Frere-Jones at Hoefler & Frere-Jones Typography (www.typography.com). Bitstream is in New England, too… and MyFonts… at least they're headquartered in Cambridge. After having lived in Providence for four years, I really get a kick out of the Providence Type Foundry (http://www.provtype.com/index2.html), too; or at least its font names. Unfortuantely, I can't say that Linotype has anything distinctly "New England." We do sell a lot of typefaces to New Englanders, though. Yves, I think that all of Linotype's products are reasonalbly priced now (no, I'm not saying this just because I work in the marketing department). I mean, the gold edition will still set you back 10 grand, but it is ENORMOUS. Most of the font collections are very nicely priced. The Take Type Collections http://www.linotype.com/6-1785-6/taketype.html are really broad, with a lot of one-off display faces, but they all have a few good text families (including the first appearance of Conrad, on Take Type 3!). Our new special packs sell five or six fonts apiece, at an average of 6 EUR a font, I think. http://www.linotype.com/6-1788-6/compilationvaluepackages.html Plus, we will soon be offering student discounts (if we haven't had them before) And in the name of good, fair competition, I should add that the best of Unger's fonts are available from us, too ;)
