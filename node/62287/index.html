I’m using Bickham Script for a wedding invitation.

The groom happens to be a Third, as in William Gates III.

Unfortunately the cap I is so heavily swashed that III looks like JJJ.

In desperation I have substituted three lowercase ells but there has got to be a better way.

How does one solve this problem generally?

Does one spell out “The Third” in the invite?

Or should I give up and switch to a non-script face?

Or does it look okay as-is? I’m too close, I can’t tell.

thanks in advance.

First, nice treatment on the “y” in the name - very pretty, and much better than the normal “y” in Bickham Script! I agree with both yourself and Solt; the three el’s look bad, capital I is worse. Roman on a skew of 15% might work but I’d be tempted to use small caps rather than making the font smaller. I would present a few versions to your client and let him decide; he may surprise you and ask you to leave off the “3rd” in favor of keeping the font, which lends itself very well to a wedding invitation. Word of caution: if you’re sending to a print shop as a .pdf, make sure this font embeds - I’ve had trouble with it in the past and had to turn to outlines.

Best of luck! :)
