2009-04-11 21:30:17 • apankrat

The scale is still way off, IMO. Break out of the tunnel vision and REALLY shake up the relationship! In my experience, in order to find something that works, you must to completely let go of what doesn’t work. In other words: stop forcing it.

If it were me, I’d search for a font with more weight – right now, it looks like the mark could lose steam, fall, pulverize the type and roll off without even noticing. The type actually looks scared of the mark, all clustered together and cowering below. They aren’t visual peers.

The logotype needs the most work; as it is now, the kerning needs attention and the tracking is too tight. Have you considered capitalization (PoweredGreen.org) or using different tones/colors for each word? With only one ascender and all those round letters, “poweredgreen.org” is kinda one, long muddy worm.

2009-04-11 22:13:54 •

I tend to agree with “litera” the colors are off. I also agree with “epsilicon” the power symbol icon is over killed.

My opinion: if you develop a symbol, similar to what you already have (without the “power” icon symbol), reduce its size, keep the text in AG, but set it in demi instead (a little heavier), adjust the kerning (a little open), and play with 2 different greens. Aqua or teal and warm green. So, you have a symbol w/ 2 color greens. For the text (use 2 same color greens): Powered (in Aqua/teal; Green (in warm green). “.org” could be med. grey. Then place the new symbol in front of the text (but don’t overpower it).
