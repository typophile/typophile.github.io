2004-07-19 17:02:00 • david_leonard
Overall, the "before" seems a little better.

Kerning is not about keeping a constant amount of space between the closest points of letters, but rather about maintaining a total envelope of space equal from one combination to the next, while avoiding confusing overlaps or merging of shapes.

Perhaps the "rt" were a little close together in the original, but if so, you could open them up about 1/3 what you did.

The "ts" really didn't need any modification from the original. Although some parts of the letters (at the top and bottom) come closer to each other, that's made up for by the white space between them in the middle. When you move them apart, you're giving them more total white space in between than the other letter combinations.

I hope that's of some help!
