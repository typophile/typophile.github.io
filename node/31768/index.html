Bodyfont for Magazin (corporate publication, science)

February 25, 2007 • Anton Badinger

Hello! For the body I want to choose a sans serif that is helvetica-like, only a bit more narrow. But not as narrow as Helvetica condensed, of course. I had Imago from BQ for a similar project, so I don’t want to use it again. It should also be a bit more contemporary and lighter (and softer) than Imago. The Magazin will not look crammed, there is a lot of white space and room for large photos. I am happy to hear your suggestions. Your choices for a serif companion (interview-passages, quotations, editorial) are also welcome. best regard, anton

I recently gathered a few typefaces that are similar to Helvetica. Paralucent Condensed would fit your requirements, but I don’t know how well it would do for long reading.

Also consider relatives of Trade Gothic, such as Bulldog, Spiegel and Brown Gothic.

Can you explain what do you mean by “softer”?

Hello Stephen,

thank you for your answer. First I took a look at paralucent light condensed. What startled me first a bit was devicefonts’ website that can not conceal the companies interest in “fun fonts”. But p. really seems fine, i.e. serious. The “g” adds some personality. Plus a real italic is rare for this kind of fonts. Is the kerning really good? “a” and “g” nearly touch each other in the preview window, it might also be a screen problem, though. But: the dot on the i seems really big, that is for sure.

Trade Gothic is always a choice, but with this font I always think of pages full of text, and not of open and somewhat airy layouts. and maybe not “european” enough.

Bulldog, hmm … it is a bit to narrow, and its elegance is maybe not so appropriate for this job (science, hiTech). But I have been using it for another client for years.

Spiegel: Here in Austria Der Spiegel is read a lot and it would seem a bit odd, if people think … hm this magazin font reminds me of someting … I think this is the reason why no magazin I know uses this (very good) font.

Brown Light looks great in the FontShop Preview Window, and it is the font that I give some consideration. It also has the modernist (helvetica, imago-like) feeling, that I wanted. Does anyone have experiences with this font? It seems worth a try. Or: is there another Brown-like font that you know?

Qualifying Softer: Hmm. Maybe I was thinking of this squarish quality, that can be found in Paralucent. But this feeling only is conveyed in the more extended versions, I think. So maybe it was a bit contradictory.
