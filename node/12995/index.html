Bulletin is actually an old metal face cast by BB&S. Designed in large sizes (18pt, 24pt) in 1925. And then in 36pt in 1933. I have all the sizes in several cases. It was quite a nice looking typewriter face. A bit absurd to have a typewriter face in such large sizes but it fit a niche (display) and sold well in its time. It was cast as a monospaced font and required special spacing. I printed a large broadside “chain letter” in it once. Quite fun. The page form weighed 70 pounds!!!

As an aside, but alluding to your remark about Kerouac and tattoos, and perhaps indicative of our changed times: as sceptical and suspicious of authority (or the powers that be) as the beats and hipsters were, I suspect they would have avoided permanent identificational markings.
