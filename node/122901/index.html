This looks like an embroidery font that does not seem to match any retail font. It seems to have been based on 19th century fonts styles ranging from “Antique” [Egyptian] to Clarendon. The serif treatment on S seems to be the key to the source fonts. Perhaps the closest source might be Kinghorn 205 Med http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/talbot/kinghorn-205/ which has strokes and serifs all of a roughly equal weight similar to the lettering in the Cap Tallie. There is also a condensed version. The reverse contrast – top and bottom thicker than sides – shown in the top of S in the Cap Tallie might be a quirk of that the digital instructions for that letter. Also consider Antique Central Heavy http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/aboutype/antique-central/ if you want reverse contrast you could consider http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/bitstream/p-t-barnum/ which suggests the S but the vertical strokes are too thin. The best match would be a Clarendon with strokes and serifs all of a roughly equal weight, which would differ from an Antique or Egyptian in that some of the terminals, such as those in E and L, are tapered. Part way there is Patrick Griffins Clarendon Text Bold https://www.myfonts.com/fonts/canadatype/clarendon-text/ Don
