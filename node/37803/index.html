Good Workaday Sans

October 4, 2007 • buddhaboy

Hi folks,

I have been using Cronos, Myriad Pro, a few weights of Helv Neau I have been able to afford… However, I’m now looking for a sans that will set my work apart from the masses.

I realise that the sum of my work depends on much more than the typeface I use, but Helvetica and Myriad are almost instantly recognisable, even by some of my clients, which although not entirely embarrassing, is something I would like to avoid in most circumstances.

The faces need to be equally at home in headlines in heavier weights, and as body text. I am looking for something a little more condensed than myriad and helvetica, which both seem to take up a lot of space compared to more recent sans'.

Anyway, to cut a long story short I have shortlisted Vista Sans from Emigre, Antenna from Font Bureau, and Scala Sans.

Scala Sans seems the most informal, then Vista, leaving Antenna as the most dominant looking face (I really do like it)…

Whichever I choose it needs to be useful for several months to come, and will be used predominantly in flyers, possibly logotype, and some corporate reports. A tall order from a single family I know.

I’d love to know what people think about FB Antenna, as I cannot find any articles discussing it.

If you want some more suggestions, take a look at the [[http://www.typophile.com/node/33690|‘Current Sans Serif favs’]] thread, from a while ago.
