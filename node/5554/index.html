Hi Dave,

I am dubious that the switch would be relatively painless. I'm not even certain that it is practical today.

InDesign uses a Unicode text engine. So he'd have to switch from a workflow using non-Unicode text to one using Unicode.

Spanish wouldn't be a problem (ID ships with Spanish dictionaries and hyphenation right out of the box). Depending on the level of typographic finesse and his need for vertical text, Chinese could require having the Japanese version of the product, and/or the third-party "InChinese" plug-in. Korean I'm not so sure about--just my ignorance here.

Vietnamese could be a real problem, since it's a "complex script" requiring support for some fancy shaping rules. Current versions of InDesign really don't support Vietnamese.

Of course, I have no idea how well QXP works in these areas. How does he do Vietnamese in QuarkXPress, for instance?

There's an article by Diane King on multilingual publishing in InDesign, that was posted by CreativePro.com. It has lots of info in this area. You could also try the InDesign mailing list on BlueWorld.com, or the InDesign user forum on Adobe.com.

Hope that's of some help.

Regards,
