Responding to a Rejection letter?

February 25, 2010 • jonseer

Any tips? Do you do it?

Do you just thank them, do you do something designed, or do you ask what you did wrong?

I don’t bother. If it’s an email, which is easy to reply to, I might respond, “Thank you for the opportunity to apply/interview.” My Creative Director is in the habit of emailing people who’ve sent in their resumés and telling them what was wrong with it that they didn’t get an interview (usually typos and such). She said none of them had ever responded, but she seems to have wanted something from them. Not sure what as it can be an awkward situation. Perhaps a simple thank you for the warning would have appeased her but I doubt they were feeling grateful.

Given how rare it is for anyone in the design field to send a rejection letter I think it might be worthwhile. Don’t beg or ask why didn’t get hired, just slip a business card into a nice thank-you note so they know you’re not holding a grudge should they need someone later.

But only if they actually rejected you after an interview. if they rejected your resume it’s probably all automated anyway.

2010-02-26 03:16:03 • Jennifer E. Dahl

Also, it is quite possible that you did nothing wrong at all. Someone else was just a better fit.

I’ve applied to many jobs where I left the interview knowing that I nailed it, and did everything perfectly. The only question then is whether the next person interviewed, or the one before, did a better job. Sometimes yes, sometimes no.

Asking why you didn’t get the job can be touchy. You are pretty much questioning the judgment of the hiring team. I would only do so if I personally knew someone on the interview team.
