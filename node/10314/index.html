Did you investigate using more of a blueprint look? As for the type while well set, its boring. You also don't have anything unified. The top of the house looks like clip art, the Initials don't mean anything. Look at a typeface like Eaglefeather based on Frank Lloyd Wrights handwriting. Make the house graphic more abstract and make the contact info part of your schematic. A large part of custom home design is on interior elements (windows, beamed ceilings etc) maybe that imagery would help you. Good start, keep working.

In your latest version loose the halftone in the dream balloon it will cause problems in smaller sizes (fill in). My problem with your house is it is a specific style of house. If I'm right your client will design any "Dream" house. That said look into making the image in the balloon abstract, like a piece of a blue print. You don't need a lot of detail to get the point across. I would loose the date and everything but the name. Those are elements that can be put in different places based on the layout. You could put the address phone etc at the bottom of a letterhead but you will always want the name and the illustration to stay together. Keep going!
