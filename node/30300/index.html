Custom Type in the Auto Industry: Where Does It Come From?

January 3, 2007 • pdxuser

Every car's logo seems to use custom type, and pretty cool type at that. Where is all this original work coming from? Are designers from major foundries commissioned? Then why aren't they making fonts this cool? And can anyone think of another industry that generates as much custom type?
Can you show us some examples of car logos you like? Maybe they just look so much cooler, because they are in chrome and not in black and white? ;-) When it comes to those script-like car logos, it makes a big difference to make ONE word look good or to make a typeface, because of the problem of all the letter ligatures. I am just in the process of making a font inspired by car logos, and I can tell you it's not easy. Without the relatively new OpenType technology it would be impossible at all. So I learned pretty quick, why there are not much fonts in this style. Ralf
2007-01-03 13:28:26 • Typography.Guru
> Then why aren’t they making fonts this cool? Well, it is a lot easier to make six or eight letters "cool" than an entire set of glyphs. With a font you might have to change the shape of one letter to provide visual consistency with others. Basically, there is a big difference between creating a logo and creating a font.

2007-01-03 19:24:49 • Nick Shinn
Porsche has all it's own fonts. It has the script it has been using for years and no one has ripped off or digitized. It has even taken Franklin Gothic and put it's own spin on it, so that when you see a genuine Porsche ad it is in genuine Porsche Franklin Gothic. Land Rover has been using fonts that the public can buy. Last year they switched their body copy to a font that is sold in England, and well, I couldn't see spending the money they were asking. (I've put it in Eurostile, and Y&R have been okay with the results -- because we are getting paid.) Many of the cars have fonts that look original, but they have been modified to get the "logo type look." It remind me of publishing -- during the days when every titled was handlettered. Only, they weren't all handlettered -- so many were handtailored. A swash added here, a line deleted there - and voila - a great looking three word title...
