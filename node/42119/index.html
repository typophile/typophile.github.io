2008-02-16 00:44:37 • Bald Condensed

I decided I would rather do a gothic poem about Fakir. But since it doesn’t count in the contest I’ll post it here.

In the same strain of Roland will I tell Things unattempted yet in prose or rhyme On whom strange serif and kern fury fell A font esteemed so wise in former time; If design, who to like cruel pass has well. Night brought my interrupted design By hourly wastes, my senses concede my skill But strength my daring promise to fulfill.

Different weights it drives, and evermore Strong design, conceptual, historic cries And it’s presence up-leading on the shore And Fakir her lovely visage spies Epic story, epic battles, epic wins Arriving quickly to the designers eyes (Through many days no news of her had shown) The beautiful Fakir is known.

And be she cheap with all except the weights. “Use Papyrus” The client did bestow Ah! False and cruel Fortune! foul despite! While others triumph, I am drown’d in woe And can it be that I treasure such slight And can I then my very Fakir forego? No! Let me die; it this a sign? A long life; if I must cease to design.
