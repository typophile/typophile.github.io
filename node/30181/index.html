I wonder if it could be Michael Neugebauer's Litera. I actually cut the first font of Litera back in 1977, following Michael's design. In those days done completely by hand in Rubylith, of course. Litera was commissioned by a well-known Salzburg architect named Garstenauer. Later on Michael developed it in several weights and as I remember sold it to Letraset, who had also published his Cirkulus and Squire designs. (Could be wrong, though.) Anyway, have a gander at this and see what you think: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/efscangraphic/litera-sb/ I still have the Ulano swivel knife and Rotring compass that I used to make that type face, although they have seen precious little use in the last twenty-five years . . .
