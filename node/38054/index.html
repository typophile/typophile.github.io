I'm a Giza fan, too. Nick Shinn's Figgins is based on the sans-serif font that Figgins (on whose Egyptian David Berlow based Giza) brought out, so that could be sympathetic or even be used instead of Giza. It's not out yet, but I believe it will be soon -- e-mail Nick if you're interested. In the same vein as Figgins, I think a slightly idiosyncratic and 19th-century-feeling sans or serif might work nicely. DynaGrotesk, Maple, National Grotesque (soon-to-be-released), and Parry and Parry Grotesque could be some really nice possibilities. And FF Bau would be a 20th-century-style alternative.

Great recommendations, Reed. All of those would play very well with Giza. Since you're using a 19th-century face for the logo you might want to consider using something more modern to reflect the product. Perhaps a grotesque sans (like FF Bau) would be best. Or maybe Maquette, Region, or Proxima Nova. Perhaps a modern gothic like Scopex.
