Edited: The almost closed capital _C_ is a feature of a font known simply as Grotesque dating from 1832 that was still in use at the time of the 1928 Olympics when various weights and sizes were available. There is a close match in the letterforms of Stephenson Blake's Grotesque No. 9, which has been digitized, without providence information, by URW [img:sites/default/files/old-images/GrotesqueNo9asCANADA_4624.gif] http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/urw/grotesque-no-9/ The Monotype Corporations's Headline Bold, which has also been digitized, [img:sites/default/files/old-images/HeadlineBoldAsCanada_4168.gif] http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/mti/headline-mt/ closely follows Stephenson Blake's Grotesque No. 9 but is slightly less condensed. The lettering is probably a lighter weight sister font sold as Montotype Grotesque Condensed No.4. I did not spot an exact digital version but Montotype Grotesque Extra Condensed [img:sites/default/files/old-images/MontotypeGrotesqueExtraCondensedAsCanada_5361.gif] http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/mti/grotesque-mt/ might work, despite being a little too light. The digital font is based on Monotype's update of it's various Grotesque fonts by F.H. Pierpont in 1926. The series has interesting variations in details between various weights and widths. Don
