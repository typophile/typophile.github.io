London In Prague

April 17, 2007 • Jan Sabach

Hello everyone, I've been working on a logo for an upcoming lecture, that will take place in Prague in the fall. It is called London In Prague (alternatives to traditional marketing research). How some of the new marketing techniques that are already being used in London (or UK) could be implemented to the Prague (or Czech) environment. My initial point was "take something British and make it Czech". My main question is—is the symbol recognizable enough as a British flag turned into a Czech flag? For the typeface I chose Sebastian Text by Frantisek Storm. It is slightly reminiscent of Johnston Sans or Gill, but still uniqly Czech (or at least I hope). Thanks for your comments.
I think the typeface choice is great. As for the flag, I got it, but it took me a little while. To me, the Union Jack is not just areas of blue and red and white, but how they interact. Maybe rather than separating the sections completely, take slices out of the British flag that are mostly one colour, but with a little bit of the others too. Not sure if my idea would work or look good. As it stands, it's very nice. - Lex
