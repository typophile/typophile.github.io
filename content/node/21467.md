  body: "While the term \"legend\" seems heroic, it is also pretty murky as well.\r\n\r\nTwo
    questions. What specific geographic region and cultural influence does the type
    need to reference? What time period does the type need to invoke?\r\n\r\nAnswers
    to both of those questions will narrow down the field of obvious choices to a
    mere few.\r\n\r\nIf the client wants something more non-specific which can work
    for a variety of time periods and cultural themes, you might consider a good all-caps
    titling font. Adobe Garamond, Perpetua, Utopia and Goudy Old Style are a few that
    come to mind.\r\n\r\nI would suggest Trajan or Trajan Pro. It is a very attractive
    typeface. But it has been terribly overused for the past decade, especially on
    movie poster titles."

    purposely vague. It is a m\xE9lange of ancient inscriptional writing, with visual
    references to Anglo-Saxon, Celtic, medieval and even a bit of ancient Greek and
    roman letterforms. It is an all caps font, so there are alternates for every letter
    in the lowercase position, plus there are a number of alternate letter forms and
    ligatures <a href=\"http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/ihof/p22-yule/heavy/charmap.html\">hidden</a>
    in the font. The heavy weight has an admittadly <a href=\"http://www.myfonts.com/search?search%5Btext%5D=neuland\">Neuland</a>
