FONT IDENTIFICATION SOFTWARE

March 3, 2011 • fredferp

Okay, so we have the ID Font forum of this website to help us find, or at least narrow, the fonts a given sample we provide may be from. And then there is, of course, www.identifont.com. Lastly, Harold Baldus at Phil’s fonts often has been able to nail down fonts for me.

But has anyone put together, or even started to put together, something better and more comprehensive, in terms of a database, or query decision-maker (like identifont.com), or the millions of brilliant typographers and graphic artists on this forum?

This sounds like an area that getting together a bunch of folks like you and me, collaborating online, might be able to put together something that would help us distinguish between the Helveticas and the Arials, between the Times and Garamonds and Palatinos, etc.

Frankly, we need more classifications of font, and font features, besides some image identification software that could really help.

But perhaps I am trying to reinvent the wheel—something which one of my college marketing professors emphasized NOT to do!

Give me your knowledge, expertise, and wisdom on this ponderous pursuit of the typographers' (or typography finders') holy grail.
