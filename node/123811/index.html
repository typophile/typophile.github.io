Not a typeface, but hand lettering (as was most poster display type of the era).

The lettering appears to be based on an old woodtype style called Latin: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/mti/latin/

Some digital typefaces with a similar vibe (but by no means a match to your sample): http://www.houseind.com/fonts/edbenguiatfonts/ [Ed Roman] http://www.google.com/fonts/specimen/Fontdiner+Swanky

AFAIK nobody has made this lettering into a font. The capital letters are fairly standard Roman letters with pulled out serifs. Fonts that include this feature include Ronaldson http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/canadatype/ronaldson/ and Tiffany http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/itc/tiffany/ The other letters are an unusual mix of roman letters with big ball terminals and sloped letters. For some ideas see Everafter Bold http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/typetrust/everafter/ and Oxtail Black http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/macrhino/oxtail/ Don
