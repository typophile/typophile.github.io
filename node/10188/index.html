I don't mean litterally 'ugly'. That was hyperbole - it's true. I should clarify that I mean the top bold 'a' specifically - not the italic form & by ugly I don't mean disgusting or horrible but rather something odd or different from the other characters. Something suprising & unprogramatic - maybe a little stinky even. An exception. There are alot of examples of this kind of thing in other arts. - In perfume you often need a stinky/musky smell to go with your pretty aromas in order to take attention ( musk ) & then reward that attention ( flower, fruits, spice etc. ). - In western musical theory you need some 'disonant' or 'sad' notes to go with your 'whole' or 'happy' notes to create a feeling of resolution - otherwise it just sounds a bit boring. - Vienamese food needs a bit of stinky fermented fish sauce (which taken straight would be terrible) - or it comes off as bland & uncomplicated. The fish sauce provides interest. I am sure there are plenty of other examples & probably better ones & even the Coasta 'a' may not be the best example of this exception in type - but the point is that type in general benefits from careful use of exceptions & this is perhaps most true in a text face. I want to try to focus in on characters that benefit from being exceptional in some way & maybe even a bit distorted or stinky for a text face. I was hoping to do this without getting into the whole legibility/readabilty/baouma (sp?) debate - or a sellability debate ( Spiekermann's often related comment ((now paraphrased poorly by me)) that type designs with unusual shaped letters often sell better). Anyway - no offence meant. Just the opposite in fact.
