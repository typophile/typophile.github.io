It is common in calligraphy. It comes from an older way of writing e with two strokes. The first is a downward curve like a c. Then the pen goes back to the top to do the loop a bit like a smaller reversed c and the swash is the connector to the next letter. Various script and calligraphy fonts have it but I presume you want something looking like metal type.

The more calligraphic you go, the more options you’ll have. [img:sites/default/files/old-images/e-swash_6217.png] These are from: Zapfino (1,2), Apple Chancery (3) and Adobe Jenson Swash Italic (4). Among those fonts is the beautiful Poetica, where the above mentioned Slimbach really let his hair down.
