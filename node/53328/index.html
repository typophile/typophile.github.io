Opinion: Typography

January 10, 2009 • _

Many designers love 1001freefonts.com or similar websites to get their hardly unique typefaces that will be used by much more than 1001 different designers. I do not use these typefaces, not because you can't at times find something good, but because I feel like using these fonts is an easy way out. If you want to make something look "evil" it is very easy to get a grungy, distorted typeface and throw it on a dark background and presto! you have your evil look. It takes the skill out of designing. I have a few favorite fonts that I use, and as you can probably assume, Helvetica is one of them. Wim Crouwel said "[A typeface] shouldn't have a meaning in itself, the meaning is in the content of the text, and not in the typeface, and that's why we loved Helvetica very much..." Using a font with too much "emotion" can take away from the overall success of a piece (even if a momentary superficial point is achieved). Now, I know that not all of these fonts are horrible and some briefly distorted fonts can be used to achieve a subtle look, but any designer can distort any font to achieve a completely unique texture and a completely unique effect. Typography is an art form and a beautiful typeface is something very hard to make and the majority of these free font sites pervert this art and produce mass amounts of drivel. My point, get a high quality font collection like Adobe's Font Folio, find something that stands out to you and if you need a texture then make a texture, if you need a word to look shaky, move the letters to achieve your look. Don't be a lazy designer. You’ll like your piece much more if you don’t skimp.
