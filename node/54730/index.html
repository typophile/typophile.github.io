My friendly local bookseller recently flogged me 16 copies of U&lc from the early/mid ’80s… she had them marked at NZ$5 a pop, but we came to an arrangement on a lesser price for the job lot. I bought them because they reminded me of my undergraduate days at Middlesex Poly, where they were used as case studies for a lecture on grid construction.

I don’t think they’re valuable, but as Ricardo says, they will be rare and/or collectable – if only because they are deteriorating faster than my (almost as old) copies of Baseline and Emigre magazines – which I still use for teaching purposes.

John D. Berry’s so right about all that crumbling newsprint in designers' attics…
