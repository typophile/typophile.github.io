July 30, 2007 • Anton Badinger

Hello!

I wonder what the expertes suggestions might be: Let’s say a bank gets a a new owner and plans to have a new corporate design: more friendly, more contemporary. They used a customized Futura so far and everything was quite the same for more than 15 years. As there will be a lot of different publications, a big Type-Family like Corpid or Corporate would propably be a good choice because of flexibility. But is there another suggestion? Especially concerning friendliness and contemporary feeling? Or maybe it is better to have a face that conveys those values for the display-sizes (headlines, advertisments, posters), and have a more neutral sans-companion for all the rest … Any suggestions /font-names are welcome.
Thank you for your ideas, I really appreciate this forum.

anton

Why not have a custom family drawn? The cost will likely come to about the same as licensing an existing family.

A quick rundown:

vista - maybe not so bad, but a little bit too display’y versa - too old fashioned sansa - too rough’n’tuff for a bank neue sans - too generic for branding leitura - again too generic absara - too bizarre for a bank legato - again too old fashioned klavika - more contemporary and visible, but perhaps too cold kievit - again too generic and old fashioned

Why not something whit a little more charcter (in general)? The face should be recognizable and unique to the bank. Also the friendly roundness is not really found in your examples, and Sauna is spoken for…
