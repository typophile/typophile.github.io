I haven't been able to find a match for the top line. It seems to me that the matter in the middle is more likely to be a Clarendon than a Modern; for example, it might be Antique No. 6. On the bottom, we do have a fat and bold version of a Modern; it could be Title No. 2. That is, if one is using the Fifteenth Book of Specimens from the Cincinatti Type Foundry. The type is all much too old-fashioned for, say, the 1923 ATF specimen book; one does, apparently, have to go back to the 19th Century. I was suitably astonished, though, in coming across Gothic No. 8 on page 217 of the specimen book of the MacKellar, Smiths, and Jordan company of Philadelphia for June of 1892. 2010-10-24 22:36:43 • oldnick
It's definitely not Cheltenham Bold Extra Condensed, because there's no big serif on the crossbar of the E. Globe Gothic Condensed has thick and thin strokes, but no serifs at all, so it's out as well. Miehle Bold Headline is very close, but the crossbar in the R is only in the middle of the character, while in the example it's significantly below the middle.
