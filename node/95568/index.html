Banknotes of the third Zimbabwean dollar (ZWR) (printed 2006-2009)

August 14, 2012 • Gimbo

I’d like to know the name of the typeface used to spell out the face values (e.g. [[http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/3e/Zimbabwe_%24100_trillion_2009_Obverse.jpg|ONE HUNDRED TRILLION DOLLARS]]) on the 2007 and 2008 Zimbabwean banknote series. According to [[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Banknotes_of_Zimbabwe#Paper_money_of_the_third_dollar_.28ZWR.29|Wikipedia]], the earliest of these notes were printed in October 2006.

It’s a sans-serif outline typeface, and the most distinctive thing about it is that some of the letters (A, [[http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/a/a2/Zimbabwe_%241000000_2008_Obverse.jpg|M]], N and [[http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/b/bf/Zimbabwe_%2420_2007_Obverse.jpg|W]]) have exaggerated overshoots (with the last three also extending below the baseline). I haven’t had any luck with WhatTheFont or Identifont.

Some other examples for the different letterforms: [[http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/70/1_Zimbabwe_Dollar_2008.jpg|ONE DOLLAR]] [[http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/7c/Zimbabwe_%2410_2007_Obverse.jpg|TEN DOLLARS]] [[http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d1/Zimbabwe_%24500_2007_Obverse.jpg|FIVE HUNDRED DOLLARS]] [[http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/2/20/Zimbabwe_%2450_billion_2008_Obverse.jpg|FIFTY BILLION DOLLARS]]

Banknotes usually have custom lettering to prevent forgery.
