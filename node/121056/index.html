Confirming Bookman. Quoting MF description text: “Bookmania (2011) is a revival of Bookman Oldstyle (1901) and the Bookmans of the 1960s, but with all the features you would expect in a modern digital font family.” Don’t know any other version of Bookman available with such an amount of alternate glyphs: /[[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/marksimonson/bookmania/semibold/glyphs.html#glyphs/519883/547|B]], /[[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/marksimonson/bookmania/semibold/glyphs.html#glyphs/519883/1653|k]], /[[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/marksimonson/bookmania/semibold/glyphs.html#glyphs/519883/1789|r]], /[[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/marksimonson/bookmania/semibold/glyphs.html#glyphs/519883/1965|y]] and /[[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/marksimonson/bookmania/semibold/glyphs.html#glyphs/519883/279|A]] for your sample Baseline is also Bookman. My suggestion would probably to keep Bookmania scaling it to match the lettering using /[[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/marksimonson/bookmania/semibold/glyphs.html#glyphs/519883/775|K]], /[[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/marksimonson/bookmania/semibold/glyphs.html#glyphs/519883/917|N]], /[[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/marksimonson/bookmania/semibold/glyphs.html#glyphs/519883/1041|P]] and /[[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/marksimonson/bookmania/semibold/glyphs.html#glyphs/519883/1050|R]] alternate glyphs.
