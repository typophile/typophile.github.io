Cintiq screen pad for hand problems?

July 17, 2007 • fontplayer

I use my computer a lot, and initially used the touchpad for years with no problem, then the tip of my finger started to get sensitive. So I switched to a roller ball, then my thumb started to get sore from doing the left click. Then I went to a regular mouse and the back of my hand is feeling like the nerves are getting tweaked.

Arthritic? sensitivity pretty much took me out of playing music a couple years ago. (and the missing $8K a year I used to pick up on weekends has seriously eroded my fun/pocket money)

In considering a long term solution to this situation, I got the bright idea of a tablet/pen solution. Has anyone used one enough to develop any hand problems with one?

And what of the Cintiq screenpad? It looks really cool, although the price is a bit scary.

http://www.wacom.com/cintiq/

When I try using tablets for extended periods I just end up with different aches and pains than I get from using mice, trackballs, and so on. You know how if you sit at a drawing table for hours your arms cramp and your lower back hurts? It’s the same if you use a tablet. Also, tablets are a huge PITA for setting type and dealing with vectors, because it detects the stylus even at short distances from the screen, which screws up your ability to just instantly click and/or release.

The only good solution to the repetitive motion problems that I have found to work is spending time at the gym. Even just loosening up my arms by doing cardio on an eliptical trainer makes a big difference, and weight training all but erases the pain and numbness. Before you drop the money on a Cintiq, try getting a pair of running shoes and a set of dumbbells.
