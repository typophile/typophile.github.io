Feedback Appreciated For Research Direction

March 17, 2007 • blammas

As a result of learning the basics of letterpress in a paper offered by my university I have formed a strong interest in letterpress typography. I want to use this interest as a basis for my final research / design project, which extends over the course of a year.

I understand that there is a current revival in letterpress typography, however I feel that the current use of letterpress is a pastiche of the past. In order for letterpress to be moderen and relevant to today it should display “its felxibility in telling stories and providing current issues in current ways. I strongly feel that to achieve this the computer should be used in conjunction with letterpress technology.

I am having some trouble with working out exactly where to go with this, but I think I need to identify a topic to test this out on, I would apreciate any thoughts on the topic.

One may argue that the combination of computers and letterpress have contributed to letterpress' usage as “pastiche”. I imagine a lecturer would suggest you explore the assertion that letterpress can be modern and/or relevant (and in which context?). I think that what makes letterpress special and unique is that it’s not perfect, that it’s more tactile than current printing technology. There’s something about it that makes it warmer and friendlier than digital output. So it may be relevant after all, just not in a commercial, large-scale kind of way. The challenging part of doing research here on that topic is that many resources covering type history, letterpress and so forth are located on the other side of the world. But good luck though!
