Typeface change at Financial Times

September 16, 2014 • PJay

See: http://www.theguardian.com/media/greenslade/2014/sep/15/financialtimes-newspaper-formats?CMP=ema_546

Whatever the virtues of the new layout, IMO this new FT typeface, at least what shows in the headline, is a bad choice - it’s a bit spiky and it’s busy - almost frenetic: it pulls the eye back and forth among the letters. Doesn’t assist comprehension in moving forward efficiently along the lines of text. Ugg.

In contrast, the face used in the Guardian article about the change at the Financial Times clarifies meaning, doesn’t impede. Never mind that I’m contrasting is between serif and sans.

That “Sil icon” in the sample image sure is jarring…

The letters in the headline on the front page of the ‘The Financial Times’ as exhibited on ‘The Guardian’s website were clear ( and I thought it was jumpy and distracting). I assumed that the same face, ‘Financial’, was used in the body type, but maybe was mistaken. I’ll have to search out a copy of the newspaper to see if that’s the case.

There have been other cases where I felt that modernization of the type went awry because might have been done more for window dressing than reading facility. The Penguin historical atlases are one case in point, IMO. The eminently readable and economical ‘Times’ was exchanged for a more contemporary face, whose name I’ve forgotten, but it was too weedy, too greedy of space ( the layout of the pages was attractively compact with ‘Times’, awkwardly sprawling with the new face ) and had those distracting ‘c’s with the stunted lower curves.
