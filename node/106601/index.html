Stone-carved Blackletter Script

October 3, 2013 • brycependleton

This stone-carved blackletter type is on the front of the main administrative building of Virginia Tech's campus in Blacksburg, Virginia, USA. The building was completed in 1936, and I believe the relief carving is original with the construction. So far I have not been able to find a match (or anything really close for that matter), which leads me to believe it may have been designed or modified by the architects of the building. Please help me find the rest of the alphabet for this font.
To be clear the text reads, Julian A. Burruss Hall (the capital J, A, and H aren't very obvious)

I agree that the lettering for the building inscription is most likely a creation of Carmel Johnston & Wright Architects. It is a pastiche of English and German Black letter styles. A large number of Blackletter fonts have been digitized, with various degrees of quality. The following are some good sources for Blackletter design revivals which may help to extend the alphabet. You may have to pick and choose from various fonts to find letters that harmonize with the 1936 inscription. Free: Manfred Klein http://moorstation.org/typoasis/designers/klein/ Paul Lloyd http://moorstation.org/typoasis/designers/lloyd/index.htm Dieter Steffmann http://moorstation.org/typoasis/designers/steffmann/index.htm Note: Many of the Steffmann fonts are pirated from other font makers and "cleaned up" by him. Commercial Gerhard Helzel http://www.fraktur.biz/ As an aside, I wonder if "Virginia Tech" was a science focused school. If it was, the choice of Black letter is somewhat unusual. In Germany Blackletter was for "Klutur" and Antiqua, i.e. Roman or Latin letters were for science. On the other hand, if the lettering inspiration was more English than German, then this makes some sense. But I'm only speculating here. Don
