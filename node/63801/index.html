Open up the PDF and view the ‘Document Properties-Fonts’. If the font was embedded you’ll see something to the effect of ‘Font Name – Embedded Subset’.

A correctly made PDF will open and print fine on a PC or a Mac (I’ve been doing this since 1993) and will contain all it needs (fonts, and images) to be viewed and printed as intended.

Insert someone who doesn’t know what they are doing and it’s a crap shoot. (72dpi RGB images, fonts not embedded, etc.)

In Essence PDF (Portable Document Format) was created by Adobe to be platform and device independent.

http://www.adobe.com/pdf/about/history/ “Regardless of the computer’s operating system, PDF was reliable and consistent, displaying and printing the same every time."
