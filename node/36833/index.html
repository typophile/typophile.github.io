2007-09-06 12:30:55 • SuperUltraFabulous

Avenir and Akzidenz Grotesk are certainly not the same typeface. But I think that, to casual readers, they may seem close enough alike that they’ll almost just sense the difference, rather than seeing them outright. This, in turn, will be almost like an unconscious message that something’s not right. It’ll be an irritation: that the two “almost match”–again, to the untrained eye of readers who are not into typography.

Why on earth would you give readers that kind of “itch” that they can’t scratch? I mean, what’s the aim? Wouldn’t using, maybe, a different version of of one of those types for each of the display heads make more sense and provide a unity of purpose?

That said, I think Sabon is a great face for body text.

I just don’t think you want to put together too fonts that are close but no cigar, as it will distract the reader—even if only unconsciously—and maybe hinder comprehension. But maybe I’m taking myself oo seriously, reading and re-reading an interview of me on the blog, Publishing Careers. I make a point there of saying—I’m paraphrasing now—that making pages is supposed to serve the reader.
