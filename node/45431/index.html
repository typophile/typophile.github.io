A unique source of high quality reproductions of virtually every possible letter form from the times of Roman empire till the beginning of the 20th century is the czech book “Krasne pismo” (~Beatuful Type) by Frantisek Muzika. It is a 2vol book, the two volumes together are about 15cm thick with thousands of reproductions, perfectly chosen to illustrate various type evolution stages (most of the images show letters magnified to show every possible detail that may be of interest to serious typographers). It is written in Czech, but the reproductions are so valuable that it has gained some popularity even among people who can not read it. I’ve heard from some of my american friends that they have invested the effort to obtain it just for the images, that may be difficult to get elsewhere. The book was written in early sixties, but remains (I believe) one of key resources of its kind. Try abebook.com to get it. Or, there exists a cheaper recent re-issue available, e.g., at http://www.paseka.cz/?produkt=145, or at any bigger bookstore in Prague.

2008-05-19 19:46:30 • typerror

I try to show examples that people might already have some general familiarity with. So I talk about stone carving and show an image of Trajan’s column. And how one of the reasons we have serifs is that the stone carver was trying to enter the stone gracefully with his chisel without cracking it. Trajan’s column connects literally to the font that bares its name which can be found on many computers today. My thought is that if I can move from something known to something unknown the audience can make the connections more quickly.

The current points of interest I use include: cuneiform as my earliest example phoenician wine trade to cause dissemination of alphabet quill, sumi brush, chisel influenced the shape of letterforms papermaking in china trajan book of kells, scribes and manuscripts gutenberg - book faces industrial revolution - Display faces WWII - rationalism, bauhaus Summer of Love - psychedelia Advent of computer - digital faces

I would love other ideas. The goal is that the participant will come away with the impression that typography/letterforms are dynamically woven into the fabric of life. How we are all interconnected is a key theme in my talk.
