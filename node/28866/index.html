2006-10-17 13:12:57 • Alessandro Segalini

Equal length of control vectors does not improve the geometric qualities of the spline, I am afraid. It seems to be one of the most widespread fallacies.

If your curve looks somehow wrong or discontinuous you almost always need to shift the position of the actual nodes. Fiddling about with the control points does not help.

as a novice, my input may not be as valuable as those above… then again…

The Briem site is terrific. His advice to draw the damned thing again and again…

and again…

is right on. I must have drawn 20 esses before i got to one i was ready to settle on… at least until i get back to balancing weights and such. The following samples each have 10 bezier pts.

[img:sites/default/files/old-images/s_5367.jpg]

If they don’t look much different to you, again, M. Briem has the right advice. sit. look at them. and then look at them some more. I take a printed page of chars with me when i ride to the beach every day. and look at them.

Once i got to where i like the basic shape, I removed the points from the top and bottom of the bowls and from the mid stroke and added points to the outsides of the bowl strokes, resulting in 8 points which gave me better control of the weight and curvature.

[img:sites/default/files/old-images/s2_3412.jpg]
