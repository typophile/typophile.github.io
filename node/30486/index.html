You must be looking at Adobe’s and Bitstream’s Goudy. Take a look at URWs…the letters fit. And I adore the punctuation on Goudy.

Not heresy, nicest thing about type, we all have opinions, loves and hates.

I knew one art director who hated ITC Eras. Use to say, almost daily, that it is neither gothic nor italic. It just leans there. He also would tell me that he would put his own personal hatred aside and if the typeface worked with the job, he’d use it. I once showed him ITC Eras obliqued another 6 degrees, and when it ever came up that he would be using that face (which I think happened once since its release) it was used with the further oblique to make it look italicized.

Fortunately, it takes all kinds to make this world what it is. And if we all like the same exact thing—yikes, from a design point, it would be pretty boring.

I think this is when we also thank Toulouse Lautrec for insisting his local printer use color on the press. Otherwise, we’d all still be printing in black & white today!
