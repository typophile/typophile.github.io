Any help would be really appreciated. Many thanks, Vit
Set all glyphs to 600 (or whatever). Example; for similar values of straight and curved sides: Select and centre glyphs H, O. Create metrics classes for left straight sides of H: B, D, E, F, I K, L, M, N, P, R Right straight sides of H: I, M, N. Left curved sides of O: C, G, Q Right curved sides of O: D, Q Do this for all glyphs with similar values, then you only have to alter the value of the base glyph to apply it to the others, plus some manual adjustment.

I have been wondering whether there could be some macro script or perhaps some trick using action-set or so? Yes, you can reset the widths mechanically… 
from robofab.world import CurrentFont
font = CurrentFont()
targetWidth = 600
for glyph in font:
    widthDelta = targetWidth - glyph.width
    glyph.leftMargin += int(widthDelta/2)
    glyph.width = targetWidth
    glyph.update()
…but what you'll find is that the contours of many glyphs, especially the widest and narrowest ones, will want to be adjusted to fit the width properly.

The thing is setting proportional shapes into a standard width and making sure they are optically centered isn't going to give a great result. The reason is that to make it work you will end up with a hideously widely spaced font. Instead, you probably want to squash that w and extend that i rather than leaving the i floating in whatever space is wide enough to fit everything. I suggest that you begin with your n & o and at slightly wider glyphs like d b p q and find your base width from these. You can probably live with adding a bit of extra space around the o and leave it alone for now. Next begin adjusting shapes so they fill this new space evenly. You may need to expand your s and maybe your a. Compress your m. And so on. This will be fairly intensive design work for certain glyphs - but that's how you make a real monospace. Best wishes!
