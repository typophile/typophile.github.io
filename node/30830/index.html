I think Will is not asking about overshoot as optical compensation, but rather whether you can have your ascender + descender extend beyond 1000. The answer is yes, you can. You can also, as you say, have your descenders actually go 10 units below your specified 'descender line'. But then you have to live with the consequences. These may be that your font will have descenders crashing into ascenders on the next line. Or ascenders or descenders that will be cut off and not appear on screen or in print. These things depend on the application and what the user does. To avoid problems, I believe that most fonts keep the ascender + descender 1000 or less, and the upper case diacritics below a line 200 units above the ascender--as the usual default line gap is 20%. This is just to stay out of trouble. Are you doing a script face? Others may give you fuller answers about script fonts, Arabic, etc., and when and how to violate what most fonts do.
