A Slab-Serif Companion to Serif?

April 17, 2007 • Maxim Zhukov

Can any of you type geeks give me an example, or several examples, of a good type superfamily with the primary design serif (better Old-Style) and the companion design slab-serif. I only know of two such superfamilies: Erik Spiekermann’s and Christian Schwartz’s DB Serif / DB News, or Jean-François Porchez’s Le Monde Livre / Le Monde Courrier. I know there are more styles to either family, but I am primarily interested in the serif/slab-serif relationship. I suspect there should be more similar designs out there. Thank you in advance.
TheAntiqua/TheSerif, altho TheAntiqua is kind of slab-ish to begin with.

ITC Stone Serif and Stone Informal have a relationship similar to the Le Mondes.

FF Nexus Serif and Mix, though there isn’t really a “primary” style in the family.

Freight Text and Micro, though the later was designed to be used specifically at small sizes it will work anywhere.
