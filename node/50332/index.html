Miss Tiffany’s right, Bill. I think the “sparkle” is a function of the contrast in the thick/thin strokes in the letters, and none of the photocomp or PostScript Bodoni’s have solved that for text-size settings.

I’ll allow that I find Bodoni for text as tough a nut as Sumner found Janson. I did rework a Bodoni Book I’m not terribly satisfied with – Rich Hendle likes it, but he’s sometimes too easily pleased. You could fix it after Janson . . .

As I remember, for a source for Bodoni used for body copy, you need look no further than older issues of Life magazine – 1960s was it?

My wife remembered that the books by/on Wallace Fowlie published by Duke University Press were set in Bodoni (Linotype metal, by Heritage printers).

While hot metal was different, it was also just one way of putting ink on paper. The changing of paper, ink, and the method of putting ink on paper might mean we can’t exactly mirror letterpress, but we could, if someone cared to put in the work, come quite close.

I think Bodoni is a fine font for books. The Google scan doesn’t show the type terribly well, but here is a link to Journal of Rehearsals:
