Ahem. Steve Matteson is the original perpetrator of Curlz, let it be known, and it was commissioned by a custom client for inclusion in a laser printer. Curlz is well known for the same reason Comic Sans is well known; it was distributed widely and made available to many, via bundling. My part in the development of Curlz was as a hired hand to finish the character set, same as the work I did on Wood Type 3 (Chromatics). Haven't we all had jobs like that? Matching the style of existing characters is a useful skill in type design for those times when the client has some lettering, a sign, a logo, that has to be preserved. You should see the "rustic", "elegant", and other period styles I've been asked to match. "calligraphy is to angst what scones are to rave parties." : D
