ADA approved typefaces

July 23, 2008 • eckertdl

Does anyone have a list of ADA approved typefaces or know of any easy way to figure out if a typeface is functional without having to do a handful of measuring? I am trying to create indoor wall signs for an upcoming apartment complex and the signs must be ADA approved. The typeface I would like to use is TheMix (caps), but again, there is no easy way to find out if it is useable or not without measuring out every letter.
I don't think there is a list of approved typefaces. Lightest weight requires at least a 1:10 stroke width to height ration. Boldest must be no heavier than 1:5 stroke width to height ratio. There are also requirements for average letter width to height ratios as well. Our ClearviewADA was designed to meet these requirements: http://www.terminaldesign.com/fonts/sets/?CID=4 JamesM
