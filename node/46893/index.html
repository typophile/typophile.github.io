There are two typefaces in the Stephenson, Blake & Co specimen, third edition (ca. 1920s) that have the appellation of “Runic.” One is called Lining Bold Runic; the other is Lining Runic Elongated. Monotype’s Runic Condensed is closely related to the latter. But these weren’t a unified family and have only some shared stylistic traits.

The concept of a unified family of weights and widths was only just taking shape at that time period and was not all-pervasive. Off-beat styles like this didn’t usually get the “family treatment.”

So I doubt that Monotype ever had a unified family of Runic styles. I believe there was only ever this one.

Why the “Condensed” in the name? I believe that Runic was used as a generic style designation – like Latin or Egyptian. So this is Monotype’s interpretation of a condensed, Runic-style typeface.
