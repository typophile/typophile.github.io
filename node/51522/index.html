2008-12-10 23:40:35 • Sye

It is commonly assumed “Troll” comes from “trawling”, as in “trawling for fish”, and is the accepted Usenet lingo for people who try to gain attention and spark off hot-headed debates by posting impopular opinions.

They usually sit back for a while after their first posting, enjoying the general confusion and hubbub that follows. If the thread threatens to die down, they post again.

People who react in earnest are “feeding the troll”, although the same term can be used for those who “edge him on”, e.g., by posting firm-tongue-in-cheek answers that usually are meant to provoke, and hopefully to induce the OP (Original Poster) into fits of rage and self-defense.
