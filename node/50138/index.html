Logo type suggestion (friendly, playful, readable)

October 3, 2008 • Jasonrobb

Background: The client is an agent for study abroad language learning. The purpose of their company is to help students research and book study aboard language courses. The media is mostly web, although I suspect biz cards will follow. Message should portray a reputable and trustworthy source for honest comparisons of language schools and their courses. The typeface will be for display. There might be illustrations with it, most likely a friendly looking “agent” or “counselor” type person.

Here’s what I have in mind so far.

Looking for something friendly like Comalle…

Playful like Bello pro…

Readable (and a little more serious) like Museo…

Asking for suggestions on which typeface might do the job well. As well as some inspiration.

I greatly appreciate any feedback/questions. Thanks very much!

(First-time post, long-time fan of Typophile! =)

I think Comalle is really fun, but dangerous for an identity as it’s not going to perform any smaller than you display it here. It was not made for business cards. Bello would need some modification to make it readable, and that’s quite the task if you’re not experienced as a letterer or type designer. Museo ain’t bad.

Other ideas: Rayuela Chocolate - slightly more open counters than Comalle, lighter weights too Pedestria Grenadine FF Kosmik Co Headline Bree
