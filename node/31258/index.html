It’s one of the version of Porchez’s Ambroise http://www.typofonderie.com/alphabets/use/Ambroise

It is a rather fine font. Yet, I believe that to a large extent it misses the point of Didot fonts. Their beauty is in their dryness, some would say their haughtiness. They should look grand, not pretty. The curves introduced in the K and k don’t really work, I think: they bring the characters back to calligraphy when the greatness of Didot fonts is that they go one step further than the Bodonis by departing even more from writing. At least, this is what I like about them and I believe is their specificity.
