I hope I’m not being thick, but in case there is an insinuation that I knocked off Berthold’s font, I am pasting what I just emailed Carl Crossgrove.

I do give credit to Poppl’s Nero face as an inspiration in the font’s blurb, and Jupiter is a clean display roman type as opposed to Poppl’s original idea of unregulated rough calligraphic forms, not to mention that the character set is entirely different, and the majority of Poppl’s original shapes had to be changed because they didn’t work as well in a non-calligraphic concept. If you would like a copy of Jupiter to compare both ideas, I’ll be happy to send you one. I do not have the digital version of Nero. Whatever I garnered from Poppl’s idea was through a 1983 Mecanorma catalog.
