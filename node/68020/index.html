March 4, 2010 • Queneau

Hi all,

I’ve been working with Cycles 11 for some time now, and I really like it, but there is something that bugs me. Whenever I type the € sign I get a /dyet/ sign (D with crossbar), which is a character in Serbo-Croatian (according to Bringhurst). There is a € sign in Cycles, which I now access through the Glyphs panel in Indesign. How can I change it so I get an € sign when I type it? (I’m using the Mac PS version)

So another solution would be preffered.

Help is greatly appreciated!

There certainly is a way for you to make the changes on your own, but as you’ve certainly licensed the typeface properly: Contact Sumner Stone before doing anything else. You want him to fix a bug, strictly speaking, and I think he is going to help you. That way, he avoids customers messing around with or, rather, in his fonts, and you can be sure that the problem is dealt with professionally. As far as my experience goes, most type designers are cooperative and quick when it comes to such issues.

Give it a try and let us know if you got what you wanted.

I just went & looked. In PS Cycles 11, € is in the “currency” symbol position. Characters in Postscript Type 1 fonts are identified by name. I don’t know the key sequence for “currency” on a Mac. Unicode for “currency” is is 00A4, perhaps your layout program would go & get it if you enter that Unicode.

PostScript Type 1 fonts in OT-savvy programs do require some work. You’ll just have to type two small-cap esses for the German double-s. There is often a significant amount of lying about character names in 8-bit fonts like PS-1.

Sumner has made OT versions of his fonts:

http://www.stonetypefoundry.com/stonetypefoundry.html

Whether or not he’ll allow you to make up an OT version based on the glyphs in your PS-1 font is up to him.
