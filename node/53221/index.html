Book recommendations, please

January 7, 2009 • paul.farrell

I am a graphic designer with an interest in type which is becoming obsessive, and I’m starting to think about designing my own fonts (I mean, how hard can it be, we all know what a ‘h’ looks like).

Can anyone recommend any good books that give a through grounding in type design? I have a copy on Fontlab Studio but I’m eager to at least find out what hinting means before I absently start drawing glyphs.

Hi Paul,

I’m just starting out with type design too, so I’m sure others will offer recommendations and opinions from more “expert” perspectives; that said, I’d like to recommend two books that have been helping me a lot along the way of designing my first typeface.

Design-wise, Designing Type by Karen Cheng is quite a thorough walk-through through sans & serif upper- and lowercase letters as well as numerals. She presents the letters in different typefaces, and points out design concerns relevant to them, how to approach them and what to keep in mind. I’ve found it quite helpful (especially when complemented by looking at, and analyzing, existing typefaces oneself).

On the more technical side, I think the universally accepted best introduction to FontLab is Leslie Cabarga’s Learn FontLab Fast, which on a side note, looks quite cramped, but works very well in introducing the workflow and tools available in FontLab.

Have fun. :-)
