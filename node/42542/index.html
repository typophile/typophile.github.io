Student Typophiles and the Typophile Hierarchy

February 26, 2008 • Dan Gayle

Is it just me, or is it always easy to tell when finals roll around in the design schools? I don’t mean to point any fingers, but around the time of finals, we get a lot of questions that seem to be looking for answers to term papers, etc.

Now, as a student myself, I appreciate the amount of information presented here on Typophile, as well as the ability to pose a question to professionals in the field. But it seems that aside from James Puckett, myself, and a few other regular contributors, that we’re the only ones to do a dang Google search before posting an inquiry.

I think it highlights the importance of two aspects of Typophile:

  1. The Typowiki. It's still fairly weak as a useful compendium of knowledge. I think that if we were to regularly update it with relevant information as it is posted, we would be able to answer more frequently asked questions by pointing to the Wiki.

    How many times do we have to answer the same questions? What sans goes with this serif? Does anyone have any information on the background of xyz serif?

    Using the Wiki more effectively should free up the boards for more topical discussions that matter.

  2. Fixing the Typophile hierarchy. There was a post a while back asking for suggestions for improving Typophile. The hierarchy of the layout was brought into question, particularly the back water known as "special interest groups."

    Most of the questions we receive from students could easily fit into the Typographic Education board. Mods could bump them there if they wanted to. But why wouldn’t you? Because hardly anyone knows it exists. If that one particular board were brought forward a bit, I certainly think that it could be put to better use.

Anyway, don’t take it all that seriously. It’s just a few thoughts that I know others have suggested as well.

Comments?

I think that most of the question repetition is a result of the search function not giving notice that it doesn’t work. At this point most people using web forums have learned to search for a topic and ask away if something doesn’t pop up.

But the big problem is just that too many students can’t be bothered to just hit the library. Unless more professors hold class in the library at the start of a project, and require students to provide detailed citations, this is just going to keep getting worse, and wikiality will take over.
