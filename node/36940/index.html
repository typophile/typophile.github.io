here's my take: if you're applying for an open position, it's probably not that important. they will review your cover letter/resume, portfolio (if asked for yet) and decide who to call in for interviews. but if you're fishing, if you're going around doing informational interviews, if the position you want isn't open at the moment (or doesn't officially exist yet), then a biz card could be effective, because in that case, you wanna keep your name in the minds of the powers that be. cards go into wallets, beside computer monitors, in roledex files, etc. etc. they are another opportunity for your name to come up when the time is right.

2007-09-11 11:13:30 • nora g
As an occasional portfolio reviewer at a design agency, I don't care if the person has a business card or not. Work samples and contact info in an e-mail is enough. BUT if the person does have a card it MUST look very good. Not a conceptual wonder and I don't care much for fancy die-cut stuff and lots of colours, but impeccable typographically and maybe with a hint of character. For me it's an extension of the portfolio, and rather than show up with something half decent, don't bring it at all. The same goes for the portfolio. I don't want to see work done if it isn't something the person showing the portfolio really likes her- or himself. Show less, rather than too much. That said, a business card is practical for all reasons mentioned above, so...

The best way to get work is through networking, it's often who you know. Meet up for drinks, give people calls. If you can call them give them a call.
