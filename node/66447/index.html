2010-01-20 18:33:37 • _Palatine_
The trouble is, though, that a punctuation mark indicating sarcasm is most sorely needed with things like USENET postings, which are viewed by other people in a typeface of their choosing. The sort of sober printed material which makes it to paper... or even to Adobe Portable Document Format (.pdf) files... generally has no need for a punctuation mark of this nature, since any irony will not be so off-the-cuff as to be likely to be missed. Thus, while this product may achieve publicity, it lacks a well-defined market.
