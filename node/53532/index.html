Last summer while leaving Barnes and Noble I was stopped in my tracks by this book cover because it reminded me of pop artist James Rosenquist, who’s work I love after seeing an exhibit of his at the Guggenheim back in 2003 (he also has a piece at NOMA). The illustration is by [[http://www.jacobmagraw.com/|Jacob Magraw-Mickelson]]and the cover was designed by [[http://www.rodrigocorral.com/|Rodrigo Corral]]. I was even more excited when I picked it up and realized it was by Chuck Palahniuk who wrote Fight Club, which is also a great book with a great cover and a great movie. Which the thought of always brings me to daydream about Brad Pitt…

Rant Cover Glare-Speed of Light 2001 by James Rosenquist

Honorable mentions below: I’m scared of heights and this one gives off that Hitchcock dolly zoom Vertigo effect which makes me nauseous.

Stefan Sagmeister “Things I’ve learned in my life so far” has a die-cut box with 15 changeable bookcovers. You can see a video demonstration of the many faces of Stefan Sagmeister [[http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IvK-IDFNrHU|paired with a fun song here]].

The Harry Harrison cover is fantastic – a very appropriate application of photo to content.

[Going off-topic a bit] Rodrigo Corral has designed some really excellent covers for Chuck Palahniuk, but as for the books' content… I thought Diary was poorly written and thought-out. I’d like to read Fight Club, though, beacuse I’ve seen the movie (and liked it).
