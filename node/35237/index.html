2007-07-19 12:18:57 • ebensorkin
>REALLY toxic Yes, and believed by in traditional Chinese medicine to have curative powers. The story is pretty fascinating. The mineral from which vermilion or true Chinese Red is made, [[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cinnabar|Cinnabar]] (mercury sulfide), is believed in traditional Chinese medicine to have curative powers. It was once very rare and expensive, and perhaps because of this and its beautiful color, was prized as a medicine. The [[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dantian|dantien]], literally the 'Cinnabar field' or red field, is an area between the navel and pubic bone that is thought to be the key source of energy or 'qi' for internal organs, and is supposed to be the key to power in the martial arts and in qi gong, the traditional Chinese 'energy exercise' or meditation. [[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qin_Shi_Huang|Qin Shi Huang]], the first Emperor, who unified China, built the Great Wall, burned books and buried scholars alive, and has the famous terra cotta army guarding his grave in Xian, tried to live for ever. He regularly ate pills containing Cinnabar to achieve immortality. Modern doctors believe that, ironically, it killed him.
