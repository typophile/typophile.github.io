Need typeface for concrete...

October 17, 2008 • aluminum

I'm about to create a concrete hearth for our fireplace. The hearth will be 1' x 5'. I'm toying with the idea of putting an inset quote into the face of it using laser-cut core 10. I really like James' Recovery revival (and this would be a fun excuse to buy it): http://www.typetrust.com/fonts/font.php?id=NzQ5 So I made a (really) quick mock-up. I didn't do any letterspacing and just used the online typesetter for now. The house was built in 1927, so I think it's roughly the same era as the typeface references. It's also a nice fat face so would be easier to inset into the concrete. It is latin, so perhaps that's a bit of conflict with the era of the language. Thoughts? Suggestions? [img:sites/default/files/old-images/recovery_5980.jpg]
I wouldn't use larger caps for the initial letters. I would make the depth extremely slight - almost invisible. Anything more and you will tire of it soon enough, and start hating it, and hence the entire house. The font choice is cool, I would just worry about that "G", especially in a foreign language. I might try out Penumbra too. hhp
