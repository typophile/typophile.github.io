You had some of your answers in your questions. > many fonts used for Business’s are straight forward, blank and to the point. Nothing fancy That is because their goal is to communicate. > Where as others depend upon something to stand out and catch the eye of the public. And these uses are trying to attract attention. Normally this will only be a heading, or a few words. Many of this type of font would be difficult to read if an entire book was printed in them. The features that make them stand out usually lessen their ability to communicate. Another type of font is less flashy, but might convey other moods. Comic Sans (for those who do not hate it) is considered less formal, and provides a more casual feel. An ornate script face is more dignified and a Blackletter is even more so. The former gets a lot of use with wedding invitations, and the latter is used in funeral cars and some newspaper nameplates (New York and London Times, for instance). And when you get good at it, you will start to develop a feel for other fonts, and what uses they might be nice for.
