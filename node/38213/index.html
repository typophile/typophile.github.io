Koppa’s reply is the best answer (and a wonderfully bizarre photo of Tschichold). I would add Jost Hochuli.

“Layout” is such a broad term – do you mean books? magazines? web pages? Even if you’re talking about books, there’s huge variety, from a well-made novel (the sort of page that makes you want to read the text, and gets out of your way while you do so) to an art book or a complex integration of visuals and text.

One of the finest practitioners of page design working today is Jack Stauffacher. His sense of the relationships between elements, and how they’ll be perceived by the reader, is very highly developed. The results are subtle, not obvious.

John Berry
