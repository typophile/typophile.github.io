Design of the 20th Century

February 1, 2009 • kt

I love dada. It random but a lot of people don’t realize that it is hard to get something random to look good because the artist knows exactly what pieces he wants where. This piece is by Raoul Hausmann and is called ABCD. Dadaists used a lot of collages mixed with nonsense type. I think its a design principle that we can use today.

I chose this De Stijl piece by Piet Mondrian because I love using grids. I think it is a really important design skill to have when you can have next to nothing on the page but it makes an impact. Clean simple design is very different from dada, but I like to be inspried by all kinds of design.

Lastly, I chose the Bauhaus. This is a poster by Joost Schmidt for the Bauhaus. It has elements of constructivism (another period I enjoy) and De Stijl. I love the slanted grid and the unity of the whole piece.
