I was 16 in England Once. 1.) Inkjet has more tolerance for different stocks with a uniform quality. Laser does not. 2.) Consistency is a matter of choice I find Illustrator as the final assembly software provides the maximum opportunity, especially as it is very compatible with the PDF format which you will no doubt need. 3.) Choose either Epson or HP the two is a matter of size and price range for you. Some Epsons can print on CD/DVD's also. 4.) I do not know, but seldom recommend Online Printers. The best Customer service will be you best choice if this is your first time. 5.) Not really, you should be able to customize your showing based on the audience. Page numbers/folios are not useful for this approach. 6.) In a sturdy case or case binding. (Post binding is flexible) 7.) Helvetica is notoriously bad (difficult to read) with light weights in small sizes... it will all depend on size. This is an opportunity for you to form an opinion. Let me know what you settle on. 8.) At your age 4 times a year, as you get older you'll do it less. I promise. 9.) Prepare your files at 300 dpi for many applications, however the inkjet printers often do a good job with less. 10.) A sentence will suffice. Practice your speaking points for interviews to extend past that. 11.) These days an address is less valuable. Keep it digital. Phone and e-mail. I have strong opinions that a card is inappropriate (for a student seeking employment). However, as a freelance designer the card is the ideal format for such contact info. These are a lot of questions I hope I'm helpful. I'm trying to be nice, but I'm so old and challenged. All the best. RG

1. Inkjet. Even if you pay a good shop to do prints on a super-high-end Xante the colors will be screwed up. 2. Photoshop’s native file format. 3. No. Don’t print a portfolio on anything under $500 if you want to be taken seriously. 4. - 5. Your portfolio should be customizable so you can show more relevant work for each job, so leave page numbers out to save time when rearranging. 6. In the format preferred by the managers of each employer. Find out what they like from someone else. Personally I use a $5.99 Muji plastic photo presentation album. People who don’t like it can kiss my ass, and people who know what it is adore me for buying Muji. 7. Yes, but use Neue Helvetica to look smart. 8. However often you have new work with using. 9. 300 DPI is fine, although if you have really amazing photographs and a 1200 DPI printer it won’t hurt to print at 1200 DPI. 10. Don’t provide extensive detail unless you know that the job has very few applicants. In this economy design jobs get hundreds of applicants, and nobody has time to read long explanations. You can use longer explanations in print than online—I have had several people tell me they did not like the amount of text on my web site and so I cut back. 11. The URL of your online portfolio (you need a real URL, deviantart etc. are NOT professional!), email address, phone number, and permanent mailing address.

> Is it more economical to print 250 full colour flyers from a home pc or send them to the printers? in your opinion. Are you asking because you are thinking of sending out flyers to prospective employers? If so, I think you will be throwing your money away. That type of advertising almost never works. The best way to get a job is to go door to door, introduce yourself, and ask if there is a possibility of making an appointment to show your portfolio to the person who does the hiring. Fifty visits of this type will be more productive than 250 flyers mailed out, or dropped off at places.
