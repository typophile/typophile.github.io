2003-09-23 00:11:29 • kakaze
The problem with the question as posed by the NY Times caption, and with the subsequent perseverating over the two logos, is that Carter wasn't hired to redraw the MoMA *logotype* – he was hired to create a revised cut of Franklin Gothic (two of them, in fact, text and display). Any comparison of four letters – well three, actually – especially these particular few, will inevitably show little perceptible difference. The real differences, I suspect (and justification for the project) will only be seen when the fonts are used in mass. Then the cumulative effect of refined letterforms and spacing may show up in a difference in overall fit and color – moderate passages of text in a catalog or brochure, large display signage, etc. A logo alone is bound to be an inadequate demonstration, IMHO. – K.
