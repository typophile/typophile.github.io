2008-04-12 13:28:19 • dezcom

You can assign quick keys to anything you see in a menu.

– Open your “System Preferences” – Open “Keyboard and Mouse” – Go to “Keyboard Shortcuts” – Scroll down to “Application Keyboard Shortcuts” – Select “All Applications” – Click on the “+” – A new window appears – With “All Applications” selected, type “Stop Speaking” (It is important to spell it exactly as it is shown.) – Assign a keyboard shortcut. For instance, I used cmd-opt-shift y (Note that you should be careful not to assign a quick key combination that is already in use.) – Click “Add” – Close “System Preferences” – Restart your machine.

Ta da!!
