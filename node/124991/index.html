Typewriters had their own typefaces which were not based on fonts of type. However, from shortly after typewriters became common, type foundries offered fonts that imitated the look of typewriter typefaces. For example the American Typefounders Specimen Book and Catalog of 1923 offered fonts called Remington, Underwood, Smith Premier, Oliver, American, Repeating Reproducing and several others. See pages 521 to 532. ATF 1923 can be read online. https://archive.org/details/1923AmericanTypeFoundersSpecimenBookCatalogue Your typescript sample is of reasonably good quality. It can be compared to the samples in this and other old foundry specimen books. A project for someone with a little bit of time on their hands. Note that the online versions are fuzzy compared to the printed book. See comments at http://typophile.com/node/100507 However if you can narrow it down to something in ATF 1923 that seems to be a match, I would be happy to post a crisper scan of the font in question Note that most of these typefaces are not available in a clean digital version or even in a grunge or dirty version.
