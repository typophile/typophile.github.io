The article says that at the time, as there wasn’t Latin type available, they were ordered from other countries for print purposes. On the poster, the Turkish characters Ç (che) and Ş (sch) where a cedilla is used, are not properly drawn. Instead of a cedilla, it looks an apostrophe is attached upside down to regular C and S. That, made me think that they’ve used existing type and manipulated the existing pieces to fix a quick solution (there must be a lot of pressure from the top).

Now that I consider it, the ‘hand-drawn’ option makes sense. I was especially suspicious of the tail of the R (as the leg of K). They looked like artistic improvisations to me.
