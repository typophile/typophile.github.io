The relations between written letters and pronounced sounds are much more complicated. Here are a few examples for "th": 1. "th" in "Thai", "Thames", etc. 2. "th" in "thank", "thatch", etc. 3. "th" in "than", "that", etc. 4. "th in "cathouse" etc. All four "th" are different phonetically. It is strange, but there are English native speakers, who think that "th" in "that" and "th" in "thank" represent the same sound.
