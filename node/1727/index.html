No it isn't, Josep. A distinctive feature of Lo-Type's lc "a" is the top of the bowl that comes back down when joining the stem, whereas Bernard Condensed's curves upwards at the join. Furthermore, Lo-Type is a lot more wobbly and curly than the sample: the top arm of the lc "k" for example is quite telling: it's very rounded and the top serif is curly and pear shaped. BTW Talk about a useless site (Berthold's): small, incomplete samples and no possiblity to test drive.
