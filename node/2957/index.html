Given the poor quality of the sample, it's difficult to say for certain, but the best match I can find is Berling. (Note: Your sample is letterspaced, the MyFonts test drive I linked to is not.)

The figures have the most clues in them, and none of the other characters in Berling contradict your sample. The one character that doesn't look quite right is the T, but I think your sample has some kind of double printing/registration problem which is obscuring some of the character shapes. The double printing also leads me to think this is actually a relatively light typeface like Berling.

Everything seems to point to Berling, so that's my answer and I'm sticking with it.
