What does it take to make you forget?

February 15, 2008 • blank

Tonight it took two steak courses, one flute of rose brut, and two glasses of some oaky cabernet blend to make me forget that the headings on the menus were set in Comic Sans.

On the upside, the oak flavor was from that delicious butter oak that the French don’t sell to California.

You serious James? The last time I was out for dinner in a restaurant and the menu headings were set in Comic Sans I laughed myself silly. I laughed at the absurdity perpetrated by the worm who “designed” the menus. My dinner companions couldn’t figure what was so funny about the menus being set in Comic Sans. So I explained, “It’s bad use of a font man. That’s all you need to know.”

And I don’t even drink alcohol. Makes people act like assholes.

It helps to point out that the guy who designed the font, Vince Connare, is a great a guy and a talented designer and artist in his own right who is also an excellent photographer, and the merit of his creation for microsoft has been exaggerated out of all proportion to its humble origin by stupid ignorant computer users and jerks who like nothing more than to grumble about it and post hate rants about it on the internet.

Get over it.
