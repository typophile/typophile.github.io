2008-07-17 13:19:27 • AzizMostafa

I’d like to quote myself as saying, “You put the period/full stop inside the quotes.” When it’s within a sentence, “like this,” the comma still goes inside the quotes.

The exception is with questions. Normally, 99% of the time, the quotation mark goes inside the quotes:

• So I asked him, “Do you think it will rain today?” • Did he ask, “Have you any Grey Poupon?”

The exception is if the sentence is a question about a quote which is not a question:

• Who said “Give me liberty or give me death”?

Hope this is useful. I believe exclamation points follow the same rules as question marks.

2008-07-17 16:48:32 • charles ellertson

JT’s guidance was correct with this caveat: in American English editing, it is preferred to put the period (what we Americans call a “full stop”) inside the quotation marks, while in British English editing, the preference is to leave the full stop outside the quotation marks.

So, Nick says, “Put the full stop inside the quotation marks.”

“Unless,” he added, “you happen to live in England, in which case you can get away with them outside of the quotation marks. Consider context”.

For a more thorough treatment of this, and other important punctuational issues, do check out Eats, Shoots and Leaves by Lynne Truss.

– Nick Hladek
