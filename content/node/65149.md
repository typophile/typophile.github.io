    name: Mark Simonson

  body: "A glyph may only be used in either \r\n\r\n- a single kerning class that
    is used for both left and right sides of a kerning pair, or \r\n- in two classes,
    one that is used for the left and one that is used for the right. \r\n\r\nThe
    errors you're getting indicate that you have the same glyphs in more than one
    class and that they conflict (that is, the classes are for the same side of a
    kerning pair).\r\n\r\nIt'd be nice if FontLab would warn you when add the glyphs
    to a kerning class rather than when you try to generate the font, but it doesn't,
    so you just have to be careful. \r\n\r\nThere is also a \"Clean Up Classes\" command
    in the Classes panel, which should help with your problem, but it can't read your
    mind and my not give the kerning classes you want."
