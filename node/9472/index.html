I agree with Patty. It doesn't look like anything familiar to me, and letters like that W seem like you would recall them. It's more likely that it was done completely by hand than that someone 'played' with a font.

If you have some calligraphic skill, you might make your own W, b and g, which seems like the most atypical letters in this sample. However, I presume your purpose is not to re-create this, but to identify the typeface to use for other purposes.

One that I know with at least a somewhat similar W is P22's Roanoke Script, and it even tries to reflect a style roughly from the Colonial era (or earlier).

You could look in Part 10 of the Script Font ID Guide for other Antique Handwriting fonts. Not sure what qualities you are looking for, but at least I know these are actually fonts.

2005-03-01 15:59:44 • bowfinpw
There are a few other earmarks indicating this is custom lettering and not a font:

- The wide looping descender on the g (which would not work in a font after another character with a descender).

- A similar case with the b. Both sides would collide with other tall characters.

- The b and u connect differently than other pairs.

- The W would be very awkward with short words or words that start with Wh (e.g., Why).
