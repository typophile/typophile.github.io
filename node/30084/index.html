December 15, 2006 • Dan Gayle

Everyone remember my design teacher that said you should almost never use drop caps because they’re a waste of time?

Well, he’s at it again.

Today he said that we should never use justified text because it causes too many rivers. Period. Oh, and nobody did justifed type until this century.

When I countered that Gutenberg used justifed text, he kinda brushed me off saying, “yeah, but he had lots of kerning.”

AAAAAAAAAARRRRRRRRRRRGGGGGGGGGGGGHHHHHHHHHHHHHH!!!!!!!!!!!

I think I’m better off never taking a class from him again. Yeck.

Oh, and never use serif type for the web

AAAAAAAAAARRRRRRRRRRRGGGGGGGGGGGGHHHHHHHHHHHHHH!!!!!!!!!!!

Did you ask him if he DIDN’T have lots of kerning? He’s obviously a lousy typographer and he’s to ignorant to learn the software. I can’t believe that. That is rediculous.

He’s never heard of H & J’s? He’s never seen a gorgeous book from Hartley and Marks? Or Hyphen? Or David R. Godine? Does he use Bringhursts Elements? Has he ever seen a book? Has he ever seen a piece of classical literature?

I feel brilliant compared to this guy. Well, your homework should be easy right. Just wipe with a couple pieces of paper, bind them together and put them on his desk, he’ll probably praise you as a genius.

This just makes me angry. I’ve had a few teachers that talk like they know about things they actually dont, but this guy takes the cake.

If you’d be so kind to your classmates and recommend to them, James Felici’s [[Complete Manual of Typography]] and regard anything he says with a grain of salt.

*When you’re done with him for good, file a formal complaint with the school, and include TP threads to back you up. * You could learn more from that book in a week.

2006-12-16 10:03:03 • poms

Our class was a Dreamweaver class. And I’m sorry, WYSIWYG doesn’t mean jack when you’re used to InDesign or even Pagemaker.

I was having the hardest time trying to figure out all of the tables and all the extra code, and HOW DO I GET IT TO DO WHAT I WANT IT TO DO. Is that too much to ask for a supposedly WYSIWYG program?

That’s why I went and got the book, which not only tells you what to do, it tells you WHY you need to do it. Purely semantic mark-up using XHTML Strict and CSS. Oh, and it teaches you to use TextEdit for all of your coding.

It makes more sense for me as a designer, honestly. I can rough out the entire page by hand in picas like any other design, then the coding is purely spec’ing everything out in ems. It’s like what I suppose designers had to do way back in the day when they didn’t do their own typesetting.

It allows me to focus on what I want to do, rather than what I can do. And, it means that my design will be better because I don’t start off on the computer.
