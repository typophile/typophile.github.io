2003-07-02 18:54:50 • taran80
I think it would be far more readable if you didn't marry the left half of the n into the a. The ambigram/inversion thing can very definitely work for this word. Try using the middle of the right stroke of the n as your centre of rotation, curving left at the top as normal, and curving into the t at the bottom, something like a cursive join. Then the left stroke of the n can invert to the t. A half serif on the end and a slightly descending stroke will give you your t.
