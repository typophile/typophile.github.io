Hi I was wondering if anyone knew what font this is. I’m even more interested in what category this typeface would fall into? I’ve tried searching for Victorian era fonts and flourish fonts with no avail. Any help would be greatly appreciated. Thanks -D

The fishtail terminations on the letters of this monogram mean that they are in the Tuscan category. The letters also have medial spurs. So they’re in the medial Tuscan sub category. You might find something you like at http://www.myfonts.com/search/tuscan/ Don

I agree Ryuk that Baelon Monogram is promising. It has medial spurs as well as Tuscan terminations with matching orientations to the image specimen. Here is the Baelon Monogram sG as generated at Myfonts and its unassembled S Compress this S for a closer match. The only aspects that are completely different are the absence of a chin on G, the location of the medial spurs on the lhs and rhs of S rather than on the diagonal stroke, and a plain rather than faceted treatment of the letters.
