2007-12-06 11:21:56 • drduckling

well I’m a designer in my second year of design and my first at this magazine. Its a larger company (Natmags) than when I first started and so there are more benefits. My experience isn’t very broad, but it seems the larger the company, the more perks/policies you get. I think they offer me life insurance and a pension, but we’ve got the NHS for health problems so no need for health insurance (not entirely true, the NHS does suck so it depends what quality of healthcare you want).

I’m on about 21k a year which does me ok. But then I’m only a designer with limited experience.

Zone 1 will cost ya. But then you’ll save on travel if you are within walking distance (and willing to do so) of your work. Oyster travelcards are not cheap to say the least (there are less polite words I could use to describe the London transport situation but this is a family restaurant…)

I live at the edge of zone 2, in North Acton and it only takes me about half an hour to get to work at Oxford Circus on the central line.

Hope I’ve been of some help.
