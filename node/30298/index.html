Hi all,

Just received my login details for Typophile, so now I can finally post a question that' s been burning my lips for some time now…

I found this typeface in a novel ( Con Sordino by Knut Hamsun, translated into Dutch ) in my father' s attic. Since I loved it instantly ( the typeface that is ) I started looking for it everywhere, but couldn' t find anything similar at first. The printers - Zuid-Hollandse Boek en Handelsdrukkerij - seems to be out of business, so no luck there.

However, a search on certain designers of that period - I assumed it was a late 19th- early 20th century Typeface - came up with a pretty similar type, namely Helga Antiqua in a pdf file about the Stempel Foundry ( downloadable here: http://www.schriftenservice-d-stempel.de/ChronikStempel.pdf , see page 5 ) In the ( pdf- ) text, it says " 1911 - Von Professor F. W. Kleukens wird die Helga-Antiqua herausgegeben… " and the name " Helga-Antiqua " is printed alongside. To me, it seems to be a very close match …

Now here' s my question(s) :

  • IS it indeed Helga Antiqua ?
  • Who designed it ? Was it Kleuskens or is it an older typeface that he " revived " - maybe with a new name ?
  • Who owns the design today ?
  • I couldn' t find any digital version of it . Does any of you know about one ?

As it is a nice and very readable Typeface, I am thinking of creating something similar in digital form. What about the legal issues ?

Thanks to all for looking into this, Regards,

Peter

I think you have identified it correctly. The Jaspert ‘Encyclopaedia of Type Faces’ shows Helga and says it is also known as Olga. It credits F. W. Kleukens as the designer, and gives ‘Stempel, 1912’ as the original foundry, but also lists Enschedé, which is Dutch.

Unfortunately the type sample in the book is not the complete typeface, but it does match the letters in your sample.

2007-01-04 15:26:20 • bowfinpw

Just for an interesting comparison, have a look at Letraset Carlton, which is an adaptation of Ehmke Antiqua designed in 1908. In some ways Helga Antiqua resembles what might have happened if one decided to use Ehmke as the basis for a text typeface. The ‘g’ is the most glaring difference that jumps out.

Another comparison is another face designed by Kleukens for Bauer, around 1900-1908, originally known as Kleukens Antiqua, but digitized by Scangraphic as Trieste. Notice it’s similarities with Helga, including the G and g resemblances.
