This is a dumb question, probably, but one that trips me up a lot. How is the Cassandre font 'Peignot' pronounced? I've always thought is was "pen-wa," but several French-speaking students of mine insist it's "peg-no." My French ain't so hot. Can anyone help? Chuck
Basically, "penYOH" (silent h). Your "penWAH" would be a pronunciation of Penoit (like Mandelbrot's given name but with a "P"). As for "pegNOH", it's somewhat un-French, but might be written as "Peguenot" (in which case the [second] "e" would be distinctly pronounced though). hhp
