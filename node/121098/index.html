2014-11-13 13:17:46 • Nick Cooke
More importantly can you read it, and does it look OK to you?
For the conventional lower-case patterns take a close look at the script fonts offered by ParaType). Segue Script (© Microsoft) is not bad either, although, I suspect, too plain for your liking. ![](/images/old-images/Segoe_Script_6561.gif) However, all of them, except two — Propisi and English 157 — feature calligraphic (not vernacular) or non-cursive, print-style capitals, which is something you don’t want.

For the generic, standard upper-case cursive forms check out Propisi, English 157, and—surprise—ITC True Grit. They all follow the same basic pattern.

Now, you said earlier, that the purpose of your font is to be used in advertisement. Is it about one word or few, or a whole paragraph? If it’s about the last one, than I have to say that it is quite difficult to read a passage set with your font. It is like a mature hand is learning to write… Which actually, is you, trying to write in Russian. And because its not your native language, of course, it is more difficult than for a native one. Maxim’s script is flowing and the letters do not disturb my brain from reading the paragraphs.

Referring to your font:

  1. What if your characters would be designed with the same or almost the same x-height? Maybe, the reading would be easier than. It depends on your final goal: what you are trying to achieve?
  2. When the height is different between the left and right part of the same character, the reading becomes even more difficult. Take a look at the following 2 words, узнал +улыбку, how the characters are displayed. But if you could manage a similar height of characters (nr.1), maybe nr. 2 could be avoided… Again, it depends on how many words will be displayed on the advertisement. And by the way, what “character” would you like to give to your font? Similar font was designed by Parachute, http://www.parachute.gr/typefaces/allfonts/libera-pro#Cyrillic check all 3 languages: Russian, Greek, Latin. You see, the height of letters is different, and even the hight of left+right parts of characters varies sometimes. Of course, you are not going to read whole passages set with this font, but in Greece it became extremely popular in ad sector. It is widely used for packages. Even, during local elections, the banners were displayed with Libera.
