These come from a 1950’s argentinian melodrama. The most difficult one is the one in which is “Luis César” is written.

Thank you so much for your help!

I searched all the script fonts that might have been like this which I have collected for my Guide and really found nothing that had its blend of casual and formal letters. The most similar (to me) was Jason Walcott’s [[http://www.veer.com/products/typedetail.aspx?image=JBT0000048#flont|Shirley Script]], and then Mark Simonson’s [[http://www.veer.com/products/typedetail.aspx?image=UMT0000346#specimen|Lakeside]], which is a bit more monotone in its stroke. Neither is very close, but at least they have the feel of this era, and they use somewhat similar letterforms. I was governed most by the L and C initials, and so the D for “Direccion” is not too similar. That D style doesn’t seem to go with most scripts that have a similar L & C.

Mariano,

Welcome to Typophile, I’m an old timer and also very new to this blog… I hope you’ll enjoy it just as much as I do!

FYI: All movie posters and titles in Argentina during this period and before the early 1960’s, they were all hand-lettered. My former father-in-law, Alfredo Faistman was one of the busiest hand-lettering artist working in downtown Buenos Aires ad agency during the late 40’s and early 60’s. Alfredo worked mainly in movie titles and posters – I considered him a genius w/ the brush and pen. I wouldn’t be surprised if this is one of his works. I can contact his living wife and ask her if that’s the case, she might remember.

Alfredo was my inspiration during my early graphic design career, and for the love I have for typography. I met him in NY in the early 60’s while dating his daughter. He used to work for Norcross then, a very famous greeting card company based in NY (similar to the Hallmark company). Alfredo was hand-lettering greeting cards until the early 1970s. That’s when the greeting card industry starting to switch to photo-lettering.

When I get more information about your movie title… I’ll pass that information to you.
