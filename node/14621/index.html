most common repeating character sets

August 16, 2005 • dux

Having just discovered that making random characters appear within a range of alternates isn’t supported by typesetting software, I’ve now resigned myself to making a large set of contextual alternates, and faking it.

Does anyone know if there is any common knowledge of the most common repeating sets of two characters in the english language?

thanks!

  • Most common two letter combinations - TH, HE, AN, IN, ER, RE, ES, ON, TI, AT
  • Most common three letter combinations - THE, AND, HAT, ENT, ION, FOR, TIO, HAS, TIS
  • Most common doubled letters - LL, TT, SS, EE, PP, OO, RR, FF, CC, DD, NN
  • Most common two letter combinations that show up as reversals of themselves - ER RE, ES SE, AN NA, ON NO, TI IT, EN NE, TO OT, ED DE

…or so the folks who make cryptograms say.
