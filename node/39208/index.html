Garamond No. 3 is one of the Jannon “Garamonds,” based on type cut by the Huguenot printer Jean Jannon, who was thrown in the slammer for printing Protestant tracts. His type was impounded, too. When they released the fonts after a number of years, some French flatfoot said “It’s Garamond.” He was wrong.

The other fonts you mention are not “Jannon Garamonds.” Monotype Garamond is also based on Jannon’s fonts, and in many ways, is a better one.

2007-11-19 02:54:46 • charles ellertson
Buy Garamond 3 (you might only need the roman) and bill the client. They can’t expect you to have every Garamond on the planet. Or print out the text in the Garamonds you have and compare it with the same text set in Garamond 3 at MyFonts or other retailer’s type-tester. One of them may be close enough. Depends partly on what letters are in the text. But I can echo Paul: don’t use ITC Garamond.
