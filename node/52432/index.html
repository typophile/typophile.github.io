2008-12-09 01:12:53 • Mark Simonson

First I rub it with me paws, then I draw a couple of elipses and, using the larger one as the outside curve and the smaller one as the counter, manipulate the nodes at extrema until I get approximately the inside and outside shapes I want.

Mostly it’s about what I want, and when it looks rite. Measuring things is for the ducks. Only ducks measure things. I find that gets in the way of what I want. Art is what the eye wants to see. If I measured the width of the vertical stroke of the o and found out it was actually four units narrower than the mean vertical stroke width of the stem letters but the o looks rite anyway, the ruler is going to lose and I won’t be using it as a tool again very soon.

When it looks rite.

Sometimes I add more nodes in between the extrema nodes and mess around with those for a while, steadily working towards what I want. Usually I’m drawing o after I’m happy with h n u m i l a e and maybe a few others in the lower case, so I’m matching o to those guys.

When I’ve got the o I want I place nodes at extrema and remove the nodes not at extrema if the nodes at extrema will hold the desired shape(s). Sometimes the shape(s) require a few extra nodes to be left in place.
