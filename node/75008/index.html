Ligature for &c (etc)

October 8, 2010 • Seth Teeters

& (the ampersand) is a ligature for 'et', latin for 'and'. Although archaic you can use '&c' the same as 'etc' as a shortened 'Et cetera'. & (the ampersand) being a ligature for 'et', I am curious to whether there are any historical examples of ligatures being designed for 'etc'. I created an example of what a ligature for 'etc' could look like: http://bit.ly/cKOH8v Does anyone know of any actual historical examples?
