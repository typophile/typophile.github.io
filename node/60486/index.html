Table of contents: break the grid?

July 31, 2009 • omeld

How do you feel about breaking the baseline grid in a more complex table of contents?

I’ve come across such a situation and have tried different solutions, yet it always seems to work best when I add some diversity and am in turn forced to break the grid. Since tables of contents often require more detailed control than the main text, I presume it’s OK to design them a bit differently while still following the main established design principles.

There’s a very interesting - and helpful - article by John D. Berry about managing table of contents; hope it helps:

http://www.creativepro.com/article/dot-font-tables-of-contents

2009-07-31 13:34:52 • riccard0

There’s nothing at all wrong with breaking the grid on a TOC. I do it all the time. The earlier suggestion about using a grid that is half the line advance of the text is a good place to start.

I’d also suggest that you pull the column of page numbers to the left so there is about 1.5 to 2 ems only from the end of the longest line to the page numbers. That will lessen the overall gap between titles and page numbers (Berry mentions this as not always very good looking; he’s right].
