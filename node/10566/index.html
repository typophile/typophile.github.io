2005-04-27 18:34:01 • hoefler
They are both gorgeous, IMHO, and I will have to keep looking at them both. I'll willingly ponder aloud the idea that Mercury and Farnham could be used together. Perhaps using Farnham for the display purposes and Mercury for other? While they are both, IMHO, serious versions of the style, I can see that the H&FJ version maintains a more serious stance, while the Farnham appears to be a little more effervescent. I wouldn't say which is best. Too many different things. Mercury offers a serious multiple-use typeface with enough style–along the lines of Requiem for instance–which can perform beyond the printed page. Farnham on the other hand does have some traits of a workhorse, but also has a willingness to show-off. I do like the serifs a lot on Farnham. I also love that it has swash characters but wonder why they only offer a Display Swash in regular. Well, I'm going to see if I can't find downloadable PDFs and keep staring, I mean of course after I work.
