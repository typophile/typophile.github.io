2006-05-24 14:18:24 • Mark Simonson

I’m not going to favour one or the other, but as Adobe has plenty of boosters, here are a couple of points pro Quark 7, which I think does a good job of supporting OpenType.

  1. With Adobe, there is no way of knowing, when you hit the “small caps” button, whether you will get true or faux; with Quark, there is a “small cap” button on both the faux menu and the OpenType menu.

  2. Also, Quark implements the OpenType figures features correctly, whereas Adobe does not. (Adobe’s method only works for oldstyle fonts that have the four main figure classes.)

  3. Quark’s glyph palette lets you scroll through your font library, very useful to see which fonts have which OpenType features.

With Adobe, I can upgrade, purchase new licenses, and download my serial numbers in a matter of minutes. To me, there is no contest.
