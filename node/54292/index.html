Whoever Reads Bourgeois Newspapers Becomes Blind and Deaf: Away with These Stultifying Bandages! Photomontage by Heartfield, 1932.

One of my favorite design movements is the Modern Art movement. I especially love the posters of John Heartfield. His works are strong political statements, but what I like best about them is how beautifully executed each of them are. I think this poster is declaring a strong, still relevant statement about people blindly following mass media. But even if the political statement is removed, I think the poster is still beautiful.

Kassama Corn Flour by Beggarstaffs, 1894.

Coming from an advertising background, I like the Pictorial Modernism movement, as it was here that the first ads were created. I especially like the designs of Beggarstaffs. Their use of clean layouts and eye-catching colors resulted in ads that are still as fresh today as they were a century ago.

Of course, I am also mad for Digital Revolution design. I particularly like David Carson’s innovation in design. I think his design is truly ground-breaking. I like to look at his work for inspiration because he does things with type I’ve never dreamed of.
