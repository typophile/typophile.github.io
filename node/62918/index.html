Hey Thomas,

My jaw was on the floor. No one had come up with [[http://www.fontshop.com/fonts/singles/richard_beatty/goudy_mediaeval/|Goudy Mediaeval]], in one of its lesser revivals, as a better match than “Magic Cards” for the title font. Even though, ostensibly, that is what the card designers used here. But, of course, you wanted a match for the numerals, so I didn’t chime in before you wisely tracked that one down.

Rest assured – I finally found those for you.

Before I get to that, though, I want to offer that the distinctive numerals in the set we had to work with are the 0 and the 3. All the others – including the 5, albeit in condensed form and with a downward-pointing lower terminal – to a great degree emulate the structure of any [[http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/urw/old-english/|Old English]] font and its dozens of freely available knockoffs and derivatives who’s creators had no qualms about ripping off numerals. Just sayin’.

Without further ado: You’ll be hard pressed to find a more exact match than [[http://www.sulucas.net/fonts.htm|Boere Tudor]]. Unless, of course, you can track down the designer of “the first Afrikaans family magazine, Die Brandwag” that inspired Su Lucas to create it!

~ William Leverette
