Cold Type Once Meant A Hot Meal

January 11, 2008 • Enjah

I am a retired cold-type typesetter. As hot type went the way of the dinosaurs, women like me entered the workplace, with our manual dexterity and lower wages, along with our lack of upper-body strength, once really a necessity for a typesetter. Now designers do their own typesetting and I stay home and blog. I am currently watching the movie "Helvetica". While I am not a big fan of the font, it is certainly ubiquitous! There are so many knockoffs it amazes me. Personally, I really love Gill Sans, but who even thinks of that these days?
I've had the privilege to work in a letterpress shop (perhaps at an age where I didn't FULLY appreciate it) but it certainly is an art form that seems to be on a consistent level of preservation at the moment. Which is nice to see. I recall my first internship at 3M in the mid-90s. At the time, they still had a typesetting department. In my 4 months there, however, it was finally disbanded. The end of an era.
