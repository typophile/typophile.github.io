Hello, If your only going for a single copy your choices are limited (unless you fancy wasting a lot of money). You are going to need to get it printed digitally, google printers in your area as it’s always much better to use a local printer than an online one.

As for paper, you need to choose, do you want a matt finish to the poster or a glossy one or maybe a rough finish? The inks will be set by the print company you use, you can use special inks (ie spot inks) but for a single poster this would be expensive.

Once you have found your printer they will usually tell you how they want your file set up (if they don’t look for anther printer).

Here in the UK you can get a singe A1 poster digitally printed for around £9 or an A3 print for £6. If you want very high quality you may be better going to a Photography Lab and using their printers but the price will increase but he quality will (hopefully) be much greater as well.

Most cities have a wide range of digital printing shops that can print posters, banners, etc. in single-copy. Places like Kinko’s. Look in your phone book under Printers and under Signs. It may not be cheap, and you may have a limited range of papers.

On stuff like this I’d not be too interested in dealing remotely with e-commerce companies. I want to be able to go to the shop, so I can see a proof, and advice on file prep. Doing business via the web may have some advantages, but in this instance I’d go for face-to-face.
