Please Critique Logo

February 21, 2007 • EmmaKuhl

This logo is for a company that builds energy efficient homes/or green homes. Let me know what you think or if you would have any suggestions.
  1. The placement of the word “the” and also the tagline both seem arbitrary and look out of place in some of your logos.
  2. I would stray away from putting the graphic element around or behind the “e” (the house reads as “the e nergy pros to me). There are better ways to tie the graphic part of the logo in (i.e with color or placement that is not on or under the type)
  3. Maybe I’m old school but I would stick to using solid colors.

It’s a good start, but I would work more on developing the graphic and keeping the type simple.

Emma,

You have chosen the font Nueva that is best used as a stand-alone display font. It distracts from any illustration because it is so intricate and full of life. I wouldn’t use fake small caps like you did in logo3. Here are some fonts I would consider purchasing for this job:

Lisboa http://www.veer.com/products/typedetail.aspx?image=FOT0000080

Cronos http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/cronos/

Logo 4 is the best of the three so far. I like the friendliness of it. I would work on the plant illustration and make it more interesting. Do research and look at some plants on stock photography websites like corbis or veer. Keep the illustration 1-color so it could be cut out of vinyl.

I look forward to seeing what you come up with next.

Of the three ideas you’ve posted so far, I think # 4 works best. But I wouldn’t use Nueva (I think Tom’s comment is right on the mark) and I would try to simplify the whole thing a little. My suggestion is that you keep “unity” in mind when working on subsequent drafts: all the different elements (image + the + energy pros + tag line, or whatever combination you choose) need to be connected and feel like a visual unit.

It would be interesting to learn a little more about your project: what image exactly your client wants to project; the core values of the “Energy Pros” brand (if they’ve gone so far as to define their market positioning, which a surprising number of clients don’t seem to give much thought to before trying to create or change their visual identity); etc.
