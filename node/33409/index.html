Dance Company Logo

April 30, 2007 • andyw

Friends, Hello! I'm the managing director of a small non-profit arts organization that performs, teaches, and hosts dance and movement events. We're the only modern dance studio in Indiana, where there is a small (at best) dance community. Although we've been around for 28 years, we are frequently being compared to the local ballet school; a more classical dance and has been around for 50 years. A few years ago, (before I worked here) the organization got a grant to improve its corporate branding. They bought a font (DaVinci) and created a logo (attached). I came along, with a minimal graphic design background, and I don't like the logo. It seemed to open, to unsubstantial. So I modified it, inversed the color, and set it inside a circle (also attached.) The 30th anniversary is coming up soon, and I'd really like to present them with a modified, better logo that isn't drastically different that what is here. I'd like it to show a sense of movement, of flow. I'd like it to be free-form without being wacky and all over the place. Basically, I want to keep a sense of modern dance within the logo, but I want something that will still look smart on a letterhead. I would love any advice you have to offer that might lead me on the right path. Thank you very much!
The line quality is rough/autotrace looking, and you have too many ideas. The spirals and chicklets are not helping. A good test for a mark is to reduce it to around 1/4" in height and fax it and see if it still reads. I would simplify the ideas and look to available type that is going to be more legible. Maybe you don't even need the two figures. Maybe type only, or just a gesture that says movement. Maybe that gesture could be part of the type.

Hi Andrew. Maybe you should zoom into the figures and take a part of a body that keeps the essence/movement [moving hand/torso/muscle structure...], or take a piece of clothing they could be wearing [twisted shirt/tie/a dress suggesting the movement of the body underneeth...] Something that has a place in the imege already but could be isolated and refined, croped in and made more powerful. [?] Maybe take a photo of two people performing this movement and take a detail and work with it... you could even have an evolving mark that zooms closer and ends up with an abstract shape [body part/clothing/hair...] that conveys the same message as this one does holistically. I find that a photograph or something tangible is a good starting point when designing anything, even type. Then you can usually give it a physical quality: weight, texture, movement. Then a simple shape can be attributed to a space or material or somehow suggest a material quality, be it finely chipped out of steel [Walbaum] or set in huge concrete blocks [Akzidenz Grotesk figures]. Or something like the Johnnie Walker logo.
