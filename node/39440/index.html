Tom, for an Indian those are two very different things! In fact Bengali, doesn't even use Devanagari. Are you designing something that looks broadly Indian to a western audience, or is it for an Indian audience? If it's the first, you might try making your forms slightly more calligraphic. Also the letters should perhaps not be so geometric and angular. A device that you can try is to knock off the vertical sroke of the 'R' which would make it resemble the consonant 'Ra' in Devanagari. I'd probably use a two-storeyed lowercase 'a' which also may add a faux devanagari quality. (And of course finally render it in Pink since they say "Pink is the Navy Blue of India ;) On the other hand if it's being designed for Indians, you'll need to have far more focussed references than now.
