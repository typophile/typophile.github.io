2006-04-29 16:25:16 • James Gareth

I’m a longtime Mac user but I haven’t taken the plunge switching to the Intel Macs yet. The 17" MacBook Pro does look very nice however. Virtualization technology (such as Parallels) that allows you to run Windows apps with decent performance also makes the Intel Macs quite attractive.

If you are considering having a laptop as your only machine, I strongly encourage you to think carefully about backups and the AppleCare extended warranty.

Although the machines are pretty expensive, after a while the data on the machine will be more valuable than the machine itself. If the laptop is lost or stolen, or if you need to send it in for repairs and they wipe the disk, you’ll be toast unless you have good backups. You can get a coupla hundred G external FireWire disk for $150-$300 depending on speed and capacity.

You should also consider the AppleCare extended warranty, which costs $349. If you carry the laptop everywhere it’ll be susceptible to damage from being jostled around, connectors being plugged and unplugged (well maybe not the cool new magnetic power plug), being dropped, having beverages spilt into the keyboard, etc. Any of these can be fatal. I’ve fortunately never had to use AppleCare. However, there are many Mac users at my workplace and several report excellent repair service under AppleCare.

These do add significantly to the cost of the machine. I recommend you factor backups and AppleCare into the cost of any new machine that you buy. Whatever you decide, happy Mac’ing! I’m sure you’ll enjoy it.

I backup my Mac portable to a cheap HD in a Firewire enclosure. I think the street value to my 80GB drive, with enclosure, is about $100. I use the free utility Carbon Copy Cloner to ‘clone’ my whole laptop drive onto a 30GB partition I’ve set up. This is something that an average user can figure out, no deep technical knowledge necessary. If my drive is stolen, destroyed, etc. I’ll just get a new one and clone my data over. I try to do this every month or so. I usually set it to clone before I go to bed, it takes 30min or so…. I recommend it.

Also, I don’t buy Applecare. I don’t necessarily think it’s a bad idea, it’s just that its just that I’m not sure that I’ll get my money out of it. Apple products already have a warranty.
