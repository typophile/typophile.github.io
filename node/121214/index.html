Fair pricing for a font?

November 11, 2014 • vr4jen

Hi all, I'm a font making noob, if you will, and I've designed a font which I sell on my website for only $10 (personal or commercial use). A very large corporation has contacted me with interest in licensing the font from me. I sell primarily to individual users so I'm at a loss for how to structure the pricing. My EULA specifies that there must be a separate license purchased for each individual user but that wouldn't work for such a large company. They said they own several fonts from larger foundries and they license it to them for a flat fee based on their usage. I know it's a personal decision but I don't want to grossly over or undercharge. Is there an average industry standard or at least a ballpark that is fair? Thanks in advance for any insight.
Corporate enterprise licenses, depending on ultimate use, generally go for somewhere between $9000 and $20,000 per font or more. Questions to ask: Desktop only? Web fonts" Applications? Commercial embedding? The more answers yes to these questions the higher the license fee. Good luck noob.
