2005-10-03 15:47:31 • winglets747
“But it’s too 70s.” Exactly. If you really want flares with that, you might consider a couple of the old ITC faces - ITC Novarese or ITC Newtext are both flared this way, but neither is unique, and the geometry of Variable makes this pairing a bit problematic to harmonise. Alternatively, if the types are not being used in close proximity on the page, you could go for a complete structural contrast in the headlines, captions etc. and try the grandaddy of flare serif types - Berthold Wolpe’s Albertus, which is also available in an italic and a light weight these days. Having said all that, I much prefer Tim’s idea of contrasting Variable with an Egyptian or Slabserif.
