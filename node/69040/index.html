Two fonts: both display, one rounded sans, the other grunge

April 2, 2010 • Blue Velvet

Hello all, a two for one special Easter deal!

I’ve been tasked with creating a sub-brand based on the organisation’s existing logotype. Unfortunately, the style guide isn’t much help, nor can I contact the original designer. I wondered if someone could please help me narrow down what these are?

There are two faces:

  1. The word ‘Plan’. A heavy rounded sans with asymmetrical counters. At first, I thought the designer had taken something like VAG or Futura Rounded into Illustrator and messed with the strokes, thicks and thins, but the lowercase ‘a’ doesn’t fit.

  2. The tagline ‘Be a part of it.’ Distressed, but both the lowercase ‘a’s have the same pattern of flecks around them. I don’t have a larger sample with more letterforms in it.

Any ideas would be much appreciated. Thanks in advance.

All of this belongs to the corporate design of Plan International, a British charity. The first sample is just the logotype of the organisation; I doubt that there are more letters than just these four. The tagline is set in ‘Plan Storytelling’, a bespoke typeface created — as far as I know — by Adobe. To learn more about it, you probably have to contact Plan.
