You haven’t told us what operating system you’re talking about but I assume you’re talking about Mac OS X.

Font Agent Pro has a few design flaws. One of them is that it always organizes the fonts on the hard drive its own way rather than having an option to leave the user’s original arrangements. If it weren’t for that, the application would be pretty good. The auto-activation is reliable.

I personally find the other two, Font Reserve and Suitcase, rather useless.

MasterJuggler is pretty useless under OS X and that’s a real shame. I used it under 6 till 9 and it was perfect (miles ahead of SuitCase). Too bad Alsoft can’t get their act together and build a solid OS X one.

For what it’s worth: I use FontAgent Pro and that’s pretty solid. Autoactivation works (once you have weeded out all superfluous and defective fonts). I also tried SuitCase for OS X – selling that piece of j… should be considered a crime. The interface of Font Reserve is so fiendish, I couldn’t stand it and wiped it of all my computers.

For most users with font collections in the hundreds (max) Apple’s own FontBook is perfectly feasible. The Tiger version is okay (earlier ones sucked).
