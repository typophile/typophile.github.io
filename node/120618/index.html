[[http://notetoself.typepad.com/note_to_self/2011/07/holiday.html|Cover lettering for Holiday Magazine]] October 1959 issue so probably never released as a font. Best would have been compressed or more contracted versions of [[http://commercialtype.com/typefaces/austin|Austin]] Hairline, [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/fontbureau/miller-banner|Miller Banner]] or [[http://www.typography.com/fonts/chronicle-display/overview|Chronicle Display]] Compressed. As digital alternative but far less elegant or contrasted: [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/typofonderie/ambroise-std|Ambroise]] Firmin, [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/mti/onyx-mt|Onyx]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/urw/torino|Torino]]/[[http://www.fonts.com/font/monotype/torino/condensed|Torino Condensed]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/ortizlopez/ol-roman-compressed|Roman Compressed]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/bagraphics/fashion-didot|Fashion Didot]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/redrooster/bodoni-rr-campanile|Bodoni Campanile]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/fontbureau/moderno|Moderno]] Compressed, [[http://www.myfonts.com/search/bordeaux|Bordeaux]], [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/urw/arsis|Arsis]]
