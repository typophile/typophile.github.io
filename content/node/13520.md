body: "<strong>[[Indices]] : [[Designers]] : Matthew Carter</strong>\r\n\r\nBorn:
  [[United Kingdom]], 1937.\r\n\r\nSon of [[Harry Carter]], Royal Designer for Industry,
  contemporary British type designer and ultimate craftsman, trained as a punchcutter
  at [[Enschede|Ensched\xE9]] by [[Paul Raedisch|Paul R\xE4disch]], responsible for
  Crosfield\u2019s typographic program in the early 1960s, [[Mergenthaler Linotype]]\u2019s
  house designer 1965\u20131981.\r\n\r\nCarter is a prolific designer who has produced
  many successful and significant faces in his career such as [[Olympian]], [[Bell
  Centennial]], [[Galliard]], [[Charter]] and [[Miller]] (in chronological order).\r\n<!--break-->\r\nHe
  has in recent years designed [[Verdana]], [[Tahoma]] and [[Georgia]] for [[Microsoft]];
  these fonts are tuned to be extremely legible even at very small sizes on the screen.\r\n\r\nCarter
  co-founded [[Bitstream]] with Mike Parker in 1981. In 1991 he left Bitstream to
  form [[carterandcone|Carter & Cone]] with [[Cherie Cone]].\r\n\r\nCarter is a Royal
  Designer for Industry, a member of AGI, chairman of the type designers' committee
  of [[ATypI]], and a Senior Critic on [[Yale]]'s Graphic Design faculty. He has received
  the [[Frederic W. Goudy]] Award for outstanding contribution to the printing industry,
  the Middleton Award from the American Center for Design, a Chrysler Award for Innovation
  in Design, and medals from the [[AIGA]] and the [[Type Directors Club]]. He holds
  the honorary degree of Doctor of Fine Arts.\r\n\r\n<strong>Additional Articles:</strong>\r\n<a
  href=\"http://designmuseum.org/design/matthew-carter\">British Council Design Museum:
  Matthew Carter</a>"
