Labels on frames were hand-drawn/hand-painted in that era (late 19th/very early 20th century).

Best match = Vogue (just widen the letter spacing on RENOIR): http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/ephemera/vogue/

Treasure trove has a hand-made feel, though much looser than your sample: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/comicraft/treasure-trove/

Same goes for Pelican and Antiquarian: http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/adobe/pelican/ http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/3ip/antiquarian/

If your after something more polished, the following may do:

http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/typetanic-fonts/columbia-titling/medium/ http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/emigre/mrs-eaves-ot/ot-bold/ http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/fontfont/ff-clifford/pro-nine-roman/ http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/baskerville-classico/ http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/adobe/versailles/ http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/fontbureau/zingha/medium/

The issue is finding something with a curved foot on the R, that little upflip on the right terminal of the horizontal stroke on the 4, and the 9s with the curled up bottom stroke. Some of the above only have two out of those three.
