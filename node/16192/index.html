Have a look at Stanley Morison 'On the classification of typographical variations' in Morison: Letter Forms (available as paper back). This article is also in John Dreyfus: Type Specimen Facsimiles 1-15 Mind you, this is a not a history of the design of letterforms or an analysis of historical letter forms, but rather a historical account of the first attempts to analyze and classify letter forms. Quite a different topic.
