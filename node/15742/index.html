2005-10-20 10:37:17 • elliot100

When you say different, how different do you mean?

American Airlines use AA.com as their primary web address but as far as I know (I’m not American) is not known as AA.

It’s complicated by redirection from multiple addresses, e.g. British Airways promote ba.com but it just redirects to britishairways.com.

continental.com belongs to Continental Airlines not the tyre company (who use conti-online.com).

ms.com belongs to Morgan Stanley and not the more obvious software giant.

2005-10-20 10:49:41 • elliot100

Many times major brands use alternate domain names for special promotions.

http://www.camelsmokes.com http://www.smokerswelcome.com

http://www.beerdrinkersagainstthemistreatmentofbeer.com (Coors Beer) http://www.bkmasks.com (Burger King - masks for Halloween) http://www.prefergorgeous.com (Jaguar autos) http://wearethemudds.com (special site for Jeep) http://www.goldengecko.com (promo site for Geico Insurance)

Also, “planet” is a frequently used prefix on some sites. I don’t think this url is active anymore, but for years it was the key domain for Reebok: www.planetreebok.com and planet.reebook.com
