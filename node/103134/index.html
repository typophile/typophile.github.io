Of course not all free fonts are bad. But pretending commercial fonts have a comparable degree of problems is not realistic. You need to be able to trust your tools.

Here is a grab of how Acrobat Reader 10.1.7 renders the font Ezra in the file texmf/tex/makor2/refcard.pdf of the makor2 TeX package (dated 2003) that I still have in my things. The cause of the distortion was not, so far as I understand, “crappy curves”. The elements in BlueValues were -320 5 -76 707, which just makes no sense. After changing BlueValues, the font was working without problem.

Interestingly, Fontforge complains about the elements in BlueValues/OtherBlues of Bebas neue. Maybe the distortion on dafont also comes from there.
