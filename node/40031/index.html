There are two or three old lead fonts called "Germania", but none of it looks like the original Bradley or, how it was called in Germany: Amerikanische Alt-Gotisch. The Bradley was the original. This font was copied more than once and offered by several foundries in US an Germany: J.W. Phinney, William Bradley, USA, 1895/97-98 Stempelschneider: Hermann Ihlenburg Verschiedene Gießereien American Type Founders Company (ATF), Jersey City, 1895 A. D. Farmer & Son, New York, 1895, als Abbey Text Inland Type Foundry, St. Louis, als St. John Schelter & Giesecke, Leipzig, 1903 / John, Hamburg Klinkhardt, Frankfurt am Main / Wagner, Leipzig Bauersche Gießerei, Frankfurt am Main, ca. 1908 Ludwig & Mayer, Frankfurt am Main / Weisert, Stuttgart Examples can be seen here: http://vau-ef-be.beepworld.de/schriftliste1.htm Georg [img:sites/default/files/old-images/2_5028.jpg]
