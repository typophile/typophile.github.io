Type suggestions for the engraving shop

March 29, 2009 • apankrat

I am putting together a simple website for a local (intaglio) engraving shop. They have never had one before so their current logo is not exactly online friendly. Specifically the type it uses is [[http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/urw/american-uncial|American Uncial]] (kudos to raph for the ID), which is heavily serif’ed and virtually illegible in smaller sizes when rasterized.

So I thought I’d explore some type options to present them for the online use. My first choice is [[http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/fontbureau/hightower/|Hightower]] followed by [[http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/agfa/centaur/|Centaur]] and [[http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/adobe/jenson/|Jenson]].

I realize that my choices is a pretty far departure from the Uncial. It’d be of course ideal to find the type that is similar to Uncial, but with a bit more “modern” feel to it. Any suggestions ?

In general, what type you guys think would work well for the ID of the engravers ?

Something along the line of the type seen on banknotes, since most currency is printed with the intaglio method and that would give an immediate suggestion of what their process is and why it is desirable. Uncial wouldn’t be right for this. Maybe something more like [[http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/nicksfonts/smackeroo-nf/|Smackeroo NF]].

Ha. What an interesting take on a question. They don’t really print the money though :) They do a lot of really elegant and upscale work and the problem with Smackeroo is that it doesn’t exactly imply either of these to me.

I was thinking more of a lighter serif type that would work well in smaller sizes. That’s where the Hightower & Co. came from.
