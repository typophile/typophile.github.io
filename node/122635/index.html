The term used to describe this style of lettering is multiline or triline. This includes variations where the ends are closed and those where they are open. It would also include this variation in which a non connecting continuous stroke loops back and forth from outside to inside. If the corners of the looping stroke were rounded rather than square, the term neon – derived from the characteristics of illuminated glass tube signs made by warping the glass into a looping strokes – could also be applied. compare to the neon font Eclectic Crumpany NF [img:sites/default/files/old-images/EclecticCrumpanyNFAsLUC_6369.gif] http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/nicksfonts/eclectic-crumpany-nf/

I’m not certain if this multiline lettering is an actual font. If it is, the most likely place to find it is Creative Market https://creativemarket.com/fonts Searches using multiline, triline, neon, experimental, lines and stripes produced some interesting somewhat similar designs but not this one. LUC might be in a font without any of these tags.
