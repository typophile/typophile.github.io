2004-06-28 20:32:47 • neonbikini
This is just my opinion, of course, and does not represent any official view of my employer. I'm neither on nor particularly close to the Premiere team.

Any time an OS vendor comes out with an application (including buying a pre-existing one), it tends to do very well against the competition in terms of market share, though not necessarily in profitability. Outside of games, this has historically been true for both Apple and Microsoft. Though there have been exceptions, of course. But the marketplace is littered with products that competed against the OS vendors and died off.

Adobe has in a few cases chosen not to develop products on the Mac. In almost all those cases there were competing products available, including ones from Apple It's just a business decision. But it's not "aggression" against Apple--quite the contrary; Adobe's dropping Premiere development on the Mac doubtless *helps* Final Cut Pro.

And of course Adobe does not have a monopoly in any of the application areas under discussion. Certainly not in font management, where there have been viable competitors all along (e.g. Suitcase, Font Reserve, and now FontAgent Pro).

In the particular area of Mac font management, I do know a fair bit about the business. I agree with something that one of the top people at one of the other companies said to me once, which was that there had been room for two products to be moderately profitable, and that with Apple bringing out FontBook, there was now only room for one retail offering. Not that there couldn't be more, but no more than one of them could make money.
