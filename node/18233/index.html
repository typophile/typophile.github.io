Over my head: subpixel font rendering in Mac OS

February 28, 2006 • Maurice Meilleur

Folks:

Being one of only two Mac users in two extended families, I sometimes get questions that strain what little knowledge I have about my machine. Here’s one for which I don’t even know where to begin to look for a meaningful answer:

Do you know if Apple still implements subpixel imaging in their font system?

My first response was, “Huh?” My second was, “I don’t know, but I know a great bunch of people who do.” Would anyone be so kind as to enlighten me: what is “subpixel imaging in a font system,” did Apple ever “implement” it, and are they doing so now? Thanks for your generosity in advance.

Each pixel on an LCD screen is made up of 1 red, 1 green and 1 blue element. Subpixel rendering involves controlling these elements independently to increase the effective resolution of the screen. Yes, recent versions of the Mac OSX (and Windows, and I think at least one Linux GUI) employ this technique to smooth text.

This Wikipedia article on sub-pixel rendering:

http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subpixel_rendering

And this part of the Apple typography article:

http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_typography#Subpixel_rendering

Should cover all you need to know including illustrations!
