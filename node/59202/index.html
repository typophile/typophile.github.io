Apology regarding my use of names

June 19, 2009 • Chris Dean

It has recently been brought to my attention that my use of names is potentially being interpreted as rude.

Regarding my use of names, using last names is a convention in academia (science). Consistently using people’s last names between disciplines (typography and psychology) assists me with my memory. As I have a neurological and seizure disorder, memory formation is a significant problem with me as a side effect of my seizures and the associated medications. I will most likely continue using this convention. No disrespect is intended.

Having come from an academic background in music, I completely understand where you’re coming from. Composers, conductors, and performers are always referred to by their last name, and this is also true in the visual arts and dance.

To clarify, this trend, of course, is in forums of discussion and writing. Face-to-face interaction or introductions, etc. would always include a title (Dr.) or first name.

I have not come across your potentially rude use of names, but if this is all you mean, I wouldn’t worry about it!
