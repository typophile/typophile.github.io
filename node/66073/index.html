Hi Steffen, your sample is Old English by ITC (or Letraset respectively – these versions seem to be more or less identical.

Old English is based on a [[blackletter]] by [[William Caslon]] (England, ~ 1760). Other foundries had their own versions of this style, like the Manuskript Gotisch that Georg has shown above. At the beginning of the 20th century, [[Morris Fuller Benton]] designed a version for ATF.

More information about the history of Old English typefaces can be found in this Linotype Font Feature.

Today, there is a number of digital ‘Old English’ fonts: Monotype’s (and Mecanorma’s) is pretty wide and loose. The URW version is lighter, with the ‘i’ dots shifted a bit to the right. It has the long s (ſ) as default. Bitstream’s version is called ‘Cloister Black’ and can be easily distinguished by its sporns on ‘b’, ‘h’, ‘k’.

Furthermore, there are numerous ‘spin-offs’, e.g. Avebury Black, an ultra-black and wide display version by [[Jim Parkinson]]. F

For alternative suggestions: well, it depends what you’re aimng at, of course …

Wilhelm-Klingspor-Gotisch by [[Rudolf Koch]] is a classic [[Textura]] beauty. So is [[Goudy Text]], although it is more normalised – see its ‘d’, ‘h’, ‘z’, ‘A’ etc.

Fleischmann Gotisch is a festive baroque rendition. Sinkwitz Gotisch is rather dynamic and lively – both are revivals by [[Ingo Preuss]]. [[František Štorm]]’s Plagwitz with its strong vertical stress or [[Karlgeorg Hoefer]]’s economic Notre Dame are also charming. F
