September 6, 2005 • björn asmussen

hello, as a part of my architectural studies at the university of darmstadt in germany, i'm currently working on an analysis of the relationship of typography and space and character of a city and the impact they have on each other. my main emphasis so far is: :: how much a city's type (ci) represents its charcter, and/or space :: can one type represent the many different charcters and atmospheres most modern citys include in their metropolitain area? :: how much does a type named after a city represent the city, what is the inspiration for the name? :: are there other ways of creating a city's typography? :: does a city have a certain typographical style? in the signs? in the graffiti? ... i'd be happy about any comment, link and every other bit of information, that would help me gather information on this subject! björn asmussen

You might want to narrow your project. Good luck!

thanx for the hint on the book! the aim of the project so far (i'm just at the start of my research!)is not to analyse each and every city and its typographic applications. my aim is to generate an overview of possible ways of dealing with citys to generate a certain typography. based on this information i want to focus on the relation of space and typography (an the other way around, as in buildings and citys based on letters f.e. the alphabet city by paul noble). or in other words: what does it need to transform space into typography and typography into (usable/interesting)space.
