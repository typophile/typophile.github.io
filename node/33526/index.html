Merits of WGL4 encoding

May 5, 2007 • Nick Shinn

It all seems quite useful until you get to the “box drawings” in Unicode range U+2013 through U+FB02. Who uses that stuff? What advantages are there for an indie foundry to include those characters in a font?

Not much that I can think of. Those are IBM linedraw characters that are included for compatibility/support of some pretty ancient linedraw graphics. We don’t include them in our fonts at Adobe, and I don’t recall ever hearing a complaint or request on it.

On the plus side, they’re easy to make, and you’d just have to make them once and you could recycle them in your later fonts.

Cheers,
