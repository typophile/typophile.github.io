Gallery Identity

September 14, 2005 • marc

I’m writing my dissertaion on the visual identity of art galleries and the flexibility between gallery and exhibition identity. In my opinion some of the most interesting and strong identites of the last five years or so have been those for galleries: Tate, Barbican, Whitechapel and MOMA…

In my understanding an identity is a visual representation of the content, personality and values of an organisation. Galleries house exhibitions with an entirely new set of contents, personalities and values. However when you look at (some) promotional material of exhibitions, they are dominated by the identity of the gallery. From a visitors perspective, these campaigns are communicating very little about the nature of the exhibition, only reinforcing the galleries branding.

My questions are:

  1. How are these issues normally addressed?

  2. Does the emphasis of gallery to exhibition identity shift as time passes, when the brand is established?

  3. Does anyone have any opinions on any other gallery identities?

If there is any other relevant information I should know that would be great. If there are any relevant books on the subject I should be reading could anyone possibly let me know?

Thanks
