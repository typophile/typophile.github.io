geraint

Try reading the ending of the book first. Often more interesting. But no, not an American thing.

Colophons first appeared in printed books shortly after the development of printing with movable type. So they would be mid-European and mid fifteenth century in origin.

They refer to the production details and other aspects of the book, not just the typeface. During the period of Incunabula (1455-1500), colophons rarely actually discussed typefaces (called "letters," by the way, not "type," up until about 1700) since castings were usually unique to the printer.

Colophons mostly appear in fine press books (where they are seemingly a requirement) and high-end trade books (where they are seemingly an affectation).
