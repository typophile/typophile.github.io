@ Symbol

October 7, 2006 • ThinkAGM

I am designing a business card that needs to include an email address, which leads me to the dreaded @ symbol. I despise this symbol, and have been toying with a substitution, (AT ligatures) especially since designing in faces such as Garamond, Hoefler etc. Do not go with such a modern symbol. What are your thoughts on this?

You can certainly replace it with the word ‘at’. Of course, then, to avoid any inconsistency, you have to treat the dot sign the same way. Yourname at hotmail dot com, for instance.

My question is, do you have a good reason to do it?

I, for one, wouldn’t break the rule at the expense of clarity.

It’s not as if the “@” is anything new. It has been around at least almost as long as there have been typewriters, if not longer. Only the usage is modern, and even that goes back to the late ’70s. I read some report on the history of the thing a while back. I wish I could recall the link.

Anyway, if you don’t like the way the character fits in with the typeface, redesign it, but make sure it will still be understood as what it is.

2006-10-07 09:42:21 • Alessandro Segalini

In my designs, I’ve used a vertical ‘at’ ligature, as well as a lot of other ideas. One im nearly done with has a very low tail, extended-right tail extending into the next letter, another looks like an underlined ‘a’, a (c) or an (r) symbol. Since none of these have had any negative feedback whatsoever, I’d say theres a ton of freedom in what you want it to look like. The places were an @ is used are pretty well understood, and not normally inside of words, so you can get away with more.
