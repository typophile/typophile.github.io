Help pairing Seravek with a Serif

February 11, 2011 • bpoliva

Hi All - I'm admittedly struggling with the task of pairing PTF's Seravek with a serif, for an identity that would ideally also carry over well to screen/web. The aim is to stay mostly neutral with the feel of it all — complementing the little bit of humanity that Seravek has. After quickly ruling out anything too old-style or didone (mistake?), and personally not wanting to explore slabs (possibly a mistake also?), I'm left wondering whether a transitional serif is most appropriate. There is something not working for me with the transitionals I have to test with (Plantin, Utopia, etc.), though I can't pinpoint what is feeling off. Of course while testing, when I pair the transitionals with something like Helvetica - something more neutral than Seravek - the transitionals work just fine. In the effort to not abandon Seravek, I know there must be serif faces I am unaware of or not remembering that will somehow work. Any advice and ideas are much appreciated. This is proving harder than expected!
It may be that you're only looking at older serifs. Try FF Meta Serif, Minion, Lyon, Charter or Arnhem.

RE = “Reading Edge.” See also: http://blog.webtype.com/?p=29 This was David Berlow’s pet initiative, to develop faces specifically for optimum screen readability at small text sizes — essentially more Georgia/Verdana-quality screen fonts for @font-face. It involved pretty much all FB staff hands on deck for a while to prepare for the Webtype launch last summer.
