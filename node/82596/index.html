Ute, concerning Lavazza, stop looking for a computer font and take the time to read. First read carefully about the [[http://www.lavazza.com/corporate/en/lavazzastyle/design/logo|Lavazza Logo Timeline]] on Lavazza’s site; in particular this logo is dated 1950: If you look at the [[http://www.lavazza.com/corporate/en/company/history/1933-1955.html|Lavazza 1933-1955]] history page, you see at the right this logo from 1945-1946. If you just look for [[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lavazza|Lavazza on Wikipedia]] you get this picture from the beginning of the 19th certury: [[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Lavazza_0002782_m.jpg|La grande storia…]]. Look at the logo on the bottom left. I call that a company with a sense of continuity.

Now [[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adobe_Illustrator|Adobe Illustrator]] dates from 1986. There were no digital fonts in the 1950s. There were no electronic computers outside “laboratories” in 1945-46.
