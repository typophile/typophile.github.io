Help me name my blog, win Adobe prize

September 24, 2005 • Thomas Phinney

[sigh]

I need to come up with a name for a type blog to be hosted at work (Adobe). So it will be work-related. My idea is to post musings on the technical, business, historical and design aspects of type/fonts and type technologies, with particular attention to OpenType and a bit to SING as well. It won’t be 100% Adobe-centric - I will certainly call attention to cool things from other foundries and companies.

But what to call it?

I have long thought that if I ever got a vanity license plate, it would read “FONT MAN” - but I’m not sure that’s a great name for the blog. Another option suggested by David Lemon (whose license plate reads “TYPE NRD” btw, if you didn’t know), was “Thomas on Type.”

I am eager to hear any other suggestions. If I end up using your suggestion, I will buy you any one Adobe font family or application of your choice. (Note that delivery of the prize may be slow, as there is an internal ordering process I have to go through. But I’m good for it.)

Cheers,

T

2005-09-24 11:54:32 • Randy
Phints (Phinney + fonts + Hints [tech.: type hinting + instruction, tip, indication]) It’s short, easy to spell and remember and it does not include the both obvious and ubiquitous “type” label that I think is not necessary in the blog title - your readers will associate it anyway. “Phints” also includes a subtle notion of “mints”, which would be small (11pt? ;-]) intensive and distinctive candies – not the worst thing to have in mind when thinking about entries of your blog. Alternatively, if you don’t trust the readers' media literacy and want to stress the word “type” like other posts have implicitly suggested, you could append “-ty” or “-type” (would then be: Phinty/Phintype), but you’d lose some of the “fonts” and “hints” associations. Perhaps a short subtitle like “Thoughts on type(, Adobe) and more by Thomas Phinney” would clarify your blog’s goal and its contents.
