Early anglo-saxon grotesque à la Bureau Grot

July 25, 2014 • lindenhayn

Here’s a typeface that a major German magazine has been using for a while. An old-school grotesque in the vein of the [[http://a1.dspnimg.com/data/l/2085910623030_Rp8aLgvk_l.jpg|Stephenson Blake]] ones which have seen [[http://www.aisforapple.fr/fonts/grotesque-6/|several]] [[http://www.fontbureau.com/fonts/BureauGrot/|revivals]] [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/urw/grotesque-no-9/|during]] [[http://www.stormtype.com/family-trivia-grotesk.html|the past few years.]] …all of which I’ve had a close look at, but this is none of them. Note the rather tame /Q/ in contrast to most of the ones I mentioned. The /t/ is a good shibboleth too. The /g/, not shown here, is of course double-storey. Yes, it could be a custom typeface, but then again, none of the four other faces that they use (AG, Sabon, Freight) is.

Looks like Founders Grotesk X-Condensed by KLIM.
