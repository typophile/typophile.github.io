I hate to say this, but that's not Interstate. Look at the cap S: the terminals are horizontal, where in Interstate they're diagonal. Likewise, the lowercase e shows a lot of variation between thick and thin, while Interstate is quite monoline. And the junctures leave the stems at an angle, while in Interstate they curve smoothly out from the stem. I don't recognize this myself, and wonder if it's deliberately crude hand lettering based on Interstate (and on the US Dept. of Transportation highway signage fonts which inspired Interstate).
