Farewell Jef Raskin

February 28, 2005 • Frank Jonen

Jef Raskin, creator of the original Macintosh died last Saturday at his Pacifica home.

You could agree or disagree with him, but you could definitely not ignore him. His views of information presentation, interface architecture and user friendly computing have shaped generations of users.

The world looses a great thinker.

The author of The Humane Interface and passionate teacher was diagnosed with pancreatic cancer died at age 61.

We'll all miss you Jef.

A longer article can be found at KRT Wire and soon all over the web.

Very sad about Jef Raskin.

The short story re Raskin and the Mac GUI: Raskin's idea for the Mac was very different from how it turned out. He imagined something much simpler and cheaper. The PARC-like GUI stuff (including the mouse) was the result of Jobs taking over the project. Raskin left not long after. He later designed a computer for Canon called the Canon CAT which was more like what he had in mind for the Mac.
