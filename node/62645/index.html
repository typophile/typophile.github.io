CJK = [ C(t) + C(s) ] + J +K ... or, is there is a problem in east asia

October 2, 2009 • hartmannsgruber

Recently I was working on a project for a client, and realised that they used different fonts for their East Asian markets. I was looking in proposing one gothic CJK font for the whole region, but had to realise that that'snot so easy. I know there are Mircosoft fontswhich offer a complete CJK set, but looking for fonts with a good variety of weights, to mach them with the latin house-type, was not easy at all. For the time we kept the 3 different fonts for C, J & K. Did anyone else have the same problem? j. [img:sites/default/files/old-images/cjk_5023.jpg]
What you're after is a Pan-CJK font, meaning a single typeface design that serves multiple CJK locales. This is a topic of one of my IUC33 presentations that will take place in about two weeks. See: [[http://www.unicodeconference.org/conference-at-a-glance.htm|IUC33]] The primary issue is that some CJK Unified Ideographs may require two or more glyphs to satisfy the CJK locales, due to Han Unification. Dr. Ken Lunde Senior Computer Scientist, CJKV Type Development Adobe Systems Incorporated lunde@adobe.com
