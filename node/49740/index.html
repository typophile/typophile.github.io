Dan,

I’m just starting out like you as well, and I’ve picked up a few things listening in on the amazing and talented typographers and designers that hang out around here.

Have you read all the great discussions on magazine redesign so far on this forum? Google “site:typophile.com magazine redesign”. You will see A LOT of discussions. They will give you lots of ideas.

http://www.google.com/search?hl=en&q=site%3Atypophile.com+magazine+redesign&btnG=Google+Search&aq=f&oq=

I found it helpful to first get familiar with why a designer went from typeface A to typeface B. What was the problem with the old one that he/she was trying to solve? How did the new one solve that problem?

Then you can begin to see how to think through what you’re trying to do and let that guide your own decisions.

For example:

What did Erik Spiekermann [[http://www.stepinsidedesign.com/STEPMagazine/Article/28761|do for the Economist]] and why?

Why did some magazines decide to go with [[http://www.type-together.com/typeinuse.php|Ronnia and Karmina]] as their sans and serif? What do these typefaces actually do for a magazine or newspaper?

What was the problem Hoefler&Frere-Jones were trying to help Martha Stuart Living solve when they [[http://www.typography.com/fonts/font_overview.php?productLineID=100033|created the slab serif Archer]]?

If you can start to think in this manner, you might arrive at a handful of choices. And then you just pick one that works. And change it when it stops working.

Have a lot of fun in your search & learning! Rahim
