Here’s the problem when one tries to identify metal fonts, is that we don’t get a full showing and have to guess at a point.

Your sample looks very close to “Alternate Gothic 1” Trade Gothic Bold Extra Condensed, based on the unique spur on the uppercase “G”.

Also, since we are looking at metal faces, there will be design differences between point sizes (scaling). So I believe Patricia is correct in identifying a digital version of Alternate Gothic #1.

I’ve been hunting through the McGrew book on American Metal Typefaces and the A.T.A. Type Comparison Book. I got pretty excited for a minute about Monotype New Gothic Condensed, because it had the large hook on the J and the right K (both features were unlike Alternate Gothic). It even had the spur ‘beard’ on the G, but the G is too closed, and the Q has the wrong tail. The $ was pretty close, but the numerals were not shaped the same. Sigh!

I think your comment about the hundreds of numbered Gothics is the true key to why we are having so little luck. It seems every foundry has a variety of weights and widths of Gothics, and without knowing the Foundry or the age of the type, we can only hope to get lucky.
