Rosem -

Welcome to type.

In the days of yore - we had text and then display/headline type. Text was just that - type up to approx. 12 points in height.

Display on a typesetter would start at 14 pts.

While headline was synonomous with typositor - or letters at 1/2 to 2 inches high. (Blown up further by a stat camera.)

The true difference between a text and a display font was the personalities of the letters. Text when hung up 12 or more inches away from you - and upside down - should appear as a gray matter, no one letter popping off the page.

While a display type turned upside down, let’s you say - oh I see those letters….
