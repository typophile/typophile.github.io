Frankenstein was sewn by hand, this was stitched by machine. A blow up of the details, fuzzy but reveals some forensics: At least some of the serifs and general proportions of some of the letters such as G might be a similar to the neo-Venetian Schneidler http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/stempel-schneidler/ The E is an Uncial style. See for example Uncial Antiqua http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/stiggy-sands/uncial-antiqua-pro/ or Marceta http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/finefonts/marceta/ or the “post-histroical” Jana Thork http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/outras/jana-thork/ Easy to make by putting a crossbar on C The initial curl swash – a nice “feminine” touch that appears nowhere else in the letters – is available on various fonts such as Bookmania The leg of R looks like it was taken from a heavy Bodoni and pasted on a Trajan style P with an open bowl. Perhaps some creative soul has made this into a Biker Bride of Frankenstein font and shared it with the world. Don
