2012-03-18 18:47:44 • eliason
The lower case of all the types mentioned lacks the basic geometric construction of Trajan. Of course, unless one goes down the Futura path, a nod must be made to the brush which underlies the Roman lapidary method, but that’s all. Dwiggins’s Metro strikes the right balance — its capitals are remarkably faithful to the Trajan forms, and its lower case is geometric with just a hint of chirographic ductus.
2012-03-18 19:02:14 • Nick Shinn
I don’t believe in the geometric construction of letters. Neither lowercase, nor uppercase. If lowercase gets “constructed”, Futura or – worse – Avant Garde is the Result. This path has never been overly promising. Roma is an interesting take, though I’d rather put it in the *Incised* category rather than in the sans, just like Robert Slimbach’s recent release. However, the R of Roma disturbs me, the lowercase of it is an enigma to me. B, E, R of Metro are not at all trajanesque to my eyes. Another worthwhile recent sans is [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/tipo-pepel/trajana-sans/regular/glyphs.html|Trajana Sans]]. But for me, it’s again the R which proves to be tricky.
