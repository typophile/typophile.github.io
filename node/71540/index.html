2010-06-25 15:05:06 • flooce

I suggest you look for fonts that produce interesting addresses and phone numbers.

For email addresses, the relationship between letters and the “@” symbol is crucial. For phone numbers, old-style figures have more personality.

I don’t know what conventions postal codes in your part of the world follow, but small caps can make this aspect of typography very tasty.

So, you will require a text font with these sophisticated typographic features: old style figures, small caps, a good “@”, (and possibly symbols for “tel”, “fax” and “cell”?)

If you pursue this look, I suspect it will impress (some “quality”) graphic designers into a positive opinion of your firm.
