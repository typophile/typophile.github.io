Garamond Slab?

March 26, 2011 • Trevor Baum

Has there ever been an Egyptian/slab serif based on Garamond’s proportions and geometry? It seems like this could be an interesting blend between the ultimate classic text face and a more ‘contemporary’ style.

Here’s a couple of quick drawings I made of what said typeface might look like. Note the classical Garamond ‘a’ (based loosely on Stempel Garamond) and subtly bracketed slabs in an Egyptienne style.

Has something like this ever been done before? Is it too incongruous? And ultimately, is it worth pursuing?

DB News, developed by Christian Schwartz and Erik Spiekermann for Deutsche Bahn AG, approaches this basic concept; however, it is based on a Jannon-derived Garamond, not a true Garamond.

I once started sketching out a light, contemporary slab based also somewhat on an ATF Garamond skeleton (not unlike the sort of relationship between Nick Shinn’s Bodoni Egyptian and his Bodoni models). But I didn’t take it very far and it’s been sitting idle for several years.
