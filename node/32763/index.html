graphic standards manual

April 2, 2007 • Finnguala

I am currently trying to put together graphic standards manuals for 7 different companies (they are all under the same corporate umbrella) These companies have been around for awhile, but no-one has created a standards manual until now. Many different people create documents for these companies, so I thought it would be best to stick to the lowest common denominator of the fonts available in Windows. I would like to pick appropriately though, and I am finding it difficult to find out the history of these windows fonts, what they were based on, and why they were created. Can anyone point me in the right direction? Thanks!!

After having done far too many GSMs in my time, the best advice I could offer would be to look at all the flavours of Windows they use, pick the common fonts, and try them out in the standard documents they produce, then create templates in whatever fonts pass the committee process. :-(

If you feel you need to read about history et al, there are lots of books out there that can go into the details, but to be honest, 99.9% of clients and users don’t want/need/care to know the “wherefore” or “why,” just the “here’s how to do this.”
