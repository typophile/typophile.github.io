Completed in 1913 for the 100th anniversary of the Battle of Leipzig, also known as the Battle of the Nations. The statues of the monument were sculpted by Christian Behrens and his apprentice Franz Metzner who finished the remaining statues after Behrens’s death in 1905. This suggests that the Jugendstyl/Art Nouveaule lettering is an original design by Christian Behrens and/or Franz Metzner. This style of lettering was very fashionable in Germany and elsewhere before WW I. The specimen books of German foundries included a fair number of similar styles. Most have not been digitized. A few digitized similars are Belgravia http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/scriptorium/belgravia/ Ortem http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/itfmecanorma/ortem/ Brillant http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/rmu/brillant/ Eckmann https://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/eckmann/

BTW the phrase “GOTT MIT UNS” was widely used by the German Army, including on the belt buckles of German soldiers. Don
