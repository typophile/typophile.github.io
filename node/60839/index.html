2009-08-11 08:52:22 • Esterhase

For one, look at the way the curved section connects to the straight section in the ‘n’ and ’t' - it’s very abrupt. Compare this to the sample set in Avenir which is optically corrected to look smooth.

Also, take a look at where the bowl of the ’d' connects to the stem. The thickness of the line should decrease in this area to accomodate the extra “ink”.

I’m sure a real font designer could elaborate on more “mistakes” but basically, the free font was constructed with perfect circles and lines with a uniform stroke width, and then wasn’t adjusted at all. Avenir (even when squooshed) has been carefully designed to look like it’s perfectly geometric with a uniform stroke width, even though it isn’t.
