Because of its construction, pointed nib pen, Bodoni’s sans counterpart is something in circle of Helvetica and other “swiss” typefaces. Massimo Vignelli had some good combinations of Bodoni and Helvetica - especially in period of 60’s and modernism.

Avenir and Avantgarde, that is a bit tough, because they are geometric fonts, and first answer would be Bodoni or anything in didone area. Avantgarde has its serif compatriot in Lubalin Graph, slab serif version of avantgarde.

Bringhurst suggests several traditional combinations. Off the top of my head:

Gill/Perpetua Futura/Bodoni Frutiger/Meridienne Helvetica/Craw Clarendon Syntax/Minion

I have had success with Helvetica Neue and ITC Century, especially due to the fact that they are both large families. The trick when combining serif’s and sans' is to counterbalance the x-height, stroke width, character width, ascenders, descenders, cap height, axis &c. That’s a lot of different variables. Look at [[http://typophile.com/node/55767|this thread on Utopia and Univers]] for a good Illustration. The traditional school of thought says go for similarity or contrast, but not in between (shit or get off the pot). Utopia/Univers is a good example of similarity while Futura/Bodoni is a good example of contrast. If you can afford to, I highly recommend investigating Berthold’s Futura Serie as it has small caps and old-style figures.

Some typefaces, such as Rotis, have both which can be convenient. And recently, I noticed that Font Bureau has a similar/compatible list of typefaces on the right hand side of their specimens. Also very convenient.

At the end of the day however, it’s not the font, it’s how you use it that counts.
