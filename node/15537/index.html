Oh, ick — I loathe pedantry. But here, if anywhere, we ought to be accurate about publishing industry terminology.

A magazine’s masthead is not its nameplate (the place where the logo appears). A masthead is the list of editorial and publishing staff.

2005-10-11 23:26:14 • Bald Condensed

Kathleen is absolutely correct. (BTW – hi Kathleen, long time, no talk)

Masthead has mistakenly been used so often instead of the correct terms of nameplate or flag that it has taken over through common misuse.

Many years ago I worked for two newspaper publishers, masthead ONLY referred to the section that carried the name of the publisher, chief staff and place of publication.

I guess it’s the same way that FONT has now become what we called typeface.

Yes – as misused by us landlubbers. But to mariners, the masthead (photo 1) is the topmost section of the mast which bears the rigging. A flag or pennant can be flown from the masthead or flagstaff, a pole extending above the masthead.

So the masthead bears the work, like the staff of a publication.
