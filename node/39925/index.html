You could (mis)use one of the huge Adobe families like Warnock, Arno or Garamond Premier Pro for that; they do not only offer a wide range of weights, but also have optical sizes for each of the weights.

progressively more ornate The italics of these faces – among other recent (super)families – come with swash alternates that could be switched on, but there’s no progression to it.

An alternative idea: If you (or your school) have a rather rich type library, you could scroll through the history of type; e.g. starting with Akzidenz Grotesk and Folio, proceed with Helvetica, then Univers, followed by Frutiger, Lucida, Stone Sans, TheSans etc. That would free you from being dependent on the range of weights/styles of one specific typeface – but there are myriads of possibilities, and it’s not an easy undertaking.
