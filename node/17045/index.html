"Front Matter" in Chapter 1 of the Chicago Manual of Style? Marjorie Skillin’s Words into Type has the following list for front matter (somewhat abbreviated). i Bastard title ii Card page (books by same author), imprimatur, series title, or blank - Frontispiece (usually unnumbered) iii Title page iv Copyright page v Dedication vi Blank vii TOC viii TOC (continued) or List of illustrations ix List of illustrations or Acknowledgements x continued, or blank xi Editor’s preface xii continued, or blank xiii Author’s preface xiv continued, or blank xv Foreword or Introduction xvi continued, or blank The card page, frontispiece, and copyright page are always on left-handed pages. Other sections appear, or at least begin, on right-handed pages.

We like to call the "bastard title" the half-title. It's more polite. The copyright traditionally is supposed to go on the back side of the title page (that is, page iv) but it can also be the very last page of the book. It can't go anywhere else as far as I know. The rules about contents pages and openers always beginning on the recto (right hand page) are pretty loose these days, especially in illustrated book publishing. You can certainly design the table of contents to run across a spread.
