I have a customer looking for a similar font to the following. Shading must be similar and at least serif font. I realize this is a logo and has been tweaked some but I need to come as close as possible. Thanks so much for any help!

It seems obvious to me that this lettering has been hand-drawn, since every ‘e’ (for example) is slightly different from each other ‘e’. I think this look is most like the sort of revivals that Nick Curtis does. Search on his name at MyFonts, or have a look through his early freeware fonts at [[http://www.moorstation.org/typoasis/designers/curtis/index.htm|TypOasis]].

His [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/nicksfonts/boxcar-willie-nf/|Boxcar Willie]] has initials somewhat like your sample, and [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/nicksfonts/packard-patrician-nf/|Packard Patrician]] (based on Cooper’s Packard type) might work for the lower case. URW’s [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/urw/artcraft/|Artcraft Bold]] may be even closer for the lower case, and is from the same time period as Packard.

Oh, and as far as the counter shading, I think you will need to add that yourself as an after effect. One of the few fonts I know with similar ‘toothed’ highlighting was called “Greco Adornado”, but it seems to have been renamed to something I can’t recall. A similar shading style is used on fonts [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/itc/vinyl/sawtooth/|ITC Vinyl Sawtooth]] and Letraset [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/letraset/zaragoza/|Zaragoza]], neither of which is much like your lettering.
