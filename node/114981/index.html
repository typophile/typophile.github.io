In my opinion the fonts shown in the image are not old fonts, but rather are modern designs, which may or may not be digital fonts. No problem with the sans-serif for BOURBON as mentioned in my earlier posts. But “barrel STOUT” is a real Frankenstein. Notice the differences between these letters and classic italics like Garamond http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/paratype/original-garamond-bt/ and Fleischman http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/bitstream/fleischman-bt-pro/. The truncated ascenders, the almost vertical slope, the strange tail at the top of b, etc. What’s this with a ball serif capital S? And how did square serifs from Egyptian end up on the T and U? This is not to say that someone has not packaged this mish mash as a font. So if the Frankenfont does not turn up you could make reasonable facsimile of “barrel” by editing Fleishman. Other candidates for editing include Verdigris MVB Pro Text http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/mvbfonts/mbv-verdigris-pro/ ITC Cushing http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/itc/cushing/ and Kis http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/bitstream/kis/ Don
