This is my first post. I spent about 30 minutes looking through various posts and this is an interesting place. I am an independent author and taught myself the basics of typography to self publish and not appear too amateurish. I also do my own graphic design on my website, business cards, letter head, and anything for my business.

Previously I have purchased Adobe CS suite with a huge font pack through an educational discount, and I use Indesign, Photoshop, Word and Powerpoint for 99% of my work involving fonts. If I use a special font in web design, I do it in photoshop and upload it as an image file.

I’d be lying if I said I had time/interest to really get deep into this subject. It seems like most here do this professionally and I already have multiple jobs. I would, however, like to avoid looking like an amateur, as much as possible. Reading through The Elements of Typography has helped me format my writing better, and I am now very much aware of orphans/widows and kerning.

My reason for this post is to solicit advice on pruning down my font collection. Searching this forum and reading the wiki has shown me what a good basic font collection looks like, but I dont know enough about this subject to make good decisions based on that.

I have about 800 fonts installed on my Mac laptop, and it’s virtually impossible for me to find browse through when thinking of a font to use for a mini project. I think if I had closer to 100 to 200 fonts, it would be a lot easier. More so, if I learned to break them down into different classifications. I read a few post on sorting and the only thing I know is the difference between Serif and Sans Serif.

I have a bunch of “free” fonts that I downloaded off random sites. I believe they are legit, non-stolen, just user created fonts. Some look good to me, but after reading some posts on here, I am learning that a lot of these are crap, and will look amateurish to use. Any recommendation on reading? The wiki and most forum posts are way over my head so I think I need to start with something more basic.

What should I do with my fonts? I’d be willing to spend a few hours reading/learning more about fonts if it will lead to a lifetime of useful basic knowledge. Is there a list of about 150 or so fonts that would make a reasonable setup for a non-power user?

Thanks in advance.

History is most useful at the intermediate level. Most of the “top” lists, especially those aggregated on the internet, are decidedly historical. Classical and conservative perhaps, but an easy step to developing taste.

You could start by going through your library and classifying according to Bringhurst, and then pruning each category to those you best like the look of – favoring those which have “expert” features such as small caps and figure variants.
