principles of design with type

June 8, 2008 • J.Strange

I’m currently working on a kinetic typography project, and i am trying to really understand the ideas and emotions that type can convey, and use it to create a balance piece that conveys a message on every level.

What I am asking for help with is this, are there principles or guideline that can be applied to type in order to convey meaning, for example what makes the use of one particular font convey the meaning of strenght while another is weak? What do you as the typography community look for within the type to convey emotion?

Believe me, I understand the broad nature of my questions but I need a starting point, so that I can make my choices based on something other than “oooh, this looks good”

certain typefaces can be described in ways that relate to ideas that we apply to other things.

Script: intimate, time-consuming, effort, hand-rendered

Sturdy type: solid, immovable, strong

Thins: excess, luxury, beauty

So with kinetic typography I imagine the movements the type takes could be related to different paradigms of movement we see in other things:

-books falling, dancing, sliding, rolling.

I seriously doubt you’re going to invent much new, it’s just the art of taking combinations of letters that mean things and building phrases that convey a certain thought progression towards a climax and maximum understanding.

Then taking the words and using the typefaces that reinforce and add to those words and set the text in that.

Then finally taking gestures and movements that we all recognize and setting the type to life in a way that helps express the thoughts even more…
