Periods at the end of list entries?

September 20, 2009 • Kiwi

Something that has been bugging me is whether periods go at the end of a list. My only reference source is my Typography iPhone app, so I feel it necessary to consult the sages on Typophile. What do the reference sources advize?

On another note: Does anyone know what the rules are for capitalization following colons? There does not seem to be an either-or rule; it is more complex than that. Does anyone know the rules or some good style guidelines to get a grip on colons? I would rather not have to avoid using colons altogether because the guidelines are so murky.

You might want to invest in a good standard style guide for your area. There will be regional differences when it comes to these sorts of things.

The Chicago Manual of Style, which is a widely accepted guide in the US, addresses your first question in two sections: 6.127 and 6.129. Basically, if the list is introduced by a complete sentence and a colon and the list items are short, then the list is not punctuated and does not end with a period. If the introduction to the list is not a grammatically complete sentence and the list elements consist of phrases or sentences themselves, then usually the list is punctuated with a semicolon between items and concluded with a period.

But there’s room in there for plenty of nuance and exception.

Your second question is addressed by the CMS in §6.64. Again summarizing: When the colon is used within a sentence, then it is followed by lowercase (unless a proper name). When the colon introduces several separate sentences, then the following word is capitalized.

I don’t know if these guidelines would apply equally in Danish.

Here’s what I recall from the Sister Agnes Virginia Manual of Style:

The initial cap and closing period are attributes of a sentence. If a list entry is a complete sentence, it should begin with a capital and end with a period.

Here are the things I have to do today: * Wash the car. * Feed the dog. * Pick up the dry cleaning.

List entries that aren’t sentences don’t need an initial cap or a period. If the list completes a sentence that was begun in the paragraph above, put a period after the last entry in the list.

National Bohemian Beer is: * wet * cold * delicious.

The same principle applies with a colon: If you can remove the words before the colon and still have a complete sentence, then use an initial cap after the colon.

If the words that follow the colon don’t form a complete sentence, then treat them as what they are: a sentence fragment.
