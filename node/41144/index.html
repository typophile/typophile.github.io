2008-01-22 03:31:36 • pattyfab
I, too, think that looking at examples of good posters is a valid way to learn. Here are four recommendations: New Masters of Poster Design is a good contemporary collection. John Barnicoats' Posters: A Concise History is exactly that, and a classic text on the subject (not a how-to, however). Up Against The Wall is an international poster collection. One more compilation: A Century of Posters. I also think that there probably isn't a "how-to" guide devoted exclusively to posters. But there are books about good design principles... which can be applied to posters, book covers, page layout, etc. A good one to start out with is the recent Design Elements: A Graphic Style Manual, by Timothy Samara.
