Note that the titles of old films are usually created by a lettering artist and it may not correspond to an existing font. Even if they are based on a font, it may not exist in the form of a modern digital font. That said, [[http://www.findmyfont.com/index.php/fonts/font-preview?fset=URW%20%2F%20%28Commercial%29&ffam=GoudyCnd-Normal%20-%20Regular&fid=626339fd9d2772337a8028f6e9379d8c&fsize=60&text=Directed%20by%20ALFRED%20HITCHCOCK&wrap=2|Goudy]] looks very close to your sample and you can also try [[http://www.findmyfont.com/index.php/fonts/font-preview?fset=Font-Bureau%20%2F%20%28Commercial%29&ffam=ThrohandInk%20-%20Roman&fid=dce775316f0b3d3791bd445dc9663fe5&fsize=60&text=Directed%20by%20ALFRED%20HITCHCOCK&wrap=2|Throhand Ink]] by David Berlow which is not so close but has longer ascenders on "b" & "d"
