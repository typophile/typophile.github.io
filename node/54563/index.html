James: I get those weird shapes all the time, but undo usually fixes things. This probably has to do with the way FL manages undo (it is: one thread by glyph, so as, after some glyph jumps, one increases geometrically undo memory usage) through multiple dynamic pointers; eventually, it may occur that, when you try to go back i.e. by typing a fast keystroke, you “catch” FL —which is modestly slow due to its complexity— performing another background task; that’s why you can fix it —actually, not exactly a bug, but an undesired undoable behavior— by repeating the command: after recovering its “conscious”, FL finishes all pending tasks and brings you where you wanted to.

“Does anybody knows why fontlab crashes unexpectedly?”

You can’t run it for too long, run long with several other applications running, with a large glyph repertoire refreshing, or edit a high resolution font at large magnification, without crashes likely to occur. Any time FL fails to immediately respond to edits, it is coming soon so I save and restart. And any time it does edits different from mine, it’s also time to restart. Once or twice a year it gets me bad by not saving work anywhere, the latest version simply vanishing to font heaven when you quite FL.

It beats little pieces of metal trapped your teeth, but you have to be careful at all times to observe its behavior and react accordingly a the right time. I’ve found it to be truly unique in this versus all the other post-1999 Mac apps I’ve used.
