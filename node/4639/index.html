Can a book printed with an inkjet printer on high quality paper, cut, folded, hand bound and carefully designed & illustrated still be considered "fine press"?

Brian

If the optimal word here is "carefully." yes. Technology does not govern quality. The great southern Californian fine printer and typographer Saul Marks modestly called himself a "careful" printer.

I've produced books on the laser printer with as much care and concern as I would have with the letterpress process. Compared to most other laser printed books I've seen I'd like to think mine turned out on a higher quality note because I put the extra effort in to making them that way.

The term "fine press," by the way, doesn't go all that far back. Probably not much before the 1970s.
