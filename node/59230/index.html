Hi Marionn,

welcome to Typophile!

There’s a lot of information in this related thread: What are the most frequently chosen type faces for novels & related books?

However, the typefaces you mentioned are not among those ‘frequently chosen for novels’. Still, you can easily find books that are set in Univers etc. You could browse your local library, especially the non-fiction sections; catalogues, guides and other genres where sans-serifs are more likely to find. Off the top of my head (and shelf): ‘Lesetypographie’ uses Univers; ‘Made with FontFont' is (partly) set in FF Meta Pro; …

The catalogues of Stiftung Buchkunst list the typefaces used in the books awarded in the annual ‘Best German Book Design' competition. ‘Beuys. Die Revolution sind wir’ is set in Univers – and won a Bronze Medal for ’Best Book Design from all over the World’ at Leipzig Book Fair 2009. So did ‘Projekt Vitra’, which is set in Futura (albeit a custom version; Vitra-Futura).

Book Cover Archive lets you filter books by typeface – but that data is only referring to the cover design, of course, not the actual book. Yet a fabulous ressource, imo. F
