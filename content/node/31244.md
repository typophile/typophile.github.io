body: "Jo De Baerdemaeker, born September 8 1974 in Brussels, Belgium, is considered
  one of the two active typedesigners of Belgium. Frederik Berlaen is the other one.\r\n\r\nHe
  is an experienced designer who has a passion for type and typography. Recently graduated
  at the University of Reading, Jo De Baerdemaeker designed and developed <strong><em>Lungta</em></strong>,
  a Tibetan-Roman typeface, and is eager to continue to work as a typeface designer,
  teacher and consultant.\r\nHe understands the complete creative process from concept
  to final output and keeps himself up-to-date regarding recent technological developments.\r\n\r\nDe
  Baerdemaeker is also ATypI Country Delegate for Belgium.\r\n<!--break-->\r\n\r\n\r\nEducation\r\n<ul>\r\n<li><strong>Since
  October 2005</strong> Jo De Baerdemaeker is undertaking a PhD entitled <em>Tibetan
  Typeforms: from their inception in 1738 up to the present day</em> <a href=\"http://www.rdg.ac.uk/AcaDepts/lt/\">at
  the Department of Typography and Graphic Communication, University of Reading</a>,
  UK.</li>\r\n<li><strong>2003-2004</strong> Master of Arts in Typeface Design, <a
  href=\"http://www.rdg.ac.uk/AcaDepts/lt/\">Department of Typography and Graphic
  Communication, University of Reading</a>, UK. Graduated with distinction.</li>\r\n<li><strong>2001-2003</strong>
  Postgraduate Aggregate Typography [[Plantin Moretus Museum|Plantin-Moretus]] Society,
