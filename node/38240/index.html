Fontin Serif - a branch of the Fontin t(h)ree.

October 19, 2007 • Jos Buivenga

With the generous help of Typophile(s) I made [[http://typophile.com/node/4372|Fontin]] in 2004. [[http://www.josbuivenga.demon.nl/fontinsans.html|Fontin Sans]] followed in 2007 and I’m now working on Fontin Serif. I’ve little or no experience with serifs, so any remarks would be very welcome.

I like the general Fontin `feel', and the subtle curves in the serif bases.  I used Fontin Semi for my résumé to good effect, and may well switch when this is released.

That said, the capital J feels like it’s missing part of its tail (Semi & Serif have distinct tails), and the joints on the lowercase k & y feel bumpy' even at small sizes.&nbsp; Also, most of the serif are symmetrical, even when most serif fonts introduce an asymmetry (<em>e.g.</em> to keep the P from falling over' &c.).

—Joel
