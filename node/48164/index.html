Hi Rui,

I have 2 thoughts: I find this looks too close to Neo Sans. It also reminds me of other square sans designs like Apex, Amplitude and Stainless. Which brings me to my second thought: There are so many square sans designs out there. It’s a saturated area in type.

Also: The more mechanical a typeface looks, the more anonymous and undifferentiated I find it. Is there something really fresh or distinctive you can do with this? Right now it doesn’t seem like it has its own identity.

Hi, I quite like this. With all the other modern sans designs out there, it might not be easy to make it a commercial success, but perhaps that’s not the reason you are making this. I like this ampersand, its forms and proportions are neat. For me, the D and N are a bit wide. I wonder if the bottom right leg of the k could be chopped like the top (not horizontal ending). I think the tail of the Q could move right a bit? j might need a longer base. y looks too narrow and needs more space, and the brackets () too thin and pointy (this kind of tapering is not seen in any other glyph). I like the @. Just some ideas…see what you think :)
