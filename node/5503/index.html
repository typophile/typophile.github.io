2004-07-07 02:30:06 • Grant Hutchinson
This has come up before (or maybe somebody asked me about this via email), but I can't find the thread.

The large capital G, PG, and R date back to when the ratings were introduced in 1969 and were custom designed. Melior is similar as is the Renault font, but there are no off-the-shelf fonts that will match these. The 13 is Korinna Bold and the NC-17 is Stone Informal.

2004-07-07 08:06:25 • Bald Condensed
I believe these characters are different (see Mark's post above). NC-17 was added much later than the original ratings (in the 90s, I think) and intended or not, became the defacto replacement for the dreadful-sounding but seldom-used 'X' rating. Also PG-13 was created in the mid 1980s after Spielberg campaigned the MPAA and argued there was too much of a gap in between PG and R. (Many, including Spielberg, thought Indiana Jones and the Temple of Doom was too dark for the PG rating it received). As mentioned in one of those earlier threads, I don't think they redrew the seemingly-custom ratings logos, but simply patched them.
