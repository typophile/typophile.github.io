January 7, 2015 • Frode Bo Helland

I would very much appreciate your critique on this text typeface I’m working on. I am not so confident yet in the domain of antikvas. I suspect the wise thing to do, would be to not attempt to reinvent the wheel, but nevertheless these shapes have been on my mind for a while and I couldn’t bring myself to draw anything boring. It is intended for 9–10 points, sets darker than most text faces and the razor thin joins gives it a rather unique texture.

Ah. I forgot this board is pretty much broken. Apologies for the large image.

Some additional comments: The thinning of joins is not primarily inspired by pen traces – and hence, does not necessarily occur where expected. This makes me a bit unsure about how to handle contrast in other shapes. For the o, I’ve borrowed an idea from Fleischmann – something I’ve also echoed to some extent in b/d/p/q. Curves get a different treatment in terms of speed/angle/dynamism for inside and outside – if there is such a thing as inside and outside here.

The a and (the bottom of the) g breaks the logical shoulder treatment of n, but they’ve got each other (as does the numerals, ampersand and Q), and I’ve been pondering adding r to that party too. And X? X is a disaster!

Is /U too narrow? The spine of /S a tad too straight? The tail of /Q a bit too straight and abruptly cut? I could imagine the latter to profit from some thickening or even a serif.

Most accents strike me as too small and too little differentiated for the intended small text sizes. The ogonek could also use more heft.
