With a publication of 1950, I doubt you are going to find an exact match to the rest of your type in a digital font today. My guess is that it was some foundry’s take on a light Corvinus. Dennis Ortiz-Lopez' OL Corvinus Versailles might be as close as you get.

I have an image in a type book of a predigital Corvinus Medium Italic that has an alternate swashed A and M. It’s was too heavy and not a match to your image - but it reflects the many variants that were being produced decades ago.

As digital alternative to Corvinus, you may like [[http://commercialtype.com/typefaces/giorgio/giorgio|Giorgio]] by Christian Schwartz with main difference on /R leg bottom but available as true italic. No slanted swashy alternates like your /A though.
