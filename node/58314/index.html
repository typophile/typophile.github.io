Short descenders, big accents, what about the undercomma?

May 23, 2009 • blank

An undercomma that is comparable in size to punctuation and to marks that sit above the characters often looks huge—at least to me—in a typeface with short descenders. I don’t think that I’m the only person who feels this way, as there are some great typefaces where the undercomma is short and gets cut off at the descender line. Is either method considered correct in most languages that use the undercomma? Is either consider unsightly or in poor taste?

Hi James

Depth of descenders aside, there are quite a few instances of punctuation (commas included) being enlarged against the character sizes to cope better with low-resolution imaging. This is a functional consideration – otherwise why (as a designer) would you run the risk of having the reader confuse a comma for a full point?

Matthew Carter’s Bitstream Charter and Erik Spiekermann’s FF Meta are specific examples of this, but I’m sure there are others.
