off-horizontal/-vertical lines: avoid or embrace?

November 24, 2008 • eliason

In my [[http://typophile.com/node/50618|first font design]] I have, by design, used some lines that are just slightly off of true vertical and horizontal. FontLab notifies me of this and offers to true them up. At first I thought this was just the software thinking (mistakenly) that it knew my intention and offering to help. But now it occurs to me that there may be technical reasons why slightly off-horizontal or off-vertical strokes should be avoided, in terms of generating the font, or displaying it, or printing it.

Will preserving my slightly off lines come back to haunt me later for some such technical reason?

I guess (and I’m no expert) that the slightly off perpendiculars won’t fare well on screen?
2008-11-24 23:20:22 • 1985
On screen it isn’t as much of an issue– with modern anti-aliasing technologies you will see a gradient line of pixels along one edge as the line crosses the pixel column between vectors. It becomes a problem in print. However highly pitched, even the best imagesetters still produce a dot matrix output, and may have trouble expressing such a minute slope.
