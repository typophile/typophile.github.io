An almost identical A – with the angled cross stroke – is found in F. W. Goudy’s National Old Style and Nabisco fonts of 1916 and 1921. National: Nabisco: National Old Style is available as a “prop font,” i.e. an unedited autotrace from HPLHS [A Lovecraft fan and gaming group] http://www.cthulhulives.org/toybox/propdocs/propfonts.html National is shown in detail at pages 300 to 305 of the ATF Catalog for 1923. Luc Devroye has an informative page http://luc.devroye.org/fonts-26299.html The distinctive leg on the R is very similar to the one on Nicolas Jenson’s Roman. See
http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/adobe/jenson/ http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/spiecegraphics/nicolas-jenson-sg/ http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/jenson-classico/ http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/linotype/jenson-classico/ Don
