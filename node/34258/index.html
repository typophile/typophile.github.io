2007-06-06 05:37:51 • reese

Hello, I am impressed with your willingness to embrace suggestions made by other readers so I hope I’m not out of line offering my thoughts on your latest logotype ideas.

I think the dot under the r/j makes it look like an up-turned j, it no longer reads as an r.

The third idea works best for me because the lowercase thing seems a bit played out; I like the shapes of the lowercase letters but there is a risk of it looking too Web 2.0 (something you’re trying to avoid).

The combination of the lowercase, the rounded type, the rounded corner (the ‘r’), and the colours are all Web 2.0 staples, so it might be worth dropping one of those elements if you want to keep away from that style. A list of Web 2.0 logos

Are there small caps in the font you’re using? this might make nicer shapes and a more classic, timeless logotype (just an idea).

The simple r shape would be very flexible across a range of media and would work without any accompanying text.

Good luck
