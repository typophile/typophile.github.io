2011-09-28 09:48:03 • bendy

I think the aspect that Cyrus was responding to, which he characterized as “wrapping outside curves around the inside,” is slightly different than what WAD was expressing with his M-formula memo. (Note: Dwiggins never used this “wrapping” language, not that I recall ever encountering in his letters anyway.)

It’s all generally related, of course, because Dwiggins was a multi-faceted thinker; but what Cyrus calls “tension” I believe Dwiggins would have referred as “snap” or “action.”

The thinking that lead to the M-formula memo only crystallized several years after Electra (and never found successful expression in any of his completed typefaces). I would say the M-formula is a subset of Dwiggins’s broader explorations of Action in type.
