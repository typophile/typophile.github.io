Farnham is a very good suggestion, but perhaps a little overused these days? I’d take a look at [[http://www.felicianotypefoundry.com/main/?page_id=88|Eudald]], which is distantly related (Farnham is loosely based on the types of Johann Fleischmann, while Eudald is a revival of his Spanish imitator Eudald Pradell’s types. The web page doesn’t mention OT versions of the typefaces, but I know Mário Feliciano started working on them more than a year ago. They’re probably finished long ago now).

2009-08-20 22:13:29 • Sindre

Base your decision on how the face looks in an online type tester, for reasons such as the fact that sifr does not support kerning or tracking.

So the display styles of Brown Gothic might be good for a sans headline, as they are tightly fitted.

Other Shinntype news faces with display styles: Nicholas, Pratt, Walburn, Worldwide.

And I would say, make your headlines big, using display faces, because it will create a more sophisticated quality than what people are used to when they see default web faces blown up.
