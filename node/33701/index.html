Sharon, at first glance, I know you said this was a place holder, but it’s not in the best practice whatsoever to paste large images and then image map them out for various links, etc.

It’s early so Ill throw some stuff in your direction. . .

Not sure about the “volume 1 first issue” perhaps you can incorporate it elsewhere without that unsightly white box. Perhaps you can centre the whole page, and drop the white box and text (therefore no white box) into the white area. Lot’s of empty space that shows off the photograph which to me is both good and bad. The navigation page is pretty. . . but sort of lacking direction and ease of use…Again I know it’s not final, but I dont really know where Im going or what Im clicking on. '

Why so we want to make it look like a magazine? The web and print are two different beasts, and crossovers can happen, but for example all those numbers, page numbers, thats decoration, not function, and if anything it might lead people astray.

The page LOOKS great, very well laid out, but its ease of navigation, coding, etc. are iffy. The typography looks nice, but guess what, the web and typography are also a different beast. Fonts are limited unless you build a flash driven site. The layout you have created could work for a complex table based site (although I prefer CSS)

The centered text on far right, news and shows. . . ick, everything else is fl/rr.

So yea I like it, its pretty. But for a real website thats coded instead of a simple jpeg image, it will rather different. The typography for starters. Look forward to seeing the final.

2007-05-11 20:50:36 • Sharon Van Lieu

Heads up on the Flash. You might get some backlash. The photographers I’ve worked with seem very gullible/interested in google search engine rankings. One of the most popular photoportfolio sites, LiveBooks is pure Flash and I guess they’re getting a lot of heat from their users because google isn’t picking up any of the content off their all-flash sites.

The other issue that I think is an issue for any photographer looking for contract work is that flash sites are quite often not bookmarkable. It can be nice to be able to bookmark specific images to pass around to internal designers/art directors who might be considering the person’s work.
