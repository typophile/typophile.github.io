2009-06-10 16:56:10 • Ricardo Cordoba

According to Hermann Zapf it’s actually lettering by John Edward Benson. I’m not sure who Benson is or if he sourced Optima in his design.

Huh? According to Hermann Zapf where and when? In Linotype’s brochure for Optima nova, there is a photo of the Vietnam Veterans Memorial lettering, because it is done in Optima. For the same reason, in at least one of Hermann Zapf’s books, a picture of a rubbing from the wall appears as well.

You should know who John Benson is… aside from being a type designer in his own right, he ran the John Stevens Shop in Newport, RI for decades, and undertook a number of prominent lettering commissions in Washington, DC (and elsewhere). His son Nick runs the business now as far as I know. I’m sure that David Berlow could fill in more details.

The story that I had been told was that Maya Lin spoke with him about the lettering. It would have been too labor intensive to letter all of those names by hand, so a typeface was used instead. Apparently, Benson suggested Optima to her, and it was used.
