If you are meaning a Sans Serif that follows the same classic feel as Helvetica:

A more “square” Sans Serif could be [[http://www.fontshop.com/fonts/downloads/linotype/univers_lt_std_65_bold_ot/|Univers]], but it is not exactly “squared”. It appears more “square” when compared to Futura or Helvetica. The “square” parts of it are the counters and the bowls, but still, like I said is only has the subtle nuance of “square-ness.” Also, [[http://www.fontshop.com/fonts/downloads/fontfont/ff_din_ot_bold/|DIN]] might fall into this as well.

If you are looking for a square typeface might want to try:

[[http://www.fontshop.com/fonts/downloads/bitstream/square_721_bold/|Square Bold]] [[http://www.fontshop.com/fonts/downloads/linotype/eurostile_lt_std_bold_ot/|Eurostile]]

These faces are as far as you can get before moving into perfectly geometric characters or “techno” fonts (I hate using that term as a classification):

[[http://www.fontshop.com/fonts/downloads/font_bureau/agency_bold/|Agency]] [[http://www.fontshop.com/fonts/downloads/elsner_flake/bank_gothic_medium/|Bank Gothic]] - small caps
