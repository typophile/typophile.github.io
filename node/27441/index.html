2006-08-04 18:38:19 • Nick Shinn

Nick:

You don’t want to buy one. You just want a scan done on one. Most large printing houses or pre-press specialists should have a drum scanner available. In your city, Grafikom (was Quebecor MIL) has a good reputation. I’m sure there are others.

The problem is that they’ll need to wrap your printed type around the drum. So bound books are out. Unless you want to involve that camera you mentioned.

Drum scanners provide great resolution. But the technology is becoming dated. Some printers are now tending towards high-end large-format flatbeds. They might offer the resolution you require. Check with your friendly neighbourhood printing house.
