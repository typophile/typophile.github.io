I suggest you take a direct approach to fix the problem beginning with a call to Apple tech support. If it's the same issue that occurred previously and was sent to them for repair, even if it is now out of warranty they will most likely fix it. Sometimes it's just a simple fix you can do that they will instruct you over the phone such as resetting the PRAM or PMU switch. (Worst case, the blank screen sounds like possibly a logic board or inverter cable problem, not the battery.) If the screen still works at times, you might want use this opportunity to backup all of your important files onto DVDs or a backup drive. Most likely you'll be sending the laptop out for repair.
