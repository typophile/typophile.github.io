body: "<strong>[[Indices]] : [[Terminology]] : Pangram</strong>\r\n\r\nPangrams are
  short passages that include all 26 characters of the [[Alphabets|alphabet]]. They're
  commonly used to help users of the font quickly assess a character set. They may
  also be useful to designers in the process of designing their own work but they
  are too compact for a more thorough assessment. Perhaps the most common pangram
  is <em>The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.</em>\r\n\r\nClever pangrams:\r\n<em>Boxer
  M. Tyson packs his bags with Jay-Z DVDs, Quik, Holyfield's ear.</em> -- BJ Harvey\r\n\r\n<em>Typophile
  Boxing! Papazian jousts with quaking, anonymous detractor. Fight at eleven.</em>
  -- [[Joseph Pemberton|Joe Pemberton]]\r\n\r\n<em>Abacist\u2019s deaf dog hijacked
  luminous parquetry studio, avows ex-yakuza.</em> -- [[Hrant Papazian]] (This is
  a rare, alphabetically ordered pangram.)\r\n\r\n<em>Wham! Volcano erupts fiery liquid
