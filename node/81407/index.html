2011-04-21 17:53:03 • tmac

“If you think technique is meaning, you might find me very simple”.

Technique IS meaning, and artists such as the Velvets went out of the way to disappear theirs, Sister Ray says, which is a technique in its own right. Isn’t that what Ruscha was trying to do by designing his own “type”? That’s why it is deliberately amateurish/vernacular (that, and the fact that he is no Wim Crouwel).

Andy Warhol was technically brilliant in many media, and he was an accomplished commercial illustrator before he dropped the a. One would have to say that any apparent lack of technique in his work is quite intentional, and that it does require skill to make something brilliant while looking like you’re not really trying.

Damien Hirst’s “nutraceutical” packaging is a terrible parody of commercial design. Bad art direction: he should have hired a branding agency to do the work, not Barnbrook, whose sensibility is far from corporate. But then, if it had looked like the real thing (see below), what would have been the point? So it’s just a bad idea.
