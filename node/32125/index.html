Font suggestion for after school program logo

March 10, 2007 • hereandthere

i am looking for some suggestions for a children’s after school arts program logo.

after speaking with the client and asking what she wanted the logo to convey, she responded:

free-flowing whimsey, creativity, and individuality within a clean organized structure. And the font clean and kid-like.

i have presented the first round and she likes the font NB Fett, which i used here (slightly altered): http://www.identifont.com/show?BT5

but she prefers this design (but not the countryhouse font)

i’m not convinced that this design is completely the best direction, but it is perhaps a good starting point. so any suggestions for other kid-like fonts are greatly appreciated. maybe something that is somewhat bulbous or rounded, but not TOO goofy. as she said, the font should be clean and kid-like.

thanks, gregg

You might like Handsome. It wasn’t made to be a “kid” typeface, but it always reminds me of a grade school student’s model script. I’d go with the monoline versions (not Classic, Rough, or Nib), as they seem more childlike compared to the refinement of a modulated stroke.
