Hi there. Since i’ll soon be done with my BA hons Multimedia Arts in a few months, its time for me to start floating my resume around :D.

Being relativey new to typography, I’d love to have some of your opinions and suggestions on choosing fonts for a 2 page resume for a guy with a year’s experience, type which is humble, non-submissive and truthful :).

I really don’t know much about typography so please pardon me if I do say something silly :o!

As of now I’m considering Helvetica Black, Shannon Book, Stone Sans, Myriad Pro, Cochin, Stempel Garamond Roman and even Frutiger 55 Roman.

first, i would say NOT to have a resume be longer than two pages, for the design fields it can look a bit amateurish. also, picking a typeface isn’t just about what you think looks nice, it is about what typeface best reflects you and your work. for example, what type of work do you want to do (corporate, fun, bands, movies, etc…)? i don’t think anyone can tell you what typeface to use because its very personal.

secondly, although i’ll go as far as saying i think its too much to use more than ONE typeface…some will say its okay to use more than that. i personally stick with one typeface and go with different weights and variations of the same face (ie, frutiger bold, frutiger roman, frutiger light, frutiger italic).

really there are no rules, just make it look right.

From my experience at work..I’m allowed to use 2 fonts in 3 sizes total per design. For a resume I would use the same font (different weights like suggested in previous post) I cant use two different serifs or san serifs in the same design. I mix a fancy original serif or script with a more plain serif like futura. I usually allow about 3-4 pts of size in between titles,subtitles and info like addresses on business cards or letterhead. For a resume you could use 10pt for info 12 for subtitles and 14 for your name. I would suggest exploring all the different weights in Frutigar..it’s a great font with allot of options. My best advice is to keep it clean and simple and maybee put a nice logo at the top to represent yourself. Some people say a logo is even too much but I think it’s helped me find work and gives it an original touch without having to spend too much time with fancy type. Good Luck

Shannon looks like a conservative comic text, its the most original looking to me out of all the ones you listed.*
