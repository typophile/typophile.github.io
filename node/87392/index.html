2011-11-15 17:30:54 • RadioB

Do you think today’s new typefaces will still be in use fifty years from now?

Of the types I published 10 or 15 years ago, some have proved to be more durable than others. Beaufort, Handsome, Bodoni Egyptian for instance; Morphica and Artefact, not so much. At the time of design, it’s impossible to know which is the mayfly, which the quahog. But I do believe that one or two may still be in use 50 years from now.

Certainly, it helps to update formats: I’ve gone from PostScript Type 1 (“legacy”) to OpenType and now web fonts. Nonetheless, as long as the legacy formats continue to be supported by layout applications, all manner of typefaces will survive. It helps that in the digiverse there is scant difference between warehouse and museum—which retailers delete poorly selling stock? The tale is long.
