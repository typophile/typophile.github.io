The T Files: Emoticon As An Ending Paren?

January 17, 2011 • Richard Fink

An old friend of mine from Brooklyn, NY, Michelle Herman, professor of creative writing/comparative lit at Ohio State University posted using an emoticon as an ending paren. As in her quip: “OK, I hereby declare myself in charge of deciding how to punctuate using a parenthetical emoticon: said emoticon WILL do double-duty as a close-parenthesis. (And that is the last word on that subject :)"

Hmmmm… Whaddaya think? It is economical. (This is certainly a case for the Typophiles :-)

Reckon her right-leaning smileys will be expected to moonlight as opening parens, then.
