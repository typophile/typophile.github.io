I like the M treatment a lot. It is unique and relates well to the concept of minimalism.

However mixing the uppercase M into lowercase text is visually disturbing. When the label is read, the eye first catches the leading M and the “minimal” is perceived as an uppercase text. Then there’s a visual break after “L” and a lowercase part starts. Something like this:

. So the resulting impression is that the text is set in two parts - uppercase and lowercase - which have the same vertical size. This looks strange and not exactly visually pleasing.

M treatment does look nice, but I think it doesn’t play well with this logo because lower case letters tend to be round. Because Ms have cut outs they are even more sharp than normal.

It’s also quite distracting that “al” are stronger/heavier compared to other letters. So the whole logo does look nice to some degree but it should be finished properly.

Someone else’s comment on having upper case letters to represent minimalism does hold water here. I guess.
