I am afraid Renko is right. Read this link: http://www.dianova.ch/en/events/archives/0901-visual-identity.html of which here is an extract:

January 2008 - For its tenth anniversary, Dianova decided to revise its visual identity in order to adjust it to the organization’s current reality. To live up to this objective, the logo had to convey the continuity of Dianova’s unchanging values, while, at the same time, present them with a new, more modern and brighter image.

To reach this objective, we utilized the flexible and dynamic typography of the DIN font family, which we felt was also capable of communicating value and innovation. In addition, in order to give more depth to Dianova’s ongoing innovative character, the letter ‘n’ was modified to make it different and unique, emphasizing the way we look, through different eyes, at the rapidly changing problems of modern society.

More information here http://www.dianova.ca/newsletter/16/fr/page03.html (in French).
