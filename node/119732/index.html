2014-09-24 23:23:07 • Thomas Phinney
One thing you didn't mention in your original post is what type of design you are doing. If it is a display font, Small Caps may not be appropriate whereas with a text face, they likely would be. Having said that, it is not an ironclad rule because some display faces would merit having Small Caps. Examine current designs done by others that are similar and use those as a starting point.

2014-09-25 15:53:49 • Aardenon
A combining mark has zero advance width, and its glyph outline is set to the left of the start. This makes it appear over the previous character when it is typed. Try to position it for the width of an ideal average lowercase character; the results will often be inexact.
