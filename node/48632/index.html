Can’t beat the LDN for the autumn. Have a great time!

Eric Gill is all over this fair city, Broadcasting House (BBC) can be found in Portland Place and has one of his sculptures (Prospero & Ariel). Also, hit the tube! Some of the stations have some beautiful typography from a bygone day, the finish to some of the stations; tiling, murals etc might catch your fancy. The outsides of some stations still have some old school type too. Check the big ones, Waterloo, Charring Cross, Great Portland etc. A little morbid, but damned grand, is Highgate Cemetery - lots of type chiseled into over the top mausoleums.

Also, as a Londoner, a nice stroll is from Waterloo, past the wheel, over a bridge up through embankment and onto Trafalger Sq, There you’ll find the National gallery, St Martins in the Fields (a real nice building, just finished renovating), and if you want you can carry on past Leicester Sq and into the West End proper. You’ll see lots of type! Sounds complicated, but you just keep heading north. Easy. Just stay north of the river. No further south than Victoria!
