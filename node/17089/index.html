2006-01-05 16:08:16 • Stephen Coles

Asvetic, I may have been the Extensis support person who handled your incident. If not, I would agree with the previous assessment; this issue is really rare. I didn’t see anything like it in our support database. In my experience, fonts that fail in Suitcase also fail in the system with Suitcase disabled. In your case, I’m not suggesting that your font has a problem, since there is so little information. You could try starting Suitcase with a fresh database to see if that changes anything. To do so find the folder C:\Program Files\Extensis\Suitcase 9.2\Data\ and move it to your desktop. Then restart Suitcase and it will generate a new, empty database. Add the font again and test it to see if the problem persists.

On Windows in general, problems with Postscript fonts are less common. My hunch is that this is partly because Windows users have been less likely to modify fonts themselves. Also, since there are fewer out there, it’s more likely that they’re newer and from a major foundry. Suitcase doesn’t do anything with the fonts internal components, so usually fonts either work or they don’t.
