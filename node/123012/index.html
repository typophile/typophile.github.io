Found a match to your font. Fontcraft/Scriptorium sells a version as “Rousseau” in its Nouveau Gothic Package http://www.fontcraft.com/scriptorium/masterfonts/ —start quote— Our first Master Fonts package features three type styles based on late 19th century designs which were influenced by Gothic calligraphy filtered through the Arts and Crafts and Art Nouveau movements. Kelmscott is based on William Morris' classic Troy type design. Joyeuse is based on an English font of the same period which was clearly influenced by Morris. Rousseau is derived from a slightly later French design which shows many of the same influences. —end quote— Rousseau is probably not the original name of the font, a heavier version of which would seem to be the original source of the lettering on your Columbia record titling. Note that Rousseau is a lighter version of the letters than the ones in the Columbia record titling. Don
2015-02-02 19:56:11 • donshottype
Solved more of the puzzle. The original name of the font seems to be Kloster-Gotisch and it is German rather than French. I believe that Scriptorium called it “Rousseau” because this name appears in the type specimen, as published by Ludwig Petzendorfer The specimen is for the “magere” or lighter weight. I believe that your lettering is from the “fette” or bold weight of Kloster-Gotisch or from an extremely similar font. Don
