Six letters

April 26, 2007 • Hiroshige

I bolted upright in bed this morning to song of birds singing their heads off, they were eating breakfast from the feed I put out the night before… Inside my head - I could still see shapes from the sleep of the night before.

The shapes were letters - six of them - spelling the word SPARKS.

And from the architecture which ran down either side of the letters I immediately recognized where these letters stood - they stood right straight down the middle of Sparks street mall - a venerable historic cobblestoned pedestrian street mall in Ottawa.

The letters stood 3-4 feet high, and had a substantial 3D volume to them - built of steel sheet and finished with a baked acrylic paint of bold primary or secondary colour. And each letter had it’s own identity coming from the typeface it was of. And built with each letter is a two sided internally illuminated display panel which the stores of Sparks use to show off their stuff.

The letters were widely tracked running quite some length of Sparks.

All day long this friggin image nagged me - not unlike like thousands of images have done before - so what could I do but pick up the phone and call the Sparks street mall merchants association and get this thing off my mind…

After a really clumsy intro I layed out my idea to the gal in charge - and she just loved it!!, wants to see thumbnail sketches with a brief description to present to her board at the end of the month. In fact - she says to me that the mall is looking for just such a fresh idea to construct now that they’re tearing down what is.

Okay… this thing now has my attention.

…and my curiousity.

Hiro
