Buying fonts for projects

October 20, 2005 • alexfjelldal

I am designing a book for a client, and i am wondering about purchasing a font for this project. The book will not be part of series, but i would sure like to keep it for later use. Who should pay for the font? Me or the customer?
If you want to keep it, you ought to pay for it. Of course, that doesn’t preclude your charging your customer a fee for use of the font.
2005-10-20 15:26:35 • oldnick
I don’t usually charge the client for fonts. If the client wants to use the font themselves, I tell them to buy it themselves. To me, fonts are overhead, like computer equipment and office supplies. Each new job is an opportunity to expand my existing font library for future jobs.
2005-10-20 16:24:08 • Mark Simonson
I agree with Mark on this one. When I license type it becomes part of my toolbox from which I can later draw for future clients. However, if the client does like the typeface, and I’ve had this happen, you can always educate them and show them how they can license it for their own use.
