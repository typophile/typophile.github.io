This cover is from the early 70’s and I believe it is related to Clarendon. Other fonts that close but were ruled out include Egyptienne, Rockwell, Chunkfive and Blackoak.

Not perfect (mainly the x-height), I like [[http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/bagraphics/champ-ultra|Champ Ultra]] by Bob Alonso for the big red central title.

I believe this early 1970s book cover for a New York publisher would very likely have used Behemoth by Dave West at Photolettering 1960, probably photo narrowed to fit the cover. Digitized as PL Behemoth http://www.myfonts.com/fonts/mti/atbehemoth/ Don

The Photolettering company, like others in the field, produced several widths of its designs. Behemoth, as used in Chariots of the Gods? appears to be the condensed version of Dave West’s Behemoth. The digitized PL Behemoth is the semi-condensed version. An expanded version [or perhaps the regular] was digitized by Bob Alonso as Champ Ultra in 1995. Don
