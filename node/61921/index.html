I have a really bad habit. Whenever I use a font in a logo (and other designs), I can’t help but to tweak it. One reason is just to meet the needs of the design. The second reason is that it messes with the heads of the competition when a client brings them my art to recreate. What can I say. I work in a very competitive market where professional courtesy and copyright infringement are secondary to money.

I’ve noticed that almost all EULAs do not allow for modification of their fonts. However, it is not clear if this includes modifying the outlines to fit a specific design? I’ve always assumed that it was to protect against changing the font file itself?

Modification of the curves in Illustrator, Freehand, CorelDraw or whatever is okay by me; in fact, my EULA even allows modification of the actual font itself IF the modified font is used internally only by the licensee, and not resold. Few other foundries allow this latter instance but, if the changes make the font work better for a specific company and/or application, I don’t see it as a problem.
