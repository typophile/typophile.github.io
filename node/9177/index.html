2005-02-19 14:45:06 • peterbruhn
What does one put in a spec book? At the one extreme, it's more specification than specimen, and that's the strtategy I followed here, 26 pages showing permutations of tracking at different sizes for all the Bodoni Egyptian family members, text block settings with different leading at a range of sizes, as well as full character complements of each font. (My inspiration was the old Alphatype CRT system spec sheets, c.1980, which I found to be most useful back in tha day.) But it's so much work, I've only ever finished this kind of specimen for one of my faces: http://www.shinntype.com/Assets/Bodoni_GIFS/Bod.pdf Erik van Blokland automated the whole procedure, of course, when he was producing the type sample part of Druk magazine (in print only, sorry Noah). For my full collection specimen, I attempted to combine everything on one page for each typeface: http://www.shinntype.com/Assets/ShinnType.pdf *** Absolutely awesome: http://www.pismolijna.cz/pdf/StormTypeLibrary.pdf (but not much color…)
