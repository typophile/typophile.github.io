The assasination of Benazier Bhutto

December 28, 2007 • James Arboghast

This must be the single worst act of political violence we’ve seen this year. A beautiful woman shot in the neck by an assassin who then blew himself up, killing other 15 people. Pakistan has been thrown into dissarray, with ten dead in riots, and Al Quaeda has claimed responsibility for the attack.

I’m struck blind with horror and grief at the sheer violence of this act, at the notion of cutting down a woman who had dedicated her life to reinstating democracy. I am sheddingtears over this. Take a few minutes away from fonts, dear typophiles, find a quiet dark corner of the house, kneel down the floo—and pray. Pray for Benezier Bhutto, pray for her family, pray for her devastated country—pray that the violence does not proliferate and spread.

[img:sites/default/files/old-images/rredrose_6379.gif]

I’m not a religious person, but I believe in the power of prayer. If our thoughts are with Mrs. Buhtto, our hearts and minds will follow thru the power of collective consciousness.

Here on Public Television, the News Hour, a former Pakistani official who had worked in her father’s government said that Pakistan had neglected its people for 60 years, with the top 30 million ignoring the needs of the other 140 million. That included the previous administrations of Benazir Bhutto, which initially supported the Taliban. He said it would take decades to remedy all the damage done by previous governments. In spite of her past failings, many felt she was the best hope for progress and stability.

A dark and ominous day for the future of Pakistan and humanity.
