I think it’s derived from [[http://new.myfonts.com/fonts/hihretrofonts/advertisers-gothic/|Advertiser’s Gothic]] which was designed in 1917. I have not seen this “Deco” version in any of my books, including McGrew’s book on American metal types. I think it’s true that Scriptorium (Dave Nalle) got a lot of his fonts from scanning specimen books, but at least my library doesn’t seem to have this flared version of the typeface.

Mike, you jogged my memory. The source for “Advertising Gothic Deco” is a set of initials sold by Barnhardt Brothers & Spindler (BB&S) called “Publicity Initials”. It’s on page 341 of their 1925 catalog. The original design shows reverse letters only. The non-reversed version is apparently derived from that by Nalle. BB&S is also the source of Advertisers Gothic and Publicity Gothic.
