2007-05-11 10:25:49 • vinceconnare
LaCie are OK and they look pretty but don't be fooled into thinking the actual hard drive is any more reliable than any other brand. They don't make their own drives, just the pretty case. they all die. anything you have on a hard drive should be backed-up somewhere else – I know too many people with external hard drives as their main storage, with no back-up of that data (do they think the external drive will last forever?) So, presuming you have everything backed-up, the one thing that would sway me when I choose a hard drive is brand warranty. Seagate offer internal 'Barracuda' drives with a five-year warranty. Western Digital offer the My Book Pro with a three-year warranty (other versions of the MyBook range have one-year warranties). most other brands offer one-year warranties. These warranties don't stop them dying but they will get you a new drive if they do (and if everything is backed-up it's not such a problem) Did I mention how important it was to back-up your back-up?

2007-05-15 06:21:45 • fontplayer
I think it is true, as someone mentioned, that LaCie doesn't manufacture hard drives as much as it packages them. Very good connections, fans, housings, etc., which contribute to long life. I had one for over nine years, long past its usefulness, and it never failed. Also it is true, to a degree, that it doesn't matter what the hard drive is designated as, SCSI, ATA, SATA, FireWire, USB, eSATA, it is only the connections that make them what they are, so yes, you can house whatever externally with the right connectors. I run my internals off of a multi port hard drive controller card and use FireWire for externals. I use an internal drive as the main system and everything else drive, a second as a scratch disk, and a third as a document backup. The externals are all full drive back ups, including a portable (OWC Mercury) and a large multi-partitioned (for sequential back ups). SuperDuper (a cloner) is great for backing up everything to the external drives, quickly and reliably, and has saved my butt a couple times now. The word of wisdom with a drive is, "not if, but when" it will fail. And yes, back up your back-ups. Likely, your whole creative, and in some case, financial, life is on these drives. Gerald
