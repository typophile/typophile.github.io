2014-09-06 21:12:09 • Michel Boyer
Used to subscribe. I guess I am a semi-regular reader. The old issues I have available ATM (2008–2011) set blockquotes smaller with tighter leading, and they set long acronyms a point or two smaller. Enough to make it look noticeably lighter than surrounding text. Although I would love to see the New Yorker use proper small caps, Caslon’s are way to small for acronyms. In that sence, dropping the font size is a less jarring solution. If they are as perfectionist as they say, the limit is six letters: UNICEF is faux SC, MSNBC is all cap. Newspaper headlines sit between double curly quotes, but in the same style and weight as the surrounding text. I don’t know if they changed this in the new redesign. The designer of their new text typeface, Williams Caslon (used to be Adobe Caslon, I believe) is a regular here. His name is William Berkson. He might know more. Edit: Five letter acronym PIIGS also faux SC, NYPD caps. Not so consistent.
