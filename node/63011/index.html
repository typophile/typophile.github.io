NBC and Licensing

October 12, 2009 • markkenny

So I read on the tech' blogs NBC is being sued for $2million for pirating a few fonts. The blogs are repeating the same copied lines (another copyright issue surely?) and the comments are “Fonts are too expensive and EULAs unworkable anyway”.

How does FB know NBC pirated their fonts and not have one person doing all the work and supplying JPGs? NBC must have a leak who grassed them up.

I agree with Font Bureau and would really like to see this come to an end. I managed licenses for 60 users and have invested about €30k in various font licenses, each with their own restrictive EULA, it’s a nightmare trying to maintain control and more and more users are saying “I just want to try it” and “how can I convince client to buy it if I can’t test it”.

From my perspective we’re now in a world where we expect client to pay to use their choice of font, rather than the company paying for fonts as well as Photoshop and Word.

Anyone else manage large groups of licenses?

“It’s a nightmare trying to maintain control and more and more users are saying “I just want to try it” and “how can I convince client to buy it if I can’t test it”.

No sympathy here. Buy it, or don’t use it. There are a lot of things in life I’d like to try, but I can’t unless I commit to it.

Boo hoo hoo

Unfortunately you can’t have it both ways - if you want to give the creatives freedom to choose any font from any vendor you will end up with them picking expensive fonts, and those with licenses that are difficult to manage (such as extra fees for use in logos and publicly posted PDFs, or prohibit commercial use as some dafont fonts do).
