Contemporary Classic?

November 16, 2006 • James Provost

Working on a grad book for my illustration program. There will be 80 students participating in the book, each with their own spread. Full page illustration to the right, name, contact information and a small spot illustration or sketch on the left.

Trying to convince my classmates, who wouldn’t be opposed to setting the whole book in Arial, that our audience (Art Directors, Creative Directors) will be more discriminating of font choice, and that we’ll be making a mistake settling with a standard Adobe Illustrator/InDesign font.

I’m partial to Mrs.Eaves. Conservative Roman that will sit quietly opposite an illustration, but with artsy ligatures that light up when you take a second look. Should complement all styles and subjects of illustrations. But it’s a hard sell at $299 USD.

The title/theme is °CELSIUS (as in ‘degrees Celsius’). We’re the first year graduating from the program with a BA. Don’t worry, no thermometers in sight for the design.

So? Any suggestions? Font choice? How to sell them on the idea?

Mrs Eaves is VERY hard to set, believe me. If you pull it off, it will be great, but there are so many practical problems that this job could turn into a mine field.

Why not try Eidetic Neo instead? Not that many ligatures, but a very peculiar italic and an intriguing Omni unicase variation. It’s almost half the price at US$159.

2006-11-16 09:12:52 • Bald Condensed

Well, Mrs Eaves is neither flesh nor fish – it has the very small x-height of a display face but humongous serifs like a micro face, and the kerning is totally whacky. You have to customise the parameters quite a bit in order to get it right.

When I set Mrs Eaves I apply every single available ligature, but it usually takes twice as much work as any other font before I’m satisfied with the results. It’s a bit like breaking in a wild horse… every - single - time you use it.
