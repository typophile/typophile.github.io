American Printing Supplies, New York. My memory for names is terrible!

If not, take the best samples of your packing, or exact measurements with crease and angle measurements to a die cutter. You will need, if you don't have them, tympan, make ready and under sheets. Complexity is in the same order.

You can make them up on your own. The paper is generally sold in rolls. The rolls are expensive. Close to the core makes inferior packing. The results, unless you are professional, will predictably cause difficulty in giving equal Drum Tone.

Gerald Lange, or Jim Rimmer may have other suggestions.

NA Graphics bought out Vandercook some years ago but still has replacement parts and various materials and supplies.

NA Graphics Fritz Klinke P O Box 467, 1314 Greene Street, Silverton, CO 81433 970-387-0212 nagraph@frontier.net

Gerald's items, in Vandercook-speak, would be top blankets (drawsheets), under blankets, undersheets (.006 manila). NAG has a catalog. Basically a mylar top blanket and Kimlon under blanket (about $50) will do you, along with the undersheets. These are all cut to fit your press, which had a long production life, so there are still lots of parts, etc. On a Vandercook, various thicknesses of professional grade tracing paper will work nicely for minor makeready (actually taped directly to the tympan - Vandercooks aren't exactly production presses and aren't all that fussy). NAG also has a lithographer's sheet, called generically, non-slip paper, in various thicknesses. Works well as makeready and/or as an undersheet.

No real outlet for any of this stuff in LA anymore.

On top of the other advice you have received from G and G, I use old litho plates as bottom packing. This is cheaper than using up you oiled packing paper, and makes for clean impression, and is easy on your type.

I have made a cardstock template of the tympan for my press, and cut pieces to this size and shape from a fat roll of oiled packing paper.
