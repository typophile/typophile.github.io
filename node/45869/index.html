I like the look of the brushed-metal number. Very smart. I’m sure I’ve seen a Muji equivalent of it, but I could be wrong.

I have two portfolios, one for my design work and another for my photography. For the design work I use the [[http://www.londongraphics.co.uk/acatalog/PRAT_Pampa_Spiralbooks.html|Pratt Pampa series]]. They’re not cheap (and London Graphics certainly aren’t – theirs was just the first link I found) but they’re good quality, using thick, fine-grain leather and have a velvety inside. They use clear pockets, so there is a glare issue, but they look smart for years. I have a couple of them, my current one is about 5 yeas old and still looks quite new. The A4 version is best, because it’s small enough to throw into a bag. Why bother lugging a big thing around …

For the photograps I use archival polyester sleeves stacked loose in a portfolio box. Looks smart. Nice to lay flat open and move prints from one side to the other. Unlike the Pratt A4 book, this is far less portable. I print everything 16x12" and use a deep-ish box. I’ve got a carrying case for it all which though ugly, but does the job well. I use [[http://www.silverprint.co.uk/|Silverprint for all this stuff.]] They’re great, but I reckon you can get ‘em elsewhere too.

There’s a London/UK bias here I realise.
