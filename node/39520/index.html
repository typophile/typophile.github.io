Some issues concerning an Arabic dictionary

November 28, 2007 • Hyperboreus

Dear Fellow Typophiles, In the middle of November I was commissioned to design and execute a fresh Finnish–Arabic–Finnish Dictionary for general readers. Since my knowledge of non-Latin scripts is restricted to classical Greek – and some Russian – there are major difficulties to surmount. (1) Technical issues It is well known that the localized Adobe InDesign ME supports right- to-left scripts, but are there any cunning methods of importing Arabic to the standard version (i.e. Adobe InDesign CS2, English, Mac)? (2) Aesthetical issues The choosing of Arabic typeface seems somewhat outrageous. (e.g. some kufic fonts seem appealing to me, but I doubt whether they have any connection whatsoever with lexica...) The Latin scripts of our book might be set with ‘Linotype Trajanus’ or ‘Brioso Pro’, which do possess certain calligraphic flavour. (3) The lack of Arabic skills Indeed, shall I have the temerity to accept this captivating commission? Any suggestions or comments will be highly appriciated! Sincerely, P. L.
