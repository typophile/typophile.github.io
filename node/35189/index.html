If you want to do this properly, acquire some magazines from that era at old paper stores/markets, and a copy of “American Metal Typefaces of the Twentieth Century” by Matt McGrew (Oak Knoll), which will enable you to identify the faces. You’ll find that the 20s and 30s were radically different – going from illustration and historicism to photography and modernism. Knockout and Belizio are 19th century styles.

Broadway (Inline) is one of the most Art Deco faces, so is Radiant, which has a useful condensed. Bernhard Gothic and Kabel are more deco than Futura. Deco slabs (Stymie, Rockwell) are very different from the Egyptians – round curves and not much stroke contrast. See Keith Tam’s essay on this at Type Culture.com. Bodonis – especially the dark weights – were exemplary Deco faces.

Some comic book stores also have old magazines. Most “collector” flea markets have stalls with magazine traders and dealers. In Toronto, there is a twice-yearly “Old Paper Show and Sale”, as well as an “Old Book” show, where some of the dealers have magazines, so I would imagine there is something similar in your neck of the woods. Dealers who specialize in antique/vintage photos, postcards, movie posters, money, maps etc. often take out booths/stalls in such shows, so ask them when and where they show. AFAIK, trading on eBay has lessened the necessity for dealers to have stores, but they still find shows are useful. Google “paper ephemera flea market”. Good luck!
