More election design (but from the other side of the pond...)

April 23, 2008 • jacobh

[[http://www.londonelects.org.uk/pdf/2008%20Candidates%20booklet.pdf|This leaflet (pdf)]] arrived through my door this morning. After reading the posts about the American Presidential elections and their design, I was a bit stunned about how bad all of these are. For instance, why didn’t they justify the text (set in tight columns) on Left List candidate’s page; why did Siân Berry’s team choice a typeface with such an ugly â; and why are there so many letter collisions on Matt O’Connor’s page (this seems to be less obvious with the PDF)? I’m also a bit surprised that Brian Paddick chose UKIP’s colours (purple and yellow) for his campaign, but I suppose that would be less noticeable if they were not on adjacent pages. The most scary thing, however, is that picture of Ken Livingstone. In print, it looks like it was taken with a fish-eye lens!

Considering the amount the main candidates are spending, you would have though that they could have done better!

-Jacob
