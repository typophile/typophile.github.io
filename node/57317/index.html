Hi :) It would be really great to see a pdf version. The things I noticed as I scanned through: Generally the type seems quite small and the look is very clean. In the intro section, I'd remove the words 'According to Wikipedia'. I'd turn off hyphenation. That ellipsis needs to go. In the Lager section, you have two spaces between the forst and second sentences. The word 'in' in the first line also looks disconnected. I'd justify the text as it's in narrow columns. The 'Beer Timeline' needs more leading between the two lines of the title. It's not centred on the box. Typo 'Hops begins...' Capitalisation of 'Beer Making', 'Legions' and 'Brewers' pokes me in the eye. 'Indians' is not the correct word for native Americans where I come from. Is WooHoo two words and does it need a capital H? On the second page, the Name column needs the heading aligned with the text, as in the other columns. Wow, what a lot of beers! Would it be a good idea to sort them by brewery rather than alphabetically by name? I'd prefer the word 'style' to be 'type'...maybe that's just me though. Like the page numbering but perhaps bold would be a better contrast for the size of text. Hope that's helpful :)

Ok, I'm going to go out on a limb and suggest Plantin. I think a serif font is good. I find Caslon a bit light on the page and a bit too elegant somehow...I'm certainly not an expert though, so perhaps others can weigh in too. >What if i full-justify the paragraphs under “ALE” and “LAGER”? Should i still turn off hyphenation? I'd see how it looks with justified non-hyphenated text. You can always play with tracking and word space manually if it looks uneven. >Would it be best to align all of it (header, items, and letters) the same? Not sure, maybe. I quite like the outdented alphabet letters. That ellipsis at the start should grammatically be a colon; but because of the layout I think a full-stop would be better. The second sentence is ungrammatical. It reads "The basics of brewing beer ... are commonly categorized into two main categories". You have a missing subject (beer) which needs to go in there. (It's not the basics that are categorised, it's the beer, isn't it?) Actually, I'd reword the sentence completely because 'categorized...categories' is strange anyway. Perhaps: There are two main types of beer, and their methods of brewing are shared across cultures and national boundaries. Or: The two most common processes for brewing beer are shared across national and cultural boundaries, and result in two main types of beer. It's still not very interesting as a sentence. That's the problem with lifting text from Wikipedia; it's not written as menu copy ;) How about listing the 'styles' of beer under the two headings so we can see whether 'Imperial IPA' or 'Dunkelweizen' is an ale or a lager? I might put more space between the hops and the text too.
