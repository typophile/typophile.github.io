I agree that “BOUTIQUE” lacks a digital version. This is a creation from the photolettering/dry transfer era in which the harsh triangular serifs from a nineteenth century font called “Latin” http://www.castletype.com/html/tipoteca/latin-not-wide.html are pasted on a base font with vertical strokes having parallel sections and a thick and thin U. Compare to Doric which has softer curved triangular serifs which do not quite match. Or “BOUTIQUE” could be made by taking a slab serif font with vertical strokes having parallel sections and a thick and thin U, and converting most of the serifs to triangles. Don
