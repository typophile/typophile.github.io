Alphabets to Order: The Literature of
Nineteenth-Century Typefounders' Specimens

by Alastair Johnston Publisher: Oak Knoll Books; (October 2000)
Paperback: 220 pages ISBN: 1584560096 Price: $39.25  Buy It!

Reviewed by Poltroon: This book started out to show how Dadaism and concrete poetry were foreshadowed in the anonymous nonsense texts of type specimen books in the nineteenth century and grew into a much larger cultural study of the reading habits, politics and amusements of that era. The type specimens (many of which are illustrated) are a springboard to a wider discussion of many topics from hermits to the alcoholism of printers. Most of the fun occurs in the footnotes (there is even a fake footnote that can be deciphered as a reference to PALE FIRE by Nabokov). There are checklists of rare specimens in public & private collections in Britiain & the US which can be consulted & very useful family trees showing how the holdings of various typefounders passed from hand to hand. In addition new light is shed on the earliest American wood types cut for display in the 1830s as well as some interesting stuff on the graphic appeal of large type.

Encyclopaedia of Typefaces

by W. Pincus Jaspert, W. Turner Berry, A. F. Johnson Publisher: Seven Dials; (October 2001)
Hardcover: 520 pages ISBN: 1841881392 Price: $20.97  Buy It!

Reviewed by José Espejo: Not a bad book but a bit dated now. Its 50 years old and although recently updated, is missing some major typefaces such as Frutiger and only has limited specimens of fonts like DIN. Not very well laid out either but it does contain most of the major fonts with a paragraph on the provencance and history of each font. All in all a useful reference.

FontBook [Digital Typeface Compendium]

by Mai Linh Thi Truong, Jürgen Siebert, Erik Spiekermann Publisher: FSI FontShop International; (June 1, 1998) Hardcover: 1500 pages ISBN: 3930023024 Price: $109.00  Buy It!

Reviewed by Joe Pemberton: This six pound volume is the most comprehensive typeface reference book ever compiled - worthy of its self proclaimed ‘compendium’ status. It is also surprisingly usable. Each typeface entry provides info about its history, the designers and platforms for which the face is available as well as a “see also” feature that allows you to find similar faces.

Treasury of Alphabets and Lettering:
A Source Book of the Best Letter Forms
of Past and Present for Sign Painters,
Graphic Artists, Commercial Artists

by Jan Tschichold Publisher: W.W. Norton & Company; (April 1995) Paperback: 236 pages ISBN: 0393701972 Price: $24.50  Buy It!

Reviewed by David Kadavy: I would argue that this book is an “essential.” A little known treasure of Tschichold writing when compared to “A New Typography,” but every bit as valuable. Tschichold will illuminate you with detailed rationale behind his preferences for various type specimens. He also reveals some of his thoughts on proportions that are a great supplement to Bringhurst’s thoughts on the same in “The Elements of Typographic Style.” I owe a huge deal of my understanding of typography to this book.

Typology: Type Design from the
Victorian Era to the Digital Age

by Steven Heller, Louise Fili Publisher: Chronicle Books; (July 1999) Paperback: 196 pages ISBN: 0811823083 Price: $20.97  Buy It!

Reviewed by Joe Pemberton: What this book lacks in sophistication it makes up for in ambition. Few type books cover this full range of periods in a single volume and the material itself does provide a great visual reference if you’re looking for inspiration.      While this book is visually rich and the samples are excellent, it is light on commentary or historical information. This volume is larger than other Chronicle design books but it doesn’t take advantage of the added size. Many of the images are small and overly crowded. Overall, though, the price reflects the quality and I enjoy it as part of my library.
